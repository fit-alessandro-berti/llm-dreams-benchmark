You start to run toward a shadow that shouldn't be there in a light. The city breathes in your lungs; the streets are made of glass and forgotten names. You find yourself standing before a clock tower where hands don't exist but move through the air like smoke. "I have an hour," you think, but time feels like syrup on the tongue, sweet and sticky.

You start to trace the floor with your fingers until they burn slightly from the touch. The crowd isn't watching you; it's breathing. It is whispering in your ear that this meeting can never be lost if you listen hard enough. But where is the door? Is it here or behind the glass? You realize you should not move yet.

But then there is a sound—a low hum vibrating against your ribs like a heart monitor. The clock tower spins backward, and suddenly the hour isn't an hour, it's a memory of something ancient, of something that happened before the dream began. *You start to walk toward something in your head where you forgot how.*
