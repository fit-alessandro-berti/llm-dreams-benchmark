a set of brushes that seem to be made of light itself.

As you reach for one, the brush dissolves into your hand—or perhaps your hand dissolves into the brush. It's hard to tell here. The distinction between you and the things around you keeps slipping, like trying to hold water in a sieve.

You touch the canvas, and instead of paint appearing, a *sound* blooms across it—a deep violet hum that spreads in rippling hexagons. You laugh, and your laughter falls onto the canvas as golden rain, pooling at the bottom edge before soaking through and dripping upward into the sky.

The canvas begins painting *you* back. It sketches a memory you don't quite recognize: a door at the end of a hallway from a house you've never lived in, yet somehow miss terribly. The door is slightly ajar. Warm light spills from behind it.

"You can finish it," says a voice. You turn to find a figure made of shifting geometry—triangles becoming circles becoming something that has no name. Their face is familiar in the way all dream-faces are: everyone and no one at once.

"Finish what?" you ask, but your words come out as small blue birds that perch on the canvas frame.

The figure gestures, and you understand: the painting, the door, the dream itself—they're all the same unfinished thing, waiting.

You lift the brush of light. The hallway in the painting stretches toward you, or you shrink toward it, and suddenly you're standing before the door itself, brush still in hand, your heart beating in colors—

*thump* (amber)
*thump* (rose)
*thump* (a color you'll forget the moment you wake)

You press your palm against the door. It's warm, like sun-soaked wood, and it breathes gently, as if the whole house is sleeping.

The question hangs in the shifting air, written in letters made of moths:

*Do you open it?*

Your fingers curl around the handle. Behind you, the canvas waits. Ahead, the light pulses like a heartbeat that might be yours, might be the dream's.

You turn the handle, and as the door swings open, everything floods with white—

—and somewhere, far away, you feel the soft weight of morning pulling at your edges, the dream already beginning to dissolve like sugar in warm water, leaving only the faint impression of a color that doesn't exist and the certainty that, whatever was behind that door, it was waiting just for you.
