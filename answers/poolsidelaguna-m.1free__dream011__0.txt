
...written not in any language you recognize, but in symbols that shift and morph as you stare at them. The first question asks, "What color is the sound of your childhood fear?" The second: "Solve for the weight of a lie you once believed." A third question blurs into view, its letters rearranging into a map of a place you’ve never been but somehow know by heart—a crossroads at the edge of a forest, where two paths diverge, one paved with coins, the other with wilted flowers.  

Your pencil trembles, but before you can write, the exam paper begins to dissolve, ink bleeding into the air like smoke. The proctor—a figure whose face keeps shifting between your mentor, your younger self, and a stranger—leans in and whispers, *"The future isn’t something to be tested. It’s something to be built."*  

Suddenly, the room is gone. You’re standing at that crossroads in the dream, holding a hammer instead of a pencil. The paths have changed: the coins are now seeds, and the flowers are stars. You realize you don’t need to choose correctly—you just need to start walking.  

As you take your first step, you wake up, the smell of morning sunlight on your pillow, and a strange certainty that whatever comes next, you’ll shape it with your own hands.
