...a brush that hums softly in my hand, as if it's already alive with intention. The bristles shift between hues I can't quite name—colors that exist only in the spaces between waking and sleeping.

I dip the brush into the air itself, and it comes away coated in liquid twilight. When I touch it to the canvas, the surface ripples like water. My first stroke becomes a door, though I hadn't meant to paint one. It swings open of its own accord.

Through the doorway, I see a version of myself painting a canvas, and on that canvas is another doorway, and another me. The recursion stretches backward into infinity, each self slightly different—one has wings of folded paper, another is made entirely of clockwork, another is simply a column of warm light.

I try to paint something definite. A tree, perhaps. But as I work, the tree's leaves detach and become birds, the birds dissolve into musical notes, the notes arrange themselves into a word I almost recognize. The word is my name, but spelled in a language of shapes rather than letters—a triangle that means *longing*, a spiral that means *the feeling of almost remembering*.

The canvas begins to paint me instead. I feel brushstrokes across my skin, redefining my edges. I look down and find I'm becoming translucent, filling with the same shifting colors as the landscape around me.

And just as I start to dissolve entirely into the painting, I wonder: was I ever the painter, or have I always been what was being painted?

The brush falls. The canvas folds itself into a bird and flies away.
