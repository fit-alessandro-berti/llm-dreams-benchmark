The moment your fingertips graze the stretched edge, the air thickens into watercolor smoke. A ripple travels across the white surface, and suddenly it's drinking the sky: bruised violets melt into tangerine spirals, then fracture into glass-blue polygons that chime like distant wind bells. You don't hold a brush, but your attention becomes the pigment. Every half-formed thought, every quiet longing, spills onto the fabric as living geometry. A memory of rain folds itself into origami kites that detach and drift upward. A word you never learned to say crystallizes into silver cubes that roll downhill, gathering moss and static light.

The canvas breathes. It pulses against your palm like a second heartbeat. You step closer, and the ground softens into wet tempera. Your footprints bloom into constellations that slowly rotate, dragging comet-tails of saffron and slate behind them. When you press your hand flat against the center, the perspective folds. You're no longer observing the painting. You're standing inside it.

The horizon curves upward like the rim of a teacup. Rivers of liquid amber flow in slow, deliberate loops, carving valleys that whisper in vowels you almost recognize. A school of iridescent tetrahedrons swims through the air, dissolves into a downpour of cobalt, and rises again as steam shaped like handwriting. Time here doesn't march; it pools, eddies, occasionally runs backward through a crack in the atmosphere.

You realize the canvas was never blank. It was a threshold, and your presence was the gravity it needed to pull its hidden world into focus. 

A low, resonant hum begins somewhere beneath your feet, vibrating up through your ribs. The shifting shapes start to align, not into anything familiar, but into something felt. The colors slow their frantic dance, settling for one suspended breath into the quiet, warm outline of a door left ajar.

You step toward it. The canvas exhales.

And somewhere, far below the surface of the dream, your eyelids flutter as morning light finds them.
