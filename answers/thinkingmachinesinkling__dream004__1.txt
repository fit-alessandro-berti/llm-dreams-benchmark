...and as your fingers brush its surface, the white expanse doesn't stay white—it drinks the swirling hues around you. The canvas begins to breathe, its blankness a portal rather than an absence. You dip your hand into a passing cloud that has melted into liquid sapphire, and when you touch the fabric, it doesn't stain; it remembers. Shapes unfold like origami made of starlight—triangles that weep music, circles that taste like forgotten birthdays.

The landscape pauses its shifting, holding its breath, as the canvas starts painting *you* back: your silhouette dissolving into brushstrokes of gold and shadow, merging with the dream until you can't tell if you're the artist, the art, or simply another color waiting to change.
