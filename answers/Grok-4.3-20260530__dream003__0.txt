You are dreaming.

You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and glowing silver filigree that seem to breathe, rising and falling like the ribs of some ancient creature. The designs coil into impossible geometries—spirals within spirals, faces that dissolve into constellations, runes that rearrange themselves into words you almost recognize: *remember*, *choose*, *awaken*.

Your fingertips hover above the surface. The metal is warm, pulsing with a heartbeat that matches your own. A faint hum vibrates through your bones. Suddenly the patterns flare bright enough to cast shadows on the walls of the room you don’t remember entering. One symbol detaches, floats upward like a living moth made of light, and lands gently on the back of your hand. It sinks into your skin without pain, leaving a warm tattoo that shifts and whispers secrets only your dream-self can hear.

The box clicks. Not the sound of a lock opening, but the soft, wet sound of a flower blooming. The lid lifts of its own accord, releasing a ribbon of silver mist that smells of rain on forgotten libraries and the laughter of children who never grew old. Inside is not treasure, not darkness, but a perfect mirror of your own eyes—except they are filled with galaxies instead of pupils. The reflection smiles at you with infinite kindness and says, in your own voice:

“You never needed a key. You were always the lock.”

You reach inside. Your hand passes through the mirror like water. The moment your fingers touch the reflection, the entire box explodes into a thousand shimmering moths that swirl around you, lifting you off the ground. You tumble through corridors of memory that never happened and futures that might still. You meet a version of yourself from ten thousand years ago who hands you a single feather. You meet a version from tomorrow who returns it, now glowing. Time folds like origami until every possible you is standing in the same impossible room, all of them holding the same box, all of them finally understanding.

The moths settle on your shoulders like living epaulets. The box reassembles itself in your lap, smaller now, no longer locked. Inside rests only a single word written in light: *Enough*.

You close your eyes. The dream does not end—it simply exhales, and you float upward through layers of sleep like a diver rising from the deep. Just before you break the surface, the box whispers one last thing:

“Next time, bring someone with you.”

You wake with the taste of starlight on your tongue and the faint silver tattoo still glowing faintly on the back of your hand—already fading, but never quite gone.
