You start to walk down the nearest street, but the pavement ripples under your feet like water, and the buildings lean in as if they're eavesdropping. A taxi pulls up without you hailing it—its driver is a shadow wearing a hat made of old newspapers. "Where to?" it asks in your own voice. You say the first thing that comes to mind: "The place with the important meeting." The taxi laughs, a sound like static, and drives straight up the side of a skyscraper.

The city warps around you. Clocks on every corner tick backward, then sideways. You check your watch; the hour has already passed, but the meeting hasn't started yet. The taxi deposits you in front of a door that isn't attached to any building. You open it and step through into a vast conference room floating in the sky. Every seat is filled with versions of yourself from different ages, all arguing about what the meeting is even about. One of them hands you a glowing folder labeled "Why You're Late."

Just as you reach for it, the entire room tilts. The city below folds up like a paper map, and you tumble through the sky, falling toward the sound of your own alarm clock.
