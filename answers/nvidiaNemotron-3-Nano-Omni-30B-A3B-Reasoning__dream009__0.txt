You wake up to the scent of pine and the distant crackle of a fire. The air is crisp, and the sky above is a deep, unblemished blue—no wires, no humming machines, just the soft whisper of wind through the trees. You find yourself in a clearing surrounded by towering oaks and maples, their leaves rustling like parchment in a quiet breeze. A wooden cabin stands nearby, its logs weathered by time, smoke curling lazily from its stone chimney. There’s no electricity, no screens, no phones—just the raw, unfiltered presence of nature and the faint sound of a blacksmith’s hammer echoing from a nearby forge.

As you step out of the cabin, your bare feet sink into the soft earth. Ahead, a dirt path winds through the forest, leading toward a small village nestled at the edge of the woods. The villagers are dressed in simple woolen garments, their faces weathered but kind, and they move with a rhythm that speaks of a life lived in harmony with the land. You notice a well-tended garden with rows of vegetables, a basket of fresh apples on a wooden table, and the faint aroma of baking bread drifting from a cottage.

You realize you need to **learn the ways of this world**—to understand how to survive, to earn the trust of the villagers, and to uncover why you’ve been transported here. Without modern tools, every task will require patience and skill: gathering firewood, tending crops, mending clothes, or perhaps even helping the blacksmith craft something useful. But first, you must **introduce yourself and seek guidance**. The villagers are your only bridge to this new life, and their knowledge will be essential to navigating this unfamiliar time.

So, you take a deep breath, steady your nerves, and walk toward the village, ready to learn, to adapt, and to embrace the simplicity of a world without technology—where every sunrise brings a new chance to build something meaningful.
