In the distance, you spot a faint light—perhaps a fire, perhaps a mirage. Your stomach growls, reminding you that time is short. You remember the stories your grandmother used to tell: of a hidden sanctuary, a place where the old world’s knowledge was preserved, and where people lived in harmony with the land once more.

You press on, your boots crunching over broken glass and rusted metal. Along the way, you encounter a wounded stranger, half-buried under debris. They whisper a name—**Elias**—and a warning: *“They’re coming. Not just the raiders. Something worse.”*

You hesitate. Trust is a luxury you can’t afford. But something in their eyes—fear, desperation, maybe even hope—makes you pause. You offer water from your canteen, and as they drink, they speak of a map, etched on a piece of metal, that could lead you to the sanctuary.

Do you:
- Accept the map and follow the stranger into the unknown?
- Leave them behind, continuing alone toward the distant light?
- Or do you search for shelter first, knowing that survival may depend on it?

The world is silent but for the wind. The choice is yours.
