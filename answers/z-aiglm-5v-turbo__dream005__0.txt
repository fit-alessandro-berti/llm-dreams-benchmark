...and **the moment you step into their circles, the conversations halt abruptly, replaced by the sound of ocean waves crashing against a distant shore.**

You approach the first group—people you vaguely recognize from a job you had ten years ago. They are standing in a tight circle, passing around a small object. As you get closer, you realize they are handing each other their own eyes, plucking them out like ripe fruit and swapping them without flinching. When one of them notices you, they smile with teeth that are made of piano keys and asks, *"Have you brought the password for the weather?"*

You stammer an apology and back away, scanning the room. The lighting has shifted; the warm chandelier glow has turned into a harsh, neon blue. You spot your friend's distinctive red scarf near a buffet table piled high with books instead of food.

You push through the crowd, but the distance isn't working right. For every step you take toward the table, the room seems to stretch, elongating like taffy. You pass a group of people floating three feet off the ground, their ankles tied together with ribbons of smoke. They are singing a lullaby you remember from childhood but can't place. You try to ask them if they've seen your friend, but when you speak, bubbles float out of your mouth instead of words. They pop against people's faces, smelling of sulfur and vanilla.

Finally, you reach the buffet table. The person wearing the red scarf turns around. It isn't your friend. It is you—or rather, an older version of you, with grey hair and tired eyes. This other version of you looks at you with pity and hands you a book titled *The End*. 

Just as you take it, the floor dissolves into sand, and you sink rapidly downward, waking up with a gasp just as the grains hit your face.
