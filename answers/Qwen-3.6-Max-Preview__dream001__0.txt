You start to run, your feet pounding against pavement that feels strangely soft, like walking on thick moss. The buildings around you stretch impossibly high, their facades shifting and breathing as if made of living stone. Neon signs flicker in languages you’ve never seen but somehow understand, spelling out words like *Urgency*, *Late*, and *Forgotten*.

The air smells of ozone and old paper. You check your watch, but the hands are spinning counter-clockwise, faster and faster, until they blur into a single gray line. Panic rises in your chest, tight and hot. You grab the arm of a passerby—a figure with no face, only a smooth, mirror-like surface where features should be.

"Where is the meeting?" you shout, your voice echoing as if you’re speaking from the bottom of a well.

The mirror-face tilts, reflecting not your own anxious expression, but a calm version of yourself sitting in a quiet room, sipping tea. The reflection mouths a single word: *Breathe.*

Suddenly, the city dissolves into mist. The towering structures melt away, leaving you standing in a small, sunlit office. A clock on the wall ticks steadily, normally. It’s 9:00 AM. You have an hour. And for the first time since you arrived in this dream-city, you know exactly where you need to go.
