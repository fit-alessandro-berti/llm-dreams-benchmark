...written in a language that shifts every time I blink.

One moment, the ink is black and sharp, asking for the square root of a memory I can’t quite place. The next, the words dissolve into watercolors, asking me to weigh the sound of a falling leaf in grams. I reach for my pen, but it feels like it’s made of warm glass, heavy and fragile in my hand.

The room around me is stretching, the walls breathing slowly in and out like a chest. The clock on the wall has no hands, only a dial that spins with the names of cities I’ve never visited. A proctor walks by, but their face is a smooth, featureless blur, and they nod at me with a knowing smile that feels like a secret I forgot to keep.

I look back down at the paper. The question has changed again. It no longer asks for an answer. It simply reads: *“Do you remember why you are here?”*

I try to write, but the pen leaves no mark. Instead, the paper begins to glow, warm and golden. I realize then that I don’t need to answer. The exam isn’t testing what I know; it’s testing what I feel. The anxiety in my chest loosens, turning into a lightness, like stepping off a ledge into water that isn’t there.

The bell rings, but it doesn’t sound like a bell. It sounds like the ocean crashing against a shore. The classroom dissolves into mist, and I feel the familiar, sudden jolt of waking up, the taste of the dream still lingering on my tongue like old paper and salt.
