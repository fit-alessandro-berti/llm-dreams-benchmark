The office around you seems normal at first, but when you sit down at your desk, the computer screen stretches into the distance like a long corridor. Every email you open turns into another door. Behind one door is a conference room filled with clocks ticking at different speeds. Behind another is a library where the books rewrite themselves whenever you try to read them.

Your boss appears beside you and calmly asks for a progress update.

"I just started," you reply.

"You're already late," they say, pointing at a clock whose hands are spinning backward.

Determined to make progress, you gather a team of coworkers. Yet each coworker transforms into a different person whenever you look away. One moment they're experts offering brilliant advice; the next they're juggling spreadsheets that flutter away as birds.

As the deadline approaches, the project itself becomes stranger. It is no longer a report or presentation. It is a giant machine made of notes, diagrams, memories, and unfinished ideas floating in the middle of the office. Somehow, you know that if you can fit the final piece into place, everything will be complete.

You search frantically for that piece.

Then you notice a small paper tucked into your pocket. On it is a simple drawing you made years ago. As you place it into the machine, the gears begin to turn. The impossible project starts organizing itself. The clocks slow down. The emails stop multiplying. The corridors shrink back into ordinary hallways.

Your boss returns.

"Finished?" they ask.

You look at the machine. It unfolds into a door.

Instead of answering, you open it.

On the other side is a bright landscape where no deadlines exist, only winding paths leading toward distant mountains. The stress that filled the office vanishes instantly. You step through, leaving the project behind.

As the door closes, you hear your boss call out one final question:

"How did you do it?"

You smile, realizing you don't know the answer either.

And just as you begin walking toward the mountains, you wake up.
