

As the colors and shapes of the world shifted like tapestry threads—each ripple a story, each hue a pulse of life—the blank canvas grew a thousand tiny eyes. They blinked at you, soft and wise, as if the universe itself were waiting for someone to touch its silence.  

Then, a ripple of light touched your palm. It no longer felt like a canvas, but a language—every stroke a word, every blur a thought. And as you looked, not just the world changed, but the world *was* you. The colors and shapes had no end, only the endless possibility of *you*—and in that possibility, the world was no longer empty, but alive.  

**The dream ends here...** *And now...*
