...written in a shifting, iridescent script that refuses to stay still. 

You rub your eyes, but when you look back down, the black ink has settled into impossibly personal, surreal prompts:

* **Question 1:** *Define the exact weight of a forgotten promise in grams. Show your work.*
* **Question 2:** *Translate the silence of an abandoned hallway into binary code.*
* **Question 3:** *Using only the decisions you regret, draw a map to the person you were supposed to become.*

Panicking, you look around the vast, echoing auditorium. Hundreds of other candidates are bent over their desks, writing furiously. The sound of their pens scratching against paper creates a rhythmic, deafening roar, like heavy rain falling on a tin roof. 

You reach down to grab a pencil, but your hand pulls out a smooth, river-washed stone instead. 

"Five minutes remaining," a voice announces from the front. You look up at the proctor, but their face is a smooth, featureless mirror reflecting the harsh fluorescent lights of the ceiling. 

You press the stone against the paper anyway. Where it touches, the ink begins to liquefy, pooling into a small, dark mirror on your desk. Looking into it, you don't see the exam room or your own reflection—you see a door standing open in the middle of a sunlit field you’ve never visited, but somehow recognize.

The ticking of the giant clock on the wall slows down, stretching out into a deep, reverberating hum. The desk beneath your hands dissolves into warm sand, spilling down into an endless void. 

You aren't falling; you are floating. The exam paper drifts away into the dark, its strange questions blurring into tiny, glowing points of light until they look just like stars. 

A sudden, sharp beep cuts through the quiet—once, twice—and the field, the stars, and the exam vanish into the hum of your morning alarm.
