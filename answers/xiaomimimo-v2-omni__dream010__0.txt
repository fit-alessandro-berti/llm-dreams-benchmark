Next, you're on a bridge made of floating books. Each step must land on a volume that represents a useful skill—empathy, patience, creativity—but some books snap shut like traps. You learn to leap toward the ones that hum with a quiet warmth.

Then, you're handed a puzzle box that seems impossible. Instead of forcing it, you listen. You hear a faint melody from inside and realize you must hum back the right tune. When you do, the box opens, revealing not a key, but a seed.

In the final challenge, you're asked to build something that can carry a wish into the world. You gather the glass person’s advice, the warmth from the books, and the seed from the box. You plant the seed in a teacup, and it instantly grows into a tiny tree that glows softly. The judges watch, fascinated, as the tree begins to sing a lullaby in a language older than words.

You wake up just before they announce the winner, but you realize—the dream job was never about the title. It was about finding the work that lets you plant glowing trees in teacups, and teach glass people to smile without breaking.
