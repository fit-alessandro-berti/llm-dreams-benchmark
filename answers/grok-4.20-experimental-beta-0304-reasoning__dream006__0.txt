and you begin to speak, but the words come out as **soap bubbles**.

Each syllable floats away from your lips in shimmering, iridescent spheres that drift over the audience. Inside the bubbles are tiny moving scenes: your childhood bedroom, a flock of flamingos doing the Macarena, the time you tried to microwave a fork. The crowd is mesmerized. Some people are laughing. Others are reaching up, popping the bubbles with their fingers, and suddenly *they* start speaking in your voice, finishing your sentences for you.

You look down. Your podium has turned into a giant rubber duck. Your notes are now a single slice of pizza with "JUST WING IT" written in pepperoni.

The spotlight feels warm, like sunlight, and you realize the entire auditorium is slowly tilting sideways, sliding into an ocean of warm vanilla pudding. No one seems to mind. They're all waving at you from their floating seats, cheering as if this is the best speech they've ever heard.

You open your mouth to say something normal, anything normal, and instead you sing the national anthem... but every note is a different species of bird that flies out of your throat and joins the bubbles.

And just as the pudding ocean reaches your ankles, you wake up smiling, still tasting vanilla and feathers.
