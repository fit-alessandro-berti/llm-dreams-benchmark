They don’t ask where you came from or why your hands are trembling. One of them simply offers you a folded map, though when you open it, the lines rearrange themselves into paths of gold. Another gives you a warm cup of tea that tastes faintly of childhood, of kitchens at dusk, of someone calling your name from another room.

Together, you walk beneath towering buildings that lean close like curious giants. The city noise softens. Car horns become distant music. Footsteps become a rhythm. With every turn, the strangers seem less like strangers and more like people you have somehow always known.

At last, they lead you to a quiet square hidden at the heart of the city. In its center stands a fountain filled not with water, but with stars. You look into it and see countless versions of yourself: frightened, brave, wandering, resting, beginning again.

“You were never lost,” one of the strangers says gently. “You were only looking for the place where you could hear yourself.”

The map in your hands folds itself into a small paper bird. It lifts into the air, circles once above you, then flies toward a narrow street lit by morning.

You follow it.

As you walk, the city begins to change. The buildings open into trees. The pavement softens into grass. The strangers wave goodbye, smiling as though they know you will be all right now.

And just before you wake, you realize the way home was not a street or a door or a destination.

It was the feeling of being guided.

It was the kindness you accepted.

It was the small, steady light inside you, still glowing long after the dream fades.
