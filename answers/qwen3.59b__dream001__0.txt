You start to **check your phone, but the screen is filled with static that forms words you haven't read since waking up years ago.** You realize that as you run, the buildings stretch upward like breathing lungs, and the streets rearrange themselves into familiar shapes from other memories—your first apartment, a childhood kitchen, places you've never visited in dream-state. You try to call someone for directions, but when you speak your own name, it echoes back from three different directions at once.

You stop running. The urgency begins to fade into a strange calmness. You look up and see the time isn't on a clock; it is written on the side of a cloud in light that matches the frequency of your heartbeat. You find yourself standing before a door that has no handle, except for a small mirror embedded in its surface. You hesitate only a second, then open it.

You realize that you didn't need to get there; you were already there. The meeting wasn't about location or logistics—it was a reflection of your own mind. You step through the doorway and find only yourself from five years ago waiting patiently with a cup of coffee.
