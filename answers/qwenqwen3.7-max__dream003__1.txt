The carved lines aren’t just decorative; they are shifting. Like dark veins beneath the surface, the patterns slowly rearrange themselves into a map of a city you’ve never visited, yet somehow remember. You trace the largest river with your thumb, and the wood yields like liquid, rippling outward. 

You realize, with the absolute, unquestioned certainty that only exists in dreams, that the box doesn't need a metal key. It needs a memory. 

You close your eyes and think of the smell of rain on hot asphalt. When you open them, the lid of the box is gone. 

Inside, there is no velvet lining, no hidden treasure. Instead, you are looking down into a spiral staircase made of woven moonlight, descending into a deep, twilight blue. The air rising from the stairwell smells like old books and ozone. Without hesitating, you step *into* the box. 

Gravity immediately forgets its purpose. You aren't falling; you are floating downward, the walls of the box expanding around you until they become a vast, cavernous library. The bookshelves stretch up into an infinite, starry sky. But the books aren't made of paper. As you drift past one, you see it is made of layered, frozen whispers. You reach out to touch the spine, and a chorus of voices murmurs a secret you forgot you knew.

At the bottom of the stairwell, there is a single, freestanding door with a brass knob. You know, instinctively, that whatever is behind this door is the thing the box was meant to protect. 

You turn the knob. The door swings open, revealing your own bedroom, bathed in the soft, gray light of early dawn. 

You step through the doorway and look at your bed. You are lying there, fast asleep, your chest rising and falling in a steady rhythm. In your sleeping hands, you are clutching the mysterious wooden box. 

As you watch yourself sleep, the eyes of your dreaming body suddenly snap open. They look directly at you standing in the doorway. Your sleeping self smiles, reaches out, and clicks the box shut.

The sound of the latch echoing is deafening—*SNAP*—and you jolt awake, your heart racing, staring at the ceiling of your room, your hands empty, but your fingertips still tingling with the warmth of the wood.
