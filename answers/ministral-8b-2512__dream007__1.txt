You’re standing in a vast, empty library at night, its shelves stretching into infinity, their spines whispering secrets in a language you almost recognize. The air hums with the weight of unspoken words. Your friend—let’s call them **Lena**—is across the room, her silhouette sharp against the moonlight filtering through stained-glass windows shaped like fractured hearts. She’s not just arguing with you; she’s *unraveling* you. Every word she speaks peels back a layer of your skin, revealing the stitches of past versions of yourself—some stitched with gold thread, others with black, fraying at the edges.

**"You don’t get it,"** she says, her voice layered like an old vinyl record skipping. **"This isn’t just about [the topic—let’s say *trust*]. It’s about the *hole* in the middle of us. The one we both keep trying to fill with words, but it’s always too big."** She gestures to the floor, where a spiral staircase descends into darkness. **"Do you want to go down there? Or are you too afraid to see what’s waiting?"**

You hesitate. The argument has always been about *how* to move forward—whether to rebuild the bridge between you or burn it down to start fresh. But now, the question isn’t just about *them*. It’s about the version of *you* who believed, once, that bridges could hold. The one who’s been carrying the weight of their silence.

Lena’s eyes flicker—her pupils dilate into swirling galaxies for a second—then she laughs, sharp and sad. **"You’re not even listening to me. You’re listening to the *echo* of what you *wanted* to hear."** She snaps her fingers. The shelves between you *melt*, revealing a mirror. In it, you see yourself—but your reflection is *moving*, shifting like smoke. It mouths: *"She’s right. You’ve been listening to yourself all along."*

The dream doesn’t resolve. It *unfolds*. The argument becomes a labyrinth. The library doors slam shut behind you. Somewhere, a clock ticks backward. And then—

You wake up.

**Dream themes woven in:**
- **The "hole" between people**: A metaphor for unspoken grief, fear of vulnerability, or the gap between who we are and who we wish to be.
- **Surreal confrontation**: The argument isn’t just about the topic—it’s about *identity*, *memory*, and the illusions we cling to.
- **The mirror**: A classic dream symbol for self-recognition (or self-betrayal). Your reflection "moving" suggests you’re caught between versions of yourself.
- **The staircase**: A choice—down into the unknown (truth, fear, change) or staying in the familiar (but hollow) argument.
