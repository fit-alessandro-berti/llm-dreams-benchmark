...written in a script that belongs to someone else. At first glance, they resemble the standard format of multiple-choice options you've seen a thousand times before, but as your eyes trace the lines, the ink begins to shimmer like oil on water.

The first question asks something simple enough to appear normal: "Choose your primary goal." But when you look down, there are only three circles. None of them contain career titles or degree programs. Instead, they hold images: a small wooden door left standing ajar in your childhood home, a keyhole rusted shut beneath a porch, and a mirror reflecting not your face, but the back of your own head.

The second question appears below it, phrased with impossible geometry. It doesn't demand an answer; it demands a choice from memory. "Select the version of you that did not exist yesterday."

You realize then that this exam does not test your knowledge of the workplace or industry standards. It tests the architecture of your own soul. You lift your hand to bring forth your pen, but when you touch your fingers to the air above the desk, there is no paper. The answers are supposed to be drawn directly onto your skin.

You look around; the classroom has emptied out. There is no bell signaling the start of class, only a heavy silence broken by the sound of rain falling inside a room that shouldn't hold weather. The clock on the wall counts down not in minutes, but in heartbeats.

The final question appears last, floating alone at the bottom of the page. It does not read text; it reads *intent*. "What are you willing to burn?"

You realize the test isn't about what degree you receive, or where you will sit in a boardroom. It is asking if you have the courage to accept that the career you seek is not a destination on a resume, but the act of showing up as yourself amidst all the questions no one else cares you can answer.

You close your eyes. You understand that there are no grades here. The only score that matters is whether you chose the path because it was safe, or because it was true. When you open your eyes to write the final answer—and the paper reappears, filled in by your own resolve—you see that the pen has vanished.

There is nothing left but the truth you have finally learned how to carry into the world, fully equipped for anything beyond this dream.
