
**You are participating in a competition where the grand prize is your dream job. As the competition progresses, you face various challenges and must navigate a series of surreal, dreamlike trials designed to test your creativity, resilience, and intuition.**  

The first challenge transports you to a vast library where books flutter like birds around you. Each book whispers a different version of your future, but only one holds the truth. You must deduce which path aligns with your deepest values—not just your ambitions. Hours pass in what feels like minutes as you sift through possibilities, realizing the job isn’t just about skill, but about *purpose*.  

Next, you’re plunged into a bustling cityscape where strangers hand you fragments of a blueprint for a machine that could solve a global crisis. But the pieces don’t fit. Frustrated, you begin collaborating with other competitors—something the rules never mention is allowed. Together, you realize the machine isn’t a literal device, but a metaphor for collective action. The challenge wasn’t to build alone, but to inspire others to join your vision.  

In the final stage, you stand before a mirror-like portal. It asks: *“What part of yourself are you willing to let go of to claim this role?”* You hesitate, then shed a memory of past failure that’s held you back. The portal dissolves, revealing your dream job—not as a position, but as a calling you’ve already begun living.  

The twist? The competition was never about winning. It was about becoming the person who could *deserve* the job in the first place.
