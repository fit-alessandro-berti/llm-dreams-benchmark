The audience is enormous now, stretching back into a haze that shouldn't exist indoors. Faces blur and reshape. I think I see my third-grade teacher in the front row, next to someone who might be a childhood friend, though their features keep shifting. They're all waiting, patient in that unsettling way dream-crowds have.

I open my mouth to speak, and the words that come out aren't in any language I recognize—but somehow the audience nods as if they understand perfectly. Encouraged, I continue, and I realize I'm explaining something vitally important, something I've always known but never been able to articulate while awake. It has to do with the geometry of forgotten rooms, or maybe the true names of colors.

The ceiling opens up to reveal a sky that's the wrong shade of purple. A slow rain begins to fall—but the drops are pages from books, fluttering down around me. I catch one and try to read it, but the text rearranges itself every time I blink.

Then someone in the back raises their hand to ask a question, and I suddenly remember I never wore pants to this...

*And then I wake up.*
