
You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and symbols carved into its weathered surface—ancient-looking runes that seem to shimmer faintly in the dim light. The metal is cold to the touch, almost unnaturally so, like it has absorbed the night itself.  

As you trace a finger along one of the symbols, the box emits a low, resonant hum. Suddenly, the runes begin to glow with a soft, ethereal blue light, and the lid creaks open of its own accord. Inside, instead of treasure or treasure maps, you find a single, iridescent key—but it’s unlike any key you’ve ever seen. It seems to shift and writhe like a living thing, its teeth rearranging themselves into shapes that hurt to look at directly.  

A voice, or maybe just the whisper of the wind, seems to say, *“Find the heart of the forest, where the weeping willow’s tears have carved the deepest grooves, and place this in the stone that remembers the oldest song.”*  

The dream fades as you wake up, your hand still clutching the key, which is now just a key—ordinary, rusted, and utterly mundane. But you swear you can still hear the hum...
