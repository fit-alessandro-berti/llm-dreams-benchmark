
The deck pitches beneath your boots like a living thing, each wave a hammer blow against the hull. Salt spray stings your eyes, and the wind screams through the rigging, a chorus of lost souls. Your crew—faces pale smudges in the gloom—cling to rails and lines, their eyes wide with fear.

You swallow the bile of your own terror and plant your feet wide, grabbing a shuddering shroud for balance.

**“Hold fast!”** your voice cracks, but you roar it again, stronger. **“Secure the mainsail! Now! Maro, to the helm—don’t fight her, guide her! Elias, check the bilges!”**

Your orders cut through the din. They move, not with confidence, but with the desperate obedience of men who have no other hope. You feel the ship’s groan in your bones, a deep, metallic complaint. A monstrous wave, black and slick as oil, rises before you, blotting out the stormy sky.

This is the moment. The dream-logic of the sea takes over. You don’t just see the wave; you *feel* its intention. It’s not water—it’s a wall of fate. Your hand tightens on the wooden railing, and for a heartbeat, the chaos stills. The wind drops to a whisper. The wave hangs, frozen.

In that impossible silence, you see it: not a monster, but a path. A narrow, churning corridor of green water, barely visible, slicing *through* the heart of the wave. It’s a way forward, but it’s madness. It means steering *into* the very thing that will crush you.

You look at your crew. Maro at the wheel, knuckles white. Young Lila, the lookout, trembling but staring straight ahead, her duty stronger than her fear.

The dream gives you a choice. Do you:

**A)** Shout for Maro to hold the current course, to ride the wave’s crest and hope to survive the crushing fall on the other side?

**B)** Scream the impossible order: “Hard to port! Into the eye of it!” and trust the phantom path only you can see?

**C)** Freeze, the weight of their lives a physical stone in your chest, as the wave begins its terrible, suspended descent?

The storm waits. The ship waits. Your dream waits for your command.
