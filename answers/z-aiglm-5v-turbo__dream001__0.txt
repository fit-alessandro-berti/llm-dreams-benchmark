Your heart hammers against your ribs as you frantically pat your jacket pocket for your phone. You pull it out, but it isn't a phone anymore; it is a small, smooth river stone etched with symbols that squirm and rearrange themselves like ants when you try to read them.

Panic rising, you look up to hail a taxi, but the cars on the street are all silent, matte-black sharks gliding through the air just above the asphalt. One of them noses down toward you. The window rolls down, revealing the driver is your third-grade teacher, though her eyes are replaced by swirling galaxies.

"The Spire," you gasp out, handing her the stone. "I need to get to the Spire before the sky turns green."

She nods solemnly and floors it. The city blurs into streaks of neon light and ancient stone. You rocket past buildings that defy gravity—some built sideways into cliffs, others made entirely of water frozen mid-splash. Suddenly, you realize you have forgotten your trousers, yet you feel no shame, only a strange, light buoyancy.

The car screeches to a halt at the edge of a precipice. Before you stands the meeting place: a colossal tower made of glass that hums with a low vibration. You sprint toward the revolving doors, but as you push through them, the floor disappears. You are no longer in a lobby; you are falling through a endless library where the books flutter around you like birds.

You land softly on a massive conference table in the center of the room. Seated around you are twelve figures dressed in sharp suits, but as they turn to look at you, you realize they have no faces—only smooth, blank porcelain where features should be.

One of them slides a contract across the table. The ink is still wet, glowing with a faint, pulsating red light.

"We've been waiting," a voice echoes, seemingly from inside your own head. "Sign here. We need your permission to turn off the stars."

Your hand moves toward the pen, trembling, just as the clock on the wall begins to melt and drip onto the floor, signaling that time has officially run out.
