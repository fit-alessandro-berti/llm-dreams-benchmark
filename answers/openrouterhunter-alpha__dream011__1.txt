...written in a language you've never seen before — not quite letters, not quite symbols, more like living things that rearrange themselves when you're not looking directly at them.

You blink. The desk in front of you is no longer a desk. It's your childhood kitchen table, and the exam booklet has become a stack of handwritten letters from people you haven't spoken to in years. Each question isn't a question at all — it's a conversation you never finished.

*Why did you leave without saying goodbye?*

You look up. The lecture hall is gone. You're sitting in an empty theater, and the exam proctor is someone you used to know, standing very far away on the stage, speaking but making no sound. You can hear your own breathing. The clock on the wall has no hands, but somehow you know time is almost up.

You look back down. The letters have changed again. Now they're maps — detailed, beautiful, hand-drawn maps of places that don't exist. One shows a coastline you recognize from a dream you had when you were nine. Another traces a route through a city where all the streets are named after questions you were afraid to ask.

In the corner of the page, someone has written in your own handwriting: *You already know the answers. The test is whether you'll trust them.*

The pencil in your hand feels impossibly heavy.

You begin to write.
