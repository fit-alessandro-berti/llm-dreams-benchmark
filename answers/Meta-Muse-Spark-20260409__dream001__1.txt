**...start to panic, checking your pockets and finding only a crumpled map with half the street names smudged out.**

The sky is that weird pre-storm purple, and every clock you pass shows a different time — 2:17, 4:03, 11:50. None of them feel right.

A streetcar glides by, silent, and the people inside are all wearing identical gray coats, reading newspapers with blank pages. One of them looks up at you and mouths something. You can’t hear it, but you *feel* the word: **"Follow."**

You step off the curb to chase the streetcar and the ground tilts. Suddenly the city isn’t flat anymore. The buildings lean toward a single glass tower in the distance that wasn’t there a second ago. Its reflection shows you, but dream-you is wearing a suit you don’t own, holding a briefcase you don’t recognize.

Your phone buzzes. No caller ID, just a calendar alert:

**"Meeting: The Room Where You Decide"**  
**Location: You already know**  
**Time: Now, always now**

The map in your hand starts bleeding ink. The smudged streets rearrange themselves into one clear path leading straight to that tower. Your feet are moving before you decide to walk, and the panic shifts into something else — not calm, but *certainty*. Like you’ve been here before, just from the other side of waking up.

You reach the tower doors with 3 minutes to spare, except all the clocks just synced to the same time: **one hour from now**. 

The doors slide open. The person waiting inside is you. Older, maybe. Or just more awake.

They smile and say, "Took you long enough. Ready to remember what the meeting’s about?"

**What do you do next?**
