You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to venture beyond the crumbling walls of the old sanctuary where you've been hiding. The sky is a permanent shade of amber, and the wind carries with it the faint hum of something mechanical in the distance.

You gather what little you have—a battered canteen, a rusted knife, and a tattered map whose edges have been eaten by time. The path ahead is overgrown, but you can see remnants of a road that once led somewhere important. Your feet ache, but your determination pushes you forward.

As you walk, you encounter a young girl sitting beside a broken fountain. She's clutching a small, wilted flower—the first sign of life you've seen in weeks. She looks up at you with eyes that have seen too much, and without a word, she extends her hand.

Together, you continue the journey. You come across an old library, its shelves collapsed but still holding books. You take one—an old atlas of the world as it once was. The girl points to a place on the map, far to the north, marked with a symbol you don't recognize but that makes your heart race with hope.

As night falls and the temperature drops, you find shelter in an abandoned subway car. The girl falls asleep against your shoulder, still holding her flower. In your dreams within the dream, you see flashes of a place where the sky is blue, the water is clean, and people are laughing. You wake with a renewed sense of purpose.

The journey continues, and though you don't know what waits at the end of the map, you know that whatever it is—you won't face it alone.
