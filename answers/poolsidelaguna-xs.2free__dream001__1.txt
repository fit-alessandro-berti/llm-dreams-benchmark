"Lost, are we?" he says in a voice like rustling papers. Without waiting for an answer, he hands you a map made of what looks like folded starlight. As you unfold it, the streets rearrange themselves into constellations, and you realize the map isn't showing you where to go—it's showing you who you need to become.

The cat grins and vanishes into a doorway that wasn't there a moment before. You follow the map's guidance through alleyways that open into grand opera houses, past fountains that flow with liquid silver, until you find yourself standing before a building that seems to be made entirely of crystallized sound—glass shimmering with musical notes and whispers of forgotten conversations.

Inside, you discover the meeting isn't with a person, but with a version of yourself from last Tuesday, who hands you a key made of morning light. "This," they say, "is how you get home. But first, you need to remember why you were looking for this meeting in the first place."

As they speak, the walls begin to fade, and you realize you're back in your bed, the alarm clock blinking 7:03 AM. But on your nightstand—impossible but undeniable—is a small key that catches the morning light like crystallized sunshine.

You never did find that meeting. But somehow, you're exactly where you need to be.
