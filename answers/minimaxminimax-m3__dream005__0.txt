...they all seem to be speaking in soft, overlapping conversations that blur together like a low hum. A trio near the refreshment table turns toward you, and though their mouths move, you hear only the sound of wind chimes. One of them smiles and gestures for you to join them, but the longer you look, the more their features seem to shift—becoming familiar faces from different eras of your life, layered like a photograph developing in reverse.

You move through the crowd, nodding politely, clutching a glass of something that keeps changing color. Everyone knows your name here, even though you don't know theirs. A woman in a long blue dress laughs at something you haven't said yet.

In the corner, you spot your friend—or someone wearing their face—but when you approach, they dissolve into a flock of paper birds that scatter toward the ceiling, which is now impossibly high and made entirely of stained glass depicting scenes you recognize from dreams you don't remember having.

A door appears where there wasn't one before. You open it.

Beyond the door is another room, identical to the first, but empty except for a single chair facing a window. And there, in the chair, sits your friend, exactly as you remember them—unchanged, unmoved, as if they've been waiting for you this whole time. They look up and say, simply, "There you are. I wondered when you'd stop looking."

You wake with the strange certainty that you were supposed to bring something back with you—but your hands are empty, and you can't quite remember what it was.

*You open your eyes.*
