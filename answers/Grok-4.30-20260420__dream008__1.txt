You glance down at the crumpled project brief in your hands—something about "revolutionizing quantum espresso delivery for interdimensional clients" with a deadline of **yesterday at 3:17 a.m.** Your boss leans over your cubicle wall, tie loosened into a noose of glowing binary code, eyes swirling like galaxy pools. "Clock's ticking," he says, voice echoing from every direction at once.

Your fingers hover over the keyboard, but the keys rearrange themselves into tiny, chattering mouths that whisper impossible formulas. The fluorescent lights flicker into fireflies. Suddenly the entire office floor tilts like a funhouse mirror; your chair grows wings and lifts you into a sky made of spreadsheets raining coffee. You leap between floating islands of half-finished code, dodging deadlines that chase you as angry clocks with teeth. A coworker swims past on a river of unread emails, tossing you a golden USB drive shaped like a key.

Heart pounding, you jam the drive into a laptop that has sprouted roots and bloomed into a tree of infinite branches. Each branch is a different version of the project—some succeed, some explode into laughter. You choose the one where the solution sings itself into existence: a perfect, humming algorithm that solves everything by simply *believing* it can. The tree shakes with applause. Confetti made of yesterday's missed meetings drifts down. Your boss's face softens into your own, smiling. "See? Nothing's impossible here."

The office folds back into itself like origami. You wake up at your real desk, coffee still warm, the actual deadline suddenly feeling... manageable. Dreams always know the cheat codes.
