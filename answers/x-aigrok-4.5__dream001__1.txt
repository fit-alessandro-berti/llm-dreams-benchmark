**You start to** run down a maze of cobblestone streets that somehow already know you’re late.

The buildings lean in like curious old men, their windows blinking. Every intersection multiplies; left becomes three rights and a spiral staircase that deposits you right back at the same cracked fountain. Your phone shows a map of the city rendered entirely in watercolor—beautifully useless, the streets dissolving when you zoom in. The GPS calmly announces, “In 200 meters, turn into the tiger.”

People stream past in night-clothes and business suits that keep swapping. You grab a man in a red scarf: “The meeting—do you know where the Helix Tower is?” He smiles with too many teeth and hands you a paper airplane. When it unfolds mid-air it becomes a city directory written in hummingbird. You hop onto a streetcar that rings its bell like a forlorn harmonica. The seats are clouds. A ticket inspector floats down the aisle and stamps your hand; the ink forms a tiny, ticking clock that already reads five minutes to the hour.

Suddenly the landscape softens into glass and fog. You are standing in an endless conference room flooded with soft light. The long table stretches into a vanishing point. At the far end a figure awaits, face hidden behind a polished void. You sit. Your notes become origami birds that nest in the rafters. The figure leans forward and says, with perfect clarity:  
“You’re already late for the next dream. But the coffee is excellent.”

You lift the cup. It holds morning.

You wake.
