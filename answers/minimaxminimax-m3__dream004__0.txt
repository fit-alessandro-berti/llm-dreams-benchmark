The blank canvas hovers before you, suspended in mid-air, its edges rippling like water disturbed by an unseen breeze. The colors around you—shifting from deep violet to electric turquoise—seem to pulse in time with your heartbeat.

As you step closer, the canvas trembles. Then, without you touching it, a single brushstroke appears. It's the most perfect shade of gold you've ever seen, glowing as if lit from within. The stroke curves and swirls, forming the beginning of a door.

A voice—not heard but *felt*—whispers through the landscape: *"Every dream paints itself. What will you create?"*

The canvas expands, growing to the size of a wall, then a building. Behind it, the dreamworld reshapes itself into a vast artist's studio that stretches infinitely in every direction. Easels of impossible geometry stand in rows—some spiraling like seashells, others balanced on single points.

You notice your hands are now holding brushes you don't remember picking up—one drips with liquid starlight, another with what appears to be solidified music. The colors in the room begin taking on personalities: a shy cerulean blue hides behind a paint pot, while a bold crimson leaps playfully from canvas to canvas.

Suddenly, you realize the dream is waiting for your first true choice. A palette appears before you, holding every color that has ever existed and some that haven't been invented yet. One of the colors—a shimmering, unnamed hue—pulses urgently, as if calling to you specifically.

Do you reach for it? 🎨

*What do you do next in the dream?*
