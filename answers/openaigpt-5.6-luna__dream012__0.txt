You are the captain of a ship in the middle of a turbulent sea. Your crew looks to you for guidance as the storm worsens, and you realize the compass has stopped pointing north.

Lightning splits the sky. Waves crash over the deck, swallowing ropes, barrels, and shouted orders. For one brief moment, you consider admitting that you have no idea where you are going.

Then, beneath the thunder, you hear music.

It rises from below the ship—a slow, distant melody, as if someone is playing a piano in the ocean. The crew falls silent. Even the storm seems to pause and listen.

You order the lanterns lowered overboard. One by one, they drift across the black water, revealing a path of pale blue lights beneath the waves. The lights lead toward a towering door standing upright in the middle of the sea.

There is no land around it. No walls. No explanation.

The ship glides toward the door.

When you reach it, you see your own reflection in the brass handle—but you are older, wearing a crown of seaweed, and smiling as though you have been waiting there for years.

Behind you, the crew asks whether they should turn back.

You place your hand on the handle.

And the ocean opens.
