The argument, we suddenly understand without speaking, wasn’t the point. But here, in this shared dream of ours, the fear turns into something tangible—a small, dark, perfectly smooth stone that levitates between our hearts. It doesn’t hurt. It fills us with a quiet, connected hum.

My friend smiles then—a smile I’ve never seen on their waking face. It’s the smile of a secret being kept, not a wound. They whisper without sound, *See? We’re still here. Even when it’s hard, we’re still here.* The dreamscape shifts again, and we’re standing in a vast, twilight-drenched library whose shelves stretch infinitely. Our argument, I realize, was just a story we were reading aloud to each other, and now we’ve closed the book and leaned back in our chairs, the tension drained into the quiet between us. We didn’t solve the problem; we remembered what the problem could never touch. The dream holds this image until it gently, mercifully, begins to fade, and I wake with the weight of that half-stone still resting warmly over my heart, as if to remind me of the argument that never truly ended, only transformed.
