Inside, there is no furniture, only floating seats made of woven light where they sit and wait for you. As you sit, you notice something impossible: the fear in your chest has evaporated, replaced by a quiet hum that resonates with the strangers. They smile and say nothing, because language had been redundant since the moment their kindness bridged the gap between you.

You understand then that you weren't looking for a place to go, but for recognition. You were never alone in the crowd; you only failed to see the eyes that were already watching with warmth from every streetlight reflection. As the dream fades toward a bright, sun-filled ending, you know that if you are ever lost again, you won't need to find your way—you will simply have to remember that everyone has hands outstretched waiting for you to join them.
