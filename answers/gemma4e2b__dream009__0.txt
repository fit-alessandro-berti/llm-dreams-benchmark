You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to **learn the signs of the wild and find the path back to the light.**

The air is thick with the scent of damp earth and pine, stinging your eyes with unfiltered sunlight. The landscape is vast, dominated by colossal, ancient trees whose branches weave a canopy so dense that the ground is perpetually shrouded in a shifting, emerald twilight. There are no sounds of traffic, only the mournful cry of unseen birds and the whisper of the wind moving through the high branches.

You are a small, bewildered figure, clad in rough, unfamiliar furs. Before you stretches a trail carved not by human hands, but by the slow, deliberate passage of enormous, moss-covered stones. To your left, a river of silver-cold water snakes through ferns the size of small houses. To your right, a wall of monolithic granite rises so high it seems to touch the clouds, silent and indifferent.

A deep, primal instinct grips you—not fear, but an urgent, bone-deep knowledge that you are utterly alone, adrift in a world governed by instinct and stone. You must choose: follow the winding path toward the glittering water, heed the silent call of the colossal forest, or attempt the impossible ascent up the impassive wall.

As you take a hesitant step, the stones beneath your feet hum, and a single, enormous shadow detaches itself from the granite wall, waiting.

**What do you do?**
