...your fingers pass through the keyboard. The keys are made of cooled oatmeal. Each press yields a soft *thup* sound, and the screen fills with recipes for cornbread instead of the quarterly report.

Your boss's face is a calendar. February. Just February, repeating. His tie is a live snake eating its own tail. He doesn't speak—he *projects* deadlines directly into your molars. *Tuesday. Tuesday. Tuesday.*

The office layout breathes. Cubicle walls inhale: expand. Exhale: contract. Karen from Accounting floats past, tethered to the ceiling by her ankles, stapling memos to the cloud layer. She waves. Her fingers are pens. Her laugh sounds like a fax machine connecting.

You realize you're not wearing pants. This is normal. You're also not wearing a shirt. Also normal. Your skin has become translucent vellum—blue lines of notebook paper running beneath the surface. The project brief is *written on your forearm* in handwriting you don't recognize. It keeps changing when you look away.

*Build a bridge. No—bake the bridge. No—be the bridge.*

The deadline is a physical weight on your chest. A small stone. A boulder. A neutrino. It shifts.

Your boss's calendar-face flips to a page that doesn't exist. *The 32nd of Never.*

"Wake up," says the snake-tie. "You're late for the meeting you already missed."

The oatmeal keys grow warm. The recipes on screen begin to burn.
