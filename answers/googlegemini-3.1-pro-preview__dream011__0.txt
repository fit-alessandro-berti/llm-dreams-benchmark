*“Question 1: Explain the exact weight of a forgotten memory. Show your work.”*

Panic sets in. I reach for my pencil, but it’s no longer solid; it feels like a stalk of damp celery that bends uselessly against the page. Worse, when I look closely at the desk, it’s not made of wood anymore—it’s shifting, pulling me in like quicksand. 

I look around the vast, echoing lecture hall for help. The other students are hunched over their desks, furiously scribbling, but as I look closer, I realize their heads are completely replaced by ticking analog clocks. The synchronized *tick-tick-tick* of their faces forms a deafening heartbeat that fills the room. 

Up at the chalkboard, the professor slowly turns around. It’s not a teacher at all, but my childhood dog, wearing a tweed suit and looking at me with gentle, profound disappointment. He barks once, but the sound that comes out is a train whistle. 

Suddenly, the walls of the classroom fall away outward like cardboard movie sets, revealing an endless, foggy ocean. The floor begins to tilt. My exam paper slips off the desk, fluttering away into the mist, and I dive to catch it. 

As I fall, I grab the paper, but it has changed again. It’s now a mirror. I look into it, but instead of my reflection, I see a glowing, bold red message flashing in the glass:

**TIME IS UP. WAKE UP.** 

Gravity completely reverses. I am plummeting upward into the sky, the sound of the ticking clocks turning into the shrill, mechanical blare of my morning alarm…
