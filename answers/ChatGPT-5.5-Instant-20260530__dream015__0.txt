You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to search for signs of other survivors rather than hide alone.

Carrying a worn backpack and a map drawn on scraps of paper, you travel through abandoned towns swallowed by vines and silence. Along the way, you encounter strange hazards: collapsing buildings, sudden dust storms, and mysterious figures watching from distant rooftops. Yet each challenge seems to leave behind a clue—a painted symbol, a radio frequency, a handwritten note pointing farther north.

One evening, as the sun glows red through the haze, you discover an old radio tower still humming with power. Climbing its rusted stairs, you hear a faint voice repeating the same message: "If anyone can hear this, there is hope. Follow the river."

The dream shifts. Days seem to pass in moments as you follow the winding river through ruined landscapes. Eventually, you reach a hidden valley where lights shine in the darkness. There, a thriving community has rebuilt gardens, workshops, and schools from the remnants of the old world.

The people welcome you warmly, as though they have been expecting your arrival. They tell you that every survivor who reaches the valley followed different clues, but all were guided by the same mysterious signal.

As you look toward the mountains beyond the valley, you notice another radio tower blinking in the distance. The voice returns, clearer than before:

"There are more communities out there."

You feel a surge of purpose. Just as you volunteer to lead an expedition to find them, the valley lights brighten into a brilliant glow—and you wake up wondering whether the dream was about surviving the end of the world, or helping build the beginning of a new one.
