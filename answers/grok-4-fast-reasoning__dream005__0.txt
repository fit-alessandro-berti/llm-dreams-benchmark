You weave through the crowded ballroom, the air thick with laughter and the clink of champagne glasses. The lighting is dim, shifting like candle flames in a breeze that doesn't exist, casting long shadows that dance across the marble floors. Your one acquaintance—let's call them Alex, the friend who invited you—promised to meet you by the grand staircase, but they've vanished into the throng.

First, you brush past a cluster of elegantly dressed intellectuals in tweed jackets and pearl necklaces, debating the ethics of time travel over tiny hors d'oeuvres. One woman with wire-rimmed glasses turns to you, her eyes gleaming unnaturally bright. "Have you seen the future?" she asks, as if it's a casual greeting. You mumble something about being lost and hurry on, but her question echoes in your mind, making the room feel like it's tilting sideways.

Next comes a boisterous group of artists, their clothes splashed with vibrant paints that seem to swirl and change colors as you approach. They're laughing hysterically at a canvas propped against a wall—a surreal landscape of floating clocks melting into teacups. A man with a beret tilts his head at you. "Join us! What's your medium—dreams?" He hands you a brush that feels warm, alive, and before you know it, you're painting a door on the wall. It swings open to reveal a hallway of stars, but you pull back, heart racing, and slip away.

Deeper in, you stumble into a circle of musicians, their instruments glowing faintly: a violin that hums like a swarm of bees, a drum that beats in sync with your pulse. They don't speak; they just play, drawing you into a melody that makes your feet want to dance. One flautist winks and whispers, "The one you seek is hidden in the rhythm—follow the off-beat." You try, stepping to the irregular pulse, but it leads you in circles, the crowd parting like water only to close again.

Finally, exhaustion sets in, and you push toward the edges of the room, where the walls blur into mist. There, in a quiet alcove beneath a chandelier dripping with what looks like liquid gold, you spot Alex—or at least, a figure that resembles them, smiling knowingly. But as you reach out, their face shifts: first to your own reflection, then to a stranger's, then to nothing at all. The room spins, the groups of people merging into a kaleidoscope of faces and voices, and suddenly—

You jolt awake in your bed, the dream's echoes fading like morning fog. The social gathering was vivid, but now it's just the hum of your alarm clock, pulling you back to reality. What a strange night.
