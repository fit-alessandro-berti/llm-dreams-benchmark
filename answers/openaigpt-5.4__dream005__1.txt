…each group seems to be arranged by some unspoken logic you can’t quite decode.

Near the entrance, people in glittering clothes are laughing too loudly, balancing drinks that never spill. In the next room, everyone is sitting on the floor folding paper into birds, though no one explains why. Down a long hallway, a group stands in complete silence, smiling as if they’ve all agreed on a joke just before you arrived. Every time you ask if they’ve seen the one person you know, they answer kindly but indirectly, pointing you onward: through the kitchen, up the stairs, across the garden, into a room that should not fit inside the house.

You begin to notice that the gathering is larger than any real gathering could be. There are balconies overlooking other balconies, and conversations echo before they happen. Someone hands you a name tag, but when you look down, it has your name written in a handwriting you recognize from childhood. You want to ask about it, but the person who gave it to you is now at the far end of the room, waving as though they were never close at all.

At some point, the search becomes less urgent and more ceremonial, as if the whole party exists to keep you moving. You pass a dining table where everyone is eating slices of birthday cake with lit candles still glowing on top. In the backyard, people are slow dancing to music that seems to be coming from underwater. A woman with silver paint on her eyelids tells you, very seriously, “You’ve already found them once tonight,” and then turns to greet someone with your voice.

Finally, you open a door expecting another crowd and instead find a small, quiet room. Your friend is there, sitting alone on a folding chair, as if they have been waiting for hours. Relief rises in you immediately, but before you can speak, they look up and seem surprised to see you.

“I was looking for you,” they say.

You laugh, because that is exactly what you were about to say. But then you notice there are two doors in the room: the one you entered through, and another behind them, half open, with warm golden light pouring through. Your friend stands and asks, gently, “Do you want to go back to the party, or do you want to see where this goes?”

And just before you answer, you hear everyone in the house, all at once, fall silent.
