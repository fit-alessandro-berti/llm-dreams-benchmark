…and the patterns seem to breathe. Thin lines of silver and deep violet twist across the lid like veins under skin, pulsing faintly in time with a heartbeat you slowly realize is your own. When you glance away, the shapes rearrange themselves; when you look back, they’ve formed constellations you almost recognize—maps of places you’ve visited only in sleep.

You run a fingertip along the rim. The wood is warm, smooth as river stone, and where your skin meets it, the carvings glow. A soft click echoes somewhere inside, though there is no keyhole, no latch. The box isn’t asking to be forced; it is waiting to be *remembered*. You press your palm flat against the center, and the patterns spiral inward, drawing themselves down into a single point of light.

The lid lifts on its own.

Inside, nestled on velvet the color of a fading sunset, lies a small, perfect mirror. But the reflection is not the room around you, nor your own face. It shows a vast ocean beneath a sky hung with two moons, and a figure standing on the shore who turns as if you’ve called their name. You feel the dream tilt, the floor becoming sand beneath your feet, the air filling with salt and starlight.

The box was never meant to stay closed, you understand. It was a door. And as the waves begin to whisper your name, you step through—carrying the box with you, still open, still shining—into the next part of the dream.
