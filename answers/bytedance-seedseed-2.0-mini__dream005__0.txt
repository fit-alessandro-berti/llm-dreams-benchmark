As you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people and first stumble into a huddle of friends bickering over a platter of neon pink guacamole, one guest waving a tortilla chip like a protest sign as they insist it’s “mango-spiked fusion” while their friend scoffs that someone just dyed the avocados with food coloring to be “aesthetic.” You dart past them, swerving a stray chip that zips through the air like a tiny frisbee, and step into a loose circle of people performing a wobbly, improvised line dance—no one follows a set beat, instead stepping only when they balance a chipped vintage teacup on their palm, and every time someone drops a cup, the group erupts in a puff of vanilla-scented confetti that sticks to your jacket.

You keep moving, avoiding a group of clowns in faded denim jackets who are juggling live fireflies, and spot a bar lined with glowing, humming ice cubes that pulse like tiny bioluminescent jellyfish. The bartender nods and slides you a free lemonade with one of the cubes floating inside, but you don’t stop to sip it—your eyes are peeled for the lavender braid your oldest friend Javi usually keeps tied at the nape of his neck, the only person you knew would be here tonight.

You round a corner and nearly crash into a crew of people in puffy silver space suits hauling a giant inflatable planet Saturn into the middle of the dance floor; when they start unzipping their suits, they’re just regular party guests in matching glittery t-shirts underneath, and everyone starts jumping on the Saturn moonwalk, even the guacamole arguers who’ve now teamed up to pass around a bowl of plain salted chips.

Then, through the throng, you spot that lavender braid peeking over a stack of hand-painted signs that all read “Free Hugs & Star Stickers.” You push through the crowd, your sneakers squelching slightly on a patch of spilled sparkling cider, and drop down next to the person sitting cross-legged on the floor behind the signs—only it’s not a stranger, it’s Javi, who grins and shoves a silver star sticker onto your jacket. “I’ve been waiting 45 minutes,” he says, “but I got distracted helping the firefly jugglers find their escaped bugs. Also, the guacamole guy was right—it’s mango, I swear.”

As you lean in to catch up, the glowing ice cube lemonade appears between you on a tiny silver tray, and the fireflies the clowns were juggling land on your drinks, buzzing softly. For a split second, the party warps: the moonwalk melts into a giant pile of fuzzy throw pillows, and the confetti from earlier clings to your hair like tiny snowflakes, but Javi just laughs and grabs your hand to drag you over to the teacup dance circle, and suddenly none of the surreal noise around you matters—even when a stray cat trots by, morphs into a small kid who hands you an extra star sticker before scampering off, you feel grounded, like you’ve finally found exactly where you’re supposed to be in this hazy, glowing dream.
