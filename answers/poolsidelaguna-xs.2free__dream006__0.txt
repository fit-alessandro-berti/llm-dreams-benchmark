
...pause for a moment, taking in the sea of faces. The lights feel blinding, but instead of nerves, you feel an unexpected calm wash over you. Then, as you open your mouth to speak, your voice echoes strangely—and the crowd begins to shift.  

Not into people, but into a circle of ancient, glowing owls, their eyes reflecting the stage lights like stars. They hoot softly, not in mockery, but in encouragement. One owl steps forward—nay, *flutters* forward—and lands on a nearby podium. To your surprise, it speaks in a voice like wind through autumn leaves:  

**"You are exactly where you’re meant to be."**  

The words settle into your chest like warmth. You realize this is no ordinary stage—it’s a threshold. The owls part, revealing a path of shimmering light leading into a forest of crystal trees. Behind you, the audience fades. Ahead, a single word etched into a tree trunk glows: *Courage*.  

You close your eyes, take a breath, and step forward—into the dream, and into yourself.  

*(When you wake up, you’ll remember this: the best speeches aren’t given to crowds. They’re given to the part of you that’s been waiting all along.)*
