...and the noise becomes a thick, humming texture against your skin.

There are figures draped in shimmering silks, laughing in a language you almost recognize—a melody made of glass and regret. Then there are clusters huddled around a flickering lantern, their shadows dancing on walls painted with forgotten memories. They look at you, but their eyes are mirrors reflecting only the empty space where your one known person should be.

You try to speak, to ask a simple question, but the words stick in your throat, dissolving into dry dust. Each group offers a song, a story, a silent, heavy judgment, all pointing away from your focus. You realize you are not seeking a person; you are seeking a feeling, and the gathering is a thousand simultaneous echoes of isolation.

Suddenly, the air shifts. The music stops. The entire crowd freezes, replaced by an overwhelming, blinding silence. In that silence, you don't find the person you seek, but you find something far more profound: a single, perfect, shimmering thread of light—a thread that connects every single face in the room, and which, for just a moment, feels undeniably, intimately yours.

Then, the light fades, and you are left standing alone in the echo of the silence, wondering if the one you were seeking was never meant to be found among the noise at all.
