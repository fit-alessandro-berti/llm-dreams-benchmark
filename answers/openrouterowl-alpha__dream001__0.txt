You find yourself in a vast, unfamiliar city, and you realize you have an important meeting in an hour but no idea where it is. You start to panic, your heart pounding as you glance around at the towering glass buildings and bustling crowds. The street signs are in a language you almost recognize—but not quite—and your phone is dead.

You take a deep breath and approach a street vendor selling glowing pastries shaped like stars. “Excuse me,” you say, “do you know where the Meridian Tower is?” The vendor smiles, hands you a pastry for free, and points down a narrow alley humming with soft music.

You follow it, the city shifting around you—cobblestones turning to mosaic tiles, the air now scented with jasmine and ozone. Suddenly, a child on a bicycle made of light rides past, calling out, “You’re late! But not too late!”

At the end of the alley stands a door with no building around it—just a freestanding oak door with a brass knocker shaped like an owl. You knock.

It opens to reveal not a room, but a sunlit meadow floating above the clouds. In the center sits a long table made of river stones, and your colleagues—dressed in clothes woven from constellations—are already seated, sipping tea from cups that chime like wind chimes.

“Ah, there you are,” says your boss, whose eyes hold galaxies. “We saved you a seat. The meeting’s about dreams… and how to build them into reality.”

You sit down, the warmth of the sun and the hum of possibility washing over you. Your phone buzzes back to life in your pocket—but you don’t need it anymore. You’re exactly where you’re supposed to be.
