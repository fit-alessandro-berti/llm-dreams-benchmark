*(The dream unfurls like aged parchment, scented with woodsmoke and damp earth.)*

You blink awake on a straw-strewn floor, the weight of a rough woolen tunic unfamiliar on your shoulders. A single high window shows a sky the color of faded ink, no electric lights marring the stars. The air smells of animals, bread, and something herbal—like rosemary, but wilder.

You stand, joints creaking, in a one-room cottage. A small fire glows in a central hearth, casting dancing shadows. A clay pitcher sits on a crude table, and a half-finished loaf of dark bread rests beside it. Outside, you hear the slow creak of a wooden wheel and the low murmur of a language you almost—but not quite—understand.

The first realization is primal: **thirst.** Your throat is dry, your mouth dusty. You need water.

You pick up the pitcher. It’s heavy, earthenware, cool to the touch. You step outside into the pre-dawn chill.

Before you stretches a valley under a canopy of stars you have never seen so clearly. A thin ribbon of mist traces the path of a stream you can hear but not yet see. To your left, a small garden of stoic vegetables—cabbages, leeks, perhaps—glistens with dew. To your right, a forest looms, dense and whispering.

Your feet, clad in simple leather shoes, find the faint path leading downhill toward the sound of water. Each step is a negotiation with the uneven ground. This is real. The cold air pinches your nose. The need for water is not a metaphor.

As you kneel at the stream’s edge, cupping your hands into the icy flow, you see your reflection—a stranger’s face, smudged with soot, eyes wide with a dawning, terrifying truth: **you are utterly, completely on your own.** And yet… not. For in the cottage behind you, there is fire. In the valley, there is life. The dream is not a punishment, but a puzzle. And you have just begun.

*Where does your dream go from here? Do you drink deeply and return to the cottage? Do you follow the stream to see where it leads? Or do you notice something strange—a glint of metal in the water, a familiar constellation in the sky that shouldn’t be there yet?*
