…grip the wheel with salt-stained hands, the wind howling like a chorus of lost souls. The waves rise like black mountains, their crests foaming with a strange, silver light—almost as if the sea itself is alive, watching. Your first mate, a grizzled woman with a scar across her cheek, shouts over the storm, *"Captain, the compass—it’s spinning wild!"* You glance down: the needle twirls like a drunken dancer, pointing nowhere and everywhere.

Then, from the mist, a shape emerges—a ship, but not like any you’ve seen. Its sails are tattered, yet it cuts through the storm as if the gale were a gentle breeze. At its helm stands a figure cloaked in shadows, one hand raised in greeting… or warning. The crew falls silent, their breath stolen by the sight. The stranger’s voice carries across the water, smooth as oil on troubled seas: *"You’ve sailed into the Edge of the Map, Captain. The rules here… are different."*

The ship draws closer, and you realize with a chill that its hull is made not of wood, but of something darker—like frozen smoke, or the bones of forgotten gods. The stranger smiles, and their teeth are too white, too sharp. *"The storm won’t stop until you make a choice. Give me a memory—one that defines you—or the sea will take your ship, and your crew, piece by piece."*

The waves crash higher, the wood groans, and your crew’s eyes burn with fear. In your pocket, your fingers brush against a locket—inside is a faded photo of a woman and child on a sunlit dock, laughing. A memory you’ve carried like an anchor.

Do you hand it over? Or do you defy the stranger and risk the abyss? The dream holds its breath… waiting.
