You are in the middle of a heated argument with a close friend. The disagreement is about something important to both of you, and you decide to **step back and notice the room beginning to dissolve into a swirling, iridescent mist**. As the chaos of your argument fades, the mist coalesces into a vast, starlit desert—your childhood home stretching endlessly around you. Your friend, still animated with frustration, suddenly freezes, their silhouette shimmering like a mirage.  

"Wait," you say, voice steadier now. "Do you remember the old oak tree behind my grandparents' house? The one we used to climb as kids?"  

They blink, confusion flickering across their face. "What about it?"  

"The branches—*they were always broken*, but we never saw them as broken. Just… different. Like this." You gesture to the desert sky, where constellations form and reform into shapes only you two recognize. "Maybe we’re just seeing each other’s cracks now. Not to fix them, but to... *see* them."  

The air hums with warmth, and the desert blooms into a garden overnight, each flower a memory of laughter you’d forgotten. Your friend’s shoulders relax. The argument is gone, replaced by the quiet certainty that some things grow only when they’re allowed to be reshaped.  

You wake up with the lingering scent of jasmine and the feeling that the truth wasn’t in the words you argued, but in the space between them.
