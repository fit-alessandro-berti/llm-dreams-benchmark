
...walking down a street that seems to rearrange itself as you pass. The buildings are tall and made of a dark, reflective glass that shows your anxious face back at you, but the street signs are written in a language you almost understand. 

A clock tower looms ahead, its hands spinning wildly. You ask a passerby for directions, but they only smile and point toward a bridge arching over a river of liquid light. As you cross, the water whispers the name of the street you need, but by the time you reach the other side, you’ve forgotten it.

The city grows denser, the alleys narrower. You turn a corner and suddenly find yourself in a vast library where the books are breathing. A woman with silver-rimmed glasses hands you a key made of frozen coffee. “You’ll need this,” she says, though you don’t know what it unlocks.

You rush outside again, and now the buildings are carved from enormous, petrified books. The address you’re seeking is etched into the spine of one, but the title keeps changing—*The Art of Arrival*, *How to Be On Time in Dreamscapes*, *Meetings: A Myth*.

You check your watch: ten minutes left. The sky darkens to deep purple. From the corner of your eye, you see the meeting room’s door—just a simple wooden door—leaning against a graffiti-covered wall. But between you and it flows a river of silent, slow-moving clocks, their hands sweeping the air like reeds.

What do you do?
