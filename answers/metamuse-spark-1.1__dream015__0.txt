You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to **plant a garden.**

It doesn't make any sense. That's how you know it's a dream.

The sky is the color of old TV static — pale gray and buzzing faintly. The city around you is quiet, not empty, just holding its breath. Cars are rusted over like sleeping animals. Vines have swallowed the billboards. The air smells like rain on hot metal, even though it hasn't rained in years.

And in the pocket of your patched-up jacket, you find them. A small, crumpled paper packet. Warm to the touch. Inside: seeds.

You climb.

You remember this supermarket from before. Now its roof has caved in on one side, but the other side is flat and bathed in this strange, soft sunlight that seems to come from everywhere at once. You push open the roof door — it creaks like it's yawning awake.

Up here, the wind is different. It doesn't carry ash. It carries distant music.

You decide this is the place.

You have no tools, so you use your hands. The soil up here isn't really soil — it's dust and broken concrete and old pigeon feathers — but when you dig, it turns dark and rich underneath, like chocolate cake. Dream soil. You make little rows. You whisper to each seed as you drop it in. You don't know what they are. Tomatoes? Sunflowers? Maybe just light?

A sound behind you. You turn.

A little girl is standing by the roof door. Then an older man with a toolbox that has no tools in it. Then two more people. They just... appear, the way people do in dreams. They must have been following the same quiet tug you felt.

They don't ask what you're doing. They just kneel down and help.

The girl has a plastic bottle, half-full of precious clear water. She pours it carefully, reverently, over the seeds. The old man shields the rows from the wind with his empty toolbox.

And then you start to hum. You don't know the song, but everyone knows it. One by one, they join in. It's not loud or perfect, but in this world where everything has been about staying quiet and staying hidden, it feels like the loudest thing anyone has ever done.

As you sing, the seeds move.

Not growing slowly — *unfurling*. Fast, impossibly fast. Green shoots that glow a little at the edges. Leaves opening like hands. And in seconds, the whole roof is green. Tomato vines heavy with fruit that smells like summer before the world ended. Sunflowers taller than you, turning their faces to that strange everywhere-sun. Corn that whispers when the wind moves through it.

You all just stare. Then the little girl laughs and picks a tomato, and juice runs down her chin, redder than any sunset you can remember.

You realize the danger hasn't gone away. Down below in the streets, shadows are still moving. Resources are still scarce.

But up here, for now, there is enough. More than enough.

You decide to build a table.
