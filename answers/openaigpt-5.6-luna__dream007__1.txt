You push open the door, but instead of the street, you step into a vast train station filled with clocks. Every clock shows a different time, and your friend is standing on the opposite platform, suddenly impossibly far away.

A train arrives without making a sound. Through its windows, you see moments from your friendship: the first time you met, shared laughter, old apologies, and small hurts neither of you ever mentioned. Your friend raises a hand, holding something familiar—perhaps a photograph, perhaps a key.

The announcement overhead says, “Last chance to say what you mean.”

You try to call out, but your voice comes out as birds. They scatter into the rafters. Your friend smiles sadly and steps onto the train.

Just before the doors close, you finally understand that the argument was never about winning. It was about being afraid of losing each other.

You run toward the train, but the station floor becomes water beneath your feet. As you sink, your friend leans out the window and says, “Wake up.”

You open your eyes with tears on your face—and your phone is ringing. It’s your friend.
