…a paintbrush made of liquid starlight, floating just above the canvas, trembling as if breathing.

You reach for it—not with your hand, but with your thoughts—and the brush responds. As your will touches it, the bristles melt into a thousand tiny constellations, swirling and humming an ancient melody only the soul remembers.

The canvas doesn’t stay blank for long.

With a sigh, the air around you *unfurls*—a mountain made of singing turquoise spirals rises behind you, its peak dissolving into a flock of paper cranes made of stained-glass rain. Below, rivers of molten amber flow upward, looping into the shape of forgotten names. Trees bloom with geometric flowers—each petal a different theorem, each seed a sleeping dream.

You dip the starlight brush into the air itself, and it drags a trail of auroras across the canvas.

The first stroke? A door.

Not just any door—*your* door. The one you walked past every morning as a child, but never opened. It’s painted in colors you’ve never named, edged in silver that hums lullabies your mother used to whisper. The handle turns when you think of her smile.

The second stroke? A child—*you*—standing on the other side, glowing softly, holding an unopened gift wrapped in twilight. You can feel the heartbeat of the child inside your chest.

The third stroke?

The canvas ripples, and the whole world *winks*.

The mountains become your fears, reshaped into gentle hills. The rivers become your regrets, now flowing into a vast, calm ocean that reflects not the sky—but your truest, quietest self.

The brush dissolves into a whisper:  
*"You don’t need to fill it. You are the painting."*

And then—you Wake.

But in your pocket, there’s a smudge of color. Not from any paint.  
Just... *you*.

And when you look at your hand in the morning light,  
the hue lingers.  
A color no one else can see.  
A shape only you remember how to draw.
