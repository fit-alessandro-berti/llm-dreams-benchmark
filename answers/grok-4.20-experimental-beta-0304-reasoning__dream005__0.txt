...and each one feels like a different layer of your own subconscious throwing a party without you.

The first group is clustered around a fountain that flows upward. They're all versions of your high school self—same awkward haircut, same nervous laugh—but they're confidently debating the best way to time-travel using only office supplies. One of them hands you a stapler like it's Excalibur and whispers, "The meeting starts in 1997. Don't be late."

You excuse yourself and weave through the crowd, the music shifting from jazz to whale song to the sound of your own ringtone from 2009. The next cluster is made entirely of people who owe you money. They smile too wide and keep offering you tiny hors d'oeuvres that turn into important documents you've been avoiding. You back away slowly.

Further on, a circle of strangers are playing a board game where the pieces are your childhood pets. The mood is strangely tender. A woman with your mother's eyes but none of her face looks up and says gently, "You've been looking for the wrong person, sweetheart."

That's when the lights dim and everyone starts moving in slow motion except you. Your heart is pounding with that specific dream-anxiety that feels both ridiculous and urgent. You push through a forest of elbows and suddenly—

There they are.

The one person you actually know is standing alone by a window that opens onto deep space. They're holding two drinks and looking exactly as you remember them, except their shadow is having a completely different conversation with someone else's shadow.

They turn to you, smile like the dream is finally making sense, and say:

"Took you long enough. I've been holding your place since we were kids."

The moment you reach for the drink they offer, the entire gathering folds up like a paper lantern and you wake up with your hand still outstretched, reaching for something that felt more real than most days.
