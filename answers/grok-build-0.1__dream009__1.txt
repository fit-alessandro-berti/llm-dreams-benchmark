You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to find a way to survive the coming night—your modern clothes already drawing suspicious glances from the villagers in their rough wool tunics and cloaks. The air smells of woodsmoke, manure, and fresh bread from a nearby stone oven. No phones, no lights, no maps. Just the sound of a distant blacksmith's hammer and the creak of wooden carts on dirt roads.

You duck into the shadow of a thatched cottage to think. This looks like the Middle Ages, maybe 14th or 15th century Europe, judging by the architecture and the lack of anything resembling machinery. Your stomach twists with hunger and panic. You need food, shelter, and—most urgently—information. Without drawing too much attention, you approach a hunched old woman selling turnips from a cart and gesture awkwardly, offering your wristwatch in trade. She eyes it like it's cursed, mutters something in a language you don't understand, and waves you away. 

A group of children points and laughs at your shoes. Realization hits harder: you need to blend in fast, learn enough of the local tongue to ask for work, or risk being driven out as a mad stranger before you can figure out how you got here... or how to get back. As the last light fades, you slip toward the glow of a tavern, heart pounding, wondering if this dream will end before the real dangers of this world find you.
