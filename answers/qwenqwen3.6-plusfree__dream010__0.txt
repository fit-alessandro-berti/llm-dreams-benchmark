...and the rules rewrite themselves with every breath. 

First, you must untangle a net of clockwork vines that chime in keys only your childhood recognizes. Each knot you loosen releases a memory you forgot you kept: the smell of rain on notebook paper, the weight of a perfectly balanced stone, the quiet thrill of fixing something broken with nothing but patience and mismatched parts. 

Next, the arena floods into a corridor of floating doors, each labeled with a version of "safe." *Steady. Predictable. Enough.* You're told to choose one. Instead, you press your palm to the wall and push. The plaster yields like wet paper, revealing a staircase made of stacked sketchbooks, blueprints, recipe cards, and ticket stubs—all the artifacts of what you actually care about. You climb. Your feet leave no footprints, only faint glows that spell out your name in a language you've never learned but somehow understand.

The final trial isn't timed. It isn't scored. It's a room without corners, lit by a single hanging bulb that flickers in time with your pulse. In the center sits a desk, a contract, a pen. The announcer's voice echoes from everywhere and nowhere: *Sign, and it's yours.* 

But as you reach for the pen, it turns to chalk. The contract bleeds into a blank page. The desk grows roots. And you realize the competition was never about proving you could do the work. It was about watching you finally stop asking for permission to begin.

You don't sign. You stand. And the moment you do, the walls fall away like stage flats. Beyond them, the world isn't handing you a title. It's handing you the tools. Your dream job wasn't a destination waiting to be awarded. It was a frequency you were always tuned to, just buried under years of "someday."

The stadium lights soften. The air grows warm. Somewhere above the dream, a ceiling fan turns. You feel the edge of morning brush your eyelids. You don't wake startled. You wake *ready*. 

And as the dream dissolves into the quiet rhythm of your own breathing, you already know: you'll be building it today.
