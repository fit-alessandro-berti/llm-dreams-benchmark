…decide to walk away before you say something you can’t take back.

The door closes behind you harder than you meant it to. Outside, the air feels wrong—too quiet, like the world is holding its breath. You start down the street, replaying the argument, each sentence growing sharper in your mind than it was when it was spoken.

Halfway down the block you realize the street isn’t your street.

The houses lean slightly inward, like they’re listening. The sky has that strange color dreams sometimes have—somewhere between evening and a storm that never arrives. Your phone buzzes in your pocket. It’s a message from your friend.

“Wait.”

You turn around, but the house you came from isn’t there anymore. Just a long hallway where the street should be, stretching impossibly far.

At the end of it stands your friend.

You walk toward them. With every step the argument fades, the words dissolving until you can’t even remember what you were fighting about—only the feeling that it mattered a lot a moment ago.

When you finally reach them, they look relieved.

“Good,” they say. “You figured it out.”

“Figured what out?” you ask.

But before they answer, you wake up with the strange certainty that whatever the argument was about… it wasn’t the real reason you were upset.
