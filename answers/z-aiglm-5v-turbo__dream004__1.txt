...and **as your fingers hover just inches above its surface, you realize the 'blankness' isn't actually empty.**

It hums. It vibrates against your retinas like a silent white noise, a void that is hungry.

You reach out, not knowing if you intend to touch it or push it away. The moment your fingertip makes contact, the canvas doesn't feel like fabric or wood—it feels like the surface of a cool lake, solidifying under your touch. But instead of getting wet, you leave behind a trail of **sound**.

Where you touched, a vibrant, neon violet blooms, but it doesn't look like paint; it looks like a liquid note of music. You pull your hand back, startled, and see that the color begins to crawl, growing legs and breathing.

Curiosity overrides caution—the logic of dreams dictates that you must feed this thing. You drag your palm across the center. This time, you aren't thinking of color; you are thinking of the smell of rain on hot asphalt. Instantly, the canvas ripples, and a storm cloud erupts from the weave, grey and swirling, dropping tiny droplets of silver that sizzle when they hit the ground (which has suddenly turned into a giant chessboard).

You step closer, entranced. You decide to paint something concrete. You try to imagine a simple red apple. You press both hands flat against the space.

But the world around you is warping. The sky has turned into a kaleidoscope of eyes. The ground liquefies. Because *you* are changing, the painting refuses to stay still. The apple morphs into a beating heart, which then stretches into a long, winding highway that seems to lead directly back into your own chest.

Suddenly, the edges of the canvas begin to dissolve, expanding outward until there is no difference between the painting and the landscape. The colors you created leap off the fabric and rewrite the air around you.

You look down at your hands. They are no longer hands; they are brushes made of light. And the canvas? There is no canvas anymore. 

*You* are the canvas now, and the shifting, chaotic, beautiful world is painting itself across your skin.
