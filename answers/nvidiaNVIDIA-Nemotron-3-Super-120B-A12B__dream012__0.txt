

You feel the cold spray lash your cheeks, hear the wind scream through the rigging, and sense the crew’s eyes fixed on you, waiting for your word. You grip the wheel tighter, shout “Hard to port! Let the mainsail reef!” and, with each steady pulse of the engine, you turn the ship’s bow just enough to ride the rising swell toward the faint break of light on the horizon. The storm, sensing your resolve, begins to loosen its grip, and the sea slowly surrenders to a calmer path as dawn whispers behind the clouds.
