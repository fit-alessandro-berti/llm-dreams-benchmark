The first raindrops fall upward. You watch them rise from the ferns and moss, silver beads floating into the darkening sky, and somehow this doesn't surprise you. You hold out your hand and a drop pauses above your palm, hovering, reflecting a tiny version of your face that isn't quite your face.

Thunder rolls, but it sounds like a voice—deep and familiar, like someone you knew long ago trying to tell you something important. You strain to make out the words, but they dissolve into the rumble before you can catch them.

Then you notice the path beneath your feet has become a river of leaves, all flowing in one direction, and you understand without knowing how that you're meant to follow them. The storm isn't chasing you. It's guiding you.

Lightning flashes, and in that split second of brightness, you see a small cabin between the trees that wasn't there a moment before. Warm light glows in its single window. The door stands open.

As you step toward it, the rain finally falls properly—warm as bathwater, soaking through your clothes to your skin—and you realize you're not afraid at all. You're almost... expected. Someone inside has been waiting for you, and the kettle is already on.

You reach the threshold, raise your hand to knock on the open door, and—
