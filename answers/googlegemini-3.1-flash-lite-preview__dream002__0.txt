...the air turns heavy, smelling sharply of ozone and crushed pine needles. The golden light that bathed the canopy moments ago curdles into a bruised, violet twilight. 

I reach out to brush against the bark of an ancient oak, but instead of rough wood, my hand sinks into something cool and liquid, like silk drawn through water. The forest begins to shift; the trees are no longer rooted in earth but are suspended in a vast, weightless void. As the first rumble of thunder vibrates in my marrow, the raindrops don’t fall—they hover.

Thousands of silver spheres hang frozen in the air around me. I walk forward, pushing through them. Each droplet catches the light, and as I pass, I hear a faint, chime-like whisper—a memory of someone I once knew, or perhaps someone I have yet to meet.

The storm isn't bringing destruction; it is bringing a flood of forgotten things. I look up, and the sky isn't gray, but a swirling whirlpool of ink and starlight. I realize then that I am not hiding from the gale. I am the eye of it. 

I close my eyes, and as the wind picks up, the forest dissolves into a rush of rushing air, leaving me drifting in a sea of sound, breathless and untethered, waiting to see where the wind decides to land me next.
