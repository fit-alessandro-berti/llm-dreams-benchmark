You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to **find a way to survive without the conveniences you once took for granted.**

The first thing you notice is the silence—no hum of electricity, no distant traffic, no faint buzz of a cell tower searching for a signal. Just wind through unfamiliar trees and the creak of wooden structures nearby. You're lying on a bed of woven straw, covered by a rough wool blanket that smells of smoke and earth. Sunlight filters through a small window, and the light is *wrong* somehow—warmer, more golden than the LED glow you're used to.

You sit up, and your body feels heavier, sluggish, as if your muscles have forgotten how gravity works in this place. The room around you is small and stone-walled. A wooden table holds a clay jug and a half-eaten heel of dark bread. On the wall hangs a hand-forged knife and a bundle of dried herbs tied with twine.

When you push open the heavy door, the world outside steals your breath. Rolling green hills stretch toward a distant village, and there are no power lines scarring the sky. People in linen and wool walk along a dirt path, leading a donkey loaded with goods. A blacksmith's hammer rings out from somewhere nearby, the most mechanical sound you've heard.

And then it hits you—you have *nothing*. No wallet, no ID, no phone, no knowledge of what year it is or what kingdom this village belongs to. Your clothes, now that you look down at them, have been changed. Someone dressed you in period garb while you slept.

Your stomach growls. Your throat is dry. And you realize the immediate crisis isn't philosophical—it's *practical*. You need water, you need food, and you need to figure out how to communicate without looking like a complete stranger to everyone you meet.

A woman passing by notices you standing frozen in the doorway. She tilts her head with concern, and when she speaks, the words are strange but *almost* comprehensible—an old dialect, perhaps, or a language riding the border between what you know and what you don't.

"Are you well, traveler?" she asks. "You look as though you've seen a ghost. Or perhaps *become* one."

She smiles, but there's a sharpness in her eyes—curiosity, and a hint of warning.

You have a choice to make: how do you introduce yourself to this world that's centuries removed from everything you know?
