But it’s not just rain; it’s a cascade of shimmering, silver droplets that seem to hang in the air for a moment before touching the ground. As they hit the forest floor, they don’t splash; they chime like tiny glass bells.

The trees around you begin to sway, not from wind, but as if breathing. Their bark shifts, revealing veins of soft blue light pulsing beneath the surface. You realize you are no longer afraid. Instead, a profound sense of curiosity pulls you forward. The path ahead dissolves into mist, but a faint, melodic hum guides your steps deeper into the woods, where the storm isn’t an end, but a beginning.
