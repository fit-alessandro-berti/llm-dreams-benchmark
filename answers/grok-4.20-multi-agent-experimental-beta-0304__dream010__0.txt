You are participating in a competition where the grand prize is your dream job. As the competition progresses, you face various challenges and they spiral into pure dream-logic absurdity: the interview room melts into a maze of floating equations that bite back if you hesitate, while your rivals shapeshift into versions of yourself from different ages—child-you cheering wildly, future-you offering cryptic warnings. You duel a panel of talking ravens (who judge you on "quantum flair") by reciting forgotten childhood songs that somehow rewrite the rules of gravity, letting you fly through clouds of staplers and coffee mugs. A shadow version of your deepest insecurity appears for the final trial, demanding you build a bridge across an endless chasm using only mismatched socks and half-remembered ideas. You embrace the shadow instead of fighting it, merging into something stronger, and the chasm blooms into a sunlit lab where every experiment succeeds effortlessly. The trophy morphs into a glowing key as the crowd erupts in applause from historical figures and cartoon animals alike. Just as you unlock the door to your dream job, the entire arena swirls into colorful fog, and you wake up—heart racing, strangely motivated, with the odd certainty that the real path to that job starts with one bold step today. 

What a wild ride. Dreams like this make waking life feel full of possibility, don't they?
