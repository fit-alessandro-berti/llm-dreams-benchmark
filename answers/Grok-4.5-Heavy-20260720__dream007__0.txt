You are in the middle of a heated argument with a close friend. The disagreement is about something important to both of you, and you decide to stop speaking.  

In the silence that follows, the walls of the room begin to breathe. Your friend’s face softens and multiplies into versions of themselves from every year you’ve known them. One steps forward, takes your hand, and says softly, “It was never about being right.” The others dissolve into fireflies as the two of you walk through a doorway that wasn’t there a moment ago, into a field of tall grass under a purple sky.
