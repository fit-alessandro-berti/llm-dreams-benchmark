You turn away, feeling a sudden, urgent need to find that one familiar face, the anchor in this sea of strangers. As you push past a table laden with fruit that hums softly when touched, you notice everyone else has stopped moving. They are all frozen mid-laugh, mid-sip, mid-step, their eyes wide and unblinking, fixed on something behind you.

Your heart hammers against your ribs as you slowly turn around. There, standing in the center of the room, is not the person you were looking for, but a mirror. It’s tall, ornate, and framed in vines that seem to be growing in real-time. In the reflection, you see yourself, but older, wearing clothes you’ve never owned, holding a key made of ice.

The frozen crowd begins to applaud silently, their mouths opening and closing without sound. The ice key in your reflection’s hand starts to melt, dripping onto the floor of the dream, where each drop turns into a small, glowing door. You realize you aren't looking for someone anymore; you're being invited to choose which door to walk through next.
