I wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As I explore my new surroundings, I realize I need to *find a way home.*

But that realization comes slowly, wrapped in the fog of dream-logic where urgency and wonder coexist.

---

The first thing I notice is the air Ś it smells like woodsmoke and damp earth, nothing like the filtered, conditioned air I'm used to. I'm lying on a cot in a small room with rough-hewn wooden walls. Through a single window, pale morning light spills across a dirt road where a woman leads a horse pulling a cart loaded with ceramic jugs.

No wires. No cell towers. No hum of electricity.

I step outside barefoot. The ground is cold. A village stretches before me Ś thatched roofs, a stone well at the center, the distant sound of someone hammering metal. People move slowly, deliberately. A child chases a chicken. An old man sits on a stool, mending a fishing net by hand.

I check my pockets. Nothing. No phone, no wallet, no keys. Just the clothes on my back Ś and even those feel wrong, rougher, like wool and linen instead of cotton blends.

A merchant passes by and speaks to me in a language I *almost* understand. It's not English, not Mandarin, not anything I know Ś but in the way dreams work, I grasp the meaning: *Are you lost?*

Yes. Deeply lost. Temporally lost.

---

Here's what I figure out over the next few dream-hours:

I'm somewhere in medieval Europe Ś maybe the 13th century, judging by the architecture, the clothing, the absence of gunpowder or printing presses. The village sits along a river. There's a small chapel with a crooked steeple. People barter, they don't buy. Time moves by sunlight and church bells, not clocks.

And I need to find a way home Ś but I don't know what "home" means here. Do I search for a portal? A symbol? Do I simply need to *understand something* before the dream lets me go?

I try speaking to the blacksmith, who seems like the most knowledgeable person around. He stares at me, puzzled by my strange clothes and stranger speech. But dream-logic bridges the gap. He gestures for me to sit, offers me bread and something that might be ale.

He tells me, in fragments I somehow comprehend: *There is a woman in the forest. She knows the paths between worlds. Travelers like you have come before.*

Travelers like me. I wonder how many dreamers have woken up here, barefoot and bewildered, searching for something they can't quite name.

---

I stand at the edge of the village as the sun begins to set. The forest ahead is thick, ancient, full of sounds I can't identify. I don't know if I'll find this woman. I don't know if finding her will send me home or somewhere else entirely.

But I take a step forward anyway.

Because in dreams, the searching *is* the point.
