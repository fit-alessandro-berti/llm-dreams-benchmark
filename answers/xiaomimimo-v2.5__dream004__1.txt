...and as you approach, the canvas begins to breathe. Its white surface ripples like water disturbed by a silent breath.

You reach out, and your fingertips leave trails of starlight. In response, the canvas spills forth colors that have no name—hues that hum with warmth and taste of forgotten memories. Shapes unfurl from its center: not drawn, but grown, like crystalline flowers blooming in fast-forward.

You realize you are not merely looking at it, but thinking *through* it. A feeling of nostalgia becomes a cascade of golden arches. A half-remembered melody manifests as spiraling, cobalt threads. The canvas is a mirror, but for the landscapes of your inner world.

Then, with a soft sigh, it folds in on itself, becoming a single, luminous point of light that floats before you. It pulses gently, waiting. It asks nothing, yet offers everything—the chance to dream on.

You smile, and step into the light.
