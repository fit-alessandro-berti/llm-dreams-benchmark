At the center, there is no grand mechanism, no final lock. Only a shallow pool of still, dark water resting on a pedestal of woven roots. Above it hangs a single, unlit lantern. No instructions appear. The silence is absolute.

You kneel. You don't search for a pattern this time. You simply reach out, dip your fingertips into the water, and whisper the one question you've been carrying but never voicing. The surface ripples. The lantern ignites with a soft, silver flame that casts no shadows, only clarity. The water lifts, weightless, and settles into your palms like a cool, luminous stone.

The maze exhales. Walls fold inward like closing hands. The floor tilts gently upward, carrying you toward a sky you recognize as your own bedroom ceiling.

You wake. Sunlight paints familiar lines across the sheets. Your hands are empty, but your chest feels lighter, as if something heavy has been quietly returned to its proper place. On your nightstand, half-hidden under a stack of books, lies a smooth river stone you don't remember leaving there. On its surface, faint as a breath, is the same symbol that opened the very first gate.

You didn't escape the maze. You understood it. And in the quiet after the dream, the puzzles keep solving themselves.
