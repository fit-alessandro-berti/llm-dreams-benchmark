You’re at a sprawling, labyrinthine mansion—part grand ballroom, part endless hallway, part something else entirely. The air hums with laughter and clinking glasses, but the faces blur like wet paint. You scan the crowd for *them*—the one person you know here—but the sea of strangers keeps shifting. A woman in a sequined dress turns to you, her smile too wide, and says, *"You’re looking for someone, aren’t you?"* before dissolving into confetti.

You push through a set of double doors and find yourself in a dimly lit library. The shelves stretch into darkness, and the books whisper when you pass. A man in a tuxedo with no face offers you a drink. *"It’s whatever you want it to be,"* he murmurs. You take a sip—it tastes like childhood summers, then like regret, then like nothing at all.

Then, a commotion: a group of people in matching silver jumpsuits are performing an elaborate dance, their movements synchronized but *wrong*, like marionettes with tangled strings. One of them locks eyes with you and mouths, *"You’re late."* You check your watch—it’s melting.

Finally, you spot *them*—your friend—across the room, waving. Relief floods you… until you realize they’re standing in front of a mirror. When you reach out, your hand passes through their reflection. They turn, and their face is yours.

*"Took you long enough,"* they say. *"We’ve been waiting."*

The lights flicker. The walls breathe. The party goes on forever.
