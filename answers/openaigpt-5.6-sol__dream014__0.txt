The strangers speak in voices you almost recognize. One carries a lantern filled with tiny stars; another scatters breadcrumbs that bloom into blue flowers the moment they touch the pavement. As you walk, the city’s noise softens into music, and the towering buildings lean closer, as if listening.

At last, they lead you to a quiet square where a single red door stands without walls. “This is your way,” they tell you.

You open it and find not a place, but a memory: sunlight across a familiar kitchen table, someone laughing in the next room, and the deep certainty that you have been loved all along.

When you turn to thank the strangers, the square is empty. Only the lantern remains in your hand, its stars glowing warmly.

You still don’t know exactly where you are going—but, for the first time, you no longer feel lost.
