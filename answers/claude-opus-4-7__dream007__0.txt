...pause, because something about the light in the room has started to feel wrong. The windows are too tall. I don't remember this kitchen having windows at all.

My friend — and I know it's my friend, though when I try to focus on their face it keeps sliding sideways like a reflection in moving water — is saying something about trust. Or maybe about a book we both read. The words matter enormously and I can't hold onto any of them.

\Wait,\ I open my mouth to begin again, I'm outside, and we're walking along a road I half-recognize from childhood, the argument still going but gentler now, folded into the rhythm of our steps. I'm trying to explain something I believe very deeply. I can feel the shape of it in my chest. I just can't find the words for it in any language I know.

My friend stops walking. They look at me with an expression of such patient sadness that I understand, suddenly, that we've had this argument before. Many times. That this is what we do.

\say what?* — because I haven't said anything yet, have I? — but the road is tilting, or I am, and I'm reaching out to steady myself against—

—and then I'm awake, or awake enough to notice I was dreaming, and the argument dissolves like sugar, leaving only the certainty that it mattered, and the small ache of not being able to remember why.
