One second it is a sleek high-rise with floor-to-ceiling windows; the next, it is an open field beneath a sky made of velvet paper that you could rip open.

The final hurdle arrives when all but one door remain closed, and in that single remaining chamber stands a desk that appears out of nowhere. There is no interview. There are no questions. Instead, there is only a reflection on the mirror behind the desk, and in it, you have aged decades, yet you look perfectly calm.

You reach forward to shake hands with your own shadow, realizing the competitors around you have all merged into walls or faded into light. The competition isn't about proving you are worthy; it's about proving that *you* are the one holding the gavel.

When you step into the office in victory, the view outside shows a world exactly as you wish to see it: balanced, creative, and quiet. You realize with a jolt of clarity waking up—this competition was never designed for you to win at all. It was designed for you to find out that the "dream job" isn't a title or a paycheck; it's the peace of mind you create by realizing the only person who holds power over your destiny is the one standing in the chair.

You step away from the desk, close the door on the competition, and walk through the office with your hands in your pockets. The job begins immediately, not because you got hired, but because you finally built it for yourself out of dust and light.
