You close your eyes and think of something you have lost.

A name. A place. A feeling.

When you open the box, there is no treasure inside—only a tiny mirror, dark as still water. You peer into it and see not your reflection, but a door standing alone in the middle of a vast ocean. The door opens slowly, spilling golden light across the waves.

From somewhere beyond it, a voice whispers:

“Wake up only if you’re ready to remember.”

The garden fades, the box slips from your hands, and just before everything goes white, you see the symbols rearrange themselves into a single word:
