You start to run.

The city seems to rearrange itself around you. Streets lengthen when you look down them; buildings lean close, their windows reflecting not your face but moments you almost remember. You stop a passerby to ask for directions, but when they turn, they are wearing your coat.

“The meeting?” they say. “You’re already late.”

They point toward a narrow alley between a library and a river. You hurry through it and emerge in a vast underground station. Every sign bears the same word: **ARRIVALS**. Trains glide in without making a sound, their doors opening onto forests, childhood bedrooms, empty classrooms.

Your phone rings. The screen shows no number.

“Where are you?” asks a voice you recognize but cannot place.

“I don’t know.”

“That’s why you were invited.”

A train marked **ONE HOUR AGO** begins to depart. You jump aboard just as the doors close. Inside, everyone at the meeting is already seated around a long table: strangers, old friends, people you have never met but somehow miss. At the far end is an empty chair beneath a bright lamp.

You take your seat.

The clock on the wall counts backward. One minute remains.

Then the door opens, and you walk in.
