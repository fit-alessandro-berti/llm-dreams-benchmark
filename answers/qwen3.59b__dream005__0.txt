You push past a group where everyone is dressed in suits made of feathers, whispering to each other about a clock that has no hands and still ticks loudly against the silence.

The air smells heavily of cinnamon and ozone. Every time you try to take a step toward the person you seek, the floor ripples like water, forcing you to adjust your balance, which only makes you stumble into different rooms within the same room—libraries filled with books that fly off the shelves and kitchens where the stovetop is hot enough to melt thoughts.

As you navigate these impossible corridors, you begin to realize the one person you know isn't just missing from the party; they are part of the architecture itself. Their absence creates a shape in the crowd, a hollow space that hums with energy. Finally, you turn around and see them standing right behind you, not as a separate figure, but wearing your own reflection like a coat. You reach out, but your hands pass through the mirror of their face.

The dream then shifts; the room expands into an entire ocean, and you wake up, the sound of the party's distant laughter echoing from downstairs where no doors exist.
