You are in a world where colors and shapes are constantly changing. As you explore this surreal landscape, you come across a blank canvas and your fingers tingle—before you can blink, a worn wooden brush has settled in your palm, its bristles glowing with the same shifting lavender that just colored the distant mountain peaks. You hover it over the stark white surface, hesitating at the void before you, and when you drag a single, shaky line across the middle, it doesn’t stay a line at all: soft, pulsing teal light spills from the stroke, drifting off the canvas to wrap around the nearby shifting sand dunes, turning them into a field of swaying sea grass that glows like bioluminescent waves.

A tiny, shape-shifting creature trots over: one moment it’s a fluffy fox with paws like cloud fluff, the next it’s a swirling patch of orange marigolds, its nose twitching at the smell of the new light. It tilts its head, then taps the canvas with its wet, pollen-dusted nose, and a splash of butter-yellow paint bursts forth, forming a cluster of floating orbs that drift upward, merging with the sky’s rapidly shifting constellations. When you reach out to touch one of the orbs, it dissolves into the thick, sweet scent of warm honey, and you notice the canvas isn’t just a flat rectangle anymore—its edges are melting into the surreal world around you, one side blending into the cool blue of a newly formed river, the other threading into the pink tendrils of a sunset that’s fading faster than you can blink.

You let the brush slip from your hand, and it begins to move on its own, painting quick, jagged lines that bloom into the sharp, red-capped mushrooms you passed an hour ago, then soft, swirling arcs that match the way the wind ripples the grass. Every mark you (and the creature) make doesn’t just stay stuck to the canvas: it bleeds into the landscape, turning the monotone shifting ground into a tapestry of every color and shape you’ve seen since waking in this world. When you step back, you realize the canvas has stretched to match the curve of the horizon, and the ground beneath your feet is now the painting itself, each step pressing down into a patch of the art you’ve created, with wildflowers popping up behind you as you walk.

The creature curls up on your shoulder, its fur soft as dandelion fluff, and as you wander your own stretched canvas, you notice tiny, faint marks dotted across the surface: other people’s brushstrokes from other dreams, the ghost of a child’s rainbow, the smudge of a traveler’s tired hand, even a single, smudged paw print just like the creature’s own. For a breath, the world stops shifting, just for a moment, and you can hear the quiet hum of every dreamer who’s ever stood in this surreal place, leaving their mark before the world reconfigures again. Just as you reach out to trace a faint, silver star someone else painted, the canvas pulls you closer, and suddenly you’re floating inside the art, surrounded by the glowing sea grass and floating honey-scented orbs, until the scene fades to the soft buzz of your alarm.
