...start to run, my bare feet slapping against cold, wet pavement that glistens under a bruised purple sky. The city breathes around me – a low, rhythmic hum like distant machinery, punctuated by the occasional cry of a bird that sounds suspiciously like a steam whistle. Buildings tower like petrified waves, their surfaces shifting: one moment dark iron, the next a shimmering, iridescent membrane that ripples like water. I pass shopfronts with no windows, only swirling, kaleidoscopic patterns that make my stomach lurch. A door swings open silently on a corner, revealing only swirling mist and the faint scent of ozone.

*An hour.* The thought claws at me. My pocket buzzes. I fumble, pulling out a phone whose screen is a featureless silver disc, unresponsive. The time display is a shifting constellation of stars that doesn't look like any clock I know. A low chime echoes, not from the phone, but from everywhere – the air itself vibrates with it. *The meeting.* I need direction.

I skid to a halt by a fountain that doesn't spout water, but instead pours out tiny, luminous fish made of light, swirling in intricate, mesmerizing patterns before dissolving into the air. Above it, etched onto a column of seamless, obsidian stone, is a single symbol: a spiral intersecting a straight line. It glows faintly, pulsing in time with the city's hum. I stare, willing it to mean something. *Spiral and line... direction? Compass?* The fish swirls intensify near the symbol, forming a fleeting arrow pointing down a side street paved with interlocking tiles that seem to shift under my gaze.

I sprint down the alley, the tiles reordering themselves beneath me. The air grows thicker, smelling of damp earth and old paper. The buildings lean closer, their windows now filled with blurred, shifting figures – faces I almost recognize, but not quite. One figure, a woman in a long coat, looks directly at me. For a split second, her face is *my* face. I gasp, stumbling. The figure vanishes, leaving only a faint shimmer.

The alley opens onto a vast plaza dominated by a colossal, intricate clock face. But the hands are made of living vines, thick with glowing, pulsating blue flowers. The numbers are abstract symbols. The clock doesn't tick; instead, the vines grow and retract slowly, like breathing, and the flowers flare with light in a complex, beautiful rhythm. The sound is a deep, resonant thrumming, like a stone tapped in a vast cathedral.

*Is this the time?* I look desperately at the vines. One hand, thick and arthritic, moves with agonizing slowness towards the number that looks like an inverted pyramid. The *other* hand, thin and delicate, traces a path across the dial. It pauses. Points. Not at the inverted pyramid, but directly at the center – where a tiny, perfectly still black circle resides. A single, perfect, unwavering point.

*The meeting is here.* The realization hits like a physical blow. The clock isn't telling time; it's *holding* it. The black circle is the destination point. But the plaza is empty except for the clock and the shifting, luminous vines.

I take a step towards the clock. My foot lands on a tile that wasn't there a second ago. It’s warm. The surface isn't stone, but something like woven light. As I step onto it, the entire plaza *shifts*. The clock remains, but the surrounding space dissolves. The vines retract into the ground. The air hums with a pure, high note. I’m standing not on a plaza, but on a vast, open plain under a night sky filled with stars I’ve never seen, swirling in impossible patterns. The black circle from the clock is now a single, perfect, impossibly bright point of light on the horizon.

The dream holds its breath. The meeting. The hour. The city. The point of light. I stand at the edge, the familiar sensation of the dream shifting, the desperate urgency warring with the profound, silent invitation of the impossible point on the horizon. The clock is done. The map is gone. Only the light remains. And the question hangs in the star-drifted air: *Do I go towards it?*
