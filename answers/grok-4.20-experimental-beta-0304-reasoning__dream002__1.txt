...and the sky cracks open like it’s been waiting for this moment.

The peaceful forest transforms in seconds. Trees that were whispering with gentle breezes now thrash wildly, their leaves flashing silver as the wind flips them inside out. The air grows thick, electric. You can taste the ozone on your tongue.

Thunder rolls through your chest like a second heartbeat. But this isn’t an ordinary storm.

The rain that begins to fall is *warm*. Not just warm—*golden*. It glows as it falls, each drop catching light that doesn’t seem to come from the sky anymore. The droplets hover for a moment before they touch you, like they’re deciding whether to become liquid or memory.

You keep walking. Your feet no longer crunch on leaves—they sink slightly into the ground as if the forest floor has gone soft, almost breathing. The trees begin to *sing*. Not with voices, but with resonance. Deep, ancient tones that vibrate through your bones.

Then you see it.

Between two ancient oaks, the storm has torn a hole in reality itself. Through the swirling violet clouds and golden rain, you can see *another forest*—but this one is upside down, floating in the sky. The trees there have silver bark and leaves made of starlight. A version of *you* is walking there too, but they’re walking toward you, as if you’re their storm.

They raise a hand in greeting.

The lightning flashes again, and for a split second, you realize the lightning isn’t electricity at all—it’s memories. Every bolt shows you a moment of your life you’d forgotten, playing out in brilliant white across the clouds.

The other-you is getting closer.

The storm isn’t coming to destroy the forest.

It’s coming to merge the two versions of you that have been walking through different realities.

You feel the dream beginning to stretch, like taffy, like the moment before waking...

Do you reach out and take their hand?
