With the first stroke, the canvas *breathes*. The paint (if it can be called paint) flows like molten light, forming a swirling galaxy where each star is a memory you’ve forgotten. A second stroke births a forest of glass trees, their leaves chiming like wind chimes in a breeze you can’t feel. Then, without warning, the shapes *leap* from the canvas— the trees stretch toward you, their branches weaving into a bridge that arches over a river of liquid time.

As you step onto the bridge, the canvas behind you begins to fold itself into a paper crane, which takes flight and leads you deeper into the dream. The colors around you now respond to your emotions: joy paints the path in gold, curiosity turns the air into a kaleidoscope of questions, and for a fleeting moment, you wonder—*are you the artist, or the art?*

Then, the crane dissolves into a whisper: *"Wake up, or keep dreaming?"*

(How would you like the dream to end—or continue?)
