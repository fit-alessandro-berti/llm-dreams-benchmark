You find yourself in a vast, unfamiliar city, and you realize you have an important meeting in an hour but no idea where it is. You start to sprint down a rain-slicked boulevard, the kaleidoscopic glow of neon signs blurring overhead—some in languages you almost recognize, others alien. Your shoes slap against wet pavement, echoing strangely as if the sound bends around glass skyscrapers that seem to lean closer when you glance sideways.

You pull out your phone—it shows no signal, but the screen flickers with cryptic symbols that rearrange themselves into a map only when you blink. Each blink reveals a new path: a bridge of floating lanterns, a tunnel lined with whispered secrets, a rooftop garden humming with clockwork birds. You feel your boss’s disappointment like static in your chest—rain or sweat?

Just as panic sharpens your breath, you duck into an alley where an old woman offers you tea from a kettle that steams with memories. You drink it, and suddenly, the city unfolds like a pop-up book. You see your meeting venue: a glass elevator ascending a mirrored column in the plaza ahead—your reflection already waiting inside. You run—but when you arrive, there isn’t a building, just the empty column and your reflection waving goodbye from its core. The hourglass inverts, grains of time rain upward, and you realize, with sudden clarity—the meeting isn’t *out there*: it was never about the location. You needed to lose yourself to find what you’d forgotten.

You nod to your reflection. The city folds. You wake, tapping 'Send' on an email you swore you’d finished at dawn—but only remembered when the dream unlocked it.
