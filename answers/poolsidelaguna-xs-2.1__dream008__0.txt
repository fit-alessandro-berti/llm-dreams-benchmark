The document glows faintly on your screen, words shifting like liquid as you stare at it. Your boss—stranger-like, with too many eyes—nods once and steps away, leaving you alone in a conference room that wasn’t there a moment ago. The clock on the wall spins backward, ticking in reverse, each second peeling away into the walls like old wallpaper.

You open the folder. Inside are hundreds of blank pages, but when you look closely, tiny words appear: *“Just begin.”*

You start typing. The keys click like bones. The project is called *“The Unseen Tomorrow.”* The deadline? Already past. Or maybe it hasn’t happened yet.

And then, gently, the room begins to hum. And you remember—this was never about the project. It was about starting before you were ready.

You close your eyes. The dream completes itself.
