…you feel a storm approaching and the air turns electric, thick with the smell of rain and pine. The trees begin to sway, their leaves turning silver as the wind rushes through the canopy in a single great breath. A roll of thunder echoes somewhere beyond the hills, low and constant, like the forest is humming to itself.

You start to run, not out of fear, but because your feet seem to know the way. The path narrows, roots twist underfoot, and the first cold drops fall heavy as coins. Lightning forks overhead, momentarily bleaching the woods to black and white, and in that flash you see it: a small stone doorway half-hidden in a tangle of ivy, leading into the trunk of an impossibly large oak.

You duck inside. The roaring storm fades to a murmur, replaced by the sound of running water and distant chimes. The air inside is warm, honey-colored, lit by lanterns that hang from branches that shouldn’t exist within a tree. A figure sits by a pool of still water—familiar, though you can’t name them—and gestures for you to sit.

“You made it before the rain,” they say, smiling. “Now the real journey can begin.”

But just as you lean forward to ask what they mean, another crack of thunder tears through the dream. The lanterns flicker, the doorway blurs, and you feel yourself drifting upward, pulled out of the forest like a kite on a string. The stone doorway shrinks to a pinprick of green light, and then—

You open your eyes. The storm is gone. Only the soft sound of your own breathing remains.
