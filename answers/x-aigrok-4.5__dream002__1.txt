**...you start to run, seeking shelter among the towering trees, but the forest path twists and softens underfoot, becoming a carpet of glowing moss that cushions every step as if the ground itself is breathing.**

The sky, moments ago a vivid green canopy filtered with golden light, darkens in an instant. Thunder doesn’t crack so much as hum—a low, resonant tone that vibrates in your chest and makes the leaves shimmer with iridescent colors. Rain begins to fall upward, silver droplets rising from the earth and gathering into luminous clouds that spiral gently overhead instead of pouring down. 

You feel no fear. The storm isn’t coming *for* you; it’s inviting you. Branches that were bare a second ago bloom with strange, soft blossoms that pulse like lanterns, illuminating the way deeper into a clearing where the air smells of rain on stone and something sweeter—like forgotten childhood summers. 

As the wind gathers, it lifts you a few inches off the ground, not roughly, but cradling you in a current of cool, electric air. Leaves and petals swirl around you in lazy orbits. In the center of the clearing stands an ancient tree whose bark is smooth as glass and reflective. In its surface you see not your own face, but fleeting scenes: a childhood bedroom, an open ocean at night, a room full of people laughing whose names you almost remember.

You reach out. The moment your palm touches the tree, the storm stills completely. Everything hangs suspended—the upward rain frozen mid-air, the humming silence perfect. A single drop of water (or is it light?) lands on your tongue and tastes of clarity. 

You understand, without words, that you can choose: step through the tree into whatever lies beyond, stay in this perpetual electric calm, or simply wake. The forest waits patiently, the storm now holding its breath with you.

Somewhere distant, softer than the previous thunder, comes the faint sound of your own heartbeat—or an alarm—or both.

And then the dream folds gently closed, like a book whom you may open again whenever you wish.
