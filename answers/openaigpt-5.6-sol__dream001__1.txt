You start to run, though every street seems to fold into another, signs rearranging themselves the moment you look away. You ask a woman made of newspapers for directions, but she only points toward the sky.

Above you, trains glide between the rooftops on ribbons of light. One stops in midair and opens its doors. Somehow, you step aboard.

Inside, every passenger is you at a different age. A child version clutches a red balloon. An older version sleeps with a map spread across their knees. Across the aisle, you find an envelope addressed in your own handwriting:

**THE MEETING IS WHERE YOU LEFT IT.**

The train descends into the lobby of a building you almost recognize. You hurry through a corridor lined with clocks, all counting backward. At the final door, you pause, breathless, and check the time.

You are exactly one hour early.

Inside the meeting room, there is only a long table and an empty chair. On the table sits the red balloon. When you touch it, the city outside goes silent.

Then someone behind you says, “Good. You finally found us.”

You turn around—and wake with the map still folded in your hand.
