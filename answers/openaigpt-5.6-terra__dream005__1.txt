You are at a large social gathering where you only know one person. As you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people and each group is speaking a different language—though somehow you understand every word.

In one room, people are exchanging old memories as if they were business cards. In another, strangers are laughing around a table covered in tiny doors, each one opening onto a different childhood bedroom. Someone gently takes your hand and tells you they have seen your friend upstairs.

But there is no upstairs.

You follow them through a hallway that seems to lengthen whenever you look behind you. At the end is a staircase made of folded coats. You climb it and arrive at a quiet kitchen, where your friend is standing with their back to you, washing a single glass over and over.

You say their name.

They turn around, but they have your face.

Before you can speak, they smile and say, “You made it.”

Then everyone downstairs begins applauding.
