Panic sets in. I start jogging down the sidewalk, but my legs feel like they are moving through invisible molasses. I try to ask a passerby for directions, but when the woman turns around, she has the face of my third-grade teacher, entirely unbothered by my urgency. 

"You'll need the blue train," she says calmly, pointing her umbrella at the sky. "It leaves exactly when the shadows touch."

Before I can ask what that means, the concrete beneath my feet seamlessly transitions into ornate, velvet-carpeted stairs. I am suddenly indoors, rushing through the sprawling, dimly lit corridors of an impossibly large hotel. The doors lining the hallway stretch up to the ceiling, numbered in random sequences: 42, 8, a triangle, 901. 

I know, with that absolute, unquestionable certainty you only possess in dreams, that the meeting is behind the door smelling of rain.

I sprint faster, the walls warping and stretching alongside me. A low, rhythmic ticking echoes through the corridor, getting louder until it sounds like a massive heartbeat. Finally, at the very end of the hall, I find a modest wooden door with a brass handle shaped like a bird. I twist it, throwing my weight against the wood, gasping out an apology for being late.

But as I stumble inside, there is no boardroom. 

I am standing on a completely empty, sunlit beach. The ocean laps gently at my bare feet—my shoes have completely vanished. Sitting on a single leather office chair in the sand is a giant, majestic owl wearing a pair of reading glasses. 

The owl looks at me, adjusts its glasses, and says in a deeply resonant, echoing voice, "Ah, you're here. We were just about to discuss the architecture of clouds. Do you have the blueprints?"

I reach into my pocket, suddenly realizing I am holding a handful of glowing sea glass. I place them on the armrest of the chair, feeling a profound, inexplicable wave of relief wash over me. 

"Perfect," the owl nods, and as it speaks, the sky begins to fold inward like a piece of origami, dissolving the world into a warm, humming white light.
