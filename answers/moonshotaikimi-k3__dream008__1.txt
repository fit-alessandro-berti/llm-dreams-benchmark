...and you open your laptop, but the keyboard has become piano keys. You press middle C and it types the letter Q. Your boss nods approvingly, like this is exactly correct.

"The merger with the ocean is due by lunch," he says, and you understand completely, even though you understand nothing. You've never been trained in ocean mergers. Everyone else in the office is already on conference calls with whales.

You start typing an email, but every sentence comes out as something you actually said in 2009. Embarrassing things. Your screen is now the size of a billboard, and the whole office is reading it.

You reach for a pen and it's a banana. It writes beautifully, though. Best pen you've ever had.

The deadline is now. It was always now. Your boss is standing behind you, except he's also your third-grade teacher and also somehow your childhood dog. "Is it done?" he asks, in your mother's voice.

"Almost," you say, and open the project file. Inside the file is a staircase. You start walking down, because obviously that's where the work is kept. The stairs descend and descend, and the deadline gets louder, humming like a refrigerator.

At the bottom is a door with your name on it, spelled wrong, and somehow that's the most terrifying part. You reach for the handle.

Your phone buzzes. It's the ocean, asking where things stand.
