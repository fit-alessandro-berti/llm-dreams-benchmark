...and the air grows thick with the metallic scent of ozone and damp earth. The golden afternoon light filtering through the canopy is abruptly swallowed by a bruised, violet sky. 

The birds stop singing all at once. In their place, a low, rhythmic humming begins to vibrate up through the soles of your feet, making the mossy ground feel alive. 

You try to run toward what looks like a wooden cabin in the distance, but your legs move with a strange, weightless slowness, as if you are wading through deep water. The wind picks up, but it doesn't tear leaves from the branches. Instead, it rips glowing, bioluminescent spores from the tree bark, swirling them into a brilliant, neon cyclone around you.

Just as the first drops begin to fall, you realize they aren't rain. They are tiny, crystalline spheres that chime like silver bells when they strike the forest floor. 

You finally reach the cabin, but as you throw open the heavy oak door, it isn't a cabin at all. It’s a vast, ancient greenhouse. Inside, the air is perfectly still, warm, and smells of vanilla and old paper. Impossible flowers bloom and fold back into buds in continuous, silent loops. 

In the center of the greenhouse stands a tall, ornate mirror. You walk toward it, the chiming of the crystal rain growing muffled outside. But when you look into the glass, it doesn't reflect the greenhouse, or you. It reflects the peaceful, sunlit forest from the beginning of your walk, perfectly still and waiting. 

You reach out to touch the glass. The moment your fingertips make contact, the surface ripples like a pond. The chiming of the rain shifts, slowing down, transforming into a familiar, rhythmic sound—the steady beat of your own heart, or perhaps an alarm clock ringing in the distance. 

The edges of the greenhouse begin to blur into brilliant white, and you feel the gentle, sinking sensation of waking up.
