I blink hard, trying to force the text to hold still. When I open my eyes, the letters lock into place, but they no longer form words. Question 1 asks: 

*“Calculate the exact weight of the color blue when it is remembered on a Tuesday. Show your work.”*

Panic flares in my chest. I reach for my No. 2 pencil, but my fingers grasp something cold and pulpy. I look down to see I am holding a stalk of celery. I try to sharpen it, but the sharpener on my desk is suddenly a small, ticking music box playing a slowed-down version of "Pop Goes the Weasel."

I look around the lecture hall. My classmates are all furiously writing, but none of them are using desks. They are suspended in mid-air, writing on the backs of giant, translucent manta rays that swim lazily through the fluorescent lighting. 

The proctor clears his throat. I look at the front of the room, but the professor has been replaced by an eight-foot-tall owl wearing my grandfather’s reading glasses. 

“Five minutes remaining,” the owl announces, though its beak never moves. The voice echoes directly inside my teeth. 

I look desperately back at my paper. The career I’m testing for—which I now suddenly realize is *Chief Architect of the Clouds*—depends entirely on passing this. But the paper has turned into a puddle of shimmering mercury. 

I plunge my hands into the liquid metal. Instead of hitting the bottom of the desk, my arms go right through, deep into an icy, rushing current. The wooden desk splinters and expands, transforming into a small wooden rowboat. The walls of the classroom fold outward like cardboard, falling away to reveal a vast, starless ocean under a sky made of swirling purple velvet.

The music box from my desk floats past me on the water, still ticking.

The anxiety of the exam violently drains away, replaced by the weightless, buzzing realization that I am in complete control. I don't need to answer the questions. To become the Architect of the Clouds, I just have to build them.

I stand up in the rocking boat, drop the celery stalk into the dark water, raise my hands toward the empty purple sky, and begin to pull the fog up from the sea.
