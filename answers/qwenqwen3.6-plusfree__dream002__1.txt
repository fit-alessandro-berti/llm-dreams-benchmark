...and the trees begin to hum, a low vibration that climbs through the soles of your feet and settles heavy in your ribs. The canopy above bleeds into bruised violet, and the first drops of rain don't fall—they rise, spiraling upward like silver threads stitching the earth to the sky. You don't run. Your footsteps leave faint, glowing prints that pulse in time with your breathing. 

The wind carries whispers, not in words, but in fragments: a childhood laugh echoing through an empty kitchen, the weight of a promise you never kept, the smell of rain on hot pavement from a summer you can't quite place. A stag steps from the underbrush, its antlers branching with quiet, steady lightning, and holds your gaze. There's no fear in you now, only a strange, swelling recognition. You understand, without being told, that the storm isn't coming to break you. It's coming to return something you left behind.

Then the sky finally opens. The rain falls warm and heavy, and where it touches the forest floor, bioluminescent moss unfurls in glowing geometric patterns, tracing a path that wasn't there a moment ago. You step onto it. The earth feels softer, alive, humming back to you. The storm doesn't rage; it breathes. And as the wind wraps around your shoulders like a familiar coat, you realize you're not walking through the forest anymore. You're walking through yourself. 

The dream doesn't fade. It simply turns the page.
