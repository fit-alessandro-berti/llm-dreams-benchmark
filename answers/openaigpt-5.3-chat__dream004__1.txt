Every time the colors in the sky shift, faint shapes ripple across its surface—triangles becoming birds, circles melting into rivers, squares unfolding like doors. When you step closer, the ground beneath your feet rearranges itself into a mosaic path that leads right up to it.

You lift your hand.

Before your fingers touch the canvas, a brush appears in your grip—its bristles made of light, dripping slow, glowing colors you’ve never seen before. When you make the first stroke, the paint doesn’t stay on the canvas. It rises, hovering, and becomes real.

A blue curve becomes a floating lake.
A quick yellow slash turns into a flock of humming, geometric creatures.
A spiral of violet opens a staircase that climbs into a sky that wasn’t there a moment ago.

Then the canvas paints back.

Lines stretch out toward you, sketching possibilities: forests made of glass leaves, cities shaped like music, oceans that reflect memories instead of light. The world waits, shifting gently, as if asking what it should become next.

And you realize something strange.

This place isn’t a dream you’re exploring.

It’s a dream that’s waiting for you to finish drawing it.
