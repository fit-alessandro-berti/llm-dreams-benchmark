You find yourself in a vast, unfamiliar city, and you realize you have an important meeting in an hour but no idea where it is. You start to walk faster, expecting panic to rise, but instead the streets seem to lean gently toward you, as if listening.

The buildings are impossibly tall, their windows glowing with colors that shift whenever you try to focus on them. Every passerby appears to know exactly where they are going. When you stop someone to ask for directions, they smile warmly and hand you a folded map.

The map is blank.

You look up to ask what it means, but the person is already gone.

A clock tower chimes somewhere in the distance. You check your watch. It says 11:03. A moment later you check again. It still says 11:03.

Relieved that time has somehow paused, you begin exploring.

The city changes as you walk. Narrow alleys open into sunlit gardens. Train stations lead into libraries. Elevators descend into cloud-filled skies instead of basements. Yet somehow, every turn feels familiar, like remembering a place you have never visited.

Eventually you notice small signs appearing on walls and lampposts. Each sign contains a single word:

"Closer."

You follow them.

The signs lead you through a quiet square where hundreds of empty chairs face a fountain. In the center of the fountain floats a sealed envelope with your name written on it in handwriting you recognize but cannot place.

You open it.

Inside is a meeting invitation.

Location: Here.

Time: Now.

Confused, you look around. The chairs are no longer empty. Every seat is occupied by a different version of you. Younger you. Older you. Confident you. Nervous you. The you who took risks. The you who played it safe. The you who followed dreams. The you who let some go.

None of them speak.

They simply watch as you walk to the final empty chair at the front.

When you sit down, the fountain becomes perfectly still. The city falls silent.

Then one of the older versions of you stands and says, "We've been waiting for you."

"For what?" you ask.

The figure smiles.

"To arrive."

A warm breeze passes through the square, carrying the scent of rain and distant oceans. The buildings soften into light. The other versions of you begin to fade, not sadly, but peacefully, like stars disappearing at dawn.

As the last of them vanishes, you realize the meeting was never about finding a place.

It was about finding yourself.

The clock tower chimes again.

This time your watch changes to 11:04.

And just before you wake up, you notice a final sign hanging above the square:

**You were never lost. You were becoming.**
