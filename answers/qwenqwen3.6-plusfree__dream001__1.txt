...walk, then jog, as the cobblestones beneath your shoes ripple like dark water. Street signs rewrite themselves with every blink—half in your own handwriting, half in constellations you've never mapped but somehow remember. You check your watch, but the glass has softened into a pool of quicksilver, reflecting a sky that cycles through twilight, dawn, and bruised violet in the span of three strides. A familiar urgency spikes, but it floats, cushioned, like falling through layered silk.

You turn into a passage that unfolds like a paper fan. The brick walls exhale. Above you, a brass key swings from a single thread of spider silk, glinting just out of reach. You reach, miss, and stop. The moment your shoulders drop, the thread snaps and the key lands in your palm, warm as a sleeping bird. It isn't for a lock. It's heavier than it looks, humming with a low, steady pulse.

You follow it. The alley spits you into a plaza where rain falls upward, gathering in suspended silver clouds before drifting back to the ground. People stand in quiet rings around empty chairs. No one speaks. No one moves. One of them turns. It's you—older, quieter, wearing the exact coat you left on a hook years ago. "You're late," they say, but their voice sounds like a drawer sliding shut. "Or early. The room isn't keeping time. It's keeping space."

You look down. The key has dissolved into a faint gold smear on your skin. Panic unwinds. You realize the address was never on paper. The hour was never real. The meeting was only ever waiting for you to stop running.

You pull out the nearest chair. The wood is sun-warm. As you sit, the plaza exhales. The upward rain stills. Somewhere, a single bell rings, and the city folds around you like a blanket you didn't know you'd been reaching for. 

You're here. You were always here. 

*...and then you begin to dream of waking up.*
