You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and symbols that seem to breathe and rearrange themselves the longer you look. They form constellations of forgotten memories, maps of places you’ve only visited in other dreams, and the faint outline of a face that might be your own from a future you haven’t lived yet. When you gently trace a single spiraling line with your fingertip, the patterns flare with soft golden light. The lock doesn’t click open—it dissolves like mist at dawn. The lid unfolds petal by petal, revealing not a treasure, but an infinite nesting of smaller identical boxes, each one slightly warmer than the last. In the tiniest heart of them all lies a folded scrap of paper in your own handwriting: “You were never locked out. You were locked in. The key was always the act of looking closer.”

And as you read the words, the dream begins to fold itself around you, and you realize you are already inside the next box, examining new patterns, smiling.
