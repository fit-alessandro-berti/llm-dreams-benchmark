You are participating in a competition where the grand prize is your dream job. As the competition progresses, you face various challenges and each one feels pulled straight from the job itself. 

**Round 1: The 5-Minute Pitch**  
You walk into a room with no chairs, just a whiteboard and three judges wearing poker faces. The task: explain your entire vision for the role in 5 minutes using only metaphors. Your heart’s pounding, but then you remember why you wanted this job in the first place. You grab the marker and draw a lighthouse, a bridge, and a messy sketch of a team around a table. By the time you say “and that’s why I build,” one judge actually smiles.

**Round 2: The Midnight Crisis**  
They lock you in a mock office at 11:47 PM. Fake email alerts start flooding in. A “client” is upset, your “teammate” is stuck, and the “deadline” moved up by a week. You realize the challenge isn’t about fixing everything — it’s about deciding what matters. You kill the inbox notifications, call the teammate first, and send the client one honest paragraph instead of ten panicked ones. When the doors open at 1:00 AM, the proctor hands you a keycard that says “Calm Under Pressure.”

**Round 3: The Team Build**  
You’re paired with the other finalist — the one who’s been your biggest competition. Together you have 90 minutes to design something from scratch with mismatched LEGO, string, and three rubber bands. At first you both try to lead. Then you stop and ask, “What are you best at?” She says structure, you say story. The thing you build is ugly and wobbly, but when you present it, the judges don’t look at the object. They look at how you two explain it together.

**The Final Door**  
They bring you to a hallway with one door labeled “Your Dream Job.” Your hand’s on the handle when a voice says, “Last challenge: write your own job description before you open it. If it matches what’s inside, the role is yours.” 

The pen feels heavier than any of the other tests. You take a breath and start writing...

What do you write on that job description? I’ll keep the dream going from whatever you put down.
