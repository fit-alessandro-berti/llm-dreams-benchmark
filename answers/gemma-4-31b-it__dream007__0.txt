There are no more words, because you can suddenly feel their thoughts—not as sentences, but as colors and memories. You realize that the argument was never about the thing itself, but about the fear of losing the connection.

As the lily pad begins to sink slowly into the luminous water, the world starts to blur at the edges. The sound of a distant alarm clock begins to bleed into the dream, sounding like a rhythmic drum in the deep, and you realize you are drifting awake, carrying the warmth of that hand in yours even as the ocean fades to white.
