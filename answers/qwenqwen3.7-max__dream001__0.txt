You realize you aren't running on the ground anymore; you are running straight up the side of a brick wall, gravity having simply lost interest in you.

You reach a heavy oak door suspended in the vertical masonry, marked with a brass "14," and burst through it. 

Instead of a corporate boardroom, you find yourself in the endless, linoleum-floored hallway of your childhood elementary school. The metal lockers are gently expanding and contracting, as if breathing. You try to sprint toward the exit at the far end, but your legs suddenly feel like they are moving through wet concrete. The harder you push, the slower you go. A giant, ticking clock echoes through the corridor, but the sound is warped, stretching out like a dying heartbeat. *Tick... ... ... ... tock.*

Just as the anxiety peaks, a janitor pushing a mop bucket glides effortlessly past you. The bucket is filled with glowing, bioluminescent jellyfish. "You're late for the hatching," the janitor whispers.

In a flash of dream-logic clarity, the anxiety instantly vanishes, replaced by a profound, soaring sense of purpose. You remember now. The meeting isn't a business negotiation at all. You are supposed to be conducting a symphony for the ocean.

You reach into your pocket and pull out a conductor's baton made of carved driftwood. The school hallway dissolves into a vast, shallow sea reflecting a sky crowded with double moons. You step up onto a podium made of white coral, raise the baton, and as the first massive wave crashes in perfect time with your downbeat, you finally feel like you are exactly where you are supposed to be. 

The water rises to wash over you, turning the world to a brilliant, blinding white, and you open your eyes.
