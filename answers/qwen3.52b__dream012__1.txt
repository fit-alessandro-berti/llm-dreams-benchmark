The captain looks up through the mist. His crew is standing close, their hands gripping the railings like ropes of fear and hope. One by one, they look at you, and you see them not as shadows in the gloom, but as reflections of the water itself—waiting for a direction that isn't visible to anyone else on this vessel.

You realize with a sinking heart that the storm isn’t just outside; it’s inside your own chest, shaking the very air you breathe. You take a deep breath until the fog clears from your mind and you speak one word into the silence: *"Hold firm."*

The ship does not move. The water doesn't crash against the hull—it hums instead. A sudden, cold wind rises up off your face before you look down; it is not rain now. It's light that touches the deck like a soft hand. You see the crew turn in unison toward what will be your signal. You hold out your left arm, palm open, and with a single finger gesture, the lightning stops from the sky above, turning into a steady golden lantern hanging in the air between you and them.

It was not a miracle, but it made the difference between the ship sinking and drifting. The crew falls back in place as if they'd been standing before a great wall. They see the wind settle around your shoulder blades; you don't need to fight anymore because you've already won the battle of the seas. You look at their shoulders and feel something else for the first time—the weight of the moment. It wasn't about the water. It was about the promise of tomorrow.

And you realize, as the waves begin to rise gently behind them, you are still here to see what comes next.
