You are participating in a competition where the grand prize is your dream job. As the competition progresses, you face various challenges and discover that each one is designed to test not only your skills, but also your character.

The first challenge takes place in a vast library where the books rearrange themselves whenever you look away. You must find a single book containing the answer to a question you have never been asked. After hours of searching, you realize the answer is not in any book—it is written on the first page of your own notebook.

The second challenge sends you across a city suspended in the clouds. Bridges appear only when you take the next step, and the other competitors race ahead, certain that speed is the key to winning. You slow down instead, helping a frightened stranger cross the gap. When you reach the other side, you discover that the judges have been watching—not to see who arrives first, but who refuses to leave someone behind.

For the final challenge, you are led into a quiet office. Behind the desk sits your future self, older and exhausted. They offer you the job immediately, but warn you that accepting it will mean becoming someone you no longer recognize.

You look around the office. The walls are covered with awards, the windows overlook a beautiful city, and your name is already engraved on the door. Yet beneath the desk, you notice a small suitcase packed with everything you once loved.

Instead of signing the contract, you ask whether there is another way.

The room begins to dissolve. The judges remove their masks and reveal themselves as people from your past—teachers, friends, strangers, and versions of you who gave up too soon. They applaud, not because you won the competition, but because you finally understood the real prize.

When you wake, there is an envelope on your pillow. Inside is a job offer.

The title is exactly what you dreamed of.

But beneath it, in handwriting you recognize as your own, is a note:

**“Make sure it is still your dream.”**
