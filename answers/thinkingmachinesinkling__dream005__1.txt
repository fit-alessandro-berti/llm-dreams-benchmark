...and each cluster seems to be acting out a scene from a life you almost lived. One group is arguing passionately about the flavor of a color that doesn’t exist; they gesture with hands that leave trails of fog. Another circle is silently building a tower out of mismatched shoes, and they smile at you with teeth like piano keys. You call out your friend’s name, but it dissolves into the air as a flock of paper birds.

The room keeps changing its geometry—doorways become throat-like passages, ceilings lower to brush your hair with the warmth of a held breath. You spot a familiar coat, a familiar laugh, just over the shoulder of a woman who is planting seeds in a floor made of water. You reach out, but the crowd parts like a curtain and reforms behind you. Your friend is there, and then they are the reflection in a window that shows a different party, one where you are already sitting together, not searching at all.

You begin to understand: the person you know is not lost, but distributed—part of the lantern held by the old man, part of the melody hummed by the children sitting on invisible chairs. The gathering is not a place to navigate, but a feeling to sink into. As you stop running, the noise softens into a single, sustained note, and you wake not with an answer, but with the strange certainty that you have found them everywhere at once.
