as you move deeper into the maze, you find that the walls are no longer made of stone, but of shifting, translucent glass panels etched with riddles that whisper against the ear. The air grows heavy with the scent of ozone and old paper, and the logic that solved the first level begins to fracture.

A corridor stretches out before you, ending in a door made of pure white light. To open it, you must solve a mechanism that requires you to rearrange the order of your own memories to fit a specific lock. You reach out, not with your hand, but with your intent. The glass shimmers. You recall the taste of a childhood winter afternoon, the smell of rain on hot asphalt, the feeling of a secret kept for too long. As these memories slot into the sequence, the door clicks.

You step through, expecting a reward or an exit, but instead, you find yourself standing in an endless library where the books have no titles. In the center of the room, a mirror floats in mid-air. You approach it, expecting to see the maze behind you, or the world waking up.

But the reflection does not show your face. It shows your eyes, wide and staring, from a different perspective—looking *out* of the dream.

The voice that speaks isn't yours; it is the silence of the room itself. *"The maze was never to be left,"* it says. *"It was only to be understood."*

The floor beneath you dissolves into static. You are falling, but the sensation is weightless, like the moment right before sleep takes hold. The light from the library door fades, replaced by the soft, blurry ceiling of your own room.

The alarm clock buzzes. You reach out to silence it, your fingers grazing the wood of your nightstand. For a split second, you feel the cool texture of a puzzle piece in your palm, the faint scent of ozone.

Then, you sit up, the dream already slipping away like smoke. The logic of the real world rushes back in, sharp and demanding. You are awake. But as you stare at your hand, you wonder: is the maze in the house, or is the house the maze?

You are dreaming. But you aren't entirely sure anymore.
