You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, the walls begin to shift and rearrange themselves. The puzzles change too—what starts as a simple sliding tile problem morphs into a riddle whispered by the stone, then into a constellation you must align by pushing floating, luminous orbs. Your mind feels both brilliantly sharp and strangely fluid, as if thought itself is a physical thing you can mold.

Then, as you finally force open a gate of interlocking gears, the corridor doesn't just lead forward—it leads *inward*. You're no longer walking on stone but on solidified whispers. The puzzles stop demanding answers and begin showing you feelings. One puzzle is a locked door that only opens when you stop wanting to open it. Another is a pool of silver water that reflects not your face, but a version of you you've forgotten—full of quiet joy, before the hurry began.

With each solution, the maze doesn't shrink; it grows more beautiful. Walls bloom with intricate, living vines that form patterns you now realize are the blueprints for everything you love. The exit isn't a door, but a realization: you were never trying to solve the maze. You were trying to solve the feeling of being lost.

You take one more step, not forward, but *down* into the now-familiar ground, and it gives way like a sigh. You are not falling, but settling into a soft, knowing darkness—the kind that holds all answers without needing to speak them. And just before you wake, you understand that the maze was never outside you. It was the beautiful, tangled map of your own mind, and you had the key all along.

You open your eyes to morning light, the complex geometry of the dream fading, but leaving behind a lingering sense of clarity and peace.
