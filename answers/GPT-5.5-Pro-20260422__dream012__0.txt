“Reef the sails!” you shout, though the wind steals half your voice and throws it into the black water.

The crew moves at once, lanterns swinging wildly, faces flashing in and out of rain and shadow. Above you, the clouds twist into a great spiral, and for a moment you see something impossible at its center: a pale doorway, glowing behind the storm.

The sea rises.

A wave taller than any mast rolls toward you, its crest burning with cold blue light. The crew freezes. Even the thunder seems to pause.

Then you understand.

The storm is not trying to sink you. It is trying to wake you.

You turn the ship straight toward the wave.

“Hold fast!” you cry.

The bow climbs the wall of water. The world tilts upward. Stars appear beneath the sea, glittering like coins scattered across a midnight floor. The ship breaks through the crest, not into air, but into silence.

The rain stops.

Your crew is gone.

The deck is dry.

You stand alone at the helm, floating on a calm black ocean beneath a sky filled with two moons. Ahead, the glowing doorway hangs just above the horizon, opening slowly, warmly, as if it has been waiting for you all along.

You take one step toward it.

And from somewhere behind you, a familiar voice whispers:

“Captain, we’re not lost anymore.”

You turn—

and wake with the taste of salt on your lips.
