You spin the wheel hard to starboard—not to fight the storm, but to *ride* it. The ship heels violently, masts creaking like old bones, and suddenly you're surfing down the face of a black mountain of water. The crew starts cheering, half-mad, half-awake.

Then the sea folds you gently into its arms, and you fall upward—through clouds, through stars, through the bottom of your own dream—until you wake up laughing in your bed, soaked in sweat, with the distinct taste of salt on your lips.

And somewhere, far away, the storm is still waiting for you to captain it again.
