You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, you notice the walls are no longer stone but living equations that shift and breathe with your thoughts. The air grows heavy, thick with the scent of ozone and old paper, and your footsteps echo in ways that suggest the maze is listening.

A new puzzle materializes in front of you: a floating sphere of interlocking rings, each engraved with paradoxes. "If a dream solves itself, was it ever a problem?" it asks. You focus, tracing the rings with your mind rather than your hands, and the sphere collapses into a single silver key that feels warm in your palm. The path ahead splits into three corridors: one lined with mirrors that show you solving puzzles from your real life, one that loops back on itself no matter which way you turn, and one that simply whispers your name in voices you almost recognize.

You choose the whispering path. The deeper you go, the more the puzzles stop testing logic and start testing memory—fragments of conversations you never had, places you've never been, versions of yourself that made different choices. Your concentration sharpens until it feels like a blade. The walls begin to dissolve at the edges, revealing glimpses of a vast, starless sky outside the maze. Yet the puzzles grow harder, more personal.

At the heart of it all, you find no final room or exit, only a single blank pedestal and a question written in your own handwriting: "What will you remember when you wake up?"

The dream holds its breath, waiting.
