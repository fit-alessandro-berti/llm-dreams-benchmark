...and you feel a strange, impossible calm settle over you, as if the dream itself is whispering that none of this is real.

The wind howls, the ship groans, and your crew’s terrified faces are lit by jagged flashes of lightning. But instead of fear, a wild laugh rises in your chest. You throw your head back, rain lashing your face, and bellow:

“Listen up, you sorry bunch of barnacles! This isn’t a storm… it’s a *tantrum.* And I’ve had enough!”

You wrench the wheel hard, not away from the waves, but *straight into* the heart of the maelstrom. The ship tilts violently. Someone screams. The deck becomes a waterfall.

And then the sea simply… stops.

The waves freeze mid-crash like a painting. Raindrops hang in the air, sparkling. Your first mate’s mouth is open in a perfect O, a droplet balanced on his tongue.

A low, ancient voice rumbles from beneath the hull, vibrating through your boots:

*“Finally. Someone with style.”*

The ocean parts like theater curtains. A colossal kraken rises, but instead of tentacles of terror, it’s wearing an enormous, slightly crooked captain’s hat exactly like yours. It looks at you with one enormous, amused eye.

“Took you long enough to stop pretending to be scared,” the kraken says in a voice like whales singing through a trombone. “Want to ditch these amateurs and go terrorize some actual gods?”

You grin, the dream now fully in your control.

You turn to your frozen crew, tip your hat, and say:

“Lads… I’m afraid I’m needed elsewhere. Try not to sink without me.”

Then you leap from the deck, land lightly on the kraken’s massive head, and the two of you descend into the glowing abyss as the storm, the ship, and the dream itself dissolve into glittering foam and laughter.

You wake up laughing, salt still somehow on your lips.
