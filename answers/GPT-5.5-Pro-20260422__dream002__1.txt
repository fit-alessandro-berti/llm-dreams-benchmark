You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the air turns electric, humming against your skin. The leaves above you twist and flash silver as the first drops of rain fall—not downward, but upward, rising from the moss like tiny stars returning to the sky.

You hurry along the path, but the path begins to move beneath your feet, curling like a ribbon through the trees. Ahead, a warm golden light glows between two ancient oaks. As thunder rolls overhead, you step toward it and find a small door carved into the side of a tree, its handle shaped like a sleeping bird.

When you touch it, the storm goes silent.

The door opens onto a room that should not fit inside the tree: a cozy library with walls of roots, shelves of glowing books, and a fire burning blue in a stone hearth. An old fox wearing round spectacles looks up from a desk and says, “You arrived just in time. The storm is looking for something you forgot.”

Before you can ask what, a gust of wind bursts through the door, scattering pages across the room. On every page is a memory: a laugh you haven’t heard in years, a place you almost remember, a promise you made to yourself and misplaced somewhere along the way.

You gather the pages one by one. With each page you touch, the storm outside grows softer. The fox nods approvingly and hands you a blank book.

“This one,” it says, “is for what comes next.”

You open the book, and the first sentence writes itself in shimmering ink:

“When I woke, I carried the forest with me.”

Then the rain stops, the fire becomes morning sunlight, and you open your eyes feeling strangely calm, as though some quiet part of you has finally found its way home.
