...and you stand tall at the wheel, voice booming over the roar of wind and waves: "Hold fast, lads! This isn't just any storm—it's the one from *last* Tuesday's dream!" 

The crew blinks, confused, because of course they do. Lightning cracks the sky open like an egg, and instead of rain, thousands of tiny glowing fish pour down, each one whispering forgotten phone numbers and the lyrics to songs you haven't heard since childhood. Your first mate, whose face keeps flickering between your old high school gym teacher and a walrus, grabs your sleeve.

"Captain... the map is singing."

You look down. The nautical chart has turned into a living thing, its ink rivers flowing upward, forming the face of your mother who tells you the sea is just your own bathtub and you've been in here too long. The ship tilts violently. For one crystalline moment you understand everything: the ocean is made of every time you almost said "I love you" but didn't. The thunder is your own heartbeat wearing boots.

You spin the wheel hard—not to fight the storm, but to *steer into* the part of the dream where the rules haven't been written yet. The ship groans, then sighs, then becomes a giant paper boat. The crew dissolves into seagulls made of old love letters. The turbulent sea flattens into a mirror, and you're standing on your own reflection, barefoot.

In the distance, a lighthouse made of birthday candles flickers. You know if you reach it, you'll wake up.

You start walking across the water anyway, smiling, because some dreams are better when you refuse to be rescued. 

The alarm clock is already ringing somewhere above the clouds... but not yet. Not yet.
