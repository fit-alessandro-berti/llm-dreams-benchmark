You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, you begin to notice that the walls are listening.

Whenever you doubt yourself, the corridors narrow. Whenever you trust your instincts, hidden doors appear.

At the maze’s center, you find a final puzzle: a mirror with a single question etched across its surface:

**“What are you trying to escape?”**

You search for patterns, clues, and secret mechanisms, but there are none. At last, you place your hand against the glass and answer, “The fear that I won’t find the way.”

The mirror dissolves beneath your fingers.

Beyond it lies no exit, only an open sky filled with slow-moving stars. The maze folds itself into a tiny silver key in your palm, and you realize that you were never trapped inside it. You were building it around yourself.

Then the stars begin to blink like waking eyes, and somewhere far away, an alarm clock rings.
