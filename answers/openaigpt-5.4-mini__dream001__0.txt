…start to hurry through streets that keep changing shape as you look at them. A tram glides past with no driver, its windows full of people you almost recognize, all turning to watch you with the calm seriousness of librarians.

You check your watch. The hands spin, pause, and then point somewhere behind you.

So you turn and find a narrow alley that wasn’t there a moment ago, lit by warm yellow lanterns. At the end of it stands a small café with a sign that says **“For Arrivals Only.”**

Inside, the air smells like rain on stone and fresh paper. The room is empty except for one person sitting at a corner table. They don’t look up, but they slide a folded note toward you.

It reads: **You are not late. The meeting is where you decide it is.**

You feel oddly relieved, though you can’t explain why. Outside, the city hums like a distant engine starting. The streets are waiting. The hour is still moving, but now it feels less like a deadline and more like an invitation.

You step back into the city, and this time you know exactly where to go.
