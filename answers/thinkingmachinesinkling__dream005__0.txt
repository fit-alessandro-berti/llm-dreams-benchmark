...and each group seems to be performing a different version of a conversation you almost remember.

The first cluster stands near a fountain that flows upward, their laughter synchronized like a choir but slightly out of phase with their lips. They greet you by a name that isn’t yours, yet sounds like it should be, and offer you a drink in a glass that has no bottom. You smile and move past them, feeling the weight of their eyes follow you—not with malice, but with the gentle insistence of people waiting for you to remember a promise you never made.

Further in, a second group sits in a circle of armchairs arranged on a ceiling that has become the floor. They are playing a card game using images instead of suits: a door that breathes, a clock melted into a tree, your own face looking back with an expression of calm you don’t feel. One of them gestures for you to sit, but the chair is filled with water that holds its shape like silk. You decline, and they nod as if this was the correct answer all along, returning to their silent dealing.

Then you see them—the one person you know—standing at the far end of a hallway that stretches and contracts like a lung. You call out, but your voice comes out as the sound of rain on glass. They turn, and for a moment you are certain they see you, but then the hallway branches into three, then seven, then none at all, leaving only a mirror in their place.

You reach out to touch it, and your hand passes through cool silver into another room entirely: the same gathering, but older, or perhaps younger, with lanterns made of paper stars hanging where the ceiling should be. Your friend is there too, closer now, holding a lantern that casts no shadow. You realize, with the peculiar certainty of dreams, that you have been searching for them in every room at once, and that they have been leaving breadcrumbs made of familiar smells—your grandmother’s soap, the ozone before a storm, the particular silence of a shared secret.

As you finally draw near, the crowd parts not with effort but with the ease of water around a stone. You don’t speak. You don’t need to. The dream holds you both in a quiet that feels like coming home to a house you’ve never visited, and somewhere in the distance, the party continues—beautiful, endless, and entirely made of the feeling that you are, for now, exactly where you need to be.
