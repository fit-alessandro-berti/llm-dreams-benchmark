"Have you seen them?" I ask, my voice sounding muffled, as if I'm speaking underwater. "The one person I know here?"

Before I can ask what he means, the crowd surges. A wave of laughter washes over the room, and the social gathering suddenly morphs into a bustling open-air market under a violet twilight sky. The noise is overwhelming—a symphony of clinking glasses, distant violins, and whispered secrets.

Then, across a bridge made of woven willow branches, I see a flash of a familiar coat. It’s you. You are standing near a merchant selling jars of blue light, looking around with the same quiet desperation I feel.

You smile, a look of profound relief washing over your face, and raise a hand to wave.
