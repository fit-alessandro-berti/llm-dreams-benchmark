You encounter various groups of people and notice that their voices have all turned into a single language from your childhood, but you can't understand it. You try to join any conversation, hoping for a glimmer of recognition, but everyone simply turns to face you with blank walls where faces used to be. 

Then, amidst the silence, you see them. The one person stands in the corner where the wall seems to breathe. They are waiting for your arrival like the sun waits for morning, unafraid that you won't come. You walk toward them, but as you near, they begin to fade. In their place, a mirror appears, reflecting not a stranger you met today, but every single version of yourself from years past. The question of who you are becomes less important than why you needed to be found in someone else at all. 

And then it ends, or perhaps it never really began, and the party dissolves into soft light that smells like burnt sugar and ozone.
