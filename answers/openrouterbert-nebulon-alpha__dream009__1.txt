You wake up on a bed of straw, the air thick with the scent of woodsmoke and damp earth. The room is dim, lit only by a flickering tallow candle, its flame casting long, wavering shadows on rough-hewn wooden walls. Outside, the distant clatter of hooves and the murmur of voices in a language you *almost* understand drift through the open window. The year? You have no idea—but the clothes on your body (a coarse linen tunic, leather boots, a cloak that smells faintly of wool and rain) suggest something pre-industrial. Maybe medieval Europe? Feudal Japan? A village in the Iron Age?

You step outside, barefoot on packed dirt, and the world unfolds like a living painting. A marketplace bustles with merchants hawking spices, woven baskets, and strange, glinting tools. Children chase each other with sticks, their laughter high and bright. A blacksmith’s hammer rings against an anvil, and the air hums with the lowing of cattle. No cars. No phones. No hum of electricity. Just *life*, raw and immediate.

But you’re not just a spectator—you’re *here*, and you need to survive.

### **First Priority: Food and Water**
Your stomach growls. You don’t recognize the coins in your pocket (if you even have any), and bartering seems risky when you don’t know the value of anything. Do you:
- **Steal an apple from a vendor’s cart?** (Risky—what if they catch you?)
- **Offer to work for a meal?** (But what skills do you have that they’d value?)
- **Follow the smell of baking bread to a tavern and hope for charity?** (Or will they demand payment in kind?)

### **Second Priority: Shelter**
Night is coming, and the air already carries a chill. Do you:
- **Find a barn or stable to sleep in?** (Animals = warmth, but also fleas and the risk of being discovered.)
- **Ask for lodging at a monastery or temple?** (If this is a religious era, they might take you in—or see you as a demon.)
- **Build a makeshift shelter in the woods?** (But what if wolves or bandits come?)

### **Third Priority: Information**
You need to figure out *where* (and *when*) you are. Do you:
- **Listen to gossip in the marketplace?** (Eavesdropping might reveal the local lord’s name, the year, or rumors of war.)
- **Find a scribe or scholar?** (If you can read, maybe a book or map will help. If not… well, that’s a problem.)
- **Follow a group of travelers?** (Merchants, pilgrims, or soldiers might know the land—but can you trust them?)

### **The Surreal Twist**
Just as you’re settling into this new reality, something *off* happens:
- The sky darkens unnaturally, as if the sun is being swallowed. The villagers whisper of omens.
- A stranger in the crowd locks eyes with you—and *recognizes* you, though you’ve never seen them before.
- You find a scrap of paper in your pocket with a single word written in your own handwriting: *"Wake up."*

Do you:
- **Ignore it and keep adapting?** (Maybe this is your new life now.)
- **Follow the clue?** (But how? And what if it leads you somewhere worse?)
- **Panic and run?** (Where? And what if you’re running *toward* danger?)

### **How Would You Like the Dream to End?**
Dreams like this can go in so many directions. Here are a few possibilities—pick one, or suggest your own:

1. **The Time-Slip Escape:** You find a hidden artifact (a pocket watch, a strange coin, a mirror) that glows when you touch it. The moment you do, the world *ripples*—and you wake up back in your bed, gasping, the taste of smoke still in your mouth.

2. **The New Life:** You adapt. You learn the language, the skills, the customs. Years pass. You marry, have children, grow old. One day, you’re telling your grandchild a story about "the strange dream you had once, where you were from a world of metal birds and talking boxes." They laugh and call you a liar.

3. **The Dark Turn:** You realize you’re not just in the past—you’re in *someone else’s* past. The body you’re in isn’t yours. The memories you’re forming aren’t yours. And the real owner of this life is *coming back*.

4. **The Loop:** You wake up in the same era again and again, each time with a new clue. A name. A date. A location. You start to suspect this isn’t a dream at all—but a test, or a punishment, or a message from someone (or something) trying to reach you.

5. **The Cosmic Joke:** You stumble upon a group of "time travelers" who laugh when they see you. "Oh, another one," they say. "Happens every few decades. Don’t worry—you’ll wake up eventually." Then they offer you a drink and ask if you’ve seen any "interesting tech" in your time.
