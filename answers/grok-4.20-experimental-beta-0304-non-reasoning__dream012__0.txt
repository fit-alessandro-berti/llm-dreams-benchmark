You stand at the helm, rain lashing your face like cold needles, the ship groaning and bucking beneath you as though it were a living thing fighting for its life. Lightning splits the sky, illuminating the terrified faces of your crew—some clutching the rails, others already beginning to pray.

The wheel fights you, trying to wrench itself from your grip, but you plant your boots wider and roar over the thunder:

“ALL HANDS! Reef the topsails! Bosun, get those men on the lines! We’re not dying tonight!”

A monstrous wave rises ahead of you, black and glassy, taller than the masts. For a heartbeat the entire world seems to hold its breath.

Then you laugh.

A wild, mad, joyous sound that cuts through the storm like a blade. The crew stares at you as though you’ve gone insane.

You grin, teeth flashing in the lightning.

“Hard to starboard! We’re not running from this bastard… we’re going to ride its back!”

You spin the wheel with every ounce of strength you possess. The ship heels so violently that half the crew is thrown to the deck, but the bow swings, slicing into the face of the monstrous wave instead of letting it broadside you.

The world tilts.

For one impossible moment the ship climbs the wave like a stallion taking a hill, water roaring past both sides in white walls. You feel the deck surge upward, upward, until you’re staring straight into the black heart of the storm itself.

And in that frozen instant you see something no one else does:

A single star, burning silver through a tear in the clouds directly above you.

You point your cutlass at it and bellow:

“THERE! THAT’S OUR WAY! FOLLOW THAT LIGHT!”

The ship crests the wave.

For a heartbeat you are weightless, flying.

Then you plunge down the other side with a sound like the end of the world, spray exploding over the bow in a white crown. The hull shudders but holds.

The crew, soaked and half-drowned, begins to cheer.

You look back at them, eyes blazing, and shout the order that will either save you all or doom you:

“Set the foresail! We’re running before the wind… straight into the storm’s throat! Let it think it’s swallowing us… we’ll punch through its guts and come out the other side singing!”

The ship surges forward like a beast finally given its head.

And somewhere deep in your chest, you feel it: this isn’t just a storm.

This is the moment the sea decides whether you are its master… or its meal.

You smile into the howling wind.

The dream is yours now, sailor.

What do you do next, Captain?
