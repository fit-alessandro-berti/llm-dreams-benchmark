**You are participating in a competition where the grand prize is your dream job. As the competition progresses, you face various challenges that test your creativity, resilience, and intuition.**  

---

### **Challenge 1: The Puzzle Box Chamber**  
The first round drops you into a dimly lit room filled with dozens of locked puzzle boxes, each glowing with a different color. A holographic voice booms: *“Solve the boxes in the order of your deepest fears, and the key to the next stage will be yours.”*  
You realize the boxes correspond to memories you’ve buried—your first public failure, a betrayal by a mentor, the moment you almost gave up. As you open each box, they release fragments of light that assemble into a map. But one box remains unlit: a black one labeled *“The Truth You’ve Refused to Face.”* Do you confront it first, risking panic, or follow the map’s safer path?  

---

### **Challenge 2: The Labyrinth of Mirrors**  
Next, you enter a maze of mirrors that reflect not your face, but your *competition rivals’*—each one showing a version of yourself who chose different paths. One mirror shows you as a corporate drone; another, a reclusive artist. A timer ticks down: *“Find your true reflection before the walls close in.”*  
You must choose which “self” to embrace or reject. A hidden ally—a mysterious contestant who’s been watching—whispers, *“Don’t chase perfection. Chase purpose.”* But is this person helping or manipulating you?  

---

### **Challenge 3: The Final Gauntlet**  
The last challenge is a physical and mental test. You’re strapped into a chair suspended above a pit of quicksand, with a single lever in front of you. To your left: a path to safety, but it requires you to betray an ally. To your right: a risky climb that could save everyone, but your fear of heights might paralyze you.  
As you hesitate, the voice returns: *“Jobs aren’t won by the strongest. They’re won by those who redefine what strength means.”*  

---

### **The Twist**  
Just as you reach for the lever, the “contestant” who helped you earlier reveals themselves: it’s your future self, sent back to guide you. They explain that the competition isn’t about winning—it’s a test of whether you’re willing to grow into the person the job demands. The real prize isn’t a title or a paycheck, but the courage to become who you’re meant to be.  

Do you trust them? Do you take the risk?  

---  
**What do you do next?**  
- **A)** Pull the lever to betray your ally, securing safety.  
- **B)** Climb the risky path, trusting your instincts.  
- **C)** Refuse to choose, demanding to know what the job *really* requires.  

Your dream job awaits—but first, you must answer the question: *Who are you, when no one is watching?*
