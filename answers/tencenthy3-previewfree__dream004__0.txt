You reach out and touch the surface, expecting cold paint or rough fabric, but it feels like warm, vibrating water. The moment your fingertip makes contact, the shifting chaos of the world around you slows down. The screaming neon pinks and twisting geometric spirals freeze in mid-air, waiting.

As you pull your hand back, a trail of liquid silver follows, dripping onto the blank surface. Where the silver drops land, they don't stain; they expand. They begin to absorb the colors of the dream around you—the violet of a passing cloud, the gold of a distant star—and stabilize them.

You realize the canvas isn't blank because it lacks substance; it is blank because it is a *reset button*. It is the anchor of the dream.

Suddenly, a gentle breeze blows through the landscape, carrying with it the faint sound of a familiar melody. You look down at the canvas and see that the silver pool is beginning to swirl, forming a shape you recognize—a doorway. The edges of the canvas begin to peel away, not like paper, but like a curtain being drawn back, revealing a quiet, static meadow on the other side where the colors remain constant and the shapes are finally, blessedly, still.
