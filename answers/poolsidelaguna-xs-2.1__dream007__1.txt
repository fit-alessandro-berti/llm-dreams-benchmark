...walk away, needing time to cool down. I head toward the old forest path that cuts behind our childhood home—a place I haven’t visited in years, not since the accident. The trees seem closer here, their branches weaving overhead like cathedral ribs, and I notice my footsteps aren’t making a sound on the moss. When I glance back, the path I came from is already fading, the argument dissolving into a low hum. I reach the old oak where we used to carve our names, but instead of bark, my reflection stares back from the trunk—*our* reflection, the one who was never quite there. The friend I was arguing with steps out of the woodwork, holding a photo I don’t recognize, and says, *“You already chose this path. You just forgot.”*
