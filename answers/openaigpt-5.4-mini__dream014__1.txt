Another stranger points to the skyline, where the towers are rearranging themselves like puzzle pieces, opening and closing to reveal hidden paths of light.

You follow them through a marketplace filled with lanterns, music, and the smell of rain on warm stone. Every corner seems unfamiliar, yet somehow reassuring, as if the city itself is remembering you. The strangers don’t speak much; they only guide you gently, one turning back now and then to make sure you’re still there.

At last, they lead you to a quiet square with a fountain at its center. In the water, you see not your reflection, but the faces of people you’ve loved, people you’ve lost, and versions of yourself from other times. The strangers step back, and one of them says, “You were never truly alone. You just forgot the way home.”

Then the fountain glows softly, the city lights dim into stars, and you wake up with the strange, comforting feeling that somewhere, even now, the way is being held open for you.
