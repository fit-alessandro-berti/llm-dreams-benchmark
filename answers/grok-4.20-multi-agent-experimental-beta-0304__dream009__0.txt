You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to blend in immediately before the locals brand you a sorcerer or spy. The air carries the sharp tang of woodsmoke, baking bread, and horse manure. Thatched-roof cottages line muddy lanes, and people in rough wool tunics and leather boots stare openly at your sneakers and graphic tee.

A blacksmith pauses mid-hammer to gawk, while children point and whisper. Your heart pounds with that classic dream illogic—you can somehow understand their archaic speech, yet every step feels both urgent and weightless. "Stranger," an old woman calls, offering a hunk of warm bread with a wary but kind smile, "ye look half-drowned in time itself. The oracle in the whispering woods might know why the stars spat ye here."

You take the bread, and the moment you bite down, the scenery ripples like water. The medieval village flickers into glimpses of ancient Rome, then feudal Japan, then something stranger still—floating lanterns and impossible beasts—before snapping back. A glowing rune on your wrist pulses: *Solve the riddle of the era or remain forever between them.*

Do you follow the woman into the woods, try to "think" yourself a sword with dream powers, or just run until the dream fractures and you jolt awake in your actual bed? The choice feels heavy, yet you already know how dreams like this end... or do they?
