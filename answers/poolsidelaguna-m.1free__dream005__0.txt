
**the dream continues...**  

The room hums with the low thrum of overlapping conversations, cocktail glasses clinking like wind chimes in a storm. You weave through clusters of guests, their faces blurring into a mosaic of half-remembered smiles and strangers’ voices. A group near the marble fireplace is laughing about something you can’t hear—until you realize they’re all wearing identical silver masks. Their eyes, when they notice you, glint with recognition. *Do they know you? Or are you just another reflection in their mirrored accessories?*  

Further on, a circle of dancers sways to a song that hasn’t started playing yet. Their movements are fluid, eerily synchronized, as if they’re all mimicking the same invisible rhythm. One catches your eye—a figure in a red dress—and for a heartbeat, you think it’s the person you’re searching for. But when you step closer, they’ve vanished, leaving only a trail of rose petals that dissolve into smoke.  

Near the terrace doors, a cluster of people sits in silence, sipping neon-colored drinks. Their whispers rise into a cacophony: *"They’re waiting for you," "You’re late," "Don’t look back."* You spin around, but the voice isn’t among them. It’s coming from the grand piano in the corner, where a child is playing a melody that twists into a laugh you recognize—your own, but distorted, echoing from the first time you ever felt alone.  

When you finally spot *them*—your person—at the top of the staircase, their silhouette framed by moonlight, they’re already fading into the wallpaper. The crowd below begins to clap, but their hands are covered in ink, dragging quills along the air like conductors. You wake up in a hotel ballroom bed, the sheets tangled with confetti and a name tag that reads *"Player Two."*  

The dream shifts. Or did it ever end?
