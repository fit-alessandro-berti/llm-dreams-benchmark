You start to move, your footsteps echoing against the slick cobblestones that seem to pulse with a faint, iridescent glow. The city sprawls in every direction, a living tapestry of towering glass spires and cramped alleyways that wind like the veins of some enormous organism. A thin veil of mist drifts between the buildings, carrying the scent of rain‑kissed steel and something sweeter—perhaps jasmine, perhaps the memory of a perfume you’ve never worn.

Your watch reads 12:47. The meeting is at 13:45. In the waking world that would give you plenty of time, but here, in this dream‑city, time feels elastic, stretching and contracting with each breath. You feel a knot tighten in your chest as you realize you have no idea where you are supposed to be.

A massive billboard flickers to life above a bustling plaza, its neon letters spelling out a phrase in a language you can’t read, yet somehow you understand it: *“Find the silver door, and the path will reveal itself.”* The billboard’s light ripples across the crowd, turning faces into silhouettes, each person moving with a purpose you cannot decipher.

You approach a woman sitting on a bench, her coat a patchwork of maps from different eras—old parchment, modern GPS schematics, hand‑drawn sketches of constellations. She looks up, her eyes reflecting the city’s glimmering lights like tiny mirrors.

“Do you know where the meeting is?” you ask, the words spilling out in a breathless rush.

She smiles, a smile that seems to stretch across centuries. “Every meeting is a crossroads,” she says, and pulls a folded piece of paper from her coat pocket. She hands it to you. The paper is warm, as if it’s just been taken from a pocket of sunlight. You open it and see a single line drawn in silver ink: a winding path that starts at the bench, loops around a fountain shaped like a colossal hourglass, and ends at a door with a silver frame.

“Follow it,” she whispers, “but be aware: the city listens to your intentions. If you truly want to find it, the streets will make way.”

You thank her and step forward. The paper’s path seems to shimmer, guiding you through a series of narrow lanes where the walls are covered in graffiti that moves, shifting from abstract swirls to a map of constellations that pulse in rhythm with your heartbeat. You pass a café where the tables are made of old book spines, their titles unreadable but humming with a low, resonant note. A barista with a beard made of swirling smoke offers you a cup of dark coffee that smells like night itself.

As you walk, the hourglass fountain in the center of the plaza begins to spin, its sand falling upward, defying gravity. You glance at your watch again—13:02. The minute hand seems to have frozen, and the second hand now ticks in slow motion, each tick echoing like a distant drumbeat.

You follow the silver line, which now glows brighter, lighting the path ahead. The city begins to shift: streets flatten into smooth marble, then rise again as steep stairs that lead to a balcony overlooking a river that flows upward, its water a liquid mirror reflecting a sky of violet and gold.

At the end of the line, a door stands—its silver frame encrusted with tiny, rotating gears that click in perfect synchrony. A soft chime rings as you place your hand on the handle. The door opens, revealing a vast atrium bathed in warm amber light. In the center, a circular table is set with a single empty chair. Around it, dozens of other chairs are filled with silhouettes—people you’ve never met but somehow recognize, each one representing a fragment of yourself, a part of your life you’ve left behind or forgotten.

You step forward, feeling a sudden calm wash over you. The hour that seemed impossible now stretches before you like an endless horizon. The meeting isn’t about a place or a time; it’s a gathering of all the selves you have yet to meet, waiting for the moment you finally arrive—exactly when you need to.

The clock on the wall chimes, a deep, resonant tone that reverberates through the atrium. You sit down in the empty chair, and the silhouettes turn toward you, their eyes flickering like distant stars. In that instant, the city outside fades, and you realize the meeting has already begun: you’ve been talking to yourself all along, navigating the unknown, learning to trust the silver path that appears when you need it most.

You wake just as the final chime fades, the taste of night‑kissed coffee lingering on your tongue, and the faint feeling that the dream‑city is still out there, waiting for you to return whenever you’re ready to explore another hidden door.
