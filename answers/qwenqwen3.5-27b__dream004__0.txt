The air around you ripples with neon violets and liquid gold, gravity shifting every few seconds so you are sometimes walking on the ceiling, sometimes floating in a sea of geometric dust. But the canvas stands on an easel of woven starlight, anchored in a pocket of absolute silence.

You reach out to touch it. Your fingers don't feel cotton or linen; they feel like cool water. As your fingertips graze the surface, the white expanse ripples like a pond disturbed by a stone.

Suddenly, the chaos of the world around you slows down. The swirling colors begin to bleed *into* the canvas instead of spinning around you. You realize there is no brush in your hand, yet you are painting. Every memory you have ever held—the smell of rain on hot asphalt, the sound of a childhood lullaby, the sharp sting of a scraped knee—manifests as pigment on the white surface.

But the painting doesn't look like your memories. It looks like *you*.

Not your face, but the shape of your presence. A silhouette made of constellations and shadows. As the image completes itself, the world around you begins to dissolve into the frame. The shifting shapes, the impossible architecture, the singing colors—they all fold inward, becoming part of the artwork.

You stand before the finished painting, and the canvas speaks with your own voice, but it sounds like a chorus.

*"You are not the dreamer,"* it whispers, the sound vibrating in your chest. *"You are the medium."*

The easel tips over. You fall, but instead of hitting the ground, you fall *into* the canvas. The white light swallows you, and for a moment, there is only the sensation of being a single brushstroke in a much larger picture. Then, the light snaps, the room tilts, and you open your eyes to the morning sun, your hand still hovering over the sheets, stained with a faint, impossible shade of violet.
