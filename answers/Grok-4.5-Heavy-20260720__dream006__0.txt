You are about to give a speech to a large audience. As you step onto the stage, you see the crowd looking expectantly at you, and you begin to speak.  

But instead of ordinary words, a soft aurora of living light, star maps, and half-formed equations spills from your mouth, drifting out over the seats like luminous fireflies. The audience gasps as gravity politely excuses itself; everyone rises weightlessly, reaching up to catch the glowing fragments. Each person who does suddenly understands a little more of the universe.  

For a moment the auditorium dissolves into deep space, and the speech is no longer yours at all — the cosmos is simply remembering itself through all of you. Then the podium quietly transforms into a small, friendly spaceship, and you smile, knowing you still have to stick the landing.
