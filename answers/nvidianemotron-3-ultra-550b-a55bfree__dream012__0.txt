"Steady, Captain. Steady."

First Mate Anders' voice cuts through the wind, but his eyes betray him. You've sailed together twenty years. You've never seen fear carve lines that deep.

The compass spins lazy circles, useless. The stars vanished hours ago, swallowed by the same bruised purple clouds that hang so low you could touch them if you climbed the mast. Which you won't. The mast might not be there come morning.

A wave breaks over the bow, green and heavy as a falling mountain. You taste it — cold, metallic, *old*. This water remembers things. Shipwrecks. Drowned cities. Names whispered by men who never made it home.

Your hands know what to do. They always do. Reef the mainsail. Hard to port. Ride the trough between swells like a knife blade splitting silk. The ship shudders, rights itself, groans again in relief.

Anders exhales. The crew, clustered near the companionway, lets out a collective breath you feel in your own chest.

Then the lightning comes — not from above, but *below*. Pale green light blooming from the ocean's depth, illuminating something vast and pale rising beneath the keel.

The water goes flat. Silent. The storm holds its breath.

You lean over the rail, staring down into light that has no source, watching something ancient wake up in the dark beneath your ship.

And you realize the storm was never the threat.

It was the *distraction*.
