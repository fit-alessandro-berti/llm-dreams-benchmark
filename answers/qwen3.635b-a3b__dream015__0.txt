The moment the thought takes hold, the graying fog ahead ripples like water disturbed by a stone. The wind, which usually howled with the voices of forgotten alarms, suddenly hums a low, cello-like note. You reach through your tattered coat, fingers brushing cold skin, and pull free the seed. It isn't hard or dry; it's warm, pulsing with a rhythm that matches the beat you hear in your throat, and it glows with a color that has no name, somewhere between the silver of moonlight and the gold of laughter.

The world resists. The ground is a mosaic of shattered glass and rusted rebar, sharp enough to slice hope. A pack of *Skitter-Things* detaches itself from the skeleton of a collapsed Overpass, their many limbs clacking against the metal, their eyes burning with a red hunger that speaks of endless nights. They are the danger, the scarcity made flesh. They slink forward, testing the air.

But you are dreaming. In the dream, fear is just a texture you can change. You decide to turn your fear into mist. It slips from your lips, drifting to the ground, and where it touches the shards, the glass softens into smooth, singing crystal.

You kneel. The ash rises to meet your knees, cool and velvet-soft. You press the glowing seed into the earth.

The Skitter-Things screech as they approach, but as they step onto the crystal paths, their red eyes widen. Their forms shudder, stretching, lightening, until they are no longer monsters but swarms of paper cranes, origami of impossible geometry, carrying whispers of the past in their wings. They circle you, folding and unfolding, and their wings whisper names of flowers, names of days, names of people you miss.

The resources of this place are no longer scarce. The riverbeds, once dry canyons, fill with liquid mercury that flows uphill, carrying silt of diamonds and moss of living sound. You cup your hands and drink; the water tastes of cool watermelon and the feeling of waking up early on a Saturday, safe and full of time.

At the center of this blooming miracle, where the starlight vine converges, a structure rises. It is a door, freestanding against the sky, with no walls to hold it. It is made of wood that breathes, painted blue like the horizon of a memory that feels too perfect to be real. A sign hangs from the knob, scrawled in handwriting that shifts and changes until it becomes your own: *FOR THOSE WHO RETURN.*

You do not hesitate. In a dream, the heart is a compass that points only forward. You step through the starlight, the paper cranes parting like ribbons of wind. You grasp the breathing wood. The knob turns; you feel the warmth of another hand, large and familiar, turning it from the other side.

The door swings wide.

There is no hallway, no room. There is only an expanse of gentle gravity, and floating down from the infinite blue above are leaves of parchment. Each one catches the light and reveals an image: a forest, a city that never burned, a garden where the fruit never rots, a room where the table is always set and the tea is never cold.

You realize then that you are not planting a garden to survive; you are planting a garden to remember what the world was, and to prove that what it *can* be. The apocalypse is just a shadow cast by the light of this new creation, and shadows have no weight when the sun rises.

You spread your arms, and your body begins to dissolve into the same starlight that grows the vines. You become the wind in the iron lace; you become the taste in the mercury; you become the paper crane carrying the name of a lullaby. You are everywhere this dream reaches, and the dream reaches everywhere.

The sensation of falling returns, not the falling of the end, but the falling of ascent, pulling you upward through the ceiling of the clouds, through the blue, through the bright, thin membrane of sleep...

You wake.

The room is gray, morning light filtering through real blinds. Your heart is drumming a slow, steady rhythm against your ribs. You lie still for a long moment, the ghost of starlight lingering in your peripheral vision. Then, you open your hand.

Resting in your palm, stark and impossible against your skin, is a single petal of metal that has turned to velvet, glowing with a faint, warm pulse, and when you touch it, you can hear, just for a second, the sound of paper wings folding.

You smile, clutch it to your chest, and begin your day, knowing that the world is just waiting for you to dream it back to life.
