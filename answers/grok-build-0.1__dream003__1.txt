You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and swirling symbols etched into its dark wood, glowing with a soft, inner light that shifts like liquid starlight. They rearrange themselves as you watch—forming faces you half-recognize from dreams you’ve forgotten, then blooming into impossible flowers whose petals whisper your name in voices that are and aren’t your own.

Your fingers trace one glowing line without thinking. The box grows warm, almost alive, and the patterns suddenly pour off its surface like ink in water, circling your wrist in a living tattoo. A low hum rises from inside, not mechanical but organic, like a heartbeat buried in velvet. You feel the urge to speak a word you’ve never learned, and when you do, the lid clicks open on its own.

Inside is not treasure, but a mirror of black glass reflecting a version of you that is still dreaming. In that reflection, you hold the key you thought was missing—it’s made of the same glowing patterns, now woven into your palm. The you in the mirror smiles sadly and presses their hand against the glass from the other side. The moment your fingers touch, the box, the room, and the dream itself begin to fold inward, collapsing into a single point of light that feels like both an ending and a beginning.

You wake with the faint taste of starlight on your tongue and a new pattern faintly visible on your skin, already fading.
