You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, you **begin to realize the walls are made of your own forgotten memories. The riddles no longer ask for answers, but for feelings. To unlock the next gate, you must recall the color of your first bicycle or the sound of a voice you haven't heard in years. The geometry of the corridors shifts with your breathing; when you panic, the path widens, but when you focus, the walls close in. Eventually, you reach the center, where there is no monster and no treasure, only a mirror that reflects not your face, but the sky. And then, the dream dissolves into the sound of morning birds, leaving you with the lingering sensation that the maze was never a place, but a feeling.**
