...written not in ink, but in something that moves. At first you think they're standard multiple-choice, but the letters keep shifting—**A** becomes a bird, **B** melts into a puddle, **C** crawls off the page entirely. You look up and the professor is gone, replaced by a version of yourself from ten years ago, sitting at the front desk and grading papers with a red pen that bleeds.

The first question asks you to *Calculate the weight of every apology you've ever given.* The second demands that you *Label the diagram of a heart you've never seen, but somehow recognize as your own.* You grip your pencil—except it's not a pencil anymore, it's a key, and the paper is a door.

You try to write, but the words come out as music. The other students (are there other students? You can't turn your head to look, only your eyes, and the periphery is filled with shadows taking notes) seem to be finishing already, their pages turning to gold. Your page is absorbing the desk, becoming a landscape. Question three is a mountain range. Question four is a river you have to cross.

You realize, with that strange certainty that only comes in dreams, that passing this exam doesn't require answers—it requires you to forget why you were afraid. So you set down the key, step into the paper, and the classroom dissolves into a library where every book is titled with a version of your name, and the final question echoes not from the page but from somewhere inside your chest: **What will you let yourself become?**

You reach to write the answer, but your hand passes through the desk, and you feel yourself falling upward, the ceiling opening into a sky full of examination papers fluttering like white birds, each one blank, each one perfect, each one already passed.
