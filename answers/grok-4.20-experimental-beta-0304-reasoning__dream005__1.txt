...and they all seem to be wearing your face, just slightly wrong.

One group is laughing too loudly at a joke that has no punchline. Their mouths open like they’re swallowing the air itself. You ask if they’ve seen your friend and they tilt their heads in perfect unison, then point toward a staircase that definitely wasn’t there a second ago. The steps are made of old birthday candles, still lit.

You climb. The wax is warm under your bare feet.

At the top you find another cluster of people playing a board game with real cities as pieces. Someone is currently moving Paris. They keep offering you tiny spoons of something that tastes like the memory of rain. Every time you try to explain you’re looking for one specific person, they smile gently, as if you’re very young and very confused, and say, “We’re all the one specific person. You just haven’t decided which one yet.”

The lights keep changing color for no reason. Your childhood bedroom is suddenly a coat closet. Someone hands you your own heart like it’s a name tag. It’s still beating, politely.

Then, across the impossible room, you finally see them—the one person you actually know—standing perfectly still while everyone else orbits them like anxious moons. They look at you with an expression that feels like the last page of a book you haven’t read yet.

They open their mouth to speak.

And that’s when you realize the entire party is inside their mouth, and you’ve been walking across their tongue the whole time.

You wake up tasting copper and birthday candles.
