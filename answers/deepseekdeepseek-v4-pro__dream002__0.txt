The wind picks up, whipping loose leaves into spiraling towers that dance between the trees like silent guardians of the coming chaos. The sky bruises from soft lavender to a deep, pulsing violet, and you notice the raindrops falling upward—defying gravity as they rise from the forest floor, shimmering like molten glass, carrying the scent of ozone and blooming night jasmine. You press forward, not out of fear, but driven by an ancient curiosity that feels both foreign and deeply familiar.

Ahead, the path dissolves into a clearing where an enormous, ancient oak stands split down the middle by a past lightning strike, yet its hollow core glows with a warm, steady amber light. The storm is not a thing of destruction here but of transformation. Leaves become tiny, luminous paper boats sailing on invisible currents; roots writhe gently under the moss, whispering in a language that hums in your bones. As you step closer to the glowing tree, the boundary between your body and the wind blurs—you can feel your edges softening, turning to mist and starlight. The storm isn't approaching you; it has been approaching *from* you all along, brewing in the quiet depths of the dream’s own logic. Then, without thunder or warning, the world folds in a silent flash of white, and you are left with just the sensation of a cool, gentle rain on your sleeping face, and the faint echo of a voice that sounds exactly like your own, saying, “Remember.”
