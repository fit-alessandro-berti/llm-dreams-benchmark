...all written in a language you don't recognize. The letters twist and shimmer on the page like living things, rearranging themselves every time you blink. 

You look around the lecture hall in panic. Everyone else is calmly writing, their pencils scratching in perfect unison, but when you glance at their papers, you see they're all answering questions about *your* life—intimate, embarrassing moments you thought no one else knew about. Your childhood fears. The thing you did in 9th grade. That one text you regret sending at 2:17 a.m.

The clock on the wall is melting. The professor is your mother, but also somehow your childhood dentist, smiling with too many teeth.

You look back down at your exam. The first question has finally settled into English:

**"Why are you pretending to be someone you're not?"**

And then, in smaller text underneath:

*(Be honest. This counts for 100% of your grade.)*

What do you do?
