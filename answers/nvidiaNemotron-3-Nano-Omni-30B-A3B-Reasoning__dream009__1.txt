You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to **learn to survive in a world without electricity or digital devices**. The air is crisp and smells of damp earth and woodsmoke, and the only sounds are the rustling of leaves and distant bird calls. You find yourself in a small, rural village nestled in a valley surrounded by rolling hills. The people here live a simple, agrarian life, using hand tools and animal labor. You notice that the village is preparing for a festival in two days, and you’re expected to help with the preparations. To survive and be accepted, you must learn the traditional skills—farming, weaving, cooking, and storytelling—while also understanding the rhythms of village life. The first step is to find someone who can teach you the basics of daily life, so you begin by approaching the village elder, a wise woman named Maelis, who sits under a large oak tree, mending a woven basket. She looks up, smiles gently, and says, “Welcome, traveler. The path ahead is long, but not impossible. What would you like to learn first?”
