...and realize that every time you look away, the geography of the room changes. 

First, you pass a circle of people dressed in shimmering, liquid-like evening gowns, tossing a small glowing glass orb back and forth. Each time someone catches it, their voice shifts pitch, and they burst into synchronized, melodic laughter that sounds like wind chimes. You ask if they’ve seen your friend, but when they turn to answer, their mouths move without sound, leaving only the scent of pine and ozone in the air.

Slinking away, you step into what should be a hallway, but it opens into a massive conservatory filled with towering, clockwork trees. Underneath a canopy of ticking bronze leaves, a group of scholars in velvet coats is intensely debating a painting hanging on the air itself. The painting is of a sunset, but the colors are actively flowing and changing, spilling off the canvas and pooling around their polished shoes. 

A familiar laugh echoes from the far side of the conservatory. *That's them.* 

You break into a run, but the floor suddenly feels heavy and soft, like wet tide-pool sand, slowing your stride. The louder the party noise gets, the further away the sound of your friend's voice seems to drift. You push through a thick velvet curtain, expecting another room, but find yourself standing on a glass balcony overlooking an infinite ocean under a sky full of two moons.

Standing right at the edge of the balcony, looking out at the water, is your friend. Relief washes over you. 

"I've been looking everywhere for you," you say, reaching out to touch their shoulder.

They begin to turn around. But as they do, the wind catches their silhouette, and their form blurs into a flock of small, silver birds that burst into the night sky. Where they stood, a single golden key rests on the marble railing. 

You pick up the key, and the entire dream begins to fold inward like an origami crane, the sound of the party fading into the slow, steady rhythm of your own breathing as you drift toward wakefulness.
