Then, the first drop hits. It isn't cold, but a single, warm, fat pearl of water that lands on my cheek. For a suspended moment, the entire forest holds its breath. And then the sky releases—not in anger, but in a sudden, sighing rush of silver. The rain comes down in sheets, each drop a tiny, soft hammer on the canopy, creating a deafening, rhythmic roar that is somehow immensely peaceful. I look up, mouth open, tasting the clean, charged water, and realize the storm isn't happening *to* the forest. It *is* the forest, dreaming of itself in another form. The wind is its voice, the rain its memory of the sea, and I am just a thought passing through its mind.
