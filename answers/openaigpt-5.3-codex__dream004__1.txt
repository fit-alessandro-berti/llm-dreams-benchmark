…as you step closer, the canvas inhales.

Not metaphorically—you hear it: a soft, tidal breath, as if the cloth is a sleeping chest.  
The sky above you shifts from violet triangles to liquid gold spirals, and each change sends a tiny shiver through the white surface.

A brush appears in your hand.  
It has no bristles—only light.

You touch it to the canvas, and instead of paint, you lay down a sound: a single piano note, blue and cold. It spreads outward in ripples, becoming a frozen lake under three green moons. You paint again—this time with the smell of rain—and a forest of transparent trees grows instantly, their leaves shaped like little clocks ticking backward.

Then you realize the canvas isn’t in front of you anymore.

It surrounds you.

You are inside the painting, walking across your own strokes. Every thought becomes color. Every memory becomes architecture. The laugh you had as a child becomes a flock of orange birds. A heartbreak becomes a cracked mountain glowing from within. A hope you forgot you had rises as a staircase made of dawn.

At the top of the staircase sits someone painting, back turned.

You move closer and see it is you—older, calmer, eyes full of constellations.  
They don’t look surprised.

Without speaking, they hand you another brush, this one made of shadow, and point to a final empty corner of sky.

You hesitate.

“What goes there?” you ask.

Your older self smiles and says,  
“The part where you wake up… and remember you can still create this world.”

You make one last stroke: a door, painted in impossible white.

It opens.

And morning spills through.
