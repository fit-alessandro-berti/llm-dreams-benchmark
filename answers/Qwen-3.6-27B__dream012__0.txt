"Hard to starboard!" you roar over the howling wind, your voice barely audible above the crash of waves. "Drop the mainsail! Now!"

The ship lurches violently, throwing men off their feet. Chaos erupts—shouts, slipping boots, the clatter of loose rigging.

You grip the wheel tighter, your knuckles white. You close your eyes for just a moment, not to sleep, but to *listen*. Beneath the chaos, there is a rhythm to the storm. A pattern in the madness. You feel the pull of the currents, the shift in the wind’s direction. It’s subtle, a whisper in the gale.

"Steady!" you command, opening your eyes. They are no longer filled with panic, but with a strange, calm clarity. "We do not fight the sea. We dance with it."

You turn the wheel slowly, deliberately, guiding the ship into the trough of the next wave rather than trying to ride over it. The crew watches, confused at first, then mimicking your movements. One by one, they find their footing. The ship dips, rises, and slides through the water with a grace that seems impossible in such violence.

As the storm reaches its peak, the sky suddenly tears open—not with more rain, but with light. A blinding, golden beam pierces the clouds, illuminating the churning waters. And there, in the center of the eye of the storm, you see it: an island made entirely of glass, reflecting the sunlight in dazzling prisms. It shouldn’t be there. No map shows it. But as your ship glides toward it, the turbulence ceases. The sea becomes mirror-still.

You look down at your hands. They are translucent, fading like mist. You realize you are not on a ship of wood and iron, but on a vessel of memory. And the island... the island is home.
