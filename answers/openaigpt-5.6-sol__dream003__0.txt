When you trace the staircase with your fingertip, the room tilts.

The walls stretch upward into darkness, and the box begins to hum—not with sound, but with a memory. You remember hiding something inside it long ago, though you have never seen it before. A warmth gathers beneath your palm. The tiny carved eye opens.

“Keys are for things that wish to remain locked,” whispers a voice from inside.

You close your eyes and think of what you have lost.

The lid clicks.

Inside is no treasure, but a small patch of night sky, deep and endless, with stars drifting just beneath the rim. One star rises like a firefly and settles in your hand. At once, you remember the way home.

But when you look up, the room is gone.

You are standing in a moonlit field, holding the open box, while thousands of locked doors wait silently among the tall grass. One by one, as you approach, they begin to dream of opening.
