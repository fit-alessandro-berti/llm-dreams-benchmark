*Inhales slowly, eyes adjusting to the shifting hues around me*

A blank canvas. How... unexpected. Everything else here refuses to hold still — the sky ripples between violet and amber, the ground beneath my feet pulses like a living thing, and the horizon folds in on itself like origami made of light.

But this canvas. It's still. Quiet. Waiting.

I reach out and touch its surface. It's cool, smooth, almost like water frozen in a single moment. The edges of the canvas shimmer faintly, as if it's not entirely sure it wants to exist.

I look around. The landscape behind me has already changed — the mountains that were there a moment ago are now spiraling towers of translucent glass, humming a low, resonant tone. The trees have become ribbons of silver, swaying to music I can't quite hear.

I turn back to the canvas.

And I realize — I don't have a brush. I don't have paint. I don't have anything.

But then I notice something. My fingertips, where they touched the canvas, have left faint traces of color. Not the colors of this world — not the shifting, unstable spectrum that surrounds me. These colors are *mine*. Deep indigo. Warm ochre. A green that tastes like the first day of spring.

I press my palm flat against the surface.

And the canvas begins to breathe.

Slowly, gently, an image starts to form — not from my hand, but from somewhere deeper. A memory, maybe. Or a feeling I didn't know I was carrying. The colors spread outward in soft, organic shapes — not quite a landscape, not quite a face, not quite anything I can name. But it's *familiar*, in the way that dreams are familiar. In the way that the feeling of home is familiar, even when you can't remember the address.

The world around me shifts again. The glass towers dissolve into flocks of luminous birds. The silver ribbons become rivers of starlight. And the canvas — the canvas holds steady, glowing softly with the image I've left on it.

I step back and look at what I've made.

It's not finished. It's not perfect. But it's *real*, in a way that nothing else in this world seems to be. It's the one thing here that isn't changing.

And for a moment — just a moment — the colors around me stop shifting too.

Everything is still.

And it's beautiful.
