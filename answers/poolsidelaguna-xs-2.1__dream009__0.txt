You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to **find shelter, food, and safety before nightfall**. The air is thick with the scent of woodsmoke and earth, and the sky stretches endlessly over a landscape of dense forests, muddy paths, and distant hills dotted with thatched-roof huts. In your hand, you clutch only a smooth stone and a scrap of fabric—your last remnants from the world you knew.  

A low, rhythmic *thump* echoes in the distance, like a heartbeat. You notice a group of people gathering near a stone circle, their clothes rough-spun, their faces wary. One of them, a woman with calloused hands and a spear, locks eyes with you. She says something urgent in a language you don’t understand, then gestures toward the forest. A growl rumbles from the underbrush—not a beast, but something else: a machine, ancient and rusted, half-buried in the undergrowth. It’s the only clue you’ve found so far that this era is *not* entirely unchanged.  

You have to decide: trust the strangers and their cryptic warnings, or venture deeper into the unknown to uncover what—or *who*—brought you here. The choice could shape your survival… or your return.  

What do you do?
