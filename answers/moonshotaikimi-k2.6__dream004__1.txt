The sky dips its brush into your thoughts. Mountains rise from your memories, melting into rivers that sing in voices you almost recognize. You try to move, but your limbs are drying—no, *settling*—becoming part of the composition. Just as panic flickers, a bird made of cobalt geometry lands on your shoulder and whispers, *"This is how you wake up."*

But you don't wake up. Instead, the canvas exhales, and you step back out into the landscape, carrying the white space in your chest. Now, wherever you walk, the colors stabilize for a heartbeat before swirling into new patterns that follow your footsteps like loyal shadows. The canvas remains behind, no longer blank—it holds the exact shade of your absence, cast by a sun that hasn't decided what shape it is yet.
