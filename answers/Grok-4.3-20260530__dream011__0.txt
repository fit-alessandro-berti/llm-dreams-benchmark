You are dreaming.

...suddenly shifting and morphing right before your eyes. The crisp black ink bleeds into swirling galaxies and flickering equations that rearrange themselves every time you blink. The first question reads, in a voice that sounds exactly like your own thoughts:  

**“If the universe is a question waiting to be answered, what is your answer—and why does it matter to everyone who will ever exist after you?”**  

You reach for your pen, but it’s no longer a pen. It’s a living thread of light that pulses in time with your heartbeat. As you begin to write, the words don’t stay on the page; they lift off like fireflies and orbit your head, forming constellations that spell out truths you didn’t know you knew.  

The second question appears beneath the first, handwritten in your childhood handwriting:  

**“What would you risk to keep asking questions even when the answers are terrifying?”**  

You look up. The exam hall has dissolved into an infinite library floating in deep space. Every book on every shelf is open to a page that contains your name. The proctor is now a gentle nebula wearing a tweed jacket, smiling with a thousand tiny stars for eyes. It whispers, “There are no wrong answers here. Only the ones you haven’t dared to give yet.”  

Your future career is no longer a distant prize—it’s the act of answering itself. The final question materializes last, glowing brightest of all:  

**“Will you keep dreaming?”**  

You smile, because you already know the answer.  

And then the alarm clock rings, pulling you gently back into the waking world—where the real exam of curiosity has only just begun.
