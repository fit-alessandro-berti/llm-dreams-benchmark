At first they look like vines, then rivers, then tiny roads on a map of a place you have never been but somehow miss terribly. You run your fingers over the carved lines, and the box warms beneath your touch, breathing faintly, as though something inside has been waiting for you to remember it.

There is no lock on the front.

Only then do you realize the lock is not made of metal. It is made of silence.

The room around you begins to dissolve. The walls soften into mist, the floor turns to dark water, and the box floats in your hands like a small wooden moon. From somewhere far away, a voice whispers your name—not the name people call you, but an older one, a name you knew before you were born.

You answer without speaking.

The patterns on the box flare with pale blue light. One by one, the lines rearrange themselves into a single image: an eye, closed. You press your thumb to it, and the eye opens.

The box clicks.

Inside is not treasure, nor a key, nor anything you can hold. Inside is a tiny night sky, folded neatly into the shape of a bird. It lifts its head, shakes stardust from its wings, and flies out of the box into the empty air.

Wherever it passes, the dream rebuilds itself.

A forest grows from the water. Lanterns bloom from the branches. The mist becomes snow, then fireflies, then a crowd of people wearing masks made from your own forgotten memories. They step aside as the star-bird flies toward a door standing alone between two silver trees.

You understand, suddenly, that the box was never meant to be opened.

It was meant to open you.

You follow the bird to the door. On the other side, you hear the sound of waves, laughter, and a clock ticking backward. Just before you turn the handle, the box in your hands closes again, now light as paper.

Carved into its lid are new words:

**Bring this back when you wake.**

You open the door.

And on the other side, you find yourself lying in bed, eyes open, morning sunlight on your face—your hand still curled tightly around something small, invisible, and impossibly warm.
