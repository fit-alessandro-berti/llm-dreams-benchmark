The grey concrete beneath your feet starts to ripple like water, turning into a path of iridescent sea-glass that glows with a soft, amber light.

The strangers don’t speak with words; instead, their thoughts drift into your mind like fallen leaves. They aren't leading you to a specific address or a landmark on a map, but toward a feeling. With every block you cross, the towering skyscrapers begin to lean inward, not to crowd you, but to shield you from a wind you can no longer feel. The neon signs above flicker and change, transforming from advertisements into poetry written in a language you’ve never seen, yet somehow perfectly understand.

"Almost there," a voice echoes, though no one has opened their mouth.

They lead you to a nondescript red door standing alone in the middle of a wide, empty plaza. There are no walls attached to it, just the frame and the door, standing defiant against the horizon. 

One of the strangers leans in and whispers, "You weren't lost; you were just taking the long way home."

As you reach for the handle, the city behind you dissolves into a cloud of gold dust. You push the door open and step through, not into a room, but into a vast, silent forest where the trees are made of silver moonlight. The moment your foot touches the soft moss, the sensation of loneliness vanishes completely, replaced by a profound sense of belonging.

Just as you turn back to thank the strangers, you realize they have become the stars above you, blinking in a slow, synchronized rhythm, guiding you deeper into the quiet. 

And then, slowly, the light fades, and you wake up.
