As you climb, the notes sing a melody you recognize from your childhood, a song you thought you had forgotten. At the top, there is no panel of judges, no resume, and no handshake. There is only a single, glowing mirror.

You step up to it, expecting to see your reflection. Instead, the glass shows you not as you are, but as you are capable of being—older, calmer, and radiating a quiet confidence. A voice, resonating from within the glass itself, speaks: *"The position is not a title to be held, but a state of being to be practiced."*

The mirror shatters into a thousand light particles that swirl around you. You feel a sudden, weightless pull, the sensation of falling upward. You realize the competition wasn't about beating others; it was about recognizing that you already possess the skills you are chasing.

The light intensifies, warm and blinding. You feel the familiar tickle of an alarm clock against your pillow. You open your eyes to the morning sun, but the melody from the staircase is still humming in your chest. You sit up, knowing that while the dream has ended, the work has just begun.
