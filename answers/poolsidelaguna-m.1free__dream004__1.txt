The canvas isn’t blank after all, but a window. Beyond it, you glimpse a realm where shapes are solidifying into tangible things: a forest of crystalline trees, their bark etched with constellations, and rivers of liquid gold winding between them.  

You dip a brush into a nearby pool of iridescent paint (its hue shifting between indigo and tangerine as you watch) and begin to paint. Each stroke doesn’t just move across the canvas but *resonates* through the world. The triangles and hexagons around you morph into the shapes you paint—a flock of origami birds takes flight from the canvas, their wings scattering prismatic light. The ground quivers, and the air fills with the scent of possibility, sharp and electric.  

But then the canvas shivers again, and the painted forest begins to crumble, its edges dissolving into pixelated smoke. You realize the trick: to keep the world alive, you must keep painting, feeding the landscape with your imagination. Yet even as you work, the canvas murmurs a question without words—is the dreamer dreaming the dream, or the dream dreaming the dreamer?  

The last thing you feel is the brush dissolving into your hand, becoming a part of you, as the colors and shapes swirl into a kaleidoscope of infinite beginnings.
