You feel a strange mix of panic and detachment—the kind where you know you should be terrified, but everything has that hazy, faraway quality to it.

Your boss is still talking, but his mouth is moving out of sync with his words. "...need it by yesterday," he says, sliding a folder across his desk toward you. When you reach for it, the folder is empty. Inside, there's nothing but a single sticky note that reads: *"Don't forget what's underneath."*

You nod, as one does in dreams, as if this makes perfect sense.

You walk back to your desk, but the layout of the office has changed. Cubicles stretch in every direction like a maze, and the ceiling seems impossibly high now—fluorescent lights buzzing louder than they should. Your coworkers type away at their keyboards, but none of them look up. Their screens flicker, showing the same document over and over—a page of text you can't quite read, no matter how hard you focus.

You finally find your desk. Your computer is already on. The clock in the corner of the screen reads **3:00**, but you can't tell if it's AM or PM. The deadline notification blinks: **DUE: NOW**.

You start typing, but the words appear on screen as something else entirely—sentences about places you've never been, conversations you don't remember having. Somewhere in the distance, a phone rings. No one answers it. The ringing gets louder.

And then the floor beneath your desk feels... soft. You look down. The carpet is turning into grass—cool, damp, green blades pushing up between your fingers where you grip the edge of your chair.

Your boss appears behind you, hand on your shoulder. "Don't worry," he says, but his voice has changed—softer now, almost familiar. "You were never really here."
