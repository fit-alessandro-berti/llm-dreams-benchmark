You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to... 

...find water first. Your throat is parched, and the air smells of woodsmoke, wet stone, and something earthy you can't quite name. The ground beneath your bare feet is cold cobblestone mixed with mud. 

Looking around, you're in what looks like a medieval market square at dawn. Thatched roofs, timber-framed houses leaning drunkenly against each other, a few chickens pecking at the ground, and a distant clang of a blacksmith's hammer. People in rough wool and linen are starting to stir. A woman carrying a yoke with two wooden buckets gives you a strange look—probably because you're still wearing your modern pajama pants and an old band t-shirt.

You realize you need to blend in quickly before someone decides the strangely dressed stranger is a witch or a madman. Your heart is racing with equal parts terror and ridiculous excitement.

So you do the only thing that makes sense in the moment:

You walk up to the woman with the water buckets, give her your most disarming smile, and say in your best attempt at an old-sounding accent:

"Good morrow, goodwife. I... appear to have taken a most peculiar tumble. Might a traveler trouble thee for a sip of that water? And perhaps... directions to the nearest tavern?"

She squints at you, clearly suspicious of your strange clothes and even stranger way of speaking.

Then, with the dream-logic that only exists in sleep, she suddenly smiles, hands you one of the buckets, and says:

"Ye speak like a fool from the future, lad. Come. The alewife owes me a favor. We'll get ye proper clothed before the reeve sees ye and decides ye're a faerie changeling."

As you walk with her through the waking town, the dream starts to shift again. You can feel the edges of it fraying—smells of baking bread and horse manure mixing with the faint beep of your real-world alarm clock somewhere far away.

But for now, you're following a medieval woman through narrow streets, carrying a heavy wooden bucket, trying not to laugh at the sheer absurdity of it all.

And somehow, deep in the dream, you realize this might not be just any random era...

It's exactly the year and place your great-great-great-grandparents lived.

The dream is waiting for you to decide what happens next.
