and you just... *smile*.

Not the polite workplace smile. The real one. The one that says "oh, this is *perfect*."

Your boss is standing there with the impossible brief, veins popping, but his head is slowly turning into a giant analog clock that's melting like Dalí painted it. The deadline is written on the project file in letters made of your own childhood handwriting: "YESTERDAY."

You look down at your hands. They're glowing faintly, like you're buffering. Your keyboard has turned into a piano. The entire office is now floating in space, and your coworkers are all versions of you at different ages — the 7-year-old you is stress-eating crayons, the 19-year-old you is trying to flirt with the coffee machine, and the 80-year-old you is already napping under the desk.

So you do the only logical thing.

You take the impossible project, roll it into a paper airplane, and throw it out the window (which is now just a hole into the heat death of the universe). It flies beautifully, catches fire, and turns into a flock of tiny glowing owls that spell out "no" in the void.

Your boss-clock melts completely and becomes a very confused golden retriever wearing a tie. He drops a tennis ball at your feet.

You pick it up.

The entire office erupts into applause because the project is now, somehow, already done. It was finished by the version of you that never existed — the one who wasn't scared. It's perfect. It's also a pizza.

You take a bite.

The dog-boss wags his tail.

And somewhere in the distance, your actual alarm clock is screaming like it's personally offended, but you're not quite ready to leave yet.

You throw the tennis ball.

The dream catches it.
