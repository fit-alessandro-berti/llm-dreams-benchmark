...and you follow them through winding streets that seem to shift and breathe around you.

The strangers are an odd, gentle group—an old woman with silver earrings shaped like crescent moons, a young man carrying a paper lantern though it's daytime, a small child humming a song you somehow remember from long ago. They lead you past shop windows displaying bottled starlight and doors that open onto different seasons. The city no longer feels cold. It feels like it's slowly introducing itself to you.

As you walk, they ask about your life, and their questions land like warm hands steadying your shoulders. You tell them things you've never said out loud.

Finally, they stop at a small café glowing with amber light. "This is where we leave you," the old woman says, smiling. "But you're not lost anymore."

You look around and realize she's right. The street signs, once written in an unreadable script, now spell names you know by heart. The city hasn't changed—you have.

When you turn back to thank them, the strangers have already dissolved into the crowd, but their kindness remains, warm as a coin held too long in your pocket.

And just before you wake, you understand: you were never truly lost. You were only learning the shape of a place you hadn't yet dared to call home.
