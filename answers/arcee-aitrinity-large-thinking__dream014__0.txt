
The strangers don’t give you a map or point to a landmark. Instead, they take your hands. Their palms are warm, and their touch is steady.

“Look,” one of them says, and you realize the city’s roar has softened into a hum, like a giant machine finally settling into a peaceful rhythm.

You look down the street you were so frantically trying to navigate. The familiar gridlock of taxis and pedestrians is still there, but now you see the *pattern* in it. The way the sunlight glances off a thousand windows, creating a river of light that flows down the avenue. The way the scent of pretzels from a cart mingles with the damp stone of an old building, telling a story of this exact corner at this exact hour.

One stranger, an older woman with silver in her hair, smiles. “You were never lost. You were just reading the wrong language.”

She gestures, and you see it: the city isn’t a puzzle to be solved, but a living thing breathing around you. The helpful stranger was the city itself, wearing a human face for a moment. The loneliness you felt was just the quiet before you learned to hear its song.

You thank them, but when you turn to look back, they are gone, dissolved back into the crowd. But the map is in your eyes now. You know which way to go—not to a specific address, but *forward*, into the beautiful, bewildering, and suddenly welcoming heart of the dream-city.
