You start to run.

The streets rearrange themselves as you move. A narrow alley opens into a sunlit plaza, then becomes the lobby of a hotel you somehow recognize. Every clock shows a different time. You stop a woman in a blue coat and ask for directions, but she points upward.

High above the city, suspended between two towers, is a glowing sign with your name on it.

You find a staircase and climb. The steps are made of pages torn from books, each one describing a moment you remember but never lived. At the top, you enter a train station. A train is waiting, its doors open, though there are no tracks beneath it.

Inside, everyone in the carriage is wearing your face.

The train begins to move. Through the windows, the city falls away, revealing an ocean beneath the streets. In the distance, on a small island, stands the building where your meeting is supposed to take place. You can see a single light burning in one of its windows.

When the train stops, you step out onto the water.

It holds you.

You walk toward the island, but with every step the meeting seems less important. By the time you reach the building, the door is already open. Inside is a quiet room with a long table, a single chair, and a clock counting backward.

Someone is sitting at the far end.

You cannot see their face.

“I’m sorry I’m late,” you say.

The figure smiles.

“You’re not late,” it says. “You’re early.”

Then it slides a folder across the table. On its cover is a date from twenty years in the future—and beneath it, a photograph of you standing in this same room.

You look up.

The room is empty.

The city is gone.

And somewhere, very close by, an alarm begins to ring.
