One of the strangers, a woman with eyes like polished river stones, presses a small, warm stone into your palm. "This is a wayfinder," she says. "It remembers the paths you’ve forgotten." As you hold it, the city around you fades into mist, and suddenly you’re standing in a sunlit meadow, the stone glowing softly in your hand.

But then—you realize the strangers are gone. The tree is gone. Only the stone remains, and in its light, you see a single word carved into your wrist: *"Begin."*
