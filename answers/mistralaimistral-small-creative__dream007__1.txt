You realize: this is a dream. But the pain in your chest is real. You reach for your friend, but they’re already dissolving, their edges unraveling like thread. **"Wait—"** you say, but the word turns into a flock of black birds that burst from your mouth and scatter into the dark.

The library collapses inward, shelves folding like origami, and you’re falling—not down, but *sideways*, through a corridor of doors. And just like that, the argument is gone. The weight in your chest lifts. You sit beside them, and together, you build the tower higher, higher—until it touches the ceiling, which isn’t a ceiling at all, but the underside of a vast, star-strewn sky.

Then the blocks start to *sing*.
