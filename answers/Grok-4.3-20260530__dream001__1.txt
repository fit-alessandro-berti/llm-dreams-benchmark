You start to run.

The city doesn’t so much have streets as it has suggestions of streets—long ribbons of cobblestone that ripple like water when you step on them, skyscrapers that bend gently at the waist to watch you pass, their windows blinking like curious eyes. The air tastes like old books and distant rain. Your heart is a frantic drum; the meeting is in fifty-eight minutes now, and you have no address, no name, only the vague certainty that it matters more than anything.

You stop a woman in a coat made of living fog. She smiles with too many teeth.

“Excuse me,” you gasp, “I’m late for—”

She hands you a single playing card: the Ace of Keys. On the back it says, in handwriting you recognize as your own from childhood: *Turn left at the fountain that remembers your name.*

You turn left.

The fountain is already there, a marble thing that weeps silver fish instead of water. As you approach it whispers your name in your mother’s voice. A door opens in its base. You step through.

On the other side the city has become a library the size of a continent. Bookshelves tower into clouds. Librarians with paper wings glide between the stacks. One of them—tall, silver-haired, wearing spectacles that reflect entire galaxies—floats down to you.

“You’re early,” she says kindly. “The meeting is in the Restricted Section. Follow the sound of your own footsteps.”

You do. Your footsteps echo backward in time. With every step the city peels away like old wallpaper until you’re standing in a small, quiet room that smells of ink and thunder. A single table. Two chairs. Across from you sits… you. Not quite you—older, calmer, eyes full of the kind of peace that only comes after many lifetimes.

The other you smiles. “You made it.”

You sit. Your watch says you have exactly one minute left.

The other you leans forward. “The meeting was never about where. It was about whether you would keep running long enough to remember that the map was inside you the whole time.”

Outside the window the impossible city folds itself into a single perfect paper crane and flies away.

You wake up smiling, the Ace of Keys still warm in your pocket.
