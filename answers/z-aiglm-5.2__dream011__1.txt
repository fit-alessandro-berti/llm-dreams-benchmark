...written in a language you've never seen before. The letters twist and shimmer on the page—somewhere between ancient runes and something that looks like it was scribbled by a child who invented their own alphabet.

You look around the room. Your classmates are scribbling furiously, completely at ease, as if this is the most natural thing in the world. The girl to your left is writing with such speed her pen is smoking. The guy in front of you has somehow finished already and is folding his exam into an origami crane.

You raise your hand. The proctor—a woman whose face keeps shifting, sometimes your professor, sometimes your mother, sometimes no one you've ever known—walks over. You try to ask about the language, but when you open your mouth, the words come out as birds. Small ones. Sparrows. They fly toward the ceiling, which is suddenly much higher than it should be, disappearing into a sky that has replaced the fluorescent lights.

The proctor leans down and whispers, "You already know this. You've always known this."

You look back at the exam. The strange letters begin to rearrange themselves slowly, like iron filings responding to a magnet. They form shapes—no, memories. You see your childhood bedroom. Your first day of school. A conversation you had yesterday that you thought you'd forgotten. Every moment that led you here, laid out in questions that don't have answers so much as they have *directions*.

The last question simply reads: *"What will you become?"*

And beneath it, the blank space waits—vast and patient.

You pick up your pen. It's warm in your hand. You realize you're not afraid anymore.
