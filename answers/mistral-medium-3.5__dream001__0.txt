Just then, a stranger in a long, flowing coat taps your shoulder and hands you a folded piece of paper. On it, in your own handwriting, are the words: *"The meeting is where the clock strikes thirteen."* You look up, and the stranger is gone.

Ahead, you spot a clock tower looming over a narrow alley. Its face shows only twelve hours, but as you watch, the hands begin to spin wildly—then stop at thirteen. The crowd around you freezes, and the alley suddenly feels alive, as if the walls themselves are breathing. A door you hadn’t noticed before creaks open, revealing a dimly lit staircase spiraling upward. At the top, you hear the faint sound of voices—some familiar, some not. The meeting, it seems, is already beginning. Do you step inside?
