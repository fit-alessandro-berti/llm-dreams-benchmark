...and you suddenly realize your hands have turned into floppy fish. Like, actual salmon. They're flopping around on the desk, slapping against the keyboard, leaving little scales on the Enter key.

Your boss is standing there with that terrifying half-smile, holding a stopwatch that seems to be counting *backwards*. Every time the fish-hands slap the keyboard, the deadline on your screen gets even shorter. 47 hours becomes 12. 12 becomes negative 3.

You try to say "I need more time," but instead of words, bubbles come out of your mouth and float up to the ceiling, where they pop into tiny versions of your performance review from last quarter. All the "meets expectations" have turned into sad trombone sounds.

Then the office floor turns into pudding.

You're now sinking into chocolate pudding up to your knees while your fish hands desperately try to open the project files. The files are named things like "final_final_final_v2_FINAL_I_swear_its_final.doc" and they're all empty except for one GIF of a dancing cat that screams "YOLO" in Comic Sans every time you click it.

Your boss leans in and whispers, "The client just added one more requirement: it needs to be made of clouds."

And that's when you notice the entire office is now underwater, but everyone is acting like this is normal. Your coworker in the next cubicle is just peacefully typing with octopus tentacles.

You look down at your fish hands. They look back at you with little fish eyes that somehow convey both pity and mild disappointment.

...and then your alarm clock goes off.

Or does it? You're still not sure if you're awake.
