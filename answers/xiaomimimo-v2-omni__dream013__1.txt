The walls shift as you solve each one — the solution becomes part of the architecture.  

At first, you use pure logic: patterns, grids, sequences.  
Then the puzzles begin to use *you* — your focus alters their shape; your assumptions become hidden switches.  

You encounter a door that only appears if you stop looking for it.  
A corridor that is a metaphor for recursion — each step forward is also a step backward, until you realize the path isn’t spatial but temporal.  

Deeper still, the puzzles aren’t about finding answers, but about understanding the nature of the questions. One asks: *What disappears when you name it?*  
Silence, you answer.  
The wall melts into mist.  

But the maze notices your growth. Now the puzzles aren’t just in front of you — they’re inside you. A riddle unfolds in your own thoughts: *Why are you solving what you are part of?*  

You stop moving. You look at the walls — they’re built from old solutions, from puzzles you’ve long forgotten.  

And in that moment of recognition, the maze stops being a maze.  
It becomes a mirror.  

And you — the dreamer — wake up…  
or do you simply realize you’ve been awake the whole time, and the dream was the only way to see the pattern?
