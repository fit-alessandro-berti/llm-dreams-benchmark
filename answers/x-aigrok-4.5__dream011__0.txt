**...written in a swirling, ever-shifting script of glowing symbols that rearrange themselves into nonsense equations, riddles about your childhood fears, and multiple-choice options that include things like “the color of Tuesday,” “the square root of your unfinished ambitions,” and “none of the above because the test is already over and you already failed.”**

You flip the page frantically. The next section demands you calculate the trajectory of a falling star while simultaneously defending a thesis on why your future career should exist at all. The clock on the wall isn’t counting down—it’s laughing. Every tick seems to whisper the name of someone who already graduated, already succeeded, already left you behind.

Your polished pen turns into a live worm. The exam booklet sprouts legs and begins crawling across the desk. You try to raise your hand for help, but your arm is made of smoke. The professor (who now looks exactly like every authority figure who ever doubted you, fused into one) only chuckles and says, “You had one job. This is it.”

Then the room floods with golden light—or maybe failure-light—and every answer key you’ve ever needed bursts into butterflies that fly just out of reach. Right as you lunge for them, you hear a doorbell that shouldn’t exist, the distant sound of an alarm, or someone calling your name from the waking world, and the whole exam hall starts to dissolve like wet paper…

…and you jolt awake, heart pounding, feet kicking, still muttering half-finished solutions to problems that never made sense.

The dream (or nightmare) ends there.  
You survived it. The real exam, if it ever comes, will almost certainly be kinder.
