The silence is the first thing that hits me—not a true silence, but the heavy, velvet absence of the *hum*. There is no electric drone of a refrigerator, no distant white noise of a highway, no rhythmic pulse of a digital clock. The world feels thick, as if the air itself is made of something more substantial than oxygen.

I am standing in a meadow where the grass is the color of oxidized copper, swaying in a wind that smells of crushed cloves and old parchment. My clothes are heavy, a coarse tunic of undyed wool that chafes against my skin, grounding me in a way that feels uncomfortably real. Above, the sky isn't blue; it is a swirling bruised violet, and the sun is a pale, oversized pearl that doesn't seem to radiate heat, only a soft, diffused glow.

As I stumble through the metallic grass, the realization hits me with the weight of a physical blow: **I realize I need to find the Weaver of Echoes.**

Because the shadows are wrong. I watch a flight of white birds pass overhead, and their shadows linger on the copper grass for several seconds after the birds have vanished, drifting like spilled ink before evaporating. I look down at my own feet, and my heart stammers—my shadow is not attached to my heels. It is walking several paces ahead of me, a dark, flickering silhouette that seems to be leading the way, moving with a purposeful stride I do not possess.

If I don't catch my shadow, if I don't find the Weaver to stitch my silhouette back to my heels, I fear I will begin to drift. I will become a person without an anchor, a ghost of a presence, floating through this era like a scent on the wind, unable to touch, unable to be seen, eventually dissolving into the violet sky.

I begin to run, but the ground feels like silk under my feet, making every stride a rhythmic, swaying dance. Ahead, through the haze of amber light, I see the silhouette of a spire made entirely of frozen smoke. The shadow ahead of me quickens its pace, beckoning me toward the tower, and I realize that in this dream, I am not the protagonist—I am the pursuit.
