...you quickly realize that every conversation you stumble upon is eerily specific and oddly intense.

In the first circle, a group of people in formal wear are debating the exact shade of blue that the sky was on the day you were born. They hold up swatches of fabric, shaking their heads dismissively at each other, speaking in hushed, urgent tones as if the fate of the world depends on getting the hex code right. You try to squeeze past them, but the floor feels like it’s made of memory foam, sucking at your shoes.

You then wander into what looks like a kitchen, but everyone here is speaking in movie quotes. Not just any quotes, but exclusively lines from films you watched during a fever in the third grade. A woman with no face is stirring a pot and says, "Nobody puts Baby in a corner," while a man in a suit made of bubble wrap responds, "Life is like a box of chocolates." You open your mouth to ask for water, but only a puff of glitter comes out.

Panic begins to set in. You need to find your friend, the only anchor in this shifting reality. You push through a curtain of heavy velvet and find yourself in a library where the books have no titles. The people here are "reading" by holding books upside down and humming loudly. Suddenly, the lights flicker, and the crowd parts like the Red Sea.

There, at the very end of the room, is your friend. They are sitting at a small table, waving at you. You sprint toward them, but the distance seems to stretch. Just as you are about to reach them, the dream logic shifts one last time:

Your friend stands up, but they aren't looking at you. They are looking at a giant clock on the wall that is melting like wax. Your friend points at the clock and shouts over the sudden roar of a waterfall that wasn't there a second ago, "We have to go! The dream is about to reset!"

You reach out your hand to grab theirs, but as your fingers touch, the floor vanishes, and you are falling upward into a bright, white light...
