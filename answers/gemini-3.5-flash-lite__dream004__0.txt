...a paintbrush that feels impossibly heavy, carved from a material that seems simultaneously like polished obsidian and condensed starlight. 

As my fingers wrap around the handle, the shifting landscape around us freezes—just for a heartbeat. The cerulean mountains stop melting into rivers of liquid gold; the jagged, breathing triangles hovering in the violet sky pause their slow dance. The entire world holds its breath, waiting.

I step up to the pristine, blinding white of the canvas. My hand moves instinctively, sweeping the brush across the surface in a wide arc. 

Instead of leaving paint, the brush leaves a tear in the fabric of the canvas. Through the opening, I don't see a new color, but a memory—the smell of rain on hot pavement from a childhood I’ve never lived, the sound of a voice calling my name from very far away. 

I press the brush deeper into the tear. The canvas begins to pulse, glowing from within, and the shifting world behind me shatters like stained glass, reforming into something entirely new. 

I look down at my hand. The paintbrush is gone, replaced by a key made of glass. And in the distance, a door begins to materialize out of the mist...
