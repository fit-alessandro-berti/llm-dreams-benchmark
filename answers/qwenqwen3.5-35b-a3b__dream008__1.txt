You find your hands turning into digital pixels, glitching in and out of existence every time you try to grab the keyboard. The screen in front of you ripples like a dark pond, and the deadline isn't written in numbers—it's a physical hourglass embedded in your chest, the sand already running out too fast to see.

Your boss is no longer a person; they are a silhouette made of shifting shadows that projects their demands directly into your mind. The office walls stretch like taffy, pushing the ceiling higher until it vanishes into a cloudless, twilight sky. The project you need to complete is a tower of smoke that keeps collapsing and re-forming into shapes you recognize—your childhood home, a car you sold years ago, a letter you never sent.

Panic spikes in your throat, but then the fluorescent lights above begin to hum a lullaby. You stop trying to push the smoke tower down. You sit back, and the impossible weight in your chest vanishes. The boss dissolves into a flock of moths. The hourglass shatters into glitter. You take a deep breath, the room tilts gently, and suddenly, you are standing on the ground of your own bedroom, the alarm clock silent, the impossible deadline already forgotten.
