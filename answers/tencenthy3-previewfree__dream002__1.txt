...the air turns heavy, thick with the scent of ozone and damp earth. The vibrant greens of the forest deepen to near-black, and the wind picks up, whipping your hair across your face. Leaves begin to swirl in a chaotic dance around your ankles.

Just as the first heavy drop of rain hits your forehead, the trees ahead seem to part—not by the wind, but as if they are stepping aside. In the small clearing that appears, the rain doesn't fall. It’s a pocket of stillness in the chaos. In the center, there is a single, ancient oak tree, but it is glowing with a soft, pulsating blue light from within its bark.

You feel an irresistible pull toward the tree. As you step into the dry clearing, the sound of the storm vanishes completely, replaced by a low, harmonic hum that vibrates in your chest.

As you reach out to touch the glowing bark, the scene shatters like a reflection in water. The forest dissolves, and you find yourself floating in a vast, starry sky, weightless. Below you, the stormy forest is just a tiny, swirling cloud mass, and ahead of you, a giant, silver moon is rising rapidly, lighting up a path made of stardust leading toward the horizon.
