...learn the language of the wind before you can speak to the people.

The air here is thick with the scent of pine resin and woodsmoke, a stark contrast to the sterile, recycled oxygen of your old world. You are standing in a village carved into the side of a colossal, ancient tree that stretches far beyond the reach of your eyes. The inhabitants move with a fluid grace, their skin adorned with intricate patterns that seem to shift like living ink when they catch the light.

You realize quickly that modern concepts like money or status mean nothing here. Instead, value is measured in **stories** and **silence**. To gain trust, you must offer a memory so vivid it feels like a shared dream, or sit quietly until the forest whispers its secrets to you.

Your first challenge arrives not as a threat, but as an invitation: an elder approaches you, holding out a hollowed-out gourd filled with water that reflects not your face, but the sky above. They gesture for you to drink, but you know from the subtle cues in their posture that this is a test. If you drink greedily, you will be seen as selfish. If you refuse, you will be seen as ungrateful.

What do you do?
