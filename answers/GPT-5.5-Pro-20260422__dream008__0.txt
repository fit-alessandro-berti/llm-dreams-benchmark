…realize you can’t find your desk.

Everyone else is typing furiously, but their keyboards make the sound of rain on a roof. Your boss points to a glowing folder in your hands and says, “It’s due yesterday.” When you open it, the pages are blank except for a single sentence:

**Begin where you left yourself.**

You try to ask what the project is, but your voice comes out as printer paper. Sheet after sheet spills from your mouth, covered in tiny unreadable words. Your coworkers calmly gather them, nodding as if this is normal.

Then the office lights flicker, and the walls stretch upward into glass towers. Your computer screen becomes a window. Outside, instead of a parking lot, there is an ocean at night, and floating on the waves are hundreds of little desks, each with a lamp still burning.

Your boss checks their watch, but the watch has no hands.

“You know what to do,” they say.

So you sit at the nearest desk, place your fingers on the keyboard, and type the only thing you can think of:

**I need more time.**

The moment you press enter, the whole office exhales.

The deadline on the calendar peels itself off the wall, folds into a paper bird, and flies out through the window. One by one, your coworkers stop working and look up. Their faces are no longer tired. They are relieved.

Your boss smiles, almost sadly, and says, “That was the project.”

Then the ocean rises gently through the floor, warm around your ankles, carrying the desks away. You stand there as the workplace dissolves into moonlight, and somewhere far off, an alarm clock begins ringing like a buoy in the dark.
