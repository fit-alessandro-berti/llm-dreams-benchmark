You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to find water, shelter, and somehow orient yourself in this unfamiliar world.

The air smells different — earthier, without exhaust fumes. The light filters through rough-spun linen curtains, and beneath you is a straw mattress that crackles with every movement. A wooden bucket sits by the door. The walls are wattle and daub, and through a small window, cobblestone streets stretch toward a market square where vendors shout prices in a language you almost understand.

You rise on unsteady legs. Your clothes have changed too — simple wool and linen, leather shoes that fit too perfectly to be coincidence. Around your neck hangs a small iron key you've never seen before.

Stepping outside, the world hits you in waves. A blacksmith's hammer rings somewhere nearby. Children chase a dog through an alley. The sky is wide and unpolluted, a blue so deep it hurts.

You follow the sound of running water. A communal well stands at the center of the next street, women gathered around it exchanging gossip. They regard you with cautious curiosity but not hostility. One offers you a cup.

The key grows warm against your chest, pulling gently eastward, toward a narrow lane that curves out of sight.

*What waits down that path?*
