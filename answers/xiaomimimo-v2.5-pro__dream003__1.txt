# The Dream of the Locked Box

You are dreaming. Can you complete the following dream?

---

You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and symbols spiraling across its surface — not carved, but seemingly *growing* from the wood itself, like veins of living silver.

The patterns shift when you're not looking directly at them.

You hold the box to your ear and hear something impossible: **a familiar voice** — yours — whispering from inside. It's reciting the words of a conversation you haven't had yet.

Your fingers trace a symbol that looks like a keyhole, and the metal grows warm. The patterns begin to rearrange, forming a sentence in a language you've never learned but somehow understand completely:

> *"The box opens not when you find the key, but when you remember why you locked it."*

A wave of emotion hits you — grief, relief, longing — and you realize this box has been with you before. In another dream. Or another life. You can't quite recall.

The symbols pulse once, twice — and then the lock clicks.

You lift the lid slowly. Inside, resting on velvet the color of a night sky, is:

**A mirror.**

But in its reflection, you are younger. Much younger. The child-you looks up, smiles, and says:

*"Don't forget what you loved before you learned to be afraid."*

The box dissolves in your hands like dust. You wake up with the scent of rain on your pillow, and a single silver thread caught between your fingers.

---

*You were dreaming. But the thread is still there.* 🌙
