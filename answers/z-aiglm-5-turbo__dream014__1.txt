...follow them through a narrow alley that shouldn't exist, but does — squeezed between two buildings like a secret the city has been keeping.

They don't speak with words, exactly. Their intentions arrive as warmth in your chest, like remembering something you never knew you forgot. One of them — a woman in a yellow coat that seems to glow from within — turns back and smiles, and you realize her face keeps slightly changing. Not in a frightening way. In the way that water is always a different shape.

The alley opens into a plaza that isn't on any map. There's a fountain in the center, but instead of water, it's light — slow, thick light that rises and falls in gentle arcs. Children are playing in it, and when the light touches them, they laugh in a key you've never heard but somehow recognize.

Someone takes your hand. You look down and see it's a version of yourself — younger, maybe, or just less tired. They lead you to a bench where an old man is sitting, holding a small cardboard sign. You expect it to say something cryptic, something dreamlike and strange.

It just says: *You're allowed to rest here.*

And for the first time in the dream — maybe for the first time in a long time — you do. The noise of the city fades to a hum, then to silence, then to the sound of your own breathing, slow and even.

The strangers sit around you in a loose circle, not watching, just *being there*, and the feeling of being lost dissolves into something quieter:

The feeling of being found.
