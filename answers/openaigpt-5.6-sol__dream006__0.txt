The audience gasps. The birds circle the chandeliers, trailing silver ribbons of light, then settle along the edge of the stage. One of them tilts its head toward you as if waiting.

You look down at your notes. Every page is blank except for a single handwritten sentence:

**Tell them what you forgot.**

At once, the theater grows impossibly quiet. You remember a summer afternoon that never happened, a yellow house you have never seen, and someone standing in its doorway, waving goodbye.

“I was supposed to come back,” you whisper.

The birds take flight. The ceiling opens into a vast night sky, and the audience rises—not to applaud, but to follow them upward. Soon the seats are empty, the stage is grass beneath your bare feet, and the yellow house waits at the top of a distant hill.

Its front door is open.

From inside, someone calls your name in your own voice.
