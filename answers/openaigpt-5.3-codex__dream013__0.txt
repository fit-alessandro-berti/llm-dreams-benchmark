At first, the puzzles are familiar: rotating tiles, number locks, mirrored symbols. Your mind clicks through them with sharp, satisfying precision. Every solved chamber opens with a deep stone sigh, and you move forward, certain there is a center, a prize, an answer.

But deeper in, logic bends.

A room asks you to **prove** that silence has weight.  
Another asks for the missing color between blue and green.  
In one corridor, the walls are covered in equations that rewrite themselves the moment you understand them.

You keep solving anyway.

You begin using memories as tools.  
Your childhood bedroom becomes a key.  
A forgotten song becomes a bridge.  
The scent of rain deciphers a lock no language can touch.

At last, you reach the heart of the maze: a circular chamber with no doors, no markings, only a still black pool in the center. Above it, words appear in light:

**FINAL PUZZLE: WHO IS THINKING THIS DREAM?**

You answer with your name. The pool stays still.  
You answer with your fears. Nothing.  
You answer with your achievements, your regrets, your story. Silence.

Then, exhausted, you kneel by the water and whisper, “I don’t know.”

The pool ripples.

The chamber cracks open like an eggshell, and behind it is not treasure, not escape, but dawn—soft gold light spilling across your closed eyelids.

You wake up with your heart racing, a strange calm in your chest, and one clear feeling:

The maze wasn’t trying to trap you.  
It was trying to introduce you to yourself.
