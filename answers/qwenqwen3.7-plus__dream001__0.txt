You start to run, but your legs feel heavy, as if the air itself has turned to thick, warm syrup. The cobblestones beneath your feet shift like loose puzzle pieces, rearranging themselves with every step you take. You pull out your phone to check a map, but the screen displays only a swirling nebula of indigo and gold, accompanied by a single, pulsing message: *The compass points to the loudest silence.*

You look up. The city is a dizzying, impossible collage of eras. Gothic stone spires are woven through neon-lit glass tubes, and bridges made of intertwined, glowing tree roots connect towers that stretch up into a twilight sky the color of bruised peaches. There are no cars. A few pedestrians glide by rather than walk, their faces pleasantly blank, humming melodies that sound like wind chimes.

Panic flutters in your chest. You grab the arm of a passing figure wearing a suit that looks like it’s tailored from pressed autumn leaves. 

"Excuse me," you say, but your voice sounds muffled and distant, as if you are speaking underwater. "I have an important meeting. I don't know where it is."

The figure turns. They don't have a face, just a smooth, mirror-like surface reflecting the peach-colored sky, yet you feel a sense of profound, gentle amusement radiating from them. They point a leafy finger not down the street, but straight up.

"It is in the pendulum," a voice echoes in your mind, though the figure makes no sound. "But you must arrive before you leave."

You look toward the center of the city. There, defying all logic, hangs a massive, floating pocket watch the size of a building. Its hands are spinning wildly in opposite directions. 

Strangely, as you stare at it, the panic in your chest begins to dissolve. The urgency of the "important meeting" unwinds, revealing a sudden, quiet knowing: the meeting isn't with a boss or a client. It’s with a version of yourself you forgot to visit a long time ago. 

You take a deep breath. Instead of fighting the heaviness in your legs, you surrender to it. You step off the edge of the sidewalk, but you don't fall. The city's gravity simply decides to stop applying to you. 

You begin to float gently upward, drifting past stained-glass windows and chimneys puffing clouds that smell distinctly of old books and petrichor. The spinning clock grows larger, its rhythmic, booming *tick-tock* perfectly syncing with your own heartbeat. 

Just as you reach the glass face of the clock, a small, brass door swings open from nowhere. You step through. 

The vast, unfamiliar city folds away like origami, leaving you in a quiet, sunlit room with hardwood floors. There is a single, comfortable armchair, a window looking out over a calm, endless ocean, and a steaming cup of tea on a small table. On the table rests a note in your own handwriting: 

*You made it. Take your time.*
