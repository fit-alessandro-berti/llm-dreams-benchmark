A woman with a face like a smudged charcoal drawing hands you a map. The paper is warm, pulsing faintly. You unfold it: the streets are written in a language that shifts when you blink. *Calle de los Olvidos.* *Rue de l'Heure Perdue.* *Forgotten Alley.* The meeting location is marked in red ink that seems to breathe—**The Glass Tower, 14th Floor, Room 1408.** But there's no Glass Tower on the map. Only a blank white space where the city center should be.

You ask her for directions. Her mouth opens but a flock of starlings flies out instead, spiraling upward into a sky that's the wrong color—bruised purple, no sun, two moons. She dissolves into newspaper pages scattering on wind you can't feel.

Your watch reads 7:43. The meeting is at 8:00. The second hand moves backward.

A tram rattles past—number 14, destination simply *THERE.* The doors fold open like origami. Inside, the seats are occupied by versions of you: one in a wedding dress, one in a hospital gown, one wearing armor made of license plates, one small as a child clutching a briefcase too heavy for its arms. They all turn to watch you. The child-you holds up three fingers. Lowers one. Lowers another.

The tram doesn't wait.

You run. Your legs move through honey. The buildings lean inward, whispering in languages you almost understand. A clock tower strikes thirteen. Somewhere, a phone rings in an empty room.
