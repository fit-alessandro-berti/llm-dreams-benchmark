You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, you begin to notice that the walls are listening.

Every time you make a choice, the corridors rearrange themselves with a low, thoughtful hum. Doors appear where none existed before. Symbols carved into the stone blink like patient eyes. You solve riddles written in languages you have never learned, yet somehow remember.

At the maze’s center, you find a circular room containing a single mirror.

Your reflection does not copy you. Instead, it stands with its back turned, studying a small model of the maze. As you approach, it moves a tiny figure through the miniature corridors. You feel your own feet carry you along the same path.

“Who is solving whom?” you ask.

Your reflection turns. It has your face, but looks impossibly tired.

“The maze isn’t testing your intelligence,” it says. “It is testing what you refuse to leave behind.”

The mirror cracks. In every shard, you see a different memory: a forgotten promise, an unopened letter, a face whose name you can no longer recall. The final puzzle becomes clear. To escape, you must choose one memory to keep. The rest will vanish when you wake.

You reach toward the brightest shard—but before your fingers touch it, the entire maze folds upward like paper. Corridors become staircases, staircases become wings, and the stone ceiling opens onto a sky filled with enormous turning keys.

You fall upward.

Just before you reach the stars, you wake with your hand outstretched, clutching nothing.

On your palm is a tiny ink-drawn map.

At its center, someone has written:

**You are still inside.**
