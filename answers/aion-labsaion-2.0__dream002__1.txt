The sky darkens to a bruised purple, and the wind begins to howl through the trees, bending them at impossible angles. The air crackles with static, making your hair stand on end. You look for shelter, but the familiar path vanishes into swirling mist. That's when you see it: a figure standing between the trees, not moving, just watching you. It's humanoid but indistinct, as if made of the storm itself. Lightning flashes, and for a split second, you see its face—it's your own, but twisted with an emotion you can't name. The rain starts, but it's not water; it's warm and thick like honey, slowing your movements. The figure reaches out a hand, and you feel a pull, not on your body, but on your very consciousness. You realize the storm isn't outside you—it's erupting from within. The forest dissolves into a vortex of light and sound, and you are falling, not down, but into yourself.
