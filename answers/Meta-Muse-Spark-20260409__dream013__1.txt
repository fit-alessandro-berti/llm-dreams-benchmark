When you solve the third layer, the floor underfoot becomes translucent and you realize the maze isn’t flat. It’s a sphere, and you’ve been walking toward its center all along.  

The last chamber has no door. Just a table with one unfinished puzzle: a handful of mismatched pieces that look like fragments of sky, clockwork, and handwritten notes in your own handwriting. No instructions. You understand without being told that the only way to solve it is to decide what picture you want it to be.  

As you place the first piece, the maze exhales. The walls begin to dissolve into light, and the intense focus you’ve been carrying starts to feel like momentum, like it could carry you into whatever you dream next.  

What picture would you complete with those last pieces?
