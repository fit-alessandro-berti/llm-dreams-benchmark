I look up. The sky is no longer blue; it is a churning, sickly green-gray tapestry, bruised and impossibly low. The wind doesn't just sound like a breeze; it sounds like a collective, monstrous *sigh*, a deep, resonant whisper of something vast and angry.

The ground begins to tremble. It’s too deep for just thunder. It feels like the earth itself is groaning under enormous strain.

A single tree—an ancient oak whose massive roots had always been a reassuring anchor—rattles violently. Its delicate leaves, which had only moments ago been fluttering idly, are ripped from the branches in a single, panicked rush, swirling around me like golden rain.

Panic finally forces my legs to move. I turn, scrambling away from the open space, seeking the sheltered embrace of the thickest grouping of trees, stumbling over exposed roots that seem to writhe in the deepening twilight.

I realize I am not just witnessing a storm; I am *inside* the storm’s approach. It feels personal, like the atmosphere itself is pressing down on my chest, making it hard to draw a full breath.

Then, right before the first, ear-splitting peal of thunder cracks across the sky, the storm doesn't just arrive—it *reveals* something.

Near the base of the largest oak, where the light is failing fastest, a shimmer appears. It’s not lightning, nor is it merely dust. It’s a patch of light, iridescent and humming, pulsing with colors that do not exist in the waking world—impossible shades of lavender, glowing emerald, and deep, liquid gold.

The humming intensifies, drawing me irresistibly forward, towards the edge of the chaos. I stop dead, stumbling slightly, drawn against my will to look at the shimmering anomaly. It smells like rain, but mixed with burnt sugar and forgotten memories.

As the first massive drops of rain slap down, impossibly heavy and sudden, hitting the forest floor with the force of thrown stones, the light pulses, and I see the source of the shimmer. It is not a gateway, or a portal, but a single, floating object—a spiral of glowing, crystalline threads, spinning in slow motion, seemingly woven from pure emotion, and it appears to be calling my name.

I lean forward, ready to reach out, ready to step into the inexplicable, as the sky finally rips open and a deluge of roaring, electric rain washes over me.
