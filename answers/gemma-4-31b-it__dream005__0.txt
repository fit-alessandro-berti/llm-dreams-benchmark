I spot a flash of a crimson coat—the person I’m looking for—but as I step toward them, the floor beneath me softens, turning into a slow-moving river of deep purple velvet. I am no longer walking; I am gliding, pushed along by the current of the conversation. I pass a group of philosophers who are debating the color of the wind, and as they speak, actual ribbons of iridescent light spiral out of their mouths, weaving a shimmering canopy over the ballroom.

The walls begin to stretch, the ceiling ascending until it becomes a midnight sky filled with floating chandeliers that look like dying stars. The noise of the party fades into a heavy, rhythmic silence, like a heartbeat echoing in a cavern.

Finally, I see them again. They are standing at the edge of a balcony that overlooks a city made of clockwork and mirrors. I call their name, but the sound comes out as a shower of silver coins that clatter softly on the velvet floor. When they turn around, they are smiling, but their face is a mirror reflecting not me, but a version of me from a childhood I don’t remember having.

They reach out a hand, and just as our fingers touch, the mirror shatters into a thousand white flower petals. I feel myself falling upward, the room dissolving into a pale, morning mist, until the weight of my own blankets pulls me back into the waking world.
