You look closer at the brief. The impossible deadline suddenly morphs into a playful, almost nonsensical phrase: "Before the coffee gets cold."

A wave of relief washes over you, followed by confusion. Is this a sign? A metaphor? You look at your half-empty coffee cup. It’s lukewarm. The squirrel nods sagely, then leaps onto your shoulder, whispering secrets about optimal caffeine-to-productivity ratios directly into your ear.

You start typing, not with the panicked urgency of before, but with a newfound, albeit bizarre, clarity. Ideas flow, absurd but somehow effective. You’re building intricate spreadsheets made of Lego bricks, crafting presentations that involve interpretive dance, and coding in a language composed entirely of cartoon sound effects. Your colleagues glance over, a flicker of surprise on their faces, but then they shrug and go back to their spreadsheets.

You look down at the colorful mess of bricks, then at the squirrel, who winks. The deadline? It’s already passed. The coffee is definitely cold. But somehow, the project is… done.
