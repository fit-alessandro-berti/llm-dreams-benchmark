You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to find a way home—but no one understands what a phone is, why you keep looking at your empty wrist, or why the absence of electric light feels like a physical loss.

The town is built of crooked timber and pale stone. Horses clatter over muddy streets. Smoke curls from chimneys beneath a sky so dark at night that the stars seem close enough to touch.

An old woman at the market watches you struggle with a strange coin in your pocket. She takes it from your hand, turns it over once, and goes pale.

“You came through the clock,” she whispers.

Beyond the town, on a hill surrounded by black pines, stands a ruined tower. Its clock has no hands, yet every midnight it rings thirteen times.

The woman tells you that if you climb the tower before the final bell, you may return to your own time.

But as the first bell begins to ring, you realize something terrible:

You can no longer remember what year you came from.
