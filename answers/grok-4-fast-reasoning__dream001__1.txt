You start to wander the labyrinthine streets, the city's spires twisting like forgotten memories into the sky. Neon signs flicker in languages you half-remember from childhood, whispering directions that lead nowhere. Your watch ticks louder with each step, the hands spinning erratically—five minutes left, then ten, then back to an hour. Panic bubbles up, but so does a strange calm; you hail a cab that's more like a glowing beetle, its driver a shadowy figure with eyes like shattered glass.

"Where to?" it buzzes. You stammer an address you invent on the spot—42 Eclipse Boulevard—and the beetle hums to life, weaving through crowds of people who walk backward, laughing at jokes yet to be told. Buildings fold and unfold around you, revealing hidden alleys lined with clocks that melt into puddles of time.

Suddenly, the cab erupts into a plaza bathed in golden light. There, atop a fountain of liquid stars, sits your meeting: not a boardroom, but a council of dream-weavers, nodding approvingly as you arrive exactly on time—or is it never? You wake with the echo of their applause, the city's hum fading into the rhythm of your breath.
