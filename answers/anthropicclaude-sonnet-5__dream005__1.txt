…snippets of conversation drift past you like scraps of paper caught in wind. You keep scanning faces, half-recognizing strangers who turn out to be no one you know.

The house seems to expand every time you enter a new room—hallways branching into more hallways, doors opening onto staircases that shouldn't fit inside the building. You catch a glimpse of a familiar jacket disappearing around a corner and your pace quickens, but when you round the same corner, it's just an empty room with a single lamp glowing in the corner, humming faintly.

A group near a bookshelf laughs at something you didn't hear the beginning of. One of them looks at you like they expect you to laugh too, so you do, a beat too late, and they turn away, unbothered, already talking about something else.

You keep moving. The floor tilts slightly, or maybe that's just the wine you don't remember drinking. Someone hands you a name tag with a name that isn't yours, but you put it on anyway because it feels rude not to.

Then—there. Across a room that's somehow both crowded and cavernous, you spot the one person you know. They wave, mouth your name (or the name on your tag, you can't tell), and start walking toward you. But the space between you seems to stretch, the crowd shifting and multiplying, and no matter how many steps either of you take, the distance holds steady, like the room itself doesn't want you to meet.

You call out to them. Your voice sounds strange, slightly delayed, like it's arriving before you've finished speaking it.

And then you wake up—

—or do you?
