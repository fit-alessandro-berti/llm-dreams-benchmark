You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to rally a small band of survivors—fellow wanderers you've met along the ruined highways—and build a hidden sanctuary in the overgrown ruins of an old national park. With scavenged solar panels flickering to life under your makeshift ingenuity, you turn rusted greenhouses into thriving hydroponic gardens, coaxing potatoes and greens from irradiated soil. Nights are spent sharing stories around a fire pit, forging bonds stronger than any pre-collapse society. But whispers of raiders on the wind remind you vigilance is key; one dawn, as you spot their dust trail on the horizon, you lead your group in a clever ambush using the park's natural traps—vines rigged like snares and echoes amplified by canyons. Victory tastes like clean water from a newly purified stream, and in that moment, hope blooms amid the desolation. As the dream fades, you awaken with a spark of determination, wondering if the real world could use a little of that grit too.
