I try to lift the lid, but it's heavy, not with weight, but with importance. I know, with the sudden certainty you only have in dreams, that the key isn't an object. It's a sound. I open my mouth to speak, but instead of words, a melody spills out—a tune I haven't heard since childhood.

The lock clicks, not mechanically, but like a sigh of relief. The lid dissolves into a swarm of blue butterflies that scatter into the air. Inside the box, there is no object. Instead, there is a hole in the world, a perfect circle showing a night sky filled with stars I don't recognize. I reach in to touch a star, and my hand passes through the wood, through the sky, and into cold, rushing water.

Suddenly, I am sinking. The box is gone. I am breathing underwater, but it feels like air. I look up and see the surface of the water is actually the lid of the box, closing slowly above me, shutting out the light...
