...and you freeze. The project brief materializes on your screen in glowing red letters: **"Build a fully functional quantum espresso machine that also predicts the stock market—due in 47 minutes. No pressure."**

Your boss leans in, but his face is now a swirling vortex of Excel spreadsheets and unread Slack messages. "Clock's ticking," he says, except the words come out as a dial-up modem screech.

You reach for your mouse. It scurries away like a startled mouse (because it is now, literally, a mouse wearing tiny sneakers). Your keyboard melts into warm caramel. Colleagues morph into office plants that whisper productivity tips in Latin. The fluorescent lights start pulsing in Morse code: *YOU ARE DREAMING YOU ARE DREAMING YOU ARE—*

You try to stand up. Gravity inverts. You're suddenly floating upside-down above your cubicle while the impossible deadline chases you like a cartoon anvil with legs, yelling "But what about the quarterly OKRs?!"

Just as the anvil is about to crush you, your phone rings. It's your future self calling from 2027. "Hey, don't worry about the espresso machine. I figured it out. Also, invest in pineapples."

You wake up in your actual bed, heart racing, with one sock on and the faint smell of burnt coffee in the air. The impossible project? Still impossible. But at least the deadline is now... whenever you decide to open your laptop.
