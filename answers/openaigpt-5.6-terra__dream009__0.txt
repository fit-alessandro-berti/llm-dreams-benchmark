…survive long enough to understand why you were brought here.

The village beyond the hill is made of timber, smoke, and candlelight. People stare at your clothes as though you have stepped out of a prophecy. There are no phones, no engines, no familiar voices—only the sound of wagon wheels on mud and a church bell counting an hour you cannot name.

At first, you try to explain. You say you are from the future.

They laugh.

Then an old woman takes your hand and whispers, “No. You are from the dream before this one.”

That night, beneath a sky brighter than any you have ever seen, you discover a strange mark on your palm: a small, glowing circle that pulses whenever you remember home. And with every pulse, the world around you changes slightly—a road appears where there was none, a face becomes familiar, a distant tower draws closer.

You begin to understand: you have not traveled into the past.

You have been sent here to prevent something from waking.
