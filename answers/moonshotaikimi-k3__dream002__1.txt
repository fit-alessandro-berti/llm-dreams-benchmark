...the air grows thick and silver, the way it only does in dreams. The trees begin leaning toward each other, whispering in a language you almost understand. You realize the storm isn't coming from the sky—it's rising up from the ground. Mist curls between the roots like slow lightning.

You start to run, but the path keeps rearranging itself behind you. Each step lands softer than it should, as if the earth is breathing. Then, through the trees, you see a small house you've never seen before but somehow recognize—the house from a childhood you can't quite remember, warm yellow light glowing in the window.

The rain begins, but it's falling *upward*. Drops rise past your face toward the canopy, each one holding a tiny reflection of the whole forest inside it. Strangely, you aren't afraid. You understand, with the perfect logic of dreams, that the storm is searching for something—and it isn't you.

The door of the house is open. Inside, someone is humming a tune you know but can't name. You sense that if you step across the threshold, you'll remember something important, something you knew long before you were born. Your hand reaches for the doorframe. The humming stops. A voice says your name the way it was spoken to you when you were very small—

And that's where the dream begins to dissolve, the way dreams do, leaving only the faint smell of rain and the feeling that you were just about to understand everything.
