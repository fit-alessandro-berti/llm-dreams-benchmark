First it drinks the blue from the sky, then the green from the impossible trees, then the silver from the wind as it passes. Your fingers hover over its surface, but before you touch it, the canvas begins to dream too.

A single line appears, trembling like a thought not yet decided. It curves into a door. The door opens into a room made of light, and inside the room every wall is painting itself in real time: mountains becoming birds, shadows becoming music, colors changing places as if they’ve forgotten their names.

You step closer, and the blank canvas whispers, “Finish me.”

So you do—not with paint, but with a feeling. You give it the memory of a sunset you never saw, the shape of a word you almost remember, the softness of a place that doesn’t exist. The canvas blooms. Spirals rise like flowers, edges melt into starlight, and the whole thing becomes a living map of your dreaming mind.

Then, just as you realize you are part of the painting too, the canvas turns white again.

And somewhere behind you, the world begins to sketch itself anew.
