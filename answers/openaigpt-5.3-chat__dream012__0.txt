…tighten your grip on the wheel as the wind screams through the rigging. The deck tilts hard to port; somewhere behind you a crate breaks loose and skids across the planks.

“Captain?” someone shouts. You can’t see who. Rain lashes your eyes like thrown sand.

The compass spins uselessly.

Lightning tears open the sky and for a moment the ocean is bright as day. In that flash you see something impossible ahead of the bow—an opening in the storm, a perfectly still circle of water, black and glassy while mountains of waves rage around it.

Your crew sees it too. No one speaks.

“Hold her steady,” you say, though you’re not sure if you mean the ship or yourself.

Another wave rises, taller than the mast, its crest glowing faintly as if lit from within. The ship climbs it like a stubborn animal. The wheel fights you. The wood groans. The wind suddenly drops, just for a breath.

In that quiet, you hear music drifting from the calm water ahead.

Not from an instrument—more like the sea itself humming.

The storm surges again, but now the ship is already sliding down the far side of the wave, straight toward that silent circle in the ocean. Your crew waits for your command.

You realize you have no memory of how this voyage began.

Only the strange certainty that whatever waits in that still water has been calling your name for a very long time.
