You are participating in a competition where the grand prize is your dream job. As the competition progresses, you face various challenges and begin to realize that none of them test the skills you expected.

The first challenge is to cross a ballroom without touching the floor. The chandeliers swing low enough to grab, so you leap from crystal to crystal while judges in silver masks silently take notes. In the second, you must persuade a locked door to open by telling it something true. You whisper a secret you have never admitted to yourself, and the door sighs before letting you through.

With each round, the other competitors disappear—not because they lose, but because they find doors labeled with things they want more: *A Quiet Life*, *Someone Who Understands You*, *Another Chance*. Eventually, you are the only one left.

The final challenge takes place in a perfect replica of your childhood bedroom. On the desk sits a contract for your dream job. The title is exactly right. The salary is beyond anything you imagined. At the bottom, however, the signature line bears a strange condition:

**To accept, you must remain here and continue dreaming.**

Then the closet door opens.

Inside is the life you would actually have: difficult mornings, uncertain choices, ordinary meals, laughter you cannot predict, and work that matters in ways no title could capture. It is messy and unfinished. No judges are waiting there. No one will tell you whether you have won.

Behind you, the masked audience begins to applaud.

You pick up the contract, fold it into a paper airplane, and throw it toward the ceiling. It circles once, catches fire, and becomes a small golden bird.

The bird flies into the closet.

You follow it.

As you step through, you hear an alarm ringing somewhere far away. Just before you wake, a voice announces the winner.

It says your name—but somehow, it sounds like you saying it.
