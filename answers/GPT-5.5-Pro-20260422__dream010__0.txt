…and discover that each challenge is less about proving you are better than everyone else and more about remembering who you are.

The first round takes place in a glass maze suspended above a city of clouds. Every corridor shows a different version of your future: one where you are wildly successful but alone, one where you play it safe and never fail, one where you keep waiting for permission to begin. Competitors sprint past you, chasing the brightest reflections, but you notice a small door with no handle. On it are the words: **What are you willing to learn?**

You press your palm against the glass, and the door opens.

Beyond it, the competition becomes stranger. You are asked to solve problems with tools that keep changing shape. A pencil becomes a key. A keyboard becomes a garden. A clock melts into a compass. Every time you panic, the room grows darker; every time you breathe and try again, the walls lift a little higher.

In the final round, you stand before a panel of judges whose faces are hidden in shadow. They ask you to present your greatest accomplishment.

You begin to list achievements, awards, skills, all the things you think they want to hear. But the words fall from your mouth like paper birds and scatter across the floor.

Then you stop.

You tell them about the times you failed and kept going. You tell them about the work you did when no one was watching. You tell them about the quiet stubborn hope that brought you here. As you speak, the judges’ shadows dissolve, and you realize they all have your face: younger, older, frightened, confident, tired, brave.

The final judge smiles and hands you an envelope.

Inside is not a job offer, but a mirror.

At first, you feel cheated. Then the mirror ripples like water, and behind your reflection you see a door opening into a bright office, studio, lab, stage, workshop—some place that is all of these and none of them. On the desk is your name, already written there, as if it has been waiting for you.

A voice says, “The prize was never the job. The prize was becoming the person who could walk into it.”

You step through the door.

Just as your hand touches the desk, your alarm rings.

You wake up with the strange certainty that although it was only a dream, some part of you has already accepted the offer.
