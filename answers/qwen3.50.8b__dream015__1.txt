Winter was not cold; it was frozen, a blanket of ice around the earth that seemed to swallow the shadows. Dust settled like snow into a world that had been cut off from the main stream, the air thin and heavy with dry heat and ash.

Silas looked at his hands, those hands that usually held the weight of his burden now felt light against his skin. He moved forward until he found the hollow, cracked place where it should be watered by a drought-drenched world. It was small, dark, and smelled of nothing but ozone and cold iron.

He didn't know what to do with his hands or where to turn. The wind whispered in his ears at an angle that seemed impossible; there was no sun above the horizon that would warm him. But he could feel the ground beneath him hummed, a rhythmic pulse that felt alive. It was the heartbeat of the world itself, waiting for a response that none had given yet.

Silas took the first step. He didn't look back to see if there were others in this place. He moved into the silence where no one could see unless he walked into it. He placed his hand on the cold ground and let his breath fog the earth.

He realized the world did not demand him. It demanded that he find something new for survival. The decision was simple, but the landscape demanded a different answer to survive.

Silas stood before the first light that appeared just beyond the edge of the darkness, and in that moment, the cold stopped being an enemy and became a memory.
