You are in a world where colors and shapes are constantly changing. As you explore this surreal landscape, you come across a blank canvas and a brush floating above it.

The brush moves before you touch it, painting a door in the middle of the white space. When you open it, you find another version of the world—quieter, made entirely of soft blue shadows. In the distance stands a house with no walls, only windows, and in every window you see a different memory.

You step closer. One window shows a childhood summer. Another shows a future you have never lived. In the last, you see yourself standing here, watching yourself look into the window.

The brush suddenly drops to the ground.

You pick it up and realize the canvas is no longer blank. It holds a single unfinished line, stretching toward the horizon. You follow it through forests of glass, across rivers flowing upward, and beneath a moon that changes shape each time you blink.

At last, the line leads back to you.

You understand then that the landscape has been waiting for you to give it a name. So you dip the brush into the changing sky and paint one word across the canvas:

**Awake.**

The world pauses.

Then everything becomes white—not empty, but full of possibility—and somewhere beyond the silence, something begins to dream you back.
