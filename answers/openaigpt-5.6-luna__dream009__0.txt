You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to find a way back home.

The streets are narrow and crowded with people dressed in unfamiliar clothes. Horse-drawn carts rattle over the stone roads, while merchants call out from wooden stalls piled high with fruit, spices, and strange mechanical objects. Above you, banners bearing a silver crescent flutter from the rooftops.

You reach into your pocket and find the only thing that came with you: a small glass key, glowing faintly blue.

An old woman notices it and grabs your wrist.

“You have the key,” she whispers. “Then the door has opened again.”

Before you can ask what she means, the bells of the city begin to ring. Everyone stops. The sky darkens, though the sun has not moved, and a crack of golden light appears in the air at the end of the street.

The old woman points toward it.

“Go now. Whatever you see, do not speak your name.”

You run.

The glowing doorway grows larger with every step, revealing flashes of your own world—the room where you fell asleep, the clock beside your bed, the pale light of morning. But just as you reach it, someone calls your name from behind.

You turn instinctively.

Standing in the middle of the street is another version of you, dressed in the clothes of that ancient era. They are holding a second glass key.

“You can’t go back,” they say. “Not until you remember why you came here.”

The doorway begins to close.

And suddenly, you remember: this is not the first time you have woken in this world.
