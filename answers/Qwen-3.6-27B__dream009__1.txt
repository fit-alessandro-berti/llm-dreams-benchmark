...learn the language of the wind before you can speak to anyone.

The air here is thick with a scent like crushed pine needles and old parchment. You are standing in a courtyard paved with stones that hum faintly under your bare feet. The architecture is impossible—towers spiral upward like frozen smoke, defying gravity, connected by bridges made of woven light rather than wood or stone.

You look down at your hands. They are translucent, shimmering with a faint blue luminescence. You aren't just *in* this era; you are becoming part of its physics.

A figure approaches from the shadows of an archway. It has no face, only a smooth surface reflecting the sky above. It doesn’t walk so much as glide, leaving trails of floating petals in its wake. It raises a hand, not in greeting, but in warning.

From the corner of your eye, you see the horizon begin to unravel. The sky is peeling back like old wallpaper, revealing gears and clockwork beneath the clouds. Time isn't linear here—it’s a fabric, and it’s tearing.

To survive, you realize you must do the one thing modern logic forbids: **you must forget who you were.**

The faceless figure extends its hand toward your chest. If you take it, you will lose your memories of smartphones, electricity, and your name. But if you refuse, the unraveling sky will consume you entirely.

What do you choose?
