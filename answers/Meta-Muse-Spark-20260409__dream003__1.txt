You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and **symbols that seem to shift when you're not looking directly at them**. 

The metal is cold, older than anything you’ve touched, but there’s no rust. No hinges either. Just one seamless seam running around the edge, and in the center, a circular indentation shaped like a hand. 

Without thinking, you press your palm to it. 

The patterns stop shifting. They lock into place and start to glow faintly blue, like moonlight trapped under ice. The box is warm now. It hums — not a sound, but a feeling in your teeth and your chest, like a memory trying to surface. 

Then you hear it: a *click*. Not from the box. From somewhere behind you. 

The box lid doesn’t open. Instead, the entire thing dissolves into light in your hands, weightless, and leaves behind a single folded piece of paper. 

You unfold it. In your own handwriting, it says: 

*“You weren’t supposed to open it from the outside.”*

When you look up, the room you’re in isn’t the one you started in. The walls are bookshelves that stretch too high to see the top. And every book has your name on the spine. 

What do you do next?
