You see a familiar silhouette near a fountain—your friend—but as you call out, the fountain's water freezes mid‑spray, and the silhouette turns to reveal a mirror where their face should be. In the reflection, you see yourself, but older, holding a key that glows faintly. The mirror cracks, and a voice from behind you says, "You've been looking for the wrong person." You spin around, but now you're alone in an empty ballroom, the only sound the ticking of a giant pocket watch dangling from the chandelier. The dream hums, waiting for you to decide which way to walk.
