You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to **blend in immediately** before the locals decide your strange clothes and bewildered expression mean you're either a god, a spy, or dinner.

The air smells of woodsmoke, wet stone, and something roasting that makes your stomach growl despite the confusion. You're standing in a bustling market square where the year feels somewhere between 1350 and 1780—it's deliberately fuzzy, the way dreams love to be. A woman in a heavy wool dress eyes you suspiciously. A blacksmith is hammering something that rings like a church bell. No phones. No cars. No escape hatch.

You realize you need to **speak their language**, but the words that come out of your mouth are strangely fluent, like the dream is dubbing you in real time. "Good morrow," you hear yourself say, and it feels both ridiculous and completely natural. Your modern accent is gone. In its place is something that makes the woman smile instead of scream.

That's when the dream gets playful.

A small boy tugs at your sleeve and whispers, "The old one said you'd come. Said you'd have no shadow at noon." You look down. Sure enough, the sun is directly overhead and the ground beneath you is suspiciously well-lit. No shadow. The dream is showing off.

You spend the next few hours (or minutes—dream time is drunk) learning to trade a silver button from your jeans for a warm loaf of bread and a cloak that smells like herbs and sheep. Every time you think "I need my phone," the dream gently mocks you by making a nearby raven tilt its head and croak what sounds suspiciously like "no signal."

Eventually you find yourself sitting on an ancient stone wall as the sun begins to set, eating bread that somehow tastes better than anything you've had in the real world. A quiet, dangerous thought appears: *What if I just stay?*

The moment you think it, the world starts to soften at the edges like watercolor. The market sounds grow distant. The woman from earlier walks by one last time and gives you a knowing look, as if she understands exactly what's about to happen.

You wake up with the taste of that bread still in your mouth and the distinct feeling that somewhere, in some other layer of reality, you're still sitting on that wall—perfectly content, shadowless, and finally, blissfully unreachable.
