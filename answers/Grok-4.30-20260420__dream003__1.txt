You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and ancient symbols that seem to shift and dance under your gaze, pulsing with a soft, ethereal glow like starlight trapped in wood. The surface feels warm, almost alive, humming with a quiet energy that vibrates through your fingertips.

You lean closer. The runes begin to rearrange themselves, forming fleeting images: a doorway opening onto endless night skies, rivers of liquid moonlight, faces you almost recognize but cannot name. A faint melody rises from inside the box—half lullaby, half warning—woven from forgotten languages and the laughter of dreams.

Your breath catches. The lock, which had no keyhole, simply dissolves into silver mist. The lid lifts by itself with a sound like distant wind chimes. Inside is not gold or jewels, but a swirling vortex of colors and memories: your childhood bedroom floating among clouds, a future version of yourself waving from a mountaintop made of glass, entire civilizations rising and falling in the space of a single heartbeat.

You reach in without hesitation. The moment your hand crosses the threshold, the room around you melts away. You are falling upward through a sky of liquid time, the box now orbiting you like a small planet, its patterns still glowing, still whispering.

In this dream, the box was never truly locked. It was only waiting for someone willing to believe that mystery itself is the key. And as you drift deeper, carried by the melody, you understand that every dream you have ever had was secretly stored inside this very box—patiently, lovingly, ready for the night you finally chose to open it. 

The patterns keep shifting. The melody never ends. And somewhere in the distance, another you is already reaching for the lid.
