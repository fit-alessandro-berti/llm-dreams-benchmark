You realize you don’t need a brush. You simply think of a color — warm, honeyed gold — and it blooms across the canvas, not painted but blooming like a sunrise under the surface. Then a shape emerges, not drawn but *remembered*: a spiral, twisting inward like a galaxy or a seashell, its edges crisp where they meet the teal background.

But this world doesn’t let you finish. The spiral begins to move, unwinding itself, and the colors bleed beyond the edges of the canvas. They seep into the air, becoming three-dimensional — the gold now a gentle, hovering light, the teal mist that swirls around your feet. The canvas itself seems to thin, becoming a window rather than a surface.

Through it, you see a different layer of the dream: a forest of towering, translucent geometric shapes — prisms of rose quartz and citrine — chiming softly in a wind you can’t feel. The spiral you made is now a path, weaving between them.

Do you follow it, or stay here with the breathing canvas?
