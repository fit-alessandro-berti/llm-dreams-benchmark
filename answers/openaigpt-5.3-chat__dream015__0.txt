You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to follow the thin column of smoke rising beyond the broken skyline. Smoke means fire, and fire means people.

The streets are quiet except for the wind pushing dust through the skeletons of cars. Your pack is light, your canteen lighter. Every step feels louder than it should. Somewhere in the distance, metal clatters and echoes like a warning.

When you finally reach the source, you find a small camp hidden inside the ruins of an old library. A handful of strangers sit around a careful fire, cooking something that smells almost like soup. They look up when you appear in the doorway—tired eyes, cautious hands.

For a long moment no one speaks.

Then someone scoots over and pats an empty spot beside the fire.

In a world that ended, it feels strangely like the beginning of something.
