You grip the wheel, knuckles white against the salt-slick wood. Rain lashes your face like needles of ice, but you don't flinch. You can't. Not now.

"Reef the main sail!" you roar over the howling wind. The words tear from your throat, barely audible even to yourself. But your first mate, Old Tomas, hears. He always does. He signals to the rigging crew above, and they scramble like spiders across the ropes, fighting canvas that wants to kill them.

A wave rises before you — black, mountainous, impossibly tall. It blocks out the lightning-choked sky. For one terrible heartbeat, you are certain it will swallow the ship whole.

*Hold course. Hold course.*

You turn the wheel into the wave. The bow lifts. The deck tilts to forty degrees. Cargo shifts below with a groan like a wounded animal. Someone cries out. Someone prays.

Then the crest breaks beneath you and the ship slides down the other side, shuddering, groaning — but whole.

"Tomas!" you shout. He appears at your elbow, sea-spray streaming down his scarred face. "Get every hand below deck except the watch. No one sleeps in the rigging tonight."

"And you, Captain?"

You stare into the darkness ahead, where the next wave is already forming.

"I'll be here."

The dream shifts. Time becomes liquid. The storm lasts an hour, or a lifetime. But gradually — almost imperceptibly — the swells grow shorter. The rain thins to mist. A pale line appears on the horizon.

Dawn.

Your crew emerges on deck, blinking, salt-crusted, alive. They look at you. You realize your hands are trembling now. You let go of the wheel. Tomas catches it.

"Land ho," someone whispers from the crow's nest.

And ahead, green hills rise from the sea like a promise kept.
