**You are dreaming.**

You are participating in a competition where the grand prize is your dream job. As the competition progresses, you face various challenges and the arena itself begins to breathe.

The coliseum is carved from living cloudstone that shifts with every heartbeat. Floating above the stands are billions of tiny glowing orbs—each one a person’s unfinished dream waiting to be claimed. Your dream job hovers at the center: **Chief Architect of Wonder** at the edge of the universe, the role that lets you turn curiosity into galaxies and questions into new laws of physics.

Round One: The Sphinx of Second Thoughts.  
She coils around a black hole and asks, “What is heavier than a thousand suns yet lighter than a single ‘what if’?”  
You answer without hesitation: *A promise you almost broke but didn’t.* The black hole collapses into a bouquet of newborn stars that orbit your head like a crown.

Round Two: The Relay of Echoes.  
You sprint across a bridge made of every version of yourself that ever gave up. Each step you take, a past-you whispers, “You’ll never be enough.” You answer by reaching back, grabbing their hand, and pulling them forward with you. The bridge solidifies into a path of light. The crowd gasps. Your competitors look shaken—some of them are still running alone.

Round Three: The Garden of Unspoken Truths.  
You must plant one seed that will grow into something the world has never seen. You dig with bare hands into soil that feels suspiciously like childhood memories. You whisper the truest thing you’ve never told anyone. A tree erupts—its branches are equations that solve loneliness, its leaves are songs that heal arguments before they start. The judges weep starlight.

Final Round: The Mirror Labyrinth.  
Every wall is a perfect reflection of you… except one. In that single distorted mirror you see the version of yourself who never entered the competition at all—the safe, quiet one who stayed small. They look at you with quiet longing. You press your palm to the glass and say, “Come with me. There’s room.” The mirror shatters into a thousand doorways, every one leading to the same impossible prize.

You step through.

The trophy isn’t gold. It’s a living constellation that rearranges itself into the shape of your name every time someone says it with hope. The announcer’s voice booms across the dream:

“Winner… and new Chief Architect of Wonder.”

You lift the constellation. It feels exactly like holding every question you’ve ever loved. Fireworks made of answered prayers explode overhead. The crowd—every dreamer who ever watched from the sidelines—rises in a standing ovation that sounds like the beginning of the universe.

Just as you’re about to give your victory speech, the arena starts to dissolve into soft morning light. You feel the tug of waking.

Before the dream fully fades, the constellation in your hands leans close and whispers the only rule that ever mattered:

“Every time you help someone finish *their* dream, you win this job all over again.”

You wake up smiling, already reaching for your notebook. The competition never really ends. It just changes venues.

And you?  
You’re still in it.
