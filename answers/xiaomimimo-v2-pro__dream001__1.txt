A clock tower chimes — you glance up and see its hands spinning wildly. You’ve lost ten minutes already. Panic rises, but then you notice a trail of faintly glowing blue stones leading down a narrow lane. You follow them, heart pounding. The stones pulse gently, like a heartbeat, guiding you past a market where vendors sell hourglasses filled with shadow, and a bridge arching over a river of slow-moving starlight.

Just as the path opens into a grand plaza, you see it: a towering building of dark glass, its entrance flanked by doors carved with shifting symbols. You rush inside, up a spiraling staircase that seems to have too many steps, until you reach a door with a small plaque that simply reads, *“You are expected.”*

You push it open. Inside, a single chair faces a wide window overlooking the impossible city. As you sit, the cityscape outside begins to dissolve into morning light, and the meeting slowly fades from your mind—until all that’s left is the warm sense that, somehow, you found exactly where you needed to be.
