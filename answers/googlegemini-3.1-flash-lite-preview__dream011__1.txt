The professor walks down the aisle, his footsteps making no sound. He stops beside me. He has no face, only a smooth, polished surface reflecting my own frantic expression back at me. He leans in close, and his voice doesn't come from a mouth, but vibrates from the air itself, smelling of ozone and old parchment.

"You have been studying the wrong subject," he whispers, his voice sounding like dry leaves skittering on pavement. "This isn't a test of your knowledge. It’s a test of your willingness to vanish."

I look back down at the exam. The shifting symbols have settled into a single, terrifying sentence: *Describe the color of the room you were in before you were born.*

My heart hammers against my ribs, echoing through the empty hall like a drum. I try to pull my hand away, but the desk has dissolved into a deep, endless indigo fog. I realize then that the paper isn't a test at all—it's a map. And as the ink spreads up my arms, I realize that I am not just taking the exam; I am the ink, and I am finally beginning to fade.
