One stranger carries a lantern that glows with warm blue light. Another hums a tune you somehow recognize, though you can’t remember where you first heard it. As you walk together, the noise of the city softens, and the towering buildings lean back to reveal a sky full of slow-moving stars.

They lead you to a quiet square with a fountain at its center. In the water, you see not your reflection, but a map—glimmering paths, familiar places, and one bright point pulsing gently like a heartbeat.

“That’s where you were trying to go,” one of them says.

You look closer and realize the bright point is not a place at all, but a feeling: home.

When you turn to thank the strangers, they are smiling, fading into the golden morning light. The city no longer feels frightening. The streets still twist and turn, but now you know you are not truly lost.

You take a deep breath, step forward, and wake with the feeling that someone, somewhere, is quietly guiding you.
