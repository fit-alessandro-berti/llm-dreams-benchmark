You try to pull your hands away, but your fingers have fused to the desk, which is slowly turning into the texture of warm, rising bread dough. 

"We need the Q3 projections by yesterday," your boss says. You look up at him, but his face is blurring. His features slide down his skull like wet paint, and his tie has transformed into a swinging grandfather clock pendulum. *Tick. Tock. Tick. Tock.* 

"I can't," you try to say, but when you open your mouth, a flock of tiny, folded origami birds flies out of your throat instead of words. They flutter around the fluorescent lights, which are now humming the tune of "Pop Goes the Weasel."

Your boss doesn't seem to notice the birds. He taps his pendulum-tie against your monitor. "Just open the spreadsheet," he whispers, his voice sounding like it's coming from deep underwater. 

You manage to peel your hands free from the dough-desk and click the mouse. The screen doesn't show Excel. It shows a live feed of a vast, endless ocean. Suddenly, a giant, spectral koi fish swims up to the "camera," opens its mouth, and projects a hologram of the project brief. 

The brief doesn't ask for financial projections. It reads: *Task 1: Count the grains of sand on the moon. Task 2: Translate the color blue into a symphony. Task 3: Do not let the geometry escape.*

"Get to it," your boss says. He begins to shrink, folding in on himself like a piece of laundry until he is just a neatly stacked pile of manila folders sitting in his office chair. 

Panic sets in. You stand up to run, but the office carpet has become a thick, sticky molasses. You look toward the exit, but the door is now a mile away, and the hallway is stretching like taffy. The cubicle walls around you begin to breathe, expanding and contracting. 

From the breakroom, the coffee machine starts screaming. 

You realize with sudden, absolute dream-logic clarity that you are completely naked, and you forgot to feed your childhood pet hamster, who is somehow the CEO of the company. You try to run toward the distant door, but gravity suddenly shifts ninety degrees. 

You fall sideways, crashing through the drywall, plummeting down into a sky made of blue graph paper. As the wind rushes past your ears, a giant red stamp descends from the clouds, slamming onto the sky above you. It reads: **PAST DUE.**

You jolt awake, heart pounding, tangled in your bedsheets, only to realize your alarm clock is blinking 7:00 AM, and you actually *do* have a project due today.
