You find yourself in a vast, unfamiliar city, and you realize you have an important meeting in an hour but no idea where it is. You start to panic, but then you notice the streets aren't made of asphalt — they're rippling water, reflecting a sky with three moons. 

Your phone buzzes. No map, just a single line of text: **"Follow the silver thread."**

You look down. Looped around your wrist is a thread, thin as spider silk, glowing faintly silver. It tugs, gentle but insistent, leading you through alleys where the buildings lean in to whisper. The signs are in a language you don't know, yet you understand them: *The Clockwork Café. The Library of Unwritten Letters. The Plaza of Second Chances.*

The thread pulls you onto a tram that has no driver. Inside, the other passengers are all versions of you — you at 7, you at 17, you with gray hair you don't have yet. None of them speak, but older-you slides a folded note into your hand. 

It reads: *You’re not late. The meeting is with yourself. Room 0. It moves.*

You check the time. 57 minutes left. The tram doors open to a stairwell that descends into clouds. The silver thread dives down, unraveling from your wrist as you follow. With each step, the city above gets quieter and your own heartbeat gets louder, like it's syncing with a drum somewhere below.

At the bottom: a single door with no number. It opens before you touch it.

Inside is a round table, one chair, and a mirror. The thread ends at the mirror’s surface. In the reflection, you’re already sitting down, smiling like you’ve been waiting.

The you-in-the-mirror mouths something. It takes you a second to read your own lips: *Ready to remember why you came here?*

The hour chimes — all three moons ring like bells — and the door behind you dissolves into light.

What do you do next?
