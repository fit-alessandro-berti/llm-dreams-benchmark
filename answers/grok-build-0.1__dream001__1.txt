You start to walk faster, scanning the towering glass buildings for any clue—a street sign, a familiar landmark, anything. But the city warps around you: sidewalks ripple like water, and the people you pass have faces that shift between strangers and old friends you haven't seen in years. Your phone is dead, and every time you try to ask for directions, the words come out in a language you've never heard before. A cab pulls up unbidden. The driver, who looks exactly like your childhood pet dog dressed in a suit, grins and says, "Hop in. The meeting's already started."

You climb in. The car doesn't drive on roads—it glides straight up the side of a skyscraper, windows flashing with scenes from your life: missed deadlines, half-remembered conversations, that one time you aced something you thought you'd fail. Time stretches and folds. What feels like an hour passes in seconds. You arrive at the top floor, burst through the door into a conference room where everyone is waiting... and they're all versions of you, from every age. They applaud politely as you take your seat, and the agenda on the screen simply reads: "Why are you always late to your own life?"

The room dissolves into light. You jolt awake in your actual bed, heart pounding, the details already slipping away like sand through fingers.
