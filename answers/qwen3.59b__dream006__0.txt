...and you begin to speak, but your voice refuses to become sound. Instead, as air leaves your lungs, words materialize like silver bubbles that drift upward and vanish before reaching the ceiling. The script in your hands dissolves into ink that leaks onto the floor, turning wet where it touches the wood. You look down at your arms; you are no longer wearing a suit jacket, but a coat woven from midnight shadows.

The audience begins to applaud, yet when you listen closely, their clapping sounds like leaves skittering over pavement in a dry forest rather than fingers against skin. Every time a face in the crowd blurs and turns into a mask, then immediately shifts back into a human expression just as quickly as a flickering candle flame. You reach for the microphone to steady your grip, but it has transformed into a heavy stone that feels warm, pulsing with a quiet heartbeat. You open your mouth one more time, but this time you realize the stage is no longer flat; you are standing on the edge of a cliff overlooking an ocean of clouds, and the speech you have been trying to give is simply the sound of wind moving through the sails of a ship that has already sailed away.
