You follow them through winding alleys lit by glowing paper lanterns that float just above the sidewalk, casting warm pools of amber light in the humid night air. The strangers don’t speak much—just smiles, gentle gestures, a hand placed briefly on your shoulder to guide you around a corner, a shared loaf of bread passed to you wrapped in a cloth embroidered with constellations you don’t recognize.

One of them, an old woman with silver-streaked hair tied in a braid that reaches her waist, murmurs, “The city doesn’t forget anyone. It just waits for you to remember you belong here.”

As you turn a corner into a square you’ve never seen before, the crowd thins. In the center stands a massive, ancient tree with roots like twisted staircases and leaves made of shifting stained glass. Beneath it, a mirror stands—no frame, just floating glass. The stranger who brought you here points to it and says, “Look closely. You’ve been searching for the path. But the path was you.”

You step forward, heart pounding.

Your reflection doesn’t look like you.

It’s younger. Smiling. Eyes bright. Wearing the same clothes you wore yesterday—the ones you thought you lost. Behind it, the city hums—not as a place of noise and anonymity, but as a living thing, breathing in rhythm with your pulse.

Tears come without warning.

You don’t need to ask how to get home anymore.

Because you’re already there.

And when you wake, your hand is still outstretched toward the empty space beside you in bed… and the pillow smells faintly of jasmine and rain.
