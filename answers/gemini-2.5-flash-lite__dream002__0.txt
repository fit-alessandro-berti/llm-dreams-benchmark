Okay, here's how my dream continues from "You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and..."

...and the leaves above, which were moments ago a dappled emerald under a gentle sun, begin to writhe and twist with an unnatural energy. The wind, previously a soft whisper, transforms into a low, guttural moan that seems to emanate from the very roots of the ancient trees. It’s not just the sound; it’s a feeling that presses against my chest, a pressure that makes my breath catch.

The sky, a serene blue a heartbeat ago, is now a bruised, angry purple. Jagged veins of lightning, silent at first, begin to snake across the darkening canvas, illuminating the trees in stark, fleeting flashes. The peaceful rustling of leaves is replaced by a frantic, chaotic clatter as they are ripped from their branches and flung through the air like desperate birds.

Rain doesn't just start; it *arrives*. Not in drops, but in sheets, thick and heavy, like a waterfall unleashed. It’s cold, stinging against my skin, and instantly soaks through my clothes, clinging to me like a second, unwelcome skin. The forest floor, once soft with moss and pine needles, quickly becomes a muddy, churning torrent, swallowing the path I was following.

A tremor runs through the ground, subtle but undeniable. It’s not an earthquake, not a violent shaking, but more like a deep, resonant hum that vibrates up through my feet. The trees groan, their trunks swaying precariously, their branches thrashing with a violence that feels personal, as if they're trying to push me away.

Fear, a cold, sharp thing, begins to prickle at the edges of my mind. It’s not just the physical danger of the storm, though that's certainly present. It’s the *feeling* of the storm. It feels sentient, angry. Like the forest itself has decided to turn on me.

Then, I see it. Through the blinding downpour and the swirling leaves, at the edge of my vision, a single, massive oak tree, older and larger than any other, begins to glow with an eerie, internal luminescence. The light isn't natural; it pulses with a deep, emerald green, mirroring the color of the leaves that were just moments ago so peaceful. The moaning wind seems to concentrate around this tree, swirling faster, forming a miniature vortex at its base.

And from the depths of that glowing vortex, I hear a sound that is not wind, not thunder, but something far older and far more profound. It’s a voice, deep and resonant, speaking a language I don’t understand, yet somehow, I feel its message directed at me. It's a summons, a warning, a challenge.

My feet, though sinking in the mud, are drawn towards the glowing oak. The rain continues to lash, the wind howls, but the fear begins to morph into a strange, compelling curiosity. I have to know what this storm, this forest, and this ancient, glowing tree want from me. I take another step forward, the glowing light beckoning me deeper into the heart of the furious, awakened woods.
