You reach out, expecting the rough texture of linen, but your fingers sink into the surface as if it were warm water. There is no paint, no brush, only the impulse. You realize that in this place, intention is the only medium that matters.

You think of a memory: the smell of rain on hot pavement. Instantly, the canvas shimmers. A streak of grey-blue erupts from your fingertip, not staying flat, but rising into the air like smoke, solidifying into a three-dimensional storm cloud that hovers just above the frame.

You laugh, and the sound turns into a flock of geometric birds that scatter across the white void, leaving trails of gold dust.

The world around you begins to slow. The melting shapes freeze in mid-transformation. The colors dim, turning from neon brilliance to soft, pastel twilight. You understand now that the canvas isn't waiting to be filled; it is waiting to be *seen*. It is a mirror that doesn't reflect your face, but your potential.

You step back, and the canvas begins to glow from within. The white light expands, swallowing the shifting landscape, the melting ground, and the humming sky. It doesn't feel like an end, but like a deep breath held too long, finally released.

As the light becomes absolute, you hear a voice that sounds like your own, whispering from the edges of your mind: *"Now, you are awake."*

And you are.
