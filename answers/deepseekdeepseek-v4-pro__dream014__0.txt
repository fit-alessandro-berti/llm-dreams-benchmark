You gratefully accept their offer, and as you walk together, the city around you begins to shift. The neon signs and faceless crowds soften into a familiar path you remember from childhood—a cobblestone lane lined with glowing lanterns. The strangers’ voices become warm and oddly recognizable, like echoes of old friends. One of them hands you a small, folded map, but when you open it, it’s blank except for a single handwritten sentence: “You’ve always known the way.” When you look up to thank them, they’ve vanished, but you’re no longer lost—you’re standing outside the house where you grew up, and a light is on in the window, waiting just for you.
