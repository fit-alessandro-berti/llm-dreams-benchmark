You approach the canvas, your footsteps leaving brief, shimmering impressions on the ground that melt back into shifting hues. The air itself feels thick with potential, humming softly. You reach out and touch the canvas—and it’s not paint or pencil you find.

The moment your fingertips make contact, the blankness *ripples*. Instead of applying a medium, you find you are drawing *out* what is within it. A line of pure, deep cerulean flows from your touch, not drawn but *summoned*. It swirls, growing into a river that snakes across the surface, but then it leaps off the canvas, becoming a real, flowing body of water that cuts through the landscape, its banks made of floating, geometric moss.

You laugh, a sound that seems to echo as small, floating musical notes. Inspired, you press your palm flat. A burst of golden light explodes from the contact point, solidifying into a sun with soft, pulsing rings that casts warm, dappled light over everything. The light doesn’t just illuminate; it *changes* the colors it touches, weaving new patterns into the very fabric of the world around you.

The shapes in the distance—rolling hills that were once pyramids, trees that resembled spirals—begin to settle and stabilize under this new light. But you sense they are not fixed. They are waiting.

You realize the canvas isn’t a surface to decorate. It’s a mirror, or perhaps a portal, to your own inner landscape. To dream is not just to wander the whims of this world, but to shape it.

What will you think of next to pull into being?
