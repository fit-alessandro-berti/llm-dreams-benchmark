Suddenly, the patterns stop swirling. They align into a single, recognizable shape: an eye. And then, it blinks.

A soft voice, not heard but felt directly in your mind, whispers, *"The key was never meant to be found. It was meant to be remembered."*

You look down at your own hand and realize your fingers are beginning to glow with the same blue light. The lock clicks open—not with a mechanical snap, but with the sound of a deep breath being released. Inside, there is no treasure, no gold, no secret document. There is only a small, perfect mirror.

When you look into it, you don’t see your reflection. You see yourself standing in this exact moment, holding the box, but from a perspective far above, as if watching a scene from a movie. And in that reflection, you notice something behind you in the dream-world that you hadn't seen before: a door made of starlight, slightly ajar, waiting for you to step through.

Do you step toward the mirror, or do you turn to face the door?
