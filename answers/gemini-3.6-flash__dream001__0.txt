Driven by a sudden, instinctual urgency, you break into a run. With every step, the pavement beneath your feet shifts—turning from cold asphalt to warm sand, then to polished marble, and finally into a grid of pure, white light. A golden plaque on the wood reads simply: **ROOM 0:00**.

You reach out and grab the brass handle. It's ice-cold. As you turn it and push the door open, you don't step into an office or a boardroom. You step out onto a narrow ledge suspended over an endless ocean of clouds. 

Sitting at a floating glass table in the middle of the open sky is a single empty chair, waiting for you.
