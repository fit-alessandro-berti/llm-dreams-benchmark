You are in a world where colors and shapes are constantly changing. As you explore this surreal landscape, you come across a blank canvas and an ethereal brush materializes in your hand, formed from swirling nebulae and starlight. Without hesitation, your fingers move across its surface, and with each stroke, impossible pigments erupt—fiery crimson twisting into liquid sapphire, geometric prisms melting into singing dragons of light that lift off the frame and orbit your head like curious fireflies. 

The canvas breathes, pulsing in rhythm with your heartbeat, expanding until it swallows the horizon. You step through the rippling surface and become part of the painting itself: your body shifts from a floating cube of laughter to a river of forgotten memories, then into a flock of winged clocks that fly backward through time. Mountains hum lullabies in ultraviolet, trees grow upside down with roots reaching for distant moons, and every thought you have blooms into new shapes that rewrite reality around you. 

Deeper you tumble, laughing as the dream logic folds in on itself—until the colors begin to fade into the soft glow of waking, leaving only the faint taste of star-paint on your tongue and the whisper that the canvas is still waiting, just behind your eyelids.
