**...find a way to survive without immediately getting burned at the stake.**

You look down and realize you’re wearing soft linen and wool that definitely didn’t come from your closet. The air smells like woodsmoke, wet stone, and something baking with too much rosemary. In the distance you hear the low clang of a blacksmith and the lazy clop of horses on a dirt road. No power lines. No cars. No Wi-Fi. Just the wind and the very real possibility that your hoodie and sneakers are about to get you labeled a witch, a demon, or a very confused jester.

Your modern brain immediately starts screaming *“I need my phone, I need Google, I need coffee—”* but all you have is the knowledge in your head and the clothes on your back.

So you do the only sensible thing a time-lost dreamer can do:

You start walking toward the smoke with the calm confidence of someone who knows how vaccines work, how steel is made, and that washing your hands actually matters. You figure you’ve got roughly three advantages:

1. You remember how to make soap.
2. You know the germ theory of disease.
3. You can draw a halfway decent trebuchet from memory if things get messy.

A woman carrying a basket of leeks stares at you as you pass. You give her your best “I’m a traveling scholar from a faraway land, please don’t set me on fire” smile and say, in your most medieval-sounding voice:

“Fair morning to you, goodwife. Tell me… what year might this be?”

She squints. “The year of our Lord 1347. You hit your head, stranger?”

You feel your stomach drop.

*1347.* The year the Black Death begins its rampage across Europe.

You look at your hands, then at the village ahead, then at the sky as if the dream might suddenly glitch and yeet you back to 2025.

Instead, the dream leans in, gets right in your face, and whispers:

“Well? You gonna do something about it, or are you just going to stand there looking modern?”

And that’s when you realize the dream isn’t just a dream.

It’s a test.
