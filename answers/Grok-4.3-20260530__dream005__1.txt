You are dreaming.

...and the crowd parts like liquid silk, revealing clusters of people who seem to glow with their own private light. The first group you approach is a circle of astronomers in velvet cloaks, standing around a floating globe that spins with impossible constellations. Their voices overlap in a language that tastes like starlight and old paper. One of them, a woman with galaxies in her irises, notices your searching eyes and smiles gently. “The one you seek is always moving,” she says, “but the path to them is made of questions you haven’t asked yet.” Before you can reply, the globe flares and they dissolve into a shimmer of silver dust that swirls around your ankles like curious fish.

You turn toward the next knot of people: a long table where musicians play instruments that have no strings or keys—hollowed moons, whispering flames, a violin made of frozen breath. The music they create is the color of nostalgia. A flutist with a face you almost recognize leans close and offers you a single note on a silver spoon. “Taste it,” he urges. You do, and suddenly you remember the exact laugh of the person you’re looking for, bright and slightly crooked, the way it always made rooms feel smaller and safer. The memory is so sharp it hurts. When you ask where they are, the musicians only laugh in harmony and point upward.

Above you, the ceiling has become a velvet sky full of drifting paper lanterns, each one containing a tiny scene: childhood bedrooms, rainy train stations, arguments that ended in forgiveness. You climb a staircase that wasn’t there a moment ago and find yourself among a group of storytellers who are literally made of words—their arms are paragraphs, their hair is footnotes. They are arguing passionately about whether endings should be happy or true. One of them, a figure whose voice sounds like your own thoughts, hands you a blank page and a pen that writes in light. “Write their name,” it says. You do, and the ink rises off the page, forming a glowing arrow that points through a doorway you hadn’t noticed.

Through the doorway is a quiet garden where time moves sideways. Lanterns hang from trees that hum lullabies. In the center, beneath a fountain that pours liquid moonlight, stands the person you’ve been searching for. They look exactly as you remember—same smile, same slightly tilted head—but when they turn, their eyes are mirrors reflecting every version of you that ever existed. “I was never lost,” they say softly. “I was just waiting for you to stop looking so hard.”

You reach out. Their hand is warm and real and impossible all at once. The music from the impossible instruments swells, the astronomers’ constellations rearrange themselves into a single bright thread connecting you both, and the storytellers begin to clap in perfect, wordless rhythm.

The dream holds you there for one perfect, endless heartbeat.

Then the lanterns dim, the garden folds in on itself like origami, and you begin to surface—carrying with you the strange, sweet certainty that somewhere in the waking world, the person you were looking for is also dreaming of finding you.
