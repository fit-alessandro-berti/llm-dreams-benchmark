The patterns begin to glow with a soft amber light, and you realize the symbols are rearranging themselves, flowing across the surface like water finding its course. They're spelling something—not in any language you've learned, but somehow you understand it anyway, the way you always understand things in dreams:

*"The key was never lost. It was only forgotten."*

The box trembles slightly, and you become aware of a weight in your pocket that wasn't there before. Reaching in, you pull out a small brass key, worn smooth as though handled by a thousand hands across a thousand years. It hums faintly, vibrating in harmony with the box itself.

But here's the strange thing—as you lift the key toward the lock, you hesitate. Some part of you knows that the moment you open it, the dream will shift, the way dreams do. The mystery is somehow more precious than its solution.

You open it anyway.

Inside, there is no treasure, no scroll, no jewel. Instead, you find a small mirror, and in it, you see yourself—but younger, or perhaps older, or perhaps simply *other*. The reflection smiles at you and speaks without sound: *"You've been looking for me."*

The edges of the room begin to soften and blur. The box dissolves into golden light that pools around your feet like luminous sand. You feel yourself rising—or maybe the world is sinking—and somewhere far away, a bell is ringing, or an alarm, or a voice calling your name...

And you realize you're holding the key still, clutched tight in your palm, and you wonder—as the dream unravels into morning—whether it will still be there when you wake.
