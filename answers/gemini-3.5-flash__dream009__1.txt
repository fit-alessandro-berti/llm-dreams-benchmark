...find the scribe who is writing this world into existence before they turn the page.

The stone beneath my bare feet is cold and worn smooth by centuries of footsteps. I am standing in a vast, open-air gallery of pale marble, overlooking a valley of terraced fields and ancient stone towers. The air smells of rosemary, damp earth, and woodsmoke—not the chemical smog of the city I left behind, but a clean, primordial breath of the world. There are no power lines cutting across the violet sky, no hum of distant traffic, no satellites blinking among the stars. Instead, two pale, amber moons hang low on the horizon, casting long, twin shadows behind me.

I look down at my hands. They are calloused, stained with a dark indigo dye. In my pocket, instead of a smartphone, I find only a heavy brass key with no teeth and a small, dried sprig of lavender. 

A sudden, deep-seated urgency sets in—the strange, unquestioned logic of a dream. I am an interloper here, a ghost from a future that hasn’t been written yet. If I stay in the shadows of this era too long, the dream will forget me, and I will dissolve into the background.

I begin to run down the winding, cobblestone streets of the silent city. The houses are built of timber and clay, their windows dark and shuttered. The few people I pass have soft, smudged faces, like charcoal sketches left out in the rain. They do not look at me, but their whispers follow me, sounding like dry autumn leaves scraping across stone.

*“He is looking for the ink,”* they murmur. *“He must find the Scriptorium.”*

Guided by an instinct I didn't know I possessed, I head toward the center of the city, where a colossal tower of dark slate rises to meet the twin moons. The heavy oak doors creak open at my touch, revealing a spiral staircase that seems to stretch infinitely upward.

I climb, the air growing colder and smelling intensely of old vellum and melted beeswax. With every step, I can feel my memories of the modern world—the glare of screens, the sound of sirens, the faces of the people I love—slipping away like water through my fingers. I am forgetting who I was.

At the top of the tower, in a circular room open to the night sky, a single figure sits at a massive wooden table. They wear a hood of deep grey, and their hand moves with feverish speed, scraping a long goose quill across a scroll of thick parchment. 

As I step closer, the figure pauses. The scratching of the quill stops, and a profound, terrifying silence falls over the world. The twin moons freeze in the sky. The wind dies.

The scribe does not turn around, but they speak in a voice that sounds exactly like my own:

*"You are just in time,"* they say, dipping the quill back into the indigo ink. *"I am on the very last paragraph. Tell me how the story of your world ends, before I close the book and wake us both up."*

I open my mouth to speak, but the words are dry. Outside the window, the ancient white city is already beginning to dissolve into a soft, grey mist, and the room starts to fade into the sound of my morning alarm...
