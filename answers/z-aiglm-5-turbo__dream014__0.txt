...and you reach out to take the hand of the nearest stranger. The moment your fingers touch, a profound warmth rushes up your arm, and the harsh, overwhelming noise of the city—the blaring horns, the screeching tires, the chaotic chatter—doesn't stop, but it *changes*. It softens, melting into a rhythmic, comforting hum, like a thousand people whispering a lullaby in perfect unison.

You look up at the strangers, expecting to see their faces, but their features are constantly, beautifully shifting. One moment they look like an old friend from childhood, the next a favorite teacher, then a gentle animal, and finally, just pure, shimmering light shaped like a smile. You realize you aren't afraid of this; in the dream logic, it makes perfect sense. They are guides, wearing the faces of everything that has ever made you feel safe.

"Come," they say, but their voices don't come from their mouths. The word blooms directly inside your mind, accompanied by the smell of rain on dry earth. 

They lead you forward, but the city begins to bend. The concrete pavement turns soft, yielding like moss beneath your bare feet—you don't remember taking off your shoes, but it feels right. The towering skyscrapers stop reaching for a grey sky and begin to lean inward, their glass windows turning into mirrors that reflect a sprawling, violet nebula instead of the street below. 

You aren't walking down a city block anymore; you are moving through a canyon of stars. 

The strangers step aside, forming a circle around a single, ordinary-looking wooden door standing unsupported in the middle of the cosmic avenue. A soft, golden light bleeds from the cracks around its edges. 

"You aren't lost," the guides whisper into your thoughts. "You just outgrew the map."

You reach for the doorknob. It feels like solid sunlight. As you turn it, the bustling city behind you dissolves into a shower of golden dust, and you step through, not into a room, but into the open sky, floating weightlessly in a warm, endless blue. Just as you let out a breath you feel like you’ve been holding for years, the light swallows you completely, and the dream gently lets you go.
