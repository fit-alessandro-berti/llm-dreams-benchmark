You look down, expecting to see the polished wooden floorboards of the stage, but your feet are bare, standing on a mirror-like expanse of dark water. With every slight shift of your weight, ripples of bioluminescent blue spread outward, illuminating a vast, starlit ocean beneath you. The auditorium walls have dissolved entirely. You are standing in the center of the night sky.

The anxiety that gripped your chest moments ago has vanished, replaced by a strange, floating weightlessness. You realize you don't need a microphone. You raise your hands, not to gesture, but to conduct. 

As you sweep your arms upward, the golden light from your voice swirls around you, transforming into a flock of thousands of silver moths. They spiral into the air, catching the starlight, and the audience below opens their hands to let the moths land on their palms. 

A sound begins to fill the space. It isn't applause, but the rhythmic, soothing sound of gentle rain on a tin roof. The silver moths begin to glow brighter, their light growing until it bleaches the colors from the ocean, the stars, and the faces of the crowd. 

The light becomes absolute, warm, and heavy. The rain fades into the steady, rhythmic sound of your own breathing, and the dream gently dissolves into the white ceiling of your bedroom.
