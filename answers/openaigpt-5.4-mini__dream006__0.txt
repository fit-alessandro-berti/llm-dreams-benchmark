…speak, but the microphone is made of moonlight, and your first word floats upward like a bubble.

The audience leans in closer. Their faces shimmer, as if painted on the surface of a lake. You clear your throat, and instead of a voice, a flock of tiny paper birds spills from your mouth, circling above the stage before settling neatly into the front row.

Somewhere behind the curtains, a clock is melting softly.

You smile, because suddenly you remember: this isn’t a speech at all. It’s a story.

So you open your hands, and the lights dim like closing eyes, and the whole room begins to breathe with you.
