...each time you approach a group, the crowd seems to shift and rearrange itself, like a deck of cards being shuffled. You catch a glimpse of your friend's blue jacket ahead, but when you reach the spot, there's only a cluster of strangers debating something intensely in a language you almost understand.

You realize the room has gotten larger. The ceiling is now impossibly high, like a cathedral, and the lighting has shifted from warm amber to a pale, sourceless gray. You try to call out your friend's name, but the sound gets caught somewhere in your throat and comes out as a whisper.

A woman with too many fingers touches your elbow and asks if you've tried the punch. You look down at the cup already in your hand — you don't remember picking it up. The liquid inside is the exact color of the blue jacket you've been chasing.

You round a corner and suddenly the gathering thins out. There's a hallway that shouldn't exist, lined with doors that are each slightly the wrong size. Behind the last door, you hear familiar laughter. You reach for the handle, but your hand passes through it.

You wake up with the faint taste of something bittersweet on your tongue and the lingering sense that your friend was trying to tell you something.
