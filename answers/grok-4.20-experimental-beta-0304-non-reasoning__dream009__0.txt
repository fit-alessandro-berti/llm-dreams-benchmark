You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to...

...find water first. Your throat is parched, and the air smells like woodsmoke, wet stone, and something alive𤪔orses, maybe, or oxen. The ground under your bare feet is packed dirt mixed with straw. You're wearing a rough linen tunic that scratches your skin and feels far too short.

The sky is the color of pale honey, early morning. In the distance you can hear the clang of a blacksmith's hammer and the low murmur of voices speaking a language that feels both familiar and utterly foreign. You understand the words even though you've never heard them before.

A woman in a woolen dress and headscarf is carrying a wooden yoke with two buckets. She stops when she sees you, eyes widening.

"By the saints," she whispers in Old English, "you look as though you've been dragged through the devil's own hedge. Are you hurt, stranger?"

You realize two things at once:

1. This is roughly 9th century England, somewhere near what will one day be called York.
2. Your modern knowledge is still intact, but your body feels... different. Stronger. Like it's been waiting for this.

The woman sets her buckets down and approaches cautiously. "You've got the look of someone who's seen the veil between worlds. The monks at the minster say such travelers sometimes come to us. Do you remember your name?"

Before you can answer, you feel something heavy and warm against your chest under the tunic. You reach in and pull out a small iron key on a leather cord. It wasn't there a moment ago.

The key is old, covered in strange angular symbols that look almost like runes, but not quite. When your thumb brushes across them, they glow faintly blue for a second.

The woman gasps and takes a step back.

"That mark," she says, pointing at the key. "The one on your chest now too. I've seen it in the old carvings beneath the church. They say it opens the door that should never be opened. The one the Danes tried to break during the last raid... and the one that bit back."

She suddenly drops to one knee.

"Speak, traveler. Are you the one the dreams warned us about? The one who comes from the time of iron birds and glass windows that show moving pictures?"

You look down at your hands. They're calloused now, with faint scars that weren't there yesterday. And yet you can still remember the exact layout of your apartment, the smell of your morning coffee, the way your phone felt in your hand.

The key pulses once, like a second heartbeat.

What do you do?
