...you reach out with two hands made of smoke. One holds a brush that hums, the other grips a palette that tastes like silence and regret. You don't apply pigment so much as you let go of memories; every stroke bleeds through the void and colors the air around you. A red wave floods the floor in the shape of your own fear, while a blue fog rises from the paint to form mountains where there are none.

You realize that you aren't painting the landscape at all. You're simply remembering what it should have been before the world fractured into shifting hues. As the sun tries to set but has no color to fade into, you lift your eyes and look directly at the blankness in your mind's eye. You decide that a circle is too small for this. You paint the end of the story instead—a single, perfect comma hanging in the sky, waiting to be breathed out into reality. 

When the ink dries, you know the colors will stop moving again, as they always do when someone goes to sleep.
