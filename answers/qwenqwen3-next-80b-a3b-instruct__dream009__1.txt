You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to **find the Clockmaker**.

The air smells of wet earth and woodsmoke. Cobblestones glide beneath your bare feet—no sneakers, no phone, no GPS. Above you, the sky is a deep indigo, stippled with stars you’ve never seen before—the constellations warped, unfamiliar, as if the heavens themselves had been rearranged. A distant bell tolls seven times, though you know it’s barely noon.

A child runs past, clutching a brass key the size of your palm, whispering, “He’s waiting at the Tower of Whispers.”

You follow.

Through narrow alleyways lined with tapestries that move when you’re not looking—threads weaving themselves into scenes of your own childhood, your lost dog, your grandmother’s kitchen—until you reach a crumbling stone tower. Its entrance is a door of interlocking gears, humming softly.

A figure emerges from the shadows, cloaked in a coat stitched from clock faces. His eyes are glass lenses, each reflecting a different year.

“You’re late,” he says, not unkindly. “The Dreaming Hour is almost over.”

You stammer—“I don’t know how I got here. I don’t even know *when* I am.”

The Clockmaker lifts a hand. From his sleeve, a second hand drifts like a firefly, ticking backward. “You don’t need to know *when*,” he murmurs. “You need to remember *why* you were sent.”

A memory surfaces—not yours, but deep, ancient: *You once made a deal with time. To save someone. You traded your memories of home… and became the guardian of forgotten moments.*

Your hands tremble. You look down—your skin is etched with faint, glowing numerals.

The Clockmaker offers you a small hourglass filled not with sand, but with whispers.

“Find ten lost seconds before the moon bleeds silver,” he says. “Or the world you knew… will never remember you.”

You clutch the hourglass.

And somewhere, in a house you don’t remember living in, a clock starts ticking—again—for the first time.

The dream isn’t over.

It’s just… resetting.
