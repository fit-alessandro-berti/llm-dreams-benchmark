The proctor—who moments ago was a stern-looking professor with half-moon spectacles—is now a towering figure wearing a cloak made of falling autumn leaves. They don't look down at me; instead, their voice echoes from everywhere and nowhere at once. 

*“Begin,”* the voice whispers, carrying the weight of a thousand ticking clocks. *“Choose your path.”*

I look back down at the exam paper. The questions are no longer asking about economics, physics, or philosophy. Instead, the first one reads: 

*“If you could un-invent one memory to save a stranger, would you remember the empty space left behind?”*

My pen hovers over the blank space below. My hand feels impossibly heavy, yet as I press the nib to the paper, the ink doesn't leave a mark—it absorbs into my skin, sending a warm, electric current racing up my arm. 

I smile in the surreal haze of the dream, realizing with sudden, profound clarity that I don't need to study for this. I just have to decide who I want to be when I wake up.
