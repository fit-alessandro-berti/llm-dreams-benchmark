You are at a large social gathering where you only know one person. As you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people and each group seems to know something about where they have gone.

One group is gathered around a piano that plays by itself. They tell you your friend was just there, but left through a door behind the music. When you turn around, there is no door—only a long hallway lined with mirrors.

In the mirrors, you can see your friend walking ahead of you.

You follow, but the hallway gradually becomes a beach at night. The guests are still there, now standing in the surf in formal clothes, speaking quietly as the waves erase their footprints. Someone hands you a drink filled with tiny glowing fish and says, “They’ve been waiting for you upstairs.”

You look up and see that the moon has become an elevator button.

When you press it, the entire beach rises like an elevator car. The doors open onto a familiar room from your childhood, except it is filled with all the people from the party. Your friend is standing by the window, facing away.

You finally reach them and say their name.

They turn around, but their face is your own.

Before you can ask anything, they smile and say, “You found me. Now you can wake up.”

And then you wake up, still hearing distant party music from somewhere in the house.
