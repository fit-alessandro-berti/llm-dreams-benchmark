As you trek through the ash-choked streets, your boots crunching over broken glass and rusted metal, you notice something glinting in the distance—a flicker of green amid all the gray. It's a garden.

Not just any garden, but an enormous hydroponic farm, its transparent walls still intact despite the devastation outside. Through the glass, you can see rows and rows of thriving plants, their leaves glistening with morning dew. A small sign reads: "New Eden Agricultural Complex - 500 crates of premium produce, weekly distribution."

Your heart pounds. Food. Medicine. Seeds. Anything could grow here.

You approach cautiously, checking for threats. The airlock mechanism looks functional. Through the peephole, you see movement inside—a figure tending to the crops. When they notice you, they don't run or raise an alarm. Instead, they nod calmly and hold up five fingers.

"Five people maximum," the figure mouths silently. "We've been waiting."

You realize this isn't just about survival anymore. It's about rebuilding. And whoever is inside has been planning something much bigger than simply hoarding resources.

What do you do?
