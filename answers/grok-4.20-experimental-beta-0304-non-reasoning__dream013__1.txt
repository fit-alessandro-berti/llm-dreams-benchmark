...feel the walls of the maze subtly *breathing* around you, as if the labyrinth itself is alive and watching. The deeper you go, the more the puzzles begin to shift from pure logic into something stranger—each solution seems to require not just reasoning, but a piece of a memory you didn't know you had forgotten.

You solve the first puzzle (a rotating stone dial covered in glowing runes) by remembering the exact smell of your childhood bedroom after rain. The wall slides open with a sigh.

The second puzzle is a mirror that shows you solving it *before* you do. You have to match your future self's movements perfectly. It feels like dancing with your own ghost.

But now the path splits three ways.

Down the left corridor, you can already hear soft, familiar laughter—someone you loved but lost is calling your name, promising that if you come this way, the maze will let you stay with them forever. The puzzles will stop. You can rest.

The middle path is completely silent. The walls here are made of black glass that reflects nothing. A single word is carved above the entrance: *Truth*.

The right corridor is flooded with warm golden light. You can smell fresh bread and hear birdsong. It looks... safe. Simple. The kind of place where you could just live a quiet, happy life and never solve another thing again.

You feel the maze gently pressing against your back, urging you forward. A low voice (maybe your own, maybe the maze's) whispers directly into your mind:

"Every solution costs something. The deeper you go, the more *you* become the solution."

What do you do?
