You feel an immediate wave of relief wash over you, like a cool breeze on a hot day. The tallest of them, a woman with a scarf the color of autumn leaves, smiles and asks where you’re trying to go. Before you can even answer, another one—a man with glasses that reflect the city lights—pulls out a map, its edges softened with use.

You find yourself walking together, their easy chatter weaving through the noise of the streets. They point out landmarks you’d never noticed: a hidden mural of a blue whale on a side street, a bakery where the scent of cinnamon makes the air sweet. The loneliness that clung to you begins to dissolve, replaced by a quiet sense of belonging, as if the city itself has unfolded its palm to guide you.

They walk you all the way to the doorstep of the place you were seeking—a small, glowing café you’d been looking for. As you turn to thank them, they’re already waving, merging back into the crowd like they were always part of it. You step inside, warm and grounded, knowing the city is no longer just a maze, but a place where kindness can be found, even in the middle of a dream.
