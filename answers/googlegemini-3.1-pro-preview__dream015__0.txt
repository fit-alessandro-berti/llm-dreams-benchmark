…despite the hardships, you decide to walk toward the impossible horizon, carrying nothing but a fractured pocket watch and a seed made of pure, humming light. 

The world around me operates on dream-logic. The sky is a bruised canvas of violet and rust, and the ashes falling from the clouds drift downward in slow motion, like heavy snow. The ruined skyscrapers don’t just stand; they lean at impossible angles, bending like melted glass over streets paved with crushed machinery. 

Danger is everywhere, but it isn't quite human. Shadows detach themselves from the rubble, prowling like wolves made of rusted wire and howling wind. They circle me, their static-filled eyes locked on my hands, but the warmth of the glowing seed I hold keeps them at bay. It pulses in time with a heartbeat I realize isn't mine, but the earth’s.

I don’t know why I need to plant this seed, only that the waking world depends on it. 

I reach a vast, dry ocean made entirely of dark, jagged obsidian. In the very center stands a gargantuan, petrified tree, its barren branches scratching at the low-hanging clouds. I step onto the glass sea. With every footfall, the obsidian chimes like a massive tuning fork, sending ripples of sound out into the wasteland.

The journey feels both instantaneous and like an eternity—a classic trick of the dreaming mind. Suddenly, I am at the base of the dead tree. 

I use the fractured pocket watch to dig into the obsidian soil, scooping out a hollow because, in this dream, time is the only thing that can carve through stone. I gently place the humming sphere of light into the dark earth.

For a moment, there is absolute, suffocating silence. 

Then, a sudden, blinding fracture runs up the trunk of the petrified tree. Green light bleeds from the crack, bright as a sun flare. The tree shudders, and in a matter of seconds, leaves woven from bioluminescent threads erupt from the branches. 

A shockwave of life repels backward from the tree. As the wave washes over the ruined world, the obsidian ocean shatters into cool, blue water. The twisted metal ruins in the distance unravel, blooming into massive, towering ferns and moss-covered stones. The shadows of rusted wire dissolve into flocks of golden birds taking flight.

The warmth of the newly formed sun hits my face. I look down at my hands; they are beginning to turn translucent, dissolving into pixels and morning light. 

The dream is ending, but as I fade away into waking consciousness, I leave behind a world that is no longer broken.
