...written in a language you've never seen before. It isn't just a different alphabet; the symbols seem to shift and rearrange themselves on the page the moment you look away.

Panic sets in. You glance at the clock on the wall, but instead of numbers, the face is covered in eyes that are all staring directly at you. The second hand is moving backward.

You look down at your desk and realize you aren't holding a pen, but a large, wobbly carrot. You try to dip it in the inkwell, but the inkwell is filled with hummingbird nectar.

Suddenly, the proctor—who looks suspiciously like your third-grade teacher riding a unicycle—rolls up to your desk. She leans in close and whispers, "The essay question requires you to recite the history of the future, but only using the thoughts of a goldfish."

You look back at the paper. The shifting symbols have settled into a clear message: *In Section C, please draw a map of the route you took to get here today, but include the turn you didn't take that would have led you to a castle made of cheese.*

Just then, the desk begins to sprout leaves, and you realize the exam hall is slowly filling with water, while the eyes on the clock begin to blink in unison.
