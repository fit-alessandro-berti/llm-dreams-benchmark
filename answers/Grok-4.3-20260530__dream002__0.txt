You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the air thickens with the scent of ozone and forgotten rain. The canopy above begins to sway in slow motion, as if underwater, while the sky bruises into deep violets and electric greens. Lightning forks silently—no thunder, only the low hum of something ancient waking up.

The path beneath your feet dissolves into soft moss that glows faintly with each step. Rain begins to fall, but the drops are warm and luminous, carrying whispers of voices you almost recognize: your childhood laughter, a lost love’s sigh, the echo of a decision you never made. You reach out your hand and one droplet lands on your palm, blooming into a tiny, perfect image of the life you almost lived.

The trees lean closer, their bark shifting into intricate patterns that look like maps of constellations. A great stag made of starlight emerges from the mist, its antlers branching into glowing rivers of light. It regards you with eyes like polished obsidian and speaks without sound: *“The storm is not here to destroy. It is here to remember.”*

You follow the stag deeper into the heart of the forest. The wind rises, but instead of howling it sings—a haunting melody in a language your bones understand. Leaves spiral upward, becoming flocks of luminous birds that circle once and vanish into the clouds. The temperature drops sharply, and suddenly you’re standing in a vast clearing where the storm has condensed into a single, swirling pillar of light and shadow.

At the center of the pillar floats a mirror made of rain and memory. You step toward it and see not your reflection, but every version of yourself you’ve ever been or could become—child, wanderer, lover, stranger, ghost. They all smile the same gentle, knowing smile.

The storm reaches its crescendo. A single bolt of lightning, softer than silk, strikes the mirror. The world shatters into a thousand glittering fragments… and you awaken.

You’re lying in your bed. Outside your window the real sky is clear and full of quiet stars. Your heart is calm. The dream lingers on your skin like the ghost of warm rain, and for a long moment you can still hear the forest singing somewhere just beyond the edge of waking.
