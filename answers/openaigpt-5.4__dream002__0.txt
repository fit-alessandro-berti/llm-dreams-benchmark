…the air tightens, as if the whole forest is holding its breath.

The leaves that were softly whispering above you begin to hiss and shudder. A cold wind slips between the trees, circling your ankles first, then rising in spirals around your body. The sky darkens too quickly, changing from gentle gold to deep violet, and the path ahead seems to stretch farther than it should.

Then you notice something strange: the rain is falling upward.

Silver drops lift from the ground and stream into the clouds like reversed waterfalls. The roots of the trees glow faintly blue beneath the soil, pulsing like veins. Somewhere in the distance, a bell rings—slow, deep, impossible to place.

You start walking faster, though you don’t know toward what. The storm isn’t chasing you exactly; it feels more like it’s guiding you. Lightning flickers soundlessly between the branches, illuminating shapes just beyond sight: a deer with antlers full of stars, a fox made of smoke, a doorway standing alone between two ancient oaks.

When you reach the doorway, it is already slightly open.

Warm amber light spills out onto the wet moss. Inside, you glimpse not a room but another forest, brighter and stranger, where the trees are white and the sky is full of drifting lanterns. The bell rings again, now from somewhere beyond the doorway, and you realize the storm behind you has gone completely still.

Everything waits.

You place your hand on the door, feeling it hum softly under your fingertips, and step through—

—and wake up with the feeling that you left something important behind.
