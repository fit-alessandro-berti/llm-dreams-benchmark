…speak—but no sound comes out.

You clear your throat, try again, and this time the audience leans closer. Instead of words, bright paper birds fly from your mouth, circling above the hall. The crowd begins to laugh—not cruelly, but with delight—and soon they are catching the birds in their hands.

Each bird unfolds into a sentence you meant to say.

By the time the last one lands, the audience rises in applause. You bow, feeling strangely relieved, and notice that the stage has become a boat drifting through a moonlit sea.
