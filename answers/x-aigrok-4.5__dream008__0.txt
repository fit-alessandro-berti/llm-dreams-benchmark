**...and you suddenly realize the project is to redesign the entire company logo using only mayonnaise, glitter, and pure anxiety.**

Your boss stands there tapping a stopwatch that isn’t counting seconds—it’s counting down the number of remaining brain cells you have left. The conference room walls start melting into cubicle fabric that smells faintly of burnt popcorn and old printer toner. You open your laptop and the cursor blinks mockingly while the screen displays a single PowerPoint slide titled “Q3 Synergies: Make It Happen Or Else” in Comic Sans.

You try to type, but every key morphs into a miniature version of your boss’s disappointed face. The deadline clock above the door flips from “Tomorrow Morning” to “You Already Missed It Three Years Ago” and then to “The Heat Death of the Universe (approx.).”

In classic dream logic you decide the only logical next step is to deliver the project by folding yourself into a perfectly fitted manila folder and sliding under a door that now leads to the break room, the ocean, and your childhood bedroom all at once. Your boss casually sips coffee that is somehow also a portfolio review and says, “Great energy. Just get it on my desk before the kitchenette becomes sentient.”

Then the entire office tilts sideways, everyone starts floating upside-down like lazy helium balloons, and you’re left wondering whether you should finally start the spreadsheet… or wake up and check if the actual deadline is still only “ASAP.” 

You jolt awake just as the microwave ding-dongs a notification that meeting notes from the dream have been auto-emailed to the whole company.
