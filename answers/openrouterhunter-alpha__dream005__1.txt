...and each group seems to be engaged in some activity that makes perfect sense until I get close enough to join in.

The first cluster is gathered around a long wooden table, sorting objects — seashells, old keys, someone's childhood retainer — into categories that only they understand. They glance up when I approach and one of them says, "Oh good, you brought the green ones," even though my hands are empty. I look down and, for a moment, they're right. I *am* holding green things — marbles, maybe, or grapes. Then they're not there anymore, and no one seems to notice the shift.

I move on.

A woman is standing by a window that opens onto a parking lot at night, except the parking lot is also somehow underwater, and she's talking about a deadline. "It was Thursday," she says firmly, "but they moved it to the feeling you get when a song ends in an elevator." Everyone around her nods. I almost understand what she means.

Further in, a man I don't recognize waves at me like we're old friends. He's holding a leash attached to nothing. "She doesn't like crowds," he explains, and I feel a wave of genuine sympathy for the invisible dog. He asks me if I've seen the one person I'm looking for, and I realize — with that strange dream-logic certainty — that he means them too, that everyone here is looking for the same person, and we've all been circling each other in overlapping spirals.

The room keeps shifting. A hallway that was a wall becomes a staircase. The music changes from something orchestral to something that sounds like rain inside a piano. I pass through a kitchen where someone is making toast but the toaster is a small fireplace and no one finds this unusual.

And then — the way it always happens in dreams — without transition, I'm standing next to the person I came to find. They're sitting on a folding chair in the middle of what is now a very quiet, very ordinary room. They look up and say, "There you are. I've been waiting."

I sit down next to them. The relief is enormous, almost physical, like setting down something heavy I didn't know I was carrying.

"You won't believe the people I passed on the way here," I start to say.

But the dream doesn't let me finish, because now I can feel myself starting to wake up — the edges of the room going soft, the colors thinning — and I'm trying to hold onto it, the feeling of that chair, the sound of their voice saying *there you are*, but it's already fading into the ordinary gray of morning.

And for a moment, lying there with my eyes still closed, I can't remember what the person looked like. Just the warmth of having found them.
