There, rising from the black, churning water, is not a wave, but a massive, slowly ticking grandfather clock, reaching up into the heavy storm clouds. The hands of the clock are spinning wildly backward. 

Dream logic takes over. You suddenly understand exactly what to do. 

You don't fight the storm. You let go of the rooted wheel, walk entirely unbothered across the violently tilting deck, and climb the bowsprit. You reach into your coat pocket and pull out a perfectly dry, lit candle. You toss it into the raging ocean.

The moment the flame strikes the water, the terrifying, icy sea instantly shatters like black glass, dissolving into millions of glowing, blue butterflies. The deafening thunder turns into the sound of a symphony orchestra tuning their instruments. 

Your ship is no longer falling into the trough of a wave. It is gliding softly through a deep, starry nebula. The crew is now calm, sitting on the deck and drinking tea from porcelain cups. You look up and see two giant, silver moons smiling down at you. You feel a profound sense of peace. 

You close your eyes to feel the warm cosmic breeze on your face, and as you do, you feel yourself becoming incredibly light, drifting upward, and slowly opening your real eyes to the morning sunlight streaming through your bedroom window.
