The puzzles evolve, too. They are no longer mathematical or linguistic. To pass through a doorway, you don't solve a riddle; you have to hold your breath until the room forgets you are there. Another corridor requires you to walk backward while thinking exclusively about the concept of "tomorrow" to move forward.

You push deeper, your brow furrowed in that intense, dream-logic concentration, and the floor beneath your feet dissolves into a grid of floating clocks. Each clock is ticking at a different speed—some backwards, some fast, some not moving at all. You realize you aren't navigating space anymore; you are navigating time.

Finally, you reach the center. There is no trophy here, only a single, perfect red rose blooming out of a void of white nothingness. As you reach out to touch it, the petals unfurl to reveal not a flower, but a pair of lips. The maze leans in and whispers a secret to you that you understand perfectly in the dream, but the second your eyes snap open in the dark of your bedroom, the logic evaporates, leaving you with only the lingering smell of rain and the absolute certainty that you solved everything.
