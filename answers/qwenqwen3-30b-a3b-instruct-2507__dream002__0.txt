...a cold, electric wind snatches the breath from your lungs. The sky, moments ago a soft blue, bruises purple and swirls with impossible colors—fuchsia, deep emerald, streaks of molten gold. The trees don't just bend; they *lean*, their trunks twisting like arthritic fingers reaching for the sky. Leaves don’t fall—they *explode* from the branches, not in pieces, but as shimmering, iridescent creatures—dragonflies with wings of stained glass, moths the size of birds, their wings humming with subsonic frequency that vibrates your bones.

You stumble, clutching your head as a wave of dizziness hits. The forest floor isn't earth anymore—it’s a mosaic of shifting tiles, each one showing a different moment from your life: laughing at a childhood birthday, standing alone at a funeral, holding a letter you never opened. The tiles shift beneath your feet, pulling you sideways, forcing you to step *onto* memories you’d buried.

Then, the silence. The storm holds its breath.

And from the center of the clearing, where the oldest oak should be, a figure emerges. Not a person, not an animal—but a *library*. A towering, impossible structure woven from living wood, pages, and glowing ink. Books float like seeds, drifting through the air, their covers pulsing with soft light. One drifts toward you, its title shimmering: *"What You Left in the Rain."*

The wind is gone. The air tastes like old paper and lightning. You reach out—just as the first drop falls. Not rain. *Ink*. It strikes your hand, not wet, but warm, seeping into your skin like a secret. The dream holds its breath.

And you know… you have to open the book.
