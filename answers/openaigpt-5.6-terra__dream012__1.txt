…you grip the wheel with both hands, though the wood is slick with rain and salt. Lightning opens the sky in white seams, revealing mountains of water rising around the ship.

“Hold her steady!” you call, but the wind tears your words apart.

The crew moves anyway. They tie down the cargo, cut loose a shattered spar, haul at ropes that seem to lead into the black throat of the storm. Each face turns toward you between flashes of light—not asking whether you are afraid, but whether there is still a direction to follow.

Then, beneath the roar of the sea, you hear a bell.

Once.

Twice.

It is not from your ship.

Ahead, through curtains of rain, a pale green glow appears. At first you think it is lightning trapped beneath the waves. But it remains: a lighthouse standing on a narrow island of stone, its beam sweeping slowly across the water.

You turn the wheel toward it.

The ship groans as though waking from a long sleep. A wave breaks over the deck, carrying away a barrel, a lantern, and the hat from your head. The crew cheers anyway. The lighthouse grows brighter. Its beam touches your face, and for one impossible moment the storm goes silent.

In that silence, you understand that the sea is not trying to drown you.

It is trying to teach you how to steer.

You wake with the taste of salt on your lips, your hands clenched around an invisible wheel, while somewhere far away a bell continues to ring.
