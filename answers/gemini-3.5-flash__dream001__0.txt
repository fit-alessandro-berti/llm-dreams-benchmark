You pull out your phone to check the address of the meeting, but the screen is a shifting kaleidoscope of glowing, liquid glyphs. Every time you try to read the numbers of the address or the time, they scramble like startled minnows. Panic, cold and sharp, spikes in your chest. You can’t remember *what* the meeting is about, or who it is with, only that your entire life hinges on you getting there on time.

You look up at the skyline. The buildings are dizzying, constructed of dark basalt and iridescent mother-of-pearl, curving inward at the top to block out a bruised, lavender-tinted sky. You spot a passerby—a tall figure in a trench coat whose face seems to blur and smudge whenever you try to focus on their features.

"Excuse me," you say, but your voice sounds muffled, as if you are speaking underwater. "Where is the glass tower? The one with the..." 

The stranger doesn't speak. Instead, they point a long, gloved finger upward. Not toward a skyscraper, but toward a solitary, glowing white door hovering effortlessly fifty feet in the air, with absolutely no stairs leading up to it. 

The clock is ticking. You can hear a giant metronome echoing from somewhere beneath the city streets. *Tick. Tock.* 

Realizing that the physical rules of this place are malleable, you take a deep breath. You bend your knees and push off the ground. Instead of falling back down, you float upward, swimming through the thick, warm air. The gravity here is light, like being at the bottom of a swimming pool. You drift higher, your heart hammering, until your fingers grasp the cool brass handle of the floating door.

You turn the handle and push it open, bracing yourself for a room full of impatient executives in suits. 

Instead, you step through the threshold and find yourself standing on a tiny, weathered wooden dock jutting out into a silent, midnight-blue ocean. The city is gone. The sky above is filled with thousands of glowing, translucent jellyfish, drifting lazily through the air like living stars. 

At the end of the dock sits a single, empty wooden chair. Resting on the seat is a small, hand-painted ceramic cup of hot tea, steam rising into the cool night air. Next to it is a note written in your own handwriting. 

You walk over, pick up the paper, and read the words:

*“Relax. You made it. You can wake up now.”*
