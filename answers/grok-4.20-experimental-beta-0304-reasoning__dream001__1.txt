You start to run, heart hammering like it's trying to escape your chest, but the sidewalk keeps rearranging itself under your feet—bricks sliding away like dominoes, turning into escalators that carry you backward no matter how fast you sprint forward.

Street signs flicker between languages you've never learned and symbols that look like they were drawn by a bored god. You spot a taxi, wave desperately, but when it stops, the driver is a giant raccoon in a three-piece suit who simply hands you a melting pocket watch and says, "You're already late. Also, the meeting is about you."

The city begins folding like origami. Skyscrapers bend toward each other, forming a tunnel of glass and neon that funnels you into what used to be a plaza but is now your old high school auditorium. Everyone you’ve ever disappointed is sitting in the audience, patiently waiting. Your boss (or is it your mother? they keep swapping faces) stands at the podium holding a single red balloon.

You try to apologize for being late, but the words come out as bubbles. The balloon pops.

Suddenly you're falling upward through the ceiling, through clouds made of yesterday's to-do lists, until you wake up in your actual bed, heart still racing, phone buzzing with a calendar reminder.

The meeting was never real.

But the raccoon left his business card on your nightstand.
