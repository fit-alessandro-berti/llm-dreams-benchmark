You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, you notice the walls shifting subtly, like the breath of some ancient, living entity. The air grows thicker, laced with the faint scent of forgotten libraries and rusting gears.

The first puzzle blocks your path: a towering door etched with interlocking gears that must be aligned by solving a riddle whispered by the wind—"What has keys but opens no locks, space but no room, and you can enter but not go inside?" Your mind races— a keyboard! With trembling hands, you twist the central gear, and it clicks open, revealing a corridor lined with mirrors that reflect not your image, but alternate versions of yourself, each one a step closer to unraveling a different mystery.

Deeper still, the puzzles evolve into illusions. A bridge spans a chasm of swirling colors, but stepping on the wrong tile sends ripples of doubt through your thoughts, making logic feel slippery. You pause, breathing deeply, and deduce the pattern: primes, woven into the tiles like hidden stars—2, 3, 5, 7. Crossing safely, you emerge into a chamber where the final enigma awaits—a holographic chessboard where pieces move themselves, demanding you predict their dance three moves ahead.

As the board yields to your strategy, the maze dissolves like mist in sunlight. You awaken not in confusion, but with a spark of clarity, the puzzles lingering as tools in your waking mind, ready to unlock the real world's riddles.
