…and the canvas is not flat.  
It is a stretched oval of porous moon-silk, breathing softly, ribs rising and falling like a sleeping animal’s.  

Every color you have just seen—the violet that tasted like peppermint, the triangles that hummed in B-flat, the spiraling ribbons of bruised gold—slides toward the canvas as if pulled by slow, polite gravity. They pool at its edge, not spilling, but queuing.  

You realize the canvas is listening.  
Its surface dimples when you think of a word, and rises into a hill when you hold your breath.  
You dip your index finger into the gathered puddle of peppermint-violet and press a single point.  

The point cracks open into a door.  

From the door steps a creature made of negative space, wearing a coat woven from half-remembered melodies.  
It bows, then raises one hand and plucks a thread from the edge of the canvas.  
As the thread comes away, the landscape behind you softens, the shapes relaxing into new names:  
a mountain becomes the letter N,  
a river remembers it was once a chord,  
the sky folds into origami and sits quietly in your palm.  

The creature gestures to the canvas, inviting you to paint not with color but with consequences.  
You smile, because in this dream you know that every brushstroke is also a footstep,  
and every footstep is a door.  

So you step onto the canvas,  
and it opens.
