You are participating in a competition where the grand prize is your dream job. As the competition progresses, you face various challenges that test your creativity, resilience, and instincts.  

**The First Challenge:** A maze of mirrors appears, each reflecting a moment from your past—failures, missed opportunities, and moments of doubt. To move forward, you must choose a reflection to "burn" with a single thought, releasing its hold on you. Do you destroy the image of your younger self, afraid of not being "enough"? Or let go of a memory of someone who once discouraged you?  

**The Second Challenge:** You’re given a blank canvas and an hour to paint your vision of the future—but every stroke you make withers into ash unless you first solve a riddle about your deepest fear. Is it failure? Losing those you love? Or the fear of becoming invisible in a world that demands constant proof of worth?  

**The Third Challenge:** A rival appears—not a person, but a shadow version of yourself. This doppelgänger embodies every path you *didn’t* take: the career you abandoned, the relationships you neglected, the risks you played it safe to avoid. You must outwit or out-negotiate it to proceed. But what if, in the process, you realize the shadow isn’t your enemy—it’s a part of you you’ve ignored?  

**The Final Test:** You’re offered the dream job—but only if you betray someone who helped you along the way. The catch? The person in question is your own mentor, who has been secretly orchestrating the competition to see if you’ll sacrifice integrity for ambition. Do you walk away empty-handed or claim the prize by sacrificing trust?  

As the dream shifts, the walls dissolve, and you stand in a room bathed in light. A voice whispers: *"The prize isn’t the job—it’s the person you become to earn it."*  

Do you seize the title, or choose to redefine "winning" on your own terms?  

What happens next is up to you.
