The room stretches longer than it should, the walls breathing in and out like a sleeping lung. To your left, a cluster of people are toasting with glasses filled with water that refuses to spill, no matter how vigorously they shake their hands. They turn to you, smiling with too many teeth, and ask if you've seen the exit, but their mouths don't move when they speak.

You push through them, your feet sinking slightly into the carpet, which feels less like fabric and more like moss. You are looking for the silver watch. That is the only anchor. The only person who knows your name in this place is the one wearing that watch.

You turn a corner and the hallway elongates, the ceiling rising into a vault of stained glass that depicts scenes from your childhood, but the colors are wrong. The sky is purple; the grass is blue. A group of musicians is playing instruments made of glass, and the sound is a hum that vibrates in your teeth. They are playing a song you know, but the melody is looping backwards.

You spot the glint of silver near a balcony overlooking a garden that doesn't exist outside. You rush toward it, your heart hammering a frantic rhythm against your ribs. You call out, but your voice comes out as a puff of white smoke.

You reach the figure. They are standing with their back to you, watching the impossible garden. You reach out to touch their shoulder, to say, *Finally, I found you.*

But as your fingers brush the fabric of their coat, they turn around. It is not the person you know. It is a mirror, and in the reflection, you are not standing in a crowd. You are standing alone in a white room, wearing the coat, holding the silver watch.

The music stops. The glass instruments shatter into silence. The ceiling dissolves into a soft, gray fog, and you feel the sensation of falling upward, just as the alarm clock begins to ring in the distance, vibrating through the floor of the dream.
