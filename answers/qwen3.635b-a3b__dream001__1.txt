You start to pull your phone from your pocket to check the address, but your fingers pass through the fabric as if it were smoke. Your pocket is bottomless, a well of velvet shadows, and instead of a phone, your hand closes around a handful of wet, glowing sand. When you open your palm, the sand turns into a flock of origami cranes that flutter up and spell out the words: *Follow the silence.*

You look up. The sky has turned the color of a bruised plum, and the buildings are no longer made of stone, but of stacked books, ticking clocks, and whispered secrets. Their spires twist into helixes of steam that smell like ozone and childhood rain. Panic flares in your chest, but it tastes like mint, and where you stomp in fear, cobblestones rise up like lily pads on a dark river.

You turn a corner and the street stretches infinitely, elongating like taffy. Every step you take moves the destination further away, yet the horizon pulls toward you with magnetic inevitability. A streetlamp leans down, its bulb pulsing like a slow heart, and whispers in your own voice, *"Time is in the walls. Listen."*

You press your ear to the nearest skyscraper. It hums with the sound of a cello string being bowed by the wind. The hum vibrates your teeth, and suddenly, the architecture shifts. The books on the facades fly open, releasing swarms of paper butterflies that spiral into a funnel cloud above you. The funnel points down, and where it touches the ground, the city folds inward like a map being crumpled by a giant hand.

The cranes circle back, tugging at your wrists. They lead you to a door that wasn't there a moment ago㻖reestanding in the middle of a river of stars flowing upward. The door has no handle, only a keyhole shaped like a tear. You realize the meeting isn't a place; it's a state. The hour has shrunk to a second, and the only way to be on time is to stop running.

You drop the cranes. You sit on the edge of the star-river. You close your eyes and exhale.

As your breath leaves you, the door dissolves into mist, and the mist reveals a room you know intimately, though you've never been here. It's the room you slept in, but viewed from the inside of a snow globe. Outside the glass, the city is spinning, rotating slowly, revealing that it is actually a key floating in an endless sea of white.

A figure approaches the glass from the other side. It's you, but calm, holding a cup of tea that rises into a nebula. The dream-you taps the glass and mouths: *Wake up.*

The sensation of falling rushes back to swallow the city whole, the sky cracks open like an egg, and the smell of mint floods your lungs as the world folds inward one last time...

*A distant alarm clock rings, muffled as if heard through water.*
