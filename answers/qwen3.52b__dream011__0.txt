The questions appear not in black or red, but shifting colors that correspond with your own pulse when you breathe too deeply.

They are not shapes on paper, but floating constellations of light. When you press the button, the ink turns into a river and rushes back into the sky before your eyes even leave the page.

It feels like you've been holding the test for months. The answers were written in whispers that only you could hear, yet they seem to exist right now. You realize, slowly, that you aren't answering them, because the ink is moving on its own, tracing the path through your mind toward where you're headed anyway.

The room tilts sideways for a moment, then pulls away as if waking from a fever dream. You take one last breath, and find that the questions are finally blank again—you've passed the first question by simply stopping looking directly at the screen.
