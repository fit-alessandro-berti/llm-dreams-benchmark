You find yourself in a vast, unfamiliar city, and you realize you have an important meeting in an hour but no idea where it is. You start to walk quickly through crowded streets, hoping something will look familiar. Towering buildings stretch into the clouds, their windows reflecting colors that shift like liquid rainbows.

You pull out your phone to check the address, but the screen displays only a moving map with no labels. Every time you choose a direction, the streets rearrange themselves behind you. A tram glides silently overhead, traveling through the air instead of on tracks.

As your anxiety grows, you notice that everyone around you seems to know exactly where they are going. You stop a passerby to ask for help, but they hand you a folded paper crane instead of answering. To your surprise, the crane unfolds itself and begins fluttering through the air.

With no better option, you follow it.

The paper crane leads you through hidden alleys, across floating bridges, and into neighborhoods that seem to belong to different centuries. One street is medieval, another futuristic, and another covered entirely in flowering trees. Despite the strange journey, you feel strangely calm.

Eventually, the crane lands in front of a small door squeezed between two enormous skyscrapers. Above it hangs a sign displaying your name.

You open the door and step inside.

Instead of a meeting room, you find a circular chamber filled with mirrors. In each mirror, you see a different version of yourself: one who chose a different career, one who traveled the world, one who became an artist, one who stayed exactly where they started. They all look at you expectantly.

At the center of the room sits an empty chair.

A voice echoes gently through the chamber.

"Everyone is here. The meeting can begin."

You sit down, and the versions of yourself begin sharing stories—successes, mistakes, regrets, and unexpected joys. As they speak, you realize none of them know the future any better than you do. They simply chose a direction and kept moving.

The mirrors slowly fade. The chamber brightens. Outside the window, the impossible city stops shifting and settles into place.

Then the paper crane lands on your shoulder and whispers the final item on the agenda:

"You were never late. You were looking for the right destination."

At that moment, the city dissolves into a sky full of stars, and you wake up with the feeling that, somehow, you have just attended the most important meeting of your life.
