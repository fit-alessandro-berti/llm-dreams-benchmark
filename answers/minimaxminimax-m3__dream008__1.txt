Your boss keeps staring at you with too many eyes, all blinking in different rhythms, and he's saying something about "synergy" but the word keeps changing shape in the air, becoming a small bird that flies into the fluorescent lights.

The project itself keeps shifting. First, it's a presentation. Then it's a building. Then it's a feeling you had once as a child but can't quite name. You try to explain to your boss that the deadline is impossible, that time itself seems broken—the clock on the wall is counting backward, then sideways, then in colors instead of numbers—but he just nods and hands you a pen that, when you take it, welds itself to your fingers.

And then, just as the panic becomes a living thing sitting on your chest, you notice something strange: the walls of the office are made of paper, and through them, you can see your childhood bedroom, and your mother is calling your name from far away, telling you it's time to wake up, that the morning is here, that you are safe, that you are loved, that none of this was ever real—

—and you open your eyes to sunlight, and your heart is still racing, but the deadline is gone, dissolved like morning fog, and you think: *it was only a dream.* And for a moment, you can still feel the weight of that impossible task, before it fades entirely, leaving only the warmth of the real day beginning.
