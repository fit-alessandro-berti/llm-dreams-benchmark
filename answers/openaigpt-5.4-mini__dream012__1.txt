“Hold fast,” you say, though your voice is swallowed by thunder. “We do not run from the storm. We find its eye.”

The crew springs into motion, ropes snapping through wet hands, sails straining like white wings against the dark. Lightning forks across the heavens and for one impossible moment the sea becomes a mirror, and you see not waves but a road—silver, trembling, leading forward.

The ship lurches, groans, and then obeys.

You stand at the helm while the wind howls around you like a living thing. The crew watches, trusting, afraid, alive. Ahead, through sheets of rain, a faint light appears on the horizon: not land, but a lantern, bobbing gently above the waves.

Someone whispers, “Captain… what is that?”

You smile, because in dreams you always know.

“It’s our destination,” you say.

And the ship sails toward the light, the storm parting just enough to let you through.
