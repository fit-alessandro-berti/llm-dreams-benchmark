You are at a large social gathering where you only know one person. As you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people and none of them quite hold still. One cluster earnestly debates a map that redraws itself every few seconds, routes looping into places that cannot exist. Nearby, dancers spin in perfect silence, their feet hovering just above the floor. In a quieter corner strangers lean close and whisper secrets that turn out to be memories you had completely forgotten—the exact smell of a childhood hallway, a name you once loved.

Every so often you catch a glimpse of the person you know: a familiar silhouette, a particular way of standing. But when you push through the crowd the figure turns and is someone else entirely, or simply dissolves into the next group. Conversations bleed into one another. The room expands and contracts like a lung.

At last the crowd thins. Across an empty stretch of floor you see them waiting by a door that wasn’t there a moment ago. They smile the way only someone who truly knows you can smile. As you step closer their features soften and settle into a perfect reflection of your own.

“There you are,” they say softly. “I’ve been looking for you too.”

The walls begin to breathe. The music warps into the sound of your own heartbeat. The entire gathering folds inward like paper, leaving only the two of you floating in a soft, endless gray.
