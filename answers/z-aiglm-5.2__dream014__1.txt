...hesitate for only a moment before accepting. Their faces are warm but somehow shifting, the way faces are in dreams — familiar and foreign at once.

They walk with you through streets that curve and fold impossibly. A bakery smells like your grandmother's kitchen. A street musician plays a melody you almost recognize. The tallest stranger points toward a narrow alley you hadn't noticed, lined with paper lanterns that glow soft amber.

"This way," she says gently.

The alley opens into a courtyard with a single tree — massive, ancient, its roots breaking through cobblestones. Something loosens in your chest. You realize you aren't anxious anymore. The loneliness has dissolved.

"Do you know this place?" one of them asks.

You look at the tree. You look at your hands. You know this place. You've always known it. It's the place you came from, somehow, before everything got complicated.

"Thank you," you whisper, turning back.

But the strangers are gone. Only the lanterns remain, swaying in a breeze you can't feel. The tree's leaves rustle — and you wake, with the strange, lingering certainty that you are not as lost as you thought.

*The feeling stays with you through morning, like warmth from a dream you can't quite hold.*
