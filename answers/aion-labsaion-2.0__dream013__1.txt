The dream continues:

You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, the puzzles start reflecting your own subconscious fears and desires. The walls shift from cold stone to translucent, shimmering membranes that pulse with your heartbeat.

You reach a circular chamber. In the center, there is a pedestal holding an obsidian sphere. Around the room, there are seven doors, each marked with a symbol: a key, an eye, a heart, a clock, a mirror, a flame, and a question mark.

You know you must choose one door to proceed, but first, you must solve the puzzle of the sphere. As you approach, your reflection warps on its surface. A voice, which is your own but distorted, echoes in the chamber: "To see the path, you must first see yourself. Solve me."

The sphere presents a logic puzzle that feels familiar—it's based on your own memories, choices, and regrets. Solving it requires confronting a difficult truth about a past decision. As you concentrate, the sphere grows warm. The correct sequence clicks into place not through external logic, but through honest self-acceptance.

The obsidian sphere cracks open, revealing a luminous key inside. You take it, and suddenly all doors except one vanish. The remaining door bears the symbol of the mirror.

You walk through, and the maze ends. You find yourself in a vast library where every book is a record of a dream you've ever had. The final puzzle is to find the book titled with your own name. When you open it, the pages are blank. You realize the dream is not about solving puzzles to escape, but about understanding that you are both the maze and the dreamer.

Then you wake up.
