You gratefully accept their offer. The strangers smile warmly, their faces somehow familiar yet strange—like people you might have known in another life. One takes your hand gently, another points down a quieter side street glowing with soft lantern light, and the third murmurs that the city always feels less overwhelming once you know where home is.

As you walk with them, the noise of the bustling streets fades into a gentle hum. The buildings begin to shift: glass towers turn into cozy brick houses, neon signs soften into starlit windows, and the strangers start sharing quiet stories about places they’ve loved—parks at dusk, bakeries that smell like cinnamon, rivers that reflect the moon like a mirror. You feel the loneliness ease with every step.

They lead you to a small park you somehow recognize. In the center stands a single empty bench under a tree heavy with fireflies. The strangers gesture for you to sit. “This is where your path continues,” one says kindly. “We’ll be nearby if you need us.”

You sit. The fireflies swirl around you like living stars. When you look up again the strangers have blended into the soft night, but the feeling of being lost is gone. In its place is a quiet certainty: you were never truly alone, and the city itself has always been waiting to guide you home.  

You wake with that warmth still lingering.
