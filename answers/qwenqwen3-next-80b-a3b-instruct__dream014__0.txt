You follow them down a narrow alley where the streetlights aren’t electric—they’re glowing lanterns made of woven moonlight, floating just above your head like obedient fireflies. The air smells of cinnamon and rain, even though the sky is clear.

One of the strangers—a woman with silver-streaked hair and eyes that shift color like a hummingbird’s wing—hands you a small, folded paper crane. “This remembers where you’re meant to go,” she says, voice soft as a lullaby. You unfold it gently, and inside, written in shimmering ink, are no words—just the pattern of your own heartbeat.

The others lead you past cafés where the patrons are made entirely of mist, sipping tea that steams with memories. A child with no face offers you a popsicle that tastes like your fifth birthday. You don’t remember having one, but you cry all the same.

At the end of the alley, the city dissolves into a vast, quiet field under a sky stitched with constellations that aren’t stars—but faces. Your mother’s. Your grandfather’s. Your future self’s, smiling sadly, waving.

One of the strangers, now wearing a coat made of old letters, whispers, “You were never lost. You were just waiting to remember how to be found.”

You don’t need directions anymore.

You wake up—still in your bed, phone buzzing with missed calls and unread texts—but your hand is clenched around something.

A folded paper crane.

And on your pillow, where it didn’t exist before... a single, perfect poppy petal.

You smile.

Because now you know: you’re not alone.

You were just dreaming…  
…of the people who never left you.
