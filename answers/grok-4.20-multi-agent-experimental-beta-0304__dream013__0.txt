**You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, you notice the walls beginning to shimmer like liquid starlight.**

The riddles stop being about numbers and patterns. They become living things—fragments of half-remembered conversations, forgotten childhood fears, and questions you’ve never dared ask while awake. Every time you solve one, a door opens, but the maze doesn’t just expand; it *breathes*. The corridors pulse gently in time with your heartbeat. Gravity loosens its grip. You start floating between floors that fold into themselves like origami made of midnight.

Talking ravens (who sound suspiciously like your own inner monologue) offer hints that are actually riddles about *you*. A version of yourself from ten years ago appears as a puzzle piece, arguing with the version of you from next year. The deeper you go, the more the distinction between “solving” and “being solved” disappears.

At the center there is no trophy or grand exit. There’s only a mirror. When you look into it, the reflection steps out, smiles, and says:

“You kept looking for the way out… but the maze was the only place we could finally talk.”

Then the walls dissolve into billions of tiny lights. You’re no longer in the maze.

You *are* the maze.

And for one perfect, impossible moment, everything makes sense—until you feel the familiar tug of waking life pulling you upward, like surfacing from the deepest, clearest water.

You wake up smiling, with the faint feeling that somewhere, the maze is still waiting… and maybe, just maybe, you left a better version of yourself there to keep exploring. 

Sweet dreams. Want to keep going, or shall we build another one together?
