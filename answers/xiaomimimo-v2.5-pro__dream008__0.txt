# The Dream

You are at your workplace, and suddenly, you are given a project with an impossible deadline. Your boss is waiting for you to start, and you...

...glance down at the project brief, but the words keep rearranging themselves like puzzle pieces refusing to click into place. You look up to ask a clarifying question, but your boss has turned into your high school math teacher — the stern one who always said you'd never amount to anything without showing your work.

"You have until lunch," they say, tapping a ruler against a whiteboard that's now somehow in the middle of the office.

You rush to your desk, but it's not your desk anymore. It's your childhood kitchen table. The project files have become a stack of homework you forgot to do. Your coworkers are there, but they're all kids now too, whispering and giggling, offering no help.

You start typing — furiously, desperately — but every keystroke produces a different sound: a doorbell, a dog barking, your mother calling your name. The screen fills with perfect, beautiful text, but when you look closely, it's all written in a language you've never seen before, yet somehow... you *understand* it.

The lunch bell rings.

You stand up, project in hand, heart pounding. You walk to your boss's office, but the hallway keeps stretching longer and longer. You start running. The floor turns to sand. The walls dissolve into open sky.

You hand over the project. Your boss — who is now a golden retriever — sniffs it, wags their tail, and says in a perfectly calm voice:

*"This is exactly what we needed. Well done."*

You wake up. Your alarm clock reads 6:47 AM. Your heart is still racing, but somewhere beneath the absurdity, you feel... oddly confident.
