You reach out and touch the canvas. The moment your fingers meet its surface, the blankness ripples outward like disturbed water, drinking in the chaos of the world around it. Colors from the shifting landscape flood across it in impossible sequences—crimson bleeding into ultraviolet, squares folding into spirals that sing in voices you almost recognize.

The canvas no longer stays still. It rises, hovering at eye level, and begins painting *you* instead. Your silhouette appears first, but it’s wrong in the most beautiful ways: your skin is made of moving constellations, your eyes are open doors leading to other dreams. As you watch, the version of yourself on the canvas turns its head and looks directly at you. It smiles with your mouth, then steps forward—out of the canvas and into the air.

The two of you stand face to face. The world has gone quiet, colors frozen mid-swirl like a held breath. The other you reaches out and presses a hand to your chest. In that instant you feel every version of yourself that has ever existed in this dream (and some that never did) rushing through you at once. You become the landscape. You become the changing colors. You become the canvas.

For one perfect, terrifying second, there is no difference between the dreamer and the dream.

Then the canvas folds in on itself like paper, shrinking smaller and smaller until it is no larger than a playing card. It drifts into your palm, weightless. On its surface is a single, simple image now: a tiny, perfectly still blank square in the center of all the chaos.

You wake up with the card still in your hand.

Except it isn’t a card anymore. It’s just your empty palm, and the memory of what you chose to leave blank.
