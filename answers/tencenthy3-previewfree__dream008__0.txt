"This needs to be done by yesterday," your boss says, but when you look up, your boss is no longer human. They are a towering filing cabinet with a single, blinking red eye on top. The cabinet drawers open and close rhythmically, making a sound like polite applause.

You look back at the screen. The monitor is made of water, suspended in a rectangular frame. The cursor is a small, live goldfish swimming frantically in circles. You try to click the mouse, but it has transformed into a ripe peach—fuzzy and sweet-smelling, but completely unresponsive.

"The deadline is moving up!" the filing cabinet booms. You look at the calendar on the wall, but the numbers are dissolving like sugar in rain. The days are slipping away, turning into puddles on the floor.

Panic sets in. You need to work, but the office has turned into a jungle. Vines are crawling up the cubicle walls, and your project files are now heavy stones you have to carry across a rickety rope bridge that spans a bottomless void. 

You take a step onto the bridge, the peach-mouse in one hand and the mashed-potato keyboard in the other. Halfway across, you look down and see that the void is filled with giant, judgmental owls staring up at you.

Suddenly, the rope snaps. You fall, but you don't plummet down. Instead, you float gently upward toward the ceiling, which has opened up to reveal a bright, sunny sky. You hear the filing cabinet boss yelling from far below, "You forgot the attachment!"

And just as you are about to float out of the office and into the clouds, you wake up, your heart racing, only to realize... you left the stove on. Or did you? In the dream, you can never be sure.
