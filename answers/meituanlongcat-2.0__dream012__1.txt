The cry of the wind is a living thing, a howling beast at the timbers. The deck bucks beneath my feet, a frantic, living thing trying to throw us all into the churning black water. I taste salt and fear on my lips.

"Captain!" My first mate, Elias, his face pale as foam in the lightning flashes, clings to the rigging. "The foresail's shredded! We can't hold this course!"

The crew, shadows drenched and fighting, turn their eyes to me. In this world of screaming wind and heaving deck, I am the only fixed point. The dream-logic is absolute: my will is the keel that keeps us true.

I don't shout over the storm. I move, bracing against a roll, and grab the heavy wheel. The wood is alive with vibration. "Reef the mainsail!" My voice, when it comes, is not my voice. It is deeper, a rumble from the chest of the ship itself. "Elias, take the helm when I give the word! We're going *into* the waves!"

A collective gasp. Going into the mountain of black water ahead is madness. But to turn broadside is to be rolled like a toy.

They move, trusting the madness of the order. Men scramble into the rigging, tiny figures against the fury. I feel the ship groan, a deep, wooden shudder of terror and effort.

"Hard to port! *Now!*"

I throw my weight against the wheel. The ship resists, then with a scream of timber, she lurches. The wave, a moving cliff, is no longer beside us but ahead. We are climbing it.

The deck tilts to an impossible angle. For a breathless second, I look straight up into the heart of the storm, into a maelstrom of cloud and rage. Then we crest, and for a single, crystalline moment, we are above it all. The wind drops. The sea below is a mountain range of foam. The crew hangs on, breathless.

And in that suspended second, the dream clarifies. I see their faces individually now—not just shapes. The young powder-monkey, knuckles white. The old sailmaker, lips moving in prayer. Elias, giving me a grim, defiant nod.

This is not my storm. It is *our* storm. I am not the master of the sea, but the focus of their courage.

"Helm to starboard! Steady as she goes!" I roar, the command cutting through the eerie calm at the peak. We slide down the back of the wave, into the valley of shadow, but the ship feels different. She answers quicker. We are no longer being battered; we are *sailing*.

Another wave rises. I meet it. "Hold!" I command, not just my men, but the wind, the water, the dream itself.

And in the holding, in the shared act of defiance, the storm begins to recede. Not fading, but... passing. The first mate’s face transforms from terror to awe as a sliver of moonlight, cold and silver, breaks through the clouds ahead.

It illuminates the peak of a wave, and beyond it, the faintest line of a calm horizon.

"Captain," Elias whispers, his voice raw. "Land."

I don't smile. I grip the wheel, muscles burning, and give the final order, not to face the storm, but to greet what lies beyond it.

"All hands. Set course for home."

And the dream, satisfied, begins to dissolve into the soft light of dawn.
