# The Dream

You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to **keep moving forward.**

The cracked highway stretches endlessly under a pale, ash-filtered sun. Your backpack feels lighter than it should—just a half-full water bottle, a rusted pocket knife, and a faded photograph of people whose faces you almost remember.

You walk until you crest a hill and see it: a valley below, impossibly green. A faint column of smoke rises from somewhere among the trees. Life. Other survivors, perhaps.

Your heart races. You want to call out, but instinct keeps you silent. You've learned that in this world, not everyone who breathes is friendly.

You descend carefully, stepping between broken asphalt and stubborn wildflowers pushing through the rubble. The air grows warmer. You hear something you haven't heard in what feels like years—**birds singing.**

Then a voice carries on the wind.

"Hey! Over here!"

A figure waves from a makeshift shelter—smiling, unarmed, holding out what looks like a bowl of something steaming.

You hesitate. Every survival instinct screams warning. But something deeper—something older than fear—pulls you forward.

You realize the hardest part of the apocalypse was never the scarcity or the danger.

It was the loneliness.

And maybe, just maybe, this is where that ends.

You take another step toward the firelight, and the dream begins to shimmer at the edges—

—you feel yourself waking, carrying with you an unexpected warmth, a quiet reminder that even in the bleakest landscapes, **connection is worth the risk.**
