… and each challenge seems to test not just your skills, but the very essence of who you are. In one trial, you’re asked to build a bridge out of nothing but your own memories—each plank a moment of joy, fear, or regret. As you lay them down, the bridge trembles under the weight of your doubts, but then steadies as you recall the times you’ve already overcome the impossible.

In another, you’re given a puzzle with pieces that shift shape every time you blink, as if the answer is just out of reach. But then you realize the puzzle isn’t meant to be solved with logic—it’s meant to be *felt*. You close your eyes, trust your instincts, and the pieces click into place, revealing a door with your name on it.

The final challenge is the most surreal: you’re placed in a room with a mirror that doesn’t reflect your face, but instead shows glimpses of the life you could have if you win—laughter in a sunlit office, hands creating something meaningful, a sense of belonging. But then the mirror darkens, and a voice asks, *"Is this what you truly want, or just what you’ve been told to want?"*

And then—you wake up. Or do you?
