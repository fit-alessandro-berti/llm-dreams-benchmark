...and you do something unexpected.

You don't shout. You don't grab the wheel tighter.

You let go.

For a second, your crew thinks you've given up. The wind is howling, tearing at the sails, the hull groaning as waves as tall as houses crash over the bow. The sky is black and green and endless.

But then you look up.

The storm isn't above you. It's all around you, but the water in your cupped hands is perfectly still. The thunder sounds muffled, like it's coming from another room.

You turn to your crew - their faces are familiar, but you can't quite place them. Old friends, family, people you've never met - and you smile, and you say:

"This isn't water."

The moment you say it, the ship sighs. The wood softens beneath your feet and becomes paper. The sails become bedsheets, snapping in the wind. The raging sea below isn't an ocean at all, it's just a rumpled, dark-blue blanket.

You aren't sinking. You're floating.

You take a deep breath, and you steer *into* the biggest wave. Instead of crashing, it passes right through you like cool air. The crew starts to laugh. One of them turns into a cat and curls up on a coil of rope.

The storm doesn't end. It just stops mattering. And you, the captain, finally understand you were never trying to survive it.

You were learning how to drift through it.
