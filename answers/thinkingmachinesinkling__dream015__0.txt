...plant a garden in the ruins of an old library.

You gather seeds not from fields, but from the crumbling pages of books—words that survived the fire—and press them into the cracked earth between toppled shelves. Despite the scarcity, despite the dangers lurking beyond the broken walls, you decide that care itself is a resource worth hoarding. You tend the sprouts not just for food, but to prove that even in ashes, something green can learn to grow again. You decide to build a sanctuary where silence isn't empty, but full of waiting—waiting for rain, waiting for others, waiting for the world to remember how to be soft. And in this dream, the dust in the air begins to smell like rain.
