…the sky above you fractures into deep shades of violet and bruised amber. 

The wind begins to howl, but the trees don't sway violently; instead, they stretch and bend in slow motion, rolling entirely like seaweed caught in a gentle ocean current. The air grows heavy with the scent of ozone and crushed peppermint. 

The first drop of rain hits your outstretched hand, but it isn't water. It’s a tiny, glowing silver sphere, weightless and cool to the touch, which dissolves into a fine mist of starlight upon impact. Suddenly, millions of these glowing droplets begin to pour from the sky—but they aren't falling down. They are falling *upward* from the forest floor, rushing past your face to meet the heavy clouds above. 

Instead of a clap of thunder, a deep, resonant cello chord vibrates through the soles of your shoes. The sound is almost comforting, urging you to move. 

You run, your footsteps completely silent on the moss, floating slightly with each stride as gravity begins to lose its grip. Up ahead, you spot a massive, ancient oak tree with roots as thick as twisting columns. Nestled between the roots is an ornate, free-standing wooden door with a brass handle shaped like a sleeping fox.

You pull the door open and step through. 

Instantly, the violent pulling of the storm cuts off, replaced by absolute, velvety silence. You look around and realize you are no longer in the forest. You are standing inside a vast, circular library. A crackling fireplace burns in the center, and the flames are a cool, calming blue. Through a large stained-glass window opposite you, the storm silently rages on in the forest you just left. 

You walk toward an oversized velvet armchair, sitting down as a book floats gently off the shelf and lands in your lap. The pages are blank until you touch them, and then ink begins to bloom beneath your fingertips, writing a story about a peaceful forest...
