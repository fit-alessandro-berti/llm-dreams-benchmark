a brush that seems to be made of liquid light. The bristles drip upward, defying gravity, scattering droplets that become tiny birds before dissolving into the shifting sky.

You pick up the brush, and the moment your fingers close around it, you understand—somehow, the way you understand things in dreams—that whatever you paint will become real.

You touch the brush to the canvas and draw a single line. The line hums. It vibrates with a sound like a cello played underwater, and then it lifts off the canvas entirely, floating in the air before you, growing wider, deeper, until it becomes a doorway.

Through the doorway, you see an ocean made of green glass, frozen mid-wave. You step through.

Your feet don't sink—the glass waves hold you, warm as sunlight. In the distance, a staircase spirals out of the sea, leading up to a cloud shaped like a house you remember from childhood, though you can't recall ever living there. The windows glow amber. Someone inside is humming a song you almost know.

You begin to climb. With each step, the stairs change color beneath your feet—violet, then gold, then a color that has no name, one you've never seen while awake. You realize you're still holding the brush.

When you reach the door of the cloud-house, it has no handle. Instead, there's a blank space, canvas-white, in the shape of a keyhole.

You understand what to do. You raise the brush and paint a key—but the brush trembles in your hand, and the key comes out shaped like a question mark.

You use it anyway. The door swings open.

Inside, sitting at a long wooden table, is someone with your face—but older, or maybe younger, it shifts when you try to look directly. They slide a cup of tea toward you. The steam rising from it forms words:

*"You finally made it. We've been painting this place together for years. Would you like to see what's behind the last door?"*

At the back of the room, you notice it now—a small door, no taller than your knee, pulsing gently like a heartbeat.

The brush in your hand grows warm.

What do you do?
