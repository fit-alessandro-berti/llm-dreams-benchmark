**Round 2: The Weight of Other People’s Dreams**  
Now you’re handed a backpack. Inside are everyone else’s expectations — parents, mentors, that one teacher who said “don’t get your hopes up.” With every step up a mountain trail, someone shouts what you *should* want instead. Halfway up, you realize the bag has a zipper. You keep the notes that feel like love, leave the rest at a trail marker, and the pack gets light. You reach the summit first.  

**Round 3: The Final Pitch**  
The last round is a blank stage. No rubric. Just a single prompt: “Show us why the job needs *you*, not the other way around.” Your voice shakes for three words, then steadies. The room shifts and suddenly you’re at the desk of your dream job. The chair fits. The work on the monitor is the exact thing you’d do on a Saturday for fun. Outside the window, the sky is that impossible dream-color that doesn’t exist when you’re awake.
