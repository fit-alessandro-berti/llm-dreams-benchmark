...and you realize with a jolt that your hands have turned into floppy fish. Not just any fish—slippery, rainbow-scaled trout that keep flopping wildly, smacking against the keyboard and leaving little puddles of dream-water on the desk.

Your boss is standing there in an impeccably tailored suit, but his head is a giant ticking clock. The second hand is spinning so fast it's making a sound like an angry wasp. "The client needs this by yesterday," the clock-head says in a voice that echoes like it's coming from the bottom of a well. "Why are your hands... doing that?"

You try to type, but every time your trout-hands slap the keys, the screen fills with bubbles and the words "glub glub deadline" in Comic Sans. Your coworkers are all staring. Some of them have become filing cabinets with human legs. One of them is quietly eating a stapler.

Suddenly you remember: this is a dream. With the last of your rapidly vanishing lucidity, you look your clock-headed boss directly in the face (or rather, in the 12) and say:

"Actually... I'm going to need that in writing. Also, I'd like to speak to the manager of this dream."

The entire office freezes. Your boss's clock face slows down... then starts spinning backwards. The walls begin to melt into warm honey. Your trout hands turn back into normal hands, except now they're holding a comically large cup of coffee that says "World's Okayest Employee."

You take a sip.

The coffee tastes like the concept of Tuesday.

And that's when the ceiling opens up and a giant rubber duck wearing a tie drops down and says, "You're promoted. Your new job is Professional Cloud Architect for the Department of Unresolved Childhood Emotions."

You wake up laughing, still slightly smelling like fish and deadlines.
