You start to sprint down the nearest boulevard, phone in hand, but the map only displays an endless fractal of the same unfamiliar skyline. You grab a stranger’s sleeve for directions; they open their mouth and a flock of paper airplanes flies out, each stamped with a different address. The buildings lean and stretch like taffy while clocks melt or spin backward. An hourglass appears on your wrist with sand flowing upward. At the center of the city a skyscraper of interlocking clocks floats above the ground. A cat in a business suit operates the only elevator and demands a riddle before you can ascend — where a younger version of yourself is already waiting at an enormous conference table, tapping a watch made of melting glass. You realize you are both late and exactly on time.
