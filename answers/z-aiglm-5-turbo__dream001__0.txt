...run, but your legs move the way they do in dreams—that slow, liquid resistance, like wading through thigh-deep water. The sidewalk stretches and stretches ahead of you, and each building you pass is slightly wrong. A door opens onto a wall of clocks, all showing different times. A newsstand sells only maps of cities that don't exist.

You check your phone, but the screen has turned into a small pond with a tiny fish swimming in circles. You know, with dream-certainty, that the meeting is at a place called *The Clearing*, though you have no idea what that means. You ask a woman walking a dog, but the dog says, "You're going the wrong way," and the woman nods sadly, as if she's heard this before.

Then the city tilts.

Not violently—slowly, like a lazy Susan. The street slides beneath you, and now you're facing a staircase that wasn't there before, spiraling upward into fog. You climb. Each step changes material—concrete, then carpet, then loose sand, then piano keys that sound a note when you step on them, playing some song you almost recognize.

At the top, there's a room. A long table. People sitting, waiting. They all have your face.

One of them checks a watch and says, "You're late."

But you wake up before you can sit down.
