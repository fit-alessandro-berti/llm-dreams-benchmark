The floor softens into clouds, then sand, then the texture of old dreams you thought you'd forgotten. Conversations loop back on themselves; people morph mid-sentence into celebrities who died before you were born, or into birds that take flight carrying scraps of your secrets in their beaks.

The room stretches like taffy, doors appearing where walls once stood. Anxiety coils in your chest like a familiar snake, but you keep moving, drawn by the faint pull of that one known face. Finally you spot them across an impossible distance—only when you reach them, breathless and relieved, they turn and it's not them at all. It's you, older and calmer, holding a lantern made of starlight. "You've been looking in the wrong direction," they say in your own voice. "Everyone here is searching for the same person. The gathering is inside you." 

The birds explode into laughter that sounds exactly like your alarm clock, and the entire party folds in on itself like paper, leaving you hovering above it all, watching your smaller self still wandering through the crowd... until the dream shifts again, as dreams do.
