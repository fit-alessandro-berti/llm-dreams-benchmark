He points a finger, and where he points, the air turns into thick, viscous honey, slowing down time itself. You realize that if you don’t start *now*, you’ll be trapped in this amber-like moment forever.

You look down at your hands. They aren’t yours anymore. They’re long, spindly branches covered in leaves. You try to scream, but instead of sound, a flock of paper cranes flies out of your mouth, each one folded from a page of the project brief. They flutter toward the chocolate clock, pecking at it, trying to eat the time away.

The room begins to tilt. The walls peel back like old wallpaper, revealing not the outside world, but an endless library where every book is blank except for the last page, which reads: *"Wake up."*

You reach for the nearest book, but as your branch-fingers touch the spine, the floor dissolves into water. You fall, not down, but *up*, through layers of floating spreadsheets and cascading emails, until you break the surface of a calm, dark lake.

Above you, the sky is a spreadsheet grid. Below you, the water is clear ink. And somewhere in the distance, a phone starts ringing. It’s your alarm.
