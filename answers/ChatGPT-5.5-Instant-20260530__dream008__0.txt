You are at your workplace, and suddenly, you are given a project with an impossible deadline. Your boss is waiting for you to start, and you rush to your desk, only to discover that the computer screen is made of flowing water. Every time you try to type, the words ripple away like fish darting through a pond.

The office begins to stretch into impossible shapes. Hallways grow longer each time you walk down them, and stacks of papers rise into mountains that touch the ceiling. Coworkers glide past on rolling office chairs as if they are skating across ice, calmly carrying cups of coffee while you struggle to keep up.

A giant clock appears on the wall. Its hands spin faster and faster, ticking loudly enough to shake the floor. Determined to finish the project, you gather floating documents from the air and piece them together like a puzzle. As soon as one section is complete, however, it transforms into a flock of paper birds and flies away.

Just when panic begins to set in, you notice a small door hidden behind a filing cabinet. You open it and step through, finding yourself in a quiet library filled with glowing books. One book bears the project's title on its cover. When you open it, every page is blank except for a single sentence:

*"The deadline was never the point."*

The spinning clock suddenly falls silent.

Outside the library window, the office dissolves into a peaceful landscape of clouds and sunlight. Your boss appears beside you, smiling for the first time, and hands you a paper airplane. You throw it into the sky, and it expands into a magnificent flying machine carrying all the work you thought you had left to do.

As it disappears beyond the horizon, you feel an unexpected sense of relief, and the dream slowly fades into something else.
