I feel a storm approaching and the sky splits open like a wound, but instead of rain, thousands of white moths pour out. They blanket the trees, the ground, my arms. The air tastes like copper and old photographs.

I start to run, but my legs move through water — slow, heavy, meaningless. The forest floor has become shallow glass, and beneath it I can see rooms. Living rooms, kitchens, staircases — all flooded with light that moves like liquid.

A crack of thunder, but it sounds like my name spoken by someone I used to know.

The moths lift off the trees all at once and spiral upward, forming a shape I almost recognize — a face, maybe, or a word I forgot how to say. The wind picks up and the trees bend not away from the storm but *toward* it, like they're reaching for something.

I realize I'm not afraid. I realize I've been here before.

The ground beneath me dissolves and I fall slowly — *gently* — through warm dark water into a field of black grass under a sky with no stars but somehow full of light.

And then I hear footsteps behind me.

I don't turn around. But I smile.

Because somehow, in the logic only dreams allow, I know exactly who it is.
