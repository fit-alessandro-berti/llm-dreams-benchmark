You start to pat your pockets.

Your phone is there, but the screen shows only static and the time: 10:00. The meeting is at 11:00. The battery is at 1% and somehow getting *colder* in your hand.

You start to run.

The city is too big. The buildings are too tall and too clean, like they're made of wet paper. All the windows reflect a sky you can't see. No one else seems to be in a hurry. Everyone is walking slowly in the rain that isn't wet.

You try to ask a woman with an umbrella for directions.

"Excuse me, do you know where--" and you realize you don't know where. You don't know the address, the name of the building, or who you're meeting. Only that it's *very* important. The most important thing. If you miss it, something terrible but vague will happen.

She smiles like she knows you. "You're late already," she says, not unkindly, and points down a street that wasn't there a second ago.

You take it. The street is lined with doors. Hundreds of doors, all slightly ajar, all leading to the same coffee shop where you worked when you were 19. The barista in each one waves at you. You check your phone again. 10:00. Still.

You find a crumpled piece of paper in your back pocket that you are certain was not there before. It's an invitation, hand-written in your own handwriting:

*YOU. 11TH FLOOR. DON'T BE LATE.*
* - M*

Below it is a crude map. It's just a circle with an X in the middle that says "HERE."

You look up and you are already *here*. In the lobby of an impossibly tall building. The elevator has no buttons, just a single mirror. You get in and it doesn't go up — it goes sideways, then forward, then somehow inward, with a soft *ding*.

11th floor.

The floor is one enormous, sunlit room with no walls. One table in the center. One chair. And sitting in the chair — waiting patiently, checking a watch, looking exactly as anxious as you feel — is you. A little older. Wearing a better suit.

He looks up and is so relieved to see you.

"Oh, thank god," he says. "I thought you weren't going to make it."

And you finally remember what the meeting was about.
