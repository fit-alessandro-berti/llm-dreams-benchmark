
You trace the grooves with your fingertip, and the patterns seem to shift under your touch, rearranging themselves into a sequence of constellations you don’t recognize. A low, resonant hum vibrates through the wood—or is it metal? The material feels both ancient and unclassifiable.

As you press a particular swirl that now glows with a faint silver light, a hidden seam appears. The box doesn’t open, but a single, rolled parchment slips out from a narrow crack. It’s blank except for a single sentence written in a language that feels familiar, yet you can’t read the words—until you realize they’re not meant to be read with your eyes. The meaning blooms directly in your mind:

**"The lock is the last thing you will see."**

Before you can process it, the box trembles and the patterns flare with a warm, golden light. The air fills with the scent of old paper and something like ozone after lightning. The whisper isn’t in your ears—it’s in the space behind your thoughts.

What do you do next?
