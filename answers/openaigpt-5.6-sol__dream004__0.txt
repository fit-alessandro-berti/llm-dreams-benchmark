The moment you touch it, the landscape falls silent.

You draw a small blue door on the canvas. It opens.

Beyond it stretches a staircase made of moonlight, winding upward into an ocean where silver fish swim between stars. You step through, and gravity loosens its grip. Each thought becomes a shape beside you: your curiosity turns into a flock of golden birds, your fear into a trembling glass sphere, your hope into a warm red thread tied gently around your wrist.

At the top of the staircase sits another canvas. This one is not blank. It shows you asleep, dreaming of a world where colors and shapes are constantly changing.

Then the painted version of you opens its eyes.

It raises a brush.

And begins to paint you.
