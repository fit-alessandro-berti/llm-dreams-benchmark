...and you realize you don't need shelter.

The wind does not blow the trees over; instead, they bend and float as if suspended in thick honey. As your breath leaves you in sudden gasps, you notice that the drops falling from the sky are not water—they are small, glowing letters spelling out secrets you've tried to hide from yourself for years.

You step closer to a lightning bolt mid-air and see it illuminate not the forest, but a bridge made of vines stretching from your feet to the highest branch. It doesn't look like danger anymore; it looks like an invitation. The cold rain turns warm against your skin, melting the tension from your shoulders. And right before you cross the archway of the storm, the sky clears instantly, revealing that the forest has been folded neatly around you all along, protecting a small cottage made entirely of living roots.

You walk through the rain without getting wet, knowing now that the path you were walking was never about escaping the weather, but learning how to swim in it.
