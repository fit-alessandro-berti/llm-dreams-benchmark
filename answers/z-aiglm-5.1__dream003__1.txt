There is no keyhole, you realize, because the box doesn't require a key made of metal. It requires a key made of intent. You press your palm flat against the silver eye. The veins pulse in rhythm with your heartbeat. *Thump. Thump. Thump.*

Inside, there is no velvet lining, no gold, no parchment. Instead, there is a tiny, swirling nebula—a miniature galaxy of blues and purples, turning slowly in the hollow of the box. As you reach in to touch it, the feather dissolves into a fine, glowing mist. It drifts upward, brushing against your face, and you inhale it. It smells of ozone after a thunderstorm, and of a house you lived in as a child but can no longer find on any map. 

The mist fills your lungs, spreading warmth through your veins. The locked box, the room, and the floor beneath you begin to dissolve into that same starry expanse. You realize the box wasn't holding a secret—it was holding a piece of you, waiting to be remembered.
