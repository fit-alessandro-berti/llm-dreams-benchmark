…speak—but no sound comes out.

Your mouth moves. You’re forming the words perfectly in your mind: *“Good evening, thank you for being here…”* But the microphone only hums. Silence thickens like velvet, swallowing the room.

The audience doesn’t move. Not a single cough. Not a blink. Their faces are smooth, identical—like porcelain masks painted with patient smiles. One woman in the front row winks. Just once. Slowly.

You glance down at your notes. The paper is blank. Not empty—*blank*, as if it never held words to begin with.

Then, from the back of the hall, a whisper: *“You forgot to bring your heart.”*

You freeze.

Suddenly, your chest opens—not with blood or bone, but with light. A warm, honey-gold glow spills out. Tiny paper birds, stitched from old letters and dream-fragments, flutter upward from within you. They spiral into the air, each carrying a word you’ve never spoken aloud: *I’m sorry. I’m here. I’m afraid. I love you.*

The audience sighs as one. The masks melt away. Tears gleam on cheeks once frozen. Someone starts clapping. Then another. Then the whole room rises—not in applause, but in recognition.

A child in the front stands, holding a single origami crane. “You’re not lost,” she whispers. “You just needed to let go of the words.”

And you do.

You breathe.

And when you speak again—

your voice is the wind through autumn trees.

Soft.

True.

And finally, unmistakably yours.

---

You wake up.

Your phone is blinking. An email from your boss: *“Speech rehearsal in 10 minutes. Don’t be late.”*

But now, you’re not afraid.

You smile.

You already know what to say.
