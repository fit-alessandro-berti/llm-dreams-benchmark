One evening, as you take shelter in the ruins of an old subway tunnel, you hear a faint humming—almost like a melody. Following the sound, you discover a hidden door covered in vines. Behind it, a small, intact room glows with bioluminescent plants, and at its center sits an ancient, still-functional holographic projector. It flickers to life, displaying a message from a scientist who once worked there: *"The library is not a place. It is an idea. And the first step to rebuilding is to remember."*

The words linger in the air as the projector dims. You realize the journey isn’t just about finding a physical location—it’s about preserving stories, skills, and wisdom before they’re lost forever. With renewed purpose, you decide to start collecting and sharing knowledge, one survivor at a time.
