You descend, not into darkness, but into a vast library where gravity is optional. Books float like birds, their spines glowing in hues that correspond to the emotions they contain: deep blues for sorrow, vibrant golds for joy, and stark whites for truth.

At the center of this floating archive stands a single desk. On it lies a pen and an open notebook. As you approach, you realize the handwriting on the page is your own, but the words are being written in real-time by an invisible hand. The final sentence reads: *"The maze was never meant to keep you in; it was meant to teach you how to write your way out."*

You pick up the pen. The moment your fingers touch it, the maze collapses into a single point of light, and you wake up with the faint taste of ink on your tongue and a solution to a problem you didn’t know you had.
