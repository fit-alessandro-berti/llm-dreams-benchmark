You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching, and the warm sunlight fades beneath a ceiling of dark, swirling clouds. Wind rushes through the trees, scattering golden leaves across the path. Thunder rolls in the distance—not frightening at first, but deep and ancient, like something waking beneath the earth.

You hurry forward and discover a small wooden cabin hidden between the trees. Its windows glow with a soft amber light, though you are certain it was not there a moment ago. As the first drops of rain begin to fall, the door creaks open by itself.

Inside, the cabin is warm and silent. A fire burns in the hearth, and beside it sits a table covered with strange objects: a silver key, a cracked compass, and a photograph of you standing in this very forest.

Lightning flashes.

When you look again, the photograph has changed. In it, you are no longer alone. A shadowy figure stands behind you, one hand raised as if waving.

Then you hear three quiet knocks from the other side of the door.
