Colors explode outward from your touchdeep indigo spirals into electric teal, then fractures into thousands of floating geometric shards that rearrange themselves into impossible architectures: floating staircases that loop into M—bius strips, trees whose leaves are perfect fractals, and rivers flowing upward into the sky in shimmering ribbons of liquid gold.

You realize you don't need brushes. Your thoughts are the paint.

The moment you imagine a crimson rose, it blooms across the canvas in hyper-real detail, its petals unfolding into tiny galaxies. But when you try to focus on a single shape, everything mutters and shifts again. A flock of birds made of stained glass bursts from the rose and dissolves into musical notes that hang in the air like colorful fireflies.

You take a step back and notice something strange: the canvas isn't just receiving your dreams. It's dreaming *you* right back.

Your own silhouette begins to appear on the canvas, but it's made entirely of ever-shifting stained glass. As you watch, the glass version of yourself steps out of the painting, takes your hand, and whispers in a voice made of wind chimes:

"Shall we paint the next layer of reality together?"

The world around you holds its breath, waiting for what you'll dream next.
