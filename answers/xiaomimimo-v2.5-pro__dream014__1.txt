# The Dream Continues...

...and you feel a wave of relief wash over you. Their smiles are warm, genuine — the kind that make you forget you were ever afraid.

One of them, a woman with silver hair and a gentle laugh, takes your hand and says, *"We've all been lost here before. That's how we learned the way."*

As they lead you through the winding streets, the city begins to transform. The cold gray buildings soften into warm amber light. Street vendors offer you small, glowing fruits that taste like memories — sweet and familiar, though you can't quite name them.

You notice something strange: every person you pass is humming the same melody, a quiet song that seems to rise from the cobblestones themselves. The strangers guide you through a garden hidden between two skyscrapers, where flowers bloom in impossible colors and the air smells like rain after a long drought.

At the center of the garden stands an old wooden door standing alone, frame and all, with no walls attached.

*"This is where you were heading all along,"* the silver-haired woman says softly. *"You just didn't know it yet."*

You reach for the handle. It's warm to the touch.

You open the door and step through — not into another place, but into a feeling. A feeling of *belonging*, so complete and certain that you realize...

...the city wasn't keeping you lost.

It was bringing you home.

---

*And then you wake up, but the warmth stays with you.* 🌅
