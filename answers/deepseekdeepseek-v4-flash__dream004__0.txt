...a blank canvas and a single brush resting beside it, its bristles shimmering with an ethereal light. As you approach, the chaotic swirl of colors and shapes around you begins to slow, as if the canvas itself is drawing the world into a moment of stillness. You pick up the brush, and the moment its tip touches the canvas, you feel a pulse—a heartbeat. The canvas is alive.

You dip the brush into the air, and the shifting hues of the landscape cling to the bristles like liquid light. With your first stroke, a streak of emerald green spreads across the white expanse, but it doesn't stay still—it ripples, grows roots, and blossoms into a forest that breathes. The colors you choose become real, but they also become part of you. Every shade you paint changes the world around you: a dab of gold turns the floating geometric shapes into singing birds; a sweep of deep blue creates a river that winds through the canvas and spills into the dreamscape, carrying your reflections.

But then you notice something strange. The canvas isn't just receiving your strokes—it's pushing back, showing you images you didn't intend. Faces, memories, fragments of waking life you’d forgotten. The dream is no longer yours alone. The blank canvas was a door, and you have to decide what to paint next before the dream paints you.
