You decide to stop trying to change their mind and instead try to wake up from this specific nightmare.

But you can't move your arms, because they have been turned into roots that hold you to the floorboards beneath you. The room is stretching, stretching until the walls stretch like gum, and every time I blink, my friend's face flickers—a second ago they are standing with a cup of coffee, and now, just moments later, they are a bird singing in a circle around my head, but there is no music to hear it, just the sound of wind.

You try to grab the edge of a chair, but it turns to smoke. The smell of the argument lingers—burnt sugar? No, something sweet and sharp like burnt metal. You can't decide whether you want to shout or if you want to say nothing at all. In this dream, shouting is what makes the walls come down, and silence is what makes them rise back up.

"Who was I fighting?" your friend asks suddenly, their voice sounding like a record skipping over and over again as they reach out a hand that isn't there anymore.

You look toward the door to your bedroom. It's locked with chains of glass.
