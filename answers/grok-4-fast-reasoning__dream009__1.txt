You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to adapt quickly to this medieval world of thatched-roof villages, dirt roads, and flickering torchlight. The air smells of woodsmoke and fresh-baked bread, but danger lurks—bandits on horseback thunder past, and whispers of a brewing war echo from the tavern. 

Your modern clothes draw suspicious stares from villagers in rough woolen tunics, so you barter your watch (a shiny oddity they mistake for a talisman) for a simple cloak and a dagger. Hunger gnaws at you, and without your phone or wallet, survival means learning the lay of the land. You befriend a grizzled blacksmith who teaches you to forge tools, and soon you're joining a band of travelers heading to the king's court, hoping to unravel the mystery of how you got here—and how to return. But as the dream deepens, the line between past and present blurs, and you wonder if this era is where you truly belong.
