...not on paper, but projected onto the glass walls around you. The questions are not about equations or historical dates. They shimmer like heat haze, asking things like:

*   *What is the weight of a promise you cannot keep?*
*   *Calculate the precise moment a stranger becomes a friend.*
*   *Diagram the connection between a childhood memory and a choice you made last Tuesday.*

You reach for a pen, but your hand passes through it. There’s no desk. You’re sitting on a cloud that feels like firm gelatin. A quiet hum fills the air, and you realize it’s the sound of your own nervous system. The first question morphs, the letters dripping down like wet ink, reforming into a new one:

*   *If your future self could send one sentence back in time to you right now, what would it be?*

You don’t have to write an answer. You just have to *know* it. And as you think, the room begins to change. The walls dissolve into a panorama of potential paths—forests, bustling cities, quiet libraries, roaring oceans—all waiting. You understand that the exam isn’t about having the right answers. It’s about being willing to sit with the questions, and then choosing which path to step onto, knowing the questions will follow you there. You take a deep breath, and instead of writing, you stand up and walk toward the wall that looks most like a question itself.
