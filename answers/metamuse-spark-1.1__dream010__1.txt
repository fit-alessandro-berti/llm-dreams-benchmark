You are participating in a competition where the grand prize is your dream job. As the competition progresses, you face various challenges and...

...and you realize you don't recognize any of the other contestants, but they all recognize you.

The competition is being held inside your old elementary school gym, except the bleachers go up forever and the basketball hoops are made of that soft, acoustic office ceiling tile. A banner flutters overhead that says CONGRATULATIONS ON BEING SELECTED! in Comic Sans. It smells like floor wax and coffee.

A woman with a headset that isn't plugged into anything calls your name. She is also your 4th grade teacher. She says, "Great, you're here! Let's begin."

**Challenge One: The Resume.**

You have to write your entire life's work on a single grain of rice. Only the rice keeps sprouting. Every time you etch a word into it with a tiny pencil, it grows into a little plant, and the plant has more rice on it, and you have to write on those too. All the other contestants are done in seconds. You look over and their rice grains are huge, perfect, glowing. You get anxious, your hand is shaking — and then you look closer. Their rice is blank. They just *pretended* to write something and everyone believed them.

You decide to leave yours blank too. The judges nod solemnly and write down: "Shows initiative."

**Challenge Two: The Interview.**

They send you into a small room for the interview. Inside, sitting behind a massive oak desk, is you. You at 8 years old, wearing a suit that's way too big.

Little you swings your legs and asks, "So, what do you actually want to do all day?"

You start to give the grown-up answer, the one with "synergy" and "passion" and "five-year plan" in it. Little you frowns.

"Wrong answer," little you says.

You try again. You say, "I want to make things that make people feel less alone. I want to have enough time to go outside. I want to not be scared on Sunday nights."

Little you lights up. "Okay! You can go to the next round."

As you leave the room, you realize all the other contestants are still in there, arguing with their 8-year-old selves.

**Challenge Three: The Final Task.**

The gym floor falls away and now you're all standing on a tightrope stretched over a city made entirely of clocks. All the clocks are melting a little, like in that painting. The headset woman says, "For this final challenge, you must get to the other side before time runs out!"

Everyone else starts sprinting, wobbling, screaming. 

You look down at the clocks. Your clock down there has no hands. It never did.

That's when you remember: you're dreaming.

The tightrope, which was rope a second ago, is now the hallway carpet from your childhood home. It's soft under your bare feet. The city of clocks is just the ceiling fan spinning above your bed, throwing shadows.

The other contestants stop running and turn to look at you. Of course — they were all you. You in a business suit, you in a graduation gown, you in the uniform from that job you hated. They all smile, relieved, because you remembered for them, too.

The headset woman takes off her headset and she is also you, but older, kinder, tired in a good way. She holds out an envelope. Inside isn't a job offer. It's a single brass key.

"The grand prize," she says.

You ask her what it unlocks.

She says, "Whatever you want it to."

You take the key, and you can feel the weight of your blanket in the real world. You can feel yourself starting to wake up. You have a choice:

You can use the key to wake yourself up right now and go apply for that job you actually want in real life.

Or you can stay in the dream a little longer, and see what door appears.

What do you do?
