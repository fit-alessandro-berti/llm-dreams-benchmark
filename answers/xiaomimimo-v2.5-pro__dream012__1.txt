I grip the wheel until my knuckles turn white, the ship’s timber groaning as a wave crashes over the bow. Salt spray hits my face like stinging needles. In the dim, swinging light of the compass binnacle, I see the faces of my crew—fear etched in their eyes, but they’re watching me.

*You are the captain. The storm is a test. The sea respects strength, not fear.*

My voice, when it comes, is rough but steady, cutting through the howl of the wind.  
**“Hold fast! Keep her into the waves! We don’t run—we *ride*!”**

I point toward a break in the clouds, a patch of charcoal sky where the stars are faintly visible.  
**“That’s our true north. Lock your eyes on it. We’ll find the calm beyond.”**

A sailor, his face pale, climbs toward me. “Captain… the charts—”  
I nod, but my hands never leave the wheel.  
**“Stow the charts. For now, trust the ship. She’s built for this.”**

And then, as if hearing my words, the vessel seems to lift—not fighting the sea, but moving with it. The crew’s motions become sharper, more certain. The chaos outside still rages, but inside, there’s a growing silence of purpose.

The dream shifts, as dreams do. The storm doesn’t vanish—it softens. The black clouds tear open, and a single, clean beam of moonlight falls on the deck, illuminating a path through the waves. Ahead, barely visible through the rain, the faint silhouette of a lighthouse pulses.

I turn to the crew, a slow smile breaking through the exhaustion on my face.  
**“Steady, now. Our course is true.”**

And somewhere, in the deeper currents of the dream, I know this isn’t just about the sea. It’s about any storm we face—and the quiet, unshakeable act of steering through it.
