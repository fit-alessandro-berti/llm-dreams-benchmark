A single wind chime made of bronze bells tinkles softly in the breeze—its melody haunting and familiar, like a half-remembered lullaby.

As you step forward, a rustling in the nearby overgrown garden catches your eye. An old woman in a flowing brown robe and woven hat emerges, her hands cradling a basket of herbs. She looks at you with eyes that seem to hold centuries.

“You’ve come through the veil,” she says, not startled, but resigned. “The great rift has opened again.”

Your breath catches. *The rift?* You never heard of anything like that—except in a dream… or a story you read as a child about the "Threads of Time" that connect all eras.

“You’re not supposed to be here,” she murmurs, placing a gentle hand on your forearm. “But the **heartstone** chose you. It’s been calling for a century.”

She points to the center of the courtyard, where a cracked stone pedestal holds an object pulsing with faint, blue light—a smooth, teardrop-shaped gem embedded in the rock, glowing like captured starlight.

“It’s your anchor,” she says. “To return, you must prove you carry something the world has forgotten: courage, not of the sword—but of truth. You must speak one truth so pure and unguarded, it shatters the veil.”

You look down at your hands—still wearing your modern watch, its digital face dark and dead. But the stone… it hums.

You understand now.

You need to **tell the truth**—not just any truth, but the one you’ve been hiding from yourself since you woke up this morning… the true reason you were running.

Your voice trembles.

"I was afraid," you whisper. "Afraid I wasn’t enough… afraid I’d fail everyone I love.”

The moment the words leave your lips, the heartstone flares—white light erupts like a supernova across the courtyard.

And then, silence.

The world fades.

But this time… you don’t feel lost.

You feel seen.

And when you open your eyes again, you’re back in your bed—sunlight streaming through the curtains.

But on your pillow lies the stone. Glowing faintly.

And you know… it’s not over.
