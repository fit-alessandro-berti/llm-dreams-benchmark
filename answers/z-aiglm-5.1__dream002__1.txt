...and the sky, moments ago a canopy of dappled emerald, bruises into a deep, violent violet. The wind doesn’t just blow; it arrives with a voice, whispering urgently through the pines until the forest sounds like a thousand people shushing in unison. 

You try to steady yourself, but the moss beneath your boots begins to ripple like water, and the roots of the ancient trees lift from the soil, twisting and writhing as if trying to escape the earth themselves. The air grows heavy, humming with static, and you feel the hair on your arms stand on end. 

Then, the rain begins—but it isn't water. 

The first drops that strike the canopy shatter like glass, leaving behind trails of cold, blue fire on the bark. You hold out your hand, and a drop lands on your palm. Instead of soaking in, it hovers for a fraction of a second before blooming into a glowing, bioluminescent butterfly that flutters away into the darkening woods. 

A crack of thunder shakes the ground, but instead of a roar, it rings out like the striking of a colossal, cosmic bell. The vibration rattles your ribs, and suddenly, the forest floor drops away. You don't fall; instead, you find yourself standing on a bridge of woven starlight, suspended just above the treetops. 

Below you, the storm is drowning the forest in a sea of liquid light, the trees melting like ink into the glowing tide. Above you, the clouds part to reveal not the moon, but a massive, unblinking eye the color of a summer storm. It watches you with a calm, ancient curiosity. 

You realize you aren't afraid. The storm isn't here to destroy; it's here to wash away the world you know. As the luminous rain patters against your bridge, your own hands begin to turn translucent, your veins mapping out like silver rivers. You close your eyes, tilt your head back, and let the storm take you, feeling yourself dissolve into a breath of wind, scattering like seeds across a newly born, endless sky.
