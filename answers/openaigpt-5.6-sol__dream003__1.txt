You trace one of the symbols with your fingertip. The box trembles.

A tiny heartbeat begins inside it.

The room around you stretches into a long, moonlit corridor lined with doors. Behind each door, someone whispers your name in a different voice—your childhood voice, the voice of an old friend, the voice you will have when you are very old.

The patterns on the box glow silver. You suddenly understand: it was never meant to be opened with a key. It opens with a memory.

You close your eyes and think of something you had forgotten—a summer evening, the smell of rain on warm pavement, a hand holding yours as fireflies rose from the grass.

The heartbeat stops.

With a soft sigh, the lid opens.

Inside is not treasure, but a small patch of night sky, deeper than the box could possibly contain. A single star floats within it. As you reach down, the star rises and settles in your palm, warm and weightless.

All the doors in the corridor swing open at once.

Beyond them is the same place: a vast field beneath a violet dawn. In the distance stands a figure you recognize, though you cannot remember from where. They wave to you, then turn and begin walking toward the horizon.

The box dissolves into a flock of black moths.

You follow.
