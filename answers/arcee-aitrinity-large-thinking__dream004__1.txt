
The canvas shivers as you approach, its white surface rippling like water. You reach out, and where your fingers hover, colors begin to bloom—not painted, but *growing*, like flowers opening to sunlight. A stroke of cobalt blue spreads in a slow spiral, then bleeds into a ribbon of gold that curls around its edges. The brush in your hand feels less like a tool and more like a key.

As you paint, the world around you responds. The sky outside the canvas’s frame blushes peach, then deepens to violet. The ground softens from stone to moss under your feet. Each mark you make on the canvas sends a tremor through the dreamscape—a tree outside its border suddenly sprouts crystalline leaves, a distant mountain flattens into a perfect circle and begins to hum.

You realize the canvas isn’t just a surface. It’s a lens. A translator. The dream is painting *you* as much as you are painting it.

What do you paint next?
