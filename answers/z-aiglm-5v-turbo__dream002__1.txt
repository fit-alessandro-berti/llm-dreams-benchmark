# The Storm Dream

...the air grows heavy, electric. The birds stop their singing all at once—not gradually, but as if someone flipped a switch. You notice this detail with the peculiar certainty that dreams grant you.

The sky doesn't just darken; it *bruises*, purples and greens swirling like ink dropped in water. But here's the strange part—the trees around you begin to *whisper*. Not rustling leaves—actual voices, too quiet to make out words, speaking in a language that feels familiar, like a song you knew as a child and forgot.

Lightning strikes without sound. Each flash reveals something different: a door hanging between two branches. A staircase leading up into the clouds. A figure standing thirty feet away, facing away from you, wearing your coat.

You want to run, but your legs move through the air as if it were water—slow, deliberate. The first raindrop hits your palm, and it isn't water. It's a small glass marble, cold and smooth, and when it touches you, you suddenly understand something you've never been able to articulate about your mother.

The storm is here now. The figure turns.

And you realize, with dream-logic clarity: **you are not afraid because this storm came to show you something.**

What does the figure look like?
