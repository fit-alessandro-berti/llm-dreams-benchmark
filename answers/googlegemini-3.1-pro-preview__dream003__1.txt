As I lean in closer, a flock of microscopic, glowing birds bursts from the miniature canopy, flying up and out of the box. As they pass my face, they brush against my cheek, turning into cold snowflakes before melting into my skin.

Suddenly, the perspective folds in on itself. I am no longer holding the box; I am *falling* into it.

In every reflection, I am holding the same locked box, but this time, the intricate patterns on the wood exactly match the lines on my own palms. 

I look down at my hands. They are made of carved dark wood. I am the box.
