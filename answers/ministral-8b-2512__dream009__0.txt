**The Morning After**
You wake to the scent of damp earth and woodsmoke, your body stiff from unfamiliar bedding—perhaps a woven mat or a rough-hewn pallet of straw. The air is thick with the hum of cicadas or the distant lowing of cattle. Sunlight filters through a thatched roof, casting shifting patterns on the walls. No clocks, no electricity—just the slow turn of a sundial’s shadow outside.

You’re in a **medieval village**, but not the romanticized one from stories. The thatched roofs sag under rain, the streets are muddy ruts, and the villagers eye you with a mix of curiosity and suspicion. The year is **1348**, the Black Death is ravaging Europe, and you’re a stranger in a land where even the air feels tainted by fear.

**The Realization**
You check your hands—no watches, no phone. Your clothes are strange: the fabric is too fine for a peasant, the cut too modern. A child points and whispers to their mother, *"The witch’s clothes."* You try to explain, but your words sound like gibberish to them. They don’t understand "21st century" or "AI." They think you’re either a sorcerer, a lost noble, or worse—a spy for the devil.

**The Need: Survival**
You’re hungry. The villagers have no concept of "fast food" or "convenience stores." You must:
- **Find food**: Scavenge from a market (if you’re lucky) or forage—berries, roots, whatever grows. But beware: some plants are poisonous, and starving is a real risk.
- **Find shelter**: The village inn is crowded, and the innkeeper charges you in grain or coin (which you don’t have). Maybe you’ll be forced to sleep in a barn or under a bridge.
- **Find answers**: You need to know where you are, why you’re here, and how to get back. But no one believes your stories. A wandering scholar (if you’re lucky) might take pity on you—or sell you to a noble for ransom.

**The Twist: A Hidden Gift**
As you struggle, you notice something strange—**your modern knowledge isn’t entirely useless**. You can:
- **Heal**: You remember basic first aid. A child with a cut? You clean it with wine and herbs. The villagers call you a healer.
- **Build**: You fix a broken tool or mend a fence with surprising skill. A blacksmith offers you work in exchange for food.
- **Predict**: You notice patterns in the weather or stars (if you’re in a rural area). A farmer might hire you to predict the harvest.

**The Choice**
Do you:
1. **Flee**: Try to find a ship or a traveler who might take you back. But how? You don’t know the roads, and bandits lurk in the woods.
2. **Stay**: Learn the old ways. Marry, raise a family, become part of this world. But you’ll never see your old life again.
3. **Change**: Use your knowledge to help this era—build a better plow, invent a primitive "gunpowder" weapon, or even warn of the plague before it spreads. But is that ethical?

**The Dream’s Ending**
As you stand at the village well, a traveler arrives—a merchant from the east, speaking in a language you almost recognize. They offer you passage on a ship… but it’s heading west, toward the New World. You have a choice: go back to the unknown future, or sail into the unknown past.

You wake up—**breathless**, your sheets damp with sweat. The world outside is normal again, but for a moment, you *remembered* the scent of smoke and the weight of a wooden spoon in your hand.
