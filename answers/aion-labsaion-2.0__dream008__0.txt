You feel a cold sweat break out on your forehead. The project brief in your hands seems to writhe, the letters swimming and rearranging into ancient, unreadable symbols. Your boss’s expectant smile is frozen, his eyes unblinking, like a porcelain doll’s. You open your mouth to protest the deadline, but instead of words, a swarm of tiny, metallic butterflies pours out, fluttering towards the fluorescent lights overhead.

The office floor softens beneath your feet, becoming a spongy, moss-covered ground. Your cubicle walls sprout vines and bright, poisonous-looking flowers. The impossible deadline is now a physical object—a large, ticking grandfather clock in the corner, but its hands are spinning backwards at a dizzying speed.

You look at your boss, but he has transformed into a giant, benevolent owl perched on what was your office chair. He hoots softly, “The project is not in the documents. It is in the hive.” He gestures with a wing towards the window, which now looks out onto a massive, pulsating honeycomb structure suspended in a peach-colored sky.

A sense of urgency mixes with profound curiosity. You know, in the way one knows things in dreams, that you must climb out the window and enter the honeycomb. Each hexagon contains a memory, a skill, a forgotten idea. Completing the project means navigating this labyrinth of your own mind before the clock’s hands spin back to zero.

You take a step towards the window, the mossy floor sighing beneath your step. The metallic butterflies land on your shoulders, their tiny wings whirring like gears. You are ready to begin.
