Your heart starts pounding. You glance around the room—everyone else is calmly writing, their pens moving with eerie confidence. The clock on the wall is melting, dripping down the plaster like a Dali painting. 

Then you notice something even stranger: the questions aren't about the subject you studied at all. 

Question 1: "What did the moon whisper to you on the night you were born?"

Question 2: "If your shadow could speak, what secret would it reveal about the version of you that never existed?"

Question 3: "Solve for the color of regret."

You suddenly realize—this isn't an exam for your career.

This is the test that decides what kind of dream you'll be allowed to have for the rest of your life.

And you left your dreams in your other pants. 

The professor leans over your shoulder. It's you. Older. Sadder. Whispering:

"You studied the wrong life, didn't you?"

What do you do?
