...build a massive loom out of scavenged bicycle spokes, rusted rebar, and copper wire. I am not weaving cloth; I am trying to stitch the fraying, static-filled edges of the sky back together.

The "thread" I use isn't physical. I reach up into the heavy, grey air and pull down the silver, humming light of a fractured moon. It spools into my hands, cool and vibrating like a plucked guitar string. In this dream, my hands don't blister or bleed. They move with a blurring, impossible speed, guided by a muscle memory I didn't know I possessed. 

The dangers of this world aren't mutants or raiders. They are the *Silences*—tall, faceless figures made entirely of compacted ash and forgotten regrets. They glide toward me without moving their legs, reaching out with elongated, smoky fingers to unravel my work. Every time they touch the woven sky, a patch of the horizon dissolves into television static. 

But I just weave faster. I tie the silver light to a rusted streetlamp, then to the skeleton of a collapsed skyscraper, pulling the torn sky taut. The physical labor feels strangely light, like moving underwater, and with every pass of the shuttle, the grey clouds above begin to bruise into deep, beautiful shades of twilight purple and gold.

As I tie off the final knot, the dream-logic shifts. 

The giant metal loom softens beneath my hands, expanding and contracting. It becomes a massive, breathing lung. The sky inhales. 

A sudden, violent wind sweeps across the wasteland, blowing the ash and the Silences away into the distance. But beneath the ash, there is no ruined earth. There is only a scuffed, polished wooden floor. The destroyed buildings around me are just painted cardboard flats. The sky I just stitched together is a heavy, seamed velvet curtain. 

I am standing in the center of a colossal, empty theater. 

From high up in the shadowy rafters, a booming, gentle voice echoes through the space: *"Beautiful. Now, let's bring up the lights for Act Two."*

The velvet curtain I stitched begins to rise, and blinding, warm, golden sunlight spills across the stage. I close my eyes against the glare, feeling the profound warmth on my face, and as the light consumes me, I finally wake up—with my fingertips still faintly glowing with silver thread.
