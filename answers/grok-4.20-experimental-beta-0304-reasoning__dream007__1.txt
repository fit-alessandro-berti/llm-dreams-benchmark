**...and you decide to levitate.**

The words are still flying between you—something about whether the stars actually care what we do down here, or if friendship is just two people agreeing to ignore the void together—when your feet leave the ground. The room doesn't notice at first. Your friend is mid-sentence, jabbing a finger toward you like it's a sword, when they realize you're now hovering three feet above the carpet, arms crossed, looking down at them with the serene expression of someone who has just cheated at reality.

"Wait," they say, the heat draining from their voice. "That's not fair."

"Neither is being wrong," you reply, drifting lazily toward the ceiling. Your voice echoes like it's coming from underwater. The light fixture above you starts blooming into tiny galaxies, spinning slowly.

They try to stay mad. They really do. But their feet are lifting too, betraying them. Soon you're both floating in this ridiculous argument in the middle of the air, voices getting softer as the walls melt into starfields and the floor becomes an ocean of ink. The fight keeps going, but now it's gentle, almost tender, like two astronauts arguing about the color of home while drifting through the same beautiful disaster.

Eventually you both start laughing because none of it matters and all of it does, and the dream lets you stay there—weightless, unresolved, and strangely at peace—until the alarm clock (or the void) decides it's time to wake up.
