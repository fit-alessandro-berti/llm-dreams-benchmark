You are about to give a speech to a large audience. As you step onto the stage, you see the crowd looking expectantly at you, and you begin to **realize that the podium is made of a thousand tiny, shimmering feathers, each one whispering a different, nonsensical word. The microphone, instead of being metal, feels like a cool, smooth river stone, and as you bring it to your lips, it hums a forgotten melody that you somehow know all the lyrics to. You open your mouth to speak, and the first word that comes out is not yours, but a burst of iridescent bubbles that float and pop, releasing the scent of rain on hot pavement and the laughter of children. The audience, instead of looking bored or impatient, leans forward, their faces illuminated by the gentle glow of the popping bubbles, their mouths open in silent astonishment. You try again, and this time, a single, perfect, bright red apple rolls out of your mouth and lands on the stage with a soft thud. You pick it up, and it feels warm, as if it's just been plucked from a sun-drenched tree. The audience gasps, and a few people in the front row start to clap, not for your words, but for the apple. You look down at your notes, but they are no longer paper; they are woven from strands of moonlight, and the words are written in constellations that shift and rearrange themselves as you watch. You feel a strange sense of calm, as if this illogical performance is exactly what everyone has been waiting for. You take a bite of the apple. It tastes like starlight and your happiest childhood memory. As you chew, the audience begins to hum the forgotten melody with you, and the feathered podium rustles in time, each whisper adding a new, vibrant color to the air. The speech you were meant to give is gone, replaced by this shared, sensory experience, and you feel a profound connection to every single person watching you, a connection forged in the magic of impossible things.**
