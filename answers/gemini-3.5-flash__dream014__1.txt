The screech of a subway train transforms into the distant, rhythmic sigh of ocean waves. 

As you walk together, the towering skyscrapers on either side of the street begin to stretch and curve upward, transforming into a canopy of ancient, glowing willow trees. The asphalt beneath your feet turns to cool, damp sand. You realize you aren't walking anymore; you are gliding, effortlessly, guided by the gentle pull of their hands. 

"We're almost there," one of them whispers, their voice sounding like wind chimes in a quiet breeze. 

Ahead, in the middle of the glowing forest, stands a simple wooden door painted a familiar, faded blue—the exact door to your childhood bedroom. There are no walls around it, just the door, standing upright under the starlight. 

The strangers let go of your hands, smiling warmly, and step back into the shadows of the trees. You realize you are no longer lost. You never were. 

You reach out, turn the brass doorknob, and as the door swings open, a warm, golden light spills out, gently pulling you awake.
