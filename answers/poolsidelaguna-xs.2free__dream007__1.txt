
You're standing your ground, fists clenched, but your friend’s voice rises—sharp, desperate. The room spins. Suddenly, the walls melt into a starry void, and your friend’s face flickers between their usual self and a shadowy figure. They reach for your hand, but it passes through like smoke.  

“Wait,” they whisper, but their voice echoes backward. The argument shifts—you’re now children again, laughing in a sunlit field, but the laughter twists into static. A clocktower looms nearby, its hands spinning counterclockwise. You realize the fight was never about the *thing* you argued about… it was about a memory you both forgot.  

The dream fades as their voice softens: *“We’re still here, you know. Even if the world forgets.”*  

You wake with the taste of ozone and a single silver hair on your pillow.
