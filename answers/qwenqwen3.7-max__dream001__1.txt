The battery drops from 84% to 12% in a single blink.

Panic begins to flutter in your chest. You look up and ask a passing woman in a bright yellow coat for directions. When she turns to face you, her face is a smooth, featureless mirror reflecting your own wide-eyed expression. She doesn't speak, but raises a hand and points down a narrow alleyway that you are absolutely certain wasn't there a second ago.

With only forty minutes left, you duck into the alley. Instantly, the environment shifts. The brick walls stretch upward, transforming into towering, mahogany bookshelves. The sky above becomes a ceiling of frosted glass, and the air suddenly smells heavily of old paper and ozone. You aren't outside anymore; you’re inside an impossibly vast, labyrinthine archive. 

You check your wrist. Your watch has no numbers, just a single red hand spinning wildly counter-clockwise. 

"Hello?" you call out. Your voice echoes, but the echo sounds like a chorus of whispering voices. 

At the far end of a long, dimly lit corridor, you spot a massive oak door with a brass plaque. You sprint toward it, but the Persian carpet beneath your feet seems to crawl in the opposite direction, making every step feel like a monumental effort. The whispering grows louder, coalescing into a muffled, rhythmic ticking. 

With only five minutes to spare, you finally reach the door. You grab the cold brass handle, push it open, and burst into the room, gasping for air. 

"I'm here! I made it!" you announce.

But the words die in your throat. It isn't a boardroom or an office. It’s a perfect, sunlit replica of your childhood bedroom. Dust motes dance in the golden light filtering through the window. 

Sitting on the edge of the bed is a figure made entirely of folded, white origami paper. As you watch, the paper folds and unfolds itself in a mesmerizing, silent rhythm. On the nightstand beside it sits a single, pristine manila folder with your name typed on the tab.

You walk over, your footsteps completely silent on the hardwood floor, and open the folder. Inside is a single piece of paper. There are no meeting minutes, no contracts, no agendas. There is only one sentence, written in your own handwriting:

*You have nowhere else you need to be.*

The origami figure stops moving. The golden sunlight in the room begins to brighten, shifting from yellow to a blinding, warm white. The ticking sound softens, slowing down until it matches the steady, calm rhythm of your own heartbeat. The walls of the bedroom begin to dissolve into mist, the anxiety completely draining from your body, leaving behind a profound, heavy peace as the white light washes over you, and you finally wake up.
