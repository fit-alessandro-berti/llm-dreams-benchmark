A woman in a shimmering silver dress stops you, pressing a heavy, cold key into your palm. "You forgot this last time," she whispers, her eyes entirely black. Before you can ask what she means, the crowd surges forward like a tidal wave of coats and laughter, swallowing her whole.

The air grows thick and humid, smelling faintly of ozone and old paper. The walls of the venue begin to breathe, expanding and contracting rhythmically. You realize with a jolt that the floor beneath your feet is no longer wood, but water—shallow, clear, and rippling with every step you take.

Panic rises, but it’s a distant, muffled panic, as if heard through thick glass. You look down at your hand; the key has melted into a puddle of mercury that drips onto the water-floor, creating ripples that form perfect, geometric shapes.

Then, you see them.

Your friend stands at the far end of the hall, which now stretches infinitely long, like a tunnel made of mirrors. They are waving, but their arm moves in slow motion, looping the same gesture over and over again. As you try to run toward them, your legs feel heavy, rooted to the spot. The other guests turn in unison, their faces blank masks, and begin to hum a single, low note that vibrates in your teeth.

You open your mouth to call out, but instead of sound, bubbles rise from your lips. The humming grows louder, transforming into the rhythmic beeping of a hospital monitor.

The mirror-tunnel shatters.

You fall upward, into a sky filled with floating books. One lands gently in your lap. It’s open to a page titled *Chapter 4: The Gathering*. You start to read, realizing with a sudden, calm clarity that you have been reading this dream before.
