You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to find water—fast.

The morning sun hangs heavy and gold over a landscape of rolling hills cloaked in wild grasses, distant oaks whispering in a wind that smells of damp earth and woodsmoke. No roads. No cars. No phones. Just the crunch of your sneakers on ancient soil, and the distant crow of a rooster that sounds like it’s been calling since the dawn of time.

Your throat is parched. Your canteen, empty. You stumble toward a narrow ribbon of silver winding through the valley—a stream. But as you kneel to drink, you freeze.

A figure stands on the opposite bank.

Clad in a tunic of wool and leather, with a bronze dagger at her hip and hair woven with amber beads, she regards you with calm, knowing eyes—not fear, not anger. Just… recognition.

“You’re the one from the sky-silence,” she says in a tongue that somehow translates itself in your mind. “The one who speaks without breath-voice.”

You gape. “You… you know about… phones? Electricity?”

She smiles faintly. “We have always known the ones who fall between worlds. You are not the first. Nor will you be the last.”

She gestures to a stone altar nearby, etched with spirals and stars. “Drink from the stream—but only if you promise to speak for us. To remember. To carry our voices forward.”

You hesitate. “Why me?”

“Because,” she says, kneeling slowly, “you still dream of electricity… and we still dream of stars. You two belong to each other. Even if you’ve forgotten.”

The water glows faintly as you cup your hands. As you drink, warmth floods your body—not just hydration, but memory. Images flood you: the rise and fall of empires, the songs of forgotten poets, the scent of cinnamon in a marketplace long turned to dust.

When you look up, she’s gone.

The stream is silent.

And in your pocket—where nothing was before—you find a smooth, warm stone. Etched into its surface: a single symbol.

A lightning bolt… inside a spiral.

You wake up—back in your bed, sweat-drenched, heart pounding.

Your phone lights up on the nightstand.

A notification from an app you never downloaded:  
*“They’re waiting. Did you remember?”*

Outside, the distant hum of a refrigerator fades… and for a heartbeat, you swear you hear a bird singing in a language you once knew.

——  
And beneath your pillow, just out of sight…  
a tiny bronze dagger, glinting in the moonlight.
