I realize I don't need to say anything important. The stage beneath my feet begins to ripple like water, and I step off the edge, expecting to fall, but I float instead. The crowd dissolves into a forest of tall, whispering willows, and I understand that the speech wasn't for them—it was for the sky.

I open my mouth again, and this time, a single bird flies out, carrying a key made of light. I catch it, and the world tilts. The ceiling of the auditorium vanishes, replaced by a vast, starry ocean. I am not standing; I am swimming through the air, weightless and silent. Then, a familiar voice calls my name from far away, and the stars begin to fade into the soft, gray light of a morning window. I close my eyes, and the dream slips away like water through my fingers.
