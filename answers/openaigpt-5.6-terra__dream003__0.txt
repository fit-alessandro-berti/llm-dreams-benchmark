…symbols etched into its surface—some familiar, some shifting when you try to focus on them. The box is warm in your hands, pulsing faintly like a sleeping heart.

Then you realize the patterns are not decorations at all. They are scenes: a door beneath the ocean, a staircase rising through clouds, a small child holding a lantern in a dark forest. As your thumb passes over the child’s lantern, the box clicks.

A seam opens along its edge.

Inside is no key, no treasure, but a tiny room lit by moonlight. You lean closer and see yourself standing inside it, holding the same box. The smaller you looks up, smiles, and reaches forward.

From within the box, a whisper says, “You have been looking for the key in the wrong place.”

Your reflection places its hand against the inside of the lid. Without knowing why, you place your hand on the outside.

The box opens completely.

For a moment, everything is filled with warm golden light—and then you wake up with your hand closed around something small beneath your pillow.
