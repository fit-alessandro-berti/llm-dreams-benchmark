You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to plant a garden.

At first it feels ridiculous. The ground is gray with dust, the sky a dull metal color, and the wind carries the distant groans of broken cities. But you kneel anyway, pressing scavenged seeds into the stubborn soil beside the cracked shell of an old highway.

Days pass. Water is rationed between yourself and the tiny patch of earth. Some seeds never sprout. Some are taken by birds that look half-starved and suspicious. But one morning, pushing through the dirt like a quiet rebellion, a thin green shoot appears.

You laugh. It echoes strangely across the empty world.

Soon the garden grows—crooked rows of beans, a few stubborn tomatoes, herbs that smell like a memory of better times. Travelers begin to notice the patch of green in the wasteland. A woman trades you a jar of clean water for two carrots. A child helps you build a fence from rusted metal.

The world is still broken. Storms still roll over the horizon, and distant fires still glow at night.

But in the middle of it all, something small and alive keeps growing.

And in the dream, you realize the garden isn't just food.

It's proof that the world hasn't finished dreaming yet.
