...and I do not take my eyes off the horizon, nor the compass, but instead I look deep into the faces of my crew and find their fear reflected back in mine. But then I realize something peculiar about this dream: there is no water beneath us, only a vast expanse of memory and salt-stained glass reflecting the stars above.

I place my hand firmly on the tiller, not to steer away from the crashing waves that loom like mountains behind us, but to guide us directly toward the dark heart of the storm. I shout for the helm to lock, though there is no water to drown in. With a sudden, terrifying sense of calm that defies my own trembling hands, I tell them what I believe even if it makes no logical sense to an awake mind: "We are not lost."

In the center of the cyclone, where logic dictates only ruin should exist, we stop drifting and begin to move forward. The rain turns into silver mist, the thunder becomes a melody, and when I look back at the horizon, the ocean has given way to infinite plains of light, stretching endlessly upward and down. We did not escape the nightmare; we sailed out of it because we finally believed that in dreams, belief alone is sufficient to build a harbor.
