...the walls begin to change color with your heartbeat.

At first, the puzzles were mechanical—sliding tiles, weighted levers, cryptic symbols that clicked into place like the tumblers of a lock. But the deeper you go, the less the maze seems to obey ordinary rules. The corridors breathe. Doorways appear only when you look at them sideways. The floor is tiled with questions written in a language you somehow understand without ever having learned.

You reach a hall of mirrors where every reflection is solving a different version of the same puzzle, and you realize *you* are the variable each reflection is testing. In one mirror you are younger, hesitating before a door; in another, older, already stepping through. The puzzle asks not for logic but for choice: which self do you trust?

Beyond the mirrors, the maze opens into a garden where the flowers are made of folded paper covered in equations. Bees of golden light drift from bloom to bloom, rearranging numbers mid-air. You sit on a stone bench that is also a riddle, and the answer rises in your mind before you’ve finished reading it—because here, thinking and happening are the same thing.

Then the walls fall away, and the maze becomes a single enormous question written across the sky in constellations. You do not solve it so much as remember that you already knew it. The last door is not in front of you, but behind your own eyes.

You reach for the handle.

And then—
