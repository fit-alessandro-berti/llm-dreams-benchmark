The first round takes place in a library where the books rewrite themselves as you read them. You're asked to summarize *Moby Dick* but the whale is now a metaphor for student loans and Captain Ahab is your landlord. The interviewer is a woman with three faces—she rotates them like a lazy susan depending on which question she asks. Her middle face has your mother's eyes.

You pass. The acceptance letter arrives via carrier pigeon that crashes through the window, feathers exploding into confetti.

Round two: a kitchen. You must cook a meal for a panel of judges who are all versions of you at different ages. Seven-year-old you wants dinosaur nuggets. Seventeen-year-old you wants something vegan and performative. Thirty-seven-year-old you just wants coffee and someone to acknowledge the exhaustion behind your eyes. You have twenty minutes and a pantry stocked with ingredients labeled things like *courage*, *compromise*, *Friday nights*, *the ability to say no*.

You serve them a stew made of all of it. Seven-year-old you cries. Seventeen-year-old you takes a photo for Instagram. Thirty-seven-year-old you asks for seconds.

The building shifts. Elevators move sideways. Hallways breathe.

Final round. A door with no handle. A voice—your voice, but deeper, slower—asks: *What would you do if you knew you couldn't fail?*

You answer. The door dissolves.

On the other side: a desk. Your name on the door. A window overlooking a city that looks like every city you've ever loved. The job description is blank. You get to write it.

You wake up before you can read what you wrote.

But your fingers remember the shape of the pen.
