# The Competition

You are participating in a competition where the grand prize is your dream job. As the competition progresses, you face various challenges and each one feels stranger than the last.

The first challenge is a maze, but the walls are made of stacked resumes — thousands of them, printed on paper so white it almost glows. You navigate by reading fragments of strangers' qualifications. *Fluent in three languages. Managed a team of fifty.* The words blur together until you find the exit: a door with your name handwritten on it in gold ink.

The second challenge takes place in a room filled with clocks. They all show different times. A voice overhead says, "You have exactly the right amount of time." You don't know what that means, but you begin answering questions — not with words, but by arranging objects on a table. A compass. A seed. A photograph of someone you love. Each arrangement feels deeply correct.

By the third challenge, the other competitors have vanished. You're standing on a stage with no audience. You're asked to simply *describe what you would do* — not in theory, but as though you're already doing it. You speak. Your voice echoes.

Then you notice the walls dissolving. The floor becomes grass. Someone is handing you a key.

You wake before you learn what it opens.
