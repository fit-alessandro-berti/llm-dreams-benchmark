You start to panic, but then you notice a lamppost flickering in a peculiar rhythm—not on-off, but with swirling colors that seem to hum. Drawn to it, you see tiny etched symbols on the metal. One looks like a clock, another like a bridge. You remember that dreams often answer if you ask them clearly.

You whisper, “Where is the meeting?” The lamppost glows brighter, and a passing cloud above you slowly takes the shape of an arrow, pointing toward a distant skyline dominated by a tower of glass and growing ivy. In your hand, you suddenly hold a transit pass made of warm paper.

You run, weaving through streets that smell of rain and old books. Trams shaped like silver fish glide past without tracks. You ask a woman selling flowers for directions—she hands you a white lily and says, “Follow the one who sings backwards.” Before you can question it, you hear a man walking ahead of you, softly humming a familiar tune in reverse. You follow.

He leads you to a vast plaza where a skybridge connects two buildings. As you cross, the city below shifts like a kaleidoscope. At the end of the bridge stands a door of dark wood, with a nameplate you can’t quite read until you’re right before it.

It says: *Your Name*. You realize you’re early. The door swings open into a room filled with soft light and the murmur of voices. A seat at the head of the table is empty, waiting. As you sit, you remember—this is the meeting about a dream you once had. And now you’re here, just in time to begin.
