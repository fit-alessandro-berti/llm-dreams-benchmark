...a blank canvas that hums faintly, like a tuning fork struck in silence. It’s not just a flat surface; it pulses with a soft, inner light, waiting.  

You reach out, and your fingertip doesn’t touch paint—instead, it *pulls* something from the air. A ribbon of liquid gold unspools from the sky, coiling around your wrist like a promise. You dip your hand into the air, and the gold drips onto the canvas.  

It doesn’t stain. It *sings*.  

A single brushstroke blooms across the surface—not forming a figure, but releasing a wave of scent: rain on warm stone, the first breath of morning. The canvas warps, bending inward like the inside of a seashell. You step through.  

Now you’re inside the painting. Trees grow upside-down from the ceiling, their roots tangled in swirling constellations. A bird made of static flutters past, its wings cracking with tiny lightning. You hear your own laughter—faint, echoing, but not quite your own.  

Then you see it: a child’s drawing, sketched in the air by drifting pollen. A sun with too many eyes. A house that breathes. A door with no handle, but the doorknob is a question mark.  

As you move toward it, the dream shifts. The colors don’t change—they *remember*. A shade of blue flashes—your childhood bedroom, the one with the window that looked onto a field of dandelions. The canvas is still there, but now you’re the paint.  

You realize: you weren’t holding the brush.  

You *became* the canvas.  

And the dream isn’t over.  
It’s just learning to remember you.
