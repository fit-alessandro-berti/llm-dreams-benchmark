You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to **adapt quickly**.

The air smells of woodsmoke and damp earth. Your clothing—soft, strange fabric in a style you don’t recognize—feels entirely out of place. Thatched roofs line a muddy lane, and the low murmur of voices carries from a nearby market. You clutch at the hem of your tunic, wondering what year it might be, and feel a rough patch sewn into the fabric.

A baker with flour-dusted hands nods at you from a stall. “First day in the city?” he asks, sliding a dark, dense loaf toward a customer. You nod, unsure how to explain that you just opened your eyes here, minutes ago. You realize you need **to find food, shelter, and answers—before the sun sets**.

Do you approach the baker, or continue down the lane?
