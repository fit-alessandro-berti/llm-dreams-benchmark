As you take the lantern, the frantic roar of the traffic and the endless sea of faceless pedestrians begin to dissolve into a soft, ambient hum. The towering concrete skyscrapers stretch upward, melting into a twilight sky the color of bruised plums and spun gold. The harsh streetlamps transform into floating fireflies that guide your steps without a map.

You look down at your hands and realize you are no longer cold. The loneliness that weighed so heavily on your chest lifts, replaced by a sudden, weightless curiosity. 

"Where are we going?" you ask, your voice echoing strangely in the open air.

The strangers don't answer with words; they simply turn down an alleyway that wasn't there a moment ago—an alleyway paved with cobblestones that shimmer like wet stars. You follow them, stepping lightly, feeling for the first time tonight that no matter where this path leads, you are exactly where you are supposed to be. 

Around the next corner, the air smells suddenly of rain-soaked pine and roasted chestnuts, and you hear the distant sound of music playing, waiting just for you to arrive...
