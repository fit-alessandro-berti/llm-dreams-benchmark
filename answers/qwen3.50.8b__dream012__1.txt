The stars seemed to fade behind the horizon, leaving only the rough gray clouds gathering over the dark water below. The silence was heavier than it should be here, but I knew I had to listen closer now or something would break loose before the crew ever learned their place.

"Captain's call," I whispered to the stormy water, my voice a low rumble that vibrated in the ribs of my ship as the wind howled through the deck. "Don't move. Hold on."

Then came her voice from the bow—sharp and angry, cutting through my calm thoughts: *"We're losing our way now,"* I heard, feeling the weight of my words pressing against my own ears. "I am not a captain anymore. Not yet." She shouted over the crashing waves. Her anger was raw, a storm brewing within me as I stood amidst the chaos.

"They think we are lost, they want to take it all with them," she cried out, her eyes burning into the dark with the heat of desperation. "They want to see who will lead us home," she murmured, tears streaming down my face as the storm howled past my chest like a physical pain I could not endure.

I thought for a moment that I should run, but instead I knew the way forward led back to where we came from. The ship was battered and broken by the elements, but I felt strong. I held the line of fire against her angry words, trying to keep my eyes on the horizon where she had come.

"I am not a captain anymore," I heard myself whisper to my own heart, the words trembling with every breath in the storm. "We are gone." She cried out from me, and for a second the world went still. Then I saw that she was gone too; the storm had swallowed up her voice as it hit the shore of the storm's mind. But now I realized the truth: there had always been a person like me in this place before—the one who could hold the line through the storm until someone else was stronger to save the ship.

And so, I remained captain, holding onto the anchor of hope that would finally make our path clear once more.
