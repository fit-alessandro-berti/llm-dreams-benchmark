The air smells of woodsmoke, wet earth, and something metallic—perhaps a nearby blacksmith’s forge. Cobblestones press hard against the soles of my modern sneakers as I step out from the narrow alleyway into a bustling, sunlit market square. 

Carriages rattle past, pulled by straining horses. People in coarse woolens, linen shifts, and leather jerkins bustle about, shouting prices in an accent that sounds vaguely archaic, yet somehow I can understand every word. 

*A dream,* I tell myself, pinching my arm. *Just a vivid, hyper-realistic dream.* But the sting is sharp and undeniable. 

I pat my pockets again. My phone is dead, useless anyway. My wallet holds plastic cards that are basically shiny pieces of colored garbage here. If I'm going to survive—or figure out how to wake up—I need three things immediately: period-appropriate clothes, a handful of whatever coin they use as currency, and absolute silence about where I came from.

Scanning the market, my eyes land on a wooden stall draped with cast-off garments and heavy cloaks. An old woman with keen, suspicious eyes watches me from beneath a woolen hood. 

I take a deep breath, step out of the shadows, and try to remember every historical fiction novel I’ve ever read as I walk toward her.
