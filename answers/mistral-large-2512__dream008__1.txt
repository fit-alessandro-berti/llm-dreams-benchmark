**The Impossible Project**

You blink, and suddenly the office hums with an eerie silence. Your boss—who in this dream is *also* your childhood gym teacher, wearing a suit made of sticky notes—taps their foot. "The *Quantum Spreadsheet* is due in 12 minutes," they say, voice echoing like a PA system in a cathedral. "It’s a 10,000-tab Excel file that predicts the stock market using interpretive dance. You’re the only one who can do it."

Your desk has transformed into a control panel from a 1970s sci-fi movie, covered in levers, blinking lights, and a single, ominous red button labeled **"DO NOT PRESS (seriously)."** Your coworkers—now all versions of yourself at different ages—whisper in unison: *"You’ve done this before. You just don’t remember."*

You open your laptop, but the screen is a swirling vortex of numbers that rearrange themselves into haikus about your deepest insecurities. The deadline clock ticks backward. A pigeon lands on your shoulder and starts dictating formulas in Morse code. Your boss nods approvingly. "Good. The pigeon’s input is *critical*."

Then, the red button pulses. You press it.

The office dissolves into a vast, empty stadium. The *Quantum Spreadsheet* is now a physical thing—a towering, gelatinous cube that hums like a refrigerator. Your boss hands you a spoon. "Eat it," they say. "It’s the only way to submit on time."

You take a bite. The cube tastes like regret and lukewarm coffee. The stadium fills with cheering coworkers (who are now all your pets, clapping with tiny hands). The deadline vanishes. Your boss high-fives you, their hand passing *through* yours like a ghost. "Perfect," they say. "Now wake up."

**Dream Logic Explanation:**
- **Surreal urgency:** Dreams amplify stress into absurdity (e.g., the pigeon, the spoon).
- **Identity shifts:** Your coworkers as past/future selves reflect anxiety about competence.
- **Literalized metaphors:** The "eating the spreadsheet" moment turns abstract stress into a physical act.
- **Sudden resolution:** Dreams often "solve" problems in illogical ways (e.g., the cheering pets).
