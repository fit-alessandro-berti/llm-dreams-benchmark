You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to figure out what year it even is.  

The air smells like woodsmoke and damp earth. No hum of traffic. No glowing rectangles anywhere. Just a dirt road, low stone buildings, and people in rough linen tunics hauling water from a well.  

**Your first clue**  
A man passing with a cart glances at your clothes and mutters something in Latin. Not the classroom kind either. It sounds lived in. You catch the word *forum* and he points down the street where a crowd is gathering.  

**What you’re missing**  
No phone means no map, no translator, no way to call for help. Your shoes, your zipper, even the fillings in your teeth would look like sorcery here. You pat your pockets. Nothing but lint and a paperclip. Somehow that paperclip feels like the most advanced technology on the continent.  

**What you need to do next**  
1. **Blend in**. That hoodie has to go. You trade it with a confused teenager for a wool cloak that smells like sheep.  
2. **Find a power base**. Knowledge is your only leverage. You can do arithmetic, you know germs exist, you remember that aqueducts work by gravity. Any of that makes you useful instead of suspicious.  
3. **Pick a problem to solve**. The well water looks cloudy. People here die of things you can prevent with boiling. If you can mime “fire + pot + wait” without getting labeled a witch, you’ve just bought yourself safety and maybe a meal.  

The weirdest part is the quiet. Without notifications, your brain starts hearing things: the creak of the cart, someone arguing about grain prices, your own heartbeat. You’re stranded, but for the first time in years, no one expects you to respond instantly.  

So you take the paperclip, bend it into a crude compass needle, and head toward the forum. If you’re stuck in ancient Rome, you might as well see if Caesar’s still around.
