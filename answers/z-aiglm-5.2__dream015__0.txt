You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to keep walking. You don't really have a choice. The sky has been the color of a bruise for as long as you can remember, and your boots are wearing thin, but something pulls you forward.

You come across an abandoned gas station. The pumps are dry, the windows shattered, but in the back room you find a crate of canned peaches and a dusty transistor radio. You eat a can of peaches in the dark, listening to the static, and for a moment you feel something close to happiness.

Then the static shifts. A voice cuts through, faint but clear.

"…to anyone still out there… there is a place… water… shelter…"

The signal fades. You sit up, heart pounding. You don't know where the voice came from or if you can trust it, but you realize you've been waiting for exactly this—a reason.

You pack the remaining cans into your bag, adjust your pack, and step back into the grey light. The road stretches ahead, cracked and weeds pushing through, but for the first time in a long while, it feels like it leads *somewhere*.

You walk through the night. Shapes move in the periphery—animals, or worse—but you keep moving. By dawn, you see something on the horizon. Structures. Smoke rising.

People.

You pause, weighing hope against experience. Then you take another step forward.

You always do.
