The world reacts. The shifting colors pause, holding their breath. The melting cubes of the horizon solidify into towers of obsidian. You realize then that the chaos isn't random; it is waiting for a command.

You paint a circle. The air inside the circle turns blue and cool, smelling of rain. You paint a jagged line, and the sound of thunder rumbles, though the sky is clear. With every stroke, you aren't just creating an image; you are editing reality. The canvas begins to glow, humming a low, resonant note that vibrates in your chest.

Suddenly, the white surface turns into a window. Through it, you see a room with a window, and in that room, a person is sleeping in a bed. You recognize the room. You recognize the person.

The canvas pulls you forward. The boundary between the dream world and the sleeping world dissolves. You are no longer painting the dream; the dream is painting you. The colors rush into your veins, the shapes settle into your bones, and just as the light becomes too bright to bear, you feel the gentle weight of a blanket, the hum of a refrigerator, and the slow, steady rhythm of your own heartbeat.

You open your eyes, and for a fleeting second, the morning light on your wall looks like a stroke of gold paint, still wet, waiting to dry.
