...written in a language you’ve never seen before—flowing, shimmering symbols that seem to rearrange themselves as you stare at them. Your pulse quickens, but as you reach for your pen, the symbols begin to glow softly, rearranging into words you *understand*:  

*"Answer not with ink, but with intention. Choose the path that aligns with your authentic self, not the one you think is expected."*  

You glance around the room—others are staring blankly at their exams, erasers clutched tightly, mouths moving silently as they second-guess every answer. But you remember, in this dream, you *are* the dream. The questions aren’t testing your knowledge—they’re reflecting your fears.  

The first question appears:  
**"What are you afraid to create?"**  

Your hand moves on its own, and the answer flows in your native tongue: *"A life that honors my truth."*  

The paper shifts, the symbols dissolving, and in their place is a single, simple instruction:  
**"Walk out of this exam hall as the author of your story—not the character in someone else’s."**  

You close the exam booklet. It doesn’t matter that your answers are unconventional, that they might not fit the rigid boxes of the "real world." Here, in the dream, you realize: the exam was never about passing or failing. It was about remembering you were never powerless.  

You stand, and the floor beneath you transforms into a path lined with doors—each labeled with a version of "should." You pause, then walk past them all, choosing a door with no label at all. On the other side, sunlight awaits, and the sky is written in your own handwriting.
