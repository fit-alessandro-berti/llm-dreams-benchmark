The silence is heavy, broken only by the distant chime of silver bells. I feel a strange, pulling sensation in my chest—a magnetic tug guiding me toward a spire that pierces the violet sky. As I navigate the winding alleys, I pass merchants selling bottled laughter and weavers spinning moonlight into silk. No one speaks; they communicate through melodic hums that vibrate in the marrow of my bones. I realize that if I don’t reach the Librarian by dusk, the "blankness" of my soul will become permanent, and I will fade into one of the translucent ghosts that drift aimlessly through the pearl plazas.

"You have come for your thread," the Librarian hoots, the sound echoing like a closing book.

The owl reaches into its own wing and pulls out a single, shimmering golden strand. As it hands it to me, I realize the thread isn't a memory of who I *was*, but a blueprint of who I *want to be*. 

As my fingers touch the gold, the world begins to pixelate. The pearl city cracks like an egg, and the scent of thyme is replaced by the smell of ozone and warm plastic. I feel myself being pulled backward through a tunnel of light, the golden thread winding around my wrist, tethering me to a place that never existed.

I blink. 

The screen is back. The data is flowing. But for a split second, I can still feel the cool sea-glass beneath my feet.
