The air smells of woodsmoke and wet wool. Gas lamps flicker overhead, casting long, wavering shadows that seem to detach themselves from the cobblestones and slither toward your ankles. You are wearing clothes that feel too heavy, too coarse, like burlap sacks stitched together.

You start walking, your boots echoing too loudly in the narrow alleyways. The people you pass don't speak; they only tilt their heads, their eyes wide and unblinking, watching you with a mixture of pity and recognition. You try to ask for directions, but your throat feels full of sand. The words dissolve before they can leave your lips.

The fog thickens, turning the world gray and soft. You panic. You try to remember your name, but it slips away like water through cupped hands. You only know that you are *You*, but the shape of that identity is fading.

Ahead, a tall, narrow building looms, its windows dark except for one on the top floor. A single candle flickers there. You know, with the absolute certainty of a dream, that the mirror is up there. You run, your legs moving faster than they should, climbing the spiral staircase that seems to stretch upward infinitely.

When you reach the top floor, the room is empty except for a large, ornate mirror framed in gold that has turned green with age. You step forward, breathless. You lean in close, searching for your eyes, your nose, the familiar lines of your expression.

But the glass doesn't show you.

Instead, it shows the room you are currently standing in, but empty. You are not in the reflection.

Then, the candle on the mantelpiece flares blue, and the glass shatters outward. You fall backward, not onto the floor, but into the soft, familiar darkness of your own bedroom ceiling. The smell of woodsmoke is gone, replaced by the scent of laundry detergent. You blink, your heart racing, and for a moment, you can't remember if you are the one who woke up, or the one who was left behind in the mirror.
