The sea lifts the ship like a toy, then drops it into valleys of black water. Lightning reveals impossible things beneath the waves: the rooftops of a drowned city, a whale with windows glowing along its side, your own face looking up from the deep.

“Captain,” the first mate whispers, “the stars have fallen into the ocean.”

You look up. The sky is empty.

Then you realize the compass needle is not pointing north. It is pointing toward the cabin door.

You open it, and find your childhood bedroom beyond it—calm, moonlit, dry. On the bed lies a small paper boat, folding and unfolding itself with every breath you take.

Outside, the crew calls for you.

You step back onto the deck, holding the paper boat in both hands, and the storm becomes suddenly quiet, as if the entire ocean is waiting for your command.
