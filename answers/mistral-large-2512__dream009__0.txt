---

You wake up on a bed of damp straw, the air thick with the scent of woodsmoke and earth. The room is dim, lit only by flickering torchlight that casts long, wavering shadows across rough-hewn stone walls. Outside, the distant clatter of hooves and the murmur of voices in a language you *almost* understand drift through a narrow, glassless window.

Your clothes are gone—replaced by a coarse linen tunic and woolen breeches, the fabric scratchy against your skin. Your boots are sturdy but unfamiliar, the leather stiff and unbroken. As you sit up, a wave of dizziness hits you. *Where—when—am I?*

You step outside into a bustling village square. The year, you realize with a jolt, is **1347**. The Black Death hasn’t yet arrived, but the air hums with tension. Peasants in patched tunics barter over chickens and turnips, while a group of armored men on horseback—knights, perhaps—ride past, their surcoats emblazoned with unfamiliar crests. A woman in a wimple glares at you as you stumble, muttering something about "the stranger who speaks like a demon."

You need to **survive**. But how?

### **Step 1: Find Shelter & Allies**
- **The Tavern:** A low-ceilinged inn reeks of ale and unwashed bodies. The barkeep eyes you suspiciously but slides a bowl of pottage your way when you mime hunger. A grizzled old soldier at the corner table—missing an eye, his face lined with old scars—watches you. If you can prove you’re not a spy or a witch, he might offer work (or a dagger in the ribs).
- **The Church:** The local priest speaks Latin, and you catch fragments of his sermon about "the sins of the flesh." If you can convince him you’re a pilgrim (or a repentant sinner), he might offer you a pallet in the rectory. But beware—heresy is a quick path to the stake.
- **The Outcasts:** On the edge of town, a group of lepers huddle around a fire. They’re shunned, but they know the land. One, a woman with milky eyes, whispers, *"You don’t belong here."* She might help—for a price.

### **Step 2: Learn the Rules (Before You Break Them)**
- **Language:** You understand some words (thanks, high school Latin), but the dialect is thick. Nodding and pointing will only get you so far. Steal a book? Bribe a scribe?
- **Money:** You have none. Bartering is key—can you trade labor, a skill, or (if you’re desperate) a "miracle" (i.e., sleight of hand)?
- **Danger:** Accuse someone of witchcraft, and you’ll burn. Steal, and you’ll lose a hand. Question the lord’s authority, and you’ll hang. But *help* the right person, and you might earn protection.

### **Step 3: Uncover the Truth (Why Are You Here?)**
As you adapt, strange things happen:
- **The Mirror:** In the lord’s castle, you catch your reflection in a polished silver plate. For a split second, your modern clothes flicker beneath the tunic. The lord’s daughter gasps. *"You’re not of this world."*
- **The Dreamer’s Mark:** A traveling monk examines a birthmark on your wrist—one you’ve never noticed before. *"The sigil of the Dreamwalkers,"* he murmurs. *"You’re not the first to wake here."*
- **The Portal:** Deep in the forest, you find a standing stone covered in runes. When you touch it, the air hums. A voice whispers: *"Find the key before the eclipse, or you’ll be trapped forever."*

### **Step 4: Choose Your Path**
Do you:
- **Seek the Key:** Risk the forest’s dangers (wolves, bandits, *things* that aren’t wolves) to find the artifact that might send you home?
- **Embrace the Past:** Maybe this is where you’re meant to be. The blacksmith needs an apprentice. The lord’s daughter needs a tutor. The village needs a healer (do you remember any first aid?).
- **Exploit the Chaos:** The peasants are restless. The knights are corrupt. A rebellion is brewing. Do you lead it—or betray it for a chance to escape?
- **Wait for the Eclipse:** But time is running out. And something is *watching* you from the shadows.
