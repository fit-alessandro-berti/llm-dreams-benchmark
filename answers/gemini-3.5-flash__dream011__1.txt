You blink, trying to clear your vision, but the letters refuse to settle. You lean in closer, and the first question finally resolves into words:

*1. Calculate the weight of a secret you kept when you were eight years old. Show your work in autumn leaves.*

Panic, cold and sharp, pools in your stomach. You look around the vast, amphitheater-style lecture hall. The other students are writing furiously, their pens scratching against the desks in a rhythm that sounds like a gathering storm. To your left, a classmate you haven't seen since middle school is weeping silently, her hands turning slowly into blue glass. No one else seems to notice.

You reach for your pen, but when you pick it up, it feels soft and heavy, like a melting candle. When you press the tip to the paper, it leaves behind a trail of blue smoke instead of ink. 

You look back down at the exam. The page has grown. It is now the size of a map, and the questions are multiplying, spiraling outward in concentric circles:

*2. If a train leaves Chicago at 3:00 AM carrying all of your unfulfilled potential, at what time will it collide with your current reflection?*
*3. Translate the sound of your mother’s voice into a geometric shape.*
*4. Identify the syntax error in your own soul.*

The proctor—a tall figure whose face is entirely obscured by the blinding glare of an overhead projector—begins to pace down your aisle. Their footsteps sound like dry glass shattering. 

"Ten minutes remaining," the proctor announces, though you have only just sat down. "Please ensure your answers are written in the key of G-minor."

You stare at the final question at the very bottom of the page, which is glowing brighter than the rest:

*5. Who are you when the lights go out?*

The room begins to tilt. The desks slide backward into a dark, yawning abyss. You try to call out, to tell the proctor that you didn't study for this, that you weren't given the syllabus, but when you open your mouth, only the soft hum of television static comes out. 

The walls of the classroom dissolve into a cool, gray mist, leaving you suspended in a quiet, weightless void. The exam paper drifts away from your hands, floating upward like a dying star. 

Just before you slip into the deep, quiet dark of waking up, a sudden, peaceful realization washes over you: 

*You didn't need to pass. You already know the answers.*
