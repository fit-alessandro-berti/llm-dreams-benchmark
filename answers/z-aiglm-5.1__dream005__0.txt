You approach a cluster of people laughing around a fountain that shouldn't be indoors. As you get closer, you realize they're all wearing nametags with *your* name on them. You look down at your own chest—your nametag is blank.

You keep walking. The room stretches. A woman in a blue dress grabs your arm and says, "You're late for the toast." She leads you through a door that opens into a childhood bedroom you haven't thought about in twenty years. The bed is made. There's someone sitting on it, facing the wall.

"I've been waiting," they say.

You know this is the person you're looking for. You're certain of it. But when they turn around, their face is just—your face. Older. Tired. Smiling anyway.

"Everyone's been you all along," they say. "Didn't you notice?"

The walls begin to breathe. You wake up with the feeling of almost remembering something important.
