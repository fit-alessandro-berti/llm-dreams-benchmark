and you begin to speak—but instead of words, luminous ribbons of color unfurl from your lips, twisting into living story-threads that drift out over the audience. You float with them, no longer chained to the stage, shaping galaxies of imagery with a wave of your hand: mountains of soft bread, rivers of whispered confessions, cities built from half-remembered childhood songs.
