...the sky splits open like a cracked egg, spilling not rain but thousands of glowing monarch butterflies. They spiral downward in a furious golden vortex, and I realize the thunder I'm hearing isn't thunder at all — it's the collective beating of their wings, a deep resonant hum that I feel in my chest like a second heartbeat.

The trees around me begin to bend away from the storm, their bark peeling back to reveal surfaces of polished mirror. I catch fragments of my reflection in each one as I run, but in every mirror I look slightly different — older, younger, a child, a stranger with my eyes.

I try to find shelter but the forest floor has turned soft, almost liquid, and my feet sink slowly as if the earth is trying to hold me in place. The wind picks up and I hear something beneath the wing-thunder — a voice, maybe, or maybe just a word-shaped pattern in the chaos. It sounds like my name, but spoken by someone who has never seen my face.

I reach up to grab a branch and my hand passes right through it. That's when I notice I'm becoming translucent at the edges, and the butterflies are passing *through* me now, each one carrying a tiny warm light somewhere deep behind my ribs, collecting there like embers.

I stop running. Something tells me the storm isn't chasing me.

It was always trying to catch me the way you catch someone's attention — gently, insistently — because I was the one who was asleep, and the forest had been waiting a very long time for me to stop pretending I was awake.
