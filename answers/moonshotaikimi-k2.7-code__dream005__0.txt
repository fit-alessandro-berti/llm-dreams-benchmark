…the rooms keep changing shape. You pass through a doorway that was a closet a moment ago, and suddenly you’re in a garden courtyard under glass chandeliers. A circle of people in matching pastel suits turns in unison and applauds, though you don’t know why. You smile, trying to look like you belong, and keep moving.

You ask a woman holding a peacock feather if she’s seen your friend, but she only answers in song lyrics. A group near the punch bowl is laughing at a joke that hasn’t been told yet; when you approach, the laughter stops, and they all stare at you with the same gentle, unreadable expression. You try to describe your friend, but the words come out wrong—*tall, but maybe short; dark hair, or perhaps light; someone I once knew very well*—and each person nods as though they understand completely, then points in a different direction.

You climb a spiral staircase that sinks slightly under every step, like breathing. At the landing, a band is playing music that sounds like the memory of a birthday party. The bass player looks almost like your friend, but when you get closer the face rearranges itself into a stranger’s. “They were just here,” the stranger says kindly, and hands you a mask that looks like your own face.

You put it on without thinking, and now everyone seems to recognize you. They pull you into conversations about places you’ve never been and introduce you to people who share your name. You laugh at stories you can’t remember, but underneath the mask your heart is still scanning the crowd.

Then the lights flicker, and the whole room tilts. The crowd parts in a slow wave, and at the far end of the hall you finally see them—your one familiar person—standing in front of a door that opens onto daylight. They haven’t noticed you yet. You push through the warm bodies, but the floor becomes softer, deeper, like walking through honey. You call out, yet your voice is muffled by the mask.

You reach up to tear it off, and as you do, the room begins to dissolve. The music stretches into a single long note. Your friend turns around, smiles, and waves once—slowly, as if from a great distance.

You wake with the sound of applause still echoing, uncertain whether you ever reached them, or whether, in the dream, just knowing they were there was enough.
