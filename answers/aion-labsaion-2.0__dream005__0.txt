You move through the crowded room, the murmur of voices blending into a single oceanic hum. The first group you pass is laughing uproariously, but their faces are blurred, as if smudged by a painter's thumb. One of them turns to you and says, "We were just talking about you," but you don't recognize any of them. You smile politely and drift away.

The next cluster is gathered around a grand piano, but no one is playing. They are all listening intently to the silent keys. Someone mentions your friend's name—"Oh, he was just here"—and points towards an archway draped with velvet. You push through the curtain and find yourself not in another room, but in a sun-drenched garden party at midday, though you were sure it was night. The groups here are frozen in mid-gesture, like statues. You walk between them, and as you do, their eyes follow you, though their heads don't move.

You see a familiar silhouette by a fountain and your heart lifts. You call out, but the figure turns and it's a stranger wearing your own face, smiling an empty smile. "Looking for me?" they ask. Behind them, the one person you know is suddenly there, waving, but they are small and far away, at the end of an elongating corridor that has opened up in the lawn. You start to run towards them, but the ground softens like taffy, slowing your steps. The voices around you rise in a chorus, repeating your name, and the sky darkens abruptly to a starless navy. Just as you reach out, your friend turns and you see they have no features at all, just a smooth, blank oval. You wake up with a start, the echo of the party still ringing in your ears.
