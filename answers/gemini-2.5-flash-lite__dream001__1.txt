...I start to **run**.

Not a panicked, flailing run, but a focused, desperate sprint. The city is a dizzying kaleidoscope of impossibly tall buildings that twist and shimmer like heat haze. The streets are paved with a material that hums softly under my feet, and the air is thick with the scent of something sweet and metallic, like blooming circuit boards.

I glance at my wrist. There's no watch. My pockets are empty. The important meeting gnaws at me, a heavy, undefined dread. What is it about? Who is it with? The knowledge is *almost* there, a phantom limb of my consciousness, but just out of reach.

I weave through throngs of people who move with a fluid, almost choreographed grace. They're dressed in iridescent fabrics that shift color with every step, their faces serene and unreadable. They don't react to my urgency, their gazes sliding past me as if I'm a glitch in the perfect design of their reality.

I try to ask for directions, but my voice comes out as a jumble of half-formed syllables, like trying to speak through static. A woman with eyes like polished obsidian turns her head, and for a fleeting second, I see a tiny, intricate city reflected in their depths. She offers a small, almost imperceptible smile, then continues on her way.

The buildings blur into streaks of impossible geometry. One moment I'm on a wide boulevard, the next I'm squeezed into a narrow alleyway where steam vents hiss with a chorus of alien whispers. I see signs, but the characters are fluid, shifting like liquid gold. I try to focus, to make sense of them, but they taunt me with their illegibility.

A pang of anxiety, cold and sharp, shoots through me. An hour. I have an hour. The weight of the unknown meeting presses down, heavier than the oppressive atmosphere of the city. What if I miss it? What are the consequences? My mind races, conjuring images of lost opportunities, broken promises, a future dissolving into dust.

I see a towering structure in the distance, its peak shrouded in a nebula of soft light. It feels significant, a landmark in this disorienting labyrinth. I surge towards it, my lungs burning, my legs aching. Perhaps the meeting is there. Perhaps the answer, whatever it is, lies within its radiant embrace.

As I get closer, I realize the building isn't made of brick or steel, but of something that looks like crystallized time. Gears, impossibly delicate and intricate, interlock and spin silently within its translucent walls, each one representing a moment, a decision, a possibility.

I reach the base, and a colossal door slides open, revealing an interior that pulses with a gentle, rhythmic light. I can feel a presence within, a quiet hum of expectation. My heart pounds, a frantic drumbeat against my ribs. I have minutes, maybe less, now.

I step inside, my footsteps echoing in the vast, silent hall. The air is warm and still. In the center of the hall, bathed in a soft, golden glow, is a single, empty chair. The meeting. It's right here.

And then, a voice, not spoken aloud, but resonating directly within my mind, calm and ancient, says:

"You are exactly where you need to be. The meeting is with yourself. The hour has already begun."

And I look at the empty chair, and I realize that the most important meeting of all is the one I've been avoiding. The dread doesn't dissipate, but it transforms, becoming a strange kind of anticipation. I take a breath, a deep, shaky breath, and slowly, I sit down. The dream, for now, holds its breath with me.
