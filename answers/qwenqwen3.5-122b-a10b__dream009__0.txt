You walk toward the horizon where the wheat meets the sky, your pockets heavy with keys that don't fit any lock. A clock tower strikes thirteen times, and the hands begin to melt like wax. You understand now that this isn't a place you can stay. You need to find the door that isn't there, the one hidden behind the baker's shop, before the sun rises in the west.

As you reach for the invisible handle, the ground beneath you softens into mist. The wheat turns to feathers, the gas lamps flare into stars, and the cold air rushes out of your lungs. You are falling upward, the smell of coal fading into the scent of your own pillow, and just as the lighthouse beam cuts through the dark, you open your eyes.
