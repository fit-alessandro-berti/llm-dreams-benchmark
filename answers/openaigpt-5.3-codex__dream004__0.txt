…without touching it, the canvas begins to breathe.

At first it’s only a soft ripple, like wind across water. Then colors spill into it from the air around you—liquid cobalt, molten gold, a green that hums like a violin note. Shapes drift past: a staircase made of moonlight, a flock of square birds, a door that opens into a heartbeat.

You lift your hand, and the canvas listens.

A single line appears—then curves, blooms, and fractures into a whole horizon. Mountains unfold like paper cranes. Rivers run upward into a sky full of floating lantern-fish. Every thought you almost think becomes paint: old laughter turns into orange spirals, forgotten fears into gentle gray rain, hope into a bright, impossible sun with many eyes.

When you step closer, you realize the painting is not in front of you.

It is a window.

On the other side, someone is standing at another blank canvas, in another shifting world, dreaming of you. They raise their hand as you raise yours, and for one suspended moment, your fingertips meet through color.

Then everything stills.

The changing world pauses to watch as one final shape forms in the center of the canvas: a small, steady door painted in your favorite color—waiting for you to choose whether to wake up, or walk through.
