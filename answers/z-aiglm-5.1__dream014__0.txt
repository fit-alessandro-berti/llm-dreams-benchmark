Your new friends point out things along the way—a mural on a brick wall that moves and shifts as you watch, a garden growing from cracks in the sidewalk with flowers you've never seen before, petals shimmering in colors that don't quite have names.

One of them takes your hand. Their fingers are cool and smooth like river stones. "Almost there," they say, smiling.

You turn a corner and find yourself at the entrance to a park. But it's unlike any park you've known. The trees are enormous, their canopies weaving together into a cathedral of green and gold. Light filters through in warm shafts, dust motes dancing lazily within them. A path of smooth pebbles winds inward.

You look back to thank the strangers, but they're already walking away, dissolving gently into the city air like morning fog. You want to call after them, but something tells you it's alright. They were never really strangers.

You step onto the pebble path. With each step, the feeling of being lost melts further away—not because you know exactly where you are, but because you realize you were never truly alone. The wind chimes sound again, and somewhere deeper in the park, you hear the distant, familiar melody of a song you loved as a child.

You keep walking, drawn toward it, feeling the warmth of the dream settle around you like a blanket.
