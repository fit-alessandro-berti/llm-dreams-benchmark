As you move through the warm, crowded space, the murmur of conversations feels like a gentle tide pulling at you. The air is thick with the scent of perfume, roasted coffee, and something sweet, like aging paper.

The first group you pass is huddled in a circle, speaking in low, urgent tones. Their faces are animated, but their words blur into a single, rhythmic hum—like bees in a distant garden. One of them, a woman with silver rings on every finger, glances at you and smiles, but her eyes look right through you, as if you’re a trick of the light.

You drift onward, drawn by a thread of laughter from a alcove. Here, a man in a velvet jacket is telling a story about a ship that sailed backward through time. The listeners are captivated, their heads tilted like flowers. You try to catch the man’s eye, to ask if he’s seen your one acquaintance, but as you open your mouth, his story shifts—he’s now talking about a clock that runs on whispers, and everyone laughs again, a sound like cracking ice.

The room seems to elongate. The floorboards stretch between groups like bridges over a silent, dark sea. You see a woman playing a chess game against herself, her two hands moving with opposing intentions. Another person stands alone, staring at a painting on the wall—a landscape that seems to rearrange itself when you’re not looking directly at it.

You start to feel a gentle panic, not of being lost, but of forgetting why you’re searching. The music from somewhere—a lone cello, melancholy and deep—seems to be playing the rhythm of your own pulse.

Then, you see them. Across the room, by a window that looks out onto a sky of swirling twilight purple. They’re talking to someone, but they pause, just for a second, and glance over the crowd. Their eyes meet yours. In that moment, all the murmuring, the stories, the shifting art, and the stretching room coalesce into a single point of clarity. They give a small, knowing nod, as if to say, *There you are. I was just about to look for you.*

The dream doesn’t end. It simply settles, like dust after a sudden breeze, and you begin walking toward them. The path is clear now.
