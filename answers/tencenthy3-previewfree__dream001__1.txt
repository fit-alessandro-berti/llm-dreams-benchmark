You start to run, though your feet feel like they’re moving through invisible honey. The sidewalk beneath you shifts—tiles turning into cobblestones, then soft moss, then solid glass that threatens to crack with every heavy step. The buildings lean in closer as you pass, their windows blinking like eyes, whispering fragments of directions you can’t quite piece together: *"Turn left at the clock that doesn't tick,"* someone murmurs, but there is no clock in sight.

You round a corner and the city changes completely. The air smells of rain and old books. People rush past you, but their faces are blurred, shifting like water—you grip your briefcase (when did you get a briefcase?) tighter, feeling the weight of papers inside that you’re terrified to look at. A street sign swings wildly in a wind you can’t feel: *“Via Del Mirage – Next Meeting: Now.”*

Ahead, a massive skyscraper of white stone looms, its doors wide open like a welcoming mouth. You know, with the heavy certainty of dreams, that this is the place. But as you reach the threshold, the floor inside drops away into a swirling galaxy of stars. You teeter on the edge, heart hammering, as an elevator music version of a song you loved as a child begins to play from somewhere deep within the void.

You take a breath, close your eyes, and step off the edge.
