You start to sprint down the labyrinthine streets, heart pounding, frantically checking your empty pockets for a phone that isn't there. The buildings lean in closer with every step, their windows blinking like eyes, and the street signs rearrange themselves mid-glance—one moment "Atrium Plaza," the next just a string of glowing symbols you almost understand.

A woman in a business suit made of newspaper offers directions in a language that tastes like static, pointing toward a train station that elevates itself on spider legs and scuttles away. You board anyway. Inside, the seats are filled with versions of yourself from other meetings you never attended, all glancing at watches that spin backward.

Time folds: the hour stretches into a golden corridor of endless elevators. You punch buttons for floors labeled "regret," "almost," and "the one that mattered." At the top, the doors open onto a gleaming conference room floating above the city. Your important meeting is already underway… and every chair is occupied by a mirror reflection of you, waiting for the real you to sit down and begin. 

The moment you do, the entire city sighs, folds neatly into an envelope, and stamps itself with the words *Dream Deferred—Please Reschedule.* You wake up just as the stampede of alarm clocks starts.
