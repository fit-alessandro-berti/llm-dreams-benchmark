As you stand there, the morning sun filters through towering oak trees, and you notice something peculiar—a small leather-bound journal half-buried in the dirt beside an ancient oak. Inside, you find sketches of strange devices and notes in a language that seems both familiar and foreign. One entry, dated centuries ago, speaks of a "threshold day" when the world shifts.

A figure approaches through the mist—a woman in period clothing, carrying a basket. She introduces herself as Elara, a scholar from a hidden village that preserves knowledge from lost eras. She explains that you're in 16th-century England, but something is wrong. The village has been expecting a "gift" from the future—a person who can bridge the gap between worlds.

As you spend more time with Elara and the villagers, you begin to notice that your modern clothes and knowledge make you seem almost magical. You help them solve problems using principles they've forgotten: basic hygiene, crop rotation, even simple engineering. But the further you delve into their history, the more you realize that staying might mean abandoning everything you once knew—or worse, changing the past in ways that could unravel your own existence.

One evening, as you read by candlelight, you discover a hidden passage in the journal. It speaks of a way to return home, but warns that doing so might trap you forever between eras, unable to belong in either time.

Do you choose to stay and build a new life, or risk everything for a chance to return?
