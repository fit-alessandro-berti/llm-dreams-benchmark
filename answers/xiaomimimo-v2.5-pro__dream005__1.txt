...you overhear their conversations in fragments, like radio signals fading in and out. One group is passionately debating whether pineapple belongs on pizza (a debate you realize, with dream-logic clarity, has been raging for decades). Another is singing a folk song in perfect harmony, their voices blending so beautifully you stop to listen, momentarily forgetting your quest.

The rooms shift and expand like a funhouse. You pass through a hallway lined with mirrors that reflect not your image, but your emotions—a flicker of anxiety here, a spark of curiosity there. In a sun-drenched conservatory, a woman with kind eyes hands you a glass of lemonade that tastes like Saturday mornings.

Just as a pang of loneliness begins to set in, the crowd, as if guided by an unseen current, parts gently. And there, by a window overlooking a garden that seems to glow with its own inner light, is your friend. They’re not looking for you; they’re simply standing there, watching the light catch the leaves, as if they knew you’d arrive at that exact moment.

They turn, smile a familiar smile, and say, “There you are. I was just about to come find you.” The dream-sound of the party softens to a hum, and the world narrows to that one point of connection. You feel anchored, and all the fragmented conversations and shifting rooms fold into a single, warm sense of belonging.

*The dream dissolves into soft, golden light.*
