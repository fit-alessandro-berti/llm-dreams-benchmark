You are in a world where colors and shapes are constantly changing. As you explore this surreal landscape, you come across a blank canvas and a brush that hums faintly in your hand, warm like something living.

You dip it into the air itself—there's no paint, but somehow the bristles come away dripping with the color of *almost remembering*. You touch it to the canvas.

The mark you make doesn't stay flat. It blooms outward, becoming a door, then a window, then the surface of a lake you once swam in as a child—except you never had a childhood like this one, with its violet water and its sky full of slow-turning clocks.

You lean closer to the canvas, and it leans closer to you. The boundary dissolves. You're not painting anymore; you're stepping through, or it's stepping through you—the grammar of the dream won't hold still long enough to decide.

*and then you wake up, the shape of a single unpainted stroke still bright behind your eyes.*
