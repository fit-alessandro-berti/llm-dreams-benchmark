You are dreaming. Can you complete the following dream?

You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to...

...build a sanctuary—not of walls or weapons, but of stories.

The sky is bruised violet and ash hangs thick in the air, yet your hands remember warmth. You trip over rubble and salvage a half-melted radio from a collapsed library. When you finally coax it to life with scavenged wires and a cracked solar panel, all that emerges is static—then, faintly, a child’s voice reading a poem in a language no one speaks anymore.

You learn that sound.

And then you speak it aloud.

Time coils strangely here—what might be days feels like seasons, what might be minutes stretches like years. A traveler hears you reciting verses beneath the dead city’s skeletal water tower. They stop. Listen. And for the first time in years, tears cut tracks through the grime on their face—not from sorrow, but because they recognize the rhythm of hope.

One by one, others come. Not for food or shelter—they’ve known none for years—but to share what remains: a crushed photograph, a recipe whispered like scripture, half a melody sung wrong but with all the heart in the chest.

From this cracked crown of silence, you forge a fortress of shared memory into something new.
