You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the sky darkens from clear blue to a deep, bruised purple in what feels like seconds. The trees, which moments ago were gently swaying, now bend and groan under the weight of the rising wind. Leaves swirl around you in spiraling columns, golden and amber, as if the forest itself is trying to speak.

You instinctively seek shelter and notice a narrow path you hadn't seen before, winding between ancient oaks with trunks wider than you are tall. You follow it, driven by something deeper than logic — the way dreams often pull you forward without explanation.

At the end of the path, you find a small stone cottage with a warmly glowing window. The door is slightly ajar. You step inside and find a fire already burning in a hearth, a cup of tea steaming on a wooden table, and a chair pulled out as if waiting for you. Yet no one is there.

The storm rages outside, rattling the windows, but inside there is perfect stillness. You sit down and notice something strange — the tea is exactly the temperature you like it. The fire crackles in a rhythm that matches your heartbeat.

Then you see it: a book on the table, its pages open. The words on the page are moving, rearranging themselves, and as you lean closer, you realize they are describing *this moment* — you, reading the book, in the cottage, in the storm.

The last line reads: *"And then you looked up from the page."*

You look up.

The storm has stopped. The door of the cottage is open again, revealing the forest — now calm, now familiar, but somehow changed. The trees seem closer, as if they've been waiting for you all along. You step back outside, and beneath your feet, small wildflowers are blooming where your footsteps fall.

You feel a strange sense of peace, like you've returned something that was lost. And as you walk deeper into the forest, you realize you are no longer afraid of storms.

You wake with the scent of rain still on your skin.
