# The Dream

...I decide to stop.

Not because I've run out of words. I have plenty. The argument has been building for what feels like hours, maybe days — the way time folds in dreams — and there's a stack of points I haven't made yet, arguments that feel devastating in their logic.

But I notice something strange.

My friend is crying, but the tears are falling *upward*. They drift off their cheeks like steam, dissolving into a ceiling that keeps shifting between the roof of my childhood bedroom and a sky full of heavy, bruised clouds.

And I realize: *we're not even arguing about the same thing anymore.*

They're talking about something that happened to them last Tuesday. I'm responding to something I'm afraid will happen next year. We're standing on the same ground but facing opposite directions, each of us shadow-boxing with a version of the other that doesn't quite exist.

So I stop.

And I say something I never say in real arguments: *"What are you actually afraid of right now?"*

The room goes completely silent. The walls start peeling back like pages of a book, and underneath is this enormous, open field at dusk — golden light, no edges, no end. My friend looks at me, and their face keeps shifting between every friend I've ever had, every person I've ever been close to.

They say: *"That you'll leave and I won't know why."*

And then I wake up, and for about thirty seconds, I can't tell if I'm still dreaming or not — and that feeling of *what are you actually afraid of right now* sits in my chest like a stone I need to carry somewhere.

---

There's something honest in dreams, isn't there? The way they strip away the performance of being right and leave you with the raw question underneath every argument: *Am I safe? Are we okay?*

What do you think the dream was really about?
