
You step closer to the canvas, its surface rippling like liquid glass. As your fingers brush it, the blankness shivers—and suddenly, strokes of neon gold and violet begin to bleed outward, forming shapes that defy logic: spirals that hum, geometric creatures that blink in rhythm with your heartbeat, and words written in a language you almost recognize.  

The voice of the dream itself seems to whisper from the canvas: *"What will you paint with the colors you’ve forgotten?"*  

Behind you, the landscape shifts again—the clouds part to reveal a sky painted in gradients of your childhood memories. A path of glowing footprints appears, leading back the way you came, but the canvas pulses urgently, as if it’s waiting for you to choose:  

Will you let it show you a hidden truth about yourself, or will you turn away, leaving it to dream on without you?  

The brush hovers. The colors wait. What happens next?
