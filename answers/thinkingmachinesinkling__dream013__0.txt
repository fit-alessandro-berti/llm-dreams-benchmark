You solve one not by turning a key, but by forgetting the question. The corridor bends upward, then sideways, and suddenly you are walking through the ceiling of the previous room.

Deeper still, the air grows thick and silver. The puzzles become beautiful and impossible: a door that only opens if you stop wanting to leave; a mirror that shows not your face, but the path you haven't taken yet. Your intense concentration softens into something like floating. Logic doesn't disappear—it transforms into intuition, and you realize you aren't navigating the maze anymore. You are the maze, folding inward, solving yourself.

At the very center, there is no exit, only a quiet pool of starlight. You lean over to look, and in the reflection, you are both awake and still dreaming—holding the answer in a language you haven't learned yet, smiling as the puzzle dissolves into morning.
