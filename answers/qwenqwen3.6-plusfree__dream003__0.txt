One seed breaks free, floats upward, and as it passes your lips, it tastes like the word you've been trying to remember all afternoon. The walls around you begin to soften, bleeding into watercolor washes of indigo and gold. The floor tilts gently, not to spill you, but to guide you forward. 

You step onto the black sand inside the box. Your shoes leave no prints. Ahead, a staircase spirals into the miniature sky, each step carved from a single phrase you once wrote in a journal and later forgot. At the top waits a door with no handle, only a keyhole shaped like a question. You don't have a key, but you realize, with the quiet certainty that only dreams provide, that you were never meant to turn one. You simply raise your knuckles, tap twice, and the wood remembers you before you even say your name.
