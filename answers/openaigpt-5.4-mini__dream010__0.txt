In the first round, you’re handed a blank notebook and told to “prove you belong.” You write furiously, but every page fills itself with answers you didn’t know you knew. Judges nod. One smiles and says, “Interesting. You remember what you haven’t learned yet.”

In the second round, you must solve a problem that keeps changing shape. Just as you think you’ve found the solution, the floor rearranges into a maze, the walls become mirrors, and your reflection starts giving you advice. You follow it. That’s when you realize your reflection has a better sleep schedule than you do, a stronger portfolio, and more confidence.

By the semifinal, the competition becomes public. A crowd gathers to watch you climb a staircase that grows longer with every step. Each landing offers a different version of your future: one where you’re successful but exhausted, one where you’re happy but underqualified, one where you’ve taken a safer path and spend years wondering what might have happened. You pause on the middle landing and laugh, because none of them are quite right. The staircase applauds.

Then comes the final challenge.

A door appears with your dream job written on it in gold letters. But there’s a catch: to enter, you must answer one question honestly:

“Do you want this job, or do you want what you think it says about you?”

The room goes silent. Your heartbeat becomes a countdown. You think of all the reasons you started, all the late nights, all the versions of yourself that wanted permission to be proud. Then you answer:

“I want the work. I want the growth. I want the chance to become the person who can do it well.”

The door opens.

Inside, your dream job is not a single desk or a title or a perfect ending. It’s a room full of possibility, responsibility, and people who are glad you’re there. There are challenges waiting, of course. Deadlines. Mistakes. Learning curves. Moments of doubt.

But also: meaningful work, surprising joy, and the quiet realization that the prize was never just the job.

It was becoming someone who could reach for it.
