
You're walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the gentle rustle of leaves transforms into an urgent whisper. The sky, once a serene blue, now churns with clouds that seem to move against the wind—like they're alive, like they're watching you.

As you continue deeper into the woods, the path ahead begins to shimmer and blur at the edges. The trees lean closer, their bark glowing faintly with an otherworldly light. A deer with antlers made of crystallized starlight bounds past you, its eyes reflecting the same stormy sky above.

Thunder doesn't rumble—it *sings*, a haunting melody that seems to come from everywhere and nowhere at once. The ground beneath your feet pulses with a soft, silver energy, and you realize the storm isn't just in the sky—it's growing from the earth itself, feeding on something deep in the forest's heart.

In the distance, you glimpse a clearing where lightning has illuminated a circle of ancient stones, each one carved with symbols that shift and change as you watch. Something within that circle is calling your name, and the storm seems to pulse in rhythm with your heartbeat.

What do you do? Do you follow the call toward the stone circle, or do you turn back to the familiar path you left behind?
