You start to walk, but the pavement feels less like concrete and more like a heavy, dampened sponge, absorbing the sound of your footsteps. The architecture around you is impossible—skyscrapers lean inward like curious giants, their windows shimmering with a liquid light that flows upward instead of reflecting the sun.

Panic flares in your chest, a cold, tight knot. You check your wrist, but instead of a watch, there is a small, ticking clock embedded directly into your skin. The hands are spinning backward, yet the urgency only grows.

You stop a passerby—a person whose face is a blurred smudge of watercolors. When they speak, it isn't in words, but in a sequence of melodic chimes. You somehow understand them. They point toward a spire that looks like a frozen bolt of lightning piercing a violet sky. 

"The meeting is there," the chimes suggest, "but only if you forget why you're going."

You begin to run. As you accelerate, the city begins to fold. A street corner curls upward like a page in a book, and suddenly you are running along the side of a building, gravity shifting effortlessly. The wind smells of old libraries and ozone. You pass a cafe where the patrons are floating umbrellas, sipping tea made of distilled moonlight.

Just as the ticking in your wrist reaches a deafening crescendo, you see the door. It is a freestanding mahogany door standing in the middle of a vast, white plaza. There are no walls attached to it, just the frame and a polished gold handle.

You reach for the knob, your heart hammering against your ribs. You remember the importance of the meeting—the stakes, the faces of the people waiting for you—but as your fingers touch the cold metal, the memory dissolves. You can't remember who you are, who they are, or what is being decided.

You push the door open.

On the other side, there is no boardroom or office. There is only a mirror, reflecting not you, but a vast, starry nebula. A voice, sounding like a thousand whispers, echoes through the void:

*"You're late,"* it says softly. *"We've been waiting for you to wake up."*

And just as you step forward to answer, the white plaza beneath your feet turns into liquid, and you begin to fall upward into the sky.
