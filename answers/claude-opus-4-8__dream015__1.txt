You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to head toward the old radio tower on the hill—the one that's been blinking faintly at night, the only sign that someone, somewhere, might still be transmitting.

The path winds through the husks of cars, their metal softened by rust into strange organic shapes. In the dream-logic way of things, I'm not quite walking so much as *arriving* at places. The tower is far, then suddenly close, then far again.

A dog joins me. I don't remember it appearing. It's the kind of detail dreams love—a companion that feels like it's always been there. Its fur is the gray of ash, and when it looks at me, I understand that it knows the way better than I do.

At the base of the tower there's a door, and behind the door, impossibly, is the kitchen from my childhood home. The blinking light was just the refrigerator. Someone is humming in the next room. The apocalypse outside and this warm familiar space exist at the same time, and somehow that contradiction doesn't bother me at all.

I want to call out to whoever is humming, but my voice won't work—that thick, underwater feeling of trying to speak in a dream.

The dog nudges my hand.

And then—

...the details blur. Did I find the person? Was the world ever really ending? The dream slides away before I can hold onto it, leaving only that peculiar feeling: that I had almost understood something important, and now it's gone.

Would you like me to continue, or take the dream in a different direction?
