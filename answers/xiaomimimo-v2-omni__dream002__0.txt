The air grows thick with the scent of damp earth and ozone. A low, distant rumble echoes through the trees, and the canopy above begins to sway in a sudden, restless wind. The birdsong stops. The once-gentle light filtering through the leaves dims to a deep, bruised green.

You quicken your pace along the softening path, but the sky breaks open before you find shelter. Large, cold drops pelt down, thudding on the foliage like a thousand tiny drums. The wind becomes a gusting force, whipping branches into a frenzy and sending a shower of wet leaves spiraling around you.

Ahead, a flash of lightning illuminates the forest in stark, monochromatic detail for a split second, followed by a crack of thunder so close you feel the vibration in your chest. The path turns to mud, sucking at your shoes. The dream-logic tells you there is a hollow, a fallen oak, or a small cave just ahead—if you can only reach it before the sky falls completely.
