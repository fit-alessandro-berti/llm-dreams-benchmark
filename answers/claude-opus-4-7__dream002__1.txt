The path beneath my feet transforms into a river of fallen leaves, and I'm moving without walking, carried forward by some gentle current. The wind carries voices—fragments of conversations I half-remember, laughter from people whose faces I can't quite place.

A door appears in the trunk of an enormous oak. It's my grandmother's front door, the one with the brass knocker shaped like a lion's head, though she's been gone for years. I know I should open it before the storm arrives, but when I reach for the handle, my hand passes through it like smoke.

The first raindrops begin to fall, but they're warm and they glow faintly, like tiny lanterns drifting down. Each one that touches my skin leaves a small, bright memory—a birthday I'd forgotten, the smell of my childhood kitchen, a song I used to know all the words to.

Thunder rolls, but it sounds like someone calling my name from very far away. The forest begins folding itself up like origami, the trees tucking into themselves, and I realize the storm isn't something to shelter from—it's something I'm supposed to walk into...

And then I notice my feet aren't quite touching the ground anymore, and the sky is opening like a book, and—

*I wake up, the last image dissolving before I can hold onto it.*
