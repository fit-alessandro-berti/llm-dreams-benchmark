Next, a giant clock appears, its hands spinning backward. Time is unraveling. You’re given a choice: freeze time to preserve your current success, or let it flow forward into uncertainty. Heart pounding, you choose to let time move. The clock shatters into thousands of glowing butterflies, each carrying a fragment of your potential future. They swirl around you, forming a path upward toward a towering spire of light—the final stage.

At the top, there is no judge, no panel. Instead, there’s a mirror. But when you look into it, you don’t see yourself as you are now. You see yourself *in* the dream job—confident, fulfilled, creating something meaningful. The reflection smiles and extends a hand.

As you reach out, the dream begins to fade. The last thing you hear is a voice, soft and familiar, saying, *"You’ve already won. Now go build it."*

You wake up with a start, sunlight streaming through your window. Your heart is racing, but strangely, you feel calm. You grab your notebook and begin to write—not just about the dream, but about the steps to make it real.
