A young sailor stumbles near the railing, and I catch his arm before the sea can claim him. I pull him close and say, "I need every one of you alive on the other side of this. So *stay on the ship*, do you hear me?"

He nods, wide-eyed but steadying.

I turn back to the horizon, searching for any break in the wall of grey clouds. There's none yet — but storms always pass. They always do.
