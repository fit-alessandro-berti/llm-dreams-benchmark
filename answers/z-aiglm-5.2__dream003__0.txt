You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and symbols carved into its surface that seem to shift when you look away. The wood feels warm, almost alive beneath your fingertips, and you realize the patterns are actually tiny roads and rivers, entire landscapes miniature and breathing.

You tilt the box and hear something slide inside—not a rattle, but a slow, deliberate movement, as if whatever is within is choosing its moment.

Then you notice the lock. It has no keyhole. Instead, there is a small mirror set into the mechanism. You lean in close, and your reflection stares back, but it reaches up from the inside and undoes the latch.

The box opens.

Inside is a room. Your childhood bedroom, perfectly preserved, sunlight falling across the bed at the angle it did on summer mornings. A window you forgot existed. The smell of dust and crayons and something baking.

There is a note on the bed. It reads: *You left something here. You've been dreaming ever since.*

You sit on the edge of the bed, and the mattress sinks just the way you remember. Outside the window, the sky is the color of pencil shavings. A bird lands on the sill—a bird you named when you were seven—and it tilts its head as if waiting for you to say the name aloud.

You open your mouth.

You wake up, and the word is gone. But your hands smell like old wood, and you know—you *know*—the box is still out there, waiting.
