You are in the middle of a heated argument with a close friend. The disagreement is about something important to both of you, and you decide to storm out of the cozy coffee shop where it all began, the steam from your untouched lattes curling up like question marks in the air. Your heart pounds as you push through the door into a street that suddenly twists into an endless labyrinth of glowing neon signs, each one whispering fragments of your shared memories—late-night study sessions, road trips gone wrong, that one time you both laughed until you cried over a spilled pizza.

But in the dream's logic, walking away doesn't end the fight; it amplifies it. Your friend appears around every corner, not angry anymore, but multiplied into echoes of themselves: one version handing you a map made of crumpled love letters, another juggling fireflies that spell out "Why?" in the dusk. You try to shout your side—about the betrayal over that forgotten promise, the weight of unspoken expectations—but your voice comes out as a flock of birds, scattering into the sky.

Exhausted, you collapse onto a bench that morphs into a cloud, and that's when the dream softens. Your friend sits beside you, not as an adversary, but as a mirror, reflecting back the parts of yourself you've been arguing against all along. You reach out, and the argument dissolves into a shared silence, the kind that rebuilds bridges. As the first light of dawn filters through the dream's edges, you wake up with a lingering ache in your chest—and a text from your real friend, asking if you're free for coffee.
