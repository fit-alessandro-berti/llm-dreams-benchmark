You turn a corner and find a door made of dark, polished wood. Carved into its surface is a riddle:

*"I have cities, but no houses live there. I have mountains, but no trees grow there. I have water, but no fish swim there. What am I?"*

You focus, running the words through your mind. Cities without houses... mountains without trees... water without fish... The answer surfaces: *"A map."*

The door swings open silently.

Beyond it lies a circular chamber with a ceiling that spirals into shadow. In the center sits a massive stone table, and on it rest three hourglasses—one with black sand, one with white, and one with sand that shifts between both colors. An inscription reads:

*"To pass forward, you must choose the one that measures not time, but truth."*

You study them carefully. The black sand falls with a heavy rhythm. The white sand falls almost imperceptibly slowly. But the shifting sand... it moves only when you speak, as if waiting to weigh your words.

You understand now—it's the shifting one.

As your fingers close around it, the walls dissolve into mist, and you hear a voice echo from somewhere deep:

*"You think clearly, dreamer. But the center of the maze is still far away..."*

The mist clears, revealing three paths—each pulsing with a different colored light: red, blue, and gold.

Which do you choose?
