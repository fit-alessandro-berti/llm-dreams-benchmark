...and you grip the wheel until your knuckles bleach white against the dark wood. The ship groans beneath you—a living thing in pain—and for a moment you swear the wood is warm, *pulsing*, like you're holding the spine of some great sea creature rather than a vessel.

"Captain!" First Mate Vasquez shouts over the howling wind. Her face is rain-slick, eyes wide. "The starboard railing's gone! Lars fell—"

But you already know. You *felt* it. A tug at your center, as if the ocean claimed a piece of you.

Lightning splits the sky. In that flash of white, you see something impossible: the waves aren't just water. They're *faces*. Thousands of them. Mouths opening and closing in silent screams or maybe laughter—you can't tell. The storm isn't weather. It's a congregation.

Your compass needle spins lazily, then *stops*, pointing not north but *down*.

"What do we do, Captain?" someone asks. The voice belongs to your childhood dog, somehow. He's standing there on the deck, fur matted with seawater, looking at you with those amber eyes like he always did when you were sad.

The wave rises before you—a wall of black glass twenty stories high, frozen mid-crash, and inside it you see reflections: yourself at different ages, each one holding the wheel, each one making different choices.

You realize, with the strange certainty of dreams: **you have always been here.** This moment has been waiting for you since birth.

The wave begins to fall.

*What do you do?*
