...and you decide to shout, but as the first word leaves your lips, it doesn't come out as sound. Instead, a small, glowing blue bird flutters from your mouth, chirping a melody that sounds suspiciously like the very argument we were just having.

Confused, you try to speak again, but now a cascade of heavy, silver keys spills from your throat, clattering onto the floor. But the floor isn't a floor anymore; it has turned into a deep, swirling pool of violet ink. Your friend, whose face was just contorted in anger, begins to dissolve into a cloud of white dandelion seeds, drifting upward toward a ceiling that has vanished, replaced by a vast, pulsing nebula.

You reach out to grab their hand, desperate to finish what you were saying, but your arms have become long, translucent ribbons of light. As you stretch toward them, the gravity shifts. You aren't standing; you are drifting. The anger feels miles away, muffled by the rhythmic, low-frequency humming of the stars. 

Just as your glowing fingers are about to touch the last remaining dandelion seed of their presence, the ink beneath you turns into a sudden, rushing wind, and you are falling—not down, but *up*—into a sky that tastes like salt and old memories.
