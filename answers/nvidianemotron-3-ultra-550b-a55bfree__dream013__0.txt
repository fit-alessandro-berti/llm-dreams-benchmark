Not metaphorically. The stone *expands and contracts* — a slow, geological inhale-exhale that dusts your shoulders with grit. The puzzles change too. The first ones were clean: gear assemblies, light-bending prisms, Fibonacci lock sequences. Satisfying clicks. The logic was *external*, something you imposed on the world.

Three levels down, the puzzles start requiring *you*.

A door opens only when you recall the exact shade of blue in your grandmother's kitchen tiles — not the color name, the *wavelength*, felt in the bones behind your eyes. A bridge manifests plank by plank as you recite, in reverse chronological order, every lie you've told someone you loved. The maze doesn't care about the lies' magnitude. It only cares that you *witness* them.

Your hands tremble on a console grown from fused vertebra and copper wire. The next puzzle: a single button. The instruction reads:

**INPUT: THE MOMENT YOU STOPPED BELIEVING YOU DESERVED TO BE HERE**

Not "arrive here." *Deserved*.

The air grows colder. The breathing walls sync with your pulse. Somewhere deeper, something that might be another dreamer — or the maze's original architect — coughs once, wetly, and begins to laugh.
