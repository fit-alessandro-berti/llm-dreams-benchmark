I clear my throat, and suddenly, the podium morphs into a rickety wooden crate, wobbling under my weight. No, it was something profound, something that would change the world.

Panic rises as the lights flicker, and the crowd begins to murmur, their whispers growing into a chant: "Speak! Speak!" I glance down at my notes, but they're blank pages fluttering like startled birds. In desperation, I improvise: "We are all dreamers in this waking nightmare, chasing shadows that dance just out of reach!"

The audience erupts in applause—or is it laughter? The stage tilts, and I stumble forward, tumbling into the front row. Hands catch me, pulling me into the crowd, where we all dissolve into a whirlwind of colors and laughter. The speech forgotten, the fear evaporates, and for a moment, it's pure joy.

Then, a jolt—like falling from a great height—and I wake up, heart pounding, staring at the ceiling, wondering what I was really trying to say.
