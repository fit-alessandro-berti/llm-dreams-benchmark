…realize you have no memory of what the speech is supposed to be about.

The microphone hums softly. Hundreds of faces blur together under the stage lights. Someone in the front row smiles in a way that suggests they already know the ending.

You open your mouth anyway.

“Good evening,” you say, and your voice echoes much louder than it should. The words float out over the audience and hang in the air like slow-moving balloons.

Behind the crowd, the walls of the auditorium quietly stretch upward, becoming impossibly tall. The ceiling dissolves into a sky full of slow, drifting clouds. A few people open umbrellas, though nothing is falling.

You glance down at your notes. The paper is blank—except for a single sentence slowly writing itself in your own handwriting.

“Tell them what you came here to remember.”

You look back up. The audience leans forward.

And suddenly you do remember—but only the first word.
