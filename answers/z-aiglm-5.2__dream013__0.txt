...notice the walls begin to shift and breathe around you. The puzzles change too — at first they were mathematical, clean, solvable. But now the logic starts to fray at the edges.

You encounter a door with no handle, only a mirror. In the reflection, you see yourself solving a puzzle you haven't attempted yet. You watch your reflection turn a crystal key in midair. So you reach out and mime the same motion. The door dissolves.

The corridor beyond is lined with clocks, each ticking at a different speed. Some run backward. A riddle is etched into the floor: *"The more I remove, the larger I grow. What am I?"* You hesitate, but your feet seem to know — you start digging. The floor crumbles away into a vast, luminous cavern below.

You fall, but slowly, as if through water. Stained glass puzzles float around you like jellyfish, each one containing a fragment of a memory you don't quite recognize — a conversation you never had, a place you've never been, but that feel *impossibly familiar.*

At the bottom, you land softly. There is one final puzzle. It is simply a question, written in your own handwriting:

**"What are you afraid to wake up and remember?"**

You reach for the answer—

And the maze inhales.
