You notice the questions are **written in a script your own subconscious speaks back to you.** They shimmer like heat haze, shifting between “What is your purpose?” and “Does it matter if you wake up at all？” As the first question appears, I realize my pen feels warm—not metal, but something alive, pulsing just beneath my fingertips. Before I can make a mark, the page ripples; suddenly, the ink bleeds across the room in a tide of blue light, and the words on the exam no longer ask what to do. They say who I am without telling me why I need to know.
