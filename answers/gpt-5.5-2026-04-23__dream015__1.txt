You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to **help rebuild a small community hidden inside the ruins of an old botanical garden**.

At first, the survivors distrust you. Everyone has learned to guard their food, their water, and their secrets. But when you discover an underground reservoir beneath the cracked greenhouse floor, everything changes. Together, you clear the debris, repair the rusted pipes, and coax the first green shoots from the dust.

Nights are still dangerous. Strange lights move beyond the city skyline, and distant machines groan like sleeping giants. Sometimes raiders come, drawn by rumors of fresh water. But the people in the garden no longer scatter in fear. They stand together.

One morning, you wake before sunrise and climb to the top of a broken observatory. Below you, the garden glows with tiny patches of green. Children are laughing. Someone is baking bread from the first harvest. For the first time, the world does not feel like it is ending.

Then the sky begins to rain—not ash, not acid, but clean, silver rain.

You lift your face to it, and as the drops touch your skin, you realize the dream was never about surviving the apocalypse.

It was about remembering how to begin again.
