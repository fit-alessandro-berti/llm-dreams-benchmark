You find a blank canvas set upon an easel made of woven starlight. In your hand, a brush appears—its bristles shimmer with possibilities. As you dip it into a pool of liquid twilight, the colors begin to swirl: deep indigos bleeding into sunrise golds, emerald greens sparking with silver. Each stroke you lay down doesn't just paint the canvas—it reshapes the landscape around you. A single, sweeping line becomes a river of liquid glass. A dab of crimson spawns floating blossoms that hum with quiet music.  

Do you paint something from a memory, or do you let the dream guide your hand toward something entirely new?
