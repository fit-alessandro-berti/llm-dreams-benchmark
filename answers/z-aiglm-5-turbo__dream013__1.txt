You solve it anyway. You don't know how. Your hands move before your mind catches up, and the wall ahead opens with a sound like a sigh.

Deeper now. The puzzles change. They stop being visual. One room asks you a question you can hear but not remember. You answer with a feeling instead of words, and it works. Another room presents two mirrors, and you have to decide which one is lying. You choose the left one because it feels warmer, and the right one shatters silently into black sand.

The maze knows you. You understand that now. It's not testing your intelligence. It's testing something else—something about how you see yourself. Each puzzle strips away a layer of assumption.

You turn a corner and find a door with no puzzle at all. Just a simple wooden door, slightly ajar. Warm light spills through the gap. You can hear something beyond it—voices maybe, or wind through trees. Something familiar that you can't place.

Your hand reaches for the handle.

And that's when you notice the floor beneath you has become transparent, and below it is an ocean, dark and endless, and something in it is looking up at you.
