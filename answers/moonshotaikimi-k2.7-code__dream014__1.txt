One of them presses a folded map into your hands, but when you open it, the streets have rearranged themselves into a constellation. “You were never lost,” they say in a chorus of warm voices. “You were just taking the long way home.”

Together you begin to walk, and with each step, the buildings lean inward like old friends sharing a secret. Streetlamps bloom into paper lanterns. The pavement beneath your feet turns to cobblestone, then moss, then starlight. The strangers point out windows where your own memories glow—birthday cakes, rainy afternoons, the exact shade of a door you loved as a child.

Just as the city dissolves into a field of silver grass, the kindest stranger turns to you and smiles. “You can wake up now,” they say. “You know the way.”

And somehow, you do.
