…realize your computer keyboard has turned into a row of tiny locked doors.

Your boss taps their watch, but the ticking grows louder and louder until it sounds like rain on a roof. You try to open the first little door-key, and behind it is a miniature conference room where everyone is already discussing the project without you. They all turn and say, in perfect unison, “We need this yesterday.”

You look down at the project folder. It is somehow both empty and overflowing, pages spilling out like white birds. Each page has only one sentence written on it: **Start where you are.**

So you do.

You grab one of the paper birds before it can fly away and fold it into a paper airplane. When you throw it, it glides across the office, through the wall, and suddenly the whole workplace peels open like a stage curtain. Behind it is a quiet beach at sunrise. Your desk is sitting in the sand, your inbox is a tide pool, and the impossible deadline is floating offshore like a red buoy.

Your boss wades into the water in polished shoes and calls, “Well? Are you going to finish it?”

You take a deep breath and say, “Yes, but not like this.”

The moment you say it, the buoy pops like a balloon. The ocean becomes still. Your boss shrinks into a seagull, flaps once, and flies away carrying a tiny briefcase in its beak.

You sit at your desk on the beach. The first page of the project is no longer blank. It now reads:

**Wake up. You have more time than you think.**

And just as the sun rises fully over the water, your alarm begins to ring.
