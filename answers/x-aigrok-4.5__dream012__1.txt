**...feel the cold spray lash your face and taste the salt of fear mixed with resolve.**

Lightning cracks the black sky, illuminating the towering waves that crash over the bow like living walls of water. The deck tilts violently beneath your boots. One of the younger hands, white-knuckled on a rope, shouts something half-lost to the wind: “Captain—what do we do?!”

You grip the wheel harder. The wood is slick, the ship groans like a living thing in pain, and for a heartbeat the storm itself seems to whisper your name. Yet you straighten, voice cutting clean through the gale:

“Batten the hatches! Reef the mainsail—now! Bring her into the wind and hold her steady. We ride this out together or we sink as one.”

The crew snaps into motion, a sudden focus blooming in their eyes. Lines whip, canvas cracks, and the ship heels over but rights herself again and again. You keep speaking—orders short, clear, steady—until the storm feels less like an enemy and more like a wild partner in a deadly dance.  

In the dream the thunder begins to sound almost musical. The waves rise higher still, yet somehow you feel the ship grow lighter under your hands… as if the sea itself is lifting you, carrying vessel and crew toward a horizon that glows faintly green, unanswered and waiting.
