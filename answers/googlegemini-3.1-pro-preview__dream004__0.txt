I have no brushes, but in this dream-space, I inherently know I don't need them. 

I reach out my bare hand and plunge my fingers into a floating anomaly right beside me—a spiraling cube of deep magenta. It feels like warm velvet. When I pull my hand out, my fingertips are dripping with glowing, liquid light. 

I step forward and swipe my hand across the pristine surface. The color doesn’t just stain the canvas; it sinks deep into it and begins to sing—a low, resonant hum of a cello. 

Intoxicated by the sound and the sensation, I begin to harvest the world around me. I reach up and pluck a sharp triangle of icy, cyan wind, snapping it in half and dragging it across the bottom of the frame where it shatters into glittering frost. I catch a handful of silver static that tastes like peppermint and splash it across the center, watching it melt into shimmering pools. I mold gravity, time, and whispers into swirling vortexes of emerald and violet, smearing them together with my palms.

But the canvas doesn't just hold the art—it consumes it. 

As I paint, the shifting landscape behind me begins to drain of its color and movement, bleeding directly into the canvas. The surreal world grows pale and quiet, while the painting becomes a roaring, blinding window of kinetic energy. The painted shapes begin to pull at the corners of the frame, pushing outward, bending the laws of dimension. The colors spill over the edges, wrapping around my wrists and drifting into the air like weightless smoke.

I realize then that the blank canvas wasn't a surface waiting for a picture. It was a doorway waiting to be built. 

With one final, sweeping motion, I drag a stroke of pure, blinding gold down the very center. The canvas fractures along the golden line, parting like a heavy curtain. The music swells to a deafening, euphoric crescendo. 

I step right through the wet paint and into a entirely new dream.
