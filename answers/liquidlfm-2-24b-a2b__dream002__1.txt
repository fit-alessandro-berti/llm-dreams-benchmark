Suddenly, the storm isn’t just outside—it’s inside your mind. The world around you blurs and shifts. The trees morph into towering pillars of light and shadow, their trunks glowing faintly with an inner radiance. The rain becomes silver threads, weaving patterns in the air that form fleeting symbols—ancient runes, perhaps, or forgotten memories.

You hear a voice—not spoken, but felt—echoing in your chest: *“This is the storm of change.”* You realize you’re not just witnessing the storm; you’re part of it. The wind carries you forward, not away, toward a clearing where a single, luminous flower blooms in the heart of the tempest. Its petals shimmer with every color you’ve ever loved, and as you reach out, the storm begins to calm, the rain softening to a gentle mist.

In that moment, you understand: the storm was never destruction—it was transformation. And now, you step forward, changed, into the quiet that follows.
