"Crew!" I shout above the thunder, salt stinging my eyes. "Reef the topsails! Secure the cargo! Every hand matters now—not for glory, but for each other!"  

The ship groans as a monstrous wave crashes over the bow, tearing at the rigging. Young Ensign Mara stumbles near the mast, arms flailing. I lunge, grabbing her collar and hauling her upright. "Eyes forward, Mara! Trim that jib line—now!" She nods, fear giving way to fierce determination as she lurches toward the tangled ropes.  

The compass spins wildly, useless. But I know this sea. My grandfather’s stories echo in my skull: *"When the stars drown, steer by heart."* I bark orders not from charts, but instinct—adjusting rudder angles just enough to dance between the killer swells.  

By dawn, the storm bleeds into a bruised sky. The ship limps, battered but whole. Exhausted faces turn to me, not for commands now, but for hope. I offer them water, then a grim smile. "We’re still afloat. That’s all the map we need."  

In dreams, storms test captains. In life, they reveal them.
