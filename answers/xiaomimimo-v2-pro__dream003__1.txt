As you trace the grooves with your fingertips, the metal feels unexpectedly warm, humming faintly like a distant beehive. The patterns seem to shift under your gaze—spirals becoming constellations, lines turning into rivers on an unseen map.  

You notice a small, smooth indentation where the patterns converge, shaped like a teardrop or a leaf. Without thinking, you press your thumb into it. There’s a soft click, and the lid lifts on its own, releasing the scent of old parchment and starlight.  

Inside, resting on dark velvet, is not a key, but a single, polished stone that glows from within, pulsing gently in rhythm with your own heartbeat. As you pick it up, whispers seem to rise from the box—not in words, but in feelings: *curiosity, welcome, a question without an answer*.  

The stone grows warmer, and you realize it’s not a lock you’ve opened, but a conversation you’ve begun. The box itself reshapes in your hands, becoming less a container and more a compass, its needle pointing toward a door you hadn’t noticed before, standing ajar in the dream-mist.  

You step forward, holding the light, and the dream deepens.
