
You find yourself in a vast, unfamiliar city, its streets twisting impossibly upward like a maze carved from smoke and starlight. The sky above shifts between midnight blue and a sickly amber, depending on which direction you glance. Your watch—strangely, it’s still ticking—counts down the minutes, but the hands seem to mock you, spinning faster when you’re not looking.  

You start to run, but the pavement beneath your feet ripples like water, and the buildings lean away, their windows glowing with faces that aren’t quite human. A voice calls your name from an alleyway, but when you turn, it’s your own voice, older and wearier, holding a key made of glass. *“The meeting’s not in the city,”* it says. *“It’s in the space between the buildings.”*  

You follow the echo of its footsteps, which lead you to a bridge suspended mid-air, connecting two towers that don’t touch. On the bridge stands a door, unmarked and gleaming. Behind it, a figure waits—your younger self, but with eyes like burning coals. They hand you a letter sealed with wax the color of a sunset. The paper is warm to the touch, and the words dissolve as you read them: *“You’ve been here before. You just forgot.”*  

The dream fades as the clock in your pocket chimes twelve, though no such sound exists. You wake up—maybe—on your couch, your phone buzzing with a text: *“Meeting moved to 3 PM. Location: your childhood home. Don’t be late.”*  

But the key is still in your hand.
