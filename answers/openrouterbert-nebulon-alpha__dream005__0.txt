You’re in a vast, dimly lit ballroom, the kind with chandeliers that flicker like dying stars and a floor that shifts beneath your feet—sometimes marble, sometimes sand, sometimes something soft and alive. The air hums with overlapping conversations, laughter that cuts off abruptly, and the occasional sound of a glass shattering in slow motion. You scan the crowd for the one person you know, but the faces blur at the edges, their features rearranging when you look away.

A group of people in matching silver masks waves you over. They’re holding champagne flutes filled with a liquid that glows faintly blue. *"You’re late,"* one says, though you don’t know for what. *"The rules changed at midnight."* They press a glass into your hand, and when you drink, the liquid tastes like static and childhood summers. The masks peel back to reveal faces that aren’t faces—just smooth, featureless skin with mouths that stretch too wide when they speak. *"Do you remember your name?"* they ask in unison. You try to answer, but your voice comes out as a whisper, then a laugh, then nothing at all.

You turn away, heart pounding, and bump into a woman in a dress made of overlapping newspaper clippings. The headlines shift as you read them: *"LOCAL MAN DISAPPEARS INTO OWN REFLECTION"* / *"SCIENTISTS DISCOVER TIME IS A FLUID, NOT A RIVER"* / *"YOU ARE HERE (BUT NOT FOR LONG)."* She smiles, and her teeth are tiny, perfect keys. *"Looking for someone?"* she asks. You nod, and she points toward a spiral staircase in the corner of the room that wasn’t there a second ago. *"They went up. But the stairs only go one way now."*

You climb. The steps are uneven, some made of ice, some of warm bread, one of a tongue that licks your palm as you grip the railing. At the top, the room opens into a library where every book is blank except for the one in your hands. The title reads: *"THIS IS NOT YOUR STORY."* You flip through the pages, and they’re filled with your handwriting—memories you don’t recognize, futures that haven’t happened, a list of names that includes yours, crossed out in red. A voice behind you says, *"You’re almost there."* You turn, and it’s the person you’ve been searching for—but their eyes are wrong. One is your reflection, the other is a clock, ticking backward.

*"You found me,"* they say, but their mouth doesn’t move. The room starts to dissolve, the books crumbling into ash, the walls peeling like old wallpaper to reveal a vast, empty sky. *"But did you find yourself?"*

And then—

You wake up (or do you?) in a different crowd, this one smaller, quieter. The person you know is there, sipping coffee, completely unaware of the dream. They smile at you. *"You look like you’ve seen a ghost."* You open your mouth to tell them about the masks, the keys, the staircase, but all that comes out is: *"I think I lost something."*

They laugh. *"Dreams’ll do that to you."*

And just like that, you’re not sure if you’re still dreaming.

**Themes to play with if you want to expand it:**
- **The Uncanny Crowd:** People who seem familiar but aren’t, or who repeat phrases like *"You don’t belong here"* in a loop.
- **Shifting Architecture:** Doors that lead to the same room, hallways that stretch when you’re not looking.
- **The Guide:** A recurring figure (a child, an animal, a stranger) who seems to know the rules but won’t explain them.
- **The Object:** A prop that keeps reappearing (a broken watch, a key, a photograph) with no clear purpose.
- **The Exit:** A door, a mirror, or a voice calling your name—but every time you reach for it, it moves.
