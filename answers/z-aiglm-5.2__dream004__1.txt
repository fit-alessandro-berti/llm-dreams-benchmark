You are in a world where colors and shapes are constantly changing. As you explore this surreal landscape, you come across a blank canvas and feel an inexplicable urge to touch it. The moment your fingertips make contact, the canvas ripples like water, and your hand sinks through the surface.

Without warning, you are pulled inside. You find yourself floating in a vast emptiness—not dark, not light, but something in between that has no name. Thoughts appear as shimmering threads around you, curling and uncurling like smoke. You realize these are not your thoughts. They belong to everyone who has ever dreamed.

You try to speak, but your words become tiny silver fish that swim away into the void. You laugh, and the laughter scatters into a flock of geometric birds—triangles and hexagons flapping wings of pure color.

A door materializes. It has no frame, no wall—just a door standing alone, pulsing with a soft amber glow. You reach for the handle, but it keeps shifting: sometimes wood, sometimes water, sometimes warm like skin.

Behind you, a voice says: "That door only opens when you stop trying to remember where you came from."

You turn. No one is there. But your shadow remains facing the door, detached from your feet, waiting patiently.

You look down at your hands. They are becoming translucent. The dream is forgetting you.

What do you do?
