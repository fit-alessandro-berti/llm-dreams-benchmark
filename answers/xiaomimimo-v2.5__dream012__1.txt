I grip the wheel as the deck heaves beneath my boots, salt spray stinging my face. “Steady!” I call out, my voice cutting through the wind. “All hands, reef the main sails! Helm holds her into the waves—quarter rudder port side!”

The crew moves as one, their faces set but trusting. Lightning splits the sky, illuminating towering waves like moving mountains. I scan the horizon, searching for a break in the chaos, knowing that in this dream, courage is the only compass.

Then—just as the bow plunges into another wall of white water—I feel it. Not just the storm’s fury, but the quiet strength of every soul aboard, tethered together by faith in something unseen. And in that moment, the dream shifts. The sea softens. The clouds part. And we sail on, toward a dawn that wasn’t there before, guided not by stars, but by the quiet certainty that we would find our way.
