You grip the wheel, salt spray stinging your eyes, but the compass spins uselessly. "Trust the ship," you hear yourself say, though your voice sounds distant, as if someone else is speaking through you. The crew exchanges uneasy glances, but they nod—vanishing one by one into the rigging like shadows swallowed by the storm. The waves rise higher, curling into shapes almost like hands, and the sky opens, spilling not rain but a cascade of warm, golden light. You realize the ship is no longer lurching; it’s gliding. The sea beneath you has turned to glass, reflecting a sky full of stars you’ve never seen, and you understand with sudden, calm clarity that you were never lost—only dreaming yourself awake.
