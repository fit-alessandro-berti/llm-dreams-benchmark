…begin to notice that the puzzles are changing.

At first they are familiar: locked doors, rotating tiles, riddles carved into stone. But soon the answers are no longer numbers or words. One puzzle asks you to remember the face of someone you have never met. Another requires you to choose which of your shadows is real. A third is a hallway that only opens when you stop trying to find the exit.

Deeper still, the maze begins to whisper your name—not from one direction, but from every wall.

You realize the maze is not testing whether you can solve its puzzles.

It is teaching you how to wake up.
