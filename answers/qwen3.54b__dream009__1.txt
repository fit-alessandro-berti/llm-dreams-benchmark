You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to find a source of truth before the sun rises—and the world does not record anything anymore. The silence hums in the cobblestones and dust motes dance where screens would once glow; without a device to guide you, you can only trust what your hands remember how to move. There is no map, no signal, no clock—only the rhythm of wind in the trees and the slow heartbeat of a town that does not remember its own name. You realize you need to speak your own truth into the morning so something might catch it and carry it through the fog before it vanishes like smoke in a forest fire.
