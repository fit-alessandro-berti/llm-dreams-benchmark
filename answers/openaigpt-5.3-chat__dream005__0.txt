…each group seems to belong to a different kind of world.

At one table people in glittering evening clothes are laughing too loudly, as if they’re performing laughter rather than feeling it. Someone hands you a glass of something bright blue. When you look down again, it has turned into a folded map, but the lines keep shifting so you can’t read it.

You move on.

In the hallway a circle of people sit cross‑legged on the floor building tiny cities out of playing cards. They speak in whispers about wind and gravity like they’re philosophers. One of them asks who you’re looking for. When you say the name of your friend, everyone pauses, thinking hard, as if the name is a riddle they almost understand.

“No,” one of them finally says gently. “But they might be upstairs. Or downstairs.”

The staircase nearby forks in three directions.

You pick one at random. The steps feel soft, like they’re made of old books. Halfway up, you hear music and follow it into a kitchen where a band is playing between the refrigerator and the sink. The singer looks strangely familiar, and for a moment you’re certain it’s the person you’re searching for—but when they turn around it’s someone else entirely, smiling like they expected you anyway.

“Almost,” they say.

You leave the kitchen and keep wandering.

The rooms start to blur together: a library where people are swimming slowly through the air between shelves, a balcony where strangers are pointing at constellations that don’t exist, a quiet room full of coats that seem to breathe as if people are still inside them.

You realize something odd.

Everyone you pass greets you like they know you.

Some wave. Some nod. One person says, “You’re getting closer.”

Finally you reach a small room at the far end of the building. The door is slightly open. Inside, someone sits alone in a chair with their back to you.

Relieved, you step forward, certain you’ve finally found the person you came with.

But when the chair turns around—

it’s you.

And before you can ask anything, the other you smiles and says, “There you are. I was wondering how long it would take you to find me.”
