As you write, the words don’t appear on the paper—they bloom into the air, forming constellations that rearrange themselves into answers that don't make sense, yet feel true. But when you stand to open it, the exam papers fuse together into a single, endless scroll, and beneath it, a single line in your own handwriting: *You’re already here.*  

The dream shifts. You’re no longer in the classroom. You’re walking through a city made of memory—your first day of school, your graduation, the first time you held your child’s hand. And every path leads to a soft glow behind a door. You turn the keys in your pocket: they’re not metal. They’re names. Your name. The name of someone you loved. The name of someone you never met.  

Then you hear your own voice, clear and calm, from somewhere far away:  
*"The exam was never the test. It was the moment you believed you had to pass it to be worthy."*  

You wake breathless, not in your room, but standing in a sunlit hallway you’ve never seen—three doors ahead. One bears the word *hope*. One says *forgiveness*. The third is unlabeled.

And you open it.
