...and the moment your fingertips graze its surface, the canvas *inhales*.

Not metaphorically. The edges curl inward like lips, and you feel a gentle pull against your skin as if the emptiness itself is hungry. You try to pull away, but your hand has become part of the composition now—your fingers stretching into brushstrokes, your knuckles bleeding into watercolor swirls that shouldn't exist because nothing has touched the canvas except *you*.

The colors that seep from your pores aren't colors you have names for. One is the sound of rain remembered backward. Another tastes like the weight of a letter never sent.

Around you, the shifting landscape slows, then *stops*. Every impossible geometry holds its breath. Even the sky—which had been cycling through seasons like a restless eye—freezes in a twilight that exists nowhere on Earth.

The canvas releases your hand.

Where it let go, an image remains: a door. Not painted *on* the canvas. The canvas *is* the door now, frame and all, leading somewhere that hums with a frequency you feel in your teeth.

Behind you, something in this color-world begins to change again. But slower now. More deliberate.

As if it's waiting to see what you choose.
