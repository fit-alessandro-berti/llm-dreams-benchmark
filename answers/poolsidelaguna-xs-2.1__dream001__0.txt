You start to wander through the winding streets, your footsteps echoing in the hushed, indigo twilight of a city that feels both ancient and impossibly new. Neon signs flicker in languages you almost recognize, casting shifting shadows that seem to whisper the meeting’s location. You pause at a fountain, its water glowing silver under a moon that hovers impossibly low, and notice a reflection in its surface—not your face, but the silhouette of a clock tower whose hands spin backward.  

You follow the tower’s direction, but the streets rearrange themselves as you move. Now you’re in a library where books flutter open to reveal maps drawn in ink that bleeds into the floor. A child with your own eyes hands you a key shaped like a question mark, then vanishes into a wall of thorned vines. You pocket the key and press it to your palm, feeling its warmth pulse like a heartbeat.  

Suddenly, you’re in a subway station with no trains—only a single seat facing a mirror. Sitting there is a person wearing your coat, but their face is blurred. They gesture to the mirror and say, *“Time bends where you’re meant to be.”* When you look again, the glass is empty.  

You rush toward the sound of a clock ticking, its rhythm syncing with your pulse. The source is a cathedral whose spire pierces clouds made of static. As you climb, the walls close in, revealing murals of every path you’ve ever hesitated to take. At the summit, the meeting isn’t a room—it’s a door made of light, inscribed with the words: *“You were always on time.”*  

Stepping through, you find yourself in a circle of strangers who smile knowingly. They hand you a seat at a table set for one. A note beside it reads: *“The meeting is with the part of you you’ve been running from.”* You sit, and the dream dissolves into dawn.
