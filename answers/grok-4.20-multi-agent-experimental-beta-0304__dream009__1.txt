**You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to** hide the fact that your pockets still contain a dead smartphone and a half-melted credit card before the villagers decide you're a witch, a demon, or—worse—someone interesting enough to lock in the stocks.

The air smells of woodsmoke, wet wool, and fresh horse dung. Thatched roofs lean against each other like tired old men. A woman carrying a basket of turnips gives you a long, suspicious stare; you smile nervously and try to look like you belong in 1387 (or whenever this is). Your jeans have somehow become scratchy woolen hose, but the transformation didn't bother to fix your modern sneakers, which now look comically out of place in the mud.

You realize you need to eat, but more urgently you need a story. "I... fell off a cart," you mumble to a curious blacksmith who eyes your strange haircut. He grunts, apparently satisfied. People here accept oddities more easily than you expected—life is already full of plague, war, and suspiciously sudden deaths.

As night falls, you huddle by a communal fire. Someone passes you a wooden cup of something that tastes like regret and fermented apples. The stars overhead look sharper than you've ever seen them, no light pollution to dull their brilliance. For a moment the dream feels almost peaceful.

Then the twist arrives, as dreams always do: a small girl tugging at your sleeve whispers in perfect modern English, "You're not supposed to be here yet. The portal closes at dawn. But if you change anything—anything at all—you'll wake up in a different life than the one you left."

She smiles with too many teeth.

You realize you need to decide, right now, whether to play it safe and run for the portal... or finally use all that useless modern knowledge to fix something in this brutal, beautiful world before the dream ends and takes the choice away forever.

The fire crackles. The girl is gone.

And somewhere in the distance, a rooster crows at the wrong hour.
