One stranger hands you a small compass. Its needle does not point north—it points toward your chest.

“You already know the way,” she says.

As you look down, the compass begins to glow warmly in your hand. The garden gates open onto a familiar path, and you realize you are no longer lost. You wake just as the first light of morning spills across your room.
