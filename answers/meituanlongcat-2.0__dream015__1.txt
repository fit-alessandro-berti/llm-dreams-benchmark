You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to... *plant a seed*. Not of corn or wheat, but of an idea. In the cracked concrete of a ruined metropolis, you crouch and press a strange, glowing kernel into the earth, a gift from a dream-visitor you can’t quite remember. As your blood dripped onto the soil, the kernel unfolds into a library of singing trees, their leaves whispering forgotten names. People who once hid in subway tunnels emerge, drawn by the sound, and find the trees’ fruit tastes like hope. Then the dream dissolves. You wake in your own bed, sunlight streaming through the window, the taste of salt on your lips—tears or rain, you’re not sure. But on your nightstand, a small green sprout grows from a forgotten apple core.
