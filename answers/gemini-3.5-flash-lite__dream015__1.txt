The soil beneath my boots is gray and crusted over, smelling of sulfur and old rust. The wind howls through the skeletal remains of a highway overpass, carrying the distant, low rumble of something mechanical and hungry. My canteen is light, slapping hollowly against my hip, and my stomach echoes its emptiness. Every rational instinct screams at me to eat the seed, to swallow the microscopic burst of sugar and water before the dark catches up to me. 

Instead, I sink to my knees in the ash. 

I use a jagged piece of shrapnel to carve a shallow trench in the earth, praying to a sky choked with perpetual smog that somewhere, beneath this dead crust, the earth still remembers how to breathe. I drop the seed in, cover it gently with my scarred hands, and press a few precious drops of filtered sweat from my rag into the dirt.

As I sit back on my heels, the wind suddenly dies down. The silence of the wasteland presses in, heavy and vast. And then, impossibly, a single beam of golden sunlight punches through a ragged tear in the gray clouds, illuminating the patch of turned earth right where the seed rests.

I smile, the cracked skin of my lips stinging, and whisper into the quiet, *"Grow."*
