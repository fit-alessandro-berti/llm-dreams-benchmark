The warmth of their hands against your arms anchors the chaotic noise around you. The lights above seem to flicker in harmony with your steps, guiding the path through the crowd, and then they lead you down a street bathed in evening blue, where no one was supposed to be walking, but everyone was waiting for this light.

"I can see you," one of them says softly, their voice blending with the hum of the city behind you as it fades to the background. "We were looking for your name, but we only saw your reflection."

You pause. The confusion dissolves into a quiet understanding. You are not lost; you are simply waiting for someone to show you how the city truly felt when its noise becomes silence. And then, slowly, as you walk home, you feel like the map is no longer written in ink, but in the space between your heart and the stars above. You close your eyes and rest for a moment, and when you wake up, the city still stands, but it looks different to you—you have learned that some directions are meant only for those who walk with them to find peace, not just through the streets of a bustling metropolis.
