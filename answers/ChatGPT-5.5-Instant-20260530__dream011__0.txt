…written in a language you don’t recognize.

At first, panic surges through you. The clock on the wall is ticking far too loudly, and everyone around you is already writing furiously. You flip through the pages, hoping the next section will make more sense, but the symbols only become stranger. Some of them seem to move when you aren't looking directly at them.

You raise your hand for help, but the examiner simply smiles and points to a small note printed at the bottom of the first page:

*"The answers are not hidden in the questions."*

Confused, you look around the room. The walls are covered with framed photographs. As you study them, you realize they are scenes from your life: childhood achievements, mistakes you've made, late nights studying, conversations with friends, moments when you helped people, moments when people helped you.

One by one, the unfamiliar symbols on the exam begin to resemble pieces of those memories.

The first question transforms into a puzzle built from your experiences. The second asks you to explain what you learned from a failure you had tried to forget. The third has no words at all—only a blank space where you somehow know you must draw your future.

As you work, the room changes. Desks dissolve into clouds of paper airplanes. The ticking clock melts like wax and drips onto the floor, where it grows into a tree covered in pocket watches instead of fruit.

The other students vanish.

You are alone.

Then you notice that every answer you write changes the world outside the exam hall. When you write that curiosity matters more than certainty, a locked door appears and swings open. When you write that kindness is a strength, the room brightens. When you admit that you don't know everything, the strange language becomes easier to read.

Eventually you reach the final question:

*"What career should you choose?"*

Below it are thousands of empty boxes, stretching far beyond the page.

You hesitate. No answer feels perfect.

Then the note at the bottom changes:

*"This was never a multiple-choice exam."*

Instead of writing a job title, you write a single sentence:

*"I will keep learning."*

The moment your pen leaves the paper, the exam booklet folds itself into a bird. It flutters out an open window and disappears into a sunrise that seems to contain every color at once.

The examiner returns, takes the now-empty desk, and stamps the air with a large seal that reads:

**PASSED**

You ask what score you received.

The examiner laughs.

*"In dreams like this, the score comes first. The test comes afterward."*

And just as you begin to understand what that means, you wake up.
