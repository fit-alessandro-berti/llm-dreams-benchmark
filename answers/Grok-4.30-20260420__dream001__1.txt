You start to **panic**.

Your heart hammers against your ribs like a trapped bird as you yank your phone from your pocket. The screen is empty except for one blinking cursor that slowly types, in letters made of smoke:

**“The meeting isn’t a place. It’s a state of mind.”**

You curse, hurl the phone into the air—only for it to burst into a flock of white doves that wheel upward, each carrying a torn scrap of glowing map. You sprint after them, leaping over rivers of liquid mercury that reflect upside-down versions of the city. The buildings lean in to whisper secrets you almost understand. A street vendor with a thousand eyes sells bottled memories; you buy one labeled “Direction” and drink it down. It tastes like childhood rain and burnt toast.

Time stutters. Your watch now shows **59 minutes… 2 hours… 7 minutes… 41 seconds…** The numbers melt and reform into the face of someone you once loved.

You duck into an alley that wasn’t there a second ago. It opens into a vast library where books flutter like startled pigeons. One lands in your hands: *The Tower of Forgotten Appointments*. The pages flip themselves and show you the way—through the Park of Regret, across the Bridge of Almost, up the Spiral Staircase of What-If.

You run. Your legs turn into bicycle wheels, then into wings, then back into legs again. The doves circle overhead, dropping map pieces that reassemble mid-air into a single glowing arrow pointing straight up.

At the base of the impossible tower—mirrors stacked to the clouds—you climb. Each step shows a different version of you: the one who stayed, the one who left, the one who never tried. At the summit, under a sky of swirling galaxies and falling stars that sing lullabies, sits a single empty chair.

A soft voice that is somehow *your own* speaks from everywhere and nowhere:

“You made it. The meeting was always with me.  
I’ve been waiting inside you the whole time.”

The tower dissolves into light. The city folds itself into a paper crane that flutters away on the wind. You feel the dream gently releasing you, like a hand letting go of another hand.

You wake in your own bed, heart still racing, but smiling—because you finally remember what the meeting was about.

It was about **coming home to yourself**.
