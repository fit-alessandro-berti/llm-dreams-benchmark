...and the air turns thick, tasting faintly of ozone and crushed lavender. 

The dappled green canopy above doesn't just darken; it bleeds into a deep, bruised violet. The wind picks up, but it doesn’t howl. Instead, it hums a low, resonant frequency that I feel vibrating in my teeth. The leaves of the ancient trees don't rustle—they *chime*, like thousands of tiny glass bells struck by an invisible breeze.

I know, with the absolute certainty of a dream, that I am not in danger. 

The first drops of the storm fall, but they don't hit the ground. They are heavy, glowing spheres of liquid silver, and they fall in slow motion, coming to a gentle halt just an inch above the moss. They hover there, pulsing to the rhythm of a heartbeat I can suddenly hear echoing from the earth beneath my feet.

I reach out, compelled by a strange curiosity, and press my fingertip against one of the hovering silver drops. 

The moment I touch it, gravity reverses. 

The forest floor falls away from me, or perhaps I fall upward. The towering trees stretch and elongate, their bark melting into cascades of brilliant, bioluminescent starlight. The storm breaks, but not with a crash of thunder—with a sudden, profound silence. The silver drops burst into a flock of translucent, glowing moths that spiral upward, carrying the heavy violet sky away with them. 

I am left floating weightlessly in a vast, quiet ocean of glass, beneath the light of a new, impossibly large, pearlescent moon. A distant voice, familiar but unplaceable, calls my name. It isn’t a warning. It’s an invitation to dive.
