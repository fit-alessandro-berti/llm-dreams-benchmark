As you continue walking through the peaceful forest, the air grows thick and electric. The gentle rustle of leaves turns into a low, urgent hum, like the forest itself is holding its breath. Dark clouds roll in with impossible speed, swallowing the sun and painting the canopy in bruised purples and angry grays. You feel the storm approaching—not just in the wind that now claws at your clothes, but deep in your bones, as if the thunder is calling your name from somewhere inside your own chest.

The first raindrops fall heavy and warm, each one carrying tiny flashes of light like captured stars. They sizzle where they touch the ground, and suddenly the trees begin to shift. Their trunks twist into ancient, watchful faces—eyes glowing faint silver, mouths murmuring in a language you almost understand. Roots rise like serpents, revealing glowing runes etched into the soil that pulse in rhythm with your heartbeat.

Lightning forks across the sky in jagged, deliberate patterns, and for a split second you see the storm isn’t random at all. It’s forming shapes—constellations you’ve never seen, faces from dreams you forgot you had, and one that looks disturbingly like your own reflection, smiling with too many teeth.

You start to run, but the path ahead dissolves into mist. The forest rearranges itself around you, guiding you toward a massive oak whose hollow trunk now swirls with a vortex of wind and light. Inside, you glimpse other worlds flickering: one where the trees walk upright and sing lullabies to the moon, another where the storm has already passed and left behind rivers of liquid starlight.

A voice—your own voice, but deeper, older—whispers through the thunder: “The storm isn’t coming for you. It’s waking up what’s been sleeping in you all along.”

You step into the hollow. The world tilts. The rain becomes music. And just as the lightning strikes the tree and floods everything in blinding white, you realize you’re not afraid anymore.

You’re home.

The dream gently fades, leaving only the faint scent of wet earth and the echo of your own heartbeat… still racing.
