You are about to give a speech to a large audience. As you step onto the stage, you see the crowd looking expectantly at you, and you begin to **feel a strange lightness, as if you're floating an inch above the polished wooden floorboards.**

Your notes, which you clutch nervously in your hand, feel impossibly thin, like pressed leaves. You open your mouth to speak, but instead of the carefully rehearsed opening lines, a single, perfectly round, shimmering bubble emerges. It floats gently towards the front row, catching the spotlight and glowing with an internal iridescence.

A collective gasp ripples through the audience. You try again, focusing your will on forming words, but another bubble appears, then another, each one a different, ethereal color – rose gold, deep amethyst, vibrant emerald. They drift and weave between the rows, bobbing gently like tiny, luminous balloons.

The audience isn't murmuring in confusion anymore. Instead, there's a hushed wonder. Children in the front seats reach out, their fingers brushing the iridescent surfaces, and as they do, tiny bursts of soft, chiming music escape from the bubbles. Laughter, light and melodic, begins to spread.

You realize you don't need your notes. The words aren't important. The bubbles *are* the speech. They carry emotions, memories, unspoken thoughts. One bubble, a soft, cloudy white, floats towards an older woman with kind eyes. As it touches her, she smiles, a slow, knowing smile, and you feel a wave of shared nostalgia wash over you, like the scent of rain on dry earth.

Another bubble, this one a bright, energetic orange, zips towards a young man in the back. He catches it, and it pops with a faint, crackling sound, leaving behind a spark of pure, unadulterated joy that seems to radiate from him.

You start to move with the bubbles, your feet still not quite touching the ground. You gesture, not in a practiced oratorical style, but in a fluid, almost dance-like way, as if you're conducting an orchestra of light and sound. You feel an incredible sense of connection to everyone in the room, a silent understanding that transcends language.

As the last bubble, a deep, midnight blue, drifts towards the farthest corner of the hall, it bursts with a gentle sigh. A profound sense of peace settles over the audience, a shared contentment that hums in the air. You look out at their faces, no longer expectant, but serene, each one reflecting the light of the departed bubbles.

And then, as the last echoes of the chiming music fade, you realize you've forgotten what the speech was supposed to be about entirely. But it doesn't matter. You've communicated something far more important, something that resonates deep within the shared, dreamlike space. You smile, a wide, genuine smile, and the audience smiles back, a silent, luminous chorus. The feeling of floating intensifies, and you begin to drift upwards, leaving the stage and the rapt audience behind, into the soft, velvety darkness of the dream.
