...the puzzles seem to shift and evolve, as if the maze itself is alive, testing the very limits of your perception. The walls, once solid stone, now ripple like liquid mercury, their surfaces etched with glowing symbols that rearrange themselves the moment you glance away. A low hum vibrates through the air, resonating in your bones, and you realize it’s the sound of the maze *breathing*—each inhale pulls the corridors tighter, each exhale expands new pathways where none existed before.

Ahead, a bridge of floating, translucent tiles materializes over a bottomless chasm. The tiles flicker like stars, each one inscribed with a fragment of a riddle in a language you don’t know… yet somehow, you *do*. The first tile reads: *"What walks on four legs at dawn, two at noon, and three at dusk?"* You step forward, and the tile glows beneath your foot. The answer—*a human*—forms in your mind without thought, and the bridge solidifies further. But the next tile’s riddle is stranger: *"I am the beginning of eternity, the end of time and space, the start of every end, and the end of every place."* You hesitate. The answer is *the letter E*, but as you speak it aloud, the tile shatters, and the bridge trembles.

A voice, neither inside nor outside your head, whispers: *"Logic is a tool, but not the only one."* The maze shifts again. Now, the path splits into three identical corridors, each bathed in a different hue—crimson, sapphire, emerald. A sign at the fork reads: *"Choose the path that does not exist."* You close your eyes, feeling the air currents. The middle path (sapphire) carries the faintest scent of ozone, as if it’s a mirage. You step into it—and the world dissolves into a kaleidoscope of fractals.

When your vision clears, you’re standing in a circular chamber. At its center floats a perfect, mirror-like sphere. Your reflection stares back, but it’s not you—it’s a version of yourself with eyes like galaxies, mouthing words you can’t hear. The sphere pulses, and suddenly, you’re *both* of you, remembering a future that hasn’t happened yet: the maze was never a test. It was a *conversation*. The puzzles were its way of asking, *"Do you see how little you truly know?"*

And then—
The last wall crumbles. Beyond it lies not an exit, but a door made of light, humming with the same frequency as your own heartbeat. A hand (yours? the maze’s?) reaches for the knob. The dream ends just as the door begins to open... or perhaps, just as it begins to *close*.
