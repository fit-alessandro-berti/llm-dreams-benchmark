You are about to take the final exam for a course that determines your future career. As you sit down to start the exam, you notice the questions are written in a language you almost understand.

You turn the first page, hoping the next section will be easier, but each question is about a moment from your life: a choice you regret, an opportunity you ignored, a person you never thanked. The clock begins counting backward.

You raise your hand to ask for help, but the professor is gone. In their place is a mirror.

On the last page, there is only one question:

**“What do you want to become?”**

You pick up your pencil, but it has no eraser. Around you, everyone else begins writing. You look at the blank space for a long time, then realize the answer is not something you have to remember—it is something you have to choose.

You write your name.

The classroom dissolves into sunlight, and when you wake up, the exam is still ahead of you—but the fear has lost its power.
