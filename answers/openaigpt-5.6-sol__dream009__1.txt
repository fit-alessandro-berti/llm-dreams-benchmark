You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to discover **when—and where—you are** before anyone notices you don’t belong.

The room smells of smoke, damp wool, and something medicinal. Pale light leaks through a narrow window covered not with glass, but stretched hide. Your clothes are gone, replaced by a rough linen shift and a heavy cloak fastened with a bronze pin.

Outside, a village huddles beneath a gray sky. Thatched roofs bend in the wind. Horses pull wooden carts through the mud. In the distance, black walls rise around a hilltop fortress.

Then the bells begin.

People abandon their stalls and hurry toward the village square. An old woman grabs your wrist as you pass.

“You’re late,” she whispers. “The king has been waiting for you.”

Before you can ask what she means, soldiers appear. Their armor is scarred, their spears tipped with iron. They kneel when they see you.

One holds out a small wooden box.

Inside is your phone.

The screen is cracked, but glowing.

There is no signal, no battery symbol—only a countdown:

**23:59:42**

Beneath it, a single message appears:

**DO NOT LET THEM CROWN YOU.**

The soldiers rise.

From the fortress, a horn sounds.

And somewhere beyond the village, deep in the forest, something enormous answers.
