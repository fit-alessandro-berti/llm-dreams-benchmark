You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, you notice the walls are shifting subtly, rearranging themselves in patterns that seem almost alive. The air hums with a low, resonant frequency—like the maze itself is breathing.

At the first junction, you find a door sealed by a riddle etched into its surface:  
*"I speak without a mouth and hear without ears. I have no body, but I come alive with the wind. What am I?"*

You pause, focus, and whisper, “An echo.” The door dissolves into mist.

Deeper in, you encounter a room where floating symbols—numbers, letters, and geometric shapes—orbit slowly around a central pedestal. A voice echoes: *“Arrange them not by form, but by the silence between their meanings.”* You realize the symbols represent concepts: time, memory, light, shadow, sound, stillness. You group them not by appearance, but by opposition and harmony—pairing light with shadow, sound with stillness, time with memory—and the floor opens to reveal a spiral staircase descending into darkness.

At the bottom, you stand before a mirror that doesn’t reflect your face—but shows you solving every puzzle you’ve ever faced in life, real or imagined. It whispers, *“The maze was never outside you.”*

You smile, step through the glass—and wake.
