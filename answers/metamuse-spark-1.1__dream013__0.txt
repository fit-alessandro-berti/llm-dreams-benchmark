You move deeper into the maze, you start to realize the walls aren't quite walls.

They're made of things. Floor-to-ceiling shelves of old library books, stacked clocks all ticking out of time, tangled vines heavy with glowing fruit, folded laundry that never ends.

And each puzzle changes with the wall that holds it.

At first, you were solving them the hard way — sliding tiles, turning dials, counting symbols, your brow furrowed with that intense, dream-logic concentration. The deeper you go, the more you realize you're not *solving* them with logic at all. You're *remembering* the answer.

You come to a door with 100 different locks. You should be overwhelmed, but your hand just knows which three actually matter. *Click. Click. Click.* It swings open.

You come to a room where the floor is a giant chessboard, and the pieces are all lying down, asleep. You don't need to win. You just need to stop playing. You sit down among them, and the path forward appears.

You come to a mirror puzzle. The riddle written above it says: "What gets clearer the less you try to see it?" For a second you strain to figure it out, that tight feeling behind your eyes. Then you let go, you soften your gaze, and the answer is right there in the reflection — not you, but the exit, behind you the whole time.

The maze gets quieter. The air gets warmer. The puzzles stop trying to trick you and start trying to help you. One puzzle just asks you to stack stones into a little cairn. Another just wants you to untangle a knotted string. Another just wants you to rest for a moment on a bench that wasn't there before.

And finally, at the very center, there is no final boss puzzle. There is no treasure chest.

There is just a small, round, sunlit room with no roof. In the middle is a low table, and on the table is a cup of tea, still steaming, made exactly how you like it. And a note in your own handwriting that says:

*You made it. You can wake up now, if you want to.*

You pick up the tea, you can actually feel the warmth in your hands, and you look up at the open sky above you, which is vast and blue and has no maze at all.
