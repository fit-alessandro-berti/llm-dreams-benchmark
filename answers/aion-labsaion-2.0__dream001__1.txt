I start to run, my footsteps echoing against the glass-and-steel canyons. The address on my phone is a shifting string of numbers, the map app showing only a pulsating dot in the center of a labyrinth. I ask a passerby for directions, but their face is a blur, and they speak in a language of liquid mercury, the words pooling at my feet. Desperation tightens my chest. I hail a taxi, a sleek vehicle the color of a bruise. The driver doesn't turn around. "The meeting," I gasp. "Do you know it?" A mirror replaces the rearview, and I see my own face, but older, weary, nodding slowly. "You're already late," my reflection says. The city outside the window melts into a watercolor of blues and greys. The taxi isn't on a road anymore, but gliding down a wide, silent river. The buildings are now ancient trees, their branches holding glowing lanterns. I understand, without knowing how, that the meeting is at the heart of the forest. A deep, resonant chime sounds once, marking the hour. I am exactly on time.
