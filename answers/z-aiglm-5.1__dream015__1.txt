You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to keep walking.

The road stretches ahead, cracked and buckled, with weeds pushing through the asphalt like nature's slow reclamation. Your boots crunch over broken glass. The sun hangs low and red behind a permanent haze, casting everything in amber.

You come upon an old gas station. The pumps are rusted skeletons, but the small convenience store still stands, its windows boarded but its door slightly ajar. Inside, amid the debris and dust, you find something unexpected: a working radio, faintly crackling with static.

You fiddle with the dial. A voice emerges, distant but clear enough.

*"…if anyone can hear this, we're at the old lighthouse. We have water, medicine, shelter. There's still hope. We're waiting for you."*

The signal fades.

You step back outside. The horizon offers nothing but empty road in both directions. No sign of a lighthouse. No guarantee the message is real or recent.

But your hand finds a worn map in your pocket you don't remember putting there. And on it, circled in faded ink, is a lighthouse on a distant shore.

The dream shifts. You're walking again, but now the landscape changes faster than it should—ruined cities giving way to forests, forests to coastal cliffs. The air tastes like salt. Seabirds cry overhead, the first living sounds you've heard in what feels like years.

And there, far below, standing against the churning gray sea, is a lighthouse. Its light turns slowly, sweeping the darkness.

A figure stands at its base, watching you descend the cliff path. They raise a hand in greeting.

You wake before you reach them, but the feeling stays—a warmth in your chest, the sense that something was waiting to be found all along.
