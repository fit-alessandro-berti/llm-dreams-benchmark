...start to run through a mental list of clues—an old business card in my pocket, a half-remembered street name, the way the light seems to slant toward what might be a downtown—and I set off walking fast, though the streets keep rearranging themselves behind me like they're being redrawn as I go. Buildings lean in curious, unfamiliar shapes, storefronts flicker between languages I both know and don't, and every person I ask gives me a different, dreamily unhelpful answer: "It's past the fountain that isn't there anymore," or "You already went, don't you remember?"

I check my pockets again and find the business card has changed—the name is smudged, but there's a symbol on it, a spiral or a staircase, and something in me *knows* that's the way. I follow it down an alley that shouldn't exist between two buildings that were flush against each other a second ago, and the ground tilts gently, like the city itself is exhaling.

Time feels stretchy now—the hour I had is somehow both nearly gone and infinitely renewable, the way dream-clocks work. I catch a glimpse of my reflection in a window and for a second I'm wearing clothes I don't own, holding a briefcase I've never seen, and there's a strange calm in accepting that maybe the meeting isn't really about being on time. Maybe it's about arriving at the right feeling—and right as that thought settles, the alley opens into a wide plaza filled with people who all turn, as one, to look at me, as though I'm the one who's late for *them*.

Would you like me to keep going from here?
