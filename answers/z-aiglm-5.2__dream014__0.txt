You hesitate for a moment, their faces warm but slightly out of focus, the way people sometimes appear in dreams. Something about their presence feels deeply familiar, though you're certain you've never met them before.

One of them — a tall woman with silver hair that seems to glow faintly — extends her hand. "Come," she says gently. "You've been wandering for a long time."

As you walk together, the city begins to change. The harsh grey buildings soften, their edges blurring into watercolor strokes of amber and deep blue. The noise of traffic fades into a low, melodic hum, like a lullaby played on distant strings. The strangers guide you through alleyways that weren't there a moment ago, past storefronts displaying memories instead of merchandise — a childhood bicycle, a half-finished letter, a window seat on a rainy afternoon.

"We don't actually know the way," a young man in the group admits with a sheepish grin. "But that's the secret. Nobody does. The point was never to arrive."

You feel a swell of emotion — grief and gratitude tangled together. You want to thank them, but when you turn, the group has scattered like seeds in the wind, each one drifting toward a different horizon.

And then you notice it: the street ahead is dark, but your own feet are glowing faintly, leaving soft prints of light on the pavement. The path isn't ahead of you.

It's *underneath* you. It always was.

You take a step forward. The ground holds. You take another. Somewhere, a bird begins to sing — not a species you recognize, but a sound that makes your chest ache with something that feels like hope.

The city dissolves into mist. You are walking on nothing and everything at once.

And then — without any fanfare, without any thunderclap of revelation — you simply feel:

*Found.*

Not by someone else. By yourself.

You wake slowly, the dream dissolving like sugar in warm water, but the feeling stays with you — sitting quietly in the center of your chest, like a small lantern left burning in a window.

Just in case you ever need to find your way back.
