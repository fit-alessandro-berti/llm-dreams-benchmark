The air here is thin, tasting of crisp pine and woodsmoke, lacking the ozone hum of the city I just left. I am standing in a narrow valley, surrounded by sprawling fields of waist-high, violet-hued grass that ripples in the wind like a sea. To the east, a jagged obsidian mountain range pierces a sky the color of a bruised plum.

I look down at my hands. I’m wearing rough-spun linen, stained with travel and something dark. My pockets are empty—save for a smooth, heavy river stone carved with a single, weeping eye.

I need to find a settlement, or at least a path. As I begin to trek toward the treeline, a sound stops me cold: the rhythmic, metallic *clink-clink-clink* of a tethered horse, followed by a voice humming a melody that feels older than the ground beneath my feet. 

I duck behind a boulder, my heart hammering against my ribs. A figure emerges from the tall grass—not human, not quite, but tall and draped in furs, carrying a spear tipped with glowing crystal. They stop, tilting their head as if listening to a frequency I cannot hear. Then, they turn, looking directly at the boulder where I’m hiding, and smile.

They aren't surprised to see me. In fact, they look relieved.

"You're late," the stranger says, their voice echoing strangely in the valley. "The constellations have already begun to shift. If we don’t reach the High Spire by moonrise, the doorway won't just close—it will break."

I realize then that I didn't arrive here by accident. The stone in my pocket grows warm, vibrating against my palm. I have to make a choice: step out and accept this destiny, or turn and run into the violet grass, hoping to wake up in a bed that still has sheets and a clock that ticks in seconds instead of lifetimes.

I take a breath, feeling the air settle deep in my lungs, and step out from behind the rock. I am not ready, but I am here. 

"Lead the way," I say.
