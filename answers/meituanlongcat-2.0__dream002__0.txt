You notice the leaves are turning inside out, revealing shimmering, silver undersides that catch the dimming light. The path beneath your feet softens into a carpet of moss that glows faintly blue with each step you take.

A flash of lightning splits the sky, but instead of illuminating the scene, it freezes everything mid-motion—a suspended curtain of rain, a jay hanging in the air with wings spread wide. You realize you can walk through this frozen storm untouched. The thunder, when it comes, sounds like a voice calling your name from very far away, and you feel an inexplicable pull to follow it.

You step off the path and into the rain, which now falls upward, droplets rising like tiny diamonds toward the churning clouds. The forest floor dissolves, and you're walking on water, then on nothing, the ground swallowed by a gentle mist. The owl you saw earlier lands beside you, but now it's perched on the shoulder of a figure made entirely of starlight and old memories. They smile and extend a hand.

"Every storm is a door," they say, their voice rustling like pages of a book. "Will you see what's on the other side?"

Behind them, a colossal door of twisted wood and iron appears, floating in the air. The rain is a curtain before it, and the lightning is the knocker. You reach for the handle, feeling it pulse like a heartbeat.
