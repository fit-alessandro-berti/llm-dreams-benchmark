...plant a garden in the rusted hull of a dead sedan.

The soil is not soil—it's pulverized concrete and ash, grey as cigarette smoke. You carry it in your cupped hands from the riverbank where green things still remember how to be green. Your fingers bleed into the dust. The blood helps. Everything helps.

*A seed. Then another. Bean. Squash. Something your grandmother called *three sisters* but you only have two sisters left and the third is a name you forgot.*

The sky bruises purple. Acid rain hisses against the car's shattered windshield. You've propped a tarp overhead with rebar and prayer.

**They'll die. Everything dies.**

But the seeds swell. Split. Push pale necks toward poison light.

A woman walks from the waste. Her hair is wire. Her eyes are milky cataracts but she *sees* the garden. She doesn't ask. She kneels. Presses a palm to the warm dirt. Whispers something in a language that might be Spanish, might be the tongue the world spoke before words.

*You hand her a seed. She presses it to her lips. Swallows.*

"Tomorrow," she says. "Tomorrow the corn comes."

You wake with dirt under your fingernails.
