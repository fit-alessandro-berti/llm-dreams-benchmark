It is food, water, shelter, and not saying the wrong thing to the wrong person.

You need to learn the customs before your accent, your ignorance, or your habits make you dangerous. In this era, being odd is not charming. It is suspicious.

So you begin carefully.

You copy how people speak. You keep your eyes lowered when needed. You trade labor for bread. And then a second realization, colder than the first: you may never get back.

Then survival becomes something else.

Belonging.

You apprentice yourself to the healer because knowledge is the closest thing to power here. You learn to set bones, stitch wounds, dry herbs, listen more than you speak. People stop looking at you as though you might be a spy or a fool. They start knocking at your door in the night, desperate and hopeful, carrying fevers and bleeding children and broken men.

Years pass strangely in dreams. One snowfall, then ten.

You become part of the village story: the one who appeared from nowhere, who knew strange remedies, who asked stranger questions, who eventually stopped asking them.

But on the oldest hill beyond the fields, there is a ring of standing stones. The villagers avoid it. They say the place is thin there, that voices answer back on certain nights, that people who sleep within the circle dream of impossible cities full of captured lightning.

So of course, one midsummer eve, when the moon is bright and round and the air trembles with heat, you go.

You stand in the center of the stones and think of everything you lost: glass towers, screens glowing in the dark, music in earbuds, hot showers, your own name spoken by people who knew you before all this.

Then you think of everything you found: smoke rising from your chimney, hands callused by real work, faces lit by firelight, a life built not by convenience but by care.

The stones begin to hum.

The ground beneath your feet warms. The world bendsŚnot visibly, but deeply, like breath drawn before speech. In the humming air, you hear two eras calling for you at once.

And you understand, suddenly, what you need to do.
