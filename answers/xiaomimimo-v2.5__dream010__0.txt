As the final challenge materializes, you find yourself in a vast, shifting library. Books fly through the air like startled birds, their spines glowing with different colored lights—each one a potential career path, a possible future. A calm, resonant voice echoes from no particular direction: *"Construct the argument that proves your worth."*

You reach for a familiar, leather-bound volume labeled *Your Field of Expertise*, but as your fingers touch it, the cover dissolves into shimmering dust. Panic flickers—your foundation is gone. You take a breath and remember: this is a dream. The rules are different here.

You close your eyes, not to shut out the chaos, but to listen. You hear the whisper of pages turning, the hum of ideas. Slowly, a book assembles itself in your hands, page by page. It’s not written in ink, but woven from moments: a late-night breakthrough, a patient explanation to a struggling colleague, the quiet pride of a project well-done. It’s your story, not just your skill set.

You hold it up. The flying books pause, hovering. The voice returns, softer now. *"The argument is not in the book you hold, but in the quiet courage it took to write it without a template."*

A single door appears at the far end of the library. The final challenge is simply to walk through it. Your feet feel heavy, each step a negotiation with doubt. *What if the job on the other side isn't real? What if you're not ready?*

You reach the door. The handle is cool and solid. As you turn it, the library, the books, the doubt—all of it folds in on itself like an origami crane taking flight.

You step through into soft morning light, the scent of coffee and possibility in the air. A desk is there, just as you’d imagined it. A note sits on the keyboard, written in a handwriting that feels like your own future: *"You didn't prove your worth. You remembered it. Now, let's begin."*

The dream feels less like a test you passed and more like a map you finally learned how to read.
