You are about to take the final exam for a course that determines your future career. As you sit down to start the exam, you notice the questions are written in your own handwriting—but you don’t remember writing them.

The first asks: **“What did you give up to get here?”**

The second: **“Who would you be if you failed?”**

You turn the page, hoping for something practical, but every question is about a moment you tried to forget. The clock above the door has no hands, yet you can hear it ticking. Around you, the other students finish their exams and quietly leave. When you look down again, the pages are blank except for one final question:

**“Do you still want the life you’re preparing for?”**

You pick up your pen, but it has become a key.

At the front of the room, the professor—who is suddenly your future self—points toward a door you hadn’t noticed before.

“You don’t have to pass,” they say. “You only have to choose.”

You put the key in the lock. Behind the door is everything you were afraid to lose—and, beyond it, a life you had never thought to imagine.

Then the bell rings.

You wake up just before turning the key, with the answer already forming on your lips.
