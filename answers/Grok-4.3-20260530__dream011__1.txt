**In your dream, the questions are...**

...not questions at all.

They’re blank.

The entire exam booklet is empty—pure white pages that seem to breathe, pulsing faintly under the fluorescent lights that flicker like dying stars. You glance around the cavernous hall. Hundreds of other students sit frozen, pencils hovering, faces blank as the pages in front of them. The proctor at the front is you. Or rather, a version of you ten years older, wearing a suit that doesn’t fit quite right, staring back with tired eyes.

A single sentence appears on the first page in elegant, shifting script:

**“Describe the career you were never allowed to want.”**

Your hand moves without permission. The pen writes in your own handwriting, but the words feel like they’re coming from somewhere deeper:

“I wanted to be the person who notices the small miracles—the way sunlight hits a coffee cup at 7:14 a.m., the sound a book makes when you finally close it after the last page. I wanted to collect moments instead of promotions. I wanted to be the one who makes other people feel less alone in their own heads.”

The page fills instantly, then dissolves into light.

The next page appears:

**“What would you do if failure was impossible and judgment didn’t exist?”**

You write faster now, heart hammering:

“I would wake up every morning and ask the universe what it needs from me today. Then I would spend the rest of the day becoming exactly that. I would build cathedrals out of kindness. I would turn ordinary Tuesdays into legends. I would love without keeping score.”

The paper laughs—actually laughs, a soft, warm sound like wind through leaves.

The final page materializes, glowing gold at the edges:

**“Sign here to accept the position you were always meant for.”**

There’s no line. Just an open space.

You press your palm flat against the page.

The entire exam hall dissolves.

You’re standing in a meadow that smells like every library you’ve ever loved. The sky is the exact shade of the first book you ever stayed up all night reading. In the distance, a door stands open. Beyond it you can see every version of you that ever existed—child you, awkward teen you, the you who almost gave up— all smiling and waving you forward.

A voice that sounds suspiciously like your own whispers:

“The test was never about what you know.  
It was about whether you’d remember who you are when no one is grading you.”

You step through the door.

And just before you wake, you hear one last line written across the inside of your eyelids in letters made of starlight:

**“Congratulations. You passed by becoming yourself.”**

You open your eyes in the real world, heart still racing, cheeks wet, and the strangest certainty blooming in your chest:

Your future career has already begun.  
It’s the one where you get to keep dreaming on purpose.
