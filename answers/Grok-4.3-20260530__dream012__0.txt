**The Dream Continues...**

...and you stand tall at the helm, salt stinging your eyes and wind whipping your coat like a living banner. The *Starfire* groans beneath your boots as a wall of black water slams into her starboard side, sending three crewmen sliding across the slick deck. 

"Captain!" shouts First Mate Kael, his voice barely cutting through the roar. "The foremast's splitting! If we don't reef the sails we'll lose her!"

You feel the dream's strange clarity settle over you. Time slows. You see everything at once: the terrified faces of your crew, the lightning veins cracking across the sky, the compass spinning wildly as if the sea itself is trying to rewrite north.

You raise your voice, and it carries like thunder:

"**Hold fast, Starfire!** This storm thinks it owns the night—but the night belongs to those who dare to sail it!"

You spin the wheel hard to port, aiming straight into the heart of the tempest instead of running from it. The ship heels violently. Men scream. Ropes snap like gunshots. A massive wave lifts the bow until you're staring straight up at the boiling clouds—then drops her like a stone. The world goes dark for a heartbeat.

When the *Starfire* slams back down, something changes.

The storm doesn't weaken. It *parts*.

A tunnel of calm opens directly ahead, walls of water towering on either side like liquid glass. In the distance, through the churning spray, you see it: an island that shouldn't exist. Towers of white stone rising from the waves, glowing with soft blue light. Ancient. Waiting.

Kael grabs your arm, eyes wide. "Captain... that's no island. That's *Elyria*. The lost city. The stories say it only appears to captains who choose courage over safety."

You grin, rain streaming down your face.

"Then let's give the legends something new to talk about."

You drive the ship forward into the glowing passage. The storm howls behind you like a wounded beast, but it cannot touch you now. The crew begins to cheer—first one voice, then twenty, then the whole deck thundering with hope.

As the *Starfire* glides into the sheltered bay of Elyria, the clouds above you dissolve into a sky full of unfamiliar stars. The water turns mirror-still. You drop anchor with a heavy splash that echoes like a promise.

You turn to your crew, who now look at you not with fear, but with awe.

"Welcome home, lads," you say. "The storm was only the price of admission."

And just as the first golden light of dawn touches the white towers, you feel the dream begin to fray at the edges...

You wake with the taste of salt on your lips and the echo of cheers still ringing in your ears.

The storm is gone.

But the courage? That stays with you.
