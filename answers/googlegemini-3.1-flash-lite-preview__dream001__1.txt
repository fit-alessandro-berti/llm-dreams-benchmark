You start to sprint, your feet barely grazing the kaleidoscopic cobblestones. The architecture is impossible; buildings curl upward like unfurling ferns, their glass surfaces reflecting a sky that is a swirling watercolor of violet and burnt orange. 

You weave through a throng of people, but as you pass them, you realize they aren’t speaking. They are humming, their collective vibrations creating a low-frequency pulse that vibrates in your own ribcage. You stop a passerby—a woman wearing a coat made of shifting, iridescent leaves—and ask, "The meeting! Where is the meeting?"

She turns, her eyes containing tiny, spinning nebulae. She doesn't answer with words, but instead presses a cold, heavy key into your palm. It is made of carved ice, yet it does not melt. "You aren't going to a room," she whispers, her voice sounding like wind through chimes. "You are going to a convergence."

She points toward the center of the city, where a tower of spun light pierces the clouds. As you run toward it, you notice the city beginning to thin out. The buildings dissolve into mist behind you, and the ground starts to feel less like stone and more like the surface of a deep, calm lake. 

You reach the base of the tower and find no door, only a vast, polished surface acting as a mirror. You raise the ice key, but as you approach the reflection, you realize you don’t see yourself. You see the meeting room, perfectly still, waiting. 

You reach out to touch the glass, and your hand slips through it like water. Just as your shoulder clears the threshold, you hear the distant, jarring sound of an alarm—a shrill, mechanical bird—tearing through the fabric of the dream. 

You try to pull your other hand through, to grasp the handle of the door on the other side, but the fog of the city begins to claim the air around you. The violet sky turns the flat, pale gray of your bedroom ceiling. The ice key shatters into nothingness, and you wake up, your fingers still curled in the air, grasping for a meeting that no longer exists.
