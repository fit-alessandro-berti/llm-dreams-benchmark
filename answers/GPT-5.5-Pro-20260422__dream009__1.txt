You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to **find out not only where you are, but why everyone seems to recognize you**.

The streets are narrow and made of uneven stone, slick from a rain that has already passed. Lanterns swing from wooden beams above shop doors, their flames trembling in the fog. Horses clatter by, pulling carts full of apples, bolts of cloth, and cages of restless blackbirds. The air smells of smoke, bread, wet wool, and something metallic you cannot name.

You catch your reflection in a dark shop window.

You are dressed in clothes you do not own.

A long coat. Heavy boots. A silver pin shaped like an eye fastened at your collar.

Before you can touch it, an old woman selling flowers grips your sleeve.

“You shouldn’t be walking openly,” she whispers. “Not today.”

You stare at her. “Do I know you?”

Her face changes. Fear first. Then pity.

“Oh,” she says softly. “It happened again.”

A bell begins to ring somewhere far away. Not one bell, you realize. Many. The sound spreads across the rooftops, echoing through the fog like a warning passed from tower to tower.

People stop what they are doing. Doors slam shut. Shutters close. Even the horses seem to lower their heads.

The old woman pushes a bundle of blue flowers into your hands. Hidden among the stems is a folded scrap of parchment.

“Go to the clockless tower,” she says. “And whatever you do, do not let them hear you say you’re from the future.”

You look down at the parchment. The handwriting is your own.

It says:

**When the bells ring thirteen, wake the king before he remembers he is dead.**
