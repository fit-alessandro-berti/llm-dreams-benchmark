...and the air thickens with the scent of ozone and crushed pine. The canopy above you twists into dark, swirling shapes—not with menace, but with a strange, urgent invitation. Thunder doesn’t crash; it *hums*, a deep bass note vibrating in your chest like a lullaby sung in reverse. You try to run, but your feet barely touch the moss-covered ground, as if the forest itself is holding you aloft.

Lightning strikes not downward, but *across*—silver threads stitching the sky together, illuminating trees that are no longer quite trees. They are tall, silent figures woven from rain and half-remembered songs. The wind carries whispers in a language you almost understand, and suddenly the rain begins—not cold, but warm, like liquid starlight. It washes through you rather than upon you, carrying away the heavy, invisible weight you didn’t know you were holding.

In the very heart of the tempest, you find the eye: a single ancient oak, untouched and glowing with soft, bioluminescent leaves. Its roots are exposed, weaving into the earth like glowing veins of gold. You understand, without words, that the storm was never coming *for* you. It was coming *from* you—released, beautiful, and necessary.

As the thunder fades into a gentle, rhythmic heartbeat, you sit at the base of that glowing tree. The forest is quiet now, washed clean, breathing slowly around you. You realize you are not lost. You are exactly where the dream needs you to be. And as you begin to wake, the warmth of the rain lingers on your skin, a secret the waking world will never quite take away.
