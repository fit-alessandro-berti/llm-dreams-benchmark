You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to **find a way to anchor yourself to the physical reality of this world before your tether to the present fades away entirely.**

The sky above is an bruised, impossible shade of violet, and the air smells of ozone and crushed lavender. You are standing in the center of what looks like a sprawling, half-finished stone amphitheater, overgrown with moss that pulses with a faint, rhythmic bioluminescence—mimicking the beat of a human heart.

You look down at your hands. They are translucent, blurring at the edges like a watercolor left out in the rain. You are a ghost here, or perhaps a memory trying to become a person.

To survive, you realize you need to find an "anchor." In the distance, a singular, jagged spire of obsidian rises above a forest of silver-barked trees. At its peak, a fire burns a strange, cold blue—a flame that doesn't flicker, but stares. You feel an instinctual, animal pull toward it. 

You begin to move through the forest. The ground beneath your feet doesn't crunch; it hums, a low-frequency vibration that travels up your shins and makes your teeth ache. As you walk, the silver trees begin to whisper, not in a language, but in fragments of your own memories—the sound of your mother’s kitchen, the static of a radio you haven't listened to in years, the feeling of rain on a roof.

You realize the forest is feeding on your history, trying to hollow you out to keep you here. 

You reach the base of the spire. There is a heavy, iron-bound door set into the rock, embedded with a clockwork mechanism that lacks hands. It only has a single reflective glass surface in the center. As you approach, you don’t see your reflection; you see the room where you fell asleep hours ago, your own body slumped on the bed, undisturbed.

The glass is cold—colder than the air, colder than the blue fire above. To anchor yourself, you must press your hand against the glass and intentionally "forget" one thing from your life in the future. A trade: a memory for a pulse. 

You stand before the door, the silver trees closing in behind you, their whispers growing into a roar. You have to decide: what part of your old life are you willing to let go of to make this dream your waking world?
