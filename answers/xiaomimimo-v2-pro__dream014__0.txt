…you feel a wave of relief wash over you. 

They smile — not with the hurried, distracted smiles of busy people, but with the kind of warmth that makes you feel like you've known them for years. One of them, a woman with a bright yellow scarf that seems to glow like a tiny sun, says, "We've been waiting for you."

That should feel strange. It doesn't. In the way of dreams, it feels *right*.

They guide you through streets that shift and rearrange themselves like a living puzzle. Alleyways fold into open plazas. A fountain appears where there was none, and the water carries the sound of laughter — familiar laughter, though you can't place it.

"Where are we going?" you ask.

"To the place you've been looking for," a man with kind eyes says. He's carrying a lantern that doesn't cast light so much as it casts *calm*. You realize you've stopped trembling.

The city grows quieter. Softer. The towering buildings lean in gently, like trees in a forest protecting a clearing. And there — in the center — is a small bench beneath a flowering tree you somehow *remember*, though you've never seen it before.

You sit down. The strangers sit with you. No one says anything for a while. The petals drift down like slow confetti.

And then you understand: you weren't lost in the city. The city was lost without *you*. And now that you're here, it's finally whole.

You wake up with the feeling of sunlight on your face — though it's still dark outside — and a strange certainty that somewhere, somehow, a yellow scarf is glowing just for you. 🌸
