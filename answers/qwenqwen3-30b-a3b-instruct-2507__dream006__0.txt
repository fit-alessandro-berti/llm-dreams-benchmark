I try to stop, to correct myself, to speak in my own voice, but my mouth won’t obey. The words keep rising, not from my mind, but from somewhere deep beneath my ribs—like a secret I’ve carried for years, finally finding its tongue.

Faces blur and reform—not people, but creatures made of light and shadow: owls with clocks for eyes, foxes with feathers for fur, a woman whose body is entirely composed of whispering leaves. 

Still, they listen. They *understand*. 

A wave of warmth spreads through me, not fear, but recognition. This isn’t a mistake. This is memory. This is why I was chosen. 

And then, from the back of the crowd, a single child stands—small, barefoot, and holding a lantern made of frozen starlight. She smiles at me, and suddenly I know: I’m not giving a speech. 

I’m remembering. 

The dream shifts again—darkness, then fireflies rising from the stage like embers. I open my mouth to call out, to ask what’s happening… but the words don’t come. 

Because I’ve already said them. 

And the world answered.
