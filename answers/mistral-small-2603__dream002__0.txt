You quicken your pace, but the path ahead splits—one way leads deeper into the woods, the other toward a crumbling stone bridge you don’t remember seeing before.

As the storm intensifies, the trees groan and sway, their branches twisting like skeletal fingers. A bolt of lightning cracks the air, illuminating something in the distance: a figure standing motionless beneath the oldest oak. It’s too tall, too still—its silhouette doesn’t match any animal or person you’ve ever seen. The rain mutes the world into a blur of gray, and the figure raises a hand, pointing not at you, but *beyond* you.

Then the ground trembles. The bridge collapses into the ravine below, and the path behind you vanishes under a wall of thorns. The storm’s howl drowns out your thoughts. You’re trapped.

What do you do?
