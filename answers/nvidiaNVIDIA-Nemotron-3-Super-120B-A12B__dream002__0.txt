

The wind picks up suddenly, tearing at the leaves and sending a chorus of rustling through the canopy. Dark clouds gather overhead, blotting out the dappled sunlight and turning the forest floor a deep, cool gray. A low rumble builds in the distance, growing louder with each heartbeat, like the earth itself is sighing. You feel the first cold drops of rain strike your skin, sharp and unexpected, and the scent of wet pine and ozone fills the air.  

Through the thickening mist, a shape begins to coalesce—a tall figure wrapped in bark‑gray robes, eyes gleaming like amber lanterns. It does not speak, but lifts a hand toward a narrow, winding path that spirals between the trunks, inviting you to follow. The storm intensifies, thunder cracking like a whip, and the forest seems to hold its breath, waiting for your choice.
