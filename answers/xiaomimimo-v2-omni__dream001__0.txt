The city pulses with a low, humming light—neither day nor night. You clutch a ticket in your hand, its ink smudged but you can make out a symbol: a clock tower, its hands spinning wildly.

You start to run, your footsteps echoing on streets that shift like sand under your feet. Faces pass you, blurred and whispering in languages you almost understand. You see a grand archway, then a market of floating lanterns—each one glowing with a different time. A vendor calls out, “Meetings are here and there, but never where you stare!”

You pause, breathless. Above you, the sky is a tapestry of moving clocks. You realize: the meeting isn’t in a place—it’s in a moment. You close your eyes, listen for the ticking of your own heart, and follow it deeper into the dream.
