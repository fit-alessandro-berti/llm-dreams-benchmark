...each challenge seems oddly unrelated to the job itself.

The first round takes place in a giant library where the books rearrange themselves whenever you look away. You are told to find a single page hidden somewhere among millions. Every time you think you've found it, the words slide off the paper and flutter through the air like birds. Somehow, you learn to follow them instead of chasing them.

The second challenge is stranger. You must cross a lake made entirely of mirrors. Every reflection shows a different version of your life—paths you could have taken, cities you could have lived in, opportunities you missed. Some reflections try to convince you to stay. Others promise easier victories. You keep moving, stepping carefully across floating frames until the far shore appears.

As the competition continues, the number of contestants dwindles. Some vanish into mazes of ticking clocks. Others become distracted by side quests that seem more exciting than the prize itself. Eventually only three competitors remain.

The final challenge is held in a towering building that reaches above the clouds. At the top is a single door marked with the title of your dream job. A judge hands you a golden key and says, "Only one person can enter."

The other finalists rush forward, but when they touch the door it transforms into something else—a mountain, a storm, an endless staircase. When your turn comes, the door becomes a simple wooden gate.

Confused, you ask why.

The judge smiles.

"Because the competition was never about proving you were the best. It was about discovering what would stop you."

You place the key in the lock. The gate swings open.

Inside, instead of an office or workplace, you find a room filled with every challenge you overcame along the way. The moving books, the mirrored lake, the clocks, the staircases—they are all there, now quiet and still.

At the center of the room is a desk with a single envelope.

You open it.

The letter inside contains only one sentence:

*"You already became the person who could do the job."*

At that moment the walls dissolve into bright sunlight, the room fades away, and just before you wake up, you catch a glimpse of the dream job waiting somewhere in the distance—closer than it has ever seemed before.
