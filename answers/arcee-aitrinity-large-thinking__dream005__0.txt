
You push through the murmuring crowd, the air thick with laughter and the clink of glasses. The room seems to breathe, expanding and contracting with each wave of conversation. You catch a glimpse of your friend’s familiar laugh—a bright, anchoring sound—but as you move toward it, the crowd parts and reforms like a living maze.

You pass a group debating the philosophy of clouds, their hands tracing shapes in the air. Another cluster is silently eating floating, iridescent fruit that vanishes with a soft *pop*. Someone offers you a drink that tastes like nostalgia and static.

Just as you spot your friend’s scarf by a towering potted plant that wasn’t there a moment ago, the floor tilts slightly. The plant’s leaves whisper, *“She’s already gone. You’re already gone.”* You turn, but the scarf is now draped over a statue of a sleeping cat that wasn’t sleeping before.

A hush falls. The crowd parts again, not to reveal your friend, but a long, winding hallway lined with doors. Each door has a different sound behind it—a childhood birthday, a train station announcement, a song half-remembered. The hallway stretches into a soft, golden haze.

You realize you’re not just looking for your friend. You’re looking for the version of yourself that knew exactly where to go.

Do you open a door, or do you keep walking down the hall?
