The clock above the board has no hands, but it is ticking loudly.

Around you, the other students are calmly writing answers in blue ink. Their papers are already full, though none of them seem to have opened their exam booklets. Your pencil has become soft like candle wax. When you press it to the page, it leaves behind tiny drawings of doors.

At the front of the room, the professor smiles and says, “Remember: there is only one correct answer.”

You look down at the first question.

It reads:

**Who were you before you needed to become someone?**

Then the classroom windows swing open, and beyond them is not the campus, but a vast dark ocean. Somewhere beneath the waves, a bell begins to ring.
