As you push through the crowd, the air feels thick, shimmering like heat haze over asphalt. The voices of the guests have no distinct sounds; they overlap into a rhythmic, choral hum that rises and falls with your heartbeat.

You encounter a group huddled around a low table. They aren’t drinking or talking; they are methodically folding napkins into intricate, paper-thin origami cranes. As you pass, one of them looks up, their face a smooth, featureless surface of porcelain. They hold out a crane to you, and as your fingers brush the paper, it unfurls into a live, iridescent moth that flickers and disappears into the ceiling of the ballroom.

You hurry past them, your footsteps making no sound on the floor, which now feels like damp moss. 

Further on, you find a second group standing in a perfect circle, staring intensely at a grandfather clock that has no hands. As you approach, the ticking becomes deafening—a booming, metallic sound that echoes against your ribs. One person turns to you, their eyes filled with swirling star-charts, and whispers, "You’re looking for the one who remembers the path, but the path changed the moment you stopped walking."

The walls of the ballroom begin to ripple like curtains in a gale. You spot a flash of color—a silk scarf you recognize—disappearing around a marble pillar that seems to stretch infinitely upward into a night sky crowded with violet clouds. 

You break into a run. The floor beneath you transforms into a winding stone staircase. You chase the movement, desperate to reach the person you know, hoping that if you can just touch their shoulder, the hum of the crowd will stop and the horizon will finally stabilize. 

You reach the corner, lunging forward with your hand outstretched, but as you round the pillar, you don't find a person. You find a large, ornate mirror. 

In the reflection, you see the entire social gathering from above—a vast, swirling pool of light. At the center of it, you see yourself standing in the crowd, looking for someone. But as you watch, your reflection turns its head, looks directly at you through the glass, and places a finger to its lips. 

The floor turns to liquid shadows beneath you, and just as you start to fall, you hear your own voice call your name from very far away—not from the room, but from the back of your own throat. 

The light collapses into a single, blinding point, and you realize you aren't looking for someone else; you are waiting for yourself to wake up.
