You look up, expecting to see rows of anxious students scratching away with pencils, but the room is empty. The walls have dissolved into a vast, starry expanse. There are no desks, only floating islands of light where other dreamers sit, their faces obscured by masks made of old books and clock gears.

A proctor approaches you—not a human, but a towering figure woven from threads of golden time. They hand you a pen that feels warm, pulsing like a heartbeat. "There is no right answer," the proctor whispers, their voice echoing from everywhere at once. "Only your truth. Write what you fear most, and you will pass. Write what you love most, and you will fail. Choose wisely."

Your heart hammers against your ribs. You look down at the paper. The words have changed again. Now it says: *"Who are you when no one is watching?"*

You lift the pen. The tip hovers over the paper, trembling. As soon as the nib touches the surface, the ink doesn't flow out—it pulls *in*. The paper begins to absorb the light around you, darkening, swirling, creating a vortex. You realize too late that this isn't an exam to determine your career. It’s an exam to determine if you’re ready to wake up.

And then, you hear your alarm clock ringing in the distance, faint but growing louder, pulling you back toward a world that suddenly feels less real than this one.
