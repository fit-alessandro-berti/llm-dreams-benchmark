As you take the key, the bustling city around you begins to slow down. The roar of traffic fades into a soft hum, and the neon lights stretch out into long, ribbons of color that weave together to form paths in the air. The group begins to walk, not on the pavement, but along these luminous threads. You follow, feeling lighter with each step, as if gravity itself is letting go of you.

You realize you aren't looking for a physical address anymore. You are looking for a feeling—a sense of belonging that you thought was lost in the noise. As you walk deeper into this dream-city, the buildings transform into towering libraries, their windows open to reveal shelves filled not with books, but with memories: your first laugh, a moment of courage, a quiet sunrise you once witnessed alone but never truly felt lonely during.

The strangers guide you to a central plaza where a single tree grows from the center of a fountain. Its leaves are mirrors, each reflecting a different version of you—some confident, some afraid, all whole. The kind strangers bow slightly and begin to fade, their forms turning into mist that rises into the sky.

"You were never lost," the elderly woman’s voice echoes one last time. "You were just waiting to be found by yourself."

You reach out to touch one of the mirror-leaves, and as your finger makes contact, the dream shatters into a thousand shards of light, pulling you gently back toward waking life, carrying with it a profound sense of peace and direction.
