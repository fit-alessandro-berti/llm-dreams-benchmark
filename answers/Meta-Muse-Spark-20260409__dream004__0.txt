You come across a blank canvas and — even though nothing here stays the same for more than a heartbeat — this one refuses to change.

The whole world is breathing. Hills melt into violet spirals, trees unfold like origami and refold into something else before you can name them. The sky cycles through every shade you don’t have words for. Sound has color. Your shadow keeps deciding it wants to be a different shape.

But the canvas: pure, stubborn white. Waiting.

**What happens when you touch it**

- **Your hand becomes the brush**: The moment your fingers hover near it, your thoughts start leaking color. Not paint — more like memory. The first mark you make isn’t from your hand. It’s the sound your laugh made when you were 6, showing up as a streak of sunlit gold.
- **The canvas fights back**: Every line you draw tries to escape, to join the chaos around you. You have to bargain with it. Draw a circle and it wants to become a ripple, then a planet, then a pupil. You press your palm down to hold it still, and it hums.
- **The landscape notices**: As you create, the wild shifting around you slows. Shapes nearby pause mid-morph to watch. The color-storm quiets, like the world is holding its breath to see what you’ll make of a space that can be chosen instead of inherited.

**What you realize**

This isn’t a blank canvas. It’s the only stable thing in this world because *you* make it stable. The second you expect it to change, it will. Right now it’s blank because you haven’t decided who you are here yet.

So the real question isn’t “what will you paint?” It’s “what part of you is steady enough to leave a mark that the chaos can’t immediately rewrite?”

You pick up nothing — there are no brushes — and press a thumb to the center. A single dot of deep, impossible blue appears. It doesn’t shift. Doesn’t dance. It just *is*.

And for the first time since the dream started, the landscape stops changing. Everything turns to look at that dot. Then, slowly, the world begins rearranging itself around it.

Your move. What do you add next to the dot?
