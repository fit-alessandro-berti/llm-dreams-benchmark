You check your pocket for your phone—nothing. You check the other pocket—there's something folded. A piece of paper. You pull it out while walking, nearly tripping over a crack that wasn't there a second ago.

It's a map, but not a normal one. The streets are labeled with feelings: *Anxiety Avenue*, *The Road You Almost Took*, *A Place You've Forgotten*. Your meeting is marked with a red X on a street called *Remembrance*, but the ink is smudged and the route keeps shifting when you blink.

A woman appears beside you, walking at your exact pace. She's wearing a coat the color of old photographs. "You're going the wrong way," she says.

"Which way is right?"

She smiles. "The way that feels like forgetting."

You turn left at the next corner without thinking, and the city opens up. The buildings part like curtains, and suddenly you're in a lobby—you've always been in this lobby—marble floors, elevators, a receptionist who looks like your mother but isn't. She hands you a name badge. The name on it isn't yours, but it *fits*.

"Eighth floor," she says. "They've been waiting."

The elevator rises. You watch the floor numbers: 1... 4... 7... 8. The doors open onto a conference room where everyone is seated around a long table, and they look up at you with faces you almost recognize, like people from childhood photographs.

"We thought you weren't coming," someone says.

"I almost didn't," you reply, and you mean it in a way you can't explain.

You sit. The meeting begins.
