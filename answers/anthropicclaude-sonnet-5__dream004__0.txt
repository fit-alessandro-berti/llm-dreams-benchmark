a paintbrush that hovers in the air, waiting for you. The moment your fingers brush its handle, it hums—not with sound, but with color, a low violet vibration that travels up your arm and settles behind your eyes.

You lift the brush. The canvas isn't white, you realize now, but *waiting*—a held breath of a thousand possible paintings all at once. You touch bristle to surface and the first stroke doesn't stay where you put it. It curls upward like smoke, then decides to become a bird, then reconsiders and unfolds into a staircase made of turquoise light, each step humming a different note as if the color itself were a chord being played.

You laugh, and the sound comes out as small orange sparks that drift toward the canvas and embed themselves like stars.

You keep painting. A tree grows sideways out of the canvas and roots itself in the air behind you. Its leaves are shaped like punctuation marks—commas and semicolons rustling in a wind that smells like rain on hot pavement, though there's no rain here, just the memory of it painted into the atmosphere.

Someone who might be you, or might be someone you loved once, steps out of the canvas wearing a coat stitched from unfinished sentences. They don't speak. They just take the brush from your hand—gently, like passing a secret—and add one more stroke.

The whole world tilts, folds like paper, and reassembles itself into—

Do you want to keep going, or shall I let the dream land somewhere on its own?
