The city is a kaleidoscope of impossible architecture. Buildings twist toward the lavender sky like pulled taffy, their windows reflecting sunsets that haven’t happened yet. I turn a corner and find myself in a market where the vendors are selling bottled echoes and vials of captured moonlight. I stop to ask a woman wearing a cloak made of moving constellations for directions, but when she turns, her face is a mirror. I see myself—not as I look now, but as I might look ten years from today—smiling with a secret I haven't learned yet.

She points down a narrow alleyway that smells of ozone and old paper. I sprint toward it, and as I enter, the walls begin to liquefy, turning into curtains of flowing water. I push through the cool, rhythmic pressure, heartbeat thumping in my throat, praying I’m not late. 

Suddenly, the city noise drops to absolute silence. I emerge into a room that has no walls, only an infinite expanse of starlit ocean. At the center of the room sits a singular mahogany desk, and behind it, a chair is pulled out, waiting for me. 

I look down at my hands. They are glowing, soft and amber-warm. I realize then that the meeting isn’t with someone else. I sit in the chair, take a deep breath, and finally understand why I came here. 

I reach into my pocket for the notes I prepared, but instead, I pull out a single, glowing key. As I lean forward to insert it into the center of the air, the city begins to dissolve into mist, and I feel the tug of the waking world pulling at my eyelids like a heavy, velvet curtain. 

I don't know what the meeting was for, but as I open my eyes to the morning light, I feel entirely, perfectly settled.
