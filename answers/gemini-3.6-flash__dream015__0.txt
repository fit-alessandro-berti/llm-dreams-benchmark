The sky overhead is the color of bruised iron, and the ash blowing down the empty avenues feels unnaturally warm against your skin, drifting like silent, grey snow. Your boots make no noise as you walk past the rusted ribcages of overturned buses and hollowed-out skyscrapers. 

You pass other wanderers—shadowy figures huddled in doorways, their faces hidden behind crude respirators, clutching iron pipes and cans of preserved food. They watch you with hollow eyes, wondering why your hands are empty, why you carry no weapons, and why you are walking toward the center of the shattered city rather than away from it.

You climb the cracked marble steps of what used to be a grand concert hall. Half the roof has collapsed, letting in a pale, ethereal light. Deep within the cavernous, dusty gloom of the auditorium, resting on a stage covered in creeping ivy, sits a grand piano. It is battered, its black lacquer chipped, its pedal snapped—but miraculously intact.

You sit on the warped wooden bench. The air smells of rain, old paper, and ozone. 

You lay your dirt-stained fingers on the keys and press down. 

At first, there is only a dull, muffled thud. But as you play a slow, forgotten melody, dream-logic takes hold. The piano doesn't just make sound; it generates light. With every note, a ripple of soft, golden luminescence spills across the floor, washing over the decay. Where the light touches, the ash dissolves, and tiny, glowing blue moss flowers bloom instantly out of the concrete.

The shadows at the back of the hall begin to shift. The cautiously silent wanderers from the street draw closer, stepping into the warm glow. They don't fight. They don't scupper for scrap. They just stand at the edge of the stage, breathing in the sudden, impossible scent of fresh pine and blooming jasmine.

You keep playing, faster now, the music swelling until the ruined dome above you begins to dissolve into a vibrant, swirling galaxy of stars. The world isn't broken anymore; it’s just waiting to be played back into existence. 

And just as the final, triumphant chord echoes through the air, vibrating through the ground and lifting you off your feet into the starry sky... 

You begin to wake up.
