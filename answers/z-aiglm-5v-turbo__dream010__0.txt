...**surreal trials that seem designed to test the limits of your imagination rather than your resume.**

First, you find yourself in a vast, empty auditorium where the seats are filled with thousands of mannequins. You must give a presentation, but every time you try to click to the next slide, the screen behind you displays your most embarrassing childhood memory instead of the data you prepared. Instead of panicking, you laugh, turning the memory into a joke that makes the mannequins come to life and applaud wildly.

Then, the setting shifts without warning. You are standing on a tightrope stretched between two skyscrapers, but the rope is actually woven out of your own self-doubt. Balancing precariously, you realize that the "Grand Prize"—a glowing, golden key—is floating just out of reach. To grab it, you have to take a leap of faith off the rope. As you fall, you don't hit the ground; instead, you fly upward, propelled by the sheer force of your ambition.

Finally, you land in a quiet office where the CEO is waiting. But when you look closer, the CEO is just a reflection of yourself, only slightly older and radiating confidence. This version of you doesn't ask for credentials or experience. It simply hands you the contract and says, *"We've been waiting for you to realize you started this job the moment you stopped being afraid."*

You sign the paper with a pen that feels warm in your hand, and as the ink dries, a feeling of pure, electric euphoria washes over you—intense enough to shake you awake, leaving you with the lingering certainty that you are ready for whatever comes next.
