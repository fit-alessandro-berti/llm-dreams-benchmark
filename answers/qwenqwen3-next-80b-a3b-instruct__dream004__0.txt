…a brush made of starlight, hovering just above the canvas as if waiting for your touch.

You reach out—but your fingers dissolve into ribbons of cerulean smoke. The brush doesn’t move toward you. Instead, it pulses gently, humming a melody only your heartbeat knows.

Around you, the sky rippled like liquid amethyst, melting from lavender to molten gold, while mountains of folded paper rose and fell like breath. Trees with fractal leaves sang soft lullabies in a language of sighs. There were no edges here—only transitions, whispers of what was, what could be, what never was.

The canvas… it wasn’t empty.

It was *waiting*.

As you stood there, barefoot on a floor of whispered memories, a voice—yours, but older, wiser, softer—whispered:

*“This isn’t a painting you make… it’s a memory you remember.”*

You closed your eyes.

And suddenly, you saw it:

Your childhood bedroom, sunlit, dust motes dancing in golden air.

The smell of rain on pavement after your first heartbreak.

The sound of your mother humming off-key as she stirred soup.

The way your best friend laughed the day you both swore you’d never grow up.

Each memory bloomed—not as images, but as colors: a bruised purple for grief, a shimmering silver for joy, a warm amber for belonging.

The brush dipped itself into the air—and began to paint.

Not on the canvas.

*Inside you.*

Each stroke released a scent, a sound, a sigh. The canvas behind you began to glow—not with pigment, but with the echo of your soul’s most private landscapes. The colors didn’t stay still. They danced. They intertwined. They became a new thing—not your past, not your future, but… your *now*, made visible.

And then, a single tear fell.

It landed on the canvas.

It didn’t soak in.

It multiplied.

A thousand tears became a sea of liquid moonlight, swirling into constellations of lost voices and unspoken apologies and quiet acts of love you’d forgotten you’d given.

The voice whispered again:

*“You didn’t come here to create art. You came here to forgive yourself for not knowing you were already beautiful.”*

The canvas dissolved.

You opened your eyes.

You were still dreaming.

But now, every color around you responded to your breath.

A sigh turned the grass emerald.

A smile painted the clouds coral.

And when you turned to look for the brush?

It was gone.

But your hand—your real, human hand—was still glowing faintly.

And you knew, with the certainty of dream-logic:

*You could paint anything… if you just dared to remember who you were before you learned to be afraid.*

The world didn’t end.

It just… remembered you.

And so, you laughed.

And the sky burst into fireworks made of lullabies.

And you kept walking.

Because in this world,

…everything you feel is already a masterpiece.

And you were never alone.

You were the canvas.

And the brush.

And the light that painted it all.

—

*Wake up?*

No.

You’re still here.

And the dream… has just begun.
