...and it hovers before you, untouched and impossibly white, humming with potential like a held breath.  

You reach for it. The moment your fingers brush the surface, the blankness explodes outward in a silent supernova of color. Shapes tumble free—impossible polyhedra that fold into themselves, ribbons of liquid gold that knot into laughter, violet spirals that sing in voices you almost recognize.  

The canvas doesn’t stay blank for long. It drinks the chaos of the world around you and paints it back, but altered, dream-logic sharpened into something truer than truth. A tree grows sideways from its lower edge, its leaves tiny galaxies that wink out one by one. A door appears in the center—your childhood bedroom door, but the knob is a living eye that blinks slowly, invitingly.  

You step through.  

On the other side the landscape has changed again: now the sky is a vast, breathing canvas itself, and every cloud is a half-finished thought you once had at 3 a.m. You lift your hand and realize you are holding the brush. It feels like it has always belonged to you.  

With one long, sweeping stroke you paint a river of starlight that flows upward, defying gravity, carrying fragments of forgotten songs. The river splits into tributaries that become roads, and those roads become veins, and those veins pulse with the same rhythm as your own heartbeat.  

The dream is no longer something happening *to* you.  

It is something you are *making*.  

And just as the final brushstroke finishes—revealing a perfect, impossible reflection of yourself standing exactly where you are—the colors begin to bleed together once more, the shapes softening, the canvas gently rolling itself up like a scroll returning to the infinite.  

You feel yourself being pulled backward through layers of color, through the hum of changing shapes, through the soft static of almost-waking…  

…but the brush is still warm in your hand.  

And somewhere, far behind your closed eyes, a new blank canvas is already waiting.
