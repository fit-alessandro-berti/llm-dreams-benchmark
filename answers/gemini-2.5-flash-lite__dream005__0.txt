You are at a large social gathering where you only know one person. As you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people and…

…each group seems to be engaged in a conversation that’s just out of reach of understanding. It’s like listening to a radio station that’s constantly shifting between frequencies, snippets of laughter and intense whispers bleeding into each other.

The first group is clustered around a table laden with food that looks impossibly vibrant, glowing with an inner light. Their mouths move in sync, but the sounds are muted, like they’re breathing underwater. One woman, with eyes the color of polished obsidian, catches your gaze and smiles a slow, knowing smile that doesn’t quite reach her lips. You feel an urge to join them, to taste the luminous food, but an invisible barrier seems to hum in the air.

Next, you find yourself near a knot of people animatedly discussing something, their hands flying like agitated birds. Their voices are a rapid-fire staccato, too quick for your dreaming mind to process. One man, with a shock of electric blue hair, gestures wildly at a whiteboard covered in incomprehensible symbols that shimmer and rearrange themselves as you watch. He looks directly at you, his brow furrowed, as if expecting you to contribute a crucial piece of the puzzle. You open your mouth to speak, but only a puff of glitter comes out.

Further on, a circle of individuals sit cross-legged on the floor, bathed in a soft, dappled light. They are singing, a haunting melody that seems to vibrate in your very bones. The lyrics are beautiful, evocative, yet utterly alien. You can feel the emotion behind the song, a profound sense of longing and belonging, but you can’t grasp the meaning. As you get closer, one of them, a person with skin like pale moonlight, extends a hand. You reach out to take it, and your fingers pass right through theirs, leaving a trail of cool, silvery dust.

The architecture of the place itself is fluid. Walls shift, corridors stretch and contract, and doorways appear and disappear like shy fireflies. The air is thick with a scent you can’t quite place – a mix of old books, rain on hot pavement, and something faintly metallic, like starlight.

You feel a growing sense of urgency. You *need* to find this one person. You scan the faces, searching for any flicker of recognition, any familiar anchor in this sea of strangeness. You catch sight of a group playing cards, their faces illuminated by the flickering light of their own cards, which seem to be made of solidified moonlight. They are laughing heartily, their joy infectious, but you know this isn’t who you’re looking for.

Then, through a gap in a shimmering tapestry that depicts a constellation you’ve never seen, you see them. They are standing by a window overlooking a city made of crystal. They are talking to someone, their back to you. A wave of relief washes over you, followed by a pang of anxiety. Will they recognize you? Will they understand the urgency of your arrival?

As you begin to move towards them, the floor beneath you softens, becoming like warm sand. You stumble, and the groups you’ve passed dissolve into shimmering heat hazes. The voices fade, the music recedes, and the vibrant food dims. The crystal city outside the window cracks, sending ripples of light across the landscape.

You reach out your hand again, desperate to touch something, anything familiar, and just as your fingers brush against the soft fabric of your own sleeve, you feel a gentle tug. You're being pulled back, the dream unraveling like a forgotten spool of thread. The last thing you see is your friend’s profile, bathed in the dying light of the dream-city, and a single, unspoken question hangs in the air:

"Did you find me?"
