# The Dream Continues...

...need to figure out what year it actually is before you do something that gets you burned as a witch.

You're standing in a muddy street. The buildings around you are timber-framed, leaning slightly like tired old men. There's a smell—woodsmoke, unwashed animals, something fermenting nearby. A cart rattles past pulled by a horse with a limp, driven by a man in a rough wool tunic who gives you a look like *you're* the weird one.

You glance down at yourself. You're wearing clothes you don't remember putting on—a coarse linen shirt, no pockets, no phone-shaped rectangle pressing into your leg out of habit. Your hand instinctively pats your pocket anyway. Nothing. Of course nothing.

A church bell rings somewhere, slow and heavy, and a few people cross themselves as they walk. That's a clue. You start cataloguing: no visible chimneys with brick (thatched roofs, mostly), no glass in most windows, everyone's got that particular grime that comes from a total absence of modern soap. Medieval-ish, probably. Somewhere in Europe, judging by the language fragments you're catching—rough, guttural, definitely not anything you learned in school.

You realize your best move is to say *nothing*, understand *everything*, and figure out fast whether you're someone here—a name, a role, a reason to exist in this world—or whether you're about to be interrogated by a suspicious blacksmith holding a very large hammer.

He's staring at you now.

He's walking over.

*What do you do?*
