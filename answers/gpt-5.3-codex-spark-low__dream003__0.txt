  You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns
  and
  the grooves begin to warm under your fingertips, like the carving itself is remembering you. Each symbol shifts when
  you blink: circles that become stars, stars that become birds, birds that become tiny doors. The lid gives the
  faintest click without opening, and a whisper gathers in the air—your name spoken from somewhere behind you, though no
  one stands there. Instinctively, you press your palm to the center of the pattern and the metal ripples like water.

  The box does not open from the outside. It opens from within your memory. A corridor unfolds where there should be
  solid wood—an old hallway of doors in soft amber light. A small brass card appears in your hand, engraved with one
  phrase: Only what is forgotten can be found. When you look back, the box is gone, replaced by a row of keys floating
  in the dark, each with a different symbol from the pattern. As you take one, you wake to silence, and the key is warm
  in your hand, etched with the same pattern as the dream-box.
