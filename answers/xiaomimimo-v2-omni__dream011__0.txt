You are about to take the final exam for a course that determines your future career. As you sit down to start the exam, you notice the questions are...  

**written on the backs of old, faded photographs.**  

The first image is a black-and-white picture of a child standing alone on a train platform. The question below it reads: *What did they forget to pack?*  

The second shows two hands exchanging a sealed envelope. The prompt asks: *What is written inside?*  

Another is a blurry photo of a city skyline at dusk. The instruction: *List every sound you can hear from this distance.*  

You flip through more — a key half-buried in sand, an empty chair in a sunlit room, a crowd seen from behind — and realize none have any right or wrong answers. They ask only for memories, senses, and imagined truths.  

The exam isn’t testing your knowledge. It’s testing your ability to listen to quiet questions — the kind your future career, whichever it may be, will ask every day.
