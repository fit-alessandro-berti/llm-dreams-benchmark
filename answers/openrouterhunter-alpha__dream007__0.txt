You are in the middle of a heated argument with a close friend. The disagreement is about something important to both of you, and you decide to walk away.

But the hallway you walk down keeps stretching. The familiar apartment corridor becomes impossibly long, lined with doors you don't recognize — some painted in colors that don't exist, others slightly ajar, revealing rooms filled with sand, or rain, or silence.

Your friend's voice follows you, but it's not angry anymore. It's coming from behind one of the doors. You open it and find the two of you as children, sitting cross-legged on a floor, sharing something — you can't tell what — and laughing. The argument feels suddenly enormous and absurd at the same time, like trying to hold water in your hands.

You turn back to find your adult friend standing at the far end of the hallway, much closer than they should be. They're holding out a coffee mug. The mug has something written on it, but every time you try to read the words, they rearrange themselves into a different sentence. First it says *I'm sorry*, then *You never listen*, then *Remember the lake?*

You do remember the lake. But you're not sure if that memory is real or if the dream is putting it there.

Your friend says, "It doesn't matter who's right."

The hallway folds up like paper. You wake up with the distinct feeling that you resolved something — but you can't remember what.
