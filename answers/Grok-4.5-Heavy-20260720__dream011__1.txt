You are about to take the final exam for a course that determines your future career. As you sit down to start the exam, you notice the questions are written in a language that looks almost familiar but rearranges itself every time you blink. Equations and definitions dissolve into intimate questions about paths not taken, faces you’ve almost forgotten, and the exact shape of the person you might become. The final question simply reads, in your own handwriting: “Are you sure this is who you want to be when you wake up?”

The clock on the wall has no hands, yet it ticks louder with every breath. The proctor’s face keeps shifting into people from your past. When you pick up the pen, it writes in light — and the other students have turned into versions of yourself from parallel lives, all staring at you, waiting.
