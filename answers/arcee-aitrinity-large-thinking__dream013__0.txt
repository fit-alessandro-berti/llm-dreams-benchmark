Your exhaustion deepens, but so does a strange clarity. The maze isn't just testing your intellect; it's testing your perception of reality. At the heart of the labyrinth, you find not a treasure or an exit, but a single, ornate mirror framed in cold iron.

As you stare into it, your reflection smiles—a smile you didn't make—and mouths a single word. The maze shudders. The puzzles dissolve like smoke. The walls fall away, and you awaken not in your bed, but standing in a sun-drenched, open field, the scent of wildflowers thick in the air, the memory of the labyrinth already feeling more like a lifetime than a dream.

But in your pocket, you find a single, cold iron key.
