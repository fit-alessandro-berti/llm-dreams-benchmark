...and each group seems to be frozen in place, mid-conversation, like someone hit pause on the entire party. Their mouths are open, laughter caught halfway, drinks hovering in the air without spilling. The only sound is the soft crunch of your shoes on the marble floor.

You keep moving, heart beating a little faster now. Every time you pass a cluster of people, their heads slowly turn in perfect unison to follow you, but their bodies stay completely still. Their eyes are... wrong. Too wide. Too shiny. Like polished glass reflecting a light that isn't there.

Finally you spot your friend across the room, standing alone by an enormous window that looks out onto a starless black sky. Relief floods you. You wave, call their name.

They turn around.

It's not your friend anymore. It's you. An exact copy, wearing the same clothes, same nervous half-smile. But this version of you is slightly taller. Slightly wrong in ways you can't quite name.

The other-you raises a hand in a slow wave, mirroring the one you just made, and says in your own voice:

"I've been looking for you."

Behind you, you hear the soft sound of hundreds of heads turning at once.

The party has unfrozen.

And every single person is smiling at you with your own smile.
