Your boss smiles too widely and says, “Don’t worry, it only has to be done before the sun sets.”

So you open the file, and inside is not a document but a map of the building, except every hallway loops back into itself. You stand up to ask a question, but your chair is gone, and in its place is a small elevator with no buttons.

You step inside anyway.

The doors close, and the elevator begins to rise through floors you don’t recognize: Floor 12 is a library of unfinished emails, Floor 7 is a beach made of printer paper, Floor 3 is a forest of hanging ID badges. Somewhere above you, your boss calls out, “Just be creative!”

When the elevator stops, the doors open onto a conference room filled with all your coworkers, except they are all asleep at their desks, dreaming the same dream as you.

On the table is the project, completed.

You stare at it, relieved, until you realize it’s signed with your name in handwriting you don’t remember learning.

Then the lights flicker, and your boss says, “Great. One more thing.”

And you wake up with your laptop open, a blank page on the screen, and a single message already typed:

**Start now.**
