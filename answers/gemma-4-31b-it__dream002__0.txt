I stop trying to find shelter. I spread my arms wide, and as the deluge of golden rain finally breaks, I don't get wet. Instead, I feel myself becoming lighter. My feet leave the glass floor. The wind catches me, not as a force that pushes, but as a current that lifts.

Just as I reach the eye of the storm, where a blinding white sun is hidden behind a veil of lace-like clouds, I see a door standing alone in the empty air. 

There is no wall, no house, just a mahogany door with a brass handle. I reach out to turn it, and the moment the metal touches my palm, the sound of a distant alarm clock begins to bleed into the music, pulling me slowly, reluctantly, back to the waking world.
