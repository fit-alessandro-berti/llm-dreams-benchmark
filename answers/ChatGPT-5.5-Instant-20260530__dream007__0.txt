...walk away for a moment before saying something you might regret.

As you turn the corner, the hallway stretches impossibly long, lined with doors painted in colors that seem to hum softly. From behind each door comes a different version of the argument. Behind one, you hear yourself winning. Behind another, your friend wins. Behind a third, neither of you speaks at all.

Curious, you open a door.

Inside is a small room containing a single table. On the table sits a chessboard, but the pieces are made of memories: shared vacations, late-night conversations, old jokes, and promises. Two pieces near the center are missing.

A voice from nowhere says, "Those are the pieces you're fighting over."

You look closer and realize the missing pieces aren't objects. They're trust and understanding.

The room begins to tilt. The chess pieces slide across the board and rearrange themselves into a map. The map leads to a lake under a sky filled with slow-moving constellations. Standing on the opposite shore is your friend.

You call out, but your words become paper boats that drift across the water. Your friend sends boats back. When they reach the middle, they unfold into tiny birds and fly upward, carrying fragments of sentences:

"I thought you knew..."

"I was afraid that..."

"I never meant..."

The lake starts shrinking until you and your friend are standing face to face. Neither of you looks angry anymore. Instead, you both seem puzzled, as if you've forgotten exactly why the argument started.

Your friend reaches into a pocket and hands you one of the missing chess pieces. You hand over the other.

The moment the pieces touch, the ground beneath you becomes transparent. Far below, you see countless versions of yourselves having countless different disagreements, each one ending differently.

Some end badly.

Some end well.

Most simply continue.

Then all the versions look up at once.

The stars overhead rearrange into a giant question mark, and just as your friend opens their mouth to answer it, a distant alarm clock begins ringing from somewhere beyond the sky...

...and the dream starts to dissolve.
