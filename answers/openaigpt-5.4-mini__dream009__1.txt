…learn how to survive in this world before it changes you.

At first, everything feels impossible. There are no screens, no lights, no engines humming in the distance—only wind, smoke, stone, and the distant sound of voices speaking in a language you almost understand. The roads are dirt, the houses are built by hand, and every small task seems to require a skill you’ve forgotten how to do.

You wander until someone notices you. An old woman studies your strange clothes and bewildered expression, then gestures for you to follow. She gives you water from a clay cup and bread still warm from a fire. It is the simplest meal you have ever had, and somehow the most important.

Day by day, you begin to learn this era’s rules. How to barter. How to read the sky for rain. How to keep your hands steady while lighting a lamp, mending cloth, carrying wood, kneading dough. You discover that knowledge you once thought useless now matters more than anything: how to listen, how to observe, how to be patient.

But something is wrong.

At night, you dream of your old life.

In those dreams, you remember metal towers, moving pictures, voices carried through glass. Then you wake to the crackle of fire and the smell of ash, and the past feels like a myth. Still, each morning, you notice small details that shouldn’t be there: a word spoken in an impossible accent, a symbol carved into stone that looks strangely familiar, a clock with no hands hidden in a market stall.

Soon you understand the truth: this era is not just ancient. It is waiting.

Waiting for someone who has arrived from somewhere else. Waiting for you to choose whether to restore what was lost, or become part of this world and let the future unfold differently.

And as the sun rises over the rooftops, you realize you need to decide who you are before this dream decides for you.
