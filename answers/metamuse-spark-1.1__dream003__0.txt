You examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and then you realize — they aren't carved at all.

They're moving.

Thin lines of light, like liquid gold, slowly tracing themselves across the dark wood. Like rivers seen from an airplane. Like veins. When you hold your breath, you can feel it breathing too. A faint, warm hum against your palms.

There's no keyhole. There never was. Just this seamless, heavy little box that shouldn't be able to be opened, and yet it desperately wants to be.

You run your thumb over one of the swirling lines and it *reacts*. It flares under your touch. The pattern rearranges itself to follow your fingerprint.

And suddenly you can read it.

Not with your eyes. You just *understand* it, the way you understand things in dreams. The pattern says:

*I am where you left me.*

A cold feeling drops in your stomach. You know this box. You had one just like it as a kid. You buried something in it.

You try to remember what, but the dream does that dream-thing where the room around you shifts. You're no longer wherever you were. You're sitting on the carpet of your childhood bedroom. The late afternoon sun is too golden, too thick. Dust motes hang in the air like they forgot how to fall.

The box is much bigger now. Or you are much smaller.

You don't need a key.

You press both palms flat against the top and whisper the only thing that makes sense to whisper: your own name. The name you were called when you were little, the one nobody uses anymore.

The patterns all rush to meet your hands at once —

*click.*

Not a loud click. A soft, satisfied sigh. The seam around the middle disappears.

The lid opens on its own, just a crack. A pure, white light spills out from inside, not blinding, but warm. And from inside you hear... faint music. Laughter from a kitchen down the hall. The smell of crayons and old library books.

You lean in to look inside, and you realize the box isn't holding something.

It's a window.

And inside it, you are still dreaming.
