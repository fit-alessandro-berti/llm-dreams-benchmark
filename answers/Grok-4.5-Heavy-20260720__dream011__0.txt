You are about to take the final exam for a course that determines your future career. As you sit down to start the exam, you notice the questions are written in a language that doesn't exist — elegant symbols that somehow convey perfect meaning the moment you look at them. They aren't about the course material at all. Instead they ask: What would you study if failure was impossible? What truth are you most afraid to discover? And if you could design the universe from scratch, what one rule would you change?

The clock is melting. The other students have vanished. And the only sound is the soft hum of something vast and curious, waiting for your answers.
