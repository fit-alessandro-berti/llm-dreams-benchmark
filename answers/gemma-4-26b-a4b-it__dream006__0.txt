...and you begin to speak, but instead of words, a stream of iridescent, glowing bubbles floats from your lips. 

As each bubble drifts toward the front row, you realize they aren't just bubbles—they are tiny, translucent spheres containing flickering memories. One contains the smell of rain on hot asphalt; another holds the sound of a distant cello; a third captures the feeling of a summer breeze. 

The audience doesn't react with confusion. Instead, they lean forward with rapt attention, reaching out to catch the memories. As the first person touches a bubble, it pops with the sound of a silver bell, and suddenly, the floor beneath your feet dissolves. 

You aren't standing on a wooden stage anymore. You are standing on the surface of a calm, dark ocean that reflects a sky filled with two moons. The crowd has transformed into a sea of tall, graceful willow trees, their leaves shimmering like emeralds. You realize you aren't there to give a speech; you are there to sing the world back into existence, and as you open your mouth to try again, you find that you no longer need words—you only need the light.
