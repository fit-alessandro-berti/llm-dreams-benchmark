…the walls begin to breathe.

At first it’s only a trick of torchlight—stone seeming to swell and settle like a sleeping chest. But then the passages truly shift with each thought you have. When you focus too hard, corridors knot themselves into impossible angles. When you panic, doors multiply. When you calm down, symbols hidden in the floor glow softly and align into paths.

Soon you realize the maze is not testing your intelligence alone. It is listening.

Every puzzle is built from something more intimate than numbers or riddles: a chessboard where the pieces are memories, a lock that opens only when you speak a fear aloud, a bridge assembled from conclusions you once reached and then abandoned. The deeper you go, the less the maze resembles architecture and the more it resembles a mind—perhaps yours, perhaps something dreaming you in return.

At the center of one chamber stands a table set for one, with a final puzzle resting on a silver plate: a key made of clear glass, fractured through the middle. Beside it is a note in your own handwriting:

**To leave, stop trying to win.**

You look up. The ceiling is a night sky. The stars are arranged like all the dead ends you’ve taken so far. For the first time, you do nothing. You don’t calculate. You don’t force a pattern. You simply stand still and let the maze move around you.

Then, with a sound like a long-held breath released, every corridor straightens.

The broken glass key mends itself in your hand.

Ahead, a small door appears where no door was before. Warm light spills through its frame, carrying the smell of rain on pavement and morning coffee and something else—something almost forgotten, but comforting enough to make your chest ache.

You open it, and on the other side you find not an exit, but a bedroom at dawn, quiet and gold, where someone is sleeping peacefully beneath a half-open window.

And as you step inside, you understand with dream-logic certainty that the entire maze was only the inside of a single unanswered question—

and the sleeping person is about to wake up and ask it.
