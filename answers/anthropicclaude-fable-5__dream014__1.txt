One of them—a woman with silver hair that moves like water—takes your hand, and suddenly the city doesn't feel threatening anymore. The towering buildings lean down gently, as buildings in dreams sometimes do, as if they're curious about you rather than indifferent.

The group leads you to a small plaza you're certain didn't exist a moment ago. There's a fountain in the center, and floating in its waters are tiny glowing lanterns. "This is where lost people find themselves," the silver-haired woman says, and somehow this makes perfect sense.

You realize you're no longer trying to get anywhere. The destination you were so desperately seeking has dissolved from your memory entirely, and it doesn't matter. The strangers begin to hum together, a melody you swear you've known since childhood, and the loneliness you felt earlier lifts away like fog in morning sun.

One by one, the strangers begin to look familiar—a teacher you once loved, a friend you lost touch with, your grandmother's smile on an unfamiliar face. You understand, in the way you only understand things in dreams, that you were never really lost at all.

The lanterns rise from the fountain and drift upward into a sky that's turning the color of dawn, and just as you reach out to touch one...

you wake up, with the strange, warm feeling that somewhere, someone was thinking kindly of you.
