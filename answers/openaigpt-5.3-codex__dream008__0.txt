…you open the project folder, and every file inside is named **“FINAL_v2_REAL_FINAL_use_this_ONE”**.

Your boss stands behind you, sipping coffee that never seems to get emptier, saying, “We just need a quick draft. In ten minutes.”

You start typing. The keyboard has no letters—just tiny keys labeled **“PANIC,” “APOLOGIZE,”** and **“UNDO.”**  
Every time you hit “UNDO,” your screen adds another spreadsheet.

A coworker rolls by in an office chair wearing a wizard hat and whispers, “The deadline is not a time. It is a feeling.”

You decide to present anyway. In the conference room, everyone has your face. The projector flickers and shows your desktop: 47 sticky notes, 12 missed calls, and one calendar alert that just says: **NOW.**

You clear your throat, click to the first slide, and somehow begin:

“Today, I’d like to discuss realistic expectations—”

The lights cut out. The emergency exit sign glows. The sign changes to:

**“GOOD LUCK.”**

You run for the door, push it open—

—and wake up five minutes before your alarm, heart racing, genuinely relieved that real life is only *slightly* less absurd.
