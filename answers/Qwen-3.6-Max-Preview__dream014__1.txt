One of them, an elderly woman with eyes like polished amber and a scarf woven from threads that seem to shift color in the light, steps forward. She doesn’t speak, but she extends a hand. When you take it, her skin feels surprisingly solid, grounding you against the dizzying spin of the skyscrapers around you.

"Follow the thread," she whispers, though her lips barely move.

You look down and realize that where her hand meets yours, a faint, golden filament is spinning out into the air. It trails behind the group as they begin to walk, not through the crowded sidewalks, but *between* them. You notice now that the people rushing past are slightly translucent, their faces blurred by speed, while your new companions remain vividly real.

As you follow the golden thread, the architecture of the city begins to change. The steel and glass melt away, replaced by towering trees made of book spines and bridges constructed from old piano keys. The strangers lead you up a staircase that spirals into the clouds, each step humming a different musical note under your feet.

At the top, there is no map, no destination marker. Instead, there is a quiet garden suspended in the sky, filled with flowers that bloom only when you remember something happy. The leader of the group turns to you, smiling gently. "You weren't lost," she says, handing you a small, glowing seed. "You were just waiting for the right soil."

You plant the seed in the center of the garden. As it takes root, the feeling of loneliness dissolves, replaced by a profound sense of belonging—not to a place, but to yourself. The city below fades into a distant dream, and you wake up with the scent of rain and old paper lingering in the air.
