You reach for your pen, but it has transformed into a melting stick of butter. As you frantically try to write with the grease, the paper dissolves into a puddle of water. You look up in a panic, only to realize the other students aren't writing at all—they are aggressively knitting sweaters that depict scenes of your childhood.

The clock on the wall doesn't have numbers; instead, every position just says "TOO LATE," and the ticking sound is actually the sound of someone crunching on toast, getting louder and louder until the floor drops out and you are falling through a sky made of unread textbooks.
