Your phone buzzes in your hand. It’s your friend—but the screen shows a message you haven’t read yet:  

**“Come back when you’re ready to tell me the truth.”**  

You look up, and your friend is standing at the end of the street, not angry now, just waiting. Between you and them, the pavement has split into a thin line of light, like a path opening.  

You take a breath, and suddenly the argument feels smaller than it did a moment ago. The thing you were fighting about is still there, still important, but beneath it is something older: fear of losing each other, fear of being misunderstood, fear that speaking honestly might change everything.  

So you decide to turn around, go back, and try again—but this time, with less winning and more listening.  

And as you do, the whole dream softens, like a hand unclenching.
