You feel a storm approaching, and the air turns heavy, carrying the distinct scent of rain-soaked pine needles and ozone. The green canopy above shifts from a calming emerald to a deep, bruised purple as the light fades rapidly.

The wind picks up, not as a gentle breeze, but as a low, rumbling moan that causes the ancient trees to creak and sway like ship masts in a gale. You quicken your pace, looking for shelter, but the path beneath your feet begins to feel soft and springy, unnaturally so.

Just as the first heavy drop of cold rain hits your cheek, the forest floor transforms. The dirt and leaves dissolve into a shimmering, silver mist that swirls around your ankles. The trees stop swaying and instead begin to crystalize; their bark turning into jagged, translucent glass that chimes like bells with every gust of wind.

Ahead, through the shimmering mist, you see a massive ancient oak, but it isn't made of wood. It stands as a pillar of swirling blue energy. Inside its trunk, you see a staircase spiraling upward into the clouds, glowing with a soft, pulsing light.

You hear a voice, not from outside, but echoing inside your mind, whispering: *"The storm isn't coming... you are the storm."*

Lightning strikes the glass forest, but instead of thunder, the world goes silent and slow. The raindrops freeze in mid-air, hovering like diamonds, and you realize you can pluck them out of the sky as you walk toward the glowing staircase.
