A wall of black water rises ahead, towering over the mast and blocking out the bruised, violet sky. Your crew braces for the impact, but as the wave crests, it does not crash. Instead, it shatters into a million glowing, sapphire butterflies. They swarm around the ship, their wings creating a soft, rhythmic fluttering that suddenly drowns out the thunder.

You turn to look at your first mate for reassurance, but their face is no longer human. It is a swirling map of constellations, shifting and twinkling in the dark. They offer a silent, reassuring smile and point not at the horizon, but *up*.

The ship is no longer fighting the swells; it is rising. The dark, turbulent water simply falls away beneath the hull, revealing that you are sailing through an ocean of clouds made of spun sugar and crushed pearls. The sharp taste of salt in your mouth fades, replaced by the comforting scent of old paper and rain on warm pavement.

The heavy wooden wheel in your hands begins to dissolve into cool mist. You feel a profound sense of weightlessness, the adrenaline of the storm melting into a deep, heavy peace. The rhythmic, crashing roar of the sea slows down, stretching and morphing into the steady, slow ticking of a bedside clock. 

A faint, golden light presses gently against your eyelids. You realize the storm was never a threat; it was just a door. You take one final, deep breath of the cloud-air, and let yourself wake up.
