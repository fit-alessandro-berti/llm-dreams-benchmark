You grip the rail, lean into the gale, and bellow orders that are more prayer than command: **"Lash down the forecourse! Helm hard to starboard—ride *with* the swell, not against it! All hands, hold fast!"**

The ship groans in protest, but she turns, her bow rising to meet a mountainous wave. For a moment, you are suspended between sea and sky—then you crash down into the trough, water roaring over the rails. You meet the eyes of your first mate and nod. They nod back, and scramble to obey.

The storm isn't over. But now, it's a battle you're fighting together.
