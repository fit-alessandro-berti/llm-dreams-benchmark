face various challenges and obstacles that seem to shift and morph without warning, the way things only do in dreams.

The first challenge takes place in a vast auditorium with no ceiling—just open sky that keeps changing colors. You're handed a blank piece of paper and told to "draw the answer." You don't know the question, but your hand moves on its own, sketching a door. The judges nod approvingly, though you never saw their faces.

For the second challenge, you're suddenly underwater, but you can breathe. You must sort floating objects—clocks, mirrors, keys—into the correct chests. Every time you place one, it whispers something personal back to you. *You forgot to call her.* *You were kinder last year.* You keep sorting.

The other contestants are there too, but they keep changing who they are. Sometimes they're strangers. Sometimes they're people you haven't thought of in years.

The final challenge is simply a hallway. At the end, two doors. Behind one is your dream job. Behind the other is something you can't quite remember—but it frightens you.

You walk slowly. Your footsteps echo. Your hand reaches for the handle—

And you wake up, heart pounding, with the sense that the answer was always something you already knew.
