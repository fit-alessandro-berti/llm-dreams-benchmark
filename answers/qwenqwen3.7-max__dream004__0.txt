…as I step closer, I realize it isn’t truly blank. It is humming. 

The surface of the canvas ripples like a pond disturbed by a gentle breeze, reflecting the chaotic, morphing sky above. I reach out to touch it, but my fingers don’t meet fabric. Instead, they pass through a layer of cool, liquid static. 

I realize I don't need a brush. I reach up and pluck a passing shape from the air—a trembling, indigo triangle that sounds like a distant, mournful cello—and press it against the surface. The triangle melts into the canvas, blooming into a sprawling fractal of bioluminescent coral. 

But the coral doesn’t stay flat. It begins to grow outward, wrapping around my wrists, pulling me *into* the frame. 

Suddenly, the perspective flips. I am no longer the painter; I am the brushstroke. I find myself sliding down a river of wet, cerulean oil paint. The sky above me is a giant palette knife scraping away the clouds to reveal a grid of glowing, golden geometry. Gravity feels optional here. I push off the riverbank and float upward, swimming through the air as if it were thick syrup.

A voice, which tastes distinctly like peppermint and echoes from inside my own chest, whispers, *"Mix the shadows."* 

I look down at my hands. They are no longer hands, but swirling orbs of marbled clay. I clap them together. The sound shatters the golden grid above me into a million floating glass butterflies. They cascade down around me, their wings clicking like tiny wind chimes. Wherever they land, the constantly shifting colors of the world freeze for just a fraction of a second, capturing a perfect, still photograph of the chaos.

I try to catch one of the butterflies, but as my clay fingers close around it, it dissolves into a puff of silver smoke. The smoke swirls around my face, smelling of old books and rain. It begins to spin faster and faster, forming a vortex that pulls the cerulean river, the golden grid, and the morphing landscape into a single, blinding point of white light.

The light pulses, syncing with the rhythm of a heartbeat. *Thump. Thump. Thump.* 

The frantic spinning slows. The vibrant, impossible colors begin to bleed out, washing away into a soft, familiar gray. The chiming of the glass butterflies fades into the rhythmic sound of my own breathing. The white light dims into the pale morning sun filtering through my eyelashes, and I open my eyes.
