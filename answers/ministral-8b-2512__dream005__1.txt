You’re in a vast, candlelit hall that shifts between a grand ballroom, a sunlit courtyard, and a dimly lit library as you move. The air hums with laughter, clinking glasses, and the occasional hushed argument. Your one known person—let’s say *Lena*, a friend from childhood—is somewhere in this labyrinth of strangers. You spot her once, across a room, but when you turn to follow, she’s gone, replaced by a woman who looks exactly like her but wears a dress you’ve never seen before, her fingers tracing the rim of a glass that’s *too full*, spilling wine that doesn’t drip but *melts* into the carpet like wax.

### **Encounters:**
1. **The Philosophers’ Circle**
   A group of people sit in a half-circle on a dais, debating something urgent. One holds up a mirror and says, *"Look—this face is yours, but the voice belongs to someone else."* You recognize the voice: it’s *your own*, but older, tired. They ask you a question you’ve been asked before in this dream: *"Do you remember the last time you chose?"* The others nod solemnly. You don’t answer. The mirror shatters into a thousand pieces, each one reflecting a different version of you—some smiling, some weeping, some *not there at all*.

2. **The Dancers Who Aren’t Moving**
   A line of people stand in a perfect circle, their bodies frozen mid-step. Their eyes are closed, but their mouths move in perfect unison, singing a tune you’ve heard before—your lullaby, but sung backward. One dancer’s hand brushes yours. Their skin is *cold*, but when you pull away, their arm is now *yours*, the same one you lost in a dream last week. The song stops. The dancers turn to face you. They’re all wearing your face.

3. **The Librarian Who Knows Too Much**
   You find a quiet corner where a woman in a moth-eaten cardigan sits behind a desk piled with books that aren’t books—pages that ripple like water, titles that change when you read them. She offers you a volume titled *"The Names You Forgot."* The cover shows your own face, but with your eyes closed. She whispers, *"Turn the page."* On the left: a photograph of Lena, smiling. On the right: a photograph of *you*, but you’re standing in front of a door you’ve never seen, labeled *"Exit (Not Recommended)."* The librarian’s fingers hover over the page. *"Do you want to know what’s inside?"*

4. **The Feast That Isn’t**
   A long table groans under the weight of dishes that shift when you look away—a roasted chicken becomes a pile of bones, a cake turns to dust, a glass of wine refills itself with something dark and viscous. A man at the head of the table (a stranger, but his voice is familiar) raises his glass and says, *"Eat. Drink. Remember."* You take a bite of the cake. It tastes like *home*, but your teeth sink into something soft and *wrong*. The man smiles. *"You’re almost ready."*

5. **The Door That Wasn’t There Before**
   You turn a corner and see a door—wooden, slightly ajar, with a brass handle that’s *too warm*. A faint light spills from the crack. You push it open. Inside, the room is identical to the one you’re in now, except:
   - The people here are *younger*.
   - Lena is laughing with them, her arm around someone you don’t recognize.
   - A clock on the wall reads *"1998."*
   The air smells like rain and old books. A voice (yours?) says, *"You can go back. Or you can stay."* The door behind you is now *gone*.

### **The Choice:**
Do you:
- **Step through the door**, accepting that this is a dream within a dream, and the rules might be different here?
- **Wake up**, but only to find yourself in a room you don’t recognize, with a note on the pillow: *"You were never alone. You just forgot."*
- **Find Lena again**, but when you do, she’s holding a key—and it’s for the door you just left?
