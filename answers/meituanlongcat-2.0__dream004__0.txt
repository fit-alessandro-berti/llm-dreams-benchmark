*(The colors in the air slow, coalescing around the blank canvas. It hangs impossibly in the space between a melting clock and a staircase of stairs that fold into a question mark.)*

It’s not just blank. It’s *potential*. A held breath. The canvas hums with a low, quiet sound, like the memory of light.

As you reach out, not with your hand but with your *intention*, the surface ripples. It begins to fill, not with paint, but with fragments of the dream itself.

*Your first memory of kindness* appears as a soft, yellow glow in the left corner, pulsing gently.

*A shape you can’t quite name from a childhood fear* slithers across the bottom edge as a dark, velvety puddle, then dissolves into shimmering dust.

The sound of a distant laugh you once heard crystallizes into a cascade of tiny, pointed sapphire stars in the upper right.

You realize you are not painting. You are *remembering forward*. Each feeling, each half-thought, each forgotten sensation you project onto the canvas becomes part of the dream’s architecture.

The canvas isn’t being filled. It’s making room for *you*.

And as your own history and hope stains the surface, the evolving world around you stabilizes just a little, finding new shapes in your own reflection. You are both the dreamer and the dream, completing each other on a canvas that was never empty, just waiting.
