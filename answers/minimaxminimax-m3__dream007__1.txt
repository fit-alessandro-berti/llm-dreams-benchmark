The disagreement is about something important to both of you, and you decide to...

...take a breath. Just for a second, you step back from the words still hanging in the air between you. Your friend's face is flushed, their hands gesturing in that way they do when they feel unheard. You realize you recognize that expression—you've seen it in the mirror.

And then something strange happens.

The room around you begins to shift. The walls soften, the colors blur at the edges, and suddenly you're not in your living room anymore. You're standing in a vast, open field under a sky that pulses with colors you've never seen before. Your friend is still there, but they've become translucent, flickering like a candle in wind.

You realize—this is a dream. You KNOW you're dreaming.

Your friend looks at you and says, "You always do this. You run away from the hard conversations into somewhere you can control."

The words land like stones. Even in the dream, they sting because they're *true*.

You look down at your hands and notice you can see through them, faintly. The argument fades, but the feeling doesn't—the ache of having hurt someone you love, the fear of being wrong about something you believed so deeply, the strange relief of being *seen* even when it's uncomfortable.

A path appears in the field, splitting in two directions. One path leads toward your friend, who is becoming more solid now, waiting. The other path leads toward a distant house that looks like your childhood home, warm light glowing in its windows.

You understand, with the peculiar clarity dreams sometimes bring, that *both* paths are real. That growth and comfort often pull in different directions. That love and disagreement aren't opposites—they're dance partners.

You take a step toward your friend, and as you do, the dream begins to dissolve into waking, carrying with it the question you'll carry into the day:

*What would it mean to stay—to truly stay—in the hard conversation?*
