…written in a language you almost understand.

At first the letters look like ordinary words, but as your eyes settle on them they rearrange themselves into tiny moving scenes: a house you lived in as a child, a road you never took, a face you recognize but cannot name. Around you, every other student is already writing furiously, their pencils scratching like rain against a window.

You turn the page.

**Question 1:** *What did you promise yourself you would become?*

Your hand freezes. You look up at the clock, but instead of numbers it has doors. Each minute opens into a different life: doctor, artist, teacher, stranger, nobody, someone wildly happy, someone very tired. The proctor walks between the desks wearing your own face, older and calm.

“You may begin,” they say.

You try to write an answer, but the pencil bends like grass. The page absorbs your words before you can read them. Panic rises in your chest. This exam is supposed to decide everything. Your career, your future, the shape of your life. But none of the questions mention the course. None of them ask for facts.

**Question 2:** *Which failure taught you the most?*

**Question 3:** *Who were you before you learned to be afraid?*

A student beside you raises their hand. When they speak, their voice sounds like yours at seven years old.

“Is there extra paper?”

The room laughs softly, not cruelly, but like leaves moving in wind.

You flip to the final page, desperate for something practical, something you can solve. There is only one sentence printed there:

**You are allowed to wake up before you know the answer.**

The walls of the exam hall begin to breathe. The desks drift apart like boats. Outside the window, the campus has turned into an ocean, and all the buildings are floating, lit from within. The older version of you places a hand on your shoulder.

“Did I pass?” you ask.

They smile.

“You showed up.”

The bell rings, but it sounds like morning birds. Your paper is still blank, except for one word that has appeared in your handwriting:

**Continue.**
