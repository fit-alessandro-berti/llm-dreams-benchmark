With each brushstroke you don’t make, the world responds: a sweep of violet births a forest of crystalline trees; a pause between heartbeats leaves a valley of still, silver water. You realize the paint isn’t on the canvas—it’s the other way around. The canvas is painting you. Shapes dissolve into your ribs, colors rearrange the architecture of your thoughts, and the shifting horizon folds inward like a closing petal. 

The ground becomes a mirror reflecting not your face, but the memory of a place you’ve never been but always known. A wind rises, carrying the sound of distant chimes that tune themselves to your pulse. You step forward, not to fill the frame, but to see what happens when you let it fill you. 

The edges of your vision soften. The colors slow. And somewhere between waking and sleeping, you understand you were never meant to finish the dream—only to let it paint itself through you.
