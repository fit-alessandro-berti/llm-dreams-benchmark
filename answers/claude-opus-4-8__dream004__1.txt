...a brush that seems to paint itself the moment I pick it up. The bristles drip with a color I've never seen before—something between the warmth of orange and the cool of a sound, if that makes sense. In dreams, it does.

I press the brush to the canvas, and the canvas drinks the color hungrily. But instead of leaving a mark, it opens a window. Through it, I see another version of the changing world, except this one is still. Frozen. The colors here have finally settled, and somehow that frightens me more than the constant shifting.

A figure steps into view on the other side of the canvas-window. It looks like me, but its features keep dissolving and reforming, like reflections in disturbed water. It opens its mouth to speak, and instead of words, small geometric shapes float out—triangles unfolding into spheres, spheres collapsing into lines.

I try to ask it a question, but I realize I've forgotten what questions are. The concept slips away like sand.

So instead, I do the only thing that feels right: I keep painting. With every stroke, the landscape behind me rearranges itself, mountains becoming rivers becoming staircases that lead up into a violet nothing. The figure mirrors my movements, painting on its own side, and slowly I understand we're painting the same picture from opposite directions—

And then, the way dreams do, the canvas folds inward, the colors rush together into white, and I'm somewhere else entirely, already forgetting I was ever here at all.
