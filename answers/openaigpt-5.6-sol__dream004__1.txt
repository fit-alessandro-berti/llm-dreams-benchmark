a brush floating in midair, its bristles dripping with a color you have never seen before.

When you take hold of it, the world falls silent.

You touch the brush to the canvas. A small blue door appears. It opens inward, though the canvas is thinner than paper, revealing a staircase that winds down into a sky filled with golden fish. You step through.

Gravity loosens its grip. The fish swim around you, carrying tiny lanterns in their mouths. Below—or perhaps above—you see a city made of glass triangles, folding and unfolding like a sleeping animal. Every window contains a different version of you: one laughing, one old, one still asleep.

At the bottom of the staircase waits a child with your eyes.

“You left something here,” they say, holding out a warm, glowing shape.

“What is it?”

The shape changes from a key to a bird to a beating heart.

“You’ll remember when you wake up.”

The city begins to melt into ribbons of light. The golden fish scatter. You clutch the glowing thing as the staircase turns into a river, carrying you back toward the canvas.

Just before you wake, you paint one final mark: a small doorway on the inside of your eyelids.

And all morning, whenever you blink, it is still there—slightly open.
