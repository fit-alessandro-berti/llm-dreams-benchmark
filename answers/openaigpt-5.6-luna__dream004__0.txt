You are in a world where colors and shapes are constantly changing. As you explore this surreal landscape, you come across a blank canvas and a brush floating in midair.

The moment you touch the brush, it fills with a color you have never seen—something between silver and sunlight. You make a single stroke across the canvas, and the mark rises like a doorway.

Beyond it is a quiet blue forest, where the trees whisper memories you had forgotten. One whispers your name. Another whispers a question:

**“What would you create if nothing could disappear?”**

You look back at the shifting world behind you. Mountains melt into oceans; oceans fold into birds; the sky becomes a vast, breathing eye. Then you turn to the canvas and begin painting.

You paint a small house, a warm window, and someone waiting inside. With each stroke, the world steadies. The colors stop changing. The shapes settle into place.

When you finish, the figure in the house steps forward—and you realize it is yourself.

They smile and hand you the brush.

“Now,” they say, “paint the way home.”

You reach for the canvas, but it has vanished. In its place is your bedroom wall, pale with morning light. The brush rests in your hand, though you do not remember bringing it there.

On the wall, in a color between silver and sunlight, is one perfect painted door.
