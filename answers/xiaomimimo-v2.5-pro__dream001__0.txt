# The Dream Continues

...pull out your phone to check the address, but the screen keeps glitching, cycling through languages you don't recognize. You catch a glimpse of a street name — *something* Avenue — before the whole phone dissolves into a puddle of light in your palm.

You flag down a passing stranger. "Do you know where the Emerson Building is?" They smile warmly, point down a street lined with glowing paper lanterns, and say, "Of course — it's just past the clock that only runs backwards." They walk away before you can ask what that means.

You start running. The cobblestones beneath your feet shift like tectonic plates, rearranging the city as you move. Buildings stretch taller, then shorter. A bridge appears over a river that wasn't there a moment ago. You cross it anyway.

Your watch says 45 minutes left. Then suddenly it says *Tuesday*.

You round a corner and find the building — it's enormous, made of glass, but the doors keep rotating like a carousel. You step through one and find yourself in a ballroom. A crowd of faceless figures in suits turns to look at you. A voice announces: *"And now, your presentation."*

You look down. You're holding a single index card. On it is written: **"Just breathe."**

You open your mouth to speak — and the dream shifts again. You're floating above the city now, watching it sparkle below. The meeting doesn't matter anymore. None of it does. You're weightless, and the lanterns are rising like fireflies into an impossible violet sky.

---

*A classic anxiety dream — the urgency never resolves, but somehow, it never needs to.* 🌙
