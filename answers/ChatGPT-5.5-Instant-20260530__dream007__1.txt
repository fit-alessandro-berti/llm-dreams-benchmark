Wanting to reach them, you step onto the nearest boat. Instead of sinking, it carries you gently across the river. You begin to wonder whether either of you was truly arguing about the issue at all, or whether you were both afraid of losing something you valued.

When you reach the far shore, your friend is gone. In their place stands a giant tree with two intertwined trunks. Carved into the bark are sentences from your argument, but the words keep rearranging themselves. Every accusation transforms into a question. Every certainty becomes a possibility.

A door appears in the tree. You open it and find yourself back at the beginning of the argument, sitting across from your friend at a small table. This time, however, neither of you can speak. Instead, colorful birds fly out of your mouths whenever you try. The birds carry your feelings into the air, where they become visible for both of you to see.

Your friend watches a bright blue bird circle above the table and smiles sadly. You suddenly understand something that seemed impossible before: beneath the disagreement, you were both trying to protect the same thing.

The room fills with light. The birds settle on the table, folding themselves into a map. Following it with your finger, you discover a path leading somewhere neither of you had considered. Just as you are about to point it out, the map begins to glow brighter and brighter, and the dream starts to fade.

You wake up with the lingering feeling that the solution was never hidden at the end of the argument—it was waiting in the space where both sides were finally willing to listen.
