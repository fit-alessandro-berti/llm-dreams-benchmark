### **The First Group: The Mirrored Conversation**

The person with the rose turns to you and says, *"We were just discussing the art of forgetting. Do you remember how to do it?"*

You open your mouth to answer, but the words dissolve on your tongue. One of them—perhaps their twin?—whispers, *"She’s already here. You just haven’t looked in the right reflection."* They gesture to a gilded mirror on the wall. When you glance at it, your own face isn’t there. Instead, you see Lena, but she’s older, her hair streaked with silver, and she’s mouthing something you can’t hear. The group laughs softly, a sound like wind chimes in a storm.

### **The Second Group: The Children Who Aren’t There**
You stumble away, disoriented, and find yourself in a corner where a circle of children sit cross-legged on the floor, playing a game with a deck of cards that shift colors as they’re dealt. The oldest one—a girl with braids tied with ribbons that never stop unraveling—looks up at you and says, *"You’re looking for the wrong thing. She’s not a person. She’s a door."*

The other children nod solemnly. One holds up a card: it’s a picture of Lena, but her face is a keyhole. *"Turn the handle,"* the girl says, and suddenly, the card *is* a door, small enough to fit in your palm. You reach for it, but your fingers pass through like mist. The children giggle and scatter, their laughter turning into the sound of rain on a tin roof.

### **The Third Group: The Host Who Wasn’t Invited**
You press on, the crowd now thinning into a long hallway lined with doors—some ajar, some sealed shut with wax. At the end, a figure stands with their back to you, wearing a tailcoat that seems to absorb the light. When they turn, their face is a smooth, featureless oval, like a mannequin’s. *"Ah,"* they say, voice like honey and static. *"You’ve been looking for her in all the wrong rooms."*

They extend a hand, and in their palm lies a single, glowing button. *"Press it,"* they murmur, *"and you’ll find her. But know this: the door only opens once."* You hesitate. The button pulses like a heartbeat. Somewhere, a clock chimes—though you don’t remember seeing one.

### **The Choice**
You press the button.

The world *tilts*. The hallway stretches into a tunnel of swirling colors, and at the end, you see Lena—but she’s not alone. She’s standing in a field of tall grass under a sky that’s both day and night, her hand resting on the shoulder of a younger version of *yourself*. The younger you is crying, but Lena is smiling sadly. *"You were never supposed to find me here,"* she says. *"This is the place between remembering and letting go."*

The dream begins to fray at the edges. The field dissolves into static. Lena’s voice fades into a whisper: *"Wake up now. Or stay. But choose quickly."*
