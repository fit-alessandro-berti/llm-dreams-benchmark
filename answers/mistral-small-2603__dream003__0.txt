**…a faint, whispering hum that seems to pulse in time with your heartbeat.**

Your fingers trace the engravings—swirling vines that twist into impossible knots, their edges shimmering like liquid silver. The box isn’t just locked; it’s *alive* in some way, as if the metal itself is holding its breath. Then, without warning, the patterns begin to shift. The vines rearrange themselves into a new shape—a **keyhole**, but not the kind you’ve ever seen. It’s a spiral, a labyrinth, a question mark all at once, and it *pulses* with the same eerie rhythm as the hum.

A voice—no, a *feeling*—whispers in your mind: *"Turn it."*

You hesitate, but your hands move on their own, rotating the box slowly. The hum grows louder, vibrating through your bones. The lid *clicks*. Not with a sound, but with a sudden *weightlessness*, as if the box has just exhaled.

Then—**silence.**

The lid is ajar. Inside, there’s no treasure, no map, no obvious answer. Just… **a single, perfect snowflake**, suspended in midair, glowing faintly blue. It doesn’t fall. It doesn’t melt. It just *is*, and as you stare, it begins to **sing**—a melody made of wind chimes and distant laughter.

But the strangest part? The snowflake isn’t alone. It’s a **fragment** of something much larger, something you recognize from the corner of your eye when you blink too fast. A **dream within a dream.** A **memory that wasn’t yours.** A **promise** that feels like a warning.

And then—

**You wake up.**

(Or do you?)
