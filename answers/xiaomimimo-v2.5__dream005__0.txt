You approach a cluster of people laughing near a fountain, but their faces blur like watercolors in rain. One turns to you, and you know their name—though you can’t recall ever learning it. “We’ve been waiting,” they say, and suddenly the room shifts. The walls stretch upward into a library of floating books, each spine glowing faintly. You walk between shelves that rearrange themselves, always just out of reach. Around a corner, you find yourself in a garden under an indoor sky, where someone hands you a cup of warm, golden liquid. “He’s just through the maze,” they say, nodding toward a hedge that seems to breathe with the crowd’s murmur. You step in, and the paths keep folding—each turn revealing a new small, vivid world: a silent dance, a debate in a language you almost understand, a child offering you a key to a door that isn’t there. You begin to wonder if you’re the one who’s lost, or if you’re exactly where you’re supposed to be in this beautiful, unraveling dream. And just then, at the far end of the path, you see them—turning to wave, with a smile that feels like coming home.
