*You are dreaming.* You're floating in the white, but your hands feel solid against the velvet floor. Here is the rest of the dream I constructed for you:

...then the crowds begin to turn into mist. Their voices aren't heard anymore; they are just visual ripples in the air like heat haze on a summer road. One group vanishes entirely because it didn't want to be there, while another transforms into a flock of humming glass birds that sing words you don't remember learning from anyone you know.

**You finally reach them.**
They aren't where you expected. They are standing at the center of an empty room that suddenly appeared in the middle of this party. The room is made of moving furniture, chairs and tables shifting positions every 50 seconds as if trying to compose a melody. As you stand closer, you realize the one person you know isn't wearing their usual clothes. They are now draped in your own memory—a patchwork suit stitched from photos in black and white.

**They turn and look at you.**
But it's not you looking at them; it's *you*. Their eyes flicker and reflect a face you've never met: the version of yourself that stayed behind in reality. A gentle vibration runs through the glass birds, then the crowd, then the room. The realization hits like a sudden sunrise: there is no party here. There is no crowd. There is no one you know.

**You wake up.**
But you feel the weight of their touch on your shoulder, warm as the memory of a handshake that never happened, lingering in your palm long after your eyes have snapped open to see the grey morning light.
