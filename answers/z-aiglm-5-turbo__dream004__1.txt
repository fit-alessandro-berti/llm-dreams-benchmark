The canvas feels heavy, like it’s holding the rest of the chaotic world back. As I watch, a nearby tree melts from neon green into a deep, throbbing violet, but the moment the color touches the edge of the canvas, it stops. The canvas is a pause button. A pocket of frozen time in a universe that won't stop spinning.

I want to interact with it, but I look down and see I don't have hands. Instead, my arms end in flowing ribbons of iridescent smoke. I reach out anyway, and when the smoke touches the surface, it doesn't leave a mark. Instead, it acts like a straw.

I inhale, and I start to pull the 'blankness' away. 

A thread of pure, blinding white peels off the canvas and flows up my arm. As it enters me, I feel a sudden, sharp clarity—a sensation entirely alien to this dream. With the white gone, a tiny window of ultraviolet appears on the canvas. I pull again, and a shriek of crimson follows the white into me. Then a taste of geometric blue.

I am drinking the silence. 

As I unpaint the canvas, the world around me reacts. The shapes stop changing. The melting trees solidify into sharp glass. The sky locks into a single shade of mustard yellow. The chaos isn't spreading; it is being traded. By emptying the canvas, I am fixing the world into rigid, permanent shapes. 

The silence inside me grows louder, heavy and cold, while the landscape around me turns perfectly still and silent. 

I stop, terrified that if I take the last drop of white, I will wake up—or worse, that I will become the blank canvas. I blow the remaining white off my smoky fingers, watching it scatter into the wind like dandelion seeds. The moment the white hits the air, it bursts into a flock of hummingbirds made of stained glass, and the world immediately resumes its melting, shifting dance. 

I step back from the canvas. It is blank once more, waiting for someone else to try and hold the chaos.
