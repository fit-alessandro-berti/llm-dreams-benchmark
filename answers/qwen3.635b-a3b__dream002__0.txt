...and the horizon shatters like stained glass, spilling a river of liquid mercury that flows *upward* toward the roots of the sky.

The thunder doesn't crash; it hums a low, resonant cello note that vibrates in your teeth, syncing with the sudden, frantic beating of your own heart. Rain begins to fall, but the drops are warm and taste of burnt sugar and forgotten Sundays. As they touch the moss, the flowers open instantly, blooming into tiny, intricate faces that whisper secrets in a language you almost understand.

The trees step away from the path, their bark softening into velvet and their roots detaching with the sound of corks popping. They shift, elongating and twisting into sentinels made of twisted copper and fern, bowing deeply as you pass. You look down and realize your feet no longer touch the ground; you are drifting inches above the soil, pulled gently by the gravity of the mercury river rising to meet you.

The storm coalesces above you—not dark clouds, but a swirling vortex of thousands of clocks, all ticking out of sync, their hands spinning wildly. From the center of the vortex, a single eye opens, vast and gentle, its iris swirling like oil on water.

A voice resonates, not in your ears, but in your bones: *'The peace was the cage. The storm is the key.'*

You feel no fear, only a surge of recognition. You reach up. Your hand passes through the copper barrier of the storm and touches the glass eye. It ripples like water.

With a laugh that sounds like wind chimes, you dissolve. Your body expands, becoming the wind that tangles in the copper leaves, the rain that feeds the whispering flowers, the mercury flowing backward to the sky. You are everywhere at once, dancing in the updraft, holding the world in your arms.

The dream doesn't end with waking. It ends with you realizing you've been the forest sleeping all along, and for the first time, the world is finally letting you breathe.
