As you step onto the stage, you see the crowd looking expectantly at you, and you begin to...

speak in silence, but your lips move anyway like a secret code written on the skin of the world. The microphone sits between your hands, warm and pulsing with a heartbeat that belongs to no one else but you. Suddenly, the audience begins to whisper their names, not in order, but in reverse, so that when they reach the last name in the hall, your voice finally finds its echo—a sound like shattering mirrors falling on a floor made of midnight sky. You realize you aren't giving a speech to these people; they are simply waiting for the light to fade, and until you forget your own words, they will never be able to let go.
