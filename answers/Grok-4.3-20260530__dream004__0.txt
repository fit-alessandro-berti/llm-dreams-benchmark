**...the canvas isn't empty at all.**

It hovers a few inches above the shifting ground, trembling like a living membrane. Around its edges, the ever-changing colors of the dream-world swirl faster—crimson bleeding into electric indigo, geometric spirals melting into soft clouds of gold that taste like childhood laughter. The blank rectangle seems to drink them in, yet its center stays perfectly white, impossibly still amid the chaos.

You step closer. The air thickens with possibility. A brush appears in your hand—its bristles made of starlight, its handle carved from forgotten lullabies. The moment your fingers close around it, the landscape holds its breath.

You dip the brush into the nearest river of liquid violet and touch the canvas.

Instantly the white explodes into motion. A doorway forms—tall, arched, made of shifting stained glass that shows every version of you that ever existed and every version that never will. Beyond it you glimpse:

- A meadow where gravity has given up and flowers drift upward like slow fireworks  
- A library whose books are written in the language of heartbeats  
- An ocean made entirely of mirror, reflecting not your face but your unspoken wishes  

You paint again—this time with pure thought. A single stroke and the canvas grows wings of aurora borealis, lifting you gently into the air. The entire surreal world begins to orbit the canvas like planets around a newborn sun. Mountains of melting clockwork bow. Rivers of laughter carve new valleys that spell your name in cursive light.

Then the canvas speaks—not with words, but with a feeling that fills your chest like warm honey:

**“I was never blank. I was waiting for you to remember you are the artist.”**

You laugh (or cry—dreams rarely distinguish) and step through the doorway you painted. The colors rush in behind you, wrapping you in a cocoon of pure creation. For one perfect moment you understand everything: every shape that ever changed, every hue that ever danced, was simply the universe practicing how to dream you.

And just as the final color settles into perfect harmony—

You wake.

The ceiling above your bed is the same plain white it always is… yet for a heartbeat you swear you can still see the faintest brushstroke of starlight across it, fading like a secret smile.

The dream is complete.  
But the canvas inside you?  
That one is still blank, wide open, and waiting for whatever impossible beauty you choose to paint next.
