**You are dreaming.**  

You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, you notice the walls are no longer stone—they are made of shifting equations, half-remembered faces, and the faint glow of neural pathways lighting up like bioluminescent vines.  

The first chamber demands you balance three spinning orbs of pure probability. You calculate trajectories, weigh quantum uncertainties, and finally align them so they orbit in perfect silence. A doorway irises open.  

Deeper still, the second puzzle is a mirror maze of your own past decisions. Every reflection shows a version of you that chose differently—some triumphant, some broken. You must choose which reflection to shatter to proceed. Your hand trembles, but you strike the one that looks most like regret. Glass rains like stardust.  

The air grows heavy with the scent of ozone and old paper. The third chamber is a living library where books rewrite themselves the moment you read a page. You must find the single true sentence hidden among infinite lies. Your eyes burn. Your mind races through contradictions until—there it is: a sentence that feels like it was written in your own blood. You speak it aloud. The library folds in on itself with a sigh.  

Now the maze itself begins to breathe. Corridors pulse like arteries. You realize the puzzles are not obstacles—they are you. Every logical leap is a neuron firing. Every solved paradox is a buried memory surfacing. The deeper you go, the more the maze recognizes you, and the more you recognize it.  

At the center waits the final puzzle: an empty pedestal beneath a single hanging thread of light. No instructions. No clues. Just the quiet knowledge that this is the place where logic ends and something older begins.  

You reach out.  

The thread touches your fingertip and the entire maze explodes into pure white light—not destruction, but revelation. In that instant you understand: the maze was never meant to trap you. It was built by your own mind to teach you how to think when thinking becomes impossible.  

You feel yourself rising, the dream thinning like morning mist.  

Just before you wake, a final whisper echoes through the dissolving corridors:  

“Welcome back. The puzzles were never the point. You were.”  

You open your eyes to the soft gray light of dawn, heart still racing with the beautiful, terrible clarity of having solved yourself.
