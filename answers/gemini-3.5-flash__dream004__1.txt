While the ground beneath my feet ripples like warm glass and the sky above dissolves from liquid emerald into a cascade of sapphire feathers, the canvas does not budge. It feels like a pause in the universe's breath.

I reach out. My fingers are tipped with a faint, iridescent glow—a byproduct of walking through a forest of singing crystalline trees just moments ago. 

The moment my hand brushes the rough woven linen, the canvas doesn’t just receive paint; it acts as a vacuum for the dream itself. A stream of liquid amber and molten midnight rushes from the horizon, funneling into the frame. But instead of splashing, the colors arrange themselves into a shape that is steady, defying the chaotic physics of this world. 

It is a door. Not a door made of wood or metal, but a door made of quiet.

I realize then that the canvas is a mirror of my own mind, projecting a deep subconscious longing for stillness. As I stare at the painted door, the chaotic landscape behind me begins to slow. The roaring symphony of shifting shapes fades into a soft, ambient hum. 

I reach for the painted doorknob, which feels cool and solid under my palm. I turn it. 

The canvas swings open on invisible hinges, revealing not another shifting landscape, but a memory: a sunlit porch from my childhood, the smell of rain on dry earth, and a sky that is, beautifully, just one shade of blue. 

I step through, leaving the shifting dream behind, and as the canvas door gently clicks shut behind me, I finally feel asleep.
