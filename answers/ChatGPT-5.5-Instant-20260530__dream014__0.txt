One of the strangers hands you a map. At first it appears blank, but as you look closer, glowing lines begin to draw themselves across the page. Instead of streets and landmarks, the map shows memories: your childhood home, a favorite park, a forgotten conversation, a door you never opened.

The strangers encourage you to follow the path. As you walk, the crowded city gradually transforms. The noise fades into music, and the rushing crowds become familiar faces who smile as they pass. Each turn leads you somewhere meaningful, as though the city has been built from pieces of your own life.

Eventually, the map guides you to the top of a tall clock tower standing at the center of everything. Inside, thousands of clocks tick in perfect harmony. Yet none of them tell the same time.

An old caretaker appears and explains, "These are all the moments you worried about being lost. But look carefully."

You notice that every clock, despite showing a different hour, points with a golden hand toward the same place: a small door at the top of the tower.

You open it and step through.

Beyond the door is not another room but a vast landscape filled with stars beneath your feet and above your head. The strangers stand beside you once more. One of them smiles and says, "You were never trying to find a place. You were trying to find yourself."

The stars begin to arrange themselves into a path stretching toward the horizon. Feeling lighter than you have in a long time, you take the first step forward.

And just before you wake up, you realize that the strangers all have your face.
