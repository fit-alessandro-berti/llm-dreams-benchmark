...feel the deck pitch violently beneath your boots as a mountain of dark water crests ahead. The wind howls like a trapped beast, tearing the orders straight from your throat before they can reach the men. 

First Mate Thomas stumbles toward you, clutching the splintered remnants of the wheel. "She won't respond to the rudder, Captain! The starboard sail is shredded!"

Through the stinging salt spray, you look past him to the crew. Their faces are pale, illuminated only by the jagged flashes of lightning cutting through the ink-black sky. They aren't just looking to you for orders—they are looking for certainty in a world that is dissolving into chaos.

In a dream, time bends. Suddenly, the roaring thunder seems to mute, dropping to a low, rhythmic thrum. You look down at your hands and notice they are glowing faintly, pulsing with a strange, bioluminescent blue light. You realize, with the sudden, lucid clarity of sleep, that you don't fight the storm. *You are the storm.*

You place a hand on the broken wheel. Instead of splintered oak, it feels warm, like living skin. 

You turn to Thomas, your voice suddenly echoing not just across the deck, but across the entire ocean. 

"Drop all remaining sails," you command, a smile pulling at the corner of your lips. "And ready the cannons. We aren't riding out the storm... we're going to hunt it."

The crew gasps, but as your glowing hand merges with the ship's wood, the terror in their eyes transforms into awe. The *Aethelgard* stops pitching. Instead, the vessel lifts smoothly out of the churning water, rising effortlessly into the churning, purple clouds.

Below you, the sea becomes a swirling map of stars. You take a deep breath of the ozone-heavy air, ready to steer the ship off the edge of the world.
