You are about to take the final exam for a course that determines your future career. As you sit down to start the exam, you notice the questions are **not words at all, but living things**—small, fluttering equations and diagrams that crawl across the page like insects made of ink. Every time you try to read one, it scurries to the corner of the paper and dissolves into a smudge.

You grip your pencil, but it has turned into a feather. The desk beneath your hands stretches longer and longer, the rows of students fading into a mist until you are alone in a hallway that keeps adding doors. Somewhere, a clock ticks backward. You hear the distant sound of pages turning, though no one else is there.

A question finally stops moving long enough for you to understand it. It is not about the course; it asks you to draw the sound of your own name. You press the feather to the paper and, instead of ink, it leaves a trail of warm light. The light spreads across the page, spelling out answers you didn’t know you knew.

The room begins to fill with water—not threatening, but calm and blue, rising gently around your chair. You realize you can breathe it. The exam papers float upward, forming a staircase that leads to a door marked **“Tomorrow.”** You stand, no longer afraid, and take the first step.

Just as your hand reaches the knob, the water drains away, the light fades, and a familiar ringing sound pulls you backward through the dream.

You wake up. The real exam isn’t until Thursday, and your notes are still open on the desk beside a half-empty coffee mug. Outside, the morning sun is rising.
