...find a way to survive.  

The air is thick with the scent of damp earth, woodsmoke, and something sweet—wildflowers crushed underfoot. You're lying in a clearing near a forest fringed with ancient oaks, their branches heavy with moss. The sky is a vast, unbroken canvas of deep blue, streaked with the soft blush of dawn. There's no hum of electricity, no distant drone of traffic—only the chirp of unseen birds and the rustle of leaves in a gentle breeze.  

Your clothes feel strange now—too modern, too loud with synthetic fibers. You're dressed in a simple linen tunic and leather sandals, the kind you’ve only seen in history books. Your hands tremble as you touch your face. The skin feels different—cooler, more alive.  

Then you remember: you were dreaming. But this… this feels too real to be a dream.  

You rise, wincing at the stiffness in your joints. A river cuts through the valley ahead, clear and swift, its surface shimmering like liquid silver. Along its bank, someone has built a rough stone foundation—part of a hut? A fire pit, cold and ash-covered, sits nearby.  

You realize you need to:  

**Learn the language.**  

A figure emerges from the treeline—tall, cloaked in woven wool, eyes sharp and curious. They carry a wooden staff and move with a quiet grace. As they approach, they speak in a low, melodic tone—you understand it *almost* perfectly, as if your mind is translating, but not fully. They say something that sounds like *"A’thir, a’nath?"*  

You open your mouth, but only a choked whisper comes out.  

You need to learn. To listen. To survive.  

And somewhere, deep in your bones, a name echoes—not yours, but one that fits this time, this place: *Elowen*.  

The stranger smiles, offering a small clay cup filled with water—cool, clear, untainted by plastic or chemicals.  

You take it.  

Your dream has become your life. And now, you must live it.
