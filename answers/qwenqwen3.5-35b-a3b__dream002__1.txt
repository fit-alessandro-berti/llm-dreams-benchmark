The forest around you melts into the periphery, the ground softening into moss that hums against your bare soles. You look up, and the lightning strikes not as a crack, but as a bloom of gold light that pulses through the tree trunks like veins of sap. There is no need to run, no need to hide. The storm is not a threat; it is an invitation.

As the first bead of glass rain touches your cheek, your eyes close, and the world dissolves into warmth. You open them again, sitting at a desk, sunlight streaming in, the scent of rain still lingering on your lips, wondering why you are holding a key that is no longer needed.
