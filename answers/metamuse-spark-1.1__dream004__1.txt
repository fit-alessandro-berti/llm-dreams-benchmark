You come across a blank canvas and it stops.

Everything else keeps moving.

The sky is melting from orange to violet to a color you don't have a name for. The hills are breathing - inflating into cubes, then spheres, then flat again. The ground ripples under your feet like liquid glass.

But the canvas just... hangs there. Perfectly still. Perfectly blank. Floating a few inches off the ground, held up by nothing. Its wooden frame is the only thing in this whole world that isn't trying to become something else.

You walk closer. The air gets quieter.

Next to it, there's no easel. There are no paints. Just a small table made of folded light, and on it, a single brush.

When you pick up the brush, you realize it has no bristles. Just a soft tip of empty air. You almost laugh, because of course.

You lift it to the canvas anyway.

The moment the tip touches the white, something strange happens behind you.

*Drip.*

You turn. A streak of blue has fallen off the sky. The shifting horizon is leaking.

You touch the canvas again, higher this time. *Drip.* A piece of the wobbling yellow hill detaches and floats toward you, then dissolves into the canvas, leaving a warm, buttery smear where you touched.

You understand now. It's not blank because there's nothing to paint with. It's blank because it's waiting for you to take from the world.

You start to paint. Not with strokes, but with stealing.

A swirl of that nameless color from the sky - you pull it down like a ribbon and it becomes a spiral on the canvas. A sharp, triangular sound that was floating by - you catch it and it becomes a hard black line. You paint the feeling of the rippling ground under your bare feet, and the canvas shivers.

With every mark you make, the chaotic world around you gets a little calmer. A little more still. The screaming rainbow slows to a soft pastel wash. The geometry of the hills settles.

You're not just painting *on* the canvas. You're painting *the world into* the canvas.

Finally, you step back. On the canvas is not a landscape. It's a window. Inside that little painted window is a bedroom. A bed. A person sleeping peacefully. You.

And you realize the canvas has been dreaming you, all this time.

You reach out to touch the little sleeping figure —

And you wake up.
