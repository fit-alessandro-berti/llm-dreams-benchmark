
You decide to press onward, trudging through the skeletal remains of a city that once buzzed with life. The air tastes metallic, tinged with ash and something sweeter—like decayed foliage. Your boots crunch over glass and rusted metal, each step a gamble. You’re not sure why you’re still moving, only that the alternative—staying put—is worse.  

Ahead, a structure looms: a pre-collapse research facility, its sign still legible despite the cracks spiderwebbing across it. *Hope*. Or madness. You don’t know anymore. Inside, you find evidence of others—empty cans, hastily scrawled notes, a child’s toy fused into a wall by heat. But also, deeper in, something that makes your blood run cold: a working radio, crackling with static, broadcasting a looped message in a language you don’t recognize.  

You spend hours trying to decode it, fueled by caffeine pills and stubbornness. The signal grows stronger at night. You start to wonder if it’s a trap, a lure, or a lifeline. Then the ground shakes. Something massive is moving beneath the facility—something that used to be alive.  

You wake up just before it finds you.
