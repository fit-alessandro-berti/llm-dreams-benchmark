As you reach out, your fingers don’t touch bristles; they pass through a cool, misty vapor. The moment your hand connects with the floating tool, the chaotic swirling of the background snaps into stillness. The shifting hues freeze, hanging in the air like suspended dust motes.

You realize the blank canvas is not empty—it is waiting for *intent*. You think of a memory: the smell of rain on hot asphalt, the sound of a distant train, the feeling of safety. As these thoughts form, the brush moves on its own, guided by your mind. It sweeps across the surface, and where it passes, reality rewrites itself.

A street appears, wet and glistening under a twilight sky. But as you look closer, the reflections in the puddles aren’t of buildings or clouds—they are scenes from other dreams you’ve forgotten. A child laughs in one reflection; a door opens in another.

The landscape around you begins to breathe again, but now it pulses in rhythm with the painting. The colors no longer change randomly; they shift based on your heartbeat. When you feel calm, the world turns soft blues and gentle greens. When curiosity spikes, sharp yellows and electric purples crackle at the edges of your vision.

You step onto the painted street, and the canvas becomes solid ground beneath your feet. You are no longer an observer of this surreal world—you are its architect. And ahead, down the wet avenue, a figure stands waiting, holding a second brush, smiling as if they’ve been expecting you all along.
