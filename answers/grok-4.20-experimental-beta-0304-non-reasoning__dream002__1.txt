You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the tall pines begin to whisper like they're sharing secrets they shouldn't. The air grows thick, heavy with ozone and the scent of wet earth.

Dark clouds swirl overhead in impossible patterns, forming shapes that almost look like faces watching you. A low rumble rolls through the ground, vibrating up through your feet. Then, without warning, the rain doesn't just fall—it *remembers*. Every drop carries fragments of old dreams, flashing brief images as they strike your skin: a childhood home you haven't thought of in years, a lover whose name you can't quite recall, a door you were always too afraid to open.

Lightning forks across the sky in brilliant violet, and for a split second you see that the trees aren't trees anymore. They're ancient beings frozen mid-motion, their branches reaching toward you like fingers that have been waiting centuries for someone to finally walk this path.

You start running, but the forest is changing around you. The trail you were following has vanished. In its place, glowing mushrooms sprout in intricate patterns that look almost like... a map? A warning? An invitation?

The storm's voice becomes clearer now—not thunder, but something speaking directly into your mind with your own voice, only older, wiser, and slightly amused:

*"You've been sleepwalking through your life for too long. Time to wake up... or dream deeper. Choose."*

A massive oak tree ahead of you splits open like theater curtains, revealing not the other side of the forest, but an impossible staircase made of starlight spiraling down into the earth. The storm is right behind you now, playful and ancient and hungry for your next decision.

What do you do?
