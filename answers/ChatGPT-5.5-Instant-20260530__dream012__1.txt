You are the captain of a ship in the middle of a turbulent sea. Your crew looks to you for guidance as the storm worsens, and you grip the wheel with both hands. Waves tower above the deck like moving mountains, their crests glowing faintly green beneath flashes of lightning.

The compass spins wildly.

"Captain!" someone shouts. "The stars are gone!"

You look up. Where there should be clouds, there is a swirling sky filled with strange doors floating among the thunderheads. Each door shines with a different color. One is made of gold, another of ice, another of dark wood covered in vines.

A wave lifts the ship impossibly high. Instead of crashing back down, the vessel begins to sail through the air.

No one seems surprised.

The sea drifts beneath you like a silver blanket as the ship glides toward the floating doors. The crew gathers around, waiting for your command. You somehow know that each door leads to a different future.

You choose the wooden door.

The ship passes through it.

Suddenly, the storm vanishes. You find yourself sailing across a forest. Trees stretch beneath the hull as though they were water, their leaves rippling in invisible currents. Strange birds fly alongside the masts, speaking in human voices.

"Almost there," one of them says.

"Where?" you ask.

The bird only laughs.

Ahead, a lighthouse rises from the center of the forest. Its beam sweeps across the treetops, illuminating memories instead of land. As the light touches the crew, each sailor briefly becomes someone from your life—a childhood friend, a teacher, a stranger you passed on a train years ago.

The lighthouse beam finally reaches you.

For a moment, you see yourself standing on a shore, much older, watching the ship approach. The older version of you smiles and raises a lantern.

You steer toward him.

The closer you get, the calmer everything becomes. The wind softens. The trees grow silent. Even the ship seems relieved.

When you finally reach the shore, the older you hands over the lantern.

"You've been steering through storms for a long time," he says. "You can rest now."

You take the lantern and look inside. Instead of a flame, it contains a tiny ocean. On that ocean sails another ship, and on that ship stands another captain staring into another storm.

You try to ask what it means.

But the shore dissolves into mist.

The lighthouse stretches into the sky. The crew fades into shadows. The tiny ocean in the lantern grows larger and larger until it surrounds you completely.

Then a bell rings somewhere in the distance.

And you wake up, still hearing the sound of waves against the hull of a ship that may never have existed.
