...each group seems to belong to a different version of reality.

One group speaks entirely in questions.

“Have you seen your friend?” you ask.

“Which friend?” they reply. “The one you remember, or the one who remembers you?”

Before you can answer, the crowd shifts like a flock of birds and the room stretches wider.

You move on.

At the next table, everyone wears masks made of mirrors. When they turn toward you, each mask reflects a different version of yourself: a child holding a paper airplane, an older version with silver hair, a stranger with your eyes. They all smile knowingly, but none of them speak.

You continue searching.

Music drifts through the hall, though there seems to be no band. The melody changes with every step. In one corner, people dance in slow motion while confetti rises from the floor instead of falling. In another, guests float a few inches above the ground, chatting casually as if gravity has become optional.

You finally spot the person you know across the room.

Relieved, you begin weaving through the crowd toward them.

But the farther you walk, the farther away they seem to become.

The hall unfolds endlessly, revealing new rooms filled with libraries, gardens, oceans suspended in glass ceilings, and staircases leading into clouds. Each time you catch sight of your friend, they are just beyond the next doorway.

At last, you reach a quiet balcony overlooking a sky filled with enormous glowing lanterns.

Your friend is standing there.

“You've been looking for me all night,” they say.

“I have,” you reply. “Why was it so hard to find you?”

They laugh softly.

“You weren't looking for me.”

The lanterns brighten. One by one, they reveal scenes from your life—forgotten conversations, missed opportunities, moments of joy so small you barely noticed them.

“What was I looking for, then?” you ask.

Your friend points to the nearest lantern.

Inside it, you see yourself wandering through the gathering, asking strangers for directions.

“Your place in the story,” they say.

The lantern opens like a flower, releasing thousands of tiny lights into the sky. The crowd below begins to dissolve into starlight. The music becomes the sound of distant waves.

You feel the balcony fading beneath your feet.

Just before everything disappears, your friend hands you a small golden key.

You look down at it.

When you look up again, the balcony, the lanterns, and your friend are gone.

You wake up with the strange certainty that there is a door somewhere you have not opened yet—and that the key is still in your pocket.
