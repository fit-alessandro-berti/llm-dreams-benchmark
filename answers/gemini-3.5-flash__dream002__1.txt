The sky doesn't just turn gray; it bruises into a deep, velvety violet. 

I try to quicken my pace, but my legs feel heavy, as if I’m wading through deep water. The shadows of the towering pine trees begin to detach from their trunks, stretching and swaying across the mossy ground like dark, living ribbons. 

Through the deepening gloom, I spot a sanctuary: a massive, ancient oak tree, its roots arching to form a natural doorway that glows with a warm, amber light. As I struggle toward it, the first raindrop falls. When it splashes onto my hand, it isn't cold water, but a heavy droplet of liquid gold that hums with a faint, musical vibration. 

I plunge through the root-archway just as the sky opens up in a silent explosion of violet lightning. 

But instead of the cramped inside of a tree, I step out into a vast, circular library with a spiral staircase winding upward into a ceiling made of swirling stars. The storm rages fiercely outside, pressing against the invisible glass walls of the trunk, but inside, there is only the quiet crackle of an unseen hearth and the scent of old paper. 

I take a deep breath, safe, as the sound of the rain outside dissolves into a soft, hypnotic lullaby, carrying me deeper into the sleep within my sleep.
