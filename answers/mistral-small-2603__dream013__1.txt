...the puzzles grow stranger, shifting like living things. The walls whisper in a language of equations, their surfaces covered in fractal patterns that rearrange themselves when you blink. One corridor demands you solve a riddle in reverse—its answer dissolves into the next puzzle before you can speak it. Another room is a chessboard where the pieces move on their own, their strategies forming a labyrinth of their own making.

At the center, you find a door with no handle, only a mirror. When you press your palm to it, the reflection doesn’t mimic you—it *answers*. A voice, neither yours nor someone else’s, murmurs: *"The maze isn’t the puzzle. The maze is the dreamer."*

Then the walls begin to breathe.
