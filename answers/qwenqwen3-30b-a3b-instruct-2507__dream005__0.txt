…as you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people, each more vivid and surreal than the last. You pass a cluster of dancers wearing silver masks that reflect not your face, but your forgotten memories: your fifth birthday party, the exact shade of your childhood dog’s fur, the smell of rain on hot pavement. You hesitate, half-enchanted, then press on.

Next, you stroll into a library where the books float mid-air, their covers shifting like living skin. A tall figure in a coat made of old letters whispers, *"Look for the one with the missing star."* You blink — the figure is gone. On the nearest floating volume, you see your own name, but the author’s byline glows faintly: *You, but not you.*

You wander into a garden where the trees are woven from hourglasses, their sand falling upward. Around them, guests speak in reverse sentences: *"Not a moment too soon was the cake eaten,"* one says as they bite into an invisible slice. You try to reply, but your words fall into time, echoing backward from the future.

Then, in a clearing lit by paper lanterns shaped like eyes, you see them — the one person you know. They stand under a tree whose branches are made of loom threads, weaving a tapestry of all the conversations you’ve ever had. They turn, smile — but their eyes are the exact shade of the sky just before stars appear.

“Found you,” they say. But their voice is the sound of turning a page long unread.

You open your mouth to speak, to explain how lost you felt, how the dream stretched and blurred — but before you can, the gathering begins to dissolve. The dancers vanish into mirrors, the floating books fall silent, the garden turns to dust on the wind.

And in the final moment, as the world folds back into itself, you hear one last whisper, not from them, but from *yourself*, echoing from somewhere deep inside:  
*"You were never lost. You were just waiting to remember where you belong."*
