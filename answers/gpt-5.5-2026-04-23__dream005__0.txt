By the windows, a circle of people in velvet coats are discussing a childhood memory you haven’t thought about in years. One of them laughs and says, “You always forget the important part,” but when you ask what they mean, they turn toward the glass as if watching birds outside. There are no birds—only a dark ocean pressing against the building, waves folding silently against the windows.

You move on.

In the kitchen, people are preparing food that never gets served. A woman slices a pear into impossibly thin pieces, each slice showing a different room inside it. A man stirs a pot full of tiny ringing phones. None of them look up, but one phone floats to the surface and begins to buzz with your name on the screen.

You don’t answer.

You remember the person you came to find. Their face is suddenly clear, then unclear, then replaced by the feeling of standing beside them on a warm afternoon. You follow that feeling down a hallway that wasn’t there before.

The hallway is lined with doors, all slightly open. Behind one is a room full of people applauding, though nothing is happening. Behind another, everyone is asleep standing up. Behind a third, a child is building a tower out of keys.

At the end of the hall, you hear your friend’s voice.

You hurry toward it, but the floor softens under your feet like wet sand. The walls breathe in and out. The music from the gathering grows muffled, as though you are underwater.

Finally, you enter a quiet room lit by a single lamp.

Your friend is there, sitting at a table, smiling as if they have been waiting a long time.

“I’ve been looking for you,” you say.

“I know,” they reply. “But you’re not here to find me.”

On the table between you is a small envelope with your handwriting on it. You open it. Inside is a photograph of the gathering taken from above: hundreds of people arranged in the shape of your own face.

When you look up, the room is empty.

The lamp flickers.

From somewhere far away, someone calls your name—not the name people use now, but one you had forgotten belonged to you.

You turn toward the sound, and the walls of the room peel open like curtains, revealing a morning sky.

Then you wake with the strange certainty that someone at the gathering is still trying to find you.
