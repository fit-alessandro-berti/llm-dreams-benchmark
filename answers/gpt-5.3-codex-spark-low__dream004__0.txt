• In a world where colors and shapes are constantly changing, you step closer to the blank canvas, and it flickers as if
  noticing you. For one heartbeat it is stark white; the next it deepens into midnight blue, then the gray of old stone,
  then the gold of a setting sun.

  You reach out and touch the surface. The canvas ripples, and a thin line of light appears beneath your fingertip—first
  a scarlet arc, then a green lattice, then a hundred tiny red dots that arrange themselves into a map you almost
  recognize. It shifts faster than language can hold: rivers become birds, mountains turn to stairways, stairways
  collapse into eyes, eyes into windows.

  A voice arrives from somewhere both inside and outside your skull: “Paint what you fear, and it will keep shape.”
  Before you can argue, the brush appears in your hand—cold, feather-light, made of woven wind and glass.

  You hesitate, then drag it across the canvas. The mark you leave turns into a long, dark corridor running toward the
  horizon. Halfway down it appears a door, black as ink and warm as breath. You look back at the blank edges around you.
  The whole landscape has shifted again. What was a canyon is now a library. What was a field is now a room full of
  mirrored clocks. Every color you’ve ever known seems to be trying to become something else.

  You enter the corridor.

  At the door, a figure waits—its outline never settles long enough to define age, gender, or face. It offers you a
  small key that is also a raindrop.

  “Not to open it,” it says, smiling with a mouth full of stars. “To remember.”

  Behind the door, the canvas stretches wider than the sky. You see every path you ever almost took, every version of
  your life rendered in impossible dimensions. None of them are fixed; each time you look too hard, the scenes blur and
  recombine. You understand, suddenly, that this is not a place to solve anything. It is a place to choose, again and
  again, without needing a final answer.

  You lift the key, and where it hovers above the surface, the corridor closes, then opens elsewhere.
  In the distance, a white frame waits.

  You wake with paint on your hands.
