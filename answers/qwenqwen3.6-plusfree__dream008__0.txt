Your boss stands by the door, perfectly still, but their outline keeps softening at the edges, turning into watercolor. You open your mouth to ask for an extension, but instead of words, small paper boats sail from your lips, drifting toward the filing cabinets. You press a key anyway. The screen ripples like a pond, and suddenly the carpet is damp grass. The desk is a mossy stone. The ceiling is a sky of slow-swimming paperclips tethered by invisible thread.

You try to remember what the project was even for, but the question dissolves into the quiet hum of a refrigerator that definitely doesn't belong in this room. You sit on the stone. The boss's chair slowly folds itself into an origami crane and takes flight. A sticky note lands on your lap. It says, in your own handwriting: *You built the urgency. You can unspool it too.*

You close your eyes. The air grows warm. Somewhere, a calendar flips backward, month by month, until it settles on a blank page. The impossible deadline never needed finishing; it only needed noticing. And just as the dream begins to taste like rain and old library books, a distant chime pulls you gently toward the surface, leaving behind only the quiet certainty that you were never really late at all.
