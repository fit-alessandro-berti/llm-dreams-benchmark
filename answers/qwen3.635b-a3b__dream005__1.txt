One turns to you and says, “They’re where the silence gathers,” before dissolving into a flock of origami birds that scatter toward a chandelier made of glass tears.

You follow the birds through a corridor of mirrors, each reflecting a different version of yourself: some laughing, some weary, some simply waiting. The reflection nearest to you taps twice on the glass and steps out, handing you a silver key that smells like rain and old books. “Keep your eyes open,” it whispers, “dreams don’t answer to footsteps. They answer to breath.”

Beyond the mirrors lies a courtyard where the sky is the color of forgotten afternoons. People float halfway between dance and drift, their footsteps leaving ripples that bloom into fleeting flowers before fading. At the center, beneath a tree with leaves like ticking clocks, stands the one person you’ve been searching for. They don’t turn at first, but you hear their voice before you see their face: “I’ve been waiting where the quiet begins.”

You step forward, and the courtyard folds inward like a page turning. The voices soften. The key dissolves into your palm, becoming a warm pulse. You realize you’ve never really been lost—only wandering through the spaces between who you were and who you’re becoming. The dream doesn’t end with a jolt. It ends with a choice: to stay in the quiet, or to carry it forward.

You close your eyes. When you open them, the room is just a room. But your hands still remember the temperature of the sky, and somewhere, a bird made of paper rests on your windowsill, waiting for the next breeze.
