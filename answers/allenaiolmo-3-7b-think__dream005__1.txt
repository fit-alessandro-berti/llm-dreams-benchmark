

**Continuation of the Dream:**

You stand in the heart of a sprawling, neon-lit festival, the air buzzing with the hum of chatter and the thump of bass from a nearby DJ set. The crowd is a kaleidoscope of costumes and faces, but your eyes keep drifting to the silhouette of a familiar hat—a baseball cap you last saw on your coworker, *Liam*. You grip the edge of a table, your pulse racing. *How do I even start?*

Your first stop is a pulsating dance floor, where strangers move in sync to a synth-heavy beat. A group of teenagers break into an impromptu twerking contest, their laughter echoing. You shrink back, but then you spot Liam dancing with a woman in a sequined dress. Her smile is directed at him, and in that moment, you freeze. *Is that him?* Before you can muster the courage to approach, Liam glances your way, his eyes narrowing in recognition. He gives a subtle nod toward the crowd, then turns to his partner. You nod back, too nervous to speak. *Later*, you tell yourself. *I’ll find another way*.

You drift through the crowd, past a group of people huddled around a giant puzzle box. One woman, a mathematician by trade, shouts over the noise, “It’s a Fibonacci spiral! The pattern’s *right here*!” She points to a cluster of colored tiles. Intrigued, you linger, but the puzzle seems unrelated to Liam. Frustrated, you move on, only to pause at a food stall piled high with skewers and tamales. The scent of lime and chili wafts through the air. A vendor hands you a sample, and suddenly you’re thinking of Liam’s laugh when he tried (and failed) to make guacamole last month. *He’d love this*, you realize. You buy a tamale, the masa clinging to your fingers, and follow the scent like a compass.

The next room is dimmer, lit by string lights and the soft glow of a bookshelf. A small group discusses *The Three-Body Problem* in hushed tones. One woman gasps, “Did you know the protagonist’s name is *Wenwen*? That’s Chinese for ‘first snow’!” You fumble with the words, but the conversation pivots to quantum physics, leaving you clueless. Just as you retreat, a man in a tweed jacket walks past, flipping through a copy of the book on his lap. His gaze lands on you for a split second—*that’s Liam’s book*—before he vanishes into a crowd of abstract painters nearby.

Heart pounding, you sprint toward the art booth, where splashes of color bloom on canvases. There, in the corner, Liam stands frozen in front of a half-finished portrait. The artist—a woman with silver braids—turns and smiles. “He’s been here all night,” she says. “Trying to capture the ‘chaos of connection.’” Liam waves you over, his hat slightly askew. “You remembered the guac,” he teases, then softens. “I’ve been looking for you too. This place… it’s like everyone’s a stranger until you find the one thread.”  

As you laugh, the music shifts, and the crowd parts like a curtain. The dream dissolves into a sunlit meadow, where Liam and you sit on a bench, sharing the tamale you’d bought earlier. The sky blazes orange, and for once, the noise of the festival fades into silence. You realize you’ve been dreaming of this moment all along—the unspoken bond, the chance encounters, the way the world makes sense when you’re *with* someone.  

When you wake, your hand is still cradling the dream’s faint echo: the warmth of his voice, the taste of lime on your tongue.
