
Your heart pounds as you navigate the twisting stone corridors, each step echoing off walls that seem to shift subtly when you're not looking directly at them. The air grows colder, carrying whispers of ancient knowledge that coil around your mind like serpents.

At the maze's center, you discover a crystalline sphere hovering above a pedestal, its surface showing not reflections but possible futures. As you reach toward it, the whispers coalesce into a single voice—your own, speaking from a year you haven't lived yet: *"The greatest puzzle isn't solving the maze, but choosing which path leads back to yourself."*

The sphere shatters into golden light, and suddenly you remember: you've been sleepwalking this maze every night since childhood, drawn by dreams of a door that only appears when you stop trying to find it. The exit was behind you all along, hidden in the simple act of letting go.

You wake up smiling, the taste of starlight on your lips and the faint scent of jasmine in your bedroom—that's when you notice the garden outside your window has bloomed overnight, though no seeds were planted.
