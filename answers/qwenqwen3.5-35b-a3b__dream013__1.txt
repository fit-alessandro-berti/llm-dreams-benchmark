You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, you find that the logic itself begins to fray.

The stone walls are no longer just stone; they are translucent, shifting with the pulse of a slow, sleeping heartbeat. The puzzles stop looking like math equations or code, and start looking like memories you don't quite remember making. One corridor demands you solve a riddle of gravity to cross a gap of empty air. Another requires you to arrange shards of obsidian so that they reflect not your face, but your shadow's.

With every solution, the air grows colder and the silence grows heavier. You aren't just solving the maze anymore; you are becoming part of its mechanism. Your footsteps make no sound, only a ripple in the light beneath your toes.

Eventually, you reach the center. There is no treasure, no final door, and no monster. There is only a single, floating mirror suspended in the void.

You approach it, expecting to see your exhausted self, the one staring down the dark passage. But the reflection is you, standing right behind you, holding a key made of light. The reflection smiles, and in that split second, the wall of logic shatters like glass. You don't wake up. Instead, the mirror dissolves, and you find you are falling upward.

The ground dissolves into mist. You are no longer in the maze. You are floating above it, watching the corridors collapse into one another, realizing that the puzzles weren't traps designed to stop you, but tests designed to teach you that the only way out is to stop trying to understand them.

You open your eyes, not as the dreamer, but as the sleeper, waking with a start in a room that feels impossibly quiet, the scent of old books and ozone still clinging to your skin. You glance at your hand, and for a second, you can still feel the cold weight of a key that isn't there.
