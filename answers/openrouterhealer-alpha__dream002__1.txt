The air turns electric, and the hair on your arms lifts as if reaching for something invisible above. The trees, once so still, begin to sway — not with wind, but with a rhythm, almost like breathing.

You notice the bark is changing. What was rough and brown is now smooth, silver-blue, and faintly luminous, as if the forest has remembered it's not really a forest at all.

Rain begins to fall, but upward.

Each droplet rises from the ground, pooling at your feet for only a moment before drifting skyward, carrying fragments of color — amber, violet, pale gold — like lanterns released from the earth. You open your mouth to speak, and the words you'd say become small glowing shapes that float away from your lips before you can hear them.

Ahead, a path you didn't notice before splits into two. One descends gently into a meadow where the storm clouds have curled into a perfect spiral, slowly turning like a sleeping galaxy. The other rises sharply into the canopy, and you can see, impossibly, that the treetops are holding up the sky — that the sky is fabric, and it's beginning to pull loose at the edges, revealing something vast and amber beyond it.

You feel no fear. Only a deep, quiet knowing that whichever path you take, you'll finally understand something you've been carrying in your chest your whole life.

Your feet hover just above the moss, deciding.
