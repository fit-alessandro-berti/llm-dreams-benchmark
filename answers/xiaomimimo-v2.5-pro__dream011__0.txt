I notice the questions are... written in a language I've never seen before.

My heart starts pounding. I blink hard, hoping the symbols will rearrange themselves into something recognizable. They don't. Instead, they seem to *shift* when I'm not looking directly at them.

Then — I notice something strange. The student next to me is calmly writing answers, and as I glance over, the symbols near *her* pencil slowly become legible. Not because she's translating them, but because the act of writing seems to *anchor* the words into reality.

I pick up my own pencil. Hesitantly, I write my name at the top of the page.

The moment ink touches paper, the first question *clarifies* — like fog lifting. It reads:

**"What did you learn that wasn't in the textbook?"**

I stare at it. There's no multiple choice. No formula to apply. Just a blank space waiting for an answer.

I start writing about the late nights in the library where I helped a classmate understand a concept and ended up understanding it better myself. About the time I got a problem completely wrong but learned *why* in a way that no right answer ever taught me. About the professor's offhand comment that changed how I saw the entire field.

With each sentence I write, the next question appears — always the same question, just deeper:

*"And what did you learn from that?"*

The clock is ticking, but I realize... I'm not running out of answers. I'm running out of *exam.*

And just before I wake up, I understand the dream's quiet joke: the exam was never about whether I knew enough.

It was about whether I knew that I knew.
