...as I move through the room, each group seems like a little island of sound and light.  

I pass a cluster of people laughing so hard they’re holding each other up — their joy feels magnetic but impenetrable. Nearby, a quieter circle is deep in conversation, their faces serious, nodding along to someone’s earnest story. I catch a phrase here and there — something about travel, something about a project — but it’s like hearing a radio through a wall.  

Further on, I notice two people standing apart, smiling politely while their eyes scan the room — maybe they’re also looking for someone, or maybe they just arrived. We exchange a brief, knowing glance.  

The music shifts, and for a moment I think I see my friend across the room — but it’s someone else, turning away to talk to someone new. I start to feel that strange mix of curiosity and solitude that only happens in dreams — where you’re both observer and participant, and every face feels familiar even if you’ve never seen it before.  

Then, just as I’m about to give up and slip outside for air, I feel a tap on my shoulder. I turn, and there they are — holding two drinks, grinning.  
“There you are,” they say. “I’ve been looking for you, too.”  

And the crowd doesn’t seem so vast anymore.
