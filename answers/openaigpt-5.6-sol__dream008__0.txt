Your boss taps their watch. “Well?”

You type the first word that comes to mind: **BEGIN**.

At once, every printer in the office starts churning. Pages spill across the floor, covered in sentences describing exactly what you are doing. You step backward. The pages say you step backward. You stop breathing. The pages say that too.

Then one final sheet slides from the printer:

**THE DEADLINE IS WHEN YOU WAKE UP.**

The office clock begins spinning faster. Outside the windows, morning races toward you—the sky paling, birds lifting from rooftops, sunlight climbing the glass towers. Everyone in the office turns to stare. Their faces are yours.

You sit down and type as quickly as you can. Not the project, but an ending:

**The boss smiles, the work is accepted, and the door opens.**

Your boss reads the words. Slowly, they smile.

Behind you, an elevator dings. Its doors open onto your bedroom, where you can see yourself asleep beneath tangled sheets. You run inside just as the office dissolves into flying paper.

When you wake, there is a blank page on your pillow.

At the top, in red ink, someone has written:

**REVISION DUE TOMORROW.**
