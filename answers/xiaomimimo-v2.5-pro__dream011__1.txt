*You blink and rub your eyes, certain you must be seeing things.*

The questions are... in a language you've never seen before. Strange, flowing symbols dance across the pages, rearranging themselves like living things. Your heart pounds.

But then something shifts.

You realize — with the peculiar logic of dreams — that you don't need to *read* the questions. You can **feel** them. Each one hums with a kind of meaning that bypasses your eyes entirely and lands somewhere deeper.

You pick up your pen, and your hand begins to move on its own. Answers flow out — not words, exactly, but colors, shapes, and fragments of memories:

🌸 Your grandmother teaching you to fold dumplings at age five — that answers question one.

🌊 The time you got lost at the beach and found your way home by following the stars — question two.

🔥 The summer you failed spectacularly at something and learned it wasn't the end of the world — question three.

You keep writing, faster now. The symbols on the page shift and swirl around your answers, almost... *welcoming* them.

Then, just as you reach the final question, the entire exam paper goes blank. In the center of the page, a single line appears in plain text:

**"What matters most to you?"**

You sit back in your chair. The room is silent. The clock on the wall has stopped.

And you smile — because for the first time in your life, you know exactly what to write.

*You pick up your pen.*
