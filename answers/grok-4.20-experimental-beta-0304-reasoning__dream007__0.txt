...and you decide to **yell the truth so loudly that the words turn into birds**.

The argument freezes mid-sentence. Your friend’s face—twisted in that particular mix of betrayal and righteousness—starts to pixelate like an old VHS tape. The birds (your words, now flapping and iridescent) swirl around both of you, carrying scraps of the fight: “You promised—”, “I was scared—”, “After everything—”.

One of the birds lands on your friend’s shoulder and whispers the part of the story you’d both been avoiding. Their expression softens, then cracks open like an egg. Suddenly you’re both falling upward through the ceiling of the room, which was never a room at all but the inside of a giant ribcage. The heart hanging above you is beating in the rhythm of your favorite song from high school.

Your friend reaches out. Their hand is made of starlight and old apologies. You take it.

The two of you float there, suspended in the dream’s ridiculous anatomy, and for the first time in the entire fight neither of you is trying to win. The birds settle into a single glowing sentence that hangs between you:

**“We were both right, and we were both assholes. That’s allowed.”**

The ribcage around you begins to laugh. The laughter shakes loose every unsaid thing still stuck in the bones. You laugh too. Your friend laughs. The sound is so loud it rips the dream open at the seams.

You wake up with wet cheeks and the distinct feeling that the argument isn’t over… but it’s no longer a war.

It’s just a conversation you still need to have.

(And somewhere, in the place dreams go when they’re done, two birds are still singing the rest of the sentence you never got to say.)
