You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching—and then, instead of thunder, you hear the sound of distant harps, weaving through the wind like whispered lullabies. The clouds above don’t darken with rain, but shimmer in hues of amethyst and gold, swirling into the shape of enormous, glowing birds with feathers made of falling stars.

The trees around you begin to lean inward, their bark softening into skin, their branches stretching like sleepy arms. They whisper your name—not in words, but in memories: your mother’s laughter on a summer porch, the smell of burnt toast from your childhood kitchen, the silence of a hospital room you didn’t know you’d forgotten.

A path appears beneath your feet, paved not with moss or stone, but with tiny, lit lanterns—each one containing a single moment from your life, flickering gently. You reach down and pick up one. It’s the day you said “I’m sorry” to someone you loved… and they hugged you anyway.

Rain begins to fall—but when it touches your skin, it doesn’t chill. It warms you, like a lover’s breath on your neck. Each drop carries a voice: “You were never alone,” says the one that lands on your cheek. “You are still becoming,” murmurs another on your shoulder.

Ahead, through the glowing mist, stands a door—no frame, no hinges—just an opening shaped like your own silhouette. Behind it, the forest dissolves into a room you’ve never seen but know by heart. A chair by a window. A book open on a table. A cup of tea still steaming.

You hesitate.

A voice—soft, familiar, your own—whispers from the door:

*“You don’t have to wake up yet.”*

And the wind hums in reply—

*“Not all dreams end. Some just wait for you to remember you’re still inside them.”*

You step forward.

And the forest breathes with you.

—

You are not lost.

You are home.
