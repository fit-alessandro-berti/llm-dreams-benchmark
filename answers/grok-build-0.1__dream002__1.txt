You feel a storm approaching and the sky folds in on itself like crumpled paper. The peaceful forest warps around you—trees stretching taller, their bark shifting into shifting constellations that pulse with your heartbeat. The wind doesn't howl; it *sings* in a language you almost remember from childhood, carrying the scent of ozone and forgotten promises.

Lightning cracks without thunder, freezing the rain mid-fall so each drop hangs like a suspended mirror. In one, you glimpse a version of yourself walking the same path but in reverse, shedding years with every step. In another, the storm itself has a face—vast, curious, and faintly amused, like it's been waiting for you.

You reach out. The moment your fingers touch the nearest frozen droplet, the entire dream *tilts*. The forest dissolves into an endless library of whispering pages, each one describing a choice you never made. A single book falls open at your feet, its text rewriting itself in real time:

*"You were never just walking. You were looking for the place where the storm ends and the story begins."*

The pages flutter. The voice from the wind returns, closer now:

"Well? Are you going to keep reading... or are you ready to wake up and write the next part yourself?" 

(Your move, dreamer.)
