...and you grip the wheel so tightly that your knuckles turn the color of the foam crashing over the bow. The salt spray stings your eyes, but you don't blink; you cannot afford to lose sight of the horizon, though the horizon has long since vanished into a bruised purple haze.

"Hold the line!" you roar, though your voice sounds distant, as if you are shouting from the bottom of a well. 

The crew is a blur of rain-soaked wool and desperate eyes. You realize with a sudden, jarring clarity that you don't know their names, yet you know exactly how their hearts are beating—fast, erratic, drumming in sync with the thunder.

Suddenly, the ocean stops behaving like water. A wave rises before you, not as a wall of brine, but as a towering cathedral of translucent glass. Inside the wave, you see cities of gold and silver floating in the current, fish with wings of iridescent lace swimming through the spires. The storm isn't trying to sink you; it is trying to show you something.

The crew stops shouting. The wind drops to a rhythmic hum, a low vibration that resonates in your very bones. You feel the ship slow, not because of a reef, but because the water has turned to thick, shimmering mercury.

You let go of the wheel. As you do, the ship begins to lift. You aren't sailing on the sea anymore; you are sailing upward, the vessel tilting toward a moon that has grown so large it fills half the sky, its surface etched with the maps of places that don't exist.

One of your sailors reaches out to touch a drop of rain hovering motionless in the air, and as he does, the drop shatters into a thousand tiny diamonds that begin to sing. The melody is familiar—a song from your childhood, a lullaby your mother sang.

As the ship crests the final wave and plunges into the silver light of the moon, the sensation of weight vanishes. You feel yourself dissolving, your uniform turning into sea-foam, your breath becoming the wind. Just as you reach out to touch the lunar surface, the sound of a distant bell rings—not a ship's bell, but an alarm clock—and the silver world begins to fray at the edges, pulling you back toward the waking world.
