You start to check your watch, but it is silent; time is stretching between you and the building where the meeting is set. The air smells of wet concrete and something sweet like ozone. You find yourself in an alleyway made of floating tiles instead of pavement. A reflection in a puddle shows your own face, young and tired, looking at a phone that isn't there yet.

You realize the city breathes; it exhales sound through its windows. The clock hands aren't spinning; they are pulsing like a slow heartbeat.

"Meet you," you say softly to the empty space between buildings. A voice from your pocket answers it, but it sounds like rain tapping against glass. "We're already here."

You take a step forward into the shifting light.
