...feel a cold drop of sweat roll down your neck. The office lights flicker, humming with a low, electric buzz that seems to vibrate in your teeth. Your boss isn’t just standing there; they are hovering slightly off the ground, their eyes glowing with a soft, unnatural blue light.

"Tick-tock," they say, but their mouth doesn't move. The sound seems to come from the walls themselves.

You look down at the project files on your desk. As you open the folder, the papers inside begin to multiply instantly, spilling onto the floor like a waterfall of infinite documentation. The text on the pages is shifting—letters rearranging themselves into nonsense, then into ancient hieroglyphs, then into your browser history from 2012.

You try to type on your keyboard, but the keys are made of soft, squishy dough that sticks to your fingers. Every time you press one, it sinks in, and the screen displays a single word: **LATER.**

Panic sets in. You need to work faster. You stand up to run to the printer, but your legs feel like lead. You look down and realize your feet have taken root; vines are wrapping around your ankles, anchoring you to the carpet. The carpet itself has turned into a lush, green jungle floor, and a rhythmic drumming sound starts coming from the breakroom.

Your boss floats closer, leaning in until their face is inches from yours. They hand you a single stapler.

"The deadline is yesterday," they whisper, and suddenly, gravity reverses. You are pulled upward toward the ceiling, papers swirling around you in a chaotic tornado, the impossible weight of the uncompleted work pressing down on your chest as you float helplessly into the fluorescent light...
