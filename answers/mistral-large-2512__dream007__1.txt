**The Argument Shifts**
The air between you and your friend thickens, charged like the moment before a storm. Their words—sharp, accusing—hang in the air, but suddenly, the room *tilts*. The walls stretch upward, the floor beneath you softens like wet clay, and the ceiling peels back to reveal a sky swirling with colors you’ve never seen: violet streaked with gold, a hue that hums like a plucked string. Your friend’s face flickers, their features melting and reforming—first into someone else you know, then into a stranger, then back to themselves, but older, or younger, or wearing a mask you can’t place.

You try to speak, but your voice comes out as a whisper, or a shout that echoes backward. The words you meant to say twist in your mouth: *"You don’t understand"* becomes *"I don’t understand"* becomes *"We’re both wrong."* The ground beneath you cracks, and for a terrifying second, you’re falling—only to land gently on a vast, empty plain where the only sound is the wind and the distant chime of a clock tower that wasn’t there before.

Your friend is gone. In their place stands a version of *you*—same clothes, same posture, but with their eyes. This doppelgänger reaches out, not in anger, but in invitation. *"You’re arguing with yourself,"* they say. *"That’s why it hurts so much."*

**The Revelation**
The dream fractures. You’re suddenly in a childhood home, or a place you’ve never been but recognize instantly: a library with no books, a forest where the trees grow upside-down, a train car that loops endlessly. Your friend is there again, but now they’re calm, almost sad. They hand you an object—a key, a broken mirror, a single playing card (the Queen of Hearts? The Joker?)—and say, *"This is what we’re really fighting over."*

You look down. The object is *nothing*. Just your own hand, palm up, empty. Or maybe it’s *everything*: a memory you’ve buried, a fear you’ve named, a version of love that’s too heavy to hold.

**The Choice**
The dream offers you options, each one a door in the air:
1. **The Door of Apology**: You step through and find yourself in a sunlit kitchen, your friend at the table, laughing over something trivial. The argument is over, but the weight of it lingers like a bruise. *"We don’t have to talk about it,"* they say. *"Not yet."*
2. **The Door of Silence**: You turn away, and the dream dissolves into a vast, quiet ocean. You’re floating, weightless, but the water is thick with unsaid things. A voice (yours? theirs?) whispers, *"Some arguments never end. They just change shape."*
3. **The Door of Truth**: You grab your friend’s wrist and say, *"No more metaphors. Tell me what this is really about."* The dream shatters like glass, and for a heartbeat, you’re awake—only to realize you’re still dreaming, but now you’re *aware* of it. The argument continues, but now you’re both laughing, because the stakes were never real, and yet they were everything.

**The Ending (Or Is It?)**
The dream loops. You’re back in the argument, but this time, you *listen* instead of speaking. Your friend’s voice softens. The colors in the sky dim to a familiar blue. The ground solidifies beneath your feet. And just as you think it’s over, the dream pulls you under again—this time into a different memory, a different fight, a different version of the same story.

Because some dreams aren’t about resolution. They’re about the *echo*.

**Possible Interpretations (If You Want to Dig Deeper):**
- **The Doppelgänger**: Represents the parts of yourself you project onto others (or the parts of them you refuse to see).
- **The Empty Hand**: Could symbolize a fear of loss, a relationship where something vital is missing, or the idea that the "thing" you’re fighting over is intangible (trust, respect, time).
- **The Doors**: Dreams often force choices to highlight what you’re avoiding in waking life. Which door would *you* pick?
- **The Loop**: Suggests the argument is cyclical—something you and your friend (or you and yourself) keep revisiting without progress.
