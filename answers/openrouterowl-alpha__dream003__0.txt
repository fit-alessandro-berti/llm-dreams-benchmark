The box doesn’t click open — it unmakes itself. The seams dissolve into mist, swirling upward. Inside, there is no object, no treasure — only a single drop of liquid light, suspended on the nose of the box like dew. I blink, and the light and I exchange places.

Now I AM the box. I am locked. I am keyless. And somewhere far off and close, I hear my own voice saying to myself from earlier, asking gently, examining the intricate patterns, and I answer here at last with the rustle of dream filling this newfound space.
