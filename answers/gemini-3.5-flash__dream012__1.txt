...and you grip the wooden helm, only to feel it soften beneath your hands like warm wax.

The wind doesn’t just howl; it speaks in a chorus of voices you haven’t heard since childhood, whispering half-forgotten secrets into the icy spray. The rain stings your face, but it tastes strangely of sweet, fresh well-water, not the salt of the sea.

You look down at your crew. Their faces are a shifting mosaic of people you’ve known—teachers from your youth, strangers passed on a train, and shadows with no features at all. They aren't crying out in fear; their silence is absolute, heavier than the thunder rolling overhead. They are waiting for a command that makes no sense in the waking world, their eyes wide and expectant.

You look to the binnacle, but the compass needle isn't pointing North. It is spinning lazily, finally settling on a direction labeled simply: *Forward*.

Taking a deep, cold breath, you realize the ship isn’t fighting the ocean—it *is* the ocean. You shout over the gale, your voice echoing with a resonance that doesn't belong to you. "Drop the sails! Let the ropes go!"

As the crew obeys, untying the rigging with impossible, synchronized grace, a wave the size of a mountain towers over the bow. It doesn't break. Instead, as the ship rises to meet it, the water freezes into a massive, translucent arch of glowing, bioluminescent glass. 

You steer the ship directly through the arch. The roaring wind instantly dies, replaced by a profound, ringing silence. The ship begins to tilt upward, its keel leaving the water entirely, sailing now into a sky of ink and starlight. Beneath you, the turbulent sea becomes a map of glowing, distant cities, shrinking into the dark as you drift higher, leaving the storm behind, floating toward the quiet edge of waking up.
