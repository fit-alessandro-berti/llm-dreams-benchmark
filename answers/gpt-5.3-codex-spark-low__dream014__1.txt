• You are feeling lost and alone in a bustling city. Suddenly, a group of kind strangers offers to help you find your
  way, and you follow them through back streets and glowing alleyways, through markets bright with lanterns and murals
  that seem to pulse with your own heartbeat. They guide you past the crowds to a small rooftop garden hidden above the
  noise, where the city’s lights look less like chaos and more like stars fallen to earth. One of them offers tea,
  another offers a map, and the oldest among them points to a narrow sign that says You’re already on your way.

  That night, you realize the strangers were just people you had passed in every mirror on every corner—your own
  reflections of what it means to be seen. By dawn, the city is still crowded, still loud, still strange. But now when
  someone smiles, you don't turn away. When a crosswalk is confusing, you ask for help. When the path forks, you choose
  slowly, with less fear. You are no longer lost in the city. You are finally in it.
