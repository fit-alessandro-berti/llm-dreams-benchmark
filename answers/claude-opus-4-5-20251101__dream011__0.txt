*settling into the hard wooden chair, pencil trembling slightly*

...the questions are **written in a language I've never seen before**—except I *have* seen it. It's my own handwriting. Every question is something I wrote in a journal years ago, questions I asked myself at 3am and never answered:

*\d you do if no one was measuring?\*

*\ are you when you stop performing competence?\ professor walks by and she has my mother's hands but no face. She taps question three: *\ the map of every path you didn't take.\*

The paper is expanding. The desk is expanding. The other students are filling in bubbles with absolute certainty and their pencils sound like rain, like applause, like a clock.

I look down and realize I'm not holding a pencil anymore—it's a key, warm like it's been in someone's pocket.

The final question, which keeps rewriting itself, currently says:

*\ door behind you has always been unlocked. What are you actually afraid of?\ turn around and—

---

*Do you want me to continue, or would you like to tell me what's behind the door? Dreams like these usually belong to the dreamer.*
