...realize that your voice is not moving your lips at all, but the air in the room has grown thick enough to trap them inside your throat like honey trapped in a jar. The words you want to say are turning into tiny, floating candles, flickering blue and violet as they drift away from you toward their hands. You try to stop them from burning out, but they burn so bright that for a second the faces of the crowd melt away into pools of liquid silver, watching your shadow stretch up the wall until it becomes a door waiting on its own side. As the last candle goes out in the dark, you understand that they were never listening, you were speaking to yourself—and the silence is not an end, but just a pause in the story before the morning comes to collect the feathers."
