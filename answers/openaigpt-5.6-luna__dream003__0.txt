You are dreaming. Can you complete the following dream?

You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and symbols etched into the lid—spirals within spirals, tiny moons, and a door standing beneath a sky full of unfamiliar stars.

When you touch the markings, they begin to glow.

The box whispers your name.

You turn it over and discover that the bottom is not solid wood or metal, but a dark, rippling surface like still water. In its reflection, you see yourself standing somewhere else: in a vast hallway lined with hundreds of identical boxes.

One by one, they begin to open.

Inside each box is a different memory—a forgotten birthday, a face you can no longer place, the sound of rain against a childhood window. Then, at the very end of the hallway, you see a box larger than all the others. It bears the same symbols as yours, except for one: a small golden key.

You reach toward the reflection.

The hallway reaches back.

Suddenly, the box in your hands clicks open. Inside there is no treasure, no letter, no key—only a single sleeping moth. As it unfolds its silver wings, the entire room dissolves into stars.

The moth flies toward you and settles between your eyes.

At once, you understand: the box was never locked.

It was waiting for you to remember how to open it.

Then you wake—but somewhere in the darkness beside your bed, something softly clicks.
