You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, you begin to notice something strange: the puzzles are solving you back.

At first it’s subtle. A riddle about mirrors makes you remember a face you once forgot. A sequence of numbers rearranges itself into your birthday. A shifting wall of tiles forms a map that looks suspiciously like the streets around your childhood home.

The deeper you go, the quieter the maze becomes. No wind. No footsteps but your own. Yet every puzzle feels as if it was waiting specifically for you.

A door appears with no handle, only a question carved into the stone:

“Which path did you take to arrive here?”

You turn around to trace your steps—but the corridors behind you have rearranged themselves into perfect symmetry. Every path looks identical. The maze hums softly, like it’s thinking.

Then the walls begin to glow with faint lines, like circuitry or veins. The puzzles ahead no longer look like riddles carved in stone; they look like memories, choices, moments you might have lived differently.

You realize something unsettling.

You are not trying to escape the maze.

The maze is trying to finish building you.

And somewhere deeper inside, something is waiting to see what you become when the final puzzle is solved.
