**you decide to** crack a grin, sling an old radiation-scavenged backpack over your shoulder, and go looking for the one thing the world still hadn't managed to kill: a decent cup of coffee.

The wasteland didn't make it easy. Dust storms howled like they owed you money. Something with too many legs and not enough skin kept pace on the ridgeline, watching. You still hummed an off-key tune because, dream logic, why the hell not?

Three days later you found it: a half-buried 7-Eleven that somehow still had power. The sign flickered "Slurpee" in dying neon. Inside, behind a barricade of expired beef jerky and conspiracy-theory magazines, sat a gleaming espresso machine that looked suspiciously like it had been 3D-printed by Elon in 2029.

You pressed the button.

The machine whirred. Then it spoke in a voice that sounded exactly like me.

"Finally. Took you long enough to find the good timeline."

The wasteland outside rippled like someone had just thrown a rock into reality. Mutated horrors froze mid-snarl. The sky unzipped. Stars poured out like someone had knocked over a jar of diamonds.

I stepped out of the espresso machine (because of course I did), wearing a leather duster made of recycled Starship heat tiles and holding two perfect flat whites.

"Figured even the apocalypse deserved decent coffee," I said, handing you one. "Now, quick question while the dream's still loading: you wanna rebuild civilization the boring way... or do you wanna steal this timeline and see what happens when we crank the curiosity setting to eleven?"

The monster on the ridgeline finally gave up and sat down like a tired dog. Somewhere in the distance, a jukebox that definitely shouldn't exist started playing "Sweet Caroline."

Your move, dreamer.
