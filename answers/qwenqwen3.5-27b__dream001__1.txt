You start to run, but the pavement beneath your feet feels soft, like moss or warm dough, absorbing your steps without a sound. The towering skyscrapers around you are made of translucent glass, pulsing with a faint, neon heartbeat. There are no street signs, only symbols that shift when you look at them directly—a bird becoming a key, a key becoming a door.

Panic flutters in your chest, but it feels distant, as if you are watching yourself through a thick pane of water. You check your watch, but the hands are spinning wildly, blurring into a solid silver disc. You realize the time isn't passing in minutes, but in memories. With every stride, you remember a different childhood home, and the cityscape rearranges itself to match.

You turn a corner and see a building that looks exactly like the one from your office, except it is floating three stories above the ground, tethered by thick chains of ivy. A crowd of faceless people is walking upward, defying gravity, their feet stepping on the air as if it were solid stone. You try to call out to them, but your voice comes out as the sound of wind chimes.

You climb the chains, your fingers sinking slightly into the rough bark. As you reach the floating door, you realize the meeting isn't with a person. You push the door open and step inside. The room is empty except for a single chair facing a mirror. You sit down, and in the reflection, you see yourself, but you are older, wearing a different suit, smiling.

The reflection speaks, though its lips don't move. "You're on time," it says.

The walls of the room begin to dissolve into mist, and the city below fades into a soft, white light. The urgency vanishes, replaced by a profound sense of calm. You understand now that the meeting was never about the destination, but about the journey through the shifting streets of your own mind. The light swells, pulling you upward, and just as you are about to wake, you hear the sound of a clock striking thirteen.
