You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and —

they aren't really patterns at all.

They're tiny, interlocking lines of script in a language you've never seen, but that you can somehow read. Except it won't stay still. When you stare directly at it, it looks like carved wood. When you glance out of the corner of your eye, it ripples. Like constellations. Like veins under skin.

The box is warm in your hands. Warmer than it should be. It has no hinges, no keyhole, no seam. It shouldn't be able to open. And yet you know, with that absolute dream-certainty, that it *is* locked.

You bring it closer to your ear. 

Inside, there is no ticking. There's wind. And faintly, distantly, like a radio left on in another room - a beach. You can hear waves.

You run your thumb over the patterns to feel for a hidden latch, and the lines move *away* from your touch, then rush back to follow it. They start to arrange themselves. They are tracing you. The whorl of your fingerprint, the lifeline on your palm, a map of a street you lived on as a kid.

Oh.

You realize it was never locked to keep something inside.

It was waiting for you to match it.

So you stop trying to open it. You just hold it. You let your breathing slow down until it matches the faint, warm pulse coming from the box itself.

The patterns stop moving. They glow, just for a second - a soft, amber-gold light from inside the wood.

*Click.*

There is no lid anymore. The box just... unfolds. The four sides fall open like petals, but the inside is much, much larger than the outside should allow.

Inside the box is your bedroom. Exactly as it is right now. Dark, quiet. And on the bed, you are asleep, holding a small wooden box.

You look down at the you in the box, and the you in the box looks up at you.

And you both smile, remembering at the same time.

Then you wake up. 

Your hands are empty. But on your left palm, for just a second before it fades, is a faint, warm, intricate pattern.
