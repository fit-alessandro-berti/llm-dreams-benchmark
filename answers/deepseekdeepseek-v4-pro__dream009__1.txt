As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to orient yourself by the position of the sun, but the sky is unfamiliar—a greenish amber with two faint suns bleeding into each other like watercolor on wet paper. The ground beneath your bare feet is cool and smooth, not quite stone, not quite moss, and it hums faintly as if alive. A breeze carries the scent of crushed herbs and distant rain, and when you look down at your hands, they are not your own—longer fingers, a dusting of golden freckles you’ve never seen before.

You need to find shelter before the strange twilight deepens, because the air is turning sharp with an electric tension that makes your hair stand on end. Following a winding path between towering, spiraled plants that look like ferns spun from copper wire, you come upon a cluster of dwellings carved into the hillside, their doorways round and glowing with a soft, pulsing light. A low chanting spills out, a sound of many voices woven together, and though you don’t understand the words, your chest aches with a sudden, inexplicable longing—as if the song is calling you home.

Just as you step forward, a figure emerges: tall, robed in layers of moving shadow, their face hidden but for eyes that reflect the twin suns. They tilt their head, and without speaking, the question forms directly in your mind: *Are you the one who remembers?* You open your mouth to answer, but no sound comes—your voice belongs to another body, another time. The dream twists then, the scene rippling like a stone thrown into still water, and you feel yourself fading, waking, clutching desperately at the threads of a world that already feels more real than the one you're being pulled back to.
