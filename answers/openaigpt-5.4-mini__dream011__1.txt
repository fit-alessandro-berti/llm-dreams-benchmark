…written in a language you almost understand, like a memory trying to become a sentence.

The paper trembles in your hands.

Question 1 simply says: **“Name the thing you were always meant to do.”**

You look around the room. The other students are already writing, their pens moving like tiny clock hands. The proctor, who has the face of someone from your childhood and the shadow of a storm, says nothing.

You turn the page.

**Question 2:** *“What would you choose if failure were impossible?”*

For a moment, the ceiling above you becomes a night sky. One of the stars blinks twice, as if waiting for your answer.

You begin to write, but the letters come out as birds, and the birds fly off the page.

The exam continues:

- **3.** *Draw the bridge between who you are and who you pretend to be.*
- **4.** *Solve for X: the moment you stopped being afraid.*
- **5.** *If your heart were a house, which room would you unlock first?*

Your pencil melts.

Still, you keep going. You write with your fingertips, with your breathing, with the feeling in your chest that has been following you for years. The questions grow softer, stranger, almost kind:

- **6.** *What do you owe your future self?*
- **7.** *Which dream did you abandon too early?*
- **8.** *What sound does courage make in the dark?*

Then the final page is blank except for one line in the center:

**“There is no correct answer. There is only the life you choose next.”**

You set the paper down.

When you look up, the classroom has become an open road. The desks are gone. The walls have unfolded into sky.

The proctor smiles, or maybe it is just your own reflection wearing mercy.

At the bottom of your exam booklet, a new question appears in your handwriting:

**“Begin.”**

And so you do.
