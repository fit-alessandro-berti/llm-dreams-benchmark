You are about to take the final exam for a course that determines your future career. As you sit down to start the exam, you notice the questions are **written in a language you’ve never seen before—yet you understand every word as if reading your own journal.**  

The first question asks, *“What do you fear most about the path you’re about to choose?”* Your pen trembles, and before you can write, the paper ripples like water. Beneath the ink, words shift: *“Answer in three sentences. The third must be a lie.”*  

You hesitate. The lie is easy—*“I fear nothing.”* But the second sentence? You write, *“I fear success will make me selfish.”* The third sentence forms itself: *“My greatest fear is that I’ll forget to laugh.”*  

The room tilts. The proctor—a shadow with your own face—nods. The next question appears in your mind: *“If your career were a door, would you open it or lock it forever?”*  

You stand, walk to the window, and fling it open. The sky cracks like a dropped mirror, and every reflection of yourself spills onto the floor—some wearing lab coats, others in tattered clothes, all alive with possibility. The exam burns away, and you realize the questions were never tests. They were mirrors.  

The future isn’t determined by answers. It’s a choice written in the ink of your hands.
