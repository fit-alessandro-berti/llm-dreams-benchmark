You start to run, your feet slapping against pavement that feels strangely soft, like moss-covered stone. The city around you is a labyrinth of impossible architecture—skyscrapers that twist into spirals, bridges that lead nowhere, and streets that shift direction when you blink.

The air smells of ozone and old paper. You check your watch, but the hands are spinning counter-clockwise, melting into the glass face. Panic rises in your chest, not because you’re late, but because you suddenly can’t remember *who* you’re meeting or *why* it matters. Was it a job interview? A reunion with a lost love? A trial for a crime you don’t recall committing?

A street vendor with three eyes offers you a map made of translucent leaves. "The meeting is where the shadow ends," he whispers, his voice sounding like wind through dry grass. You take the map, and as your fingers touch it, the ink begins to crawl across your skin, forming arrows that point not forward, but *up*.

You look up. Above the towering spires, the sky isn’t blue—it’s a deep, velvety black dotted with stars that pulse in rhythm with your heartbeat. And there, suspended in the void, is a single door, floating freely, glowing with a warm, golden light.

You realize you don’t need to find the location. You need to let go of the ground.

You close your eyes, lean back, and fall upward.
