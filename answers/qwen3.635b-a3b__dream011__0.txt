...you notice the questions are written in water that doesn't puddle, only hovers just above the paper, trembling with each breath you take. The first one reads: *What will you carry when the room empties?* You reach for a pen, but it unspools into a thread of silver light that wraps around your wrist, tugging gently toward the far wall. 

The second question rises like mist: *Which path hums when you stop listening?* You look at the student sitting across from you. Their exam sheet is already full, but the ink crawls like ants, rearranging itself into constellations, then into fingerprints, then into nothing at all. The proctor isn't a person. It's a metronome on a steel stand, swinging in time with the low thrum of your own pulse.

Time stretches like taffy. You realize the test doesn't want answers. It wants witness. 

You stand. The paper doesn't rip; it opens. Each question unfolds into a doorway. You don't write. You walk. Through the first, you see yourself at a workbench, hands stained with clay and calluses. Through the second, you're standing on a deck, wind in your collar, charting courses you've only ever skimmed in books. Through the third, you're silent in a hospital corridor, holding a hand you've never held, learning how to be still. 

You keep moving. The room around you breathes. The walls soften into horizon lines. The questions behind you stop asking and start waiting. 

When you finally turn back, the desk is gone. The exam hall has become a threshold. Resting on the sill is a single brass key, warm as skin, with a tag tied to it that reads: *Not the answer. The door.* 

You pick it up. It fits nothing. And yet, it feels exactly like coming awake.
