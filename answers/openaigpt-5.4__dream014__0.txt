One of them hands you a folded paper map, though when you open it, the streets are not streets at all—they are rivers of light, and each intersection glows like a small golden star.

They guide you through the city, which begins to soften around the edges. The noise of traffic turns into music. Neon signs flicker and transform into constellations hanging just above the rooftops. You realize the strangers are not leading you to some distant address. They are leading you toward a place inside yourself you had forgotten how to reach.

At last you arrive at a quiet courtyard hidden between tall buildings covered in ivy and moonlight. In the center stands a fountain, its water reflecting not your face but every version of you that has ever been brave, hopeful, or loved. The strangers smile and tell you, gently, “You were never as lost as you thought.”

And as you step closer to the fountain, the whole city seems to breathe with you—vast, alive, and no longer frightening. The strangers fade into the silver air, leaving behind only the feeling of being guided, of being held by something larger and kinder than fear.

Then you wake with the strange certainty that somewhere, even in the busiest and loneliest places, the world is still full of people and paths waiting to find you.
