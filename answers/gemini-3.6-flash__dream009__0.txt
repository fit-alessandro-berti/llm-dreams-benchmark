None of them look at you with suspicion—in fact, they nod as if you’ve belonged here all along.

A deep brass bell tolls twice from a distant spire, echoing across the cobblestones. At the sound, the crowd slows, their voices dropping to a rhythmic chant in a language you have never studied, yet somehow understand: *"The tide is turning. Lock the gates."*

You look down at your hand and find a heavy brass key resting in your palm, cool to the touch and intricately carved with symbols that pulse with a faint, warm light. Dream logic takes hold, bringing a sudden, absolute certainty: 

You need to reach the ancient sun-dial at the center of the citadel before the second shadow falls. If you can use the key to unlock the mechanism hidden beneath its base, you will stop the cycle that keeps this world trapped in a single, repeating day—and finally figure out whether you are meant to save this lost era, or if it is trying to keep you from ever waking up.
