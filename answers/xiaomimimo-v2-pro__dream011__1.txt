*settling into the dream…*

The questions are written in a language I've never seen before — symbols that shimmer and shift like heat rising off pavement. My heart pounds. I grip the edges of the desk.

Then I notice something strange: the student beside me is writing furiously, confidently. I glance over — and their exam paper is *blank*. Their pen moves across nothing, yet they're beaming with certainty.

A whisper floats from somewhere behind me: *"Stop reading the questions. Start reading yourself."*

I look down again. The symbols rearrange. They're not test questions at all — they're memories. *My* memories. Each one asking me something:

*What did you learn from that failure last spring?*
*Why were you afraid to try again after that?*
*When did you stop trusting your own instincts?*

My hand trembles — not from fear anymore, but from recognition. I pick up my pen and the words come easily, pouring out like I've always known the answers. The room grows warm, golden light flooding in through windows I hadn't noticed before.

The professor — whose face I still can't quite see — collects the papers and pauses at my desk.

*"You passed,"* they say quietly. *"Not because of what you wrote. Because you stopped trying to be right and started being honest."*

The exam dissolves in my hands like morning fog, and I wake up feeling something I can't name — lighter, maybe. Like I'd been carrying a weight I'd forgotten about.
