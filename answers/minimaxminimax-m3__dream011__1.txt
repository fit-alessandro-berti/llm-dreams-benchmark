*...completely blank.*

At first, I think it's a printing error. I flip through every page, but each sheet is the same — pristine, empty, an expanse of white that seems to stretch into infinity. I look around the room. The other students are bent over their papers, scribbling furiously, the scratch of pencils filling the air like a chorus of determined crickets. I feel a cold sweat creep down my neck.

Then I notice something impossible. The papers are no longer blank. *My* questions are writing themselves, ink bleeding up from the page like something surfacing from deep water. But they're not questions about the course material. Not even close.

**Question 1:** *Describe a moment you made someone feel seen.*

**Question 2:** *What do you do when no one is watching?*

**Question 3:** *What have you been pretending not to know?*

A strange calm washes over me. I realize no amount of cramming could have prepared me for this. The exam was never about the subject at all. The proctor walks by — she's wearing a face I almost recognize, someone important from a long time ago — and she whispers without stopping, *"There are no wrong answers. There are only answers you're afraid to write."*

I pick up my pen. My hand is steady. For the first time in years, I know exactly what to say.
