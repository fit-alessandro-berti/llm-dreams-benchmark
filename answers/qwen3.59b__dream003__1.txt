You reach out, not to touch the lid, but to steady yourself against the table beneath it. But the floor feels soft, like moss or wet sand, and the gravity in this dream seems optional. The patterns on the box begin to form a language you don't speak, yet the meaning resonates directly in your chest: *remember.*

You close them. You recall nothing of why you are here.
You realize that you are not holding the box in your hands; the box is holding *you*.

The lock has never been physical all along. The keyhole is a small, dark mirror inset into the front. You lean in to look inside at the reflection waiting for you. As soon as you see yourself there, the image smiles when you are terrified of opening it. The patterns on the box glow brighter, syncing with the rhythm of your heartbeat. Suddenly, there is no fear—only the quiet realization that the door was never meant to be forced open from the outside. It is waiting for you to step in and meet a version of yourself who already remembers how to turn it.

You are handed a flashlight made of pure sound. You hear the click before you see the light. The box opens, but inside there is no treasure, only the space where your own hands used to be before this dream began.
The world turns grey, then fades into blackness, and you wake up with the taste of copper in your mouth.
