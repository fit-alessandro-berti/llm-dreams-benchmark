As you approach the blank canvas, it begins to hum softly, as if it's waiting. The surface, initially stark white, starts to ripple like water when you gently touch it. 

From the point of your fingertip, colors begin to bloom—not painted on, but emerging as if from another dimension. Swirls of iridescent silver bleed into deep, cosmic blues. Geometric shapes fold and unfold upon themselves, creating patterns that feel both ancient and impossibly new.

You realize you're not looking at a painting that's appearing, but a portal. The canvas isn't a canvas at all—it's a window, and it's looking back at you. It shows a reflection, but not yours. It shows a thousand possible versions of you, each in a world made of different dreams. 

The air smells of ozone and blooming night jasmine. You feel a gentle pull, not towards fear, but towards profound curiosity. Do you reach in?
