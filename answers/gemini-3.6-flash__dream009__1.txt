Above me, the night sky is impossibly dense—a magnificent, terrifying river of stars unmarred by the glare of modern electric light. I am standing in a narrow, winding alley of crushed stone, flanked by whitewashed plaster buildings with heavy wooden beams. Below, from the main thoroughfare, comes the sound of iron-shod hooves, low voices speaking an ancient, melodic tongue, and the soft flicker of torchlight.

I press my back against a cool stone wall. A pair of guards in bronze-studded leather armor march past the alley's entrance, their spears glinting in the dark. 

As they pass, the dream-logic settles into my mind, bringing a sudden, sharp clarity. I haven't just fallen back in time; I was *summoned*. 

High above the terracotta rooflines, perched on a hill overlooking the ancient harbor, stands a massive stone observatory. At its highest tower, a strange, azure light pulses—a light that doesn't belong in this century, beating in perfect sync with my own pulse. 

I realize I need to reach that tower before the first light of dawn. I don't know who is waiting for me up there, or what knowledge they hold, but I know that if the sun rises before I step through those wooden doors, the thread holding this reality together will snap—and I will wake up, leaving a crucial piece of myself behind in the dark.

I pull a heavy woolen cloak from a clothesline hanging between two shuttered windows, wrap it tightly around my shoulders to hide my jeans, and step out into the shadowed crowd.
