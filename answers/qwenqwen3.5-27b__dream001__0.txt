You start to run, but your feet sink slightly into the pavement, which feels less like concrete and more like firm, dark velvet. The skyscrapers around you aren't made of steel and glass, but of stacked, translucent books that hum with a low, vibrating frequency.

Panic flares in your chest, but it feels distant, like a radio playing in another room. You glance at your wrist; your watch has no numbers, only a single hand spinning wildly in reverse. You decide to ask for directions. A figure steps out from a shadow between two towering book-spires. They have no face, only a smooth surface where features should be, but they point a long, slender finger toward the center of the city.

"Up," the figure says, though their mouth doesn't move.

You look up. The street stretches into a spiral staircase made of light, winding upward into a sky that is the color of bruised plums. You don't hesitate. You step onto the light, and the city falls away beneath you. As you ascend, the air grows thick with the scent of ozone and old paper.

Halfway up, the stairs dissolve into a cloud of floating keys. You have to swim through them to reach the top. As you pass through the keys, they click and chime, each one unlocking a memory you didn't know you had.

Finally, you reach the summit. There is no building here, only a single, round door floating in the void. It has no handle, just a small keyhole shaped like an eye. You realize with a sudden, calm certainty that you don't need a key. You simply blink.

The door opens. Inside, there is no boardroom, no table, no agenda. There is only a mirror. You step closer, and in the reflection, you see yourself sitting at a desk, signing a contract. The reflection looks up and nods at you.

The room begins to spin. The walls dissolve into the velvet pavement of the street below. You feel the sensation of falling, not down, but *forward*, rushing toward a bright, warm light.

You wake up.
