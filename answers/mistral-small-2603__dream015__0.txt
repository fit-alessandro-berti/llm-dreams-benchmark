**...build a sanctuary.**

The wind howls through the skeletal remains of a once-great city, its skyscrapers now jagged teeth against the bruised sky. You’ve scavenged enough to know the world is broken—but not beyond repair. With your last working tool—a rusted wrench and a pocketknife—you kneel in the cracked earth of what was once a park. The soil is dry, but not dead. Not yet.

You start small. A fire pit, ringed with scavenged bricks. A lean-to of salvaged metal sheets and tarp, its edges weighted with chunks of concrete. You plant the hardiest seeds you’ve found in the ruins of a grocery store: dandelions, clover, a few stubborn tomato vines. They’ll grow slow, but they’ll grow.

By dusk, you’ve rigged a solar panel (salvaged from a rooftop array) to a jury-rigged battery. It flickers to life, casting a weak but defiant glow over your makeshift home. You boil the last of your water, seasoning it with crushed herbs from your pack. It tastes like ash and hope.

As you eat, you hear it—the distant growl of an engine. Not a truck. Something smaller. A motorcycle, maybe. You freeze. The firelight makes you a target. You douse the flames with a bucket of rainwater you’ve collected, plunging yourself into darkness.

The growl fades. For now.

You exhale. Tomorrow, you’ll reinforce the walls. Tomorrow, you’ll scout for more supplies. Tomorrow, you’ll keep breathing.

Because in this broken world, a single spark is enough to start a fire. And a fire, no matter how small, keeps the dark at bay.
