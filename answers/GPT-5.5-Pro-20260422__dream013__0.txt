...begin to realize the puzzles are not guarding the way out.

They are guarding memories.

The first chamber is made of mirrors, each one reflecting a different version of you: younger, older, braver, afraid. A bronze door waits at the far end with no handle, only a sentence carved into it:

**To pass, choose the reflection that is lying.**

You study them carefully. The child-you says, “I was never afraid.” The older-you says, “I solved everything alone.” The brave-you says nothing at all, but reaches toward the glass with trembling fingers.

You touch the silent reflection.

The mirror ripples like water, and the door opens.

Beyond it, the maze narrows. The walls are covered in shifting symbols, equations that rearrange themselves when you blink. You try to solve them, but every answer becomes another question. The harder you concentrate, the louder the maze hums, until the sound fills your bones.

Then you notice something strange: the symbols are not asking for numbers. They are asking for choices.

Left or right. Stay or run. Speak or remain silent. Remember or forget.

You choose.

The walls sigh and slide apart.

Deeper still, you find a room with no floor, only stars beneath your feet. Islands of stone float in the darkness, each marked with a puzzle piece. You leap from one to the next, arranging them in your mind, building a bridge that does not exist until you believe it should.

Halfway across, the maze whispers:

**What is the final answer?**

You stop.

All this time, you thought the dream was testing your intelligence. But the maze has watched you hesitate, doubt, try again, fail, return, and continue. It has learned the shape of your mind.

So you answer, “The final answer is not to escape.”

The stars beneath you flare.

The floating stones lock together, forming a path to the center of the maze, where a single wooden door stands beneath an impossible sky. It looks ordinary. Almost too ordinary.

You open it.

Inside is your own bedroom.

You are standing beside your bed, watching yourself sleep. On the nightstand lies a small folded note. You pick it up and read the words written in your own handwriting:

**You can wake up now, but you don’t have to forget.**

Behind you, the maze begins folding inward, its corridors collapsing into lines, its puzzles becoming sparks, its walls dissolving into morning light.

Your sleeping self opens one eye.

And just before you wake, you hear the maze whisper one last thing:

**Next time, bring a question.**
