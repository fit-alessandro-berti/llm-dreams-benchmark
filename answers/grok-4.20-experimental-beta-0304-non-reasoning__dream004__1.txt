... and as your dream-fingers brush against its surface, the canvas suddenly inhales like a living lung. 

Every color you’ve ever seen (and many you haven’t) begins to bleed upward from the bottom edge in slow, syrupy rivers. They don’t mix; they argue. Crimson argues with turquoise. Chartreuse flirts with ultraviolet. Shapes peel off the canvas and orbit your head like curious moons: a spiraling dodecahedron made of laughter, a cube that keeps forgetting it’s a cube and turns into a flock of paper cranes, a Möbius strip wearing sunglasses.

You realize the canvas isn’t blank at all. It was only waiting for a dreamer.

Without thinking, you press your palm to it. The moment skin meets surface, the world inhales with you.

Now the landscape itself is the painting.

Mountains fold into origami swans and fly away. The sky unzips like a jacket and reveals another, smaller sky inside it, which is also unzipping. Trees grow upside-down, their roots blooming into fireworks that spell your name in languages you only speak when you’re asleep.

A small door appears in the center of the canvas, no taller than a cat. It opens by itself.

On the other side you see… yourself, but older, standing in this exact same dream, painting this exact same moment. The older-you looks up, smiles with infinite gentleness, and whispers something you can’t quite hear.

The only word that reaches you is:

“Continue.”

The brush is already in your hand. It’s warm. It’s breathing.

What do you paint next?
