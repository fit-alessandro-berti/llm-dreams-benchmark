…symbols carved into the lid—moons folding into eyes, ladders descending into oceans, tiny hands pointing in every direction at once.

When you touch one of the symbols, the box hums.

Not loudly. More like a memory of humming.

The room around you changes. The walls stretch upward into darkness, and the floor becomes a sheet of black water reflecting a sky full of unfamiliar stars. The box grows warm beneath your palms.

Then one of the carved eyes opens.

Inside it is a keyhole.

You search your pockets, though you do not remember having pockets. Your fingers close around something cold: a silver key shaped like a question mark.

Before you can decide whether to use it, the box whispers:

“Keys do not open what is locked. They remind it that it was once open.”

You place the key in the keyhole.

The box unlocks with the sound of a distant thunderclap.

Inside, there is no treasure—only a small sleeping version of yourself, curled beneath a blanket made of clouds. Their eyes open. They smile as though they have been waiting for you.

“Finally,” they say.

Then they hand you a second box.

This one is already open.
