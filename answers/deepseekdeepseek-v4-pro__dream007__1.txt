You are in the middle of a heated argument with a close friend. The disagreement is about something important to both of you, and you decide to stop defending your position and instead plant a seed in the ground between your feet. The gesture is so unexpected that your friend falls silent, her unfinished sentence still fluttering like a torn banner in the air. You don't have a seed, but you kneel anyway and press your thumb into the floor—which has turned into soft, dark earth, smelling of rain and secrets. A tiny green sprout unfurls instantly, a delicate question mark rising from the soil.  

Your friend's anger dissolves into something older and more fragile. She kneels too, and her hand covers yours in the dirt. The sprout grows into a sapling, then a tree with silver bark and leaves that whisper with your own voices, replaying all the unkind words you've just exchanged, but now they're harmless, just wind through branches. A swing appears on one low limb, the same swing from the park where you first laughed until your stomachs hurt, years ago. You both know, without speaking, that the argument was really about how much you fear losing each other.  

The tree blooms with white flowers that drift down like apologies. One lands on her hair, and you reach to brush it away, but it melts into light. You wake with the scent of wet earth clinging to your pillow, and the words "I'm sorry" already forming on your lips before you realize the dream has ended.
