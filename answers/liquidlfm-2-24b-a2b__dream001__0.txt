The sky above is a deep indigo, streaked with faint silver clouds that glow softly in the dim light. You pass a café where the scent of coffee and pastries drifts out, but the faces of the patrons blur together, indistinct.

A distant chime echoes—perhaps a clock tower, perhaps a bell. You follow the sound, your footsteps echoing on cobblestones that feel both ancient and new. Along the way, you notice signs in languages you don’t recognize, yet somehow understand. One reads: *“The truth lies beneath the third arch.”*

You pass a mirror shop, and in each reflection, you see a slightly different version of yourself—some smiling, some worried, some holding a briefcase or a map. You pause, then step inside. Behind the counter sits an old man with eyes like polished stone. He nods at you.

“You’re looking for the meeting,” he says, not unkindly. “But first, you must find the key. It’s not in your pocket. It’s in your memory.”

He gestures toward a door at the back of the shop, slightly ajar. Beyond it, a narrow staircase spirals downward into darkness.

Do you go down?
