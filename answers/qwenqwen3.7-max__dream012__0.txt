…realize the ship’s wheel in your hands isn’t made of wood, but of cold, carved bone that hums against your palms like a tuning fork. 

You open your mouth to shout an order, but instead of your voice, the deep, mournful resonance of a cello echoes across the deck. The crew doesn’t seem to mind. Your first mate rushes toward you, but as the lightning flashes, you notice his face is a vintage clock, its hands spinning wildly backward. 

"The ink is rising, Captain!" he yells, though his mouth doesn't move. 

You look over the bow. He’s right. The ocean is no longer water; it is a churning, violent sea of black, liquid ink. Giant waves of it crest and crash against the hull, staining the wood and smelling sharply of old libraries and ozone. The rain begins to fall upward, silver droplets lifting from the deck and rushing back into the bruised, purple clouds.

With the absolute, unquestioning certainty that only exists in dreams, you know exactly what to do. 

You let go of the bone wheel. "Hoist the memories!" you command, the cello-voice vibrating in your chest. 

The crew scrambles up the rigging, but they aren't pulling canvas. They are unfurling massive, glowing tapestries woven from your own childhood recollections. A sail made of a sunlit bicycle ride catches the upward-falling rain. A jib crafted from the smell of your grandmother’s kitchen snaps taut in the gale. 

The ship lurches, lifting out of the ink. The storm roars, a sound like a thousand crumpled pages, as a massive tidal wave of black fluid towers over the stern, threatening to swallow you whole. 

But the ship doesn't sail forward. It sails *up*. 

Driven by the wind of your own nostalgia, the vessel detaches from the sea entirely, ascending into the heavy, velvet dark of the sky. The ink wave shatters below you into a million black butterflies that scatter into the wind. 

As the ship breaks through the cloud cover, the violent storm instantly dissolves into a quiet, endless hallway lined with softly glowing doors. The crew sits down on the deck, pulling out instruments and beginning to play a gentle lullaby in a language you’ve never learned, yet perfectly understand. 

You walk to the edge of the ship, look down at the endless starry floor of the hallway, and realize with a sudden, heavy eyelid droop that you are incredibly tired, and it is time to wake up.
