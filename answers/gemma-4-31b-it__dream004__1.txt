On the other side, the chaos of the shifting world vanishes. I find myself standing in a forest where the trees are made of frozen music—crystalline structures that chime softly when the wind brushes against them. The ground is a mirror of dark obsidian, reflecting a sky filled with three moons, each a different shade of pale emerald.

I look back, but the canvas is gone. In its place is a small, floating mirror. I lean in to see my reflection, but I don't see a face. Instead, I see a constellation of stars shifting in the shape of a human silhouette. 

As I watch, one of the stars in my chest begins to pulse in time with my heartbeat. With every beat, a new color explodes across the horizon, turning the emerald sky into a deep, velvet crimson. I realize then that I am not just exploring this world—I am the one dreaming it into existence.

I close my eyes within the dream, and as I do, I feel the forest begin to dissolve into a soft, warm mist, pulling me slowly back toward the surface of wakefulness, leaving only the faint scent of ozone and wet paint lingering in the air.
