...the air grows thick with the scent of ozone and damp earth. The trees, once swaying gently in a warm breeze, now creak ominously as the wind whips through their branches like a warning. The sunlight that had dappled the forest floor in golden patches is swallowed by dark, bruise-colored clouds rolling in from the horizon. A distant rumble of thunder vibrates through the ground beneath your feet, and the temperature drops so suddenly that goosebumps rise on your skin.

As the first fat raindrops begin to fall—cold and heavy—you notice something strange: the path ahead, which had been clear moments before, is now obscured by a thick, silvery mist. It swirls unnaturally, as if alive, and from its depths, you hear a sound—not quite a voice, but a melody, haunting and familiar, like a lullaby from a childhood you can’t quite remember.

Then, the mist parts just enough to reveal a figure standing at the edge of a small, moonlit clearing. It’s hard to make out their features, but you feel a pull, an unspoken invitation—or perhaps a challenge. The storm rages around you, but the clearing remains eerily calm, as if existing in a different time.

Do you step forward into the mist, or turn back the way you came? And why does the figure feel both terrifying and... *expected*?
