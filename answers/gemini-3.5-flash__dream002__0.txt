The canopy above, once a gentle filter of golden sunlight, begins to thrash violently. The leaves turn their pale undersides upward, whispering a frantic, collective warning in a language I feel rather than hear. The sky bruises into a deep, electric violet. 

I look for shelter, but the paths between the ancient trees are blurring, dissolving into a thick, low-hanging fog that glows with a faint inner light. 

Then, the rain begins. But this is a dream, and the rain does not fall down. 

As the first heavy droplets touch the forest floor, they bounce. They are glowing spheres of soft, liquid light, and they begin to float slowly upward, defying gravity. Around me, millions of luminous blue and gold droplets rise from the moss, drifting toward the churning clouds like inverted stars. 

The wind rises to a roar, but instead of pushing me back, it lifts me. My feet lose touch with the earth. I am weightless, drifting upward alongside the glowing rain. The fear of the storm evaporates, replaced by a profound, breathtaking awe. 

As I rise above the thrashing treetops, the storm clouds part. Above them is not a dark sky, but a calm, endless sea of stars. I float in the quiet space between the chaos below and the silence above, suspended in the warmth of the rising light, waiting to see where the wind will carry me next.
