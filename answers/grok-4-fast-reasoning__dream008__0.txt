You are at your workplace, and suddenly, you are given a project with an impossible deadline. Your boss is waiting for you to start, and you freeze, staring at the stack of papers that seem to multiply like rabbits on caffeine. The clock on the wall ticks louder than a heartbeat in a horror movie, each second peeling away layers of your sanity. You dive in anyway, fingers flying over the keyboard, but the words twist into nonsense—equations turning into limericks, spreadsheets blooming into wildflowers that spill out of the screen.

Your boss paces, morphing into a giant hourglass, sand pouring from his sleeves onto the floor, burying your desk. You try to shout for more time, but your voice comes out as a flock of confused pigeons that flap around the office, knocking over coffee mugs and scattering memos like confetti. Colleagues watch from their cubicles, their faces melting into Cheshire cat grins, whispering, "Just wing it, it's only the end of the world."

Panic surges, and you realize the project isn't code or reports—it's a map to a hidden door in the break room fridge. You sprint there, yank it open, and tumble into a starry void where deadlines dissolve into shooting stars. The boss's voice echoes faintly: "Overtime approved!" And just as you grab a comet's tail to ride away, the alarm blares—you jolt awake, tangled in sheets, the real deadline? Breakfast.
