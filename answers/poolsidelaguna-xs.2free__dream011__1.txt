I look up to see the professor - but it's my grandmother, except she's wearing a mortarboard made of living vines, and her eyes are glowing faintly blue. She smiles and nods toward the first question, which now seems to be floating in the air like a holographic projection.

The question reads: *"Translate your deepest fear into a valid syllogism."*

I stare at it, paralyzed. Around me, other students are frantically scribbling on papers that appear to be made of what looks like parchment, but when I glance over, I see they're actually written on the bark of trees. One student is weeping silently, and her tears are turning into small flowers as they hit the desk.

A hand touches my shoulder. I turn to see it's my childhood teddy bear, but wearing a graduation cap. He whispers, "You've been preparing for this exam your whole life. Every conversation, every choice - it was all training."

The exam sheet in front of me begins to glow, and the questions start changing based on my thoughts. *"What does the color blue mean to you?"* becomes the first prompt. I write: "It means the space between what I am and what I could be." 

The words immediately transform into a perfect score.
