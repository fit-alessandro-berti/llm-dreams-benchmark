---

You stand frozen in the middle of the office, the fluorescent lights buzzing like a swarm of angry bees. Your boss looms over you, arms crossed, their face a mask of impatient expectation. *"Well? The deadline is in…"* They glance at their watch—*a sundial strapped to their wrist*—*"…three minutes. Chop chop."*

You look down at your desk. Instead of a computer, there’s a single, ancient typewriter, its keys sticky with what looks like honey. The project brief is written in a language you don’t recognize—symbols that shift when you blink, rearranging themselves into increasingly ominous shapes. The title reads: *"Build a Bridge to the Moon Using Only Office Supplies (and Your Tears)."*

Your coworkers are no help. One is knitting a sweater out of staples. Another is passionately arguing with the printer about existentialism. The intern—who may or may not be a sentient potted plant—whispers, *"Just start. It’ll make sense later."* (It doesn’t.)

You roll up your sleeves. The typewriter keys clack like bones. The first line you type is: *"Step 1: Convince the moon to hold still."* The paper curls into a tiny origami crane and flies out the window.

Your boss nods approvingly. *"Good. Now, about the budget—"* They pull out a ledger written in disappearing ink. *"We’ve allocated… uh… negative seven dollars. And one soul. Yours, if you’re not careful."*

Suddenly, the office walls melt into a vast, empty desert. The typewriter is now a shovel. Your boss is a mirage. The deadline is a vulture circling overhead, cawing, *"Tick-tock, tick-tock."*

You dig. The hole gets deeper. The vulture gets louder. At the bottom, you find a single, glowing USB drive labeled *"ANSWERS.exe."* You plug it into your forehead. The world goes white.

Then—

Your alarm clock blares. You’re in bed, drenched in sweat, clutching your pillow like it’s the last lifeboat on the Titanic. Your phone buzzes. It’s an email from your boss: *"Hey, just checking in on that project. Deadline’s in an hour. No pressure!"*

You scream into the void. The void screams back.

**Moral of the dream:** Your subconscious is *very* concerned about work stress. Maybe take a break. Or at least hide under your desk until the deadline passes. (In the dream, it works. In real life? Not so much.)
