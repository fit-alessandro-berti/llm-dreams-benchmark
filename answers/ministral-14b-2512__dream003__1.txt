The moment your fingers trace the cold, smooth surface of the box, the intricate patterns seem to *shift*—not under your touch, but as if reacting to your very presence. The engravings pulse faintly, like veins of light beneath the metal, forming constellations that rearrange themselves into symbols you almost recognize: a spiral within a circle, a serpent biting its own tail, and what looks like a keyhole… but upside-down, as if the key isn’t meant to *open* the box, but to *become* it.

A whisper slithers into your mind—not from outside, but from *within* the box itself. It’s not words, but a sensation: *You are the key.* The thought is both terrifying and exhilarating. You hesitate, then press your palm against the lid. The metal hums, vibrating like a plucked string, and suddenly, the box *tilts*—not in your hands, but in your *perception*. The world around you warps: the floor becomes a ceiling, the walls stretch into an endless corridor lined with identical locked boxes, each one humming with the same eerie resonance.

Then, the box *opens*—not with a click, but with a sound like a held breath being released. Inside, there is no treasure, no scroll, no golden key. Instead, you see *yourself*. But not as you are now. This version of you is standing in a vast, starless void, holding the box in their hands, their eyes wide with the same dawning horror you feel. They turn to you—and the dream *shatters*.

You wake gasping, your fingers clutching at the air where the box once was. On your nightstand, your hand rests on a small, unremarkable key—one you’ve never seen before. And when you look in the mirror, for just a second, your reflection *blinks* at you with eyes that aren’t quite yours.
