…hesitate at first, unsure why they would care about a stranger drifting through the crowd. But one of them smiles in a way that feels familiar, like you’ve known it for years.

They don’t ask many questions. One points down a quiet street glowing with warm streetlights. Another hands you a paper cup of something hot. Someone else walks beside you without speaking, matching your pace.

The city noise softens as you move together. The towering buildings seem less intimidating, the streets less tangled. At some point you realize you’re no longer trying to remember where you were supposed to go.

You’re just walking.

And strangely, that feels like the right direction.

When you finally turn to thank them, the street is empty again—just the distant hum of the city and the warmth of the cup still in your hands.

But now you know the way.
