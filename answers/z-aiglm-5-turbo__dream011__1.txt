...not written in any language I recognize. Instead, the paper is covered in shifting, iridescent ink that seems to breathe, expanding and contracting like a living thing. 

As I lean in closer, the symbols suddenly snap into focus, but they make no sense. 

*Question 1: Calculate the emotional weight of a forgotten name. Show your work.*

*Question 2: Using only the memory of a sound you heard in a dream last year, construct a staircase out of water.*

A cold sweat pricks at the back of my neck. I look around the lecture hall for help, but the other students are calmly filling in bubbles on their Scantrons with pencils that are snapping in half over and over, the broken pieces floating up toward the ceiling like reversed gravity. 

I look back at my paper. The third question has appeared, written in my own handwriting, though I don't remember writing it.

*Question 3: If you remove the color blue from your childhood, what does your future taste like?*

I reach into my backpack frantically, searching for a pen, but my fingers only close around a handful of dry, crumbling leaves. I try to write with a leaf anyway, scraping it against the paper, but it just turns to dust. 

I look up at the clock to see how much time is left, but the clock is melting down the wall like warm wax, the hands spinning in reverse. The proctor at the front of the room is no longer a person; it’s a tall, ticking metronome wearing a tweed jacket. It sways back and forth, *tick-tock, tick-tock*, growing louder and louder.

Panic sets in. My career, my life, my entire future depends on this, but I am utterly, fundamentally unequipped. I try to stand up to ask for a different test, but my legs have turned to jelly. 

I look down at the final question on the page. The ink has turned the color of a bruise.

*Question 4: You are awake. Prove it.*

I open my mouth to scream that I don't know the answer, but no sound comes out. The paper goes completely blank, the edges of my vision begin to blur into a swirling gray fog, and the ticking of the metronome suddenly becomes the ringing of an alarm clock...
