...the air grows heavy and metallic, tasting of ozone and wet earth. The emerald canopy above, which moments ago dappled the forest floor in soft, golden light, suddenly bruises into a deep, stormy violet. 

The silence is absolute—so absolute that it feels heavy, pressing against my eardrums. Then, the wind arrives. It doesn’t howl; it whispers, a thousand voices rushing through the branches, turning the leaves silver as they flip in agitation. 

I see a clearing ahead, and I run toward it, but the faster I move, the more the trees seem to lean in, their bark shifting like ancient, weathered skin. 

Just as the first drop of rain hits my cheek—not cold, but unnervingly warm, like a tear—the sky splits. But it isn't lightning that tears through the clouds. It is a streak of brilliant, impossible gold, like ink spilled on a canvas of twilight. 

I reach the center of the clearing and stop. The rain pauses mid-air, thousands of glimmering pearls suspended in the thick, humid atmosphere. The storm—that colossal, bruising presence—has vanished. In its place, the forest has transformed. Everything is glowing now; the ferns pulse with a soft, bioluminescent hum, and the ground beneath my feet feels like velvet. 

I realize then that the storm wasn't coming to destroy; it was a curtain being pulled back. Standing at the edge of the clearing, where the trees meet a horizon that shouldn't exist, is a doorway framed by two weeping willows whose branches are made of starlight. 

I take a step toward it, and as my foot touches the threshold, the dream begins to blur at the edges, dissolving into the white noise of a waking mind. I reach out to touch the light, but the air grows thin, and I feel myself falling upward, through the canopy of the stars, back into the quiet gravity of my own room.
