...encounter various groups of people who seem to know you, though you’ve never seen their faces before.

First, you drift toward a cluster of guests laughing near a fountain that seems to be flowing upward, defying gravity. As you approach, the sound of their laughter doesn't fade, but it distorts, slowing down until it sounds like the groaning of heavy metal. You tap one man on the shoulder to ask if he’s seen your friend, and he turns around smoothly—too smoothly, like a mannequin on a swivel base. He has no eyes, only smooth skin where his face should be, yet he smiles and hands you a playing card. It’s the Joker. You don’t remember taking it, but suddenly your pockets are overflowing with them.

You back away, heart hammering a rhythm that feels too loud for the quiet room. You try to navigate through the crowd, but the floor has turned into a deep, plush carpet that sinks up to your knees with every step, making walking feel like wading through syrup. 

You pass a long table where a dinner party is in progress. Everyone is eating, but their plates are empty, and they are using forks and knives to cut into shadows cast by the candlelight. You recognize one of them—an old childhood neighbor you haven't thought of in twenty years. You call out their name, but the word catches in your throat and comes out as a burst of golden confetti. The neighbor looks up, confused by the paper raining down on their dessert, and points a trembling finger toward a door at the far end of the hall that wasn't there a moment ago.

Desperate now, you run. Your legs move, but you aren't getting closer to the door; the room is stretching like elastic, keeping you pinned in the center while the walls recede into darkness. Then, amidst the sea of strangers, you spot them. Your friend. They are standing perfectly still under a spotlight, wearing a coat that belongs to you. 

You scream their name, pushing against the invisible resistance of the air. They hear you and turn. Just as their eyes meet yours, the spotlight flickers and dies. In the sudden darkness, the social gathering dissolves. The people vanish, the music stops, and you realize you aren't standing on a carpet anymore—you are lying down, paralyzed, listening to the sound of your own breathing echoing in a space that feels infinitely small.

And then, you wake up.
