You start to frantically search your pockets for any clue—a scrap of paper, a phone, anything. But your pockets are empty, save for a single origami crane folded from a receipt. As you unfold it, the ink smudges and rearranges into a map, but the streets shift like rivers, and the buildings breathe. Desperate, you look up and notice that the city's clock towers all read different times, and the shadows stretch in conflicting directions. A street musician plays a tune that sounds like your own heartbeat, and you realize the melody is actually a series of directions whispered in reverse. You follow the echo, running through alleyways that spiral into impossible geometries, until you find yourself standing before a door that flickers like a memory. You knock, and the door opens inward to reveal a room filled with clocks, each ticking in a different language—and in the center, a mirror shows you already seated at the meeting, ten minutes early, sipping tea. But when you reach out to touch the glass, the dream begins to fold in on itself, and you feel yourself waking…
