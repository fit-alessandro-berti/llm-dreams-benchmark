…and you begin to notice something strange. The puzzles are no longer just carved into the walls or set on pedestals—they seem to be woven into the very architecture of the maze itself. The walls ripple subtly when you’re not looking directly at them, and the air hums with a low, resonant frequency, as if the maze is thinking right alongside you.

You approach a new chamber. At its center floats a large, translucent orb, swirling with misty light. To pass, you must solve a riddle inscribed on the floor in glowing runes. But as you bend down to read, the runes rearrange themselves, forming a new sentence before your eyes. It now reads:

> *“I am not alive, but I grow; I don’t have lungs, but I need air; I don’t have a mouth, but water kills me. What am I?”*

You hear a soft clicking sound behind you. The entrance you came through has sealed, and the walls are slowly—almost imperceptibly—beginning to close in. The orb pulses, waiting.

What do you do?
