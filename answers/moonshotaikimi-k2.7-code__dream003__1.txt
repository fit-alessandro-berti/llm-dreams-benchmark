As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and faint lines of light that seem to breathe beneath the surface, like veins under skin or rivers seen from high above. When you tilt the box toward the moon, the designs rearrange themselves—what looked like knots become stars, and the stars slowly wheel into a constellation you almost remember from a childhood you’ve never lived.

You run your fingertips along the edges. There is no keyhole, only a warm spot where the wood—or metal, or stone—softens slightly at your touch. The box is asking you a question, not with words, but with weight: *What are you willing to let go of?*

You think of a secret you’ve kept too long, and as you exhale, the lid sighs open.

Inside, resting on velvet the color of midnight, is not gold or a weapon or a map, but a small, ordinary mirror. In it, your reflection smiles a moment before you do. And then you understand: the box was never locked. It was only waiting for you to recognize who already had the key.
