"Just need a rough draft," they say, but the voice is actually coming from the air conditioning vent. "In triplicate. In invisible ink. By yesterday."

A giant, glowing clock materializes on the wall behind them. Instead of numbers, the clock face features pictures of your childhood pets, and the second hand is spinning violently backward. Panic rises in your chest, heavy and bright blue. You push your rolling chair back to stand up and run, but your legs feel like they are moving through deep water. 

Suddenly, the floor of the office gives way. But you don't fall. Instead, you begin to float gently upward. 

The gray cubicle walls melt away, transforming into a field of tall, silver grass. You look down at your hands and realize they are made of folded origami paper. You look back at the project file resting on your floating desk. The dense, intimidating text has magically rearranged itself into a simple recipe for chocolate chip cookies. 

You smile, the workplace anxiety instantly evaporating. You turn back to look at your boss, who has shrunk down and is now just a friendly golden retriever wearing a silk tie. You reach out to pet its head, the last of the office dissolves into a warm summer breeze, and you find yourself walking down a sunlit dirt path with absolutely nowhere to be.
