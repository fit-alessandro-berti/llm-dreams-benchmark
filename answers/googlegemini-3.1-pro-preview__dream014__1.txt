The strangers let go of my hand and smile. I suddenly recognize their faces—they are people I have never met, yet I know them perfectly. They are the personifications of every kind thought I’ve ever had, and every quiet hope I’ve ever held onto. 

They point toward a spiraling wooden staircase in the center of the room. "Up there," the tallest one says, and this time, the words sound like a distant, echoing wind chime. "That is where you are."

As I place my foot on the first step, the strangers dissolve into a flock of glowing, paper cranes that circle me once before fluttering up into the rafters. The anxiety of being lost completely vanishes. I am no longer trying to find my way through a city; I am just exploring a quiet corner of my own mind. I walk up the stairs, feeling perfectly awake, perfectly asleep, and finally, deeply at home.
