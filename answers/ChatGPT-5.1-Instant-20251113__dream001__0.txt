A soft breeze carries the hum of distant music, and for a moment it feels like the whole city is breathing with you.

You check your pockets for a clue. Instead of a phone, you find a small, warm compass. Its needle spins lazily before settling on a direction that doesn’t correspond to any street—pointing instead between two buildings, toward a narrow alley lit by a strangely golden glow.

As you step into the alley, the city noise fades. The walls stretch upward into darkness, and the ground beneath your feet becomes smooth and reflective, like you’re walking on the surface of a quiet lake. Far ahead, you see a figure waiting—just a silhouette, but somehow you know they’re connected to your meeting.

You hurry forward, and as you get closer, the figure lifts a hand in greeting. Their voice echoes softly, as though spoken inside your mind: *“You’re right on time. The meeting isn’t a place—it’s a choice.”*
