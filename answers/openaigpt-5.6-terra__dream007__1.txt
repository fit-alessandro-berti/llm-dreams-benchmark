…leave before either of you says something that cannot be taken back.

You step outside into a street you do not recognize. It is nighttime, but the sky is bright with slow-moving clouds, lit from within like paper lanterns. Your friend’s voice follows you through the closed door, muffled and indistinct.

You walk faster.

At the end of the street, there is a train station where there should be a park. A silver train waits with all its doors open. No one is inside except your friend, sitting by the window in the last carriage.

You hesitate.

Then the train begins to move without you.

You run alongside it, calling their name, but your voice makes no sound. Your friend presses a hand against the glass. You press yours against the outside of the window as the train gathers speed.

For a moment, your hands line up perfectly.

Then the train disappears into a tunnel beneath the city.

You are left holding something small and warm in your palm: the words you meant to say, folded into the shape of a bird. It flutters once, then flies toward the tunnel’s dark mouth.

You wake before you find out whether it reaches them.
