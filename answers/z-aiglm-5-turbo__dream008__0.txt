...open your laptop, but the keys are all in the wrong places. The letters keep rearranging themselves like insects scattering from light. You try to type anyway, but your fingers pass through the keyboard like it's made of smoke.

Your boss is standing impossibly close now, breathing loudly. You notice their face has no eyes, just smooth skin where eyes should be, but they seem to be staring at you anyway.

"You're already behind," they say. But their voice is coming from the fluorescent light above you.

You look at the deadline on the paper: **YESTERDAY.**

The clock on the wall has no numbers. The hands are spinning backward. You feel that familiar dream panic rising in your chest—that suffocating weight of something you can't name.

Then you notice your coworkers. They're all at their desks, typing calmly, but their hands are moving too fast. Blurring. And slowly you realize they're not typing at all—their fingers are just vibrating, and no one seems to notice.

You stand up to say something, and your legs don't work right. Too heavy. Like walking through wet concrete.

But then—

Something shifts. A small, quiet thought surfaces from somewhere deep:

*Wait. I don't work here anymore.*

And just like that, the walls become thin. Translucent. You can see a sky behind them that shouldn't exist inside an office building—a sunset in impossible colors, violet and gold and something that doesn't have a name.

Your boss opens their mouth to speak again, but no sound comes out. Their whole body flickers, like a bad projection.

You take a step forward, and this time your foot goes *through* the floor.

You're falling. But it doesn't feel like falling. It feels like waking up.

The office dissolves around you in strips of paper and fluorescent light, and beneath you there is only warm air and the smell of rain on dirt, and you realize you've been holding your breath for what feels like years—

—and you let it go.

And the dream, finally, lets you go too.
