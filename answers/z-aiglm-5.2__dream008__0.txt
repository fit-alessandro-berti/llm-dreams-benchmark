...you nod, though your mouth feels full of cotton. You turn to your desk, but it's not your desk anymore. It's a folding table, the kind from church potlucks, and your computer is a typewriter — no, a rotary phone — no, both, stacked unevenly on a surface that keeps shifting like a raft on water.

Your boss is still watching. You can feel it. But when you glance up, he's not at his office door anymore. He's sitting in a dentist's chair in the corner of the room, reading a newspaper from 1987, and he doesn't look up but he says, clear as anything: "The deadline is still Friday."

You don't know what Friday means here. The clock on the wall has thirteen hours and all the hands are spinning in different directions.

You open the project folder. Inside it is a single photograph: you, from behind, walking down a hallway that looks like the one you're in. You turn around. The hallway behind you stretches much farther than it should, and at the very end, there's a small figure standing very still.

You know, the way you know things in dreams, that the figure is you, and that it has already finished the project, and that it is waiting for *you* to catch up. Or maybe it failed. You can't tell from here.

You start walking.

The fluorescent lights above hum a note you almost recognize — a song from childhood, maybe, or something your mother used to sing. You can't place it, and the harder you try, the more it sounds like the hum of the copy machine, which is running, though no one is standing at it.

Pages are coming out, warm and curling. You pick one up. It's blank. You pick up another. Blank. The third page has one line typed at the top:

*"You already know what happens next."*

You put it down.

Your phone rings — the rotary phone, not your cell phone. You pick up the receiver and hear your own voice, calm and unhurried, say:

"Don't worry. You'll wake up before it's due."

Then the line goes dead, and you notice the hallway has gotten shorter. The figure at the end is closer now. You can almost see its face.

You keep walking.
