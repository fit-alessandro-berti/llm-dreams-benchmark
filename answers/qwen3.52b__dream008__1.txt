Your fingers hover over the trackpad, too stiff against the screen, like they've been carved from bone. You don't hear yourself breathing—only the hum of an electric fan spinning outside the frame of consciousness. Your boss is sitting across from you in the reflection of the window, but their silhouette is slightly melted, as if made of glass and cold air.

"You're not thinking about this, are you?" the voice echoes in your mind, a ghostly cadence that matches yours. "Or is it just time running out on itself?"

You try to move your hands, but gravity pulls them gently down. They don't respond, just as if no one else has ever seen the world from their point of view without the eyes or the mind. You feel a sudden urge to scream, but there are no walls left, and maybe that's why you can't speak. The air in the room feels heavier than it did before, pressurized with expectations like water under tires, pressing on your lungs.

You blink, and suddenly you realize you're not standing at your desk anymore. You are standing inside your dreamscape as a dream character from a novel you didn't write, and the project deadline is now a physical weight hanging from your ceiling like a blackened lantern. You don't know if there's time left to finish work or if the boss has simply become the only one who knows how the clock actually works right now.

You stare at the blinking cursor, which in this reality refuses to move across an empty space. The deadline feels less like a date on paper and more like a wound in your skin that won't heal. Do you tell them to stop? Or do you wait for the silence to take over completely, wondering if the next dream will feel any lighter or heavier than the first one.
