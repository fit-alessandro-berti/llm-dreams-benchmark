...stand firm at the helm, gripping the wheel as rain lashes your face. The wind howls like something alive, something angry, and the ship groans beneath your boots.

"Secure the mainsail!" you roar over the thunder, and your first mate, a woman with salt-white hair and eyes like flint, nods and disappears into the chaos.

Lightning splits the sky, and for one blinding instant, you see it — something *massive* beneath the waves. A shape. A shadow that stretches longer than the ship itself, moving against the current.

Your stomach drops, but you don't look away. You've seen things in these waters before. Things that don't have names.

The shadow vanishes. The storm *screams* louder, as if offended.

Then — stillness.

Not calm. Not peace. Just... a held breath. The sea turns to glass, black and mirror-smooth, reflecting a sky full of stars that shouldn't exist. Constellations you don't recognize. Colors that have no name.

Your crew freezes. No one speaks. No one breathes.

And then, from somewhere deep below, a sound. Low. Resonant. Not mechanical, not natural. Something *else*.

It sounds almost like a voice.

It sounds almost like your name.

You lean over the railing, staring into the impossible black water, and you see your reflection staring back — only it's *smiling*, and you are not.

The reflection raises one finger to its lips.

*Shhhh.*

You wake up.
