Instead of splashing, the color begins to bloom like a time-lapse flower, weaving itself into intricate, fractal patterns that hum a low, melodic chord.

I find that I don't need a brush. By simply thinking of a memory, the canvas pulls the image from my mind. I think of a childhood home, and a house constructed of amber light and peppermint swirls rises from the surface, three-dimensional and breathing. I reach into the painting, and my hand doesn't hit a flat surface; it sinks into the warmth of the amber, pulling me forward.

Suddenly, the canvas expands, swallowing the horizon. The shifting landscape around me stabilizes, anchoring itself to the images I am creating. The chaotic geometry of the world settles into a lush, velvet forest where the leaves are made of whispered secrets and the river flows with liquid silver.

But as I step fully into my own creation, I look back and see that the blank canvas is still there, floating behind me. On its surface, there is now a painting of *me*—a small, curious figure standing in a forest of velvet—and the figure in the painting is slowly turning around to look at me, smiling with a knowledge I haven't yet remembered.

Just as our eyes meet, the silver river rises up to meet the sky, and the world dissolves into a soft, white mist, tasting of ozone and old books, until the colors fade into the dim light of the waking world.
