…and the air splits open—not with thunder, but with a sound like glass being unbroken. The trees don’t bend; they *remember*, their bark peeling back to reveal veins of silver light pulsing beneath. Rain falls upward, each drop a tiny mirror showing a face you almost recognize. You feel the storm not on your skin, but inside your ribs, rearranging something.

The path dissolves into moss that breathes. You are no longer walking; you are being *read* by the forest, line by line, as lightning (which is blue and silent) writes a door in the air. You step through it without deciding to.

On the other side, the weather is the color of old music. The storm follows you, gentle now, like a hand on your shoulder, and you understand—without words—that it was never approaching. It was always inside you, waiting for the forest to become quiet enough to hear it.

Then the dream softens at the edges, and you wake just enough to feel the rain still falling upward, somewhere.
