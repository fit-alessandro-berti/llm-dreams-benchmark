*“What is the weight of a promise broken in 1998?”* the first question asks.

You blink, confused. You didn’t study dates or weights. You studied algorithms and market trends. But as you reach for your pen, it transforms into a key—a heavy, iron key that feels cold against your palm. You realize the exam hall is no longer a sterile classroom. The rows of desks stretch out infinitely into a misty forest, and the other students are faceless silhouettes, scribbling furiously on their own glowing pages.

A proctor walks by. It’s you, but older, wearing a suit made of shadows. They pause beside your desk, their eyes hollow. “Don’t think,” they whisper, their voice sounding like wind through dry leaves. “Remember.”

You look back at the test. The next question has formed: *“If you could trade one year of your life for certainty, which year would you sacrifice, and what would you lose along with it?”*

Your heart hammers against your ribs. You know this isn’t about career placement anymore. This is an audit of your soul. You dip the key-pen into the puddle of ink spreading across the desk, ready to write an answer you’re afraid you already know.
