You feel both exhausted and exhilarated. The deeper you go, the more the maze seems to respond to your thoughts, humming faintly when you have an insight, dimming when you hesitate. The air smells faintly of chalk and old books, and somewhere far ahead, you can hear a soft melody, like someone humming a lullaby just out of earshot.

There is no fear here, only the curious pull of the next challenge. The walls glow faintly with bioluminescent patterns, and you realize that you're not just solving puzzles — you are piecing together parts of yourself. Each answer seems to unlock a memory, a feeling, a forgotten dream. The maze isn't trying to trap you; it's guiding you to something.

Eventually, you reach a vast open space at its heart. In the center stands a mirror. When you look into it, you see yourself as you are, but also as you were and could be, all at once. The last puzzle is simple: step through. You take a breath, smile, and move forward, feeling the cool glass give way as you awaken with a sense of clarity and quiet triumph.
