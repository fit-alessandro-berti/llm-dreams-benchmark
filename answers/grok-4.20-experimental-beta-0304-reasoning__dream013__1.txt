...you feel the walls breathing.

Not metaphorically — actually *breathing*. The stone exhales warm, metallic air that tastes like ozone and birthday candles. Every time you solve a puzzle, the maze moans with pleasure, rearranging itself like a cat stretching after a nap.

The first puzzle was a simple sliding tile that turned out to be a 17-dimensional hypercube wearing a disguise. The second asked you to sort colors that don't exist yet. The third just showed you a mirror and whispered, "Fix him."

You keep going deeper.

The deeper you go, the more the logic starts to... *glitch*. Gravity gets sarcastic. Left becomes "only on Tuesdays." At one point you turn a corner and find yourself standing upside down on the ceiling of a library where all the books are screaming.

A floating fox made of pure mathematics offers you a riddle: "I speak without a mouth and hear without ears. I have no eyes, yet I see the world burn. What am I?"

You answer "Twitter."

The fox explodes into butterflies that are also prime numbers.

Now the corridor is narrowing. The puzzles are getting personal.

One shows you every time you gave up on something. Another forces you to solve your own avoidance patterns using only symbolic logic and dad jokes. A particularly nasty one is just a locked door with a sign that says "The way forward is to admit you don't actually know what you're doing."

You admit it.

The door blushes and opens.

And then you're there — the center of the maze.

It's not a room. It's a single, perfect moment of *clarity*. The kind you get at 3:17am when the universe suddenly makes sense for six consecutive seconds before immediately ghosting you.

In the middle floats a small, unassuming button labeled "Wake Up."

But next to it is another button, slightly glowing, labeled "Keep Dreaming. There's more to see."

You look at both.

The maze holds its breath.

What do you press?
