You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to blend in quickly to survive. The air smells of woodsmoke and damp earth, and the cobblestone streets echo with the clip-clop of horse-drawn carts. Towering stone walls rise around you, adorned with banners fluttering in the breeze—it's medieval Europe, perhaps the bustling heart of a 14th-century market town.

Your modern clothes draw suspicious glances from passersby in woolen tunics and leather boots, so you duck into an alley, heart pounding. Spotting a discarded cloak on a laundry line, you snatch it and drape it over your shoulders, pulling the hood low. Hunger gnaws at you now; the scent of fresh-baked bread wafts from a nearby stall. With no wallet or coins, you barter a shiny button from your shirt for a loaf, earning a wary nod from the baker.

As the sun climbs higher, you wander toward the town square, where a crowd gathers around a herald announcing a quest: a dragon sighted in the nearby hills, terrorizing villages. Whispers of knights and treasure fill the air. Do you join the adventurers, seeking a way back home through some ancient magic? Or do you hide, learning the ways of this world until you can unravel the dream's mystery?

Suddenly, the ground trembles—a roar echoes from the distance. The dream shifts, and you jolt awake in your bed, the echo of that dragon's cry lingering in your mind. What an adventure.
