You walk into your office, the hum of the air conditioning vibrating through the floorboards as you pull up onto a chair. Your boss is standing by the window, adjusting his tie, looking at something he hasn't noticed for a long time. He's holding the same kind of object, like a piece of scrap metal or a framed blueprint that was lost in the storm.  

You look around, feeling the cold draft outside against your own shoulders as you adjust your posture. The deadline feels distant because it’s now past a year ago, even though your project is due next week. It’s a time for change, not just work. You start to wonder if there’s something wrong with the environment, or perhaps a memory that shouldn’t exist.  

Your boss doesn't answer immediately though. He turns around and says something you couldn't quite hear clearly. "It's not about me," he says, his voice steady despite the lack of sound. "It's about how I fit into this system."
