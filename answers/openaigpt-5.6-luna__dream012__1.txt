You are the captain of a ship in the middle of a turbulent sea. Your crew looks to you for guidance as the storm worsens, and you realize the compass has stopped pointing north.

Lightning splits the sky, revealing—far beyond the waves—a towering lighthouse where no island should be. You order the sails lowered and the ship turned toward its impossible light. The crew hesitates, but you take the wheel yourself.

As the ship climbs a wave higher than any mountain, the ocean suddenly falls silent. The storm vanishes. Beneath the glassy water, stars drift like fish, and the lighthouse door opens onto a sunlit room from your childhood.

Inside, someone is waiting for you.

They hand you the compass. Its needle now points not north, but home.
