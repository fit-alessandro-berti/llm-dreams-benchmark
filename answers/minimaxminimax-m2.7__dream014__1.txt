# The Dream Continues...

You accept their help, and they guide you through winding streets that seem to shift like puzzle pieces beneath your feet. Their faces are warm but strangely familiar—like people you've met in passing, yet can't quite place.

One stranger hands you a small brass compass that spins wildly, pointing in every direction at once. "The city reads hearts," she whispers. "Just follow what feels true."

The streets grow quieter. Neon signs flicker in languages you almost understand. A melody drifts from somewhere—distant and haunting—pulling you deeper into forgotten alleys that smell of rain and cinnamon.

One stranger walks beside you in comfortable silence. Another points out a courtyard where fireflies glow despite the daylight. A third crouches to show you a cat sleeping on a windowsill, its breathing synchronized with the city's heartbeat.

They don't lead you to a destination. Instead, they lead you to a bridge overlooking still water. "You've always known the way," one says, squeezing your shoulder. "You just needed reminding."

The strangers begin to fade like morning mist, but their warmth lingers. You wake slowly, the echo of that compass spinning in your chest.

You open your eyes. The ceiling above you is unfamiliar... but somehow, that feels okay.
