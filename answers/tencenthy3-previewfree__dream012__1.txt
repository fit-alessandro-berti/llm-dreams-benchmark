"Hard to starboard!" you bellow, gripping the wheel until your own bones ache. "Ride the trough! Thomas, cut the jib if it tears! We need balance, not speed!"

The crew moves not with panic, but with a desperate, coordinated rhythm. You see your First Mate, Elara, tying a thick line around her waist before leaping into the rigging to save a young deckhand who lost his footing.

The sea tries to swallow you whole, but you hold the course. You aren't just fighting the water; you are fighting the chaos. You look up at the tattered flags whipping in the gale and see a break in the clouds—a sliver of gray light cutting through the black.

"Look alive!" you scream, pointing toward the horizon. "The worst is passing! Keep her steady! We are the *Iron Will*, and we do not sink!"

You feel the wheel respond, the ship finding its rhythm again against the swell, proving that as long as the captain stands, the ship still sails.
