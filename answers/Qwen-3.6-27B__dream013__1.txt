The air grows thinner, humming with a low-frequency vibration that resonates in your teeth. The puzzles themselves begin to change. To open a gate, you must untangle a knot made of whispered secrets from people you’ve forgotten.

As you reach the center of the maze, the structure collapses inward, not into darkness, but into blinding white light. There is no final puzzle here, only a single, floating question mark made of starlight. It pulses gently, waiting for an answer that isn’t logical, but emotional. You realize that the maze wasn’t testing your intellect, but your willingness to let go of control.

You take a breath, close your eyes, and instead of trying to solve it, you simply smile. The question mark dissolves into a flock of paper cranes that swirl around you, carrying you upward, out of the maze, and back toward the surface of your waking mind.
