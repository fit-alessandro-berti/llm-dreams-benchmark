…steady your feet against the tilting deck and taste salt in the wind. The lantern above the helm swings like a slow, nervous pendulum, painting your crew in flashes of gold and shadow. Every pair of eyes is on you.

“Hold her into the waves!” you shout, though the storm nearly steals the words from your mouth.

The sea rises like a wall, black and roaring. For a moment you feel the ship hesitate beneath you—wood groaning, sails snapping like thunder. Then you remember something strange: you know this sea. Not as a sailor knows it, but as a dreamer does.

You place your hand on the wheel.

The storm pauses.

Not stops—just listens.

The waves curl around the hull instead of over it. The wind loosens its grip. Your crew stares as the clouds above begin to twist, slowly opening like a curtain. Through the gap appears a sky that is far too bright for night, scattered with unfamiliar stars.

You realize the ship isn’t just sailing forward.

It’s sailing upward.

The ocean falls away beneath you, turning to mist, and the sails fill with a silent wind from somewhere beyond the sky. Your crew begins to laugh—half terrified, half amazed—as the ship climbs into the stars.

And you, still at the wheel, have the quiet certainty that wherever this sea leads, you are meant to steer it.
