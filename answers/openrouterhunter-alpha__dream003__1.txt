The patterns seem to shift as you stare — not moving exactly, but *reorganizing*, the way stars rearrange themselves when you look away and look back. You press your thumb against a groove along the lid and feel something warm pulse back against your skin, like a heartbeat you didn't know the box had.

You realize the patterns aren't decoration. They're instructions.

Each curling line traces a path your eyes follow involuntarily, and as they do, sounds arrive — not from outside but from somewhere behind your own thoughts. A low hum. Then a voice, neither male nor female, speaking in a language you've never heard but somehow understand perfectly:

*"The box does not need a key. It needs a question."*

You look down. The box has changed. Where there was a seam between lid and body, there is now a mouth — not a literal mouth, but an opening that breathes, dark and deep, exhaling the scent of old libraries and distant rain. Inside, you can see light. Not the bright, flat light of a room, but something layered and moving, like sunlight trapped underwater.

You lean closer.

Inside the box is another box. Smaller. Same material. Same shifting patterns. And inside that one, if you squint, you can see another, and another, smaller and smaller, each nested inside the last like a question folded inside a question folded inside a question.

The voice returns, quieter now:

*"You already know what's at the center. You've always known. The box is just making you walk the distance to remember."*

Your hands are trembling, but not from fear. From recognition. The smallest box — the one at the very center of all the others — is exactly the size of something you lost a long time ago. Something you stopped looking for. Something that, until this exact moment, you had forgotten you ever had.

You reach in.

The light swallows your arm up to the elbow. It's warm. It's the temperature of skin.

And just as your fingers close around the smallest box, you hear your own voice — from somewhere far away, somewhere above — whispering:

*"Not yet. Wake up first. You have to earn this one while you're awake."*

---

The box dissolves. The dream folds shut like a book closing. And you wake with the feeling of something small and warm pressed against your palm — but when you open your hand, there's nothing there.

Just the memory of a shape.

And a question you can't quite remember asking.
