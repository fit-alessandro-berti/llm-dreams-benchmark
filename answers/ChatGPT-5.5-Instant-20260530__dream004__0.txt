You are in a world where colors and shapes are constantly changing. As you explore this surreal landscape, you come across a blank canvas and feel an irresistible urge to touch it.

The moment your fingertips brush its surface, a streak of deep blue appears, then transforms into a flock of glowing birds. They rise from the canvas and circle overhead, leaving trails of gold that twist into impossible geometric patterns. Mountains fold into oceans. Trees bloom into clocks. The sky becomes a mosaic of shifting faces that smile as they drift past.

Curious, you paint a doorway onto the canvas. Before the paint dries, the doorway opens. Beyond it lies another landscape—one that seems strangely familiar. You step through and find yourself walking through memories you don't quite recognize: a childhood home that never existed, friends whose names you somehow know, and streets that bend gently upward into the stars.

In the center of this dreamlike world stands an enormous easel. On it is a painting of yourself, except the figure is still unfinished. Each choice you make adds a new detail to the portrait. When you laugh, the painted figure gains bright eyes. When you wonder, stars appear in its hair. When you hesitate, entire sections dissolve into mist.

As you approach, the portrait suddenly turns and looks back at you.

"At last," it says softly. "I've been waiting for you to finish the dream."

Before you can answer, the changing colors around you begin to slow. The shifting shapes settle into a single image: the blank canvas where everything began.

Only now it is no longer blank.

It shows you standing before it, reaching out to touch the surface, beginning the dream all over again. And just as you realize you are looking at the very first moment of your journey, the canvas ripples like water, pulling you gently forward—

—and you wake up with the strange feeling that somewhere, in some other dream, the painting is still waiting for its final brushstroke.
