...and you decide to **step through the wall**, because in this dream the anger has turned the drywall into thin paper.

You push through into a room that is both your childhood kitchen and a train station at midnight. Your friend is already there, but they are also you—wearing your clothes from ten years ago. The argument doesn't stop; it transforms into music, a low cello note that makes the floorboards vibrate. You realize you're not fighting about the original thing at all. You're fighting because you're both afraid of waking up and forgetting this version of each other.

So you sit down on the floor, and the disagreement becomes a shared map—drawn in coffee rings and starlight—showing all the places you've been kind to one another when no one else was watching. The heat doesn't vanish; it turns into warmth. You don't "win." Instead, you both reach out and hold hands across the table, and your palms merge like water, and for a moment you understand exactly what the other person means, not in words, but in color: a deep, safe blue.

Then the ceiling opens, not with a crash, but like an eyelid, and you wake up—not resolved, exactly, but carrying the echo of that blue.
