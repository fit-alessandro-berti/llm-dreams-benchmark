...written in a language you forgot you knew, where the letters don't move across the page—they move within you.

The room, originally a sterile white exam hall with the smell of dust and eraser shavings, begins to soften. The fluorescent lights hum a low, melodic chord that vibrates in your teeth. You look down at the paper in your hand. It feels warm, almost like skin. The first question asks: *"What is the cost of your silence?"*

You reach for your pencil, but it has no eraser. You realize then that there is no going back, no second chances to fix a mistake in this room. The next question reads: *"Who told you that you must choose only one path?"*

Across the aisle, the other students are no longer people. They are silhouettes made of smoke, their papers blank because they are afraid to write. You look back at the proctor at the front of the room, but he doesn't have a face; he only has a mirror reflecting your own back.

You feel a sudden rush of clarity. You aren't here to demonstrate what you know. You are here to remember who you are. The ink on the page begins to flow like water, pooling at the bottom of the hall and forming a river. The exam paper dissolves into a flock of white moths that flutter up toward the ceiling, which is no longer a ceiling, but the surface of a vast, dark ocean.

The questions stop being words and become sensations: The heat of a summer you remember from childhood, the ache in a limb you don't have anymore, the taste of an apple you ate today that felt like yesterday.

You realize the final exam isn't a test of your career. It is a test of your capacity to let go. You stop trying to write. Instead, you close your eyes and breathe.

*Thump.*

You wake up in your bed, the morning light hitting your face. The clock reads 7:00 AM. Your heart is still racing, but the exam hall is gone. You reach out and touch the side of your face, certain the ink is still wet, until you realize the only thing there is the morning dew on your skin from the dream.

You take a deep breath. The career choice is still ahead of you, but for the first time, you feel like you are already qualified, not because you know the answers, but because you know how to ask the questions.

And the room fades back to your bedroom, just as the sun fully rises.
