You begin to speak, but no sound comes out.

At first, you try again—louder this time—but the audience only leans forward, listening to the silence. Then you notice that everyone in the crowd is wearing your face. Some look young, some old, some frightened, some amused.

A single spotlight shifts to the back of the room, where a small door has appeared. Through it comes the sound of waves.

You realize the speech is already being delivered—not by you, but by the ocean. Each breaking wave speaks a sentence you have forgotten, and the crowd applauds whenever one reaches the shore.

You step away from the microphone and open the door. On the other side is your childhood bedroom, flooded with moonlight. Your younger self sits on the bed, holding a note.

“What does it say?” you ask.

He looks up and smiles.

“It says you don’t have to perform anymore.”

Then the audience disappears, the stage dissolves beneath your feet, and you wake just before touching the water.
