### **The Invitation**
You wake to a knock on your door. It’s not your landlord or a delivery—it’s a figure in a tailored but slightly *wrong* suit (the lapels are stitched with silver thread that glows faintly when you blink). They hand you an envelope. Inside is a single sheet of paper, written in ink that shifts color as you read:

> *"You are invited to the **Ascension Trials**, a competition where the grand prize is not gold or glory, but the *right to embody* your dream job for one year—with all its duties, privileges, and… *unintended consequences*.*
> *The rules are simple: Survive the challenges. Prove you are worthy of the role. Fail, and you wake up as you were before.*
> *But here’s the catch: The job you win isn’t just a title. It’s a *version of you*—one that carries the weight of its expectations. Will you rise to the challenge?*
> *The first trial begins at dawn."*

You don’t remember signing up. But the ink on the paper *burns* when you touch it.

### **The First Trial: The Mirror of Intentions**
The competition takes place in a grand, ever-shifting hall—like a museum of careers, but alive. The walls pulse with portraits of people in their dream jobs: a surgeon’s hands steady as they stitch air, a poet’s quill writes a sonnet that rewrites itself, a CEO’s smile holds the weight of a thousand unspoken deals.

Your first challenge: **Step into the Mirror of Intentions**, a vast, warped looking glass that shows not your reflection, but the *essence* of the job you’ve chosen. For you, it’s a **child psychologist**—but the mirror doesn’t show a kind face. It shows *you*, holding a child who isn’t yours, whispering secrets you don’t remember making. The child’s eyes are hollow. The mirror whispers: *"Do you really want to fix what isn’t broken?"*

### **The Second Trial: The Office of Echoes**
You pass. The hall dissolves into a modern office building—except every door is labeled with a job title, and the walls hum with the voices of people who’ve failed before you. You enter **"The Office of Echoes"**, a room where every employee is a *version of you*, but trapped in roles you’ve never wanted:
- One you is a **salesperson** who can’t stop lying, their smile stretched too wide.
- Another is a **teacher** drowning in paperwork, their chalk breaking into dust.
- A third is a **chef** who burns every dish, their hands blackened with soot.

A figure in a lab coat (your future self? a judge?) hands you a clipboard. *"To advance, you must help one of them. But be warned: fixing them may change you."*

### **The Third Trial: The Job Interview That Never Ends**
You’re in a glass booth, being interviewed by a panel of **your past selves**—the ones who doubted you, the ones who succeeded, the ones who quit. The questions repeat, but the answers change:
- *"Why should we trust you with this job?"* → *"Because I’ve failed before."* → *"Because I’ve never failed at all."*
- *"What’s your greatest weakness?"* → *"I’m too kind."* → *"I’m too hard on myself."*
- *"What do you want from this?"* → *"To prove I’m good enough."* → *"To stop trying to be good enough."*

The panel’s faces flicker like a broken TV. One of them leans in and says, *"You’re not here to *get* the job. You’re here to *become* it."* Then the screen glitches—you see yourself in the job, but it’s *wrong*. You’re a psychologist who never listens. A CEO who fires everyone. A poet who can’t write a single line.

**What do you do?**
- **Break the fourth wall**, addressing the dream itself: *"I don’t want to be perfect. I just want to try."*
- **Let the glitches happen**, trusting that the "wrong" version will teach you something.
- **Refuse the job entirely**, walking out of the booth—only to realize you’re already too late.

*(The dream doesn’t end. It *rearranges*. Maybe the job you win isn’t the one you picked.)*

### **The Final Trial: The Weight of the Role**
You stand before a grand throne (or a desk, or a stage) where the job you’ve chosen *awaits you*. But it’s not a title. It’s a **costume**. A set of expectations so heavy they could crush you. The dream whispers:

**What do you do?**
