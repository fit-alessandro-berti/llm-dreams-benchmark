...grip the salt-slick wheel until your knuckles match the whitecaps. The wood groans beneath your palms—an old sound, a tired sound, like your father's knees when he stood from the pew.

"Steady!" you bellow, but the wind swallows your voice whole.

The *Marauder* lists hard to starboard. A crate of salt pork breaks loose belowdecks, sliding across the hold with a sound like thunder's slower cousin. Your first mate, Elias—gray beard braided with blue glass beads, one eye milky from the cataract the ship's surgeon couldn't touch—meets your gaze from the fo'c'sle. He doesn't need orders. He's already moving, his men falling into rhythm behind him like they've been choreographed by the sea itself.

*Reef the mains'l. Batten the hatches. Secure the longboat.*

The lightning splits the sky open, and for a heartbeat you see it: not waves, but *mountains* of water, each one crowned with foam like a king's circlet. The trough between them yawns black and hungry.

Your compass spins lazy circles. The stars are gone. The horizon is a myth.

Something tugs at your memory—your mother's voice, calling you to supper through the screen door of a house that hasn't existed in twenty years. The smell of cornbread and lard. The sound of crickets in dry grass.

*Wake up,* she says.

The wheel shudders. The ship rights itself, just enough. The wind shifts—*there*, a pocket of clarity—and you catch the scent of land on it. Wet stone. Pine. Something flowering, impossibly, in the dark.

Elias grins, teeth yellow in the flash. "Knew you had it in you, Captain."

But you're already walking away from the wheel, belowdecks, toward a door that wasn't there yesterday. It's painted blue. Your mother's favorite color.

The storm roars behind you like a beast denied its meal.

You reach for the handle.
