...find the one who is waiting for you to remember.

The air here is thick with the scent of wet wool and woodsmoke, a stark contrast to the sterile, recycled air of your life before. The sky is a bruised purple, the sun struggling to break through clouds that look like spilled ink. There are no cars, no phones, no electric hum. Just the sound of horses' hooves on cobblestones and the distant ringing of iron bells.

You clutch a heavy wool coat that isn't yours, feeling its rough texture against your skin. The world around you is built of stone and timber, yet there are no wires cutting across the sky. It feels ancient, yet the faces of the people passing you are familiar, blurred like photographs left in the sun. You walk through a marketplace where vendors sell jars of glowing sap and bundles of feathers that change color when touched.

You realize you need to find the one who is waiting for you to remember.

You follow a trail of blue ribbons tied to the trees, winding toward a hill where a single white tower stands in the center of a vast field. It is quiet here, too quiet. The wind doesn't rustle the leaves; it holds its breath. At the top of the hill, there is no door, just a circular stone floor with a hole in the middle that looks like a mirror.

Standing before it is a figure, but it has no face, only the suggestion of shoulders and arms. You know, without thinking, that you cannot speak. Words are too heavy here. You must offer a memory instead.

You close your eyes and think of the smell of rain on hot asphalt, the sound of a notification chime, the warmth of a car engine. But the figure shakes its head. The dream is rejecting your modern noise.

You close your eyes again. This time, you think of the feeling of your own hand resting on a sleeping cat’s head. You think of the silence of a library, the dust motes dancing in a single shaft of light. This is the language of this place.

The figure turns. Its head fills with stars. It reaches out a hand, and you take it. The white tower dissolves into water, and you are floating. The ground falls away, not down, but *away*.

You are still in the dream, but the ground beneath your feet changes from grass to the soft fabric of your bedroom comforter. The cold air of the past era vanishes, replaced by the warm, stale heat of your room. Your eyes snap open. The sun is still setting, casting long orange shadows across the walls. You are home.

But as you reach out to touch the wall, your fingers brush against something that isn't there: a tiny, woven blue ribbon tied around the corner of your nightstand. And for a moment, just a moment, you hear the distant ringing of an iron bell.
