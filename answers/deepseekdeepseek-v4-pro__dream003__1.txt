The patterns shift and reweave themselves into words in a language you’ve never learned but somehow understand: “What you seek lies not in the opening, but in the becoming.”

Before you can pull your hand away, the wood—if it is wood—grows warm, breathing. The lock dissolves into a cluster of tiny silver moths that flutter once around your head and vanish. The lid swings open on silent hinges, but instead of objects, a staircase unfolds downward into the dark, each step humming a different note of a forgotten lullaby. You descend, and the box closes gently above you, not to trap but to give you the dark you need to see by. At the bottom, there is a mirror with no frame, reflecting not your face but the moment you first wanted to be someone else. And the dream shivers, waiting to see what you will do with that.
