The strangers, who were wearing sharp suits and hurried expressions a moment ago, are now draped in robes woven from starlight and shifting shadows. One of the strangers reaches out, their hand warm and steady, and presses a small, cold key made of polished obsidian into my palm.

"You weren't lost because you didn't know the way," they whisper, their voice sounding like leaves skittering across stone. "You were lost because you were waiting for someone else to hold the map."

I look down at the key, and as I tighten my grip, the ground beneath us begins to tilt. The city around me falls away like a velvet curtain, revealing a vast, quiet horizon where the sky and the sea have finally merged into one. I realize then that I am not just finding my way home; I am slowly waking up, and the city was only the threshold I had to cross to remember who I was before the dream began.
