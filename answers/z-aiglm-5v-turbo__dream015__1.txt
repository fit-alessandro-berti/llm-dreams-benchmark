Despite the hardships, you decide to **climb to the summit of the highest crumbling skyscraper to plant a garden in the clouds.**

It makes no sense, logically—the soil up there is just concrete dust and pulverized glass, and the wind is strong enough to peel the skin from your bones. But in the logic of dreams, this feels like the only thing that matters. You spend days—perhaps years, time moves strangely here—haunting the ruins below. You scavenge not for canned food or weapons, but for bulbs that look like sleeping hearts, for seeds that rattle with promise inside desiccated pods, and for jars of rainwater collected in the gutters of fallen statues.

The climb itself is a struggle against gravity and shadowy figures that skulk in the stairwells, but when you finally breach the rooftop, the view takes your breath away. The world is a sea of grey and rust stretching to every horizon, broken only by the silent, skeletal remains of civilization.

You clear a space near the edge, where the railing has long since rusted away. Your hands are dirty, nails cracked, as you press the bulbs into the grey powder. You pour the precious water over them.

Then, you wait.

The sun begins to set—a bruised purple eye sinking below the waste—but then the growth begins. It happens instantly, the way things only do in dreams. Vines shoot up, thick and green, wrapping around the antennae and satellite dishes, turning them into trellises. Flowers bloom in impossible colors—neon blues, violent magentas, golds that hum with their own inner light. They don't just grow; they sing. A low, resonant hum vibrates through the soles of your boots, a melody that sounds like a memory of a lullaby you heard before you were born.

Below you, the things lurking in the dark pause. The predators, the scavengers, the shadows—they all lift their heads toward the light. They do not attack. They are mesmerized. For one perfect, suspended moment, the danger doesn't exist, and the scarcity doesn't matter. You have created beauty where there was none, and the whole world stops to watch it glow against the dying light.
