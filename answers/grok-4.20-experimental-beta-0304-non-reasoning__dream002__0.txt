You feel a storm approaching and the sky cracks open like an eggshell of bruised purple and gold. The trees, which moments ago whispered gently, now bend and groan as if trying to pull their roots free and run.

Rain doesn't fall so much as *arrives*—a sudden silver curtain that hisses through the leaves. Lightning forks sideways between the trunks, illuminating things that shouldn't be there: a grand piano half-buried in moss, its keys glowing faintly; a flock of paper cranes lifting off from a branch, their wings turning into real feathers mid-flight; and in the distance, a door standing upright in the middle of the woods, no walls attached, just a tall oak door with a brass knob that's slowly turning by itself.

Your heartbeat is louder than the thunder.

You realize you're no longer walking. The forest floor has become a mirror, and you're standing on the reflection of the sky. Below your feet, storm clouds drift upside-down while you remain perfectly dry, as though the dream itself is protecting you from its own chaos.

A voice that sounds like your own—but older—whispers from somewhere behind the rain:

"Choose a direction. Every way leads somewhere you once forgot to go."

The piano begins playing a song you knew as a child but could never remember the name of.

The door in the distance swings open.

What do you do?
