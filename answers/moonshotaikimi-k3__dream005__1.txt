...each one seems to be discussing something I almost understand. A circle of people near the staircase is debating whether Thursday has a smell. I open my mouth to ask if anyone has seen the person I'm looking for, and realize I can't remember their name—only that I would know them instantly, the way you know a song.

The house is larger than it should be. Every door I open reveals another party, slightly different. In one room, everyone is my age but from different years of my life, and they all wave at me like we've been interrupted mid-conversation. My third-grade teacher is there, holding a plate of shrimp, and she tells me the person I'm looking for "just went down to the lake for ice," which makes perfect sense until I remember there is no lake.

But there is. Down a hallway that is also somehow a dock, past a woman knitting what appears to be fog, I find the water—dark and still, paper lanterns floating on it like dropped moons. And there, at the end of the pier, is a figure with their back to me. I know it's them. My heart lifts with that specific relief of finding your one person in a crowd.

I call out, but what comes out is the sound of a phone ringing in another room. I try to run, but the pier has become the longest hallway in the world, and the lanterns are all turning to look at me. The figure begins, slowly, to turn around—

—and the whole gathering exhales at once, and I am awake, or nearly, the party continuing somewhere just behind my eyes. All day I carry the strange certainty that if I pass them on the street, I'll know them. I keep almost turning around to say: *there you are. I was looking everywhere for you.*
