You try to run, but your feet feel rooted to the spot, sinking slightly into soil that has suddenly become soft and warm, pulsating faintly like the belly of a sleeping animal.

Then, the lightning strikes.

It isn’t jagged or white; it is a sheet of liquid silver fire that tears the sky open without making a sound. In that stark, silent illumination, the landscape transforms. The bark of the trees peels away to reveal they are made of polished obsidian, and the leaves turn into thousands of tiny, fluttering eyes that all snap open to look at you at once.

The rain begins to fall, but it does not wet you. It falls upward from the ground, defying gravity, shimmering droplets of liquid starlight that dissolve the moment they touch your skin, leaving behind a sensation of intense warmth and distant memories you didn't know you had.

You aren't afraid. In fact, you realize the storm isn't coming *at* you; it is coming *out* of you. You take a deep breath, and as you exhale, a thunderclap shakes the forest—a sound that is actually your own voice laughing—and you step forward into the silver light, finally ready to wake up.
