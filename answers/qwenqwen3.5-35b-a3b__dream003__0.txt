You lean closer, and the material beneath your fingertips grows warm, vibrating with a low, resonant hum that you feel in your chest rather than hear. The metal isn't cold; it feels like it is breathing. You realize then that the box isn't waiting for a mechanical key. It is waiting for an intent.

You close your eyes and focus on the feeling of letting go. The patterns flare violet, then gold, spiraling until they align into a single, glowing seam. There is no click, only a sound like wind chimes caught in a storm. The lid doesn't lift; it dissolves into mist.

Inside, there is no treasure, no weapon, and no map. There is only a single, unlit mirror. Your reflection stares back, but it doesn't move with you. It smiles, raises a hand, and taps the glass. 

Suddenly, the room you stand in begins to fade, replaced by the sensation of falling upward through layers of colored light—deep indigo, burning orange, soft cream. You hear the sound of the alarm clock, but it is distant, underwater. The dream is ending. You know you will not open the box in waking life, but you know, with certainty, that you are now carrying its weight somewhere safe inside.

And then, the light burns white, and you wake up.
