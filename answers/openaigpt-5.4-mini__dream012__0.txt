You are the captain of a ship in the middle of a turbulent sea. Your crew looks to you for guidance as the storm worsens, and you **raise your hand for silence, then point toward the breaking clouds where a single shaft of moonlight reveals a narrow path through the waves.**

“Hard to starboard!” you shout. “Trim the sails! We go where the sea allows, not where it demands!”

The crew springs into motion. Ropes snap taut, sails strain like living things, and the ship groans beneath the force of the wind. For a moment the deck tilts so sharply that the horizon disappears, and all you can see is black water and white foam rushing past like the jaws of a giant beast.

Then the storm begins to answer you.

Lightning forks across the sky, not as a threat, but as a signal—an opening. You steer into it with steady hands, and the ship surges forward, rising atop a massive wave like a creature remembering how to fly. The crew cheers, fear turning into courage, and the sea, still wild, seems to part just enough for you to pass.

At the heart of the storm, you see it: a calm circle of water beneath a sky full of stars, as if the world has been waiting for you to arrive.

And as the ship glides into that impossible stillness, you realize the storm was never only around you.

It was inside you too.

And now, at last, you have found the way through.
