In the dream, the physical room begins to hum softly. The air between us thickens but doesn't weigh it down; it feels heavy with potential. The argument is silent now because the heat has been replaced by a quiet understanding. You decide to touch your friend's shoulder instead of reaching into their pocket for something that isn't yours.

"You don't have to fight over this anymore," you whisper, and then you look up. The words hang in the air like smoke. Then they dissolve into light as you realize it wasn't a disagreement anymore, it was just two people trying to fit each other into a space that didn't want them inside at all.

In the dream of the morning light coming through the window, you decide to leave one door unlocked while the other remains closed with a heavy lock, and see if the silence is your choice or his. You let go of the words. You are just two people standing in the dark together without understanding enough to break. You realize that sometimes silence is the only thing we can speak to each other.

So, you decide to stop. You don't need to explain why you didn't. You just needed to be there.
