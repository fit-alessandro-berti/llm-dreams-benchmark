...the air turns electric, thick with the smell of ozone and wet pine. The trees begin to sway, but strangely—not all in the same direction. Each one moves to its own rhythm, as if dancing to music only it can hear.

The first raindrops fall upward.

You watch, unsurprised in the way one can only be in dreams, as silver droplets rise from the ferns and moss, drifting toward the bruised purple sky. The storm clouds churn overhead, but they're not gray—they're deep indigo shot through with veins of gold, like marble come alive.

Thunder rolls, but it sounds like a voice. Almost words. You strain to understand it.

Lightning splits the sky, and in that flash of brilliance, you see that the forest path ahead has changed. Where there was dirt and roots, there is now a staircase made of smooth river stones, spiraling up and around the trunk of an impossibly tall oak tree—a tree that wasn't there a moment ago.

The rain-that-falls-upward grows heavier. Your feet leave the ground slightly with each step, gravity becoming a polite suggestion rather than a law.

You reach the staircase. The stones are warm despite the storm. As you begin to climb, you notice doors set into the bark of the great oak—dozens of them, each a different color, each humming faintly with its own note, together forming a chord that harmonizes with the thunder-voice above.

One door, painted the exact blue of a summer evening from your childhood, swings open on its own.

Inside, you can see... light. And something else. Something familiar that you can't quite name—the way you can't quite remember a word that sits on the tip of your tongue.

The storm whispers behind you: *"You've been here before."*

And you realize, with the strange certainty of dreams, that this is true. You step through the doorway just as the sky opens completely, and you fall—or rise—into the light, wondering what you'll remember when you wake.
