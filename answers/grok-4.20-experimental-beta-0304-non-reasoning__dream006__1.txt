The crowd is loving it. Someone in the front row turns into a giant friendly moth and starts conducting with its antennae. Your microphone has grown tiny legs and is now tap-dancing across the podium.

You glance down at your body and discover you're wearing the most magnificent pajamas anyone's ever seen—deep cosmic blue with tiny glowing galaxies that actually twinkle. The audience is starting to shimmer too, their faces becoming the faces of every person who's ever been kind to you, all mixed together in a beautiful, impossible collage.

And just as you begin to conduct an entire symphony with nothing but your growing sense of wonder, the dream starts to soften at the edges, like warm taffy, pulling you gently back toward waking...
