You are about to give a speech to a large audience. As you step onto the stage, you see the crowd looking expectantly at you, and you begin to speak, your voice echoing with surprising confidence. "Ladies and gentlemen," you say, "today, I stand before you not as a leader, but as a dreamer in a world that's forgotten how to fly."

The words flow effortlessly at first, drawing nods and murmurs from the sea of faces. But as you gesture toward the horizon painted on the massive screen behind you, the stage starts to tilt like a funhouse floor. The audience doesn't notice—they lean in closer, their eyes gleaming like stars. You push on, weaving tales of impossible adventures, but suddenly, your notes dissolve into butterflies that flutter from your hands, swirling around your head in a kaleidoscope of colors.

Panic flickers, but it's exhilarating. The crowd rises, not in applause, but transforming one by one into birds—eagles, sparrows, phoenixes—filling the auditorium with a symphony of wings. You leap off the stage, no longer falling but soaring with them, the speech forgotten as the dream sky opens wide, carrying you into endless blue. And just as the wind whispers your name, you wake with a smile, the echo of feathers still brushing your skin.
