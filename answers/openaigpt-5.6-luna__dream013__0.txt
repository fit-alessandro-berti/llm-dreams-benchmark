A door of black glass opens before you, revealing a room filled with floating clocks. Their hands spin at different speeds, and beneath them a single inscription appears:

**“What can be broken without being touched?”**

You study the clocks, the walls, the shifting shadows. At last, you whisper, “A promise.”

Every clock stops.

The room folds inward, becoming a narrow corridor lit by blue flames. At its end stands a figure wearing your face. It holds a key in one hand and a mirror in the other.

“You have solved the puzzles,” it says. “But you have not yet solved the maze.”

“What is the final puzzle?”

The figure points behind you.

There is no corridor anymore—only a vast chamber shaped like a heart, with thousands of doors lining its walls. Each door bears a memory: a childhood afternoon, a forgotten regret, a moment of courage, a face you thought you’d lost.

The figure offers you the key.

“Choose the door you are most afraid to open.”

Your hand trembles as you select one.

Beyond it is not a memory, but a sunrise. Warm light pours across the floor, and you understand that the maze was never built to keep you trapped. It was built to lead you inward.

When you step through, the walls dissolve.

You wake beneath your blankets with the answer still glowing in your mind:

**The way out is the way through.**
