You are at your workplace, and suddenly, you are given a project with an impossible deadline. Your boss is waiting for you to start, and you realize your keyboard has turned into piano keys. 

Every time you try to type the project brief, it plays a jazzy little melody instead. Your boss taps their watch - but the watch face is a countdown timer made of melting ice cream, dripping seconds onto the carpet.

You look down at the "impossible" files and the pages are blank except for one line: *Due: Yesterday, at the speed of light*. 

The office walls start folding like origami, and your coworkers are all calmly sipping coffee from mugs that whisper encouragement: "You’ve done harder things in dreams before." 

So you take a breath, press the piano-keyboard, and instead of typing, you play. Notes float off the keys and rearrange themselves into paragraphs, charts, and a perfectly formatted slide deck mid-air. Your boss stops tapping, leans in, and their frown melts into the same ice cream as the clock. They take a bite and nod. 

**Deadline: met.** 

Then the fire alarm rings - but it’s just your phone alarm in the real world, and you wake up laughing at how your brain solved an impossible deadline by turning stress into music.
