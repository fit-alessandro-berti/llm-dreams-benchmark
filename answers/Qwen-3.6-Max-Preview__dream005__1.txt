...their faces seem to shift like oil on water, blurring into indistinct smudges of color and sound. The air is thick with the scent of ozone and old paper, though no one is smoking or reading. You move through the crowd, but your feet feel heavy, as if you are wading through warm honey.

Every time you think you spot your friend—a flash of a familiar blue jacket, a laugh that sounds just right—the person turns around, and it’s someone else entirely. A stranger with eyes too wide, smiling a smile that doesn’t reach their cheeks. They offer you a glass of liquid that glows faintly purple, whispering, *"He’s in the library,"* before dissolving into mist.

You push forward, the noise of the party rising into a deafening hum, like a thousand bees trapped in a jar. The walls of the room begin to stretch upward, becoming impossibly high, lined with doors that weren’t there before. Each door bears a name tag. One says **"Yesterday."** Another says **"What If."**

Then, you see them. Your friend is standing near a window that looks out not onto a street, but onto a vast, starless ocean. They turn to you, their face clear and sharp for the first time all night. They don’t speak, but they hold up a single key made of ice. As you reach for it, the floor beneath you gives way, and you fall—not down, but *up*, into the cold, dark sky above the gathering.
