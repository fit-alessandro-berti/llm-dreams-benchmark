You find yourself in a vast, unfamiliar city, and you realize you have an important meeting in an hour but no idea where it is. You start to feel a gentle panic rising in your chest, but then you remember a trick your grandmother taught you: when you’re lost, look for the oldest tree in the square. You begin to scan the streets, your eyes darting from one building to the next, searching for a landmark that feels ancient and rooted. The air is thick with the scent of rain on cobblestones, and the distant hum of a streetcar rattles through the alleys. As you turn a corner, a narrow lane opens up, and there, bathed in a shaft of golden light, stands a towering oak whose roots seem to cradle the very cobblestones beneath it. You step closer, feeling the bark warm under your fingertips, and the tree whispers a direction—turn left, then right, past the fountain with the silver swan. You follow, heart steadying, and soon the city’s rhythm slows, the meeting place revealing itself as a modest café tucked behind a mural of constellations. You arrive just in time, breathless but calm, and the meeting begins with a smile.
