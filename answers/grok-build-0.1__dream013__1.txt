You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, you begin to feel the walls breathing—slow, deliberate inhales that sync with your own. The air grows thicker, carrying the faint scent of old paper and forgotten rain. 

The puzzles no longer feel like obstacles; they feel like mirrors. One forces you to rearrange glowing symbols until they form the exact pattern of a childhood regret you’d buried. Another demands you balance impossible weights while reciting a truth you’ve never spoken aloud. With each solution, the maze doesn’t open outward—it folds inward. Corridors tighten. The ceiling lowers until you’re half-crawling, half-remembering.

Then the geometry starts lying. You solve a path that should lead left and find yourself walking straight through what was a solid wall moments ago. Your footsteps echo back as someone else’s. You turn a corner and meet yourself—older, eyes tired, holding the next puzzle like an offering. They don’t speak. They simply hand you a small, warm stone etched with a single question: “If you wake up now, will you remember what you were looking for?”

The stone pulses in your palm. The maze around you begins to soften at the edges, colors bleeding like wet ink. You can almost hear the sound of morning birds through the stone walls. But deeper still, something is waiting—something that knows your name and has been arranging these puzzles for years.

Do you keep walking, or do you set the stone down and open your eyes?
