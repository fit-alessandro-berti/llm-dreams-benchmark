The first drop hits your hand, but it isn't water. It is a warm, glowing ember that vanishes on contact, leaving a scent of cinnamon and rain. Thunder rumbles overhead, but it sounds like a choir humming a low, resonant chord, vibrating in your chest rather than your ears.

You look for shelter, and there, floating between two ancient oaks, is a door. It has no frame, just a simple archway of light. You step through, and the roar of the storm instantly becomes a whisper. Inside the door, the forest is still peaceful, but the trees are made of glass, chiming softly in a wind you cannot feel. You realize you are safe, not because you escaped the storm, but because you have become the eye of it.

Then, a bright light floods the glass trees, and you feel the sensation of falling upwards, waking into the morning sun.
