You start with a small patch of land near the remains of an old, abandoned house, determined to create a sustainable home for the last remnants of humanity.

Each day, you carefully select seeds that can survive in such harsh conditions - tough, drought-resistant crops like potatoes, carrots, and other root vegetables - that you meticulously save small portions of them from anything scavenged. These become the foundation for your garden. With limited water, you collect rainwater and filter it using any clean water source available, aiming to create a basic irrigation system.

After a few weeks, tiny sprouts begin to emerge. Slowly but surely, a green patch appears amidst the concrete debris and twisted steel, reminding you of the resilient spirit of humanity that persists even in the face of such catastropde. Every sunrise brings not just daylight but a glimmer of hope, and with this hope, you find strength. Gradually, you start growing other plants too, such as wild strawberries and tomatoes, along with beans and leafy greens, creating a diverse, resilient garden.

You develop a simple aquaponics system using an old barrel and fish scavenged from a dilapidated pond, balancing the pH levels and nutrients perfectly. The fish provide the necessary food needed for plants.

Your fellow survivors are amazed, and they too contribute their meager resources and knowledge. Together, you learn a system of bartering with other scattered communities using pre-apocalypse currency. A sense of unity grows as people rally around you, creating a makeshift communitarian oasis where everyone shares skills, knowledge, food, and stories to keep their culture and spirit alive, fostering a sense of camaraderie, even in this hostile world.

With time, the garden becomes the hub of your community, blossoming green oases, symbolizing the resurgence of growth and resistance. Your home transforms from a mere shelter to a beacon of sustenance, survival, and solidarity, showing that despite the apocalypse, humanity can still survive and rebuild, one plant at a time.

You realize that you're no longer just surviving, but living, growing and thriving in this new world order, with the seed of hope sown, watered and nurtured. The garden stands as a testament to perseverance, teaching all future generations that we can rebuild even the most devastated environment, just as we have always done.

One day, as you gaze at the thriving garden and the bustling community around, a thought occurs to you. This, this, is our new normal, a new home, and you smile, embracing your role as a guardian of new life born amidst ruins. You look forward to the future, where greenery is not just a memory, but a constant, vibrant present. And with this newfound hope, you wake up, back in bed, carrying the dream, the garden and your dreamy utopia within you.
