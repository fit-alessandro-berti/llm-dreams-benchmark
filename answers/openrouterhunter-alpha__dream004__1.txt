...and the canvas hums. Not with sound, but with potential — like the air before a thunderstorm.

You reach toward it, and the moment your fingers come close, the canvas begins to breathe. It expands and contracts in slow, organic pulses. You realize it isn't blank at all. It's *every color at once*, moving so quickly that they cancel each other into white — like a spinning top that appears still.

The landscape around you shifts. What were geometric pyramids a moment ago have softened into liquid domes that drip upward into a sky made of velvet. You feel the ground beneath you become less like earth and more like memory — solid where you believe it is, giving way where you doubt.

The canvas speaks. Not in words, but in feelings that arrive fully formed:

*You are not observing this world. You are dreaming it. And I am the place where your dreaming meets its edge.*

You press your palm flat against the surface. Color explodes — not outward, but *inward*, pouring into the canvas like water into a drain. The entire landscape folds, pulling toward your hand like fabric gathered on a thread. The pyramids, the sky, the dripping domes — all of it collapses into the canvas until you are standing in perfect silence, holding a small, heavy rectangle that now contains everything.

It's warm in your hands.

And then you notice — there's a new shape on its surface. A door. Small, intricate, drawn in a style you almost recognize.

You reach for the handle.

What does it feel like?
