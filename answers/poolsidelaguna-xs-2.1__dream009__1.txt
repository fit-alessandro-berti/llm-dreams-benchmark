They pause, tilting their head as if studying you. In their hand, they carry a staff topped with a crystal that pulses with a faint, otherworldly light. 

*“The Veil weakens,”* they say, their voice a blend of growls and whispers. *“You are not of this age… but you are needed.”* 

Behind them, the forest darkens unnaturally. Trees lean inward, their branches clawing at the sky. Somewhere in the distance, a bell tolls—once, twice, then stops. The pendant at your throat burns warm against your skin, and suddenly, you remember fragments: a library burning, a machine that hummed with starlight, a choice you never made. 

The antlered figure gestures to the huts. *“Come. Before the Hollowing comes.”* 

Do you follow? Or do you run, searching for answers in a world that no longer remembers your name?
