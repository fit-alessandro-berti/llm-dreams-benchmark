The canvas sways in a breeze made of forgotten words, its edges dissolving into liquid light before settling back into the solid ground beneath your feet. You can see colors forming on the floor, shifting like water in your memory, but there is no paint until you reach out with your hand to touch it.

The blank canvas then breathes. It does not demand a brush or ink, only a moment where the world pauses long enough for you to realize that the change isn't happening outside—it's inside of you first. The colors are yours alone, and the shapeless white is nothing but a space waiting to be filled with your own thoughts.
