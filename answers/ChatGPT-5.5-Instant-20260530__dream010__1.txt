You are participating in a competition where the grand prize is your dream job. As the competition progresses, you face various challenges and obstacles that seem carefully designed to test more than just your skills. One round requires solving complex problems under pressure. Another asks you to work with complete strangers to accomplish an impossible task before time runs out.

At first, you focus entirely on winning. Every challenge feels like a battle against the other contestants. Yet the further you advance, the stranger the competition becomes. The judges never reveal scores. Some contestants disappear after rounds only to reappear as mentors, offering cryptic advice.

One evening, between challenges, you discover a hidden hallway behind a curtain. Curious, you follow it. The hallway stretches far longer than the building should allow, lined with doors marked with different versions of your name. Behind one door, you see yourself as a scientist. Behind another, an artist. Behind a third, an explorer crossing distant deserts.

A voice echoes through the corridor.

"Which dream job are you trying to win?"

You try to answer, but the words feel uncertain. Had the prize always been the same? The memory shifts every time you think about it.

The next challenge takes place in a city floating among the clouds. Contestants race across bridges of glass while carrying glowing lanterns. Halfway through, your lantern begins to flicker. Others rush ahead, but an older contestant who had been trailing behind stumbles and nearly falls into the mist below.

Without thinking, you stop to help.

The moment you do, the city freezes.

The clouds stop moving. The lanterns stop flickering. Even the wind becomes still.

The judges appear around you, no longer wearing suits but shimmering masks made of starlight.

"You have reached the final round," one says.

Confused, you look around. "But I didn't win."

"You never needed to."

The floating city dissolves into thousands of glowing fragments. The fragments swirl around you, forming scenes from your life—moments of curiosity, kindness, determination, failure, and growth.

"You thought the competition was about earning a position," another judge says. "It was about discovering who would hold it."

A door materializes before you. Unlike the others, it bears no title.

You open it.

Inside is a vast office, laboratory, studio, and library all at once—a place that changes shape with every step. On the desk rests a contract with your name written across it in golden ink.

You reach for the pen.

Just before signing, you notice the final line:

**Position: Creator of Your Own Path**

The letters begin to glow brighter and brighter until they become sunlight pouring through your window.

You wake up, the dream fading quickly, but a strange feeling remains: the sense that the prize was never waiting at the finish line.

It was waiting for you to become the person who could recognize it.
