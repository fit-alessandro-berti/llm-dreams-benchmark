…hesitate for a moment, unsure whether to trust them. The city lights blur together, neon reflections sliding across rain-slick streets, and the noise of traffic fades like it’s underwater.

One of the strangers smiles and hands you a small folded map, though you don’t remember maps being made of warm paper that seems to glow faintly. As you open it, the streets rearrange themselves, lines shifting like living things. Your destination isn’t a place you recognize—just a small circle drawn in gold.

“Don’t worry,” one of them says gently. “You’re closer than you think.”

You follow them through narrow alleys that turn into quiet gardens, across bridges that weren’t there a moment before. The city grows softer, calmer, as if it’s exhaling. And somewhere ahead, beyond the last corner, you begin to hear something familiar—laughter, maybe, or music you almost remember.

That’s when you realize you’re not lost at all. You were just on the long way back.
