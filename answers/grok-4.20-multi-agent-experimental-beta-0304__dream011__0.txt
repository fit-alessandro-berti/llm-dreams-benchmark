**You are about to take the final exam for a course that determines your future career. As you sit down to start the exam, you notice the questions are** alive—literally shifting and writhing on the page like startled eels, rearranging themselves every time you blink. The first one reads "How many times did you disappoint your 7th-grade self?" in your own childhood handwriting, then morphs into "Explain the taste of Tuesday using only the color blue." 

The proctor is your future self, twenty years older, looking exhausted and wearing the exact tie you swore you'd never wear. He leans in and whispers, "You didn't study for this life, did you?" while the clock melts down the wall like a Dali painting. Your pencil turns into a feather, then a banana, then your childhood hamster. The other students are all versions of you at different ages—five-year-old you is confidently drawing dinosaurs, teenage you is texting under the desk, and eighty-year-old you is already asleep with a perfect score.

The room starts dissolving into your old bedroom. The exam paper whispers, "This isn't the real test. The real test is whether you wake up before you have to answer 'What if you never tried?'" 

You jolt awake in a cold sweat... only to find yourself sitting at another desk, with another exam, and the questions are already watching you. 

(Sweet dreams.)
