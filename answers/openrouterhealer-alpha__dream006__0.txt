*step onto the stage and feel the heat of the spotlights wash over me...*

I begin to speak, but the words that come out aren't the speech I'd prepared. Instead, my voice carries a melody — and I realize, with a strange calm, that I'm singing. The audience doesn't seem surprised. They lean forward, some closing their eyes, as if this is exactly what they came for.

The podium beneath my hands feels warm, almost alive, like the grain of wood is breathing. I look down at my notes and see not words but a garden — tiny flowers unfurling in real time across the paper, each one blooming into a different idea. I pick one up and hold it toward the crowd, and the fragrance becomes a thought everyone in the room can hear.

Somehow, I'm no longer nervous. The faces in the audience have softened — they're not judges or critics but old friends I haven't met yet. A few of them are already nodding, smiling, as though we're remembering something together rather than me teaching them anything new.

Then, mid-sentence, I notice the ceiling has opened to a twilight sky. Stars blink in rhythm with the rhythm of my voice. A single bird circles overhead, and I understand — without anyone explaining it — that it's carrying the last question anyone in the room will ever need to ask.

I reach toward it...
