---

The challenges get stranger. One round asks you to assemble a chair, but the pieces are warm and breathing. Another asks you to defend an idea you don't remember choosing. You keep advancing anyway. The other contestants have faces you almost recognize.

Then you reach the final round, and the judges lean forward and say: *describe the job you want.*

And you open your mouth—and realize you can't. Not because you don't know, but because every time you start, the words rearrange into a job you've never considered. A lighthouse keeper. Someone who names colors. The person who decides when songs should end.

You wake before you answer.

---

That last beat is the honest part, I think. Dreams about wanting something often stall right at the moment of naming it.
