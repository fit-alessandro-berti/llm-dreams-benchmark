...and suddenly the challenges begin to warp and shift like they only can in dreams.

The first round is a "public speaking" event, but the stage is floating in the middle of an endless ocean and the audience is every person who has ever rejected your job applications, all of them bobbing on rubber ducks and wearing extremely judgmental expressions. You open your mouth to speak and instead of words, beautiful bioluminescent jellyfish float out and arrange themselves into perfect slides about your accomplishments. The crowd actually starts applauding. One of the jellyfish turns into your dream-job boss and gives you a thumbs up.

Next challenge: a cooking competition. The ingredients keep changing into things from your actual life (childhood toys, old report cards, that one email you regret sending). You somehow make a perfect soufflé out of pure unresolved childhood trauma and imposter syndrome. It rises beautifully. The judges cry.

Then comes the "teamwork" challenge. Your teammates are all versions of yourself at different ages: the 7-year-old who wanted to be an astronaut, the 15-year-old who was convinced they'd be a rockstar, the 22-year-old who thought they had everything figured out. They're all arguing with each other. You somehow get them to cooperate by letting each of them contribute one ridiculous idea. The final project is completely unhinged but also somehow brilliant.

The final challenge is simply walking across a room to accept the prize. But the room keeps expanding. Every time you take a step toward your dream job (which is sitting on a pedestal glowing with golden light), the pedestal moves further away. You start running. The floor turns into your old resume, then into LinkedIn, then into all the self-doubt you've ever had. 

Just as you're about to give up, you realize the pedestal isn't moving away from you.

You're moving toward it in the wrong way.

You stop running. You close your eyes. You remember why you wanted this job in the first place, not for the title or the money, but for the way it would let you...

And suddenly you're there. The pedestal is right in front of you. Your dream job is in your hands.

But as you reach for it, you feel yourself starting to wake up. The competition, the other contestants, even your dream job start dissolving into that familiar fuzzy feeling of a dream ending.

Right before you wake up, your dream-job version of yourself leans in and whispers:

"You already have everything you need. The competition was never between you and others. It was between the version of you that thinks they need to prove something... and the version that already is."

You wake up with the strangest feeling that you actually won.
