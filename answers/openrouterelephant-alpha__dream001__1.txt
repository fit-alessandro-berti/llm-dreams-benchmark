You remember the meeting’s importance—you *know* it’s crucial, but the content is just out of reach, like a name on the tip of your tongue. A figure in a long coat passes you, their face a soft blur. You call out, and they turn, eyes reflecting starlight.

"Lost?" they ask, their voice like wind through old books.

You nod, helpless.

They smile. "In this city, the destination finds the traveler, not the other way around. Follow the quiet."

With that, they dissolve into a wisp of mist, leaving you with a strange certainty. You decide to walk, not with purpose, but with attention. The chaos of the city begins to soften. The clamor of unseen traffic fades. You notice a small, unmarked door set into a wall of ivy, glowing faintly. It wasn’t there a moment ago.

You push it open.

Inside is a simple room. A single chair faces a window. On the window’s reflection, you see your own face, calm, clear-eyed. The meeting was never a place—it was this moment of finding your center in the unknown. You sit. The hour is not lost; it is exactly as it should be.
