**Here’s how the dream unfolds:**

You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the air goes strangely still. The birds fall silent, and the leaves freeze mid-rustle like someone hit pause.

Then the sky darkens to a deep, bruised violet. Wind sweeps up from nowhere, carrying the scent of rain and something older, like wet stone and pine resin. The path ahead dissolves into mist.

You notice the trees are bending, but not from the wind. They’re leaning *toward* something. Following their gaze, you see it: a massive cloud wall rolling in, lit from within by silent lightning that doesn’t flash so much as *pulse*, like a heartbeat.

Your feet want to run, but the forest floor feels different now. Softer. When you look down, moss has turned to dark water that doesn’t soak your shoes, just ripples with each step. The storm isn’t coming to you. You’re already inside it.

Thunder cracks, but it sounds like a word you almost understand. The rain starts, warm instead of cold, and each drop that hits your skin leaves a brief, glowing mark that fades like fireflies. You’re not afraid anymore. The storm is looking for you, and you’ve been walking toward it the whole time.

In the distance, through the sheets of glowing rain, a figure stands under a tree that isn’t bending. They’re holding something out to you. 

What do you do next?
