You are at your workplace, and suddenly, you are given a project with an impossible deadline. Your boss is waiting for you to start, and you —

...can't find your hands.

No, they're there. They're just wrong. They're made of loose paper, and when you try to flex your fingers, the fingertips flutter.

Your boss is standing right behind your chair. Also, somehow, he's standing by the printer across the room watching you. Both of them say the exact same thing at the exact same time:

"We need it by yesterday. Can you get it to me by yesterday?"

He slides the project onto your desk. It's not a file. It's a single yellow sticky note. On it, in your own handwriting, it says: BUILD A STAIRCASE TO THE ROOF USING ONLY WHAT'S IN YOUR DRAWERS.

You open your drawer. It's full of ocean water. Cold, sloshing ocean water with little goldfish swimming between the paperclips. The goldfish all have your coworkers' faces. They look up at you and say, "Good luck!"

The fluorescent lights above you start buzzing way too loud. BZZZZZZZT. Too loud, too bright, until you realize they're not lights at all - they're long, white tubes of frozen light, and they're melting. Drops of light are dripping onto your desk, sizzling.

You look at the clock on the wall. It has no numbers, just the word "TUESDAY" written twelve times around in a circle. Every clock in the office says the same thing. It's always Tuesday.

Your boss - the one behind your chair - leans in closer. His tie keeps growing longer and longer, pooling on the floor, coiling around the legs of your chair, around your ankles.

"You've done this before, right?" he asks, but his voice is kind now. Almost sleepy.

And that's when it clicks. The heavy, syrupy logic of the dream snaps into place.

You haven't worked here in years. The office is also your old elementary school cafeteria. The project doesn't matter because paper hands can't type anyway.

You remember: *Oh. I'm dreaming.*

The second you remember, the pressure valve releases. The air comes back into your lungs. You stand up. The tie around your ankles unravels. The boss by the printer gives you a little wave like, *finally*.

You don't start the project. You walk right past him, past the melting lights, past the drawer full of ocean, and you push open the office window - even though your office never had a window.

Outside is just wide, open, bright blue sky. And you step out into it.
