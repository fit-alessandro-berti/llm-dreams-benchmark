You are at your workplace, and suddenly, you are given a project with an impossible deadline. Your boss is waiting for you to start, and you notice that the clock on the wall is spinning backward. When you look down at your desk, the papers have turned into a flock of white doves that lift into the air and circle the fluorescent lights above. 

You try to explain this to your boss, but when you open your mouth, only the sound of a dial-up internet connection comes out. Your boss nods solemnly, as if this is completely normal, and hands you a briefcase. You open it, and inside is a single, glowing key made of light. The moment you touch it, the office walls begin to melt like candle wax, dripping down in slow, golden streams.

Somewhere in the building, an elevator dings. The sound echoes, then echoes again, and again, multiplying until the entire room hums with it. You walk toward the sound, but your legs feel like they are moving through deep water. The hallway stretches longer with every step, and the door at the end becomes a mouth that whispers your name—except it's not your name, it's a word you've never heard before, but somehow you understand it means "home."

You reach for the door handle, and your hand passes right through it. You look back, and your boss is now a tall figure made entirely of filing cabinets, their drawers opening and closing like eyes blinking. 

You realize, with the strange clarity that only comes in dreams, that you are not supposed to finish the project. The project was never real. *You* are the project. And the deadline... 

...is whenever you decide to wake up.

The doves land softly on your shoulders. The clock finally stops. And for the first time all day, you breathe.
