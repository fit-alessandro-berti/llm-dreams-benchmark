
The air grows thick with electric tension, and the trees around you begin to whisper secrets in the wind. You quicken your pace, but the forest seems to stretch endlessly, branches clawing at the sky as if trying to hold back the impending deluge. A low rumble shakes the earth beneath your feet, and suddenly—*light*. Not lightning, but a soft, golden glow emanating from a small clearing ahead. 

You push through the undergrowth and find yourself in a meadow where a tiny, shimmering cottage stands, its windows blazing with warm light. The storm halts at the meadow’s edge, as if afraid to cross. From the doorway emerges a figure cloaked in starlight, their face hidden but their voice like a lullaby. *"You’ve come to witness the storm’s heart,"* they say, gesturing to a crystal suspended in the air between you. Inside it swirls a miniature tempest—thunder, rain, and flashes of lightning—all dancing in perfect harmony. 

"You must decide," the figure continues. "Will you let the storm consume you, or will you learn its rhythm?" Before you can answer, the crystal pulses, and the storm *answers*—not with chaos, but with a melody you’ve carried in your bones forever. The cottage fades. The dream sharpens. And somewhere in the distance, a new path through the forest begins to bloom, paved with petals forged from the storm’s own fury. 

You wake with the echo of thunder still humming in your chest, but the air around you feels lighter.
