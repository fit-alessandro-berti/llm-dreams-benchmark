You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the sky bruises deep purple in seconds. Wind howls, twisting the canopy into wild shapes and whipping leaves into a swirling vortex. Thunder cracks — yet instead of rain, the clouds release soft, glowing blue feathers and luminous droplets that are fragments of forgotten moments. They dissolve on your skin, leaving echoes of childhood laughter, the scent of a distant ocean, and the weight of unspoken decisions. The trees lean closer, bark forming fleeting faces that whisper your name. This isn’t weather at all. It’s the dream rearranging itself. Ahead a path of light opens through the mist. You can run for cover… or step forward and dance in the downpour.
