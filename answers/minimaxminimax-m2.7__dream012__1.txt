With each surge, you call out orders—tighten the sails, brace the hatches, pass the rope—to bind the crew together, to give them something to cling to in the chaos. The storm screams louder, but a strange calm settles in your chest, a quiet knowing that you are not merely a captain but a living compass for the souls aboard, and as the first sliver of moonlight pierces the tempest, you whisper, “We will see the sunrise together.”
