You step onto it, and the room dissolves into a sky that is upside-down rain, each drop a prism holding a different hour of your life. The box floats up beside you, its patterns rearranged into constellations you almost recognize—your name spelled in stars you haven’t been born under yet.

The last seam opens not with a click but with a sigh of relief, and inside you find not treasure but an echo: the sound of a door opening somewhere you’ve always meant to go. You reach in, and when you pull your hand back, your palm holds a small, closed key made of quiet. It doesn’t fit any lock you can see, but when you press it against your chest, the ribs bloom like petals, and the dream begins to remember you.
