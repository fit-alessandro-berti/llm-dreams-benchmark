I’m still standing at the corner where the streetlights flicker, the air thick with the hum of traffic and distant sirens. A soft voice greets me—“We’ve been waiting for you.” I nod, unsure whether to follow or turn away. The group offers directions, each offering a small kindness: a quiet café, a street performer’s song, a hidden alley that leads to a park. As we walk, the city’s chaos becomes a gentle guide, and the loneliness begins to soften. Eventually, they point us toward a familiar landmark, and together we follow, the city’s noise fading into the background. In that moment, I realize the dream wasn’t just about finding my way—it was about discovering that even the most crowded places hold quiet paths, and sometimes the best guidance comes from unexpected companionship.
