You are at your workplace, and suddenly, you are given a project with an impossible deadline. Your boss is waiting for you to start, and you freeze, staring at the stack of papers that seem to multiply like rabbits on the desk. The clock ticks louder than a heartbeat, each second stretching into eternity, and your boss's face morphs into a giant hourglass, sand pouring from their eyes.

Panic surges as you grab the first document, but your hands turn to jelly, sliding off the pages. Colleagues around you start dancing in slow motion, their laughter echoing like wind chimes in a storm. You try to type on your computer, but the keyboard sprouts vines that wrap around your fingers, pulling you into a digital jungle where emails rain from the ceiling like confetti made of fire.

Desperate, you shout for more time, but your voice comes out as a flock of birds that scatter the boss-hourglass into glittering shards. The deadline looms—a massive wall of calendars crashing toward you—and just as it engulfs everything in a whirlwind of numbers and stress, you jolt awake in your bed, heart racing, the alarm clock beeping innocently beside you. Another day, another dream survived.
