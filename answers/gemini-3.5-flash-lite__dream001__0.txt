...run. 

The pavement beneath my feet feels strangely soft, like walking on damp moss, and the buildings around me stretch endlessly into a violet sky, their glass windows reflecting scenes from my childhood instead of the street. 

Panic rises in my throat. I pat my pockets—no phone, no map, just a smooth, warm river stone that hums against my palm. 

"Excuse me!" I call out to a passing figure in a tailored grey suit, but as they turn, their face is entirely blank, a smooth expanse of pale skin. They point wordlessly toward a towering skyscraper shaped like a giant hourglass. 

Sand is pouring upward through its glass core. 

I weave through the surreal, bustling crowd—some people are floating a few inches off the ground, others are reading books that burst into harmless blue sparks when a page is turned. I know, with the absolute, unshakeable logic of dreams, that if I don't reach the top of that hourglass tower before the last grain of sand floats to the top, I will miss the meeting forever. And worse, I won't even remember what the meeting was about.

I spot a revolving door spinning wildly, flashing with neon light, and throw myself inside, hoping it spits me out where I need to be...
