Wind rushes through the canopy, bending branches toward a narrow path you hadn’t noticed before. Thunder rolls beneath the earth rather than above it, and with every step, the forest flickers between daylight and night. You follow the path until you reach a clearing where rain hangs motionless in the air, each drop shining like a tiny mirror.

In their reflections, you glimpse forgotten moments: a childhood room, a face you almost recognize, a door you once chose not to open.

At the center of the clearing stands a white stag with stars tangled in its antlers. It looks at you, and the storm becomes perfectly silent.

“You’re late,” it says.

Then it turns and walks into the darkness between two trees. You follow. On the other side, the forest is gone, replaced by a vast ocean beneath your feet. You can walk across its surface as though it were glass. Far below, something enormous and luminous begins to rise.

Just before it reaches you, you realize it isn’t a creature at all.

It is the moon.

It breaks through the water without a sound, filling the sky, and a warm silver light washes over you. The fear leaves your body. Behind you, the storm folds itself into a small black bird and flies away.

When you look back, the stag is gone. In its place is an open door standing alone on the sea.

This time, you step through.

And wake with the sound of leaves rustling in a room where there are no trees.
