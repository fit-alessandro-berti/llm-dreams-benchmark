You notice it because it's the only thing that *isn't* changing.

Everything around you breathes — hills melt from purple to gold to a color you don't have a name for, trees fold into triangles and then unfold into smoke. The sky ripples like water.

But in the middle of it all is a blank canvas.

It's floating a few inches off the ground. No easel. No frame. Just a perfect, stark white rectangle holding perfectly still while the entire world warps around it. The white is so white it hurts a little to look at, like fresh snow, like static, like forgetting.

As you get closer, you realize it's not blank at all. It's thirsty.

The changing colors around you are being pulled toward it in thin threads. A ribbon of blue detaches from a floating rock and drifts over. A spiral of yellow peels off the sky. They don't land *on* the canvas — they sink *into* it and vanish, as if it's drinking the world.

You reach out without thinking to touch it.

Your fingertip brushes the surface and it isn't canvas. It's warm. And soft, like skin. And the moment you touch it, it stops drinking the world and starts drinking *you*.

Color rushes out of your hand — not blood, but memory. The first color is the deep green of your childhood backyard. Then the faded orange of your favorite old t-shirt. Then a red you can't place, but you feel it in your chest.

Where your finger touched, the colors appear. And they stay. They don't shift or melt like everything else.

You understand, suddenly and completely, the way you only understand things in dreams:

You aren't supposed to paint a picture on it.

You are supposed to paint the *world* you want to stay.

So you place both palms on it now. You paint with your hands, with no brushes, no thought. You paint a horizon that doesn't move. You paint a sun that is just yellow and stays yellow. You paint a small, quiet house with a light on inside.

And as you paint each thing, the chaotic landscape behind you quiets. The morphing shapes slow. The screaming colors soften, settling into the shapes you've given them.

The last thing you paint is a small open door in the house.

And from somewhere very far away, you feel your real body take a deep breath, as if it's about to wake up.

Do you step through the door, or do you let yourself wake up?
