As you drift through the warm, crowded space, the faces begin to blur at the edges. A group laughs near a towering ice sculpture of a bird; its feathers seem to shimmer and melt, then reform. You ask one of them if they’ve seen your friend, but their replies are like riddles—“Maybe they’re in the room that’s also a hallway,” one says, gesturing toward a corridor that seems to stretch infinitely. Another offers you a cup of tea that tastes like the color blue.

You move on, passing a circle of people playing a silent game with glowing cards. One person, with eyes like deep wells, nods at you knowingly and whispers, “They’re looking for you too,” before turning away.

You reach a balcony overlooking a garden of whispering flowers. Standing at the railing is your friend, but when they turn, their face is someone else’s—yet you feel a surge of recognition. They smile and say, “I’ve been waiting. I just arrived.” The air smells of rain and old books, and you realize you’re not sure who was lost anymore.
