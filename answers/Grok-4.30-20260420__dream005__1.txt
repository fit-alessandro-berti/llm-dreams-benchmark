You weave through the crowd, the air thick with the scent of forgotten perfumes and spilled champagne that tastes like childhood summers. Every group you pass feels like a half-remembered scene from a movie you’ve never watched.

In one corner, a circle of people in glittering masks are debating the ethics of time travel while actually rewinding each other’s sentences mid-conversation—words flowing backward like silk ribbons. One of them catches your eye and whispers, “You’re looking for the wrong person,” before dissolving into laughter that echoes from the ceiling.

Nearby, a long table floats an inch above the floor. Guests are playing an endless game of musical chairs where the music is their own heartbeats and the chairs keep rearranging themselves into impossible shapes. A woman with your mother’s smile but someone else’s eyes offers you a seat that vanishes the moment you reach for it.

You keep moving. The crowd parts like water, then closes behind you like a secret. Laughter bubbles up from nowhere. Someone calls your name in a voice that isn’t quite theirs. A man in a tuxedo made of shifting constellations taps your shoulder and hands you a map that is actually a photograph of this exact moment—except in the photo you’re already walking away.

And then, just as the room begins to tilt and the chandeliers start dripping light like honey, you see them.

Your one familiar face.

They’re standing alone at the center of the dance floor, but the floor is now a mirror reflecting an empty sky. They turn, smile the way they always do when they’re about to tell you something important, and say:

“I’ve been looking for you too. Ready to wake up?”

The music swells, the lights flare into pure white, and the dream gently folds itself away like a letter you’ll reread for years.
