You are at your workplace, and suddenly, you are given a project with an impossible deadline. Your boss is waiting for you to start, and you freeze for a moment, your mind racing. The fluorescent lights overhead hum like distant static, and the air feels thick—too still, too warm. Your coworkers blur at the edges of your vision, moving in slow motion, their voices muffled like you're underwater.

You glance down at the project brief in your trembling hands, but the words keep rearranging themselves—dates shifting forward, tasks multiplying, deadlines collapsing into a single red clock spinning backward. Your boss looms closer, his smile too wide, his eyes reflecting not you, but a version of yourself now—older, hollow-eyed, clocking in at 3 a.m.

You try to open your mouth to say “I can’t,” but instead, time fractures. Suddenly, you’re not just doing this project—you are the project. Your thoughts become spreadsheets, your heartbeat syncs with an email subject line pinging endlessly. Your name on the org chart glows, then burns out like a dead pixel.

And then… silence. A door appears where the elevator used to be. You know you should stay and comply… but your hand reaches for the handle anyway.

You turn it—and step through into a vast, white room filled with everyone you’ve ever worked with… lying motionless on ergonomic chairs of all kinds—office chairs with neck support, pneumatic adjustable office chairs, office chairs with high-density foam, office chairs with lumbar support that reminds you of someone’s touch, ergonomic office chairs with adjustable features, office chairs made with mesh material for comfort, and even a few traditional, rigid leather office chairs with no adjustments at all—all connected by tangled power cords and Ethernet cables that pulse faintly like veins.

They’re not sleeping… they’re buffering.
