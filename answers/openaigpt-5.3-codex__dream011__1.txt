…written in a language you almost recognize—like English reflected in dark water.  
Every question has your name in it.

**1. When did you decide to become this version of yourself?**  
**2. Show your work for all the chances you didn’t take.**  
**3. If you could start over, where would you begin?**

You flip through the pages faster, heart thudding. The exam hall is silent except for pencils scratching, but when you glance around, everyone else is older versions of you: one in a lab coat, one in a suit, one in a paint-stained jacket, one with tired eyes and coffee hands. They all write confidently.

Your own page stays blank.

Then the clock above the board begins to melt, dripping silver seconds onto the floor. A proctor with no face leans over and whispers, “There is only one question that counts.”

A final sheet appears in front of you, clean and bright:

**What do you want, when no one is grading you?**

Your hand finally moves. The words come out shaky, then steady. As soon as you finish the last sentence, the room exhales. Chairs empty. Walls dissolve into morning light.

You wake up before seeing your score, but for the first time, you’re not afraid of the result.
