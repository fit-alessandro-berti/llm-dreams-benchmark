…raise your hand, and somehow the thunder pauses.

Not stops—only pauses, as if the whole sky is waiting to hear what you will say.

“Hold the bow into the waves,” you command, though your voice sounds older than your body. “Trim the sails. Tie yourselves to the mast. Nobody goes overboard tonight.”

The crew moves at once. Boots skid across the slick deck. Ropes snap like angry serpents. Lanterns swing wildly, throwing gold light over faces full of fear and trust. You grip the wheel with both hands, feeling the ship buck beneath you like a living creature.

Then, through the black wall of rain, you see it.

A light.

Not lightning. Not a star. A steady silver glow beneath the surface of the sea.

The waves rise higher, lifting your ship until the deck tilts toward the clouds. For one impossible instant, you see the ocean below you opened like a book. Deep beneath the foam, an enormous city sleeps under glass domes, its towers glowing blue, its streets filled with drifting shapes that might be fish, or people, or memories.

One of your crew cries out, “Captain!”

You look ahead. A wave taller than any mountain is coming.

The crew freezes.

The ship groans.

And you understand, with the certainty that only dreams allow, that the storm is not trying to destroy you. It is trying to wake the city below.

You turn the wheel not away from the wave, but toward it.

The ship climbs.

The sea roars.

The crew screams.

At the crest, everything becomes weightless: the rain, the ropes, the terrified faces, even your own heartbeat. The silver light bursts upward through the water, wrapping the ship in brightness.

Then the wave breaks.

But instead of falling, the ship sails into the sky.

Below, the storm folds inward like a closing eye. Above, the clouds part, revealing not stars, but thousands of lanterns floating in the dark, each one carrying the name of someone you once knew and had forgotten.

Your crew is silent now.

A small child stands beside you at the wheel, though you are certain no child was aboard before. They look up at you and ask, “Do you remember where you were going?”

You open your mouth to answer.

And just as you realize you do—

you wake with the sound of waves still moving in your chest.
