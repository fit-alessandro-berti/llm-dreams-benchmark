
You turn a corner, heart pounding from the sheer noise and rush of faces, but then a hand touches your elbow—not to grab, but to steady. An old woman with eyes like smoothed stone smiles and says, "This way, child. The river’s bend is just past the lantern." Her voice is soft, cutting through the din. You follow, and the panic unspools from your chest, replaced by the quiet certainty of being seen. The city still hums around you, but now it feels like a place holding its breath with you, not against you. When you wake, the kindness lingers like sunlight on your skin.
