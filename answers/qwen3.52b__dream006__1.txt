You begin to laugh in response to the silence that you had thought so loud was the world. As you speak, you realize your voice isn't coming from your throat anymore. It is just a hollow sound echoing between the words spoken on stage and the ones you didn't write down yet. The crowd has gone quiet, but you know you are still speaking to them with no one else around to hear. You begin to feel as if you are walking through a door of glass, looking back at where you were before you stepped out into your own reflection.
