...symbols that seem to shift and rearrange themselves the longer you stare at them. Some of the patterns look like constellations, others like flowing water, and a few seem almost like words in a language you've never seen but somehow feel you should understand.

You trace your fingertip along one of the grooves, and it feels warm — warmer than it should be. The box hums faintly, a vibration you feel more in your chest than hear with your ears. You notice that one of the symbols is slightly raised, like a button. Without thinking, you press it.

The box doesn't open. Instead, the patterns begin to glow — a soft, golden light that spills out and climbs your arm like living vines. You feel no pain, only a strange sense of *recognition*, as though the box is reading you, not the other way around.

Then you hear a voice — not from the box, not from anywhere — that simply says:

*"You already have the key. You've always had it."*

You look down at your hand. In your palm rests a small, ordinary-looking stone you picked up as a child and kept for reasons you could never explain. You place it on the center of the box.

The seams dissolve. The lid lifts slowly on its own.

Inside, there is no treasure, no paper, no object at all. Just a mirror. And in the reflection, you see yourself — but not as you are now. You see yourself as you might become, or perhaps as you truly are beneath everything.

The dream doesn't end so much as it *breathes* — and you realize the box was never the mystery.

*You* were.
