...the trees begin to shiver, their leaves turning silver as if lit by a cold, internal light. The wind doesn’t just blow—it *speaks*. A low, resonant hum vibrates through the ground beneath my feet. 

Suddenly, the rain falls upward. 

Drops lift from the soil and drift toward the sky, joining a swirling canopy of mist that glows faintly violet. The air smells of ozone and wet stone, but also something sweet, like blooming nightshade. I’m not afraid—only intensely curious. 

I reach out to touch a raindrop hanging suspended in the air. The moment my fingertip meets it, the forest *rearranges*. Trees slide sideways through the soil like chess pieces, and the path ahead becomes a mosaic of floating stepping stones, each one a different shade of green moss. 

A distant rumble sounds less like thunder and more like a deep, slow breath drawn by the earth itself. 

And then—I realize I’m not walking anymore. I’m standing still, and the world is flowing *past me*. The storm isn’t happening around me; it’s happening *through* me. The wind, the shifting light, the upward rain—they’re all expressions of some quiet, immense change taking place inside the landscape. 

Just as the feeling crescendos, everything slows. The rain halts mid-air, glittering like a chandelier of tiny prisms. The violet light softens to dawn-gold. And in the perfect silence that follows, a single, clear thought arrives, not in words, but in the feeling of sunlight warming my shoulders: 

*The storm was never the forest breaking. It was the forest waking up.* 

The dream doesn’t end—it just settles, like a sigh of contentment, and waits to see what I’ll do next.
