... and they hum.

The lines aren't merely etched into the dark wood; they are *made* of something that seems to absorb and re-emit light, like captured nebula dust settled into geometric precision. The patterns swirl and weave, suggesting both infinite loops and the straight, undeniable path of a river to the sea.

As I trace a finger—hesitantly, knowing I shouldn't—over the carvings, the surface feels unnaturally warm, vibrating with a low, infrasonic thrum that I feel more in my bones than I hear with my ears. It's a frequency of quiet *waiting*.

I realize the patterns are not random. They are equations, but not ones solvable with numbers. They are a language, a crystalline shorthand for moments: a memory of falling snow, the taste of salt in a stranger’s laugh, the sharp, agonizing clarity of realizing a fundamental truth about yourself. Each symbol is a perfectly preserved regret, a triumphant moment, a single, whispered *if only*.

I press my thumbs against a central knot of the design—a spiraling glyph that resembles an ouroboros eating its own luminous tail. The box suddenly pulses, not with light, but with *presence*. The humming intensifies until it becomes a harmonic chord, deep and mournful, calling my full attention inward.

And then, the most unsettling thing happens.

A whisper, not from the box, but *over* the box, fills the space. It sounds like my own voice, but pitched lower, stripped of its comforting familiarity, like it belongs to a person I used to be.

*“You already know the key.”*

My hands fly back as if burned, and the pressure of the whispered realization seems to cause an energetic resonance. The lock—a clasp of tarnished bronze that looked solid and permanent—doesn't pop open, or click open. It *dissolves*.

The solid metal doesn't break; it softens, losing its definition in a wash of golden-amber smoke that smells faintly of cedar and ozone. The heavy lid, now weightless, drifts open, revealing the interior.

My breath catches when I look inside. There is no treasure. No map, no jewels, no lost heirloom.

The box is filled entirely with *air*, but not empty air. It is a cloud of suspended moments—a glittering, colorless haze. Within the haze, suspended like pollen in amber, are single, perfect objects: a dried white petal, a tarnished coin with no visible mint mark, a single, perfect, translucent teardrop—and a miniature, folded paper crane.

As my eyes focus on the crane, the edges of the whole scene begin to soften. The humming fades back into the indiscernible background buzz of the dream. The light seems to come from *inside* the box, a gentle, knowing luminescence.

I reach my hand into the space, passing through the shimmering, melancholic air. As my fingers brush against the delicate origami, the box closes with a gentle, final *sigh*, the metal reforming instantly, the lock solidifying, the humming dying completely.

The box is locked again.

But now, when I look into my hand, where I was holding the space where the crane had been, I realize my palm is no longer empty. My skin feels faintly iridescent, and resting against my thumb is the scent of cedar and ozone, and a single, profound understanding: the lock was never designed to keep things out. It was designed to keep *me* from remembering what I already knew.

And as the box slips away, pulling me deeper into the haze, I fall back into the soft, silent oblivion of sleep, knowing that the secrets are not *in* the box, but that they are always waiting, folded up, right here, within me.

***

*...and then the dawn arrived, silent and impossible, pulling the weight of the luminous box into the unbearable light, until I woke up.*
