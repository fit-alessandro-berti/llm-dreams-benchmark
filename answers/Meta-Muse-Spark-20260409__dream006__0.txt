Your notes are gone. The teleprompter is just a recipe for banana bread. The crowd leans forward, silent, waiting for something profound.  

Your heart does that weird slow-motion thud it only does in dreams. You tap the mic — it squawks like a startled goose. A kid in the front row giggles. That breaks it. You decide, well, if this is a dream, I might as well say something true.  

So you smile and say:  

“Friends, I was supposed to tell you how to be fearless. But I showed up in silly socks with no plan, and you’re still listening. So maybe fearlessness isn’t about being prepared. Maybe it’s about being willing to be seen exactly as you are, mid-stumble, mid-recipe, mid-dream. The scary part already happened: I walked out here. The rest is just talking.”  

The crowd exhales. Someone claps. Then everyone does. The lights get warmer, like late-afternoon sun, and the banana bread on the teleprompter suddenly smells amazing. You wake up before you find out if they served snacks.
