The sky above is no longer just dark; it is a churning vortex of indigo and bruised purple, swallowing the stars one by one. Rain lashes against your face like handfuls of gravel, stinging your eyes, but you do not blink. You cannot afford to lose focus.

"Hard to starboard!" you roar, your voice cutting through the howl of the wind. "Brace for impact!"

The ship groans in protest, timber screaming as it tilts dangerously toward the port side. A massive wave, towering like a black mountain, rises before you. For a heartbeat, time seems to suspend. You see the terror in your crew’s eyes reflected in the flash of lightning, but then you see something else: trust. They are holding their positions, hands white-knuckled on the ropes and wheels, waiting for your command because they believe you know where the eye of this chaos lies.

You grip the wheel until your knuckles turn white, feeling the vibration of the ocean’s fury. Instead of fighting the current directly, you remember an old sailor’s tale about riding the breath of the storm rather than its teeth. You spin the wheel sharply, not away from the wave, but *into* its shoulder, aiming for the narrow gap between two cresting peaks.

"Hold steady!" you shout, though you aren't sure if anyone can hear you over the thunder.

The ship lurches violently, slamming into the water with a force that knocks the breath out of you. Darkness swallows you whole for a second—just a second—and then, miraculously, you break through. The air clears slightly. The rain softens to a mist. Ahead, through the parting clouds, a faint, golden light pierces the gloom. It isn't the sun, but something stranger—a lighthouse made of pure, humming crystal, standing alone on an island that shouldn't exist.

Your crew looks at you, panting, soaked, but alive. You look at the impossible beacon ahead and realize the storm wasn't trying to destroy you; it was trying to deliver you here. You take a deep breath, the salt air filling your lungs, and whisper, "Set course for the light."
