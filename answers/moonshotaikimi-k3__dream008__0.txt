...and you sit down at your desk, but your keyboard has turned into a piano. You try to type the project proposal anyway, and every key plays a note. Your boss taps their foot impatiently, and with each tap, the floor ripples like water.

You open your laptop, but the screen shows only your childhood kitchen. Your mother is there, stirring a pot, and she looks up and says, "The deadline is a soup. You have to stir the deadline." You nod, because somehow this makes perfect sense.

You pick up the phone to call the client, but the phone is a banana now, and that feels completely normal too. You speak into it: "I'll need an extension." The banana says nothing, which everyone in the office agrees is a bad sign.

The clock on the wall is melting, but its hands are spinning faster. Your boss is now also your high school gym teacher, blowing a whistle. "The presentation starts NOW," they say, and suddenly you're standing in front of a packed boardroom wearing only a towel — and the towel is made of spreadsheets.

You begin presenting. The slides advance on their own: a photo of your childhood dog, a lasagna recipe, a map of a country that doesn't exist. Everyone applauds. The impossible deadline has been met, somehow, though you did nothing at all.

Then the fire alarm sounds, but it's not a fire — it's raining *inside* the building. Your boss hands you an umbrella and whispers, "Excellent work. Now do it all again, but backwards."

And the rain falls upward, and the piano plays you, and—

...you wake up.
