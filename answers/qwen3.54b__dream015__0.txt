Depending on how your dream wants to go, here are three ways to complete the narrative, each with a slightly different tone.

### Option 1: The Surreal Awakening
> "...stop trying to find the one last city where resources overflow." You look around at the desolate wasteland; suddenly, a single flower blooms in the cracked asphalt—not because you planted it, but because you *stopped looking* for food when the hunger became fear. The world softens like an old painting coming back to life, and for the first time, your breath is cool enough to not turn into smoke immediately.

### Option 2: The Metaphorical Turn
> "...realize that the apocalypse wasn't the end of the world, but the erasure of a lie you told yourself." The gray sky begins to shift from ash to gold, and every shadow you see reveals an old friend you forgot existed. You pick your own lock on your life again, realizing scarcity only exists when you refuse to believe there is enough for both you and them.

### Option 3: The Philosophical Peace
> "...stop running away from the dust, and start letting it settle into your feet." You realize the dangers aren't external; they are the echoes of everything you have ever been afraid of becoming. You finally plant a tree where the smoke used to be, watching its roots go deep into something that wasn't there before, knowing the ground is no longer empty—it is waiting.
