
You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to...

...figure out *when* you are. The air smells different—less of exhaust, more of coal smoke, horse manure, and baking bread. The sounds are a symphony of clopping hooves, distant factory whistles, and the murmur of a crowd speaking in a dialect you almost recognize. Your phone is a dead, smooth stone in your pocket. No signal, no maps, no history at your fingertips.

Your first, panicked thought is *home*. But the skyline is wrong—no skyscrapers, no blinking airplanes. Instead, church steeples and factory chimneys pierce the sky. You catch your reflection in a darkened shop window: your strange, synthetic-fiber clothes mark you as an outsider immediately.

You need three things, and you need them fast:
1.  **Shelter:** A place to hide, to think, to not be questioned.
2.  **Currency:** Whatever passes for money here. Your modern cash is just paper.
3.  **Information:** A newspaper, a library, a person who will talk to you. You have to understand the rules of this world before someone decides you're a spy, a madperson, or a tempting target.

You duck into an alley, heart pounding. The dream-logic of your arrival is already fading, replaced by the visceral, overwhelming *realness* of the past. The cobblestones are uneven under your feet. The cold is sharper, wetter. You have to adapt, and fast, or this dream could become a very permanent nightmare.

**What do you do first?** (Find a newspaper to check the date? Offer to work for food? Try to find a church or charity for shelter?)
