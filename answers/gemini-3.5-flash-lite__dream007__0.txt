"Wait," I say, my voice sounding hollow and distant, like it's traveling up from the bottom of a well. "We're doing it again."

My friend blinks. Their face—which a second ago was sharp with frustration—softens, though their features begin to blur at the edges, shifting into someone else I used to know years ago. 

"Doing what?" they ask, but the sound of their voice doesn't match the movement of their mouth. 

I look down at my hands. They look semi-transparent, the floorboards visible right through my fingertips. *Ah,* I realize, a sudden spark of lucidity cutting through the dream-fog. *I'm dreaming.* 

I look back up at my friend, whose form is now dissolving into a swirl of warm, amber light. The argument no longer matters; the stakes of the disagreement evaporate, replaced by a profound, floating curiosity. 

"Nothing," I smile, feeling myself start to lift off the floor as the bedroom ceiling dissolves into a star-filled sky. "Never mind."
