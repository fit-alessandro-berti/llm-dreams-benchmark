...settle it the only way that makes sense: by climbing the staircase that has suddenly appeared in the middle of the kitchen.

The staircase wasn't there a moment ago, but neither of you questions it. That's how these things work. Your friend goes first, still talking over their shoulder, and you notice their voice has changed—it sounds like your mother now, then like a teacher you had in fourth grade, then back again.

"You never listen," they say, except their mouth doesn't move.

The stairs keep going. You climb past floors that shouldn't exist—one is your childhood bedroom, perfectly preserved, except the walls are breathing slightly. Another is an ocean, somehow vertical, with fish swimming past as if the air were water. You want to stop and look, but the argument feels urgent, even though you're starting to forget what it was about.

"It was about the door," your friend says, reading your thoughts. "You said it was blue."

"It *was* blue," you insist, suddenly furious again, though you have no idea what door they mean.

At the top of the staircase is a small room with a single window. Outside the window is the kitchen you just left—you can see yourselves down there, still arguing, tiny figures gesturing at each other. Your friend sits down on a chair that is also somehow a swing, and sighs.

"I don't want to fight anymore," they say, and their face flickers—for just a second they look like everyone you've ever loved, all at once.

"Me neither," you say, and the moment you say it, the argument dissolves entirely, the way urgent things do in dreams. You can't remember a single word of it. You sit beside them on the swing-chair, and the window shows the ocean now, the vertical one, golden with a sunset that comes from below instead of above.

"We should head back down," your friend says.

But when you turn around, the staircase is gone, and somehow you know—with the certain, untroubled logic of dreams—that this is fine, that you were always going to end up here, and that the door, in the end, really was blue.

You start to wake up. The room dissolves at the edges first...
