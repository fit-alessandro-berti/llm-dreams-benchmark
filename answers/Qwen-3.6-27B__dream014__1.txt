As you walk alongside them, the chaotic noise of the city begins to soften, transforming into a harmonious hum that feels strangely comforting. The strangers don’t speak in words, but rather share glances and gestures that convey understanding and warmth.

One of them, an elderly woman with eyes like starlit skies, points toward a narrow alleyway hidden between two towering skyscrapers. You follow her lead, expecting darkness, but instead, you step into a vibrant garden suspended in mid-air, defying gravity. Flowers bloom in colors you’ve never seen before, pulsing gently with light. The group smiles, nods once, and then begins to fade away like mist in the morning sun, leaving you alone—but no longer lost. You realize you were never looking for a place on a map; you were looking for a sense of belonging, and here, in this impossible garden, you finally find it.
