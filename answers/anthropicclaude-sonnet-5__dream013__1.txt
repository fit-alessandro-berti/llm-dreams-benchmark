...notice the walls are breathing, ever so slightly—expanding and contracting like the maze itself is alive. The first puzzle appears etched in silver light on a stone door: a sequence of symbols that shift when you're not looking directly at them. You solve it by squinting, catching them in your peripheral vision where they hold still just long enough to reveal a pattern—a spiral of numbers that resolves into the number 7. The door dissolves into mist.

Beyond it, the corridor splits into three identical paths, each humming a different note. You realize the maze is testing not just logic but intuition. You choose the middle path because it hums in a key that feels like *home*, though you can't explain why.

The next puzzle is a room with no floor, only floating platforms shaped like question marks. Each platform sinks when you stand on the wrong answer to a riddle whispered by the walls: *"I am not alive, but I grow; I don't have lungs, but I need air."* You step onto the platform labeled FIRE, and it holds. The others dissolve into the dark below.

As you go deeper, the puzzles become stranger, less logical and more emotional—one door only opens when you remember a specific smell from your childhood; another requires you to forgive someone who was never real to begin with. You start to suspect the maze isn't testing your intelligence at all.

It's showing you what your mind does when it's afraid of getting lost.

Then, ahead, a final door appears—unlocked, wide open, no puzzle at all.

You wake up right as you step through it, and you can't remember what was on the other side, only that it felt like relief.
