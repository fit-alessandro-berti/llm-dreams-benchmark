...each one seems to shift just as you approach. The first group is laughing over something you can’t quite hear—when you get close, their faces blur, and they’re suddenly discussing quantum physics in hushed tones. The second group is silent, watching you with eyes that reflect the room like mirrors. One of them mouths a word: *"Remember."* You don’t know what to remember, but the word lingers like a half-remembered song.

Then you see your friend across the room, but between you and them, the floor tilts unnaturally, and the crowd parts—not to let you through, but to reveal a mirror on the ground. Your reflection isn’t you. It’s someone older, wearing clothes you’ve never seen before, holding a key that wasn’t in your hand a moment ago. The reflection smiles, but its mouth doesn’t move. *"You’re almost there,"* it says—or maybe it’s just the echo of your own thought.

Your friend calls your name, but their voice comes from everywhere and nowhere. The room starts to dissolve at the edges, colors bleeding into white. You reach for the key, but your fingers pass through the mirror like smoke. Then—

**You wake up.**

(How did your dream end? Did you take the key? Did the reflection follow you?)
