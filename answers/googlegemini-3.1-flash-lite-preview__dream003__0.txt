As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and shifting geometries etched into the dark, cold obsidian. They aren't just carvings; they are pathways. As your thumb brushes over a jagged line, the pattern glows with a faint, pulses-like luminescence, mirroring the rhythm of your own heartbeat.

Suddenly, the air grows thin and smells of ozone and ancient parchment. The box begins to hum—a low, resonant vibration that ripples through your fingertips and climbs up your arms, settling deep in your chest.

You realize then that the box isn't locked by a mechanical latch, but by a syncopation. 

Closing your eyes, you stop trying to pry it open and instead let your pulse steady. You place both palms flat against the lid, feeling the patterns beneath your skin begin to writhe and rearrange themselves, like metallic liquid finding its true shape. The humming rises to a crescendo, vibrating your very teeth, until the obsidian turns supple and warm, softening like wax under a summer sun.

The lid doesn't swing open; it dissolves into a fine, swirling mist of starlight. 

Inside, there is no object, no treasure, no note. There is only a reflection—but it isn't your own. It is a view of the room you are currently standing in, seen from a thousand miles above, and in that reflection, you see yourself standing in the dream, holding the box. 

You reach into the mist, and as your fingers touch the air inside the box, the dream-world begins to fold. The walls of the room peel away like shedding skin, revealing a vast, infinite horizon of violet clouds. You realize with a jolt of exhilaration that the box wasn't meant to be opened to find something inside. 

It was meant to be opened so that *you* could step through it. 

You take a breath, lean forward into the cooling mist, and feel the gravity shift—not down, but *out*. You step forward, the floor vanishes beneath your feet, and you begin to fall upward into a sky filled with waking suns.
