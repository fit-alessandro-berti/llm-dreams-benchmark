You start to sprint down a cobblestone alley, the pavement slick with a rain that seems to fall only from the sky above a single, giant clock tower that chimes in a rhythm you can’t quite decipher. The air smells faintly of ozone and fresh‑baked pastries, a comforting oddity that makes your heart race faster than the ticking seconds. Around you, towering glass skyscrapers melt into baroque cathedrals, their stained‑glass windows flashing neon hues that flicker like nervous thoughts.

You pull out a crumpled piece of paper from your pocket—an old, yellowed map you don’t remember drawing. The ink shivers, the lines rearranging themselves as you watch, as if the city itself is trying to guide you. You follow the winding path that now points toward a narrow street lined with lanterns that flicker in a slow, rhythmic pulse. At the end of the street, a massive, brass door stands ajar, its hinges singing a low, resonant hum.

A stranger in a trench coat, whose face you can never quite focus on, steps out from the shadows. “The meeting is just beyond the door,” he says, his voice echoing like a distant radio. “But you must first answer the question that the city whispers.” He holds out a single, translucent card that reads: **“What do you seek more than the moment?”**

Your mind races. Do you answer with the urgency of the meeting, the need to be punctual, the fear of being late? Or do you pause, feel the weight of the dream, the surreal pulse of the city, and respond with a deeper longing—perhaps a desire for belonging, understanding, or a moment of clarity amidst chaos?

You take a breath, the cool night air tingling on your skin, and you speak the answer into the brass door. The door shudders, swings open wider, and beyond it you see a vast, softly lit atrium. In its center, a circular table made of floating glass, surrounded by chairs that hover gently above the ground. At the head of the table, a figure whose silhouette shifts between an old friend and a stranger raises a hand in greeting.

“The meeting has already begun,” the figure says, smiling faintly. “You’ve been here all along, just learning the way.” The clock tower’s chime softens, the city’s neon lights dim to a warm amber glow, and the urgency you felt fades into a strange, comforting sense that you are exactly where you need to be, even if you can’t remember why.

The dream settles, and the last thing you hear before the world blurs into soft, cotton‑like sleep is the faint echo of your own voice, repeating the answer you gave: **“I seek to understand the path beneath my feet.”** The atrium’s light flickers, and the night wraps around you like a blanket, leaving you with a lingering sense of wonder and the gentle reminder that sometimes the most important meetings happen within the labyrinth of our own minds.
