You are at a large social gathering where you only know one person. As you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people and they all already know your name.

It's in a house that is too big. From the outside it looked like a normal apartment, but inside it just keeps going. The living room opens into a high school gymnasium. The kitchen opens into a backyard that is somehow also a beach at dusk.

You start searching.

The first group is in the kitchen. They're all standing around an island that has no food on it, just a huge, untouched sheet cake that says HAPPY BIRTHDAY in blue frosting, but no name. They're deep in conversation, laughing too hard, wine glasses empty but they keep pretending to drink. You ask if they've seen your friend. One woman turns to you and says, "Oh, we thought YOU were with *them*," and gestures down a hallway. You didn't come with anyone.

You squeeze down that hallway. The walls are lined with coats that belong to no one you know, but they all smell faintly familiar. Your phone buzzes. You pull it out to text your one person - your screen is cracked and the keyboard is melting. The letters slide off every time you try to press them.

In the next room, everyone is sitting on the floor in a circle, facing inward, not talking at all. They're all looking at something in the middle that you can't quite see. As you step around them, you realize every person in the circle has your friend's jacket on. Your friend's exact jacket. When you ask where they got it, they all look up at you at once and smile politely, like you said something slightly embarrassing. One of them says, "She was just here."

The party gets louder the further you go, but the music never has a beat. You find yourself in the backyard-beach now. A group of old men are barbecuing, but they're grilling Polaroids. They pick them up with tongs and the pictures sizzle. You see your face in one of them for a second before it curls and turns black.

And then — finally — you see the back of your person's head by the fence that marks where the beach ends and the ocean doesn't start.

You call their name. You push through the last group — this group is just mannequins wearing soft sweaters, but everyone is treating them like real guests — and you touch their shoulder.

They turn around. And for a half-second of perfect, crushing dream-logic, you don't recognize them at all, even though you know it's them. Their face is wrong, or maybe it's right and you've forgotten your own face.

They light up. "There you are! I've been looking for you everywhere."

And you realize everyone at this party has been waiting for the two of you to finally find each other so the party can really start.
