You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to follow the faint, almost mythical whispers of a "Garden" said to be hidden deep within the irradiated ruins of the old city—a place where life, real life, still thrives.

The air is thick with dust and the scent of decay. Your boots crunch over broken glass and twisted metal as you navigate the skeletal remains of skyscrapers. Shadows seem to move with a purpose of their own. You have a salvaged geiger counter ticking softly on your belt, a half-empty canteen, and a knife that feels too small for the things you've seen.

Days blur. You scavenge where you can, avoiding the predatory gangs that rule the highways and the worse things that come out at night—pale, twisted creatures that were once human. Sleep is fitful, filled with visions of the world before, of green things and clear water.

One evening, as the sickly orange sun bleeds into the horizon, you find a clue: a crude map scratched into the wall of a subway tunnel, pointing toward the city's heart. It's marked with a symbol you've seen in your dreams—a spiraling leaf. Hope, a dangerous and fragile thing, flickers in your chest.

You press on, deeper into the concrete labyrinth. The whispers grow louder, not in your ears, but in your mind. They speak of renewal, but also of a price. The boundary between dream and reality thins. Are you still asleep? Is this the dream within the dream? The crumbling cityscape begins to shift, walls breathing like living tissue, streets twisting into impossible geometries.

You reach the designated place: a vast, overgrown plaza at the base of the tallest ruin. But the Garden is not what you imagined. It is a pulsating, bioluminescent fungal forest, beautiful and horrifying. At its center stands a figure—or perhaps it is a tree shaped like a woman, her bark-like skin etched with sorrow. She turns hollow eyes toward you.

"You have come to consume, like all the others," her voice is the rustle of leaves and the crack of dry earth. "To take the last life for yourself."

You have a choice now, in this dream-apocalypse. Do you reach for your knife, or do you drop to your knees and offer the last water from your canteen to the parched roots at her feet? The dream holds its breath, waiting for your decision, as the sky above fractures like a mirror.
