...you begin to notice that the puzzles are getting *easier*.

This should be a relief. It isn't.

The first chamber had a lock with seven tumblers and a riddle in a language that kept changing its mind about its own grammar. You worked at it for what felt like a season. When it opened, your hands were shaking, and you were proud in a way that felt like being warm.

The fourth chamber had a chessboard where the pieces argued about the rules. You listened to all of them. You found the move that let every piece be right at once. The door dissolved like sugar.

But the eleventh chamber has a door with a sign that says PUSH, and you push it, and it opens.

The twelfth has a door already open.

The thirteenth has no door because there is no wall, just the corridor continuing, and you understand — the way you understand things in dreams, all at once, without reasoning — that the maze has stopped testing you. Not because you passed. Because it has finished *reading* you, and it has what it needs.

You turn around to go back. The corridor behind you is a corridor. This is the wrong thing for it to be. It should be the eleventh chamber, the tenth, the seven tumblers. Instead there are new doors, and behind each one you can hear someone concentrating. That specific silence. The held breath of a mind bearing down on a problem.

You open one.

Inside is a chamber with a chessboard where the pieces are arguing, and someone is standing over it, working, absolutely absorbed, and it is you — not a mirror-you, not a symbol, just you a little earlier, with the pride still ahead of you like weather coming in.

You want to tell them something. You're not sure what. *It gets easier* seems cruel. *Take longer* seems cruel too.

So you stand in the doorway and watch yourself think, and it is the most beautiful thing you have ever seen — the small movements of attention, the way a mind reaches out and turns something over and turns it over again — and you realize that this was never the corridor between the puzzles.

The concentration *was* the maze. The walls are made of it. Every hallway you walked was a hallway you built by caring about the answer, and the reason the doors are opening on their own now is that you have started to walk without building.

You go back into the chamber. You sit down at the board. You let the pieces begin their argument from the beginning, and you don't skip ahead, though you know how it ends.

Walls rise gently on either side of you, like someone pulling a blanket up.

And you think, just before the dream lets go: *good. I would like to be lost a while longer.*
