…as you reach toward it, the canvas breathes.

A single drop of blue falls from somewhere above and lands in its center. It spreads outward like water, becoming first an ocean, then an eye, then a doorway. From the doorway steps a version of you made entirely of shifting colors.

“You forgot this,” they say, placing a small golden shape in your hand.

It is warm, and it has no name.

Around you, the landscape pauses. The clouds stop changing. The mountains hold their breath. You understand that the blank canvas was never waiting for a picture—it was waiting for a memory.

You press the golden shape against the canvas.

At once, everything you have ever dreamed floods across it: rooms with no ceilings, voices speaking backward, a childhood street made of starlight, a moon resting quietly in a teacup. The colors whirl faster and faster until they become white.

Then the canvas is blank again.

But this time, there is a small painted door in one corner.

It slowly opens.

Behind it, you hear someone whisper your name just as you begin to wake.
