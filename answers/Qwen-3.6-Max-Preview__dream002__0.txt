A single drop of rain falls, landing on your cheek with surprising warmth. Then another. And another. But these aren’t ordinary drops; they shimmer with an iridescent glow, like liquid pearls. As they hit the forest floor, tiny flowers bloom instantly in their wake—vibrant blues, electric yellows, and fiery oranges erupting from the moss in rapid succession.

The wind picks up, not with a howl, but with a melodic hum, vibrating through your bones. You realize you aren’t afraid. Instead, you feel a strange sense of anticipation. The storm isn’t coming to destroy; it’s coming to reveal.

You look down at your hands and notice they are beginning to turn translucent, glowing with the same soft light as the rain. The boundary between you and the forest begins to blur. Are you walking through the woods, or are the woods walking through you?

Suddenly, lightning strikes—not with a crash, but with a chord of music that resonates in your chest. In that flash of white light, you see a path ahead that wasn’t there before: a bridge made of woven starlight, leading upward into the clouds.

Do you step onto the bridge, or do you stay rooted in the blooming earth?
