**The dream continues...**

You decide to build a small garden.

It seems absurd at first—the sky is the color of ash, and somewhere in the distance you can hear the metallic groan of something large moving through the ruins. But your hands are already digging through rubble, pulling out a cracked ceramic pot, then another. You find a trickle of water seeping from a broken pipe and cup it in your palms.

The seeds come from nowhere and everywhere, the way things do in dreams—maybe from a pocket you don't remember filling, maybe from the wind itself. You press them into the grey soil.

A figure watches you from the shadow of a collapsed overpass. They don't approach, not yet. In the logic of the dream, you understand they've forgotten what growing things looks like, and something about the motion of your hands—patient, useless, human—holds them there.

Days pass in the compressed time of dreams. A single green shoot pushes up through the cracked concrete, absurdly bright against all that grey. Others come to look. No one speaks much. But someone brings you a strip of cloth to shield the seedling from the wind, and someone else siphons water from a rusted tank without being asked.

You realize, standing there with dirt under your fingernails in a world that has ended, that you were never really planting vegetables.

You were planting a reason for people to stand next to each other again.

The shoot bends slightly in a breeze that, for the first time, doesn't smell like smoke—
