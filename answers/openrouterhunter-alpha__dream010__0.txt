As the competition progresses, you face various challenges and strange impossibilities that only dreams allow.

---

**The first challenge** takes place in a library where every book is blank. The judges tell you to find the story that matters most. You realize the pages aren't empty — they're mirrors. You hold one up and see your own reflection, and slowly, words begin forming around your edges, describing every question you've ever asked, every curiosity that ever kept you awake. You carry the book to the podium. It's the only one with words in it.

**The second challenge** is a maze made of sound. Walls of humming, buzzing, layered frequencies rise around you — some pleasant, some disorienting. You have to navigate to the center by listening. You stop trying to find the "right" path and instead start listening to *all* of it at once. The noise organizes itself. You find the center not by filtering, but by holding everything.

**The third challenge** is the hardest. They ask you to give something up. Not an object — an assumption. A certainty you've carried so long you forgot it was there. You stand in front of the panel and realize the certainty you've been holding is that you need to *win*. You let it go, and the room fills with light so warm it feels like morning.

---

You don't remember exactly how the competition ends. Dreams are like that — they dissolve at the edges.

But you wake with a strange, settled feeling. Not the thrill of victory, but something quieter. The sense that the dream job was never at the finish line. It was in the blank books, the sound maze, the morning light.
