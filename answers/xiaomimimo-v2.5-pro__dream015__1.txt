...start scavenging through the ruins of a library, because even when civilization collapses, knowledge remains a lifeline.

The air is thick with dust and the distant hum of something mechanical. You pull a tattered backpack tighter on your shoulders. Your first find is a water purification manual—you slide it into your pack with the reverence of a sacred text. Then, your flashlight beam catches something glinting behind a collapsed shelf: a sealed, military-grade case.

With effort, you pry it open. Inside, nestled in foam, is a solar-powered radio receiver, a stack of seed packets (tomatoes, beans, hardy greens), and a small, handwritten journal. The last entry is dated a week before everything fell apart.

It reads: *"If you find this, know there's a community at the old Millson Observatory. The stars still shine. We are broadcasting a safe signal every dawn. Don't give up."*

A weight lifts from your chest. Hope is a resource too, and right now, it's overflowing. You pack everything carefully. Tomorrow, at dawn, you'll tune in. For tonight, you hunker down between the shelves, surrounded by stories of worlds that endured, and you dream not of what was lost, but of what might still be grown.
