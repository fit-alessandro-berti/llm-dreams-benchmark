You start to ask strangers for directions, but everyone answers in a language you almost understand.

A woman selling paper flowers points upward. A child in a velvet suit hands you a map made of skin-thin ice. An old man with a suitcase full of alarm clocks tells you, very seriously, “You’re already late for the meeting you haven’t missed yet.”

The streets rearrange themselves whenever you look away.

You decide to follow the tallest building, a black glass tower with your name glowing near the top. As you walk toward it, the city becomes quieter. Cars move without drivers. Pigeons sit in neat rows on telephone wires, watching you. Every shop window displays the same object: a small brass key.

At the base of the tower is a revolving door that turns much too slowly. Inside, the lobby has no floor—only a deep blue sky beneath a sheet of transparent glass. At the reception desk, a woman wearing your face smiles and asks for your appointment letter.

“I don’t have it,” you say.

She nods, as if this is exactly what she expected, and slides the brass key across the desk.

The elevator opens behind you.

There are no buttons inside, only a mirror. In the mirror, you are older, wearing clothes you do not recognize, holding a folder labeled **IMPORTANT MEETING**. Your reflection raises one finger to its lips.

The elevator begins to descend.

When the doors open, you step into a room filled with people seated around a long table. Every chair is occupied except one. At the far end sits someone hidden by a bright white light.

“You’re late,” they say.

You sit down.

The folder in front of you opens by itself. Inside is a single blank page. Slowly, words appear in your own handwriting:

**Tell us what you saw when you woke up.**

And then, somewhere far away, an alarm begins to ring.
