It tells you of doors you've walked past, of choices unmade, of paths that branched in the night while you were sleeping.

The patterns on the box rearrange one final time, forming a face—your face, but smiling in a way you don't quite recognize. The lid clicks open by itself, but inside there is no treasure, no mechanism, no explanation. There is only a small mirror, angled perfectly to reflect the sky above you. You look up, and the sky is filled with doors. Thousands of them. Each one slightly ajar. Each one waiting.

The box whispers once more: *"Choose, but choose knowing that not choosing is also a choice."*

Just as your hand reaches toward the closest door, you feel a familiar tug—the dream thinning at its edges, reality creeping back in like dawn through curtains. The doors above begin to blur. The box grows cold. And in that final moment before waking, you catch one last glimpse of something standing in the doorway you'd almost reached—a figure made of light, holding out its hand, waiting for a meeting that will have to wait for another night.
