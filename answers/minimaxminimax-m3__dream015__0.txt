And there, wedged beneath a toppled display case, you find it: a hand-crank radio, half-buried but intact.

Your pulse quickens. In this world of isolation, such a device could mean *contact*—others out there, survivors, perhaps even a signal that the old frequencies still whisper life across the wasteland.

You kneel, brushing away debris, fingers trembling as they close around the cold metal handle. For a moment, you hesitate. Turning it on means risking something terrible: the confirmation that you are truly, utterly alone.

But the dream pulls you forward. You crank.

Static. Sighing white noise, like the ghosts of broadcasts long dead.

Then—

A voice. Faint. Speaking in numbers.

*"—37.7749, -122.4194. Coordinates. If anyone can hear this, the archive is still... still... we have the seeds. We have the seeds. Find us."*

The broadcast loops, distant and crackling, but unmistakably *human*.
