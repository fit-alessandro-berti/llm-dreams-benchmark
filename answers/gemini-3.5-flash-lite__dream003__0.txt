Without a sound, the box springs open. There are no springs or gears—it simply decides to yield. Inside, there is no physical object, only a swirling vortex of deep indigo light that smells faintly of rain on warm dust and old paper. 

Drawn by a curiosity heavier than gravity, you reach out and press your index finger into the light. 

Instantly, the floor beneath you dissolves. You aren't falling; you are expanding, dissolving into a vast, starry expanse where memories you've never lived—of ancient cities made of glass, of oceans of liquid silver, and of whispering to stars in a forgotten language—flood into your mind. 

You realize with a sudden, lucid jolt that you aren't just dreaming the box. 

The box is dreaming *you*. 

And then, a voice echoes from everywhere and nowhere at once, asking a single question: 

*“Now that you are awake, what world will you build next?”*
