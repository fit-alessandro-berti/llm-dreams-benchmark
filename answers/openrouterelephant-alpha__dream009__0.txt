...find a way to understand this world without a single familiar landmark.  

The air smells of damp earth and wildflowers. Sunlight filters through unfamiliar trees, casting long, shifting shadows. No cars, no phones, no hum of electricity—only the distant call of birds and the whisper of leaves.  

You notice markings carved into a stone nearby, not quite writing, but symbols arranged with intention. They seem to map the path forward. Following them, you come to a clearing where people in simple woven garments gather around a fire, sharing food and quiet conversation. They look up as you approach—not with fear, but with a calm, assessing gaze, as if they’ve been expecting someone who arrived out of time.  

One steps forward, holding out a small, smooth object—an intricately carved wooden tablet. It bears a single symbol: a circle with a line beneath, like a horizon. They gesture to the sky, then to the tablet, then to you.  

You realize you need to learn their language, not just their words, but their rhythm of thought. So you sit by the fire, taking the tablet, and begin to mimic the sounds they make, matching the patterns they use to name the stars, the rivers, and the seasons. The dream isn’t about returning—it’s about belonging here, in this unbroken thread of time, until the world feels like home.
