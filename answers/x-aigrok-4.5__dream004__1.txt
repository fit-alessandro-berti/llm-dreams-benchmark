You are dreaming. You continue:

...you come across a blank canvas and a set of brushes that seem to grow like living vines from the ever-shifting ground. The moment your fingers close around one, the canvas hums—not with sound, but with a freckling of impossible hues that flash across its surface like heat lightning. You dip the brush into the air itself (there is no palette; the air is the palette), and a color that has never existed before—something between the memory of rain and the taste of forgotten music—blooms along the bristles.

You paint a single circle. Instantly the circle steps off the canvas, becomes a doorway, and the doorway folds itself into a spiral staircase of molten turquoise that corkscrews upward into a sky now made of quilted emerald lightning. As you climb, the steps rearrange under your feet into constellations of soft black velvet, each star a tiny eye that blinks open and whispers half-familiar names. Through one of those eyes you fall, weightless, into a forest where the trees are tall ribbons of spun glass, each leaf a tiny prism that fractures your laughter into schools of rainbow fish darting through the branches.

At the forest’s heart the blank canvas has followed you, or perhaps it has always been ahead. It now hangs between two glass trunks like a portal of pure potential. Rectangles of pure white Boulean absence wait. You realize, with dream-certainty, that every mark you make is not merely art but a rewrite of the landscape’s rules. You paint a doorway again—this time into a city of liquid geometry whose streets are rivers of copper light and whose buildings breathe, expanding and contracting like lungs filled with twilight. Figures made of mirrored smoke greet you, their faces rearranging into every person you’ve ever loved and every stranger who has ever crossed your path, all speaking in kaleidoscopic chords.

Then the colors reverse. Night becomes day becomes chrome becomes velvet cornflower. The canvas sighs, and you understand the final secret of this world: you are both the artist and the paint. With one last brushstroke you sign your true name—the one known only in dreams—across the living sky. The signature dissolves into golden pollen that settles everywhere, quilting the chaos into a quilt of quiet wonder.

You awaken just as the last petal of color fades, still tasting the impossible hue on your tongue and knowing—somewhere beyond waking—that the canvas is still out there, waiting for the next dreamer to continue the painting.
