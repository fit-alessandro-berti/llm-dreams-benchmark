...the sky begins to bruise — deep purples and greens spreading overhead like ink in water. The trees start to sway, though there's no wind yet, and you realize they're whispering to each other in a language you almost understand.

The first raindrops fall *upward*, rising from the earth toward the clouds. You look down and discover the forest floor has become a mirror, and beneath your feet stretches another sky, another storm, another version of you walking upside down through an upside-down forest.

You begin to run, though you couldn't say whether you're fleeing something or being called. Lanterns flicker to life along the path as you pass, as though the forest itself is lighting your way home — except home suddenly feels far away, like a word whose meaning you've forgotten.

Then the rain comes all at once: warm, silver, weightless. It doesn't soak you. It passes *through* you, like light. You look at your hands and find them glowing faintly, translucent, and you understand — the way you only understand things in dreams — that the storm was never coming for you.

The storm *is* you. You are the weather changing.

Thunder rolls in your chest like a second heartbeat. The trees bow as you pass. And just before you wake, you spot a red door standing alone in a clearing, slightly open, golden light spilling through the gap — and you know that if you walked through it, you'd finally remember what the trees were trying to tell you.
