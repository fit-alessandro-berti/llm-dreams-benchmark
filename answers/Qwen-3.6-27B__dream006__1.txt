The audience doesn’t gasp or laugh; they simply stand up in unison, reaching out to catch the birds. As each person catches one, they fold it again into a different shape—a boat, a crane, a house—and place it gently on the floor.

The stage begins to fill with these intricate paper creations, forming a mosaic that glows with a soft, inner light. You look down at your hands and realize you are holding a single sheet of blank white paper. Without thinking, you begin to fold it, following an instinct you didn’t know you had. When you finish, you hold up a perfect, tiny star. The moment you release it, it floats upward, joining hundreds of others rising from the crowd, turning the dark auditorium into a miniature galaxy. You feel a profound sense of peace, as if you have finally said everything you needed to say without uttering a single syllable.
