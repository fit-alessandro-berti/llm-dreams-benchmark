…reach for your laptop, but the keyboard has rearranged itself into tiny clock faces.

Every key ticks.

Your boss smiles as if this is normal. “Just begin,” they say.

You press one of the clocks, and the entire office exhales. The fluorescent lights soften into sunrise. Cubicles stretch into long hallways lined with doors, each labeled with a task you haven’t done yet: **Budget**, **Presentation**, **Client Call**, **Something You Forgot**.

You open the nearest door and find not a conference room, but your childhood bedroom. The project brief is taped to the ceiling, written in crayon. You stand on the bed to read it, but the words keep changing:

**Finish everything before you wake up.**

Behind you, your boss clears their throat. They are now wearing pajamas and holding a mug that says *World’s Best Deadline*. “Progress?” they ask.

You try to explain that this is impossible, but your voice comes out as printer paper. Sheets spill from your mouth, fluttering across the floor. Your coworkers gather them up and nod seriously, as though the papers contain a brilliant strategy.

Then the office phone rings.

You pick it up.

On the other end is your own voice, calm and amused. “You don’t have to finish the project,” it says. “You only have to start.”

So you press the smallest clock on the keyboard.

Everything stops ticking.

The deadline folds itself into a paper airplane and sails out the window. Your boss watches it go, suddenly relieved. The walls of the office become clouds, your desk becomes a raft, and the building drifts gently into a blue morning sky.

Just before you wake, you see the project title glowing on your screen:

**How to Let Go of Things That Were Never Yours to Carry.**
