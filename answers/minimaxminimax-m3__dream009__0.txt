*find your way back home, but the rules of this world are stranger than you could have imagined.*

---

The mist clings to your skin like wet silk. You sit up on the earthen floor of a thatched-roof cottage, blinking against the pale dawn light filtering through a window of oiled parchment. Your body aches—not from sleep, but as if the dream itself has reshaped your bones. You are wearing linen, hand-stitched and smelling faintly of woodsmoke and lavender.

Outside, you hear it: not the hum of traffic, not the distant whine of an air conditioner, but the lowing of cattle, the clatter of a wooden cart on a dirt road, the clanging of a blacksmith's hammer somewhere beyond the hill.

You step out into a village that looks medieval—perhaps European, perhaps 14th century, perhaps something older. The people bow slightly as you pass. They call you *Stranger*, but with a reverence that unsettles you. You are not a tourist here. You are something else.

As you explore, you begin to understand the rules:

**The First Rule:** You cannot lie. When you try, your tongue goes numb, and your words emerge as the truth you buried. A merchant asks if you like his cloth. You say, "It's plain," and the room goes silent.

**The Second Rule:** Every person you meet shows you a memory unbidden—a child waving from a window, a funeral, a first kiss, a moment of terrible shame. You see it flash in their eyes like heat lightning. You cannot look away.

**The Third Rule:** The sun sets at a different speed here. Sometimes hours pass in what feels like minutes. Sometimes a single conversation takes three days.

The villagers are kind, but they watch you with the patience of those waiting for something to begin. An old woman presses a cup of herbal tea into your hands and says, "You are not lost, *Stranger*. You are being *called*."

You realize, with a chill that has nothing to do with the morning air, that the path home is not a road you walk—it is a *memory* you must remember. Somewhere in your mind, in a life you are only just beginning to recall, is the door. And every person you meet, every moment you witness unbidden, is a thread drawing you closer to it.

But there is a complication. The old woman takes your hand and whispers it:

"Many have been *called*, *Stranger*. Few have chosen to return."

And in your chest, a small, traitorous part of you whispers back: *What if I don't want to?*

The sun climbs higher. Somewhere, a bell begins to ring.

---

*What happens next, dreamer? Do you chase the memory home, or do you sit down on the cool stone step and ask the old woman to tell you everything she knows?*
