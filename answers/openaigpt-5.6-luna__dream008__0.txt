You are at your workplace, and suddenly, you are given a project with an impossible deadline. Your boss is waiting for you to start, and you realize that every clock in the office is counting backward.

You open the project folder, but instead of documents, it contains photographs of places you have never visited. Each image is labeled with a time—*10:03, 10:04, 10:05*—and as the minutes pass, the photographs begin changing.

Your coworkers sit silently at their desks, typing with their eyes closed. The fluorescent lights flicker. Somewhere, a printer starts running, endlessly producing blank pages.

Your boss leans over your shoulder and whispers, “You’ve already finished this.”

You turn to ask what they mean, but the office has vanished. You are standing in a vast train station beneath a purple sky, holding a completed report in your hands. On its cover is your name—and beneath it, tomorrow’s date.

A train arrives without making a sound. The doors open.

Inside, every seat is occupied by another version of you, each one looking up with the same exhausted expression. One of them slides over and says, “Hurry. We’re late.”

You step aboard.

The doors close, the train begins to move, and for the first time all day, the clock starts counting forward.
