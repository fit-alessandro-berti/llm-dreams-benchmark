**Dream Continuation:**

The air hums with an eerie, resonant frequency—like the chime of a distant bell, but it’s not quite right. Your fingers twitch as you realize the walls aren’t just walls anymore. They’re *alive*, shifting slightly when you touch them, revealing hidden grooves or symbols that pulse faintly under your fingertips. One of them glows faintly blue, and when you trace it, the maze *reacts*: a narrow path opens up ahead, but it’s guarded by a figure standing in the center—a silhouette that resembles a child, yet their limbs are too long, their posture rigid as if frozen mid-jump.

*"You’ve come too far,"* the voice whispers—not in words, but in the vibration of the air itself. *"The puzzles are your teachers. Solve them, and you’ll learn the truth. Fail, and the walls will swallow you whole."*

You kneel, and the ground beneath you splits open into a series of interlocking gears. Each one is inscribed with a riddle, but the language shifts as you look closer—it’s not Latin or ancient script, but something *modern*, something you recognize from a half-remembered childhood game: *"What has keys but no locks, space but no room, and you can enter but not go inside?"*

The answer is simple, but the maze *doesn’t* accept it. The gears grind to a halt, and the child-figure tilts their head. *"Close enough,"* they say. *"But the truth is deeper."* The ground trembles, and the gears begin to spin backward, dragging you into a tunnel of shifting light. The walls now glow with shifting constellations—some familiar, others like constellations you’ve never seen before.

Then, you hear it: a sound like a thousand whispers at once, overlapping into a single, haunting melody. The music is the maze itself, weaving through the air, and suddenly, the puzzles aren’t just puzzles—they’re *questions*. Not about logic, but about *identity*. *"Who are you, really?"* the music asks. *"The person you think you are? The person you’ve become? Or the person you’re still becoming?"*

You stand at the edge of a vast, empty plain where the sky is a bruise of violet and gold. The wind carries the scent of rain that never comes, and in the distance, a single figure stands—tall, cloaked, their face obscured by a mask that’s not quite human. They raise a hand, and the ground splits open to reveal a spiral staircase descending into darkness. *"The choice is yours,"* they say. *"Step forward, and you’ll see the end. Or turn back, and the maze will remember you."*

But as you hesitate, the walls around you *breathe*. The puzzles rearrange themselves, forming a new challenge: a mirror that reflects not your face, but the faces of everyone you’ve ever known—some smiling, some hollow-eyed. *"Choose wisely,"* the mirror whispers. *"Because the maze doesn’t forget."*
