At first, you panic. Every solved puzzle should mean progress, but the maze refuses to stay solved. Symbols you memorized melt into new alphabets. Doorways become mirrors. A staircase folds upward into the ceiling like paper.

Then you realize the maze isn’t testing what you know.

It’s testing how you think.

You stop rushing. In the next chamber, a thousand clocks tick at different speeds, each one pointing to a different hour. Instead of trying to set them all correctly, you listen. Beneath the ticking is a rhythm—slow, steady, familiar. Your heartbeat.

You touch the clock that matches it.

The room goes silent.

A door opens.

Beyond it is the center of the maze: a circular room with no puzzles at all, only a single table and a small box resting on it. Carved into the lid are the words:

**“What do you seek?”**

You reach for the box, expecting another riddle, but when you open it, there is nothing inside except a tiny glowing thread. It rises into the air, passes through your chest, and stretches backward through every hallway you’ve traveled.

Suddenly you understand.

The maze was never meant to trap you. It was mapping you—your fears, your patience, your curiosity, your stubborn hope.

The walls begin to dissolve into mist.

And just before you wake, you hear a voice whisper:

**“The way out was always the part of you that kept going.”**
