…notice the walls begin to change.

At first they are stone, cold and fixed. Then they become chalkboards covered in equations you almost recognize. Then mirrors. In each mirror, a different version of you is solving a different puzzle—faster, slower, laughing, crying, giving up, trying again.

The deeper you go, the quieter it gets.

No wind.  
No footsteps but yours.  
No sound except the soft *click* each time a solution falls into place.

A door appears with no handle, only a single sentence carved into it:

**“What is the one answer that fits every puzzle?”**

You search your pockets: keys made of numbers, symbols, fragments of proofs, all useless. You sit on the floor, exhausted, and for the first time you stop trying to outthink the maze.

You close your eyes.

When you open them, you realize the pattern was never in the walls. It was in your breath—steadying when you panicked, sharpening when you listened, carrying you through every impossible turn.

You place your hand on the door and whisper, “Attention.”

The door swings open.

Beyond it is not an exit, but a vast night sky folded into geometric constellations, each star a solved riddle. A path of light stretches forward, and as you step onto it, the maze behind you rearranges itself into a single, glowing shape:

A brain becoming a heart.

Then, just as dawn spills over the horizon, you wake with the strange certainty that the hardest puzzle was never the maze—

it was learning how to stay present inside it.
