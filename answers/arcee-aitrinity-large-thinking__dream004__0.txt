
The canvas shivers, as if alive, and begins to absorb the swirling colors around it—the crimson of a dissolving rose, the electric blue of a humming cube, the soft gold of a melting sun. But instead of painting a picture, the canvas *becomes* a window.

Through it, you see not the landscape behind you, but a reflection of yourself—but it’s a self made of shifting geometries and liquid light. Your hand, when you raise it, is a cluster of iridescent polygons. The canvas-mirror shows you this, and then, with a sound like a sigh, it begins to *paint you*.

It doesn’t use brushes. It uses the very air. Streaks of violet and silver weave across your form, outlining a shape that feels both alien and intimately familiar. You realize you are not just observing the dream—you are its subject, its co-creator.

The landscape around you pauses, the colors holding their breath. The canvas, now glowing with a quiet, expectant light, seems to ask: *What will you become next?*
