...and every face seems vaguely familiar, yet none are the one you’re searching for. The room hums with overlapping conversations, a low, steady drone that feels like the background noise of a dream—distorted, slightly surreal.

You push through a cluster of people near a table laden with unfamiliar, shimmering hors d’oeuvres. Someone glances up, and for a fleeting moment, their eyes lock with yours—wide, recognizing, almost urgent. They open their mouth as if to speak, but the sound dissolves into the murmur before it reaches you. You blink, and they’re gone, swallowed back into the throng.

A hand taps your shoulder. You jump. It’s a figure half-turned away from you, their profile illuminated by the soft glow of a chandelier. “You look lost,” they say, but the voice is distant, as if echoing from underwater. You turn to them, and for a heartbeat, you think you see the face you’re looking for—your friend’s familiar jawline, their quick, amused smile—but it shifts, rearranges, becomes a stranger’s.

You shake your head, trying to clear the disorientation. The layout of the room seems to ripple, corridors bending like reflections in water. You notice doors appearing where there were none before—ornate, gilded, slightly ajar—each one promising a different path. One emits the sound of laughter, low and warm. Another whispers with a quiet, melancholic music.

Drawn to the laughter, you approach the first door. Peering through the crack, you see a circle of your closest friends—people you haven’t seen in years—dancing slowly, their faces aglow with candlelight. They don’t notice you. You knock softly. The door swings inward, and you step inside… only to find yourself back in the hallway, the door now sealed behind you.

A thought brushes the edge of your mind, unsettling and clear: *You are the one they’re searching for.*

You glance down. Your hands are fading, translucent at the edges. The crowd begins to thin, not moving away from you, but *through* you—like light through glass. You feel the gathering’s gaze turn inward, searching. And suddenly, you understand: the person you’ve been trying to find was never someone else.

It was always you.

You smile—a real, aching smile—as the room dissolves into soft white light, and you finally wake.
