
...start building a sanctuary—not just for yourself, but for any survivors who might still be out there. You scavenge the ruins of a library, salvaging books and a battered solar panel. By day, you teach children who’ve never seen a classroom how to read, using pages torn from old novels as currency for their attention. By night, you jury-rig a radio from scraps, broadcasting a single message on loop: *"We’re still here. Come find us."*  

But the wasteland has other plans. A pack of feral dogs, led by a creature with too many eyes, corners you at dusk. Just as you’re about to surrender, your radio crackles to life—not static, but a faint, repeating voice on the other end. The signal is human. The voice is familiar.  

It’s your own.  

From three days in the future.  

*(The dream shifts: you wake up in a world where time loops, and the apocalypse is a trap you’re meant to break. But first—you have to decide if the voice on the radio is really yours… or something wearing it like a skin.)*
