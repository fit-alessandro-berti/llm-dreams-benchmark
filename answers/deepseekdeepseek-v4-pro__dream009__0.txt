The air smells of woodsmoke and wild herbs, and in the distance, a low stone cottage clings to a hillside, its windows flickering with candlelight. I walk toward it, my bare feet strangely at home on the cool earth. When I knock, an old woman with eyes like polished obsidian opens the door without surprise, as if she’s been expecting me. “You’ve lost your thread,” she says, and hands me a spindle whorl carved from bone. The moment I touch it, I remember: I’m not a stranger here—I’m a weaver of time, and the dream is the loom.
