I pick up a brush that materializes in my hand — it seems to be made of light itself, shimmering between wavelengths I don't have names for.

As I touch the brush to the canvas, something unexpected happens: the canvas begins to *paint me back*. Colors swirl from its surface and wrap around my hands, my arms, as if the blankness was hungry, waiting to be filled — and it decided I was the paint.

The sky above shifts from crystalline amber to a deep violet hum (yes, colors have *sound* here). I notice the shapes around me are breathing — mountains inhale and become valleys on the exhale, and the horizon keeps folding itself like an origami crane that can't quite decide what it wants to be.

A figure approaches. They have no fixed face — just a constellation of floating dots that rearrange with every step, like a mood ring trying to read itself. They don't speak, but I understand them anyway:

*"You've been here before. Every dreamer who finds the canvas is returning, not arriving. What you paint next is what you forgot when you woke up last time."*

I look down. The canvas is now a mirror, but it's showing a version of me made entirely of the colors I've never seen — impossible greens that taste like nostalgia, reds that feel like the space between two notes of a song.

I lift the brush.

The world holds its breath.

And I paint a door.

It swings open, and on the other side, I see — 

*...a waking room, sunlight through curtains, the feeling of a morning you've had a thousand times but never quite remembered.*

The dream is already fading, but I can still feel the brush in my hand. Can you?
