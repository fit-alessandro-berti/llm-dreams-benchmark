...and something about each encounter pulls you deeper into the maze.

---

You push through a doorway that shouldn't be there — the gathering stretches into rooms you didn't see before, each one warmer and more dimly lit than the last.

A cluster of people in matching grey coats huddle near a window that looks out onto a parking lot you recognize but can't place. They're laughing at a joke that ended before you arrived. One of them turns to you and says, *Oh, you're looking for Marcus?* — but you never said his name. You nod. They point down a hallway lined with birthday candles stuck into the carpet, all unlit. *He went that way. He seemed distracted.*

You walk. The hallway narrows.

You pass a circle of teenagers sitting cross-legged on the floor, passing around a glass bowl of something that shifts color when you're not looking directly at it. One of them holds up a Polaroid — it's you, younger, standing somewhere you've never been. *Is this yours?* they ask. You reach for it, but the photo is blank on the back, and when you flip it again, it's a picture of the bowl.

Ahead, an old couple sits in armchairs that face away from the party entirely. They're watching static on a television that hums a melody you half-remember. The woman doesn't turn around but says, *He's close. He keeps circling back.* The man adjusts his glasses and murmurs, *We all do.*

The candles in the carpet are starting to flicker now — one by one, as if someone is walking behind you, lighting them.

There's a room at the end of the hall. The door is slightly ajar. You can hear someone humming the same tune as the television.

Your hand is on the handle.

---

*Shall I keep dreaming?*
