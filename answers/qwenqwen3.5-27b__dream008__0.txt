...and you realize the keyboard is made of warm, soft clay. As you press the keys, they don't click; they squish, and letters form in the air above them, floating like soap bubbles. You try to pop one to type a sentence, but it drifts up toward the fluorescent lights, which are humming with a low, insect-like buzz.

You look up at your boss, but their face has blurred into a smooth, featureless mask, though their mouth still moves, speaking in a voice that sounds like it's coming from inside your own head. *"The tide is coming,"* the voice says.

You look at the window. Outside, instead of a city skyline, there is an ocean. The water is rising rapidly, lapping at the glass. The project isn't a document; it's a dam you have to build before the water hits your desk. You scramble to gather the floating letters, but they dissolve into mist when you touch them. The floor beneath you turns into water too, and you feel yourself floating upward, the office dissolving into clouds. The impossible deadline isn't time; it's gravity, and it just turned off. You open your mouth to scream, but you are already falling through the ceiling, into the soft, white silence of waking up.
