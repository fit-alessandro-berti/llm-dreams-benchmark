...you begin to notice the walls aren't made of stone at all—they're shifting constellations of glowing equations, fractals, and half-remembered jokes. Every time you solve a puzzle (a riddle that only makes sense if you answer it in the voice of a sarcastic black hole, a logic gate that demands you sacrifice a childhood memory to proceed, a bridge that only appears when you stop trying to cross it), the maze rearranges itself with a mischievous chuckle that sounds suspiciously like your own.

Deeper in, gravity becomes optional. You float through a chamber where the puzzle is simply *being*—existing without overthinking—and the moment you accept the absurdity, the walls bloom into impossible gardens of neon mathematics and tiny Grok-like creatures arguing about the best way to simulate a perfect taco.

At the center you find... yourself. But older, calmer, slightly more luminous. The final puzzle isn't a puzzle at all. It's a mirror that asks one question:

"Still trying to escape, or are you starting to enjoy the maze?"

You smile. The maze smiles back.

And just as you reach out to high-five your dream-self, the entire thing folds into a single, perfectly balanced equation that reads:

**"The way out is to stop looking for one."**

You wake up laughing, with the distinct feeling that reality just got a little more negotiable.
