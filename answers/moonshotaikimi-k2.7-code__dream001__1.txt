You start to run.

Your shoes make no sound on the pavement, which is the first sign that the rules here are not the usual ones. The buildings lean over you like curious giants, their windows flickering with scenes from rooms you half-remember: a kitchen where someone is making pancakes, an office where a dozen versions of yourself are all on hold. You pat your pockets and find your phone, but instead of a map it shows only a clock with hands that spin backward, then forward, then melt into the glass.

You ask a stranger for directions. She turns, and her face is blank—smooth as an eggshell—until she opens her mouth and your own voice comes out. “The meeting is wherever you decide it is,” you say to yourself, which is not especially helpful.

Still, you move. The avenues widen and narrow without warning. A subway entrance appears beneath a flowering tree; you descend, and the train that arrives is the color of midnight and smells like rain. The doors open onto a library where every book is titled *Where You’re Supposed to Be*. You pull one down and read your own name on the first page, but the pages beyond are blank, waiting.

Time begins to behave strangely. An hour feels like a sip of water and also like a century. You pass a café where everyone at the tables is wearing your clothes. They wave. One of them points down an alley lined with mirrors, and in each mirror you are wearing something different: a suit, a diving helmet, pajamas, wings.

At last you arrive at a tower whose doors are made of calendar pages. The lobby is empty except for a long table and two chairs. You sit. Across from you, the chair is also empty, yet the air above it shimmers. You realize, with the strange certainty that only comes in dreams, that the important meeting was never about anyone else. You have come all this way to sit down with the part of yourself that has been trying to get your attention.

You lean forward. The shimmer leans forward too. “Sorry I’m late,” you say.

And the city, vast and unfamiliar, exhales around you like a sleeping thing, waiting to hear what you have to say.
