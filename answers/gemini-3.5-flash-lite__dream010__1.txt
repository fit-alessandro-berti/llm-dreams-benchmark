My heart races. This isn't just any job; it’s the intersection of everything I’ve ever loved—creating worlds, telling stories, and inspiring others. But the stakes in dreams are always bizarrely high. In my hand, I hold a glowing compass that doesn't point north, but rather toward the things I was too afraid to try in waking life.

As I step into the maze, the fog parts to reveal familiar faces from my past. They aren't obstacles; they are milestones. A teacher who told me to be realistic; a blank canvas that mocked me for years; a closed door that felt like the end of the world. Each one asks a riddle, and the only way forward is to answer with radical honesty. 

"Why do you deserve to build worlds?" a towering figure made of old paperbacks asks, blocking the path.

I open my mouth to speak, but the words don't come out as sound. Instead, they materialize as colorful starlight that paints the dark trees around us. The figure smiles, stepping aside, and gestures toward the center of the labyrinth, where a glowing podium awaits.

I reach the center, breathless. The grand prize—a simple brass key resting on a velvet pillow—is right in front of me. But as I reach out to grab it, the sky begins to dissolve into a pale, bruised morning violet. The edges of the dream are fraying. 

I grab the key just as my alarm clock begins to ring in the real world. The sound bleeds into the dream like a siren. 

I gasp awake, staring at the ceiling of my bedroom. My hand is tightly clenched around... nothing. 

Or so I think. 

As I sit up, I notice something glinting on my nightstand, catching the first ray of morning sun. A small, cold, brass key. And beside it, a notification glowing on my phone: *“Congratulations. Your interview is today.”*
