The wind doesn’t howl so much as *remember*—it pulls through the trees like a hand through water, tugging your coat, tugging your thoughts, and for a moment you are sure the forest has unplugged itself from the ground. The sky goes the color of a closed iris. Leaves cartwheel in reverse, climbing back toward branches before gravity remembers its job and slams them down. 

You feel the pressure drop as if the air has simply given up and left the room. Your ears pop. A hush falls, thick and electric, the way a held breath sounds when someone is about to scream. 

Then the first drop hits—not water, but something closer to mercury, cold and heavy, denting the moss. The storm isn’t arriving; it’s waking up. Thunder unzips the horizon, a slow zipper pulled by a giant, revealing the bruised purple seams behind the world. 

A fork of lightning stitches itself across the canopy, not branching outward but inward, as if sewing the forest to the sky. The smell of ozone and wet iron rises, sharp and ancient. Rain begins in earnest: not falling, but marching, a million hooves across a drumskin of leaves. The path turns into a river of reflected sky, every puddle holding a shivering moon or the blue flash of another strike.

You keep walking because stopping feels like agreeing to be struck. Branches whip like compass needles gone mad. Somewhere nearby a tree splits, not with a crash but with a sound like a ribcage finally admitting it is only bone. The wind tries to put words in your mouth, syllables of static and distance. 

And then, above the drumming, you hear it—the eye of the storm sliding into place. The rain cuts off mid-stride. The forest holds its pose, dripping, stunned. The clouds spiral overhead like a ceiling being drawn open. For a perfect minute you stand in a cathedral of wet stone and living wood, and you realize the storm hasn’t come to punish you. It’s come to rearrange you, to shake the dust of ordinary hours from your shoulders.

When the wind returns, it doesn’t bring the rain back; it brings the future. The trees lean in, whispering in a language older than names, and the path ahead glows faintly, as if every step you take will leave a footprint of lightning in the mud, leading you not out of the forest but deeper into its dreaming heart.
