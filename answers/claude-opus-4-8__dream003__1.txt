...as you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and symbols etched across its surface—spirals folding into spirals, constellations that seem to shift when you're not looking directly at them.

Your fingers trace the grooves. They're warm, somehow, pulsing faintly like something breathing beneath the wood. You realize the patterns aren't decoration. They're a question.

You don't know how you know this, but the box wants you to remember something. Not a key made of metal—a key made of *recall*.

You press your palm flat against the lid and close your eyes. And then, the way dreams do, the answer is simply *there*: the box was never locked. It was waiting for you to stop looking for a way in and notice you were already holding it open.

The lid lifts on its own.

Inside, there's no treasure, no scroll, no glittering object. Just a small mirror at the bottom, reflecting your own face looking back—except in the reflection, you're smiling, even though you're not sure you are.

And underneath the mirror, scratched faintly into the base, four words:

*You already knew this.*

The box grows lighter. Or maybe you're the one rising. The patterns lift off the surface like smoke, drifting upward, and you understand—

—but then, the way dreams do, it slips away before you can hold it.
