...a single, silver paintbrush floating suspended in the shifting air. 

I reach for the brush, and the moment my fingers wrap around its cool, metallic handle, the chaotic kaleidoscope of the world around me suddenly pauses, as if holding its breath. 

There are no tubes of paint or wooden palettes here. Instinctively, I realize how this world works. I reach out and dip the bristles directly into a floating, liquid orb of deep sapphire that is slowly turning into a heavy square. 

When I strike the canvas, the blue doesn't just spread; it hums. It sounds like a low, vibrating cello note. Fascinated, I sweep the brush through a jagged streak of neon-tangerine lightning that drifts past my shoulder. I slash it across the blue. The colors mix, emitting the scent of toasted sugar and rain. 

I begin to paint rapidly, stealing from the surreal landscape around me. I scrape velvet purples from the morphing sky, pluck emerald spirals from the shifting ground, and smear them onto the fabric. With every stroke, the wild, unpredictable world around me begins to dim and funnel its energy into the canvas. 

I am painting a door. A massive, glowing archway woven from stolen light and melted geometry.

As I apply the final brushstroke—a streak of pure, silent white—the surface of the canvas ripples like a disturbed pond. The wooden frame cracks and expands, growing larger and larger until it swallows the horizon entirely. The flat image gains impossible depth. 

A cool breeze blows from *inside* the painting, ruffling my hair. I drop the brush. It shatters into a flock of glass butterflies. Without hesitating, I step forward, crossing the threshold of the canvas, leaving the chaotic world behind and sinking into a perfectly still, indigo twilight where gravity no longer exists. 

I close my eyes, and I gently begin to float.
