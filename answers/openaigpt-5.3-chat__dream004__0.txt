:::writing
…as you come closer, the canvas starts breathing.

Not like lungs, but like tides—expanding, shrinking—pulling colors out of the air around it. The shifting sky drains into it first: ribbons of violet, liquid gold, a green that looks like it remembers being a forest.

You reach out with a brush that wasn’t in your hand a moment ago.

The instant the tip touches the canvas, the ground beneath you rearranges itself into the shape you’re painting. A blue line becomes a river rushing past your feet. A spiral of orange lifts into the sky and turns into a floating staircase. Every stroke rewrites the world.

You paint a door.

It appears in the middle of nowhere, standing upright in the humming landscape. The colors around it slow, as if holding their breath.

When you open it, you see another canvas on the other side.

And someone else holding the brush.
