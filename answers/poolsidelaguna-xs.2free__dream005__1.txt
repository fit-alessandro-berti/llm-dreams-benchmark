Each group seems to pulse with its own energy—scientists debating quantum theory, artists painting on an easel balanced on a table, a troupe of dancers mid-performance, and a circle of elders sharing stories in a language you almost recognize.  

As you push closer to listen, their voices blend into a melody that feels familiar, like a half-remembered lullaby. One dancer locks eyes with you and mouthed, *“You’re looking for the one who wears silence like a cloak.”* The words linger as the crowd parts, revealing a garden hidden behind a tapestry of streamers.  

At its center stands the person you sought—all along, they’d been waiting. But as you reach them, their form shimmers, dissolving into a mirror that shows your own reflection, younger, older, and everything in between. The gathering fades, leaving only the echo of a truth: sometimes the person we search for is the one we’ve been running from.  

You wake up smiling, unsure if the dream was about finding someone—or finally meeting yourself.
