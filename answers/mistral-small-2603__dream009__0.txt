**...find a way back.**

The air smells of woodsmoke and damp earth, and the distant clang of a blacksmith’s hammer echoes through the narrow, cobblestone streets. You’re dressed in rough-spun wool, your clothes unfamiliar yet oddly comfortable—like a second skin. A woman in a long dress and apron eyes you curiously as she carries a basket of apples, her gaze lingering on the strange, smooth fabric of your sleeves.

You need to find a way back—but how?

The first step is to understand where—and *when*—you are. The architecture is medieval, the people’s clothing suggests the 14th or 15th century, but the absence of certain details—no plague flags, no knights in shining armor, no heavy religious iconography—makes it hard to pin down. Maybe it’s a rural village in Europe, far from the courts and wars of the time.

You need supplies. A map, a guide, or at least someone who speaks a language you can understand. But your modern English might be useless here. You try a few words in Latin—*ubi sum?* (Where am I?)—but the woman only shakes her head and points toward the market square.

As you walk, the weight of the situation settles in. You have no phone, no wallet, no ID. No way to call for help. No idea how long you’ve been asleep—or if this is even real.

But dreams have rules. And in dreams, the strangest things can happen.

Maybe, just maybe, if you follow the sound of the river, you’ll find a bridge. And on the other side—

A voice calls out in a language you don’t recognize, but the tone is urgent. A child tugs at your sleeve, pointing toward the edge of town, where the forest looms dark and tangled.

*Something is coming.*

And you realize—you’re not just lost in time.

You’re being hunted.
