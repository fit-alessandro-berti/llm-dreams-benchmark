**...symbols etched in silver that shimmer like starlight, rearranging themselves whenever your eyes linger too long.**

As you lean closer, the patterns pulse in time with your heartbeat. They form a map—no, a labyrinth—spiraling inward to a single glowing lock at the center that wasn’t there moments ago. No key, just your own reflection staring back from the surface of the metal, eyes wide and slightly too bright.

You reach out. The box is warm, almost alive. The moment your fingertip traces the final curve of the design, the patterns freeze, then ignite with soft blue light. A whisper rises from inside, not in words but in memory: laughter you’ve forgotten, a place that feels like home but doesn’t exist on any map, the scent of rain on stone.

The lock clicks open of its own accord.

Inside is not treasure or danger, but a small, folded note written in your own handwriting—words you don’t recall writing. It simply says: **“You already have the key. Wake up when you’re ready.”**

The box dissolves into mist as the dream begins to fade, leaving only the quiet certainty that something important just unlocked… somewhere inside you.

(And then the morning light finds you.)
