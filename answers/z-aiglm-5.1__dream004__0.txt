You lift the brush, and the moment it touches the canvas, colors bloom outward like flowers opening in time-lapse. A spiral of deep blue unfurls from a single point, spinning outward, and where it touches the edges of the canvas, the painted world bleeds beyond the frame—spilling onto the ground, into the air, around your feet.

You paint a mountain, and you feel stone forming beneath you. You paint a river, and suddenly you hear water flowing somewhere behind you. The landscape is becoming real, or perhaps you are becoming part of the painting.

Then you notice something strange: in the corner of the canvas, someone has already painted a small door. It wasn't there before. The door is slightly ajar, and from beyond it comes a warm, amber glow and a sound like distant laughter—or maybe wind through trees.

Your brush hand trembles. The colors around you continue their slow morph—trees shift from triangular to round, the sky ripples between twilight and dawn. Everything is in motion except that door, which remains perfectly still, perfectly clear, waiting.

You step closer to the canvas, and realize you can feel its surface pulling at you gently, like a current. The painted door seems to breathe.

You reach for the handle.
