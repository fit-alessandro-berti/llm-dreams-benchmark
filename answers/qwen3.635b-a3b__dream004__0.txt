...and the air beneath your palm hums with the quiet before rain. The canvas bears no frame, no edge. It simply *is*, a sheet of held breath suspended in the drifting geometry of the sky. You press your fingers to it. Light yields like water. Color doesn't appear; it remembers.

A sweep of your wrist draws a river of cobalt. It curves toward you, then lifts into the air, shedding droplets that become floating dodecahedrons, each catching a different shade of twilight. They drift past your shoulders like fireflies made of glass. You take a step, and the ground answers: moss uncoils into fractal ferns, then dissolves into a flock of origami cranes that take flight in perfect silence.

The horizon bends. A staircase of interlocking triangles spirals upward, then melts into a pool of liquid sapphire. You reach down. Your reflection stares back, but it's made of shifting contours—sometimes round, always restless. When you blink, the reflection blinks a second later, then smiles with your mouth.

You understand now: the canvas doesn't capture the dream. It *conducts* it. Every color you pull from the air is a thought given weight. Every shape you trace is a memory learning to breathe. The sky folds in on itself like a letter being sealed, then unfolds again, wider than before. Mountains rise as you wonder about them. Valleys form where you hesitate.

The wind carries the scent of ozone and childhood summers. You begin to move without deciding to. Your hands write in the light. The landscape answers in kind. Colors bleed together. Shapes borrow from each other. The boundary between you and the dream thins, then dissolves.

When the air grows still, the canvas is gone. Or you are gone. Or neither. The world hums at the edge of recognition, waiting for your next breath to paint the next horizon. You don't wake. You simply turn, and walk deeper in.
