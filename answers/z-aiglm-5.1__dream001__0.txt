You check your phone for directions, but the screen shows only a slowly rotating compass rose, the needle spinning lazily, pointing to a different direction each second. The calendar notification simply reads: **THE MEETING** — Location: *The Place You Already Were*

A woman with an umbrella made of newspaper stops you. "You're late," she says. "You've always been late." She opens the umbrella, and rain begins falling *upward* from the dry sidewalk.

You duck into a café to ask for directions. The barista slides a coffee across the counter. "Your order. You placed it years ago." The cup is warm. The name written on it is yours, but spelled wrong — just slightly, the kind of wrong that makes your stomach drop.

Outside, the buildings have rearranged. The street you came from is now a canal. A door you don't remember is now embedded in a brick wall, standing alone, no frame, no hinges, just a door.

Your watch says you have forty minutes. Then it says you have eleven years. Then it shows only a drawing of a small bird.

You reach for the door handle.
