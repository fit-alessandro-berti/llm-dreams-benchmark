I lean into the storm, the wind tearing the breath from my lungs. I know that if I steer away from the gale, we will be tossed into the jagged teeth of the Widow’s Reefs. If I steer into it, we risk being swallowed by the abyss. 

I pull the wheel hard to the left, shouting over the roar of the gale, "Lash yourselves to the mast! We don't run from the storm—we cut it in half!"

As the ship tilts, lifting toward the heavens on the back of a black wall of water, the world suddenly goes silent. The rain stops mid-air, suspended like diamonds in the dark. The crew freezes, their mouths open in mid-shout. I look down at my hands on the wheel and realize they are beginning to turn translucent, fading into the color of sea foam. 

I realize then that I am not fighting the ocean; I am the tide. I close my eyes, let go of the wheel, and instead of falling into the salt-crusted deck, I dissolve into the spray, becoming the very wind that is howling for my ship. 

When I open my eyes again, the storm is gone. There is only a vast, infinite mirror of water reflecting a sky filled with two moons. I am no longer on the ship; I am standing on the surface of the sea, and behind me, the *Sea-Witch* has become a constellation of silver stars, drifting slowly toward the horizon of a dream from which I have not yet decided to wake.
