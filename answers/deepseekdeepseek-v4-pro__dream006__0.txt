You begin to speak, but instead of words, small glowing butterflies drift from your mouth and flutter out over the audience. They don’t seem surprised—they open their hands and let the light land softly on their palms. Then you notice that the stage is no longer a stage but a wide wooden bridge over a quiet lake, and the crowd is now sitting in little boats, each tied with a silver thread to the railing. You try to remember your speech, but all you can recall is a song your grandmother used to hum when you were very small, so you hum it now, and the butterflies pulse in time. The boats gently pull away from the bridge, still tethered, and the whole lake begins to tilt very slowly, like a mirror being angled toward the sky. You feel a deep calm, knowing that every word you ever needed has already been given to them, and as the water starts to glow from below, you wake up with the scent of rain and the faintest taste of honey on your tongue.
