...and hovering just above the center of the frame is a brush made of solidified moonlight. It waits for you, not waiting on a table, but suspended in the humid, shifting air like a promise.

You reach out. Your fingers don't meet the handle; they meet a cool ripple in the air, like touching the surface of a deep lake. When you grip it, the chaotic swirl of neon pinks and jagged grays around you instantly hushes. The world stops its violent morphing.

You feel the brush dip into an invisible pot, and when you pull it back, it's stained with the color of a memory you thought you'd lost: the warm amber of a kitchen table during a Sunday morning.

As you touch the brush to the white surface, the painting doesn't sit on the paper. It bleeds into the landscape. The amber light expands, pushing back the shifting shadows. You draw a door in the center of the white void, and as the line forms, the canvas lifts into the air. It becomes a portal.

Through the door, you see yourself sitting on a bench, reading a book, completely still while the world around you spins. The dream knows what you need. You step through, leaving the canvas behind. The colors fade into a soft grey as your eyes open, the scent of turpentine lingering on your fingers long after you've woken up, wondering if you really just painted the world, or if the world painted you.
