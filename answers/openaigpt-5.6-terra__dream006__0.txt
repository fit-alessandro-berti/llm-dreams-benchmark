…speak—but no sound comes out.

The microphone blooms into a white flower in your hand. The audience leans closer, their faces gently changing into the faces of people you have known: teachers, strangers, childhood friends, someone you miss but cannot name.

So you stop trying to speak.

Instead, you open your mouth and a flock of small paper birds flies out, each carrying a sentence you meant to say years ago. They circle the theater’s ceiling, where the stars have somehow appeared, and the crowd applauds—not loudly, but like rain falling on leaves.

Then you realize you are not on a stage at all.

You are standing at the edge of your bed, and morning is waiting behind the curtain.
