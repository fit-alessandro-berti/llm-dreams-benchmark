# The Competition

You are participating in a competition where the grand prize is your dream job. As the competition progresses, you face various challenges and each one feels strangely personal, as if the organizers somehow reached into your deepest memories to design them.

The first challenge is a maze, but the walls are made of stacked papers — old resumes, rejection letters, drafts of cover letters you never sent. You navigate by listening for a faint humming sound growing louder. At the center, you find a simple desk with a blank notebook. You write one true sentence about yourself and a door opens.

The second challenge places you in a room with five strangers. You're all given a problem to solve together, but the catch is that everyone speaks a different professional language — finance, design, engineering, marketing, law. You realize the task isn't to translate but to *listen* differently. You close your eyes and start drawing connections between their words. Slowly, a shared understanding emerges like a shape materializing from fog.

The third challenge is the strangest. You're led into a room that looks exactly like your childhood bedroom. A voice says: "Build something that doesn't exist yet using only what's here." You find old toys, books, a broken clock, crayons, a flashlight. You build a small machine that doesn't *do* anything functional — but when you turn it on, it projects light patterns on the ceiling that make everyone in the room feel something they can't quite name.

The final challenge is simply a door. On it, a single question is written:

*"What would you still do even if you were never paid for it?"*

You answer silently — not with words but with a feeling that rises in your chest, warm and unmistakable.

The door doesn't open.

Instead, you realize *you're already standing on the other side.*

A woman sitting at a desk looks up at you and smiles. "We've been waiting. When can you start?"

You wake up with your heart still pounding, and for a strange lingering moment, you feel certain of something you couldn't quite name before.

Would you like me to continue or explore a different direction?
