You find yourself in a vast, unfamiliar city, and you realize you have an important meeting in an hour but no idea where it is. You start to ask a stranger for directions. He smiles and hands you a pocket watch whose hands spin wildly backward. Every street sign you pass morphs into the name of someone you once knew. The buildings stretch and tilt like reflections in disturbed water while the sky rains paper invitations, each addressed to a slightly different version of you. You follow the spinning hands down an avenue that keeps resetting the remaining time by ten minutes. At the end of the street the entire city folds itself into a single conference room filled with empty chairs and one folder that bears your name. You reach for the folder just as all the other versions of you look up from their own watches and wait for you to choose which one gets to stay.
