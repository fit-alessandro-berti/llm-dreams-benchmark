*The fluorescent lights above you seem to flicker and stretch unnaturally. Your boss’s face hovers closer, their smile a little too wide, their voice echoing slightly.*

**Boss:** “We need the full prototype, client presentation, and risk assessment… by tomorrow morning. The investors are flying in early.”

*You glance at your computer screen—the numbers and text begin to swirl slowly, like a whirlpool. Your coffee mug on the desk starts leaking, but the liquid is shimmering, almost like liquid starlight.*

*You try to speak, but your voice comes out layered, as if multiple versions of you are speaking at once:*  
“I… will need more resources. This isn’t—”

*The office walls stretch upward, becoming an endless cathedral of cubicles. Your colleagues are now shadows typing furiously, their keyboards clacking in a rhythm that sounds like a countdown clock.*

*Suddenly, a single sheet of paper floats from the ceiling into your hands. On it, in glowing script, is a list of tasks—each one marked “URGENT” and multiplying as you look at them.*

*You feel a light tap on your shoulder. Turning, you see not your boss, but a clock with legs, tapping its foot impatiently. It says in a crisp, ticking voice:*

**Clock:** “You have 86,400 seconds. They will not pass twice.”

*You look back—the desk is now a ship’s helm, the project brief a tattered map, and the impossible deadline? It’s a storm on the horizon, rising fast.*

*Do you reach for the map? Dive into the swirling screen? Or do you… wake up?*
