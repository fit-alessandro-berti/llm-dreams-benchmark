On the table, where a cold cup of tea sits, is a single, folded piece of paper. You pick it up. It’s a page from a sketchbook, and on it is a drawing of the two of you, from years ago, smiling. The lines are messy, full of life. At the bottom, in handwriting you’d know anywhere, it says, *“I’m sorry. I miss you.”*

But the words feel like they’re from another lifetime. The anger in your chest has cooled into a heavy, hollow feeling. You realize the house around you is dissolving, the walls becoming mist, the floor softening into clouds.

You wake up with a start, your face pressed into your pillow, the name of your friend on the tip of your tongue. For a long moment, you can’t tell if the argument was the dream, or if this quiet, lonely wakefulness is the real dream.
