*The dream continues...*

...stare at the stack of papers on my desk, but the words keep shifting. Every time I focus on a sentence, it rearranges itself into something new — requirements multiplying, deadlines contracting. My boss is standing in the doorway, except now the doorway stretches impossibly long, and she's both very close and very far away at the same time.

\ this morning,\ window. When I look again, it's suddenly noon. Then dusk.

I try to open my laptop, but the keys are all blank. I type anyway, and somehow words appear on the screen — but they're in a language I almost recognize, something I feel I *should* know. My coworkers drift past my desk, nodding approvingly at work I haven't done yet.

\ and I realize with creeping dread that I've apparently already finished. The project is done. It's been done for years. I'm being promoted. I'm being fired. I'm being asked to present it to a conference room where everyone is wearing my face.

I stand up to walk to the meeting, but my legs feel like they're moving through warm honey. The hallway keeps getting longer. My boss is waiting at the end of it, tapping a watch that has no hands.

\ I realize — I haven't started yet. I never started. The deadline was yesterday. The deadline is tomorrow. The deadline is now.

I open my mouth to explain, and wake up.
