
The wind screams like a chorus of the damned, tearing at the rigging and snapping the heavy canvas like gunshot. Salt spray lashes your face, stinging your eyes. The deck rolls beneath your feet with a sickening, unpredictable lurch—one moment canted at a forty-five degree angle toward the black, boiling sky, the next plunging down into a valley of dark water where the hull groans in protest.

"Captain!" Your first mate, a grizzled sailor named Mara, clings to the wheel beside you, her voice raw over the gale. "The main mast won't hold! We have to cut the rigging!"

You squint through the driving rain, watching the towering pine mast bend like a whip, the stays taut as bowstrings. To cut it would be to lose the ship's heart, its power. But to let it snap could bring the whole mast crashing down, crushing the deck and anyone on it.

Your mind races, calculating the ship's momentum, the angle of the next wave, the integrity of the hull. The crew is a knot of desperate, determined faces—some pumping bilge, others clinging to lines, all waiting for your word.

You grab the speaking trumpet from its hook, your own voice a raw scrape. "Hold fast on the mains'l! Mara, hard to port—now! All hands, brace for the yaw!"

You don't cut the mast. You use the storm's own fury. As Mara fights the wheel, you roar for the men on the stays. "Ease the leeward line! Now! Let it *breathe*!"

It's a gamble. The mast shudders, creaks, and for a heart-stopping second, you think it's over. Then, with a groan that vibrates through the deck, it gives just enough—a dangerous, graceful arc that spills the worst of the wind. The ship, responding to Mara's desperate turn, slices across the face of the next monstrous wave instead of slamming into it.

A cheer goes up, weak but real, as the *Sea Sprite* surfs down the wave's back, finding a slightly less chaotic rhythm in the chaos. You're not out of the storm. Not by a long shot. But you've bought time. You've found a path through the teeth of the tempest.

You look at your crew, their faces etched with fear and fierce pride. You give a single, firm nod. "We ride the dragon," you shout, the words torn away by the wind but understood. "Not fighting it. Riding it."

And for now, in the roaring, wet dark, that is enough.
