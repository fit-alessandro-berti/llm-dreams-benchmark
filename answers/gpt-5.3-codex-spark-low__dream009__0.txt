• You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you
  realize you need to find warmth, food, and a way to signal the world you’ve vanished from.

  The village nearby is nothing like home—mud walls, wood smoke, people speaking in a language you can almost hear
  through memory but not understand. You clutch your empty pockets, then force your mind to slow down. Fear helps no
  one. You watch, mimic their gestures, and trade your wristwatch for a bowl of grain porridge. Night falls, cold and
  immediate, and you start gathering dry twigs by instinct. When the fire catches, a circle of faces appears in the
  glow: wary, curious, hungry eyes. You have no phone, no compass, no map. But you do have a mind that can learn fast.
  At dawn, you set a goal that feels absurd and necessary all at once—learn the road, learn the stars, and learn where
  they keep their healers. Somewhere between those, you suspect, is the thread that will pull you back to where you came
  from.
