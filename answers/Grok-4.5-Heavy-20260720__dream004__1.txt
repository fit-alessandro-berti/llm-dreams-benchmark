...the blank canvas stands impossibly still amid the chaos. The moment you step closer, a slender brush of pure, shifting light materializes in your hand. Without conscious decision you raise it, and the swirling colors of the landscape begin to stream toward the canvas like rivers obeying a new gravity. Shapes melt and reform under the brush: mountains become soft geometry, skies fold into luminous origami, oceans of liquid light pool into perfect, breathing reflections.

As you paint, the canvas grows. It expands until it surrounds you completely. You are no longer outside looking in—you are standing within the painting you have created. Behind you, the original world freezes into a single, perfect frame of stillness, while this new reality begins to shift and change once more, now responding instantly to every flicker of thought, every breath, every half-formed desire.

You realize then that the canvas was never blank. It had been waiting for a dreamer to step forward and become both artist and artwork. And somewhere, far beyond the edges of this living painting, another blank canvas is already waiting.
