**You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and...** 

...tiny constellations etched into the wood, each one faintly glowing as your fingertip passes over it. The metal corners are cold, but the center of the lid feels warm, like something inside is breathing.

You tilt it and hear no rattle. No seam for a keyhole either. Just a smooth circle in the middle with a single word carved in a language you don’t know, yet somehow understand: *Listen*.

So you do. You press your ear to the lid.  

At first there’s nothing. Then a soft click, like a heartbeat syncing to your own. The constellations brighten one by one, mapping a path across the surface. Without thinking, you trace the sequence with your finger: start at the smallest star, follow the arc, end at the crescent.  

The circle sinks inward half an inch and warm air sighs out. It smells like old paper and rain.  

Inside isn’t a thing. It’s a space. The box opens into a corridor that shouldn’t fit, walls lined with doors that all have your name written in different handwriting. On a small pedestal in the center sits a folded note and a silver key.  

The note says: *You were never meant to open this from the outside.*  

The key is warm. It’s the same temperature as your palm. And when you pick it up, you realize it isn’t for any of the doors in the corridor.  

It’s for the box itself, from the inside.  

Your dream-self smiles, because suddenly you remember: you locked it.  

What do you do with the key?
