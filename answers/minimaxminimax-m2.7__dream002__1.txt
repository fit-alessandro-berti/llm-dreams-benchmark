The wind whips through the ancient trunks, bending the branches like bows eager to snap. A low rumble rolls across the canopy, a deep, throaty growl that seems to come from the very earth beneath your feet. The sky overhead churns from a soft, hazy blue to a bruised violet, thick with clouds that swirl in slow, hypnotic spirals. Each breath you take feels heavier, as if the air itself is trying to pull you deeper into the woods.

You can feel the first drops of rain before they fall—a faint, electric tingle on your skin, like tiny needles prickling the surface. Then the heavens open. A cascade of cold, silver droplets splatters the leaves, turning the forest floor into a tapestry of glistening mud and scattered pine needles. The rain is relentless, drumming a rhythmic pulse that echoes through the trees, a heartbeat of the storm itself.

Instinctively, you duck beneath a massive oak, its roots sprawling like the veins of some ancient creature. Its bark is slick with moisture, and the scent of wet earth mingles with the sharp tang of ozone. The storm rages, but here in the shelter of the oak, the world feels oddly still. Lightning flashes, illuminating the forest in stark white flashes, and for a split second you see shadows that seem to move of their own accord—figures that flicker between the trunks, their silhouettes too tall, too slender, almost ethereal.

A voice rises above the thunder, not quite words but a melody that seems to drift from the core of the tree itself. It hums a lullaby of wind and rain, a tune that pulls at something deep inside you, stirring memories you didn’t know you had. The melody feels familiar, as if you’ve heard it before in a dream within a dream, a promise that you are not alone, that the forest is alive with unseen guardians.

The storm begins to quiet, its fury ebbing like a tide pulling back from shore. The rain softens to a gentle mist, and the clouds part just enough to reveal a sliver of moonlight, silver and cool. In the clearing that now opens before you, a small pond has formed, its surface mirror‑smooth, reflecting the night sky like a portal to another realm. You step forward, feeling the cool water lap at your ankles, and notice that the water seems to ripple in time with the distant, lingering echo of the storm’s song.

As you gaze into the pond, the reflection shifts. Instead of your own face, you see a figure cloaked in a soft, glowing light—a being of wind and rain, eyes bright as the lightning that just pierced the sky. It smiles, a gentle, knowing smile, and reaches out a hand, inviting you deeper into the mystery that lies beyond the forest’s edge. The dream folds around you like a warm blanket, and you feel yourself being drawn forward, the storm’s roar fading to a whisper, the forest alive with stories waiting to be uncovered. The night holds its breath, and you know, with a certainty that only a dream can provide, that this is only the beginning of a journey through realms both familiar and fantastical.
