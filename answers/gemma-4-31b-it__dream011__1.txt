...written in a language that doesn't exist, yet as I stare at the curving, iridescent glyphs, I realize I can read them perfectly.

They aren’t questions about mathematics, law, or medicine. Instead, they are inquiries into the architecture of my own soul. 

*Question 1: What is the exact color of the silence that follows a long-forgotten goodbye?*
*Question 2: If you could fold the horizon like a piece of paper, where would the edges meet?*

I look around the room. The other students are writing furiously, their pens scratching against the paper with a sound like dry leaves skipping across pavement. But when I look closer, their desks are beginning to dissolve into fine, white sand. The walls of the examination hall are stretching upward, the ceiling vanishing into a bruised purple sky filled with two moons.

I feel a surge of panic—this is the exam that determines my future, and I don’t have a pen. I look down at my hands and realize my fingers have turned into glass, clear and fragile, with gold currents flowing through them like circuitry.

I decide to answer anyway. I don't use a pen; I simply press my glass finger to the page, and my thoughts bleed onto the paper in shimmering ink. As I write the answer to the first question, the room vanishes entirely. 

I am no longer in a classroom. I am floating in a vast, starless void, and the exam paper has expanded to become the floor beneath me. The questions are now constellations, burning brightly above my head. 

The proctor—who I now realize is a giant, clockwork owl with eyes made of spinning gears—descends from the void and lands softly beside me. It doesn't speak, but its voice echoes in my mind:

*"You are late,"* it whispers. *"The future has already started. Do you wish to see the results?"*

Just as the owl extends a metallic talon to hand me the grade, the floor of stars gives way, and I begin to fall—not with fear, but with a strange, weightless relief—plunging downward through layers of clouds that taste like peppermint and old books, until the wind becomes the sound of my own alarm clock, pulling me back to the surface of the waking world.
