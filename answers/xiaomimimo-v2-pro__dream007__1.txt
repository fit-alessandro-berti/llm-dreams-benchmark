# The Dream Continues...

...you decide to take a breath. The anger is still there—hot, prickling—but something shifts. You notice your friend's hands are shaking too. And suddenly you realize: you're standing in your childhood kitchen, and the argument is about something you can't quite name anymore, but it feels like everything.

"I don't want to lose you over this," you say. But the words come out slowly, like you're speaking underwater.

Your friend's face softens, then blurs. "I don't even remember what we're fighting about," they say. And it's true—the specifics are dissolving like morning fog, leaving only the feeling underneath. *Hurt. Fear that we're growing apart.*

The kitchen table between you starts stretching, getting longer and longer. You're moving further apart, but neither of you is walking. You reach across the impossible distance—

And your hand finds theirs.

The room fills with golden light. Outside the window, it's suddenly sunset over a place you've never been but somehow recognize. Your friend laughs, and the sound breaks the argument completely, like glass shattering into butterflies.

"We're okay," they say.

You wake up with tears on your cheeks, reaching for your phone to send them a message.

---

*Dreams have a way of showing us what we really value, don't they?* 💭
