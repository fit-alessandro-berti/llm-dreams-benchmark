 grip the wheel with both hands, feeling the ship shudder beneath you like a living creature.

Lightning splits the sky, revealing mountains of black water rising on every side. For a moment, fear passes across the faces of your crew—but then you straighten your back and shout orders above the roar.

“Trim the sails! Secure the lines! Keep her pointed into the waves!”

Your voice cuts through the storm like a bell. The crew springs into motion, slipping and stumbling across the rain-slick deck, but trusting you completely. The ship climbs a towering wave, higher and higher, until it feels as if you are sailing into the sky itself.

At the crest, everything goes silent.

The wind stops. The rain hangs motionless in the air like glass beads. Far below, the sea glows with a strange blue light, and beneath the waves you see shapes moving—vast, gentle creatures with eyes like lanterns.

Then, from the clouds ahead, a lighthouse appears where no land should be. Its beam turns slowly, not cutting through the storm, but calming it. Each sweep of light smooths the sea, softens the thunder, and warms the air.

Your first mate turns to you and whispers, “Captain… that lighthouse wasn’t there before.”

You know this is true. You also know, somehow, that it has been waiting for you.

You steer toward it.

As the ship glides forward, the storm peels away behind you like a curtain. The crew gathers at the bow in awe. The lighthouse grows larger, its base rising not from an island, but from the back of an enormous sleeping whale.

At the door of the lighthouse stands a figure in a coat of silver rain, holding a lantern.

They raise one hand in greeting.

And just as your ship comes alongside the whale, the figure calls out in a voice that sounds exactly like the sea:

“You made it through the storm. Now choose—wake up, or sail farther.”
