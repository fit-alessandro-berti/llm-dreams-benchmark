You are dreaming.

You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and symbols carved into its silver surface—spirals, crescent moons, and tiny doors within doors. When you run your fingers over them, the box begins to hum.

A voice whispers from somewhere behind you:

“Do not search for the key. Remember it.”

You turn, but the room is empty. Instead, a warm golden light spills from beneath the box. The symbols shift, rearranging themselves into images from your life: a childhood bedroom, a forgotten garden, a face you can almost recognize. With every image, the lock grows warmer.

Then you understand. The box is not locked from the outside.

You place your hand against its lid and think of the one thing you have been afraid to lose. The lock clicks.

Inside is not treasure, but a small glass bird, beating its wings soundlessly. As you lift it out, the walls dissolve, the sky pours into the room, and the bird flies directly into your chest.

You wake with your heart racing—and a faint silver pattern glowing on your palm.
