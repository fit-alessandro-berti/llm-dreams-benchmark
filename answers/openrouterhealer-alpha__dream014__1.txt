…find yourself standing in the middle of a plaza where the sun is setting, casting long amber shadows across the cobblestones. The group doesn’t just point you in a direction—they walk with you, their conversation weaving together like a tapestry.

One offers you a warm, slightly chipped ceramic cup of tea from a cart that wasn’t there moments before. Another pulls a paper map from their coat—its edges softened with age—and traces a path with their finger, but the lines on the map shift gently, like ripples on water, showing not streets but places you’ve been longing to visit: a quiet library with ivy crawling up the walls, a rooftop garden overlooking the harbor, a bench where someone once told you a story that made you laugh until your sides ached.

The city around you begins to soften. The noise dulls into a distant hum, and the buildings seem to breathe. You realize you’re not being led *out* of the city—you’re being led deeper into a version of it that feels like home.

They stop at an archway strung with tiny lights. “This way,” one says, smiling. “You’ll know when you see it.”

And as you step through, you feel not lost anymore—just wonderfully, mysteriously *found*.

Where does the dream take you next?
