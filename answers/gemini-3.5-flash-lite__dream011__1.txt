I take a deep, steadying breath. *Don't read with your eyes,* a distant memory from Professor Vane’s lectures echoes in my mind. *Read with your intuition.*

I close my eyes and place my palm flat against the trembling parchment. The ink is cool, humming with a frequency that vibrates right through my bones. Question one pulses beneath my fingertips. It isn't asking for a historical date or a mathematical formula; it's asking for a feeling. A memory of falling, balanced by the absolute certainty of flight.

I pick up my stylus. As the tip touches the page, it doesn't leave a mark of ink—it leaves a trail of pale blue light. 

*Let's begin,* I whisper to the dark.
