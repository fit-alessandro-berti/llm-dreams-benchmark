*"The exam is not what you think. The real test is who you become when you stop waiting for the answer."*

The room begins to disintegrate around you—walls melting into trees, paper dissolving into leaves, the ceiling opening into a sky filled with floating, silent books. A voice, soft and familiar, whispers from the distance: *"You’ve been studying for the wrong exam all along."*

And then, suddenly, you’re not in a classroom anymore.

You’re standing at the edge of a forest made of glass—all the trees are crystalline columns refracting light into impossible colors. At their center, a single door made of woven starlight stands open. On the threshold, a person waits—your older self, eyes knowing, holding a key shaped like a question mark.

You look down. In your hands, you’re no longer holding a test paper.

You’re holding a blank canvas.

And you know, with a quiet certainty that floods your bones:  
This is the real beginning.
