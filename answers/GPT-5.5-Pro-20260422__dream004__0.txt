…a brush made of moonlight resting beside it.

When you touch the brush, it hums softly, as if it has been waiting for you. The canvas is blank, but not empty. It breathes. Its surface ripples like water, reflecting colors that do not have names yet.

You make one small stroke.

The line lifts itself from the canvas and becomes a silver road, winding into the sky. Another stroke becomes a flock of glass birds. A circle becomes a door. A splash of blue turns into rain that falls upward, and every drop carries a tiny memory you thought you had forgotten.

You step closer, and the canvas grows until it is no longer in front of you but all around you. The changing world pauses. The shifting colors hold still, watching.

Then you realize the landscape was never changing on its own.

It was responding to you.

You paint a sun with green flames, and warmth spreads through the air. You paint a tree with windows instead of leaves, and behind each window is a different version of yourself, waving gently. You paint a staircase made of shadows, and it leads down into a glowing ocean where stars sleep beneath the waves.

At the bottom of the staircase, you find another canvas.

This one is not blank.

It shows you sleeping in your bed, dreaming of a world where colors and shapes are constantly changing.

A voice made of wind whispers, “Now paint the way home.”

So you dip the moonlit brush into the ocean of stars and draw a single golden line across the sky. The world folds softly around it, like paper becoming a bird.

And just before you wake, you see one final shape appear on the canvas:

a door, slightly open, waiting for the next dream.
