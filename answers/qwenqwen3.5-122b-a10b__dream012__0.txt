The thunder does not crash; it hums a low, resonant chord that vibrates in your teeth. You understand then that the storm is not an enemy to be defeated, but a current to be ridden. You loosen your grip on the wheel, letting it spin freely. The ship does not sink. Instead, the hull begins to glow, turning translucent as the water beneath you rises to meet the sky.

The waves stop rising and begin to fall upward, turning into rain that flows from the ocean to the clouds. The lightning strikes the mast, but instead of burning, it blooms into a canopy of silver leaves that rustle with the sound of a thousand whispering voices. You look at your crew, and they are no longer sailors; they are memories of people you once knew, smiling with a quiet peace.

You steer the ship not toward a horizon, but inward. The sea dissolves into mist, and the ship becomes a paper boat drifting on a stream of ink. You feel the sensation of falling, not down, but *through* the layers of the dream. The ink clears, revealing a white ceiling above you. The salt smell fades, replaced by the scent of morning coffee and dust. You blink, and the storm is gone, leaving only the echo of the song in your mind as you wake.
