...speak, but instead of words, a flock of pale, glowing moths tumbles from your lips. 

You instinctively reach up to catch them, but your fingers pass right through their powder-soft wings. The crowd doesn’t gasp. They don't look confused. Instead, they lean forward in unison, their faces blurring slightly, as if you are looking at them through the surface of a swimming pool. 

You realize, with the sudden, unquestioning logic of a dream, that you aren't supposed to lecture them. You are supposed to feed them. 

You open your mouth wider, relaxing your throat, and the moths begin to pour out in a continuous, velvet stream. They carry a feeling with them—a deep, unnamable nostalgia for a place you’ve never visited. As they drift over the audience, the theater begins to change. The heavy red velvet curtains behind you dissolve into a dense wall of weeping willows. The wooden stage beneath your bare feet softens into cool, damp moss. 

You look back out at the seats. The expectant faces have transformed into hundreds of silver flowers, their petals turning toward you like satellite dishes, gently drinking in the light of the moths as they land. 

The oppressive, terrifying weight of having to say the *right* thing completely evaporates. There is no script. There are no mistakes. You just need to breathe out the light. 

Filled with a profound, floating sense of relief, you take a deep breath of rain-scented air, ready to give them the next verse, and as you exhale, the edges of the theater begin to dissolve into a warm, hazy violet blur...
