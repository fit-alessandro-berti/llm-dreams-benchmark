...breathe deeply and grip the microphone stand. The spotlight is warm on my face, almost blinding, and for a moment the words I'd prepared evaporate like morning mist.

But then something strange happens.

I notice the front row is filled with people I recognize — my childhood best friend, my favorite teacher from years ago, a stranger I once helped on the subway who smiled at me with such gratitude. They're all here, nodding gently, as if to say *"It's okay. We're listening."*

So I begin to speak — not from notes, not from memory, but from somewhere deeper. The words flow out like water finding its path downhill, natural and unhurried. I'm talking about connection, about the small moments that stitch our lives together — a shared laugh, a hand reaching out in the dark, the way a stranger's kindness can alter the entire course of a day.

The audience leans forward. The room becomes perfectly still, except for my voice.

And then, impossibly, people in the crowd begin to glow — faintly at first, like embers catching wind, then brighter, warm gold and soft amber. One by one, they illuminate the darkened auditorium until there is no need for stage lights at all.

I'm not nervous anymore. I'm not even sure I'm on a stage. I feel as though I'm standing in the middle of a constellation, surrounded by points of light that are somehow *people* and somehow *stars* at the same time.

And I realize — this speech was never about me. It was about *them*.
