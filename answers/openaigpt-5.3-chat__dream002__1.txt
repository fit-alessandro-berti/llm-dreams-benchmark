You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the air tightens around you, thick with the smell of rain and electricity. The leaves that were gently whispering a moment ago begin to shiver and twist.

The sky darkens too quickly, as if someone has pulled a curtain across the sun.

A distant rumble rolls through the trees.

You start down the path faster now, but the forest is subtly different. The trail bends where it never bent before. Trees stand closer together. Their trunks look taller, older, like silent watchers.

Wind rushes through the canopy and the first cold drops of rain tap against your shoulders.

Then lightning flashes.

For a split second the entire forest turns silver—and you see something ahead on the path. A small wooden door standing alone between two enormous trees.

Another thunderclap shakes the ground.

The door slowly creaks open, though no one is there.

Warm golden light spills out onto the dark, wet forest floor, and you realize the storm is not chasing you.

It is pushing you toward the door.
