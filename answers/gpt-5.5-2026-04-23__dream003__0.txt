You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and faint symbols etched into the dark wood, shifting whenever you look away.

One symbol glows softly beneath your fingertip—a crescent moon wrapped around a tiny flame. The moment you touch it, the air grows still. The distant sound of waves fills the room, though there is no ocean nearby. The box trembles once, then whispers your name.

A seam appears where there had been none before.

You lean closer, heart pounding, and realize the patterns are not decoration at all, but a map—one that shows the room you are standing in, the path you took to get there, and a door behind you that was not there a moment ago.

The box clicks.

Not open—awake.

Its lid lifts just enough for a ribbon of silver light to spill out. Inside is not treasure, not jewels, not a key, but a small glass mirror. When you pick it up, your reflection smiles before you do.

Then it raises a finger to its lips.

“Not yet,” it whispers.

Behind you, the new door slowly opens, revealing a staircase descending into a sky full of stars.
