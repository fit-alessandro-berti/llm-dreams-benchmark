…open your mouth, and instead of words, a flock of glowing paper cranes drifts out, circling the lights above the stage.

The audience gasps—but not in confusion. They rise together, thousands of people unfolding tiny maps from their pockets. Each map shows a different version of your life: streets you never took, houses you almost lived in, faces you loved in other timelines. You realize the speech was never meant to be spoken—it was meant to be *remembered*.

So you step away from the microphone and point to the biggest crane. It lands on your shoulder and whispers the first line for you:

“Welcome back. We’ve been waiting for you to choose.”

The stage tilts gently like the deck of a ship. The curtains become night sky. The audience becomes constellations, each star blinking in rhythm with your heartbeat. You take one breath, then another, and with each inhale your fear turns into music—low at first, then bright, then everywhere.

By the time you finish, no one applauds.

Instead, dawn appears at the back of the theater, and everyone walks into it smiling, as if they’ve just remembered their real names.

You wake up with that first line still in your ears.
