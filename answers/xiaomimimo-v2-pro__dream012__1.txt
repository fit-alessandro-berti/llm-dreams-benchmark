—I take a deep breath, the salt spray stinging my face. The deck pitches violently beneath my boots, but I steady myself against the wheel, knuckles white. The sky is a churning mass of ink and slate, waves rising like dark mountains around us.

“Hold fast!” I shout, my voice cutting through the roar of the wind. “Reef the main sail—now! We need to ride the waves, not fight them!”

The crew moves with desperate precision, clinging to ropes as the ship groans. Lightning splits the sky, illuminating faces wide with fear but determined. I spot young Thomas near the bow, struggling with a line. I meet his eyes and nod—*you can do this*.

Suddenly, the compass spins wildly, useless. We’re off course. But I remember my old teacher’s words: *When the instruments fail, feel the ship. She knows the way.*

I close my eyes, hands firm on the wheel, and listen—not just to the storm, but to the hull’s creak, the rhythm of the waves, the faint shift in the wind’s howl. There’s a pattern beneath the chaos. A rhythm.

“Port side!” I yell. “There’s a current—we follow it out of the storm’s eye!”

The crew hesitates, then trusts. We turn into a colossal wave, climbing it at an angle that seems impossible—until we crest, for a breathless moment, above the worst of it. Below, the sea churns, but ahead… a break in the clouds. A sliver of pale, dawn light.

The storm still rages behind us, but we are moving toward calm. The crew lets out a ragged cheer. I allow myself a small smile, though my arms ache with fatigue.

“Steady now,” I say softly, more to myself than anyone. “The sea tests us so we remember how to find our way.”

And as the light grows, I see an albatross gliding peacefully through the rain, leading us home.
