You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the wind begins to whisper through the trees. At first it sounds like ordinary rustling leaves, but then the whispers become words.

You can't quite understand them.

Dark clouds roll across the sky, swallowing the sunlight. The path ahead twists in unfamiliar directions, as if the forest is rearranging itself while you're not looking. Thunder rumbles in the distance.

You start walking faster.

Then you notice a faint golden light between the trees.

Drawn toward it, you leave the path and push through thick ferns. The storm grows louder behind you, yet the light remains calm and steady. Eventually you reach a clearing where an enormous clock tower stands, impossibly tall and covered in vines. It shouldn't be there, and somehow you know it wasn't there a moment ago.

The clock has no hands.

Instead, its face is filled with moving scenes from your life: childhood memories, forgotten conversations, places you've never visited, and faces you almost recognize. Every few seconds the images change.

A flash of lightning illuminates the clearing.

When the thunder follows, a small wooden door appears at the base of the tower.

You feel certain that opening it is important.

As you approach, the whispers in the wind finally become clear:

*"You only have until the storm reaches the tower."*

You look back. The wall of dark clouds is advancing unnaturally fast, rolling across the forest like a living thing. Trees bend away from it. The air vibrates.

Your hand reaches for the doorknob.

The moment you touch it, the clock begins to chime.

One.

Two.

Three.

With each chime, the clearing changes. The grass becomes a sea. The sea becomes stars. The stars become an endless city floating in the sky.

By the seventh chime, you can no longer remember why you came to the forest.

By the eighth, you can no longer remember your name.

The ninth chime echoes for what feels like years.

Then the door slowly opens, revealing not darkness, but a brightly lit room. Inside sits someone waiting at a table.

They look up.

And you realize, with absolute certainty, that the person is you—only much older.

The older version of you smiles and says:

*"You're later than expected."*

Just as you step across the threshold to ask what that means, a tremendous crash of thunder shakes the world—

—and you wake up.
