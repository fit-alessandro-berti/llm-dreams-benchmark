You start to run.

Or try to run. The sidewalk is wrong under your feet — soft, like corkboard, muffling your steps. Your shoes are suddenly too heavy. No, too light. You're wearing slippers.

You check your phone. 11:07. The meeting is at 12:00. The screen is cracked in a place you've never seen cracked before, and the maps app just shows a blue dot in a gray nowhere. No streets. Just the blue dot, pulsing. You.

The city is huge. Impossibly huge. The buildings don't end, they just fade into a kind of hazy, watercolor smog. And they're all familiar in the worst way — like every city you've ever visited stacked on top of each other. A deli from your hometown next to a laundromat with signs in a language you almost understand.

You start asking people.

"Excuse me, do you know where —"

A woman with a stroller looks at you patiently. She says something, and you understand it perfectly even though her mouth isn't moving right. *"You're close. Just down this way."* She points down an alley that wasn't there a second ago.

You go down the alley. It gets longer as you walk. The doors on either side are all numbered 12:00.

Your pocket buzzes. You don't remember putting anything in your pocket. It's a folded piece of paper, warm, like it's been in someone's hand. You unfold it. It's an invitation.

> **MEETING - 12:00 - Bring nothing. Come as you are.**

No address. On the back, in your own handwriting, but shakier: *DON'T BE LATE THIS TIME.*

You look up and the alley has opened up into a massive train station. No trains. Just a departures board with thousands of destinations flipping too fast to read, except one line that stays still:

**YOU — PLATFORM 7 — NOW BOARDING**

Your heart is hammering. You are not ready. You are wearing slippers and you haven't prepared anything and you don't even know what this meeting is *about*, only that if you miss it something terrible and quiet will happen.

You run for Platform 7. The clock above it says 11:59. Then 11:07 again. Then --:--.

And as you step onto the platform, you see who is waiting for you at the empty table in the middle.

It's you. Just you, but older, calmer. Drinking coffee in slippers exactly like yours. He looks up and smiles, like he's been waiting for an hour but doesn't mind at all.

He says, "Oh good. You found it."
