The air in the hallway was thick with static electricity that tasted of ozone and old rain. You walked toward the podium at the end of the hall, though your legs didn't really walk—your limbs felt like soft cotton that had been pulled through water. As you approached, the competition wasn't just a test of endurance; it was a test of *perception*.

"I can see," you thought internally. "But I don't remember how to dream anymore."

There was no podium, only a vast white expanse under stars. You were not there with other participants, or at least, no one else could see you. It felt like you were the first person in a century who had ever stood where I had before, yet never knew the weight of it. The prize—the job you wanted—wasn't a title or an office; it was the ability to feel what the ceiling was made of.

The challenge began at the threshold. It wasn't a wall, but a mirror that reflected what you *didn't* want. You faced a corridor where the only way forward was to step on your own shadows. You walked through the dark, and when your feet touched the ground for the first time, you found yourself standing still, paralyzed by the realization: you were dreaming of the job you wanted, not wanting it as *you*.

You reached out and touched the mirror on the wall. The reflection in the glass didn't look at you; it looked back at its own dream. It was your mind's version of success.

"You are dreaming?" the voice behind you said.

It wasn't a person, but the wind passing through your ears seemed to whisper answers. The wind felt like a hand. You turned around and found you standing alone in the white expanse. You had finally realized that the competition was never about the prize; it was about letting go of what you thought you needed for success.

The silence grew heavy, but within it, I could see everything. There was no need to win. To dream a job you wanted meant you didn't need it. The dream was already in your hands, waiting for you to stop running.

You closed your eyes. You were awake, and the dream ended, leaving just that one quiet moment between two dreams.
