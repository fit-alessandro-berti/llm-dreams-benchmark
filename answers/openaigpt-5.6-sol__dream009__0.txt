You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to discover not only where you are, but why everyone seems to recognize you.

The village lies beneath a violet dawn, its narrow streets slick with rain and crowded with carts, horses, and people dressed in rough-spun wool. Somewhere a bell tolls—not the measured chime of a clock, but a frantic warning.

A woman selling apples sees your face and drops her basket.

“You came back,” she whispers.

Before you can ask what she means, armored riders thunder into the square. Their leader carries a black banner marked with a silver eclipse—the same symbol as the birthmark that has appeared overnight on your palm.

You run.

The apple seller pulls you through a hidden door and down into tunnels beneath the village. There, by candlelight, you find a mural painted on the stone: a figure wearing your clothes, holding a small rectangle of glass that glows like a star. Your missing phone.

According to the woman, it fell from the sky a hundred years ago.

“You left us a message,” she says, handing you a rusted metal box.

Inside is a note written in your own handwriting:

**Do not let them ring the final bell.  
Do not trust the king.  
And whatever happens, do not wake up.**

Above you, the bell tolls again.

On the third strike, the tunnels begin to shake. Dust pours from the ceiling. A cold blue light spills through cracks in the walls, and your palm burns where the silver eclipse is etched into your skin.

Then you remember.

This is not the first time you have had this dream.

It is the first time you have remembered being here.
