In that suspended moment, you understand that the "important thing" you were arguing about was never actually a specific issue, but a shared, unspoken fear of growing apart. 

You step forward through the moss and take their root-like hand. It feels solid, alive, and warm. In response, the glass walls of the greenhouse shatter silently. But there is no danger, no falling. The glass turns into a million glowing dandelion seeds that drift upward into the violet sky. 

You open your mouth to say, "We're okay," but the sound is replaced by the chirping of your real-world alarm clock. You open your eyes, the lingering warmth still in your chest, wondering what the dream was trying to tell you.
