...build a sanctuary—a hidden oasis of hope in the wasteland. With scavenged materials and sheer determination, you construct a fortified greenhouse, using shattered glass and twisted metal to create a haven for the last surviving seeds. You dig a well, filtering rainwater through layers of sand and charcoal, turning poisoned skies into drinkable life.

By day, you venture into the ruins, trading your meager supplies for knowledge: a rusted book on botany, a solar panel half-buried in rubble, a map to a rumored underground library. By night, you teach others—stragglers, outcasts, the broken—how to read the stars, how to splice grafts from dying plants, how to turn rust into tools.

One evening, as the twin moons (wait, why are there two moons?) rise over the cracked horizon, a child—no older than ten—stumbles into your camp, clutching a single, glowing seed. It pulses like a heartbeat. She whispers, *"The last one. They said it grows where the world ends."*

And then—you wake up.

But the dirt under your fingernails is real.
