You start to run, though every street seems to curve away from where you want to go.

The city is impossibly tall. Glass towers lean together overhead, whispering across the narrow strips of sky. Street signs rearrange themselves whenever you look away. A woman selling clocks from a silver cart points toward an alley and says, “You’re already late.”

“For what?” you ask.

But she has vanished, leaving behind a single ticking watch. It has no hands.

You hurry into the alley and emerge inside a crowded train station. Hundreds of people stand perfectly still while trains rush through without stopping. On the departure board, every destination is your name.

Then your phone rings.

The voice on the other end is yours.

“Don’t go to the meeting,” it says. “That’s where they decide which life you’ll wake up in.”

Across the platform, an elevator opens. Inside is a long conference table surrounded by shadowy figures. At the far end sits an empty chair with a folder placed neatly before it.

The folder has your name on the cover.

The station clock begins counting backward from sixty.

You step into the elevator just before the doors close.

No one speaks. One of the figures slides the folder toward you. Inside are photographs of places you remember but have never been: a seaside house with blue shutters, a hospital corridor filled with flowers, a moonlit forest where your childhood bedroom stands between the trees.

At the bottom is a blank page headed:

**THE ONE THING YOU MUST NOT FORGET**

The figures lean closer.

The countdown reaches ten.

You pick up the pen, suddenly understanding that whatever you write will be true when you wake. You try to write a name, but the letters drift from the page like smoke. You try again, pressing harder.

The clock strikes zero.

Every window in the city bursts open at once, and a warm wind sweeps through the room. The shadowy figures scatter into a flock of black birds. The table stretches into a road. Far ahead, someone familiar is waiting beneath a streetlamp, holding the handless watch.

You run toward them.

With each step, the city peels away—towers folding into paper, streets lifting like ribbons, the sky cracking to reveal morning light.

Just before you reach the figure, they smile and say, “You found it.”

“What?”

“The meeting.”

Then you wake with your hand closed around nothing, and one sentence clear in your mind:

**You were never late. You were only on your way.**
