…open your laptop, but the keys have melted into small, frantic insects.

They skitter across the desk, arranging themselves into words you almost understand. Your boss stands behind you, smiling too widely, tapping a clock with no hands.

“Just send it before yesterday,” they say.

You try to explain that yesterday is gone, but the office windows now show yesterday outside: gray light, rain moving upward, coworkers walking backward through the parking lot. Your inbox fills with messages from yourself, each marked urgent.

One says: **DO NOT OPEN THE ELEVATOR.**

The elevator dings.

Everyone in the office turns toward it at once. The doors slide apart, revealing not a lift, but your childhood bedroom. On the floor is the project, already finished, written in crayon on pages that flutter like birds.

Your boss leans close.

“See?” they whisper. “You’ve always known how to do it.”

Then your alarm begins ringing from somewhere inside the walls.
