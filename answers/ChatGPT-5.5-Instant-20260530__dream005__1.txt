You are at a large social gathering where you only know one person. As you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people and each group seems stranger than the last.

One group is speaking in riddles, answering every question with a puzzle. Another is dressed in clothes from different centuries, laughing as if they all share a secret. A third group is made entirely of people who look vaguely familiar, though you cannot remember from where.

You move through the crowd, calling your friend's name, but the music keeps changing and swallowing the sound. Every time you think you spot them across the room, the room stretches longer, as though the building itself is gently moving away from you.

Then you notice something unusual: everyone is holding a small lantern. Some glow blue, some gold, some green. When you ask about them, an elderly woman smiles and says, "The lanterns show what people are looking for."

You look down and discover a lantern in your own hand. Its light flickers silver.

"What does silver mean?" you ask.

"It means you're searching for someone," she replies, "but perhaps not the person you think."

Before you can ask more, the crowd parts. At the far end of a grand staircase stands the friend you've been searching for. Relieved, you hurry toward them. Yet as you climb the stairs, the faces around you begin to blur like wet paint, and the music slows until each note hangs in the air.

Your friend smiles when you finally reach them.

"I've been looking everywhere for you," you say.

"I know," they answer. "But you had to meet everyone else first."

They hand you a small key. It is identical to the shape of the silver light inside your lantern.

"What does it open?" you ask.

Instead of answering, they point toward a door that wasn't there before. The door stands alone in the middle of the ballroom, glowing softly.

You place the key into the lock.

The door swings open onto a quiet sunrise over a calm ocean. Warm light spills through the doorway and washes over the crowd. For a moment, everyone becomes perfectly clear, and you recognize them all—not as strangers, but as different versions of yourself from forgotten memories, abandoned plans, old hopes, and possible futures.

The silver lantern shines brighter than the sun.

Then a wave of golden light rushes toward you, and just as you step through the doorway, the dream dissolves into brightness, leaving you with the feeling that you found exactly what you were searching for, even though you can no longer remember what it was.
