...symbols that seem to shift when you try to focus on them directly. They look almost like a language you once knew—perhaps in childhood, perhaps in another life—and your fingers trace the grooves before you even decide to touch them.

The box is warm. Warmer than wood should be.

You realize there is no keyhole. There never was. The patterns themselves are the lock, and as you watch, they begin to rearrange, slowly, like frost forming in reverse. Somewhere behind you—though when you turn, there is no room, only a soft gray nowhere—a voice says your name in your mother's voice, except your mother never sounded quite like that.

Then you understand, the way you understand things only in dreams: the box will open when you remember what is inside it.

So you sit down. The floor is closer than it should be, and it gives slightly, like moss. You press your forehead against the cool lid and try to remember. The patterns pulse beneath your hands like a heartbeat that isn't yours, or maybe one that was always yours and you had forgotten.

And then you do remember—or the box remembers for you. The lid sighs open without a sound.

Inside there is no treasure. No letter. No small impossible creature curled in sleep. Inside there is only a mirror, and in the mirror, a room, and in the room, a figure hunched over a locked box, tracing patterns they almost recognize.

You wave. They wave back—a moment too late, like an echo learning to be a voice.

Somewhere far away and getting closer, a key turns in a lock.

You wake just before you hear it open.
