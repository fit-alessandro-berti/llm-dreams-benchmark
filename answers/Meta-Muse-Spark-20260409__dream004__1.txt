You are in a world where colors and shapes are constantly changing. As you explore this surreal landscape, you come across a blank canvas and it feels like it’s waiting for you, breathing in time with the shifting sky.  

**The canvas**  
It isn’t white. It’s the absence of decision. The frame hums, and when you step closer, your reflection doesn’t appear. Instead, tiny ripples of whatever color you’re thinking of spread across the surface, then vanish.  

**What happens when you touch it**  
Your fingertip doesn’t meet resistance. It sinks in like warm mist. The moment you make contact, the world pauses. The swirling hills freeze mid-melt. The trees stop trading shapes with the clouds. Silence, except for a sound like distant chimes.  

You realize the canvas isn’t for painting on. It’s for listening.  

**The choice**  
Three thoughts drift up from the surface, written in ink that keeps rewriting itself:  
- **Remember**: Pull out a moment from your waking life and let the canvas hold it. The colors around you would finally settle, but only into that memory.  
- **Create**: Press your whole palm down and pour something new into the world. You don’t know what will spill out, only that the landscape will never be the same.  
- **Step through**: The canvas parts like curtains. You can walk into the blank and become the first color of whatever comes next.  

The air tastes like ozone and possibility. Your hand is still submerged to the second knuckle, and the dream is waiting to see which kind of courage you pick.  

What do you do with the blank?
