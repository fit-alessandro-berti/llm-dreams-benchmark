**The Argument Shifts**
As your voices rise, the room around you begins to *melt*—not like wax, but like a painting left in the rain. The walls dissolve into swirling colors, and the floor beneath you tilts, sending you both sliding toward the center of the space. Your friend’s face flickers, their features rearranging: one moment they’re themselves, the next, they’re a stranger, then a version of you from years ago, then back again. You try to grab their arm, but your fingers pass through them like mist.

*"You’re not even listening!"* they shout—or is it you shouting? The words echo, multiplying until the air vibrates with accusations you can’t quite remember making.

Suddenly, you’re no longer in a room. You’re standing on a vast, cracked plain under a sky split between day and night. Your friend is still there, but now they’re holding a mirror. Not a reflection of you—it shows *them*, but older, or younger, or crying, or laughing in a way you’ve never seen. *"This is what you’re really fighting,"* they say, voice hollow. *"Not me. Not this."*

The mirror shatters. The shards hover in the air, each one showing a different memory: the time you laughed until your sides ached, the time you lied to protect them, the time they forgot your birthday, the time you both swore you’d never change. The pieces start to orbit you like a storm.

*"Pick one,"* your friend says, but their mouth isn’t moving. The voice comes from the wind.

You reach out—

—and the dream lurches. Now you’re in a crowded subway car, pressed against strangers. Your friend is across from you, silent, staring at their phone. The train screeches to a halt. The lights flicker. When they come back on, everyone else is gone. Just the two of you, and the train is moving *upward*, through the ceiling, into the sky.

*"We’re going to fall,"* you say.

*"We already did,"* they reply.

The train dissolves into birds. Hundreds of them, black and white, swirling around you in a cyclone. You try to speak, but the birds fill your mouth, your lungs, your mind. Their wings brush against your thoughts, and suddenly you *remember* what the argument was about: not the thing you were yelling about, but something deeper. A fear. A wound. A question neither of you knew how to ask.

The birds scatter. You’re standing in a field of tall grass, the kind that sways even when there’s no wind. Your friend is beside you, but they’re not looking at you. They’re looking at the horizon, where a single figure is walking toward you. It’s *you*—but not the you of now. The you from the future, or the past, or a version that never was.

*"You have to choose,"* Future-You says, not unkindly. *"Do you want to be right, or do you want to be close?"*

Your friend turns to you. Their eyes are clear. *"I’m sorry,"* they say. *"I didn’t mean—"*

But the dream is already unraveling. The field folds in on itself like a piece of paper. You’re falling, or flying, or both. The last thing you hear is your own voice, whispering:

*"I don’t know how to do this."*

**Possible Endings (Pick One or Mix Them):**
1. **The Wake-Up:** You jolt awake, heart pounding, the echo of the argument still ringing in your ears. Your phone buzzes—a text from your friend: *"Hey. Can we talk?"* You stare at it, unsure if it’s real or the dream bleeding into waking life.
2. **The Metamorphosis:** As you fall, your body changes. You become a child, then an animal, then a tree, then a storm cloud. Your friend is there in every form, always just out of reach. The dream doesn’t end—it *expands*, until you realize you’re not dreaming *about* the argument anymore. You’re dreaming *inside* it.
3. **The Resolution (But Is It?):** The figure from the horizon steps forward. It’s not you—it’s your friend, but older, wiser. They take your hand. *"It’s okay,"* they say. *"This is how we learn."* The dream fades into warmth, into safety. But when you wake up, you can’t shake the feeling that something was *left unsaid*.
4. **The Loop:** You’re back in the argument, but this time, you *listen*. You say the thing you were too afraid to say before. Your friend’s face softens. The room stops melting. But as you reach for them, the dream *resets*—same argument, same words, same choices. You’re trapped in the cycle, and the only way out is to *change*.
5. **The Absurd Twist:** The birds return, but now they’re made of paper. They unfold into letters, into contracts, into a single, crumpled note in your friend’s handwriting: *"I was wrong. But so were you."* The sky opens up, and a giant, floating hand reaches down to pluck you both out of the dream. You wake up in a stranger’s living room, your friend beside you, both of you wearing pajamas you don’t recognize. *"Where are we?"* you ask. They shrug. *"I think we’re still dreaming."*

**Why This Works in a Dream:**
- **Symbolism:** Mirrors, birds, trains, and shifting landscapes are classic dream motifs for self-reflection, freedom, transition, and instability.
- **Emotional Truth:** Dreams often exaggerate conflicts to force us to confront what we’re *really* upset about.
- **Unreality:** The rules of physics and logic don’t apply—because in dreams, *feelings* are the only rules.
- **Ambiguity:** Dreams rarely give clear answers. The "resolution" might feel satisfying or unsettling, but it’s always *open-ended*.
