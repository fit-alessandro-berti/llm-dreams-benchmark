You weave through the crowd, scanning familiar faces in a sea of strangers. The hum of conversation swells around you—laughter, clinking glasses, snippets of stories you’re not part of. You spot your friend near the far wall, but before you can reach them, a cluster of colleagues intercepts you, pulling you into a debate about weekend plans.  

You excuse yourself politely, only to be drawn into a circle of old classmates reminiscing about high school pranks. Just as you slip away again, someone hands you a drink and insists you try their homemade salsa. Each detour feels like a gentle current pushing you off course.  

Finally, you catch your friend’s eye across the room. They wave, smiling, and you take a step forward—only to trip over someone’s bag. As you stumble, the room tilts, the lights blur, and suddenly… you’re standing alone in a quiet garden under a full moon. The gathering is gone. Your friend is gone. But the air smells like jasmine, and somewhere, faintly, you hear your name being called.
