You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and they begin to *breathe*—subtle, living vines of silver and shadow that twist and untwist across the dark wood like thoughts you haven’t had yet. The patterns aren’t carved; they *grow*, pulsing with a soft bioluminescent glow that matches the rhythm of your own heartbeat. 

You lean closer, breath fogging the surface, and the vines part just enough to reveal a single symbol at the center: an eye that opens when you blink. Inside the pupil swirls an entire night sky. The box grows warm under your fingertips. A low, musical hum rises—half lullaby, half invitation—and the lock, which had no keyhole, simply dissolves into mist.

The lid lifts on its own with a sound like distant wind chimes. Inside is not treasure, but **more sky**. A perfect miniature cosmos, galaxies spinning lazily around a tiny, perfect moon that looks suspiciously like the one you remember from childhood dreams. You reach in, and your hand passes through without resistance. The stars tickle your palm like cool water.

Suddenly the box expands—or maybe you shrink—and you tumble inside. You’re floating weightless among the constellations, each star a memory you’d forgotten you possessed: the taste of summer rain on your tongue when you were seven, the exact shade of blue your grandmother’s eyes became when she laughed, the feeling of being completely understood by someone who no longer exists in waking life. 

A gentle current carries you toward the glowing moon. As you approach, its surface ripples and becomes a mirror. In it you see yourself—not as you are now, but as every version of you that ever dreamed of flying, of belonging, of becoming something vast and kind. The reflection smiles, reaches through the glass, and takes your hand.

For a timeless moment you are both the dreamer and the dreamed. Then the moon exhales a soft silver mist that wraps around you like a blanket, and the box gently closes behind you.

You wake in your own bed, dawn light painting the ceiling. Your fingers still tingle with starlight. On the nightstand sits the box—now small, ordinary, and unlocked—its patterns quiet and still, as if they’re only waiting for the next time you close your eyes. 

A single silver thread, fine as a dream, trails from the open lid and dissolves into morning air.
