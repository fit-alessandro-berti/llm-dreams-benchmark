…speak, but no sound comes out.

The microphone is in your hand. You tap it. Nothing.

The audience waits patiently, hundreds of faces illuminated by a soft golden light. Strangely, nobody seems annoyed. They simply watch.

You clear your throat and try again.

This time, instead of words, tiny glowing shapes drift from your mouth—circles, triangles, spirals of light. They float above the crowd like lanterns. As each shape appears, people nod as though they understand perfectly.

Curious, you stop trying to speak and simply think.

A memory of a childhood summer becomes a flock of bright blue birds that flies over the audience. A worry you've been carrying turns into a gray cloud that dissolves into silver rain. A hope for the future becomes a towering tree of crystal branches growing from the center of the stage.

The audience begins contributing their own thoughts. Their dreams rise into the air as colors and symbols, weaving together with yours until the entire theater transforms into a living sky of stories.

The ceiling disappears.

Above you stretches a vast night filled with constellations that are not stars but shared memories. One constellation resembles a friend you have not seen in years. Another resembles a path winding toward a distant horizon.

Then you notice someone sitting in the very last row.

Unlike everyone else, they are taking notes.

You walk toward them, crossing rows that somehow become longer with every step. When you finally reach them, they hand you a small notebook. On the first page is a transcript of the speech you thought you never gave.

Every word is there.

At the bottom of the final page, a single sentence is written in careful handwriting:

*"The things you cannot say are sometimes the things everyone already understands."*

As you read it, the lights of the theater begin folding themselves into the pages of the notebook, one by one, until only a single glowing word remains.

Just before you can read it, you wake up.
