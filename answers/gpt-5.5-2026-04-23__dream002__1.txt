You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the air grows heavy, as if the whole forest is holding its breath.

The leaves above you twist and shimmer, their green turning silver in the strange light. A low rumble rolls across the sky, but it sounds less like thunder and more like a distant voice calling your name. You begin to walk faster, searching for shelter, when a narrow path appears between two ancient trees where there had been only ferns moments before.

You follow it.

Rain begins to fall, but the drops do not soak you. Instead, each one glows softly as it touches the ground, becoming tiny stars scattered across the moss. The storm gathers overhead in spirals of purple and blue, and from the center of the clouds descends a great white stag with antlers like branches of lightning.

It looks at you calmly.

Without speaking, it turns and walks deeper into the forest. Somehow you know you are meant to follow. The storm grows louder behind you, bending trees and tearing leaves into the air, but around the stag there is silence. Each step you take feels lighter, until your feet barely touch the earth.

At last, the path opens into a clearing. In the center stands a small wooden door, upright by itself with no wall around it. The stag lowers its head toward the handle.

You reach out, turn it, and open the door.

On the other side is not a room, but the same forest—bright, warm, and peaceful again. Sunlight pours through the branches. Birds sing as though morning has just begun. When you look back, the storm has vanished, and so has the stag.

But in your hand, you find a silver leaf, warm to the touch.

As you close your fingers around it, you hear the forest whisper:

“Remember—storms are only doors you have not opened yet.”
