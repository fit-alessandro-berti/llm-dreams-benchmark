You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the sky twists into swirling shades of indigo and violet, as if the clouds themselves are alive and dreaming. Thunder laughs like an old friend you haven't seen in years, while lightning sketches fleeting pictures in the air—faces from childhood, half-remembered maps, and impossible machines. 

The rain begins, but these aren't ordinary drops; they shimmer like liquid stars, turning into flocks of glowing butterflies the moment they touch your skin. The trees start dancing, their roots lifting from the earth in rhythmic steps, whispering secrets in a language that feels both ancient and deeply personal. One particularly wise-looking oak swings open its trunk like a door, revealing a warm golden light inside. You step through without hesitation, and suddenly the storm is behind you, the forest has become an ocean of floating islands, and you're flying alongside versions of yourself from other dreams—some wiser, some wilder, all laughing as the world bends and reshapes around you.

(And then, just as the adventure peaks, you feel the faint tug of waking... but not yet.)
