You are about to take the final exam for a course that determines your future career. As you sit down to start the exam, you notice the questions are written in a language that keeps shifting—first elegant calligraphy you almost recognize, then cascading equations that rearrange themselves mid-sentence, then fragments of your own childhood diary entries.  

The first question simply reads: “Who are you when no one is looking?” and the next blank space is already filled with your answer in handwriting that isn’t yours.  

The clock on the wall ticks backward. Every time you try to write, the pen turns into a small bird and flies off with the page. The professor (now a smiling fox in a tweed jacket) just pours tea that never fills the cup and says gently, “You already passed. The exam has always been about what you notice.”  

You look down again and the entire blue book is blank—except for one sentence in the center:  
“Wake up. The real test is waiting.”  

Then the classroom walls soft-focus into morning light, and the dream ends.  

What happens next is up to you.
