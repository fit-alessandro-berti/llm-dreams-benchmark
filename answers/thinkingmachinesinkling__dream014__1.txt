One of them, an old woman with stars tangled in her hair, presses a small brass key into your palm. "You were never missing," she says, her voice like warm rain. "The city was just waiting for you to ask."

Then the pavement beneath you turns to grass, and you wake up—just for a moment—standing on a rooftop garden you've never seen before, surrounded by strangers who are now friends, watching the sunrise paint the skyline gold. You don't need a map anymore. You belong to the maze.
