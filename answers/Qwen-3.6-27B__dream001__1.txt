The pavement beneath your feet feels less like concrete and more like warm, yielding sand, shifting with every step. The skyscrapers around you are not made of glass and steel, but of towering stacks of old books, their spines glowing with faint, pulsating light. The air smells of ozone and burnt sugar.

You check your watch, but the hands are spinning backward so fast they blur into a silver disc. Panic flares in your chest, hot and sharp. You need to find the meeting place. *Where is it?* The thought echoes in your mind, but instead of an answer, a whisper rises from the streets: *"Look for the door that isn't there."*

You turn a corner and see a crowd of people dressed in suits made of origami paper. They move in perfect synchronization, folding and unfolding as they walk. One of them turns to you, its face a blank white sheet, and points upward.

Above, the sky is cracked open like a porcelain plate, revealing a swirling vortex of deep purple clouds. Through the crack, you can see a single, floating office building, tethered to nothing, drifting slowly toward the horizon. A golden elevator cable descends from it, swaying gently in the windless air.

You realize with sudden clarity that the meeting isn’t in a location—it’s in a moment. And the clock on your wrist has stopped completely.

You take a breath, step off the curb, and begin to climb the invisible stairs that have just appeared before you.
