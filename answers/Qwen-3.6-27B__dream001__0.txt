The city itself is impossible. Skyscrapers twist into spirals that defy gravity, their windows blinking open and shut like the eyes of sleeping giants. There are no street signs, only floating neon symbols that change shape whenever you try to focus on them. One moment a sign reads "North," and the next it morphs into a picture of a fish, then a clock with no hands.

Panic begins to rise in your chest, but strangely, it feels distant, muffled by the thick, purple fog that rolls in from the horizon. You check your watch, but instead of numbers, the face displays a countdown of falling leaves. *3... 2... 1...*

Just as the last leaf hits the ground, the noise of the city stops abruptly. The twisting towers freeze mid-rotation. A figure steps out from the fog ahead—a man wearing a suit made entirely of mirrors. He holds an envelope sealed with wax the color of midnight.

"You're late," he says, his voice echoing as if spoken in a large cathedral. But before you can apologize or ask where you are, the ground beneath you dissolves into sand. You look down to see yourself standing on the edge of a vast desert, the city now just a mirage shimmering in the heat above you. The meeting, you realize with a sudden, dream-logic clarity, was never in the city at all. It was here, in the silence between thoughts.
