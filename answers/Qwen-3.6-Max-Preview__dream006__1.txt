As more bubbles escape you, the stage beneath your feet begins to soften, turning into thick, warm moss. The spotlight above shifts from harsh white to a gentle, twilight purple.

You realize you aren’t giving a speech at all; you are conducting an orchestra of silence. You raise your hands, and the crowd rises with you, not in applause, but in unison, floating gently off their seats as gravity loosens its hold.
