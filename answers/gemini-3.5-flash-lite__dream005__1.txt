You squeeze past a circle of laughing people who aren't making any sound—their mouths move exaggeratedly wide, like silent film actors, while the only audio is the roaring sound of ocean waves crashing against the floorboards. 

Further down the endless hallway, you pass a group sitting at a banquet table stacked impossibly high with glowing, translucent fruit. They all turn to look at you simultaneously, their faces blurred and shifting like water beneath a heavy rain. One of them hands you a silver pocket watch. When you look at the face, the hands are spinning backward, and instead of numbers, it just says *ALMOST* over and over again.

Suddenly, the floor beneath you turns to warm, slick moss. You stumble forward into a dimly lit courtyard where the sky above is a deep, bruised purple, filled with two slow-moving moons. 

You finally see your friend. They are standing by an old stone fountain, leaning against the edge, holding two glasses of sparkling water. They look over, smile brightly, and say, "Took you long enough. Did you bring the keys?"

You reach into your pocket to answer, but your hand passes entirely through the fabric, as if your clothes are made of mist. You open your mouth to speak, but no words come out—instead, a handful of blue flower petals drifts out into the cool night air. 

Your friend tilts their head, their features beginning to dissolve into the twilight like sugar in tea, and whispers, *"Wake up now."*

The ground beneath you vanishes. You are falling through a rush of cold wind, and—
