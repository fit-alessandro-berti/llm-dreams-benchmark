You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and swirling runes that seem to shift and rearrange themselves whenever you blink. The box is warm to the touch, almost as if it's breathing—subtly rising and falling under your fingertips.

You run your hand along the edges and feel a faint pulse. The patterns glow faintly blue, then gold, then a deep violet. Without thinking, you press your palm flat against the largest spiral. The box vibrates, and a soft voice (neither male nor female, more like wind through glass) whispers inside your head:

"Not everything that is locked wants to stay closed. Some doors open only when you stop looking for the key."

The lid springs open with a sound like a held breath released. Inside isn't treasure or a threat—there's a tiny folding map drawn on living parchment that unfolds itself into the exact shape of a room you dimly recognize as your childhood bedroom. In the center of the map rests a single object: a small silver key that looks exactly like the one you once lost years ago… except this one is still warm and slightly wet, as if it just finished crying.

As you pick up the key, the dream-room around you dissolves into floating islands of half-remembered conversations and half-written stories. The box folds itself into a paper bird and flies ahead, guiding you. Every time you think you've reached solid ground, the ground becomes water, then bookshelves, then open sky.

You realize, with the calm logic of dreams, that the box never needed a key to open. *You* were the lock. And now that it's open, the only thing left is to decide what you want to lock—or free—next.

The paper bird circles once, landing lightly on your shoulder. In a voice made of rustling pages it asks:

"Where do we go from here?"

…and the dream holds, waiting for your answer.
