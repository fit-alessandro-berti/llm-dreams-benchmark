...open a bakery in the hollowed-out shell of a rusted military tank. 

You don't have flour or water, so instead, you knead the thick morning fog and the soft, silver glow of moonlight into a warm, breathing dough. The oven is fueled not by scavenged wood, but by discarded, forgotten regrets, which burn with a surprisingly sweet, cinnamon-like heat. The smell of fresh, crusty bread begins to drift across the barren wasteland, cutting right through the usual stench of ash and ozone.

It defies all logic, yet under the fluid, forgiving rules of your dream, it works perfectly.

Soon, the dangers of the world come to your door. A pack of mutated, six-eyed hounds pads up to the tank's hatch, their low growls vibrating in your chest. But they don't attack. Instead, they sit patiently on their haunches, offering you payment in the form of smooth, iridescent river stones and lost, shiny brass buttons. You hand them warm loaves wrapped in old, faded maps. As they take a bite, their terrifying features soften, their dark fur turning the color of a summer sky, and they bound away, light as dandelion seeds.

Even the ruthless wasteland raiders stop their skirmishes to stand in a quiet, orderly line, holding out chipped porcelain teacups. When they eat your bread, they don't speak of conquest or survival. They cry quietly, their hardened faces relaxing as they suddenly remember the smell of their childhood homes and the feeling of warm rain on their skin.

For a beautiful, suspended moment, the cracked, bruised-purple sky heals itself, turning a brilliant, impossible blue. The rusted metal around your bakery blooms with glowing, bioluminescent ivy. You feel a profound, unshakeable peace standing behind your counter of scrap metal, knowing that you are feeding the end of the world with hope.

Then, the scent of the baked bread begins to shift, slowly morphing into the familiar smell of brewing coffee and fresh linen. A distant, rhythmic beeping replaces the howling wind. You take one last look at your glowing wasteland bakery, smile, and let the dream gently dissolve into the morning light.
