You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and symbols carved into its surface — spirals that seem to shift when you're not looking directly at them, and constellations you almost recognize but can't quite name.

As you run your fingers along the carvings, the box grows warm beneath your touch. The spirals begin to glow with a soft amber light, rearranging themselves into a shape that feels familiar — the outline of a door you once knew. Suddenly you remember: this is the door from your childhood home, the one at the end of the hallway that was always locked.

The box clicks softly, though you've turned no key. The lid lifts on its own, and instead of an interior, you find yourself looking *down* into a vast space — a staircase descending into warm golden light, impossibly contained within something you held in your hands.

You realize you are now standing at the top of those stairs, the box nowhere in sight, or perhaps you are *inside* it now. The air smells like rain on summer pavement and your grandmother's kitchen all at once. From below, you hear a sound — maybe music, maybe a voice calling your name, maybe both.

Each step down feels like remembering something you'd forgotten. By the third step, you recall a promise you made long ago. By the seventh, you can almost see who's waiting at the bottom...

And just as the figure at the base of the stairs turns toward you, their face beginning to come into focus — the dream shifts, the way dreams do, and you find yourself somewhere else entirely, holding something small and warm in your closed hand, certain that whatever was in the box is now with you, even if you can't remember what it was.
