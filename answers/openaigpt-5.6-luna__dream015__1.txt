You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to keep moving—not toward safety, because you no longer believe such a place exists, but toward a faint blue light flickering beyond the ruined city.

Along the way, you discover an abandoned greenhouse still humming with power. Inside, a single tree grows beneath shattered glass, its branches heavy with silver fruit. As you reach for one, you hear footsteps behind you.

A stranger stands in the doorway, carrying a lantern and a rusted rifle. Instead of raising the weapon, they hold out a small metal box.

Inside is a photograph of your home, untouched and bright beneath a summer sky.

“You’ve been here before,” the stranger says.

You look at the tree, then at the picture. Slowly, you realize the wasteland around you is not the future—it is a memory you have been trying to escape.

The blue light pulses in the distance. The tree begins to bloom.

And for the first time since the world ended, you wake up.
