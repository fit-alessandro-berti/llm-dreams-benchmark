I look not at the chaos, but at the sliver of moon breaking through the clouds just east of north. A tiny, defiant light. And I see it—not just a star, but the compass point Mama always told me to follow: *Where the light lies, the shore lies.*

"**All hands!**" My voice cuts through the din, hoarse but clear. "Listen! Sound the emergency bell! Marco, rig the storm sails – *full*! Finn, get below, check the forward bilge pump—**every second counts!**"

Mara lunges for the wheel, her face set. I grab the coiled rope beside me, not for the mast, but for the **wake-line**. A desperate gamble. I hurl it over the starboard bow, not to tie anything, but to *point*.

"**Mark that line!**" I roar. "The swell... that long heave before the crest... *that's* the wave track! Helm Hard-Over! Now!"

The ship groans, the world tilting violently as we turn broadside to the mountainous wave bearing down. Water explodes over the stern, drenching us. But we *turn*. The massive wave crashes past, the ship shuddering but holding.

"**She's climbing!**" Mara yells, her voice cracking with disbelief.

"**Yes!**" I gasp, gripping the rail. "Now, **hard a-starboard!** Full rudder! Let the wind catch her belly! She wants to dance!" I throw my weight into the wheel, feeling the ancient wood groan beneath my grip.

The ship responds, catching the wind and the surge, surfing the crest of the tempest. Below decks, the pump thumps steadily—*thump-thump-thump*. We ride the edge of the maelstrom, not fighting the sea, but *reading* it, dancing with the fury.

As the first true dawn bleeds pink and gold over the storm’s edge, the rain lessens to a drizzle. The wind drops to a sigh. Below, the crew hauls in frayed ropes, faces pale but alive, leaning on each other. The battered ship cuts a clean path through calm water, the weight of the storm behind us.

I stand at the weathered rail, the morning sun warming my face. The sea is quiet now, a vast, breathing expanse. I look down at my hands—calloused, salt-stained, trembling slightly. Not from fear. From *holding on*. The dream doesn't end here.
