You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to figure out not only where you are, but when you are.

The air smells of wood smoke and damp earth. Stone roads wind between crowded buildings, and people stare curiously at your unfamiliar clothes. Church bells ring in the distance, marking the passage of time in a world that has never heard of smartphones, airplanes, or electricity.

At first, panic sets in. You search your pockets for anything familiar, but your phone is dead and there is nowhere to charge it. The few items you carry seem almost magical compared to the tools around you, so you hide them carefully.

Determined to survive, you begin observing the people around you. You learn their customs, listen to their conversations, and slowly piece together clues. A merchant mentions a king whose name you vaguely remember from history class. A scholar speaks of a great war that has not happened yet. Gradually, you realize you are centuries in the past.

Days turn into weeks. You find work helping a local craftsman and earn enough food to get by. Although life is harder than anything you have known, you discover unexpected joys: evenings lit by candlelight, stories shared around fires, and a sky filled with stars untouched by city lights.

One night, an elderly traveler notices your unusual knowledge. He invites you to his cottage on a hill and shows you a strange brass instrument covered in symbols. "I have been waiting for someone like you," he says.

As you touch the device, the symbols begin to glow. Images of different eras swirl around you—ancient kingdoms, distant futures, and moments from your own life. The traveler explains that time is not a straight line but a vast landscape, and somehow you have wandered off the path.

"You may return home," he says, "or continue exploring."

You hesitate. The life you left behind suddenly feels distant, yet familiar. The world before you is full of mystery.

Just as you reach out to make your choice, the glowing symbols spin faster and faster. The room dissolves into light, the sound of bells echoes through the air, and—

You wake up in your own bed, unsure whether it was only a dream. But on your nightstand rests a small brass symbol you have never seen before, warm to the touch, as if it has traveled through time with you.
