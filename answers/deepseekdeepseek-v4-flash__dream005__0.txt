A woman with eyes like polished obsidian offers you one, but her mouth doesn't move when she speaks. "The time is never right," she whispers, and the cupcakes begin to melt into puddles of light that seep into the floor.

You move on, feeling the carpet turn to moss beneath your feet. Another cluster of people stands in a circle, laughing silently, their silhouettes flickering like candle flames. In the center, a man juggles glass orbs that contain miniature thunderstorms—each clap of lightning makes the room jump sideways for a split second. He nods at you, and one orb slips. It shatters, and your ears fill with the sound of rain, but no water falls.

Then you spot a familiar coat in the distance—a dull red you recognize. You push through a knot of guests who are all wearing the same expression, a serene mask with no eyes. They part reluctantly, and you see your friend standing beside a fountain that flows upward, spilling into a dark sky that has somehow replaced the ceiling. They turn, and their face is yours, but younger, smiling with teeth that are too white.

"Finally," they say, and the dream warps into a tunnel of forgotten birthdays and half-remembered songs. The gathering dissolves into static, and you realize you never needed to find them—you were the one everyone was searching for all along.
