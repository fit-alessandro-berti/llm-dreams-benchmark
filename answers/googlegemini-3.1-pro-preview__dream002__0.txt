But as I look up, the sky isn't turning the gray of a normal storm. It bruises into a deep, bioluminescent velvet-purple. The clouds roll in not like vapor, but like ocean waves crashing in slow motion upside down. 

The wind picks up, but instead of blowing the leaves off the trees, the wind pulls the gravity right out from under them. Thousands of green oak and pine leaves detach from their branches and float upward, spiraling toward the purple sky like a reverse snowfall. 

I brace myself for the rain, but when the first drops hit my skin, they aren't wet. Thousands of tiny, glowing silver beads begin to cascade from the clouds. When they strike the forest floor, they don't splash; they shatter into a chorus of musical chimes, turning the impending storm into a frantic, beautiful symphony. 

Thunder rolls beneath my feet rather than above my head. It rumbles with the distinct sound of a massive, ancient choir humming a single, vibrating chord. The sound resonates in my ribs, making me feel incredibly light. 

Suddenly, a massive fork of lightning fractures the sky, but it lingers instead of fading. The jagged strike separates, revealing itself to be the glowing, branched tentacles of a colossal, spectral jellyfish swimming through the atmospheric ocean above. 

I realize I am no longer afraid. The ground beneath me dissolves into a mirror reflecting the surreal sky. I take a step, and perfectly in tune with dream logic, I don't fall. Instead, I step upward, climbing the stairless air, walking higher and higher into the silver, chiming rain to meet the storm.
