...your fingers begin to drip with liquid starlight.

The moment you touch the canvas, it inhales. The blankness *breathes*, pulling color out of the air around you like a vacuum made of longing. Every shifting hue in the surreal landscape—impossible teals that taste like bells, fractals of burning rose that scream in frequencies only dreams can hear—rushes toward the surface and arranges itself into something that looks suspiciously like *you*, but older. Wiser. Slightly terrifying.

You try to step back, but your feet have become soft geometry. The ground is now warm taffy, and it's gently chewing on your ankles with affection.

The painted version of yourself on the canvas opens its eyes. They are black holes wearing tiny party hats. It smiles with too many teeth and speaks in your own voice, except slowed down and played backward:

*"Finally. I've been waiting since the big bang to finish myself."*

It reaches out of the canvas, but its hand keeps changing—sometimes a bird, sometimes a key, sometimes the exact hand you had when you were six and terrified of the dark. The hand grabs yours. The colors between you begin to negotiate. Your skin becomes stained-glass. Your thoughts become visible as floating equations that immediately start making out with each other.

Somewhere in the distance, a flock of Möbius strips flies by honking like geese.

The painted-you leans forward until your foreheads touch, and suddenly you're not sure which one of you is still dreaming. The canvas is now the size of the universe and the size of a postage stamp at the same time. Both feel correct.

It whispers, right against your soul:

*"Quick. Before we wake up. What color is the sound of your childhood laugh?"*

The world holds its breath, swirling, waiting for your answer so it can finish painting the rest of existence.
