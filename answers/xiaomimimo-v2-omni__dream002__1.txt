You quicken your pace as the sky darkens without warning. The gentle breeze becomes a gusting wind, bending the treetops and sending a chorus of rustling leaves through the air. The scent of rain—clean and electric—replaces the earthy smell of moss.

Ahead, you see an ancient, towering oak, its thick branches offering a promise of shelter. Just as you reach it, the first heavy drops begin to fall, splattering on the forest floor. You press your back against the rough bark as the rain escalates into a torrent, washing the world in a silver curtain. The thunder is not a crack, but a deep, resonant rumble that seems to vibrate through the very roots beneath your feet.

And yet, standing there, you feel strangely safe—the storm is a powerful, cleansing force, and the ancient tree is a steadfast guardian. You watch as the forest drinks deeply, each leaf shimmering. A part of you knows that when the clouds part, everything will be renewed, greener, and more alive. You are not afraid, only witnessing a necessary and beautiful transition.
