*frantically pat your pockets for your phone, but instead of a phone, you pull out a small brass key with no label. You hold it up, confused, and a passing stranger pauses, points at it, and says matter-of-factly, "That opens the blue door on Meridian Street—you know, across from the fountain that's always dry but shouldn't be."*

*You thank them, but when you turn around, the stranger is gone, replaced by a cat wearing a tiny scarf who seems to be waiting for you. You don't question this. In dreams, you rarely do.*

*You start walking, the brass key warm in your palm like a compass. The city shifts around you—streets that were straight suddenly curve, buildings rearrange themselves when you're not looking directly at them. A clock tower chimes, but instead of counting hours, it plays a melody you almost recognize from childhood.*

*You find a fountain. It is, indeed, dry. Across from it stands a door painted the exact blue of a summer sky just after rain. You try the key. It fits.*

*Inside, instead of a boardroom or an office, there's a sunlit garden with a long table. A dozen people are already seated, and they turn to look at you with gentle, patient smiles—as if they've been waiting but didn't mind waiting at all.*

*A woman at the head of the table gestures to the only empty chair. "We were just about to begin," she says. "You're not late. You're exactly on time."*

*You sit down, and relief washes over you like warm water. You still don't know what the meeting is about, but somehow, that feels like it doesn't matter yet.*

*The cat with the scarf hops onto the table, curls up in the center, and begins to purr.*

*And then you wake up—just enough to remember the brass key, but not what the meeting was about.*
