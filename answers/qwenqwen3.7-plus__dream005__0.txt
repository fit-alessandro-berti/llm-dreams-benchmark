...and the architecture of the room begins to breathe. The walls expand and contract like a slow, sleeping lung, pushing the crowded circles of strangers further apart just as you try to navigate through them.

One group is huddled around a high-top table, passionately debating the exact flavor of the color blue, passing around a perfectly empty martini glass that chimes like a bell whenever someone touches it. You try to slip past them, but a waiter in a crisp tuxedo blocks your path, offering a silver tray of glowing, geometric shapes. When you reach out to decline, the shapes dissolve into a flurry of iridescent blue moths that flutter up into the vaulted, starless ceiling.

You catch a glimpse of a familiar coat—the one you know so well—and your heart leaps. You weave through a quiet circle of people who are reading books made entirely of autumn leaves, the pages rustling with whispered secrets. But every time you get within arm's reach of that coat, the floor tilts gently, sliding you sideways into a different wing of the labyrinthine mansion.

The air grows thick, smelling faintly of ozone and old library books. The music playing in the background slows down until it sounds like a cello being played underwater. 

Finally, the crowd parts completely. You see them standing alone by a grand, arched window. But instead of looking out onto a city street or a garden, the window opens into a deep, bioluminescent ocean, with massive, slow-moving shadows gliding past the glass.

You rush forward, calling out their name, but the sound comes out as a rush of wind. They turn to face you, and for a moment, the world holds its breath. Their features are slightly blurred, like a watercolor painting caught in the rain, but their eyes are sharp and knowing. 

They don't speak, but you hear their voice clearly in your mind: *"You were looking in the wrong room."*

They reach out and hand you a small, heavy key made entirely of clear ice. It doesn't melt in your warm palm. Before you can ask what it opens, they smile, place a gentle hand on your chest, and push you backward. 

You don't hit the floor. Instead, you fall backward into a cool, weightless current, the sounds of the party fading into the soft, rhythmic rush of the ocean, as the dream begins to dissolve into the bright, hazy edge of morning.
