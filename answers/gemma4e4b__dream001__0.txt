**You start to** *walk*. Not with determination, but with a kind of panicked, aimless drift. The sheer scale of the place is overwhelming; the buildings don't follow any discernible architectural law. Some are crumbling monoliths draped in ivy that looks suspiciously like draped velvet; others are impossible towers of reflective material that stretch into the perpetual twilight sky, reflecting a thousand versions of an unknown sun.

The noise is a cacophony of unfamiliar sounds: melodic street hawkers selling things that don't exist, the rhythmic clang of metallic workers, and a constant, low hum that seems to emanate from the bedrock of the city itself.

You try the logical things. You stop under the dubious shade of a colonnade and pull out your phone, but the screen only flickers with static the color of deep amethyst. You ask people for directionsŚthe few who stop to help are always smiling too wide, their clothes fashioned from textiles that seem to shift colors slightly when you aren't looking directly at them. They point down streets that abruptly dead-end into walls of pure, glowing sand, or circle back to the point you started from, always with the same helpful, utterly useless smile.

Frustrated, you sit down on a carved stone bench overgrown with bioluminescent moss. You close your eyes, attempting to mentally map the city, to find a landmark, a repeating pattern.

Instead, you begin to pay attention to the *details*. You notice the way the sunlightŚif that's what it isŚfilters through the gaps in the buildings, creating geometric patterns on the dusty ground. Suddenly, the murmur of the crowd fades, and the overwhelming hum drops in frequency, becoming a single, deep, melodic note. Your focus snaps completely to a single structure across the street: a public clock, but it has no face. Instead, circular glass panels are embedded in its surface, and visible inside them are gears of impossible complexity, turning counter-clockwise, powered by some unseen, crystalline energy.

The final gear in the clock mechanism whirs and clicks, and the deep, resonant note ceases. You feel a strange pull, a gravitational tug, and you find yourself standing on a narrow cobblestone alleyway. The cacophony of the city is replaced by an absolute, beautiful silence. And there, waiting at the end of the alley, is the heavy, ornate door to the meeting.
