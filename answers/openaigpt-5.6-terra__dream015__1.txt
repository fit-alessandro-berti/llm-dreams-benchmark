…plant a garden in the cracked asphalt beneath a dead highway.

Each seed feels impossibly precious. You trade a working flashlight for a handful of soil, collect rainwater in rusted cans, and build a fence from old road signs to keep the hungry things away at night.

For weeks, nothing happens.

Then one morning, beneath a sky the color of ash, a single green shoot breaks through the black earth.

You kneel beside it, laughing and crying at once. Somewhere beyond the ruined city, something howls—but for the first time since the world ended, it does not sound like the only thing alive.
