...and as you weave through the crowd, the faces start to feel... off. Not wrong, exactly, but *too* familiar in the way dreams love to do. A woman in a red dress laughs exactly like your childhood piano teacher. A man in an expensive suit gestures with your father's hands. Everyone is sipping from the same glass.

You spot your one friend across the room—finally—but the moment you lock eyes, they smile in a way that isn't theirs. Their mouth moves, but the voice that comes out is yours. "There you are," they say with your own vocal cords. "I've been looking for me everywhere."

The crowd parts like they're expecting something. All eyes turn to you at once. You realize with a slow, sinking feeling that every single person here is wearing some version of your face, just slightly rearranged. The red-dress woman has your eyes. The suited man has your nervous smile. They're all you, but none of them are the *real* you.

Your friend—who is also somehow you—leans in close and whispers with your voice, "So which one of us is dreaming this time?"

The lights dim. Everyone raises their glass in unison.

And that's when you notice you're the only one not holding a drink.
