...symbols etched into its surface—spirals that seem to shift when you look away, and tiny constellations pressed into the dark wood. The box is warm, almost like it's breathing.

You run your fingers along the seams, and the patterns begin to glow faintly beneath your touch. There's no keyhole, you realize. The lock isn't a thing—it's a question.

A voice that isn't quite a voice, more like a feeling rising up from the box itself, asks: *\ dreams, you suddenly *know* the answer, though you couldn't say it aloud. It's something about a room you've never been in but somehow miss. A name spoken in a language you don't speak. The color of a sound.

You press your palm flat against the lid, and the box understands. The seams unfold—not opening so much as *blooming*, petals of wood and light peeling back to reveal...

...the same box, smaller, glowing brighter, asking the same question.

And you laugh, because of course. Of course it's boxes all the way down. Of course the key was never going to be a key.

You reach for the next one, smaller than a heartbeat now, and—

The dream shifts. You're somewhere else. You were always somewhere else. The box is still warm in your hand, or maybe that's just your hand, remembering the shape of holding something that mattered.

*(The dream doesn't end. It just stops being about the box.)*
