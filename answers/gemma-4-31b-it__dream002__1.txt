...the sky doesn’t turn grey; it turns a deep, bruising shade of violet. 

The wind arrives not as a gust, but as a melody—a low, humming vibration that I can feel in my teeth. As the first flash of lightning streaks across the horizon, it isn't white or yellow, but a brilliant, shimmering gold that freezes the world for a heartbeat. In that moment of stillness, I see that the leaves on the trees have turned into translucent shards of emerald glass.

Then, the rain begins. But it isn't water. Small, glowing spheres of liquid light drift down from the clouds, floating slowly like bubbles rather than falling. As they touch my skin, they don't feel wet; they feel like memories. One touches my hand, and I suddenly smell my grandmother’s kitchen from twenty years ago; another touches my shoulder, and I hear a laugh from a friend I haven't spoken to in a decade.

Panicked yet mesmerized, I begin to run, but the forest floor has softened into something like velvet moss that swallows my footsteps. The faster I run, the more the trees begin to shift, their trunks twisting into elegant, spiraling staircases that lead upward into the swirling violet clouds.

I stop at the base of the tallest spiral and look back. The path I came from has vanished, replaced by a mirror-still lake that reflects a sky filled with three moons. I realize then that I am not afraid of the storm; I am the storm. 

As I take the first step up the wooden spiral, my feet leave the ground entirely. I begin to float, weightless, drifting upward toward the golden lightning, and just as I reach out to touch the electricity, the sound of a distant alarm clock begins to bleed into the humming wind, pulling me slowly, reluctantly, back to the waking world.
