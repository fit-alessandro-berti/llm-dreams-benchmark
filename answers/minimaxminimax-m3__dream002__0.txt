You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the sky above you, which was just moments ago a soft blue, now swirls with deep purples and electric greens. The leaves overhead begin to whisper, then roar, as a wind unlike any you've ever felt sweeps through the trees. It doesn't push you—it *pulls* you, like the forest itself is breathing you in.

The rain arrives not as drops but as silver threads, falling upward into the clouds. Your feet lift gently from the mossy ground, and suddenly you realize you're floating, drifting between the trunks of ancient trees whose bark shimmers with veins of gold.

In the distance, you see a light. Not lightning—something warmer. A lantern, hanging in midair, swaying as if held by an invisible hand. As you drift closer, you notice the storm isn't frightening at all. It feels *alive*, and it's humming a low, resonant tune that vibrates in your chest.

The lantern grows brighter, and from its glow, figures begin to emerge—shapes made of rain and wind, dancing between the trees in a slow, graceful waltz. One of them turns toward you. It has no face, but you feel it smiling. It extends a hand woven from mist.

You reach out, and—

You wake up, the taste of petrichor on your tongue, the sound of that humming still echoing softly in your mind, fading like the last note of a song you almost remember.
