...written in a language you've never seen before. The characters shimmer and shift on the page like heat rising off pavement. You blink, and now they're symbols — spirals, fractals, what look like constellations rearranging themselves into something almost readable.

You look around the room. Everyone else is writing furiously, calm and confident. The girl to your left is filling in answers with a pencil that writes in watercolor. The guy ahead of you is *whispering* his answers into the exam paper, and the paper absorbs his words like a sponge.

You look back at your own exam. The first question is now in English:

**"Question 1: Describe the color of the wind on the day you were born. Show your work."**

You realize you *know* this. You pick up your pen and begin writing — not words, exactly, but something close. The ink flows upward off the page in thin ribbons of smoke. You feel certain you're doing it right.

Then the professor walks past. You look up — it's you. Older, or maybe younger. You can't tell. They lean down and whisper:

*"Don't worry. You already passed. You just haven't realized it yet."*

The exam paper dissolves in your hands. The room dissolves. You're standing in a field you half-remember from childhood, and the wind has color — yours — and it's spreading across the sky like ink in water.
