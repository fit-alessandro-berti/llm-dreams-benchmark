You start to panic, your heart pounding as the unfamiliar skyline towers over you like jagged teeth. The streets are a chaotic blur of languages you half-understand, neon signs flickering in colors that don't exist when you're awake. Your phone is in your hand but the screen keeps glitching—every map app shows the city as an ever-shifting maze, streets rearranging themselves like a living thing.

You begin running, shoes slapping against wet cobblestones that weren't there a second ago. Time is slipping; you can feel the hourglass in your chest emptying. People brush past you, their faces blurred like half-remembered memories. One of them—a woman in a red coat—pauses just long enough to whisper, "You're late. You've always been late," before dissolving into a flock of white birds that explode upward into a sky that's suddenly twilight.

Then you see it: a building that feels *right*, an old clock tower fused with a sleek glass skyscraper, both existing in the same impossible space. The doors are massive, carved with symbols that make your eyes water. As you reach for the handle, your hand passes straight through it like smoke.

Inside, the meeting is already happening.

Everyone you’ve ever known is sitting around an endless table—your childhood self, your future self, your mother, that barista who always gets your name wrong, the version of you that never left your hometown. They're all staring at an empty chair with your name on it.

The clock above them has no hands.

Your boss (who is also somehow God, also somehow your first-grade teacher) looks up and smiles with too many teeth.

"You're here," they say. "We've been waiting for the you that finally shows up."

The chair starts to pull you toward it like gravity.

You wake up.

Or... do you?
