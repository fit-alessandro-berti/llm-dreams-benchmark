The patterns rearrange themselves. A keyhole appears where there wasn't one before, shaped not for a key but for something else—your thumb, perhaps, or the tip of your tongue. You press your thumb to it. The box *breathes*, a soft exhalation of dust and something sweeter underneath: old paper, dried lavender, your grandmother's kitchen.

Inside, there's no object. Only a folded square of light. You unfold it carefully, and it expands into a doorway standing upright in the palm of your hand. Through it, you see a version of yourself sleeping in a bed you recognize, dreaming of a box.

The you-in-the-doorway stirs. Their eyes open. They look *out* at you.

The box snaps shut. The patterns lock back into stillness.

You're holding a wooden spoon. The kitchen smells of onions browning in butter. Someone calls your name from another room.
