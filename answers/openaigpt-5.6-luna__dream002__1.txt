You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching, and the gentle breeze turns into a fierce wind that sends leaves spiraling through the air. Thunder rolls across the sky, though the clouds above are strangely silver and glowing.

You hurry along a narrow path until you find a small wooden door standing by itself between two ancient trees. With nowhere else to go, you open it—and step into a warm, candlelit room beneath the forest floor.

Inside, everything is calm. Rain taps softly against an invisible roof, and a fox sits beside a crackling fire, wearing a tiny blue scarf. It looks at you knowingly and points toward a window.

Through the glass, the storm has transformed the forest. Lightning illuminates thousands of floating lanterns drifting between the trees. One lantern comes to rest in your hands. Inside it is a small golden key.

The fox leads you to another door at the far end of the room. When you place the key in its lock, the door opens onto a bright morning meadow. The storm is gone, and the sun is rising.

As you step outside, you realize the forest has been quietly guiding you all along. In the distance, someone calls your name—but when you turn around, the door, the fox, and the storm have vanished. Only the golden key remains, warm in your palm.
