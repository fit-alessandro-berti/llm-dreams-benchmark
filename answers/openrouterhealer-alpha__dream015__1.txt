The ground beneath your feet is cracked earth, the color of rust and forgotten things. The sky hangs low — not with rain, but with a haze that tastes of iron and old fires. Somewhere, a radio crackles with a signal that might be music, might be a voice, might be static playing tricks on a lonely mind.

You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to **keep moving forward.**

Not because you're brave. Not because you have a plan. But because standing still feels like agreeing with the ruins around you, and something in your chest — stubborn, irrational, still beating — refuses that.

You adjust the strap of your pack, which holds:

- A dented thermos, half-full of water that tastes like metal
- A hand-drawn map someone left behind, edges burned, destinations unclear
- A photograph of a place you don't recognize — but the light in it feels like home

The road ahead forks. To the left, a collapsed overpass shelters what might be an abandoned supply depot. To the right, smoke rises in thin columns — either survivors or a trap. Straight ahead, the highway disappears into a forest that has swallowed civilization whole, branches knitting together like clasped hands.

Your stomach tightens. Your hands are shaking slightly.

But you take a breath. Then another.

And you choose.

---

*Where does the dream take you? Left toward the depot, right toward the smoke, or straight into the wild? Or does your path go somewhere else entirely?*
