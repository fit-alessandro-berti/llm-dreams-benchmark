...you feel a warmth spread through your chest as they gather around you. One of them, an old woman with silver hair and eyes like lanterns, takes your hand—and somehow you know her, though you've never met.

"You've been walking in circles," she says gently. "The streets here rearrange themselves when you're afraid."

As you walk with them, the city begins to soften. The towering buildings lean in like curious friends rather than looming strangers. A man in a patchwork coat hums a melody you remember from childhood—a song your grandmother used to sing, though the words come out in a language made of colors instead of sounds.

The group leads you down an alley that shouldn't exist, and it opens into a garden growing impossibly between skyscrapers. Paper lanterns float upward without strings. One of the strangers, a child with paint-stained fingers, hands you a small brass key.

"This is what you were looking for," she says. "You just forgot you were carrying the map the whole time."

You look down and realize your own palms are etched with streets—the city was inside you all along.

When you look up to thank them, the strangers are dissolving into golden light, but you don't feel abandoned. You feel *accompanied*, somehow, by everyone you've ever known. The old woman's voice lingers in the air:

"You were never lost. You were just learning the way."

The lanterns rise. The garden glows. And as the dream begins to thin like morning fog, you wake with the strange, certain feeling that the key is still in your hand—and that somewhere, the kindness was real.
