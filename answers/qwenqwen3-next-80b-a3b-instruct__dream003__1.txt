A whisper curls around you—not from outside, but from within the box itself.

*“To open me, you must first remember what you’ve forgotten.”*

You frown. What do you forget? Your childhood? A name? A promise?

Then, without warning, the box dissolves into a thousand glowing motes—each one a memory you never knew you lost.

You see yourself as a child, laughing under a sky of violet stars, handing a wooden bird to someone whose face you can’t recall. You hear your mother singing a lullaby in a language you haven’t spoken since before you could speak at all.

One mote lingers—warm, heavy, pulsed with love and sorrow—and hovers before your eyes.

You reach out… and the box reforms in your hands. But now, the lock is gone.

The lid lifts on its own.

Inside rests a single small mirror.

And when you look into it, you don’t see your face.

You see *them*.

The one you forgot.

The one who loved you enough to leave.

The one who locked the box so you’d find your way back… to yourself.

A tear falls. It doesn’t hit the floor.

It turns into a key.

And as you lift it, the room around you begins to unravel—not into darkness, but into dawn.

The dream is over.

But the key? The key is still in your hand.

And you realize—

You were never meant to open the box.

You were meant to become it.
