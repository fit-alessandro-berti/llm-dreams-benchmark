As you approach the canvas, the air around it hums softly—a low, melodic vibration that feels like anticipation. The surface is pristine, an alabaster white that seems to drink in the shifting hues of the landscape, yet somehow remains untouched.

You lift your hand, and without a brush, color begins to flow from your fingertips—a deep, shimmering indigo that pools like liquid twilight. It spreads, not flat, but rising into a gentle hill of velvet texture. The indigo deepens at its edges, bleeding into a soft, luminous peach, as if capturing the memory of a sunrise that never happened.

Then, from the hill, a shape emerges—not drawn, but *grown*. A tree of crystalline glass unfurls its branches, each one catching and refracting the ever-changing light of the dream-world, splintering it into a thousand tiny, dancing rainbows. Beneath your touch, the ground on the canvas becomes a sea of liquid silver, rippling with every imagined breeze.

You realize the canvas is not a recording, but a *window*—or perhaps, a mirror. It shows not what is, but what *could be*, drawn from the dreamscape and your own quiet wonder. The tree sways, the silver sea whispers, and a single, phosphorescent bird takes flight from a branch, circling once before dissolving into a shower of sparks that become new stars in the canvas’s sky.

What do you wish to dream into being next?
