the air grows thick with the scent of ozone and damp earth. The gentle rustle of leaves is replaced by a low, vibrating hum that seems to emanate from the ground itself. Above you, the canopy twists unnaturally, branches interlocking like fingers to block out the sky, turning the daylight into a bruised purple twilight.

You try to run, but your feet feel heavy, as if the forest floor has turned to soft clay. Suddenly, the rain begins—not as drops, but as shimmering threads of silver light that hang suspended in the air before dissolving into mist upon touching your skin. Wherever the mist touches, memories bloom: fleeting images of places you’ve never been and people you’ve never met flash before your eyes, vivid and emotional.

In the center of the clearing ahead, an ancient oak tree stands split down the middle. Inside its hollow core, instead of wood, there is a swirling vortex of stars, mirroring the night sky above despite the storm. A voice, calm and familiar yet impossible to place, whispers from within the vortex: *"You are not lost; you are arriving."*

As you step toward the tree, the storm breaks, not into sunshine, but into a profound silence. The world around you fades into white, and you wake up with the lingering sensation of starlight on your fingertips.
