You are about to take the final exam for a course that determines your future career. As you sit down to start the exam, you notice the questions are written in glowing, shifting script that changes every time you blink—like the ink is alive, whispering secrets only you can half-hear.

The first question reads: "If a cloud dreams of being a fish, how many raindrops does it need to forget the sky?" You stare at it, pencil hovering, but your hand starts to dissolve into mist, turning the paper into a swirling map of forgotten constellations.

Panic rises as the proctor—a towering figure with your childhood pet's eyes—leans in and murmurs, "Time's an illusion, but your answer isn't." You scribble wildly: "Infinity, minus one stolen sunset." The room tilts, desks floating upward like helium balloons, and suddenly you're not taking the exam—you *are* the exam, questions blooming from your skin like tattoos that rewrite your memories.

You wake up gasping, the alarm blaring, but for a split second, you swear you aced it. Or did you?
