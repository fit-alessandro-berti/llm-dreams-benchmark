Every corner you turn looks almost familiar—like a city you've visited before, or maybe several cities stitched together. There's a cathedral from Prague next to a diner you swear is from your hometown.

You reach into your pocket for your phone, and it's there, but the screen won't cooperate. The numbers keep sliding around when you try to dial, and the maps app shows only a spiraling street that doesn't exist. You ask a passerby for directions, and they answer warmly, at length, gesturing with great confidence—but the moment they finish speaking, you realize you can't remember a single word they said.

The clock on a tower reads 4:15. Then you look again and it reads 11:50. You don't know which is true, but the anxiety in your chest insists the meeting is *soon*, that it's enormously important, though strangely you can't recall what it's about or who will be there. Only that you must not be late.

You begin to run. The sidewalk turns soft, like running through deep sand, and your legs feel heavy and slow no matter how hard you push. A bus glides past—you're certain it's *your* bus—and you chase it, but it's always exactly half a block ahead.

Then a door appears. Just a door, standing in the middle of the plaza, and somehow you know this is it. You're late, you must be terribly late. You push it open—

—and you're in a conference room, except it's also your childhood classroom, and everyone is already seated, turning to look at you. You glance down and realize you're not entirely sure you're wearing shoes. Someone at the head of the table—their face keeps changing, your old boss, your third-grade teacher, someone you can't place—smiles and says, "We've been waiting for you. Go ahead and begin."

And you open your mouth, having no idea what you're supposed to present, when somewhere far away, a sound begins—soft, insistent, pulling at the edges of the room. The walls grow thin and papery. The faces blur.

Your alarm is ringing. The meeting, the city, the door—already dissolving, leaving only that strange residue of urgency, and the lingering feeling that you almost, *almost* found out where you were supposed to be.
