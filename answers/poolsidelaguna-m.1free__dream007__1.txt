
You are in the middle of a heated argument with a close friend. The disagreement is about something important to both of you, and you decide to *take a walk to cool down*. As you step outside, the world around you begins to shift, though you don’t notice at first—the trees stretch unnaturally tall, their leaves shimmering like stained glass, and the sky pulses with colors that don’t exist in daylight. Your friend follows, still fuming, but their voice softens when they notice the surreal landscape.  

You both stop at a clearing where a mirror-like lake reflects not your faces, but scenes from your friendship: moments of laughter, old wounds, unspoken fears. Your friend points to the water and says, *“Look. We’re arguing about whether we’re growing apart, but maybe we’re just… changing shape.”* The surface ripples, showing you as children building a fort from couch cushions—a memory neither of you has mentioned in years. You laugh, the tension cracking, and realize the dream (or is it?) was never about the topic of the argument, but about remembering why your bond matters more than being right.  

You wake up with the resolution still humming in your chest, and a text from your friend waiting on your phone: *“Can we talk? I think I figured it out.”*
