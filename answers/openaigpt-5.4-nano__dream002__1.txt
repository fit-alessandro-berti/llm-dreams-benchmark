The light dims in a hurry, as if someone has turned the sun down one notch at a time. Leaves stop their gentle flutter and hover, caught between breaths. The air thickens with the smell of wet stone and something metallic—like coins warmed in your palm. Your footsteps sound louder than they should, each one landing on the ground with a soft, final certainty.

Ahead, the path bends toward a darker stand of pines. Wind threads through them, tugging at the branches in quick, restless motions. But the storm isn’t coming the usual way. It doesn’t sweep in as a wall of rain. Instead, the sky seems to peel back, opening into a layered darkness—one cloud stacked over another, each one moving at its own speed.

You try to step back to where the forest felt safe, yet the trail subtly rearranges itself. The trees are the same, but the distance between them keeps changing, like the forest is deciding where you are allowed to go.

Then the storm arrives—not with a crash, but with a sudden silence. The thunder pauses mid-roll. The wind holds its breath. Even the birds seem to forget how to sing.

In that held moment, a thin ribbon of lightning flickers in the distance, not branching outward like usual, but drawing a line straight toward you. It traces the shape of an invisible doorway between two trees. The branches around the gap bend inward as if bowing. Rain begins to fall only inside that line, a curtain so precise it looks stitched into the air.

You feel compelled to step toward it. Not because you’re brave—because you’re already halfway there in your mind, as if you’ve dreamed this choice before.

When you cross the invisible doorway, the temperature changes instantly. The storm’s roar becomes muffled, like you’ve walked into a different room. Water beads on your skin, but it doesn’t soak you; it slides off as if your body has become smooth glass. Around you, the forest keeps moving—branches swaying, leaves trembling—but within the doorway everything is still.

A sound comes from somewhere close: the soft drip of rain, counted like a metronome. With each drip, a faint pattern appears on the air, glowing and fading—symbols or letters you almost understand.

You look down and see your own footprints, but they’re not in mud. They’re in light. Each step leaves a brief trail of luminous footprints that curl like handwriting before disappearing. The forest is mapping your movement as it’s happening, translating you into something you can read.

The symbols gather into a single sentence made of bright points, and you realize—without knowing how—that the sentence is asking you a question:

**What are you trying to outrun?**

The rain within the doorway intensifies, then stops abruptly, as if the storm is waiting for your answer. Your heart beats too loudly. The wind returns in one sudden gust, and the trees sway hard enough to nearly blur, as though they’re trying to erase the doorway behind you.

You can feel two paths forming at once: one that leads deeper into the storm’s quiet, shining interior, and one that opens back into the regular, familiar forest—safe, ordinary, forgettable.

So you answer, out loud or in thought, choosing the truth that hurts the most.

And the doorway reacts—turning the sentence into a key of light, fitting itself around your chest like a lock finding its partner.

The moment you accept it, the rain releases. The storm pours out of the doorway and washes through the forest—not destructive, but cleansing. The ground glitters where it hits. The air clears. The clouds above split, and a thin, pale dawn begins to form at the horizon like someone gently lifting a curtain.

Then you notice something impossible: the trees you passed earlier are leaning in your direction, not from wind, but as if listening.

Somewhere ahead, at the end of the path you thought you lost, a figure made of mist stands waiting. It doesn’t look like a monster or a guide. It looks like you—only steadier, older by a few dreams’ worth, holding something small and warm in its hands.

It raises its head as the last of the storm’s light fades, and it asks you the next question in the same glowing language of dripping symbols:

**Now that you’ve found what you were running from… are you ready to meet what you’ve been avoiding?**
