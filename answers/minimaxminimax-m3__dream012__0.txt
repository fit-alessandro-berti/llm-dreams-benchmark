The wind howls like a living thing, tearing at the sails with claws of salt and fury. The ship groans beneath you—a great wooden beast wrestling against waves that rise like dark mountains. Lightning splits the sky, and for one impossible moment, the clouds form the shape of a face watching you.

Your first mate, Mara, grips the wheel beside you, her knuckles bone-white. "Captain! The hull's taking water in the starboard hold!"

The crew scrambles in the half-light, their faces pale and streaked with rain. Young Tomás has frozen near the mast, eyes wide and staring at something in the water only he can see. Old Brennan is shouting prayers to a god whose name has been forgotten.

You step forward, boots sliding on the soaked deck, and your voice cuts through the chaos—not with shouting, but with the strange calm that only comes in dreams. The storm *listens*. It always does.

"Secure the rigging!" you command. "Mara, bring us about to face the swell—let the bow cut through, not the broadside. Tomás—"

The boy doesn't respond. He's pointing now, trembling, at the horizon where a light burns that shouldn't be there. A lighthouse, tall and impossibly pale, growing out of the waves themselves. It pulses in rhythm with your heartbeat.

"Captain," Mara whispers. "That... that wasn't on any chart."

A voice rises from the water—not the wind, not the sea, but something older. It speaks your true name, the one you had before you became captain, before you became *you*. It asks if you're ready to remember.

The ship tilts. The crew cries out. The light grows closer.

And you realize, with the peculiar clarity of dreams, that the storm was never trying to drown you.

It was trying to *wake* you up.
