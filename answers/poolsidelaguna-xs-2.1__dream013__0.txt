Solving one puzzle causes the floor to give way, revealing a mirror. In it, you see not your reflection, but a younger version of yourself, eyes wide with the same determination you now feel. The mirror whispers, *“Trust the path you’ve already walked.”*  

Deeper still, you encounter a paradox: a door marked *“Exit,”* but beyond it lies only the same maze, unchanged. You realize the solution isn’t to escape, but to retrace your steps, carrying the knowledge that every wrong turn was a lesson, every wrong key a part of the whole.  

At the maze’s heart, a single candle flickers—its flame spelling out your name in smoke. You understand: the labyrinth was never about leaving, but about arriving at the truth you sought to find.  

You wake—not to a room, but to the memory of having always been here, in the space between thought and dream, where puzzles dissolve into light.
