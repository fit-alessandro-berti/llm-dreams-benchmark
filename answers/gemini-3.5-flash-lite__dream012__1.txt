I look down at my hands on the wheel. They aren't mine. They are older, weathered, glowing with the same pale light. 

The storm doesn't abate, but the fear drains out of me, replaced by an absolute, terrifying calm. I realize then that this isn't water we're sailing through, and this isn't the ocean at all.
