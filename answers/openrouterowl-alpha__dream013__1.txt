You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, you notice the walls are shifting subtly, rearranging themselves in patterns that seem almost alive. The air hums with a low, resonant frequency—like the maze itself is breathing.

At the first junction, you find a stone pedestal with three levers, each inscribed with a symbol: a spiral, a triangle, and a circle. Etched into the floor is a riddle:  
*"I speak without a mouth and hear without ears. I have no body, but I come alive with the wind. What am I?"*

You pause, focusing. The answer echoes in your mind—*an echo*. You pull the lever marked with the spiral, the symbol of cycles and repetition. A hidden door grinds open.

Deeper in, the maze narrows. Glowing runes pulse along the corridor, forming a sequence: 2, 4, 8, 16, __. You recognize the pattern—each number doubles. You whisper “32,” and the runes flare bright, revealing a bridge of light over a chasm below.

Finally, you reach the heart of the maze: a vast chamber with a mirror at its center. But instead of your reflection, you see… yourself, smiling knowingly. It steps forward and says, “You’ve solved every puzzle—but the real question is: who built this maze?”

Before you can answer, the mirror shatters, and from the shards rises a key made of starlight. You grasp it—and wake up, the dream dissolving like mist, but the key’s warmth still lingers in your palm.
