As they guide you forward, the chaotic roar of the city begins to warp. The blaring car horns stretch and soften into the resonant, melodic humming of cellos. The hard concrete beneath your shoes turns spongy and green, sprouting thick, velvet moss that silences your footsteps. You look up and watch as the towering glass skyscrapers bend gently toward one another, their windows blooming outward like giant, translucent lotus flowers catching the twilight.

You try to thank your guides, but when you look at their faces, they are beautifully impossible. Their features shift like reflections in a rippling pond—one moment they look like forgotten childhood friends, the next like ancient marble statues, and then like silhouettes filled with swirling, starry nebulas. They don’t speak; instead, their directions are felt as a gentle, magnetic pull right in the center of your chest. 

They lead you away from the shifting streets and into a quiet, cobblestone plaza that smells faintly of petrichor and old paper. In the very center of the plaza stands a single, freestanding wooden door. It is painted a peeling, familiar yellow—the exact shade of your childhood front door.

The strangers stop. They let go of your hands and step back, bowing their heads in unison. As they do, their bodies dissolve into a cloud of glowing, pale-blue moths that flutter upward, scattering into the indigo sky like reverse snowfall. 

You are alone again, but the crushing loneliness is gone, replaced by a profound, vibrating sense of peace. You reach out and turn the brass knob. 

The door opens not to another street, but into a vast, impossibly cozy room. A fire is crackling in a stone hearth, casting a golden glow over walls lined with books that seem to breathe softly in and out. Sitting in a plush armchair by the fire is a younger version of yourself. They look up from a book, offering a bright, knowing smile that says, *You finally made it.*

You walk over and sit in the empty chair opposite them. As you sink into the cushions, the warmth of the fire seeps deep into your bones, making your limbs feel heavy and perfectly relaxed. The edges of the room begin to blur, dissolving into a soft, pearlescent morning light. You close your eyes within the dream, only to open them in the waking world, your heart steady, and your hands still tingling with the faint, lingering warmth of woven sunlight.
