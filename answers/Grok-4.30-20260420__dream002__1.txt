You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the air thickens with electricity, the gentle rustle of leaves twisting into a low, mournful groan. The sky, once a soft canopy of green-filtered light, churns into bruised violets and angry blacks. Wind lashes at your face like invisible claws, carrying the scent of wet earth and something older—ozone laced with forgotten incense.

Lightning forks overhead, not the usual white flash but veins of liquid gold that briefly illuminate the trees as if they're breathing. Rain begins to fall, but it's no ordinary rain: each drop lands with a soft chime, like tiny bells, and where it touches the ground, glowing mushrooms sprout in spirals, pulsing in time with your heartbeat. The path ahead dissolves into mist, yet you press forward, drawn by a pull you can't name.

Deeper in, the forest transforms. Ancient trunks twist into impossible shapes—faces half-formed in the bark, whispering secrets in languages you almost understand. One massive oak reaches out a branch like a hand, and as your fingers brush it, the storm peaks: thunder roars your name, and the world tilts. You're lifted, weightless, spinning through the clouds on a current of pure dream-stuff. Below, the forest becomes a living map of your memories—childhood trails, lost loves, unspoken fears—all flashing in the lightning's glare.

Then, in the eye of the storm, calm descends. A single shaft of sunlight pierces through, revealing a clearing where a figure waits: yourself, but older, wiser, holding a lantern made of starlight. "The storm was never the enemy," it says with your voice. "It was the key." You reach for the lantern, and as your hands meet, the entire dream shatters into a million glittering shards.

You wake in your bed, heart still racing, the faint chime of phantom rain echoing in your ears... and a single glowing mushroom resting on your nightstand, already fading with the dawn.
