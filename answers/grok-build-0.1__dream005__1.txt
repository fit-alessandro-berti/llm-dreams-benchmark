You are at a large social gathering where you only know one person. As you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people and they all seem to be speaking in a language that shifts mid-sentence—words melting from English into something that sounds like wind chimes and static. 

One cluster is huddled around a floating table covered in unlit candles that somehow cast shadows of people who aren't there. They're debating the best way to fold time into origami, passing around a sheet of paper that keeps unfolding into a map of the room you're already standing in. You ask if they've seen your friend, but they only smile and hand you a candle that feels warm even though it has no flame. 

Another group is playing a game that looks like musical chairs, except the chairs are people and the music is the sound of your own heartbeat amplified through the ceiling. Every time the rhythm stops, someone vanishes and reappears as someone else you vaguely recognize from a dream you had years ago. You push through them, calling your friend's name, but your voice comes out as someone else's—deeper, older, like it's been borrowed. 

The crowd thickens. A woman with no eyes but perfect vision asks you why you're looking for someone when the person is right beside you. You turn, and for a second it *is* them, but their face is made of the same shifting wallpaper as the walls, and they dissolve into laughter that echoes from every direction at once. 

You keep moving, the room stretching like taffy. Doors appear that lead to the same party from slightly different angles—same people, same conversations, but now everyone is wearing your clothes. The air smells like rain on hot pavement and forgotten birthdays. Your friend is always just ahead, just out of reach, turning corners that shouldn't exist. 

Eventually you reach a balcony that overlooks the entire gathering from above. Down there, everyone has stopped and is looking up at you. They all have your friend's face. One of them waves. 

And then the floor tilts, gently, like the whole dream is deciding it's time to end. You feel yourself sliding toward wakefulness, the sound of the party fading into the ordinary hum of your bedroom ceiling fan.
