You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the air turns electric, thick with the scent of wet earth and ozone.  

The birdsong cuts off all at once, like someone pressed mute on the world. Leaves overhead flip to their pale undersides and start whispering in a wind that wasn't there seconds ago. The sunlight thins, then vanishes behind clouds the color of bruised slate.  

You feel the first cold drop hit the back of your neck, and it runs like a finger down your spine. Then the sky opens. Rain doesn't fall so much as arrive, a silver curtain sweeping through the trees. Thunder rolls through your chest before you hear it with your ears.  

But you don't run. Something about the storm feels familiar, like it was waiting for you. The path ahead glows faintly where the rain hits it, each puddle lighting up with soft gold light, leading deeper into the woods. Lightning flashes and for a split second you swear the trees are leaning in to watch where you go next.  

Your breath catches, half from the chill, half from the strange pull in your gut that says: keep walking. The storm isn't here to chase you away. It's here to show you something.  

What do you do when the glowing path forks, one way toward the roar of water, the other toward a silence so deep the rain can't touch it?
