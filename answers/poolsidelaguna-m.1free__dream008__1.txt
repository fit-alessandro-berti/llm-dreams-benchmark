suddenly remember you left your laptop charger at home, but when you check your bag, it’s there—except it’s plugged into a wall outlet that’s floating in midair, humming with electricity. Your boss morphs into a giant duck wearing a monocle and starts quacking in Morse code. The deadline? It’s written in hieroglyphs on a sticky note that keeps changing languages. You realize the project is to build a time machine out of office supplies, but the only materials available are paperclips, existential dread, and a stapler that screams when you touch it.  

As you work, the walls melt into a sunset beach, and your coworkers transform into penguins wearing tiny business suits. They nod approvingly at your progress, even though you’re clearly just arranging paperclips into a frowny face. The duck boss honks once, triumphantly, and hands you a trophy made of recycled coffee cups. You wake up just as the stapler starts singing *Bohemian Rhapsody*.  

The end. Or was it a dream? Your laptop charger is still missing.
