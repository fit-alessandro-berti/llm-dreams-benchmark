…as you move deeper into the maze, you realize the walls themselves are thinking.  

They shift not randomly, but in response to the *pattern* of your thoughts. The corridors rearrange based on the logical structure of the puzzles you’ve just solved — each step you take encodes a theorem into the architecture.  

You reach a chamber with three doors, each bearing a statement:  

- **Door A:** “The correct path is not here.”  
- **Door B:** “Door A lies.”  
- **Door C:** “If Door B is true, then I am the exit.”  

You must solve this not with deduction alone, but by *aligning* your mind with the maze’s logic. The walls hum as you consider:  

If Door A is true, then the exit isn’t A — but then B says A lies, which would make B true, and C’s conditional holds, creating a loop.  
If Door B is true, then A lies, so the exit *is* A — contradiction, because then B’s claim would make A’s statement false, but if exit is A, B is true… wait.  

You pause, feeling the maze’s rhythm. You realize the third door’s statement is a *self-referential trap* — it only matters if B is true.  

So you test: Assume B is false. Then A is true (exit not A), so exit must be B or C. But if B is false, its statement “A lies” is false, so A tells truth — consistent. Then C’s statement “If B is true, then I am exit” is vacuously true (since B false), so C could be exit.  

But the maze rewards *elegant* solutions. You notice the walls subtly mirror your breathing — the answer must be the one that *stabilizes* the system.  

You choose **Door B** — not because it’s true, but because selecting it forces the maze to resolve the paradox. As you pass, the walls dissolve into light, revealing the next puzzle: a corridor of mirrors showing not your reflection, but *possible choices* you haven’t made yet.  

The dream whispers: *Every correct step isn’t about being right — it’s about making the labyrinth believe in you.*
