Towering timber-framed houses lean over the narrow lane, and the muffled sound of lute music drifts from around the corner. No cars, no sirens, no hum of electricity. Just the clip-clop of a horse's hooves and the chatter of people in heavy woolen cloaks and tunics.

Panic whispers at the edge of my mind, but the dreamlike logic keeps me strangely calm. *Adapt,* I tell myself. 

I slip into the shadows of an alleyway, eyeing a burlap laundry sack hanging beside a wooden wash-tub. If I'm going to survive whatever century this is—or figure out how I got here—I need to blend in, fast.
