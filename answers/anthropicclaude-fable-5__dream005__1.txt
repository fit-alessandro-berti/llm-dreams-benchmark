...each group seems to know *you*, somehow. They turn as you pass, smiling with too many teeth, saying your name in voices that almost sound familiar — like recordings of people you loved, played at slightly the wrong speed.

"You're looking for someone," a woman says. She's wearing your mother's coat, but she isn't your mother. "They were just here. They went toward the kitchen."

But the kitchen keeps moving. You walk through doorways that open into the same room you just left, except now the party is at a different stage — earlier, when guests were still arriving, or later, when only crumbs and half-empty glasses remain on long tables. The music never changes. You can't quite name the song, but you've heard it before, maybe at a wedding, maybe in childhood.

A man hands you a drink. The glass is warm, like someone was holding it for hours waiting for you. "It's good you came," he says. "We weren't sure you would. Not after last time."

You don't remember a last time. You want to ask, but he's already turned away, dissolved into a circle of laughter.

Then — across the room — you see them. The one person you know. They're standing by a window that shouldn't exist, because outside it is daylight, although you arrived at night. They're looking right at you. Relief floods through you like warm water.

You push through the crowd, which thickens like syrup, everyone suddenly slow, everyone in your way. The harder you push, the farther the window seems. You call their name, but what comes out of your mouth is a different name — one you've never heard, but which feels truer than any name you've ever spoken.

And the person turns. And smiles. And you realize, the moment before they speak, that you can't actually remember their face — you never could — and that the face they're about to show you is the answer to a question you've been afraid to ask for years.

They open their mouth.

The music stops.

And someone, very gently, taps you on the shoulder from behind and says: *"There you are. We've all been waiting. It's time for you to give the toast."*

You turn around. Everyone is holding up their glasses. Everyone is silent.

And you realize you have always been the person you were looking for.
