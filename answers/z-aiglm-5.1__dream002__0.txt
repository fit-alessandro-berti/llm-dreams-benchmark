You start walking faster, though you're not sure where you're going. The path beneath your feet has changed—it's narrower now, and the trees seem closer together than they were moments ago. You don't remember the oaks being this tall, their branches twisting overhead like grasping hands.

Then the rain comes, but it's not rain. The drops are too slow, too large. They hit the leaves with individual sounds—*plink, plink, plink*—like coins dropped on a countertop. You hold out your hand and catch one. It's warm.

A crack of lightning splits the sky, but it doesn't flash and fade. It lingers, frozen, a bright vein across the clouds. In its light, you notice something between the trees—a structure you hadn't seen before. A small stone building, moss-covered, with an arched doorway that pulses with faint amber light from within.

You should be scared, but you're not. The dream logic tells you this is where you were always heading.

You step inside. The storm vanishes instantly—no sound of rain, no wind, just silence so complete it rings in your ears. The room is circular, lit by candles that float at different heights near the ceiling. In the center sits a wooden table with a single object on it: a compass, its needle spinning lazily, as if undecided.

You reach for it, and the moment your fingers touch the brass casing, the needle stops. It points not north, but toward a door in the wall you're certain wasn't there before.

Behind you, the storm rages. Ahead, the door stands open, revealing not another room, but a meadow bathed in golden light, wildflowers nodding in a breeze you can't feel from where you stand.

The compass grows warm in your palm.

You step through.
