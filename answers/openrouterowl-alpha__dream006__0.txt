You are about to give a speech to a large audience. As you step onto the stage, you see the crowd looking expectantly at you, and you begin to feel the quiet hum of microphones adjusting. The lights are warm overhead, and for a moment, time slows—you can see faces: students, scientists, dreamers, skeptics, all waiting.  
You open your mouth—and instead of words, birdsong flows out. The sound takes shape, forming sentences in every language at once, but no one seems confused. They lean in, listening not with ears but with something deeper.  
Then, gently, you realize: this isn’t a speech. It’s a bridge—built from breath, built from courage, built from the shared silence before understanding begins.
