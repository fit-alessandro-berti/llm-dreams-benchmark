• You are in a world where colors and shapes are constantly changing. As you explore this surreal landscape, you come
  across a blank canvas and a brush that appears to be made of moonlight.
  You dip the brush into nothingness and paint a single line across the void. The line shimmers, then unfolds into a
  staircase, then into a river, then into a flock of birds made of glass. Each stroke changes what it means before the
  last one is even dry.

  You walk forward and touch the canvas once more. A door appears, carved from the edge of your own shadow. Beyond it is
  a room filled with clocks that run backward, their faces turning to match the color of your breathing. You reach for
  the largest one, and it rings like a bell underwater. With that sound, the ground beneath you tilts and the sky folds
  inward like fabric.

  Everywhere you turn, the horizon rewrites itself into impossible things: houses that bloom like flowers, mountains
  that hum, trees that grow stairs instead of branches. You realize the canvas is not separate from this place at all—
  you are drawing, and the world is answering.

  Then, at last, you lift the brush and hold it over the center of the page. You hesitate. The blank space stares back
  like a pupil.

  You paint a face. It smiles.

  And the world stops changing for one heartbeat long enough for you to feel peace. Then it changes again, and the face
  you painted opens its eyes, waiting for the next line.
