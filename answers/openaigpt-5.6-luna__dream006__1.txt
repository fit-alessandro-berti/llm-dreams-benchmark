You are about to give a speech to a large audience. As you step onto the stage, you see the crowd looking expectantly at you, and you begin to speak—but your voice comes out as birdsong.

At first, everyone laughs. Then the lights above you flicker, and the audience slowly grows feathers. Their faces become familiar: childhood friends, forgotten teachers, strangers from passing trains. They lean forward, listening intently, as if they understand every note.

You look down at your hands and find a small silver key resting in your palm. Without knowing why, you hold it up. The entire theater falls silent.

Behind the audience, a door appears where there was only a wall. It is painted the same blue as the sky just before dawn.

You stop speaking and walk toward it. As you turn the key, the crowd rises—not to applaud, but to follow. The door opens onto your childhood bedroom, except the window looks out over an endless ocean, and someone on the other side is calling your name.

You step through.

Then you wake up with the key still warm in your hand.
