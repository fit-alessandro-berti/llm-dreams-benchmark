Where the fireflies touch, the crew begins to move in perfect, silent harmony. They don't fight the storm; they begin to untie the ropes, letting the sails fly free, laughing as the wind catches the paper and tears it away into the dark.

A wave the size of a cathedral rises before the bow, casting a shadow that swallows the entire ship. There is no steering around it. You look down at the compass mounted to the binnacle, but the needle isn't pointing north—it's spinning wildly, finally settling on a point pointing straight *up*, toward the sky behind the clouds.

With a sudden, deep certainty, you spin the wheel hard to port, driving the ship directly into the vertical wall of the liquid mountain. 

But as the bow pierces the dark water, there is no crash. The sea parts like heavy velvet curtains. The ship doesn't sink; it begins to fall upward, leaving the turbulent sea behind, sailing into a silent, starry void where the storm dissolves into the soft, quiet hum of a world waiting to wake up.
