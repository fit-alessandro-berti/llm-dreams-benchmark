You are in a world where colors and shapes are constantly changing. As you explore this surreal landscape, you come across a blank canvas and hesitate. The air hums with static, and the ground beneath your feet ripples like liquid mercury. You reach out to touch the canvas, and it shivers under your fingers—warm, almost alive.  

Suddenly, the canvas *breathes*. A single drop of iridescent ink spills from nowhere, spiraling downward in slow motion. It splashes onto the surface, blooming into a fractal of hues that don’t exist in the waking world: a shade of "midnight laughter," the texture of "unwritten lullabies." The colors begin to shift, coalescing into a doorway—a swirling vortex of your own memories, but twisted, exaggerated.  

A whisper emanates from the canvas, neither male nor female, neither voice nor sound: *"What do you wish to unmake, or remake?"*  

The ground beneath you dissolves into a flock of origami birds, each carrying a fragment of your forgotten dreams. You realize the canvas isn’t blank—it’s *waiting*. The question isn’t rhetorical. It’s a challenge.  

Do you step through the doorway, risking the unraveling of everything you know? Or do you pluck one of the origami birds and let it guide you deeper into the dream? The choice itself begins to paint the world in real time.  

In the distance, the sky—if it can still be called that—is melting into a symphony of geometric constellations, humming your heartbeat. The canvas glows brighter.  

What do you choose?
