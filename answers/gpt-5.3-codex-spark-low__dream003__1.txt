• You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns
  and symbols spiraling across its surface, like constellations mapped in metal. The latch doesn’t open, but your thumb
  brushing one etched line sends a faint vibration through the lid. Then, on a whim, you trace the symbols in the exact
  order they appear—left eye of the raven, open hand, crescent moon, and finally three short lines.

  The box clicks.

  A breath of cold air slips out, carrying the scent of old paper and wet stone. Inside, wrapped in dark velvet, lies a
  compass that points not north, but upward. Beneath it, a mirror no bigger than a coin shivers, reflecting not your
  face but the ceiling above your apartment—only now the ceiling stretches into a long corridor of doors.

  You step forward and the dream does what dreams do: the room disappears.

  You find yourself in a hallway the length of a library, every door labeled with years: 1999, 2006, 2011, 2023… the
  compass needle hums against your palm, steadying toward the door marked Tonight. You choose it.

  Inside is a workshop filled with unfinished clocks and open notebooks. On a drafting table sits a final sketch: a key.
  Not for locks. For doors in the dark. You pick it up and the compass needle steadies to a stop. The mirror in your
  hand grows warm. Across the room, behind a glass wall, a figure is writing by lamplight. You think you should call
  out, but the figure doesn’t turn.

  When you wake, the box is in your real hand. Metal cool, impossibly heavy, and in it are only two objects: the tiny
  mirror and the tiny key. The compass is gone.
  Or maybe the key and mirror were never meant for the locked box. Maybe they were the opening, and the lock was just
  you.
