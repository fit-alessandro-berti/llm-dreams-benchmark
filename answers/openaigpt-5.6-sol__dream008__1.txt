open the project folder, only to find it filled with hundreds of blank pages. Each page is stamped **OVERDUE**.

Your boss taps their watch. “Well?”

You reach for a pen, but it bends like warm wax between your fingers. Around you, keyboards clatter faster and faster until the sound becomes rain. The office walls stretch upward, cubicles shrinking below like a maze. Everyone keeps working, though their chairs are empty.

Then the deadline appears at the end of the hallway: a huge red clock rolling toward you on spindly legs.

You run.

Doors flash past—BREAK ROOM, CONFERENCE ROOM, CHILDHOOD BEDROOM, OCEAN—but every one opens back into the office. At last, you dive through a door marked **SUBMIT** and tumble onto a quiet beach beneath a violet sky.

The blank pages flutter down around you. As they touch the sand, words appear:

**The project was never yours.**

Behind you, your boss clears their throat. You turn, but they are now a small, nervous penguin holding a clipboard.

“Do you know how to get back?” they ask.

The red clock reaches the shore, sighs, and sinks into the sea. You realize there was no deadline—only a sunrise.

You sit down to watch it.
