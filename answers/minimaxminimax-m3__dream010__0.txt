# The Dream Job Competition

You are participating in a competition where the grand prize is your dream job. As the competition progresses, you face various challenges and **the boundaries between reality and illusion begin to blur**.

---

The first challenge is simple on paper: build something extraordinary in 24 hours. But as you work, the clock on the wall seems to move backward. Your teammates appear, then disappear, then reappear wearing different faces. The judges ask questions in voices that sound like your own. You answer perfectly, though you're not sure if the words are yours or someone else's.

The second challenge demands you solve an impossible puzzle. The pieces don't fit any earthly logic. One piece, when you touch it, shows you a memory from childhood you never had. Another whispers your grandmother's name—your *real* grandmother, one you'd forgotten. The puzzle completes itself and reveals not an answer, but a door. You walk through.

Now you're in a vast hall lined with mirrors. Each mirror shows a different version of you: a you who already won, a you who never entered, a you who chose a completely different life. They all wave. They all beckon. The final challenge is announced:

**"Choose the version of yourself that deserves to win."**

You reach toward a mirror that shows you standing on a stage, holding a trophy shaped like your own dream job title. The glass is warm. As your fingers touch it, you realize—

The competition is you. Has always been you. Every challenge was a test, but not of skill. Of *recognition*. Of knowing which reflection is the truest one.

The mirrors begin to shake. The hall dissolves. And you wake up in your own bed, sunlight on your face, the lingering taste of victory on your tongue—even though you're not sure if you won, lost, or if the dream is still happening somewhere just beyond your eyelids.

You smile.

In your pocket, there's a business card. Your name. Your dream job. A phone number you've never seen before.

You're not sure if you're awake.

---

*What happens next? Do you call the number?*
