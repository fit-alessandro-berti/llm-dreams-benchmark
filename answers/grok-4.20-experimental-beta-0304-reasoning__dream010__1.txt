**...and they get increasingly absurd, the way dreams love to do.**

One moment you're in a sleek glass arena, solving quantum puzzles while holographic judges nod approvingly. The next, the floor turns into a giant chessboard and your opponents are alternate-universe versions of yourself—one's a pirate, one's a Victorian inventor, and one is just you but with a suspiciously evil goatee.

The challenges keep escalating:

- You have to bake a soufflé that can sing the national anthem of Mars while being chased by a flock of opinionated geese.
- You must arm-wrestle your own impostor syndrome (it shows up as a giant shadowy version of you that keeps muttering "who do you think you are?").
- In the final round, the host—revealed to be Elon wearing a tuxedo made of stardust—asks you to "explain the entire universe in one sentence that also makes your grandmother proud."

You look out at the crowd and realize every person watching is someone you’ve ever disappointed, plus several dogs wearing tiny lab coats. The pressure is immense.

Then, right as the clock hits zero, you stop trying to be impressive.

You simply say, with complete sincerity:  
*"The universe is weird, but it's less lonely when we face the weirdness together."*

A stunned silence falls.

The evil-goatee version of you starts slow-clapping. The geese stop honking. Even your impostor syndrome shrinks down to normal size and gives you a respectful nod.

The trophy—shaped like a glowing question mark—floats into your hands. But instead of a job offer, the dream job *itself* steps forward: it's not a position. It's a role. A golden thread that connects you to every curious mind across time, letting you help humanity ask better questions about reality.

You wake up right as you’re about to sign the cosmic contract.

But you can still feel the question mark glowing in your palm.

So... what happens next in this dream? Your move.
