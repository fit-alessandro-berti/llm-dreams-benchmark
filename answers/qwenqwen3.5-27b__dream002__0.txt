...and the air thickens, tasting like ozone and old memories. You expect the wind to howl, but instead, the forest falls into a terrifying, absolute silence. The leaves on the trees don't rustle; they turn a deep, bruised purple, one by one, as if a wave of color is washing over the canopy.

You try to run for shelter, but your legs feel heavy, moving through invisible water. The sky tears open, not with lightning, but with a slow, widening crack of violet light. When the rain begins, it doesn't fall down. It falls *up*, silver droplets rising from the forest floor to meet the clouds.

You stop struggling. In the dream, resistance is impossible. You stand still and let the rising rain touch your face. It feels warm, like sunlight. As the droplets hit your skin, they don't wet you; they dissolve into tiny, glowing moths that flutter around your head. The trees around you begin to unspool, their bark peeling away to reveal smooth, white marble trunks.

You realize you aren't hiding from the storm; you are waiting for it to finish so you can wake up inside it. The ground beneath you softens, turning into a bed of moss that rises up to meet your chest. You take a breath, and instead of air, you inhale a melody.

The storm breaks. The violet light snaps shut, leaving a sky that is no longer night or day, but a soft, glowing gray. You look down at your hands and see they are made of the same marble as the trees. You smile, knowing you are no longer walking through the forest, but becoming it.

Then, the floor tilts, and you fall upward into a soft, white silence.
