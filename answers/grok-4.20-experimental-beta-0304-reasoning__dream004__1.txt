You come across a blank canvas and the moment your shadow falls across it, the canvas *breathes*.

It inhales the riot of color around you—pulling streaks of liquid sapphire and molten rose gold into itself like veins. The surface ripples like water, then suddenly flattens into a perfect mirror. You see yourself staring back... except your eyes are tiny swirling galaxies and your hair is made of slowly orbiting moons.

A paintbrush appears in your hand, but it has no bristles—only a single, living flame at the tip that changes color every time you blink. The first stroke you make doesn't draw a line. It *unlocks* one. A hairline fracture races across the canvas and the dream itself splits open along it, revealing another version of this world where gravity is optional and trees grow upside-down, singing in frequencies you can taste.

You step *through* the canvas instead of painting on it.

On the other side, the colors have grown teeth. They nibble gently at your edges, trying to borrow pieces of you to complete themselves. A flock of impossible shapes—things that are simultaneously cubes, spheres, and emotions—swarm around you, begging to be named. Every name you give them becomes a new law of physics. You say "melancholy" and the sky turns into soft velvet that sobs quietly. You say "laughter" and the ground becomes ticklish.

In the distance, you notice another blank canvas floating in mid-air, waiting for *you* to step out of the painting you're currently becoming.

You smile, and the flame-brush laughs with your mouth.

What do you name the next color?
