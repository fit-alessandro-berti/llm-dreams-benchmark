You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to listen for something that isn't noise. You realize the silence is your own breath. As you walk through the ruins, dust settles on your skin like static memory, but your mind finds a quiet voice in the cracks where nothing should have existed. The wind blows gently over the broken structures—it's not an empty echo, but it's the sound of the earth exhaling again. You decide to build a shelter out of what remains. It might just be a roof, or perhaps just a small bench for when the sun finally returns.
