"We need this done yesterday," your boss says. 

You look up to apologize, but your boss’s face is a blur of digital static, like a television tuned to a dead channel. Their voice doesn't come from their mouth, but from the fluorescent lights humming overhead, vibrating inside your teeth. 

The deadline is written on a whiteboard across the room. It reads: **00:00:00**. But the numbers aren't counting down—they are spinning wildly, flashing red. 

You try to stand up to ask for help, but the office floor has turned into a slow-moving conveyor belt, quietly pulling your desk backward, away from the door. Your coworkers are all sitting at their desks, typing furiously, but they have no faces. They are just empty suits filled with shifting code, completely indifferent to your panic.

Suddenly, the stack of papers for the project begins to grow. It rises from your desk, punching through the ceiling tiles, revealing not a crawlspace, but a dark, starry night sky above the office. A cold wind blows down, scattering the papers. They flutter around the room like paper birds.

Your boss steps closer. The static on their face grows louder, a rushing sound of wind and dial-up internet. They raise a hand to point at your screen. 

You take a deep breath, close your eyes, and decide to jump off the conveyor belt. 

Instead of hitting the floor, you fall. You fall through the carpet, through the foundation of the building, drifting down into a quiet, weightless dark blue void. The sound of the office fades into a peaceful hum. The deadline disappears. You are just floating, suspended in a sea of glowing, silent code, waiting to wake up.
