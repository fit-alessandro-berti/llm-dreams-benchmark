You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, you notice the walls themselves shifting into interlocking riddles drawn from your own memories—a childhood hallway that never ended, equations whose variables are faces you’ve half-forgotten. Solving one doesn’t merely open a path; it folds the corridors so that the past becomes the way forward. The air thickens with the weight of half-formed thoughts. Deeper still, the puzzles turn inward, asking not for answers but for the questions you’ve been avoiding. You realize the maze is learning from every solution you offer, reshaping itself around your mind. In the final chamber there is no puzzle at all—only a quiet understanding that you have been both the architect and the wanderer all along, and the only true exit is to wake.
