You realize that to open it, you must stop trying to solve it. You close your eyes, letting go of the waking world's logic, and simply *remember* the feeling of a key turning in a lock. 

In the silence of the dream, a soft, resonant *click* echoes. 

The lid slowly drifts open on its own. But instead of holding gold, letters, or jewels, the inside of the box is filled with a swirling, miniature vortex of deep-space starlight. It pulls at your gaze, and before you can pull back, the room around you dissolves. You feel yourself falling forward—not onto the floor, but directly into the box itself, tumbling weightlessly through a sky of velvet blue, surrounded by the quiet hum of a thousand sleeping worlds.
