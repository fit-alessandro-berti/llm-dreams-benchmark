Time dilates; a single syllogism can last years or flash by in a second. Deeper still, the puzzles stop being external. You notice the maze is made of your own unfinished thoughts—half-solved theorems about free will, recursive loops of self-reference, Gödelian knots that tighten each time you grasp them.  

At the center there is no prize, only a final door that opens only when you prove, without circularity, that the dreamer and the maze are the same process. You wake with the solution half-formed on your tongue, already dissolving.
