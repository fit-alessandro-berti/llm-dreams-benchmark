The music is just loud enough that every conversation feels like a secret. 

First, a circle of people in linen suits laughing over a story you caught the end of — something about a dog, a boat, and a stolen hat. One of them makes eye contact and raises a glass like you belong there, and for half a second you almost stop. But your friend’s face isn’t among them, so you smile and keep moving.

Next, the kitchen island. It’s the gravitational center of the whole party. Someone’s debating sourdough starters, someone else is trying to open wine with a shoe, and there’s a kid in a dinosaur costume solemnly eating olives from a crystal bowl. You ask if they’ve seen Alex — that’s the one person you know — and the dinosaur kid points toward the back doors with an olive-stuffed seriousness that feels prophetic.

Outside, the air is cooler and the string lights make everything look like a memory. There’s a group around a fire pit trading embarrassing travel stories. Another cluster is barefoot in the grass, trying to teach each other a dance that nobody actually knows. You weave through, catching fragments: “…then the goat ate my passport,” “…best dumplings in Queens,” “…I swear it was a meteor.” 

Each group tugs a little. You could sit. You could become part of the dog-boat story. But the pull to find Alex is stronger — it’s the thread you’re following through the maze.

Then you hear laughter you recognize. It’s from behind a hedge where a smaller group is huddled around a tiny yappy dog wearing a bowtie. Alex is there, crouched down, letting the dog sniff their hand. They look up, see you, and their whole face does that *oh-thank-god* thing friends do when they find each other in a crowd.

“Took you long enough,” Alex says. “This is Bruno. He’s the mayor of this party.” 

Suddenly you’re not the person who only knows one person anymore. You’re the person Alex is introducing: “This is the one I was telling you about.” And the circle opens to let you in.

What happens next in the dream? Does Bruno the bowtie dog become central to the plot, or do you and Alex sneak off to debrief on the kitchen weirdness?
