**The Dream Continues:**

The air hums with an electric charge, thick with the scent of old parchment and something metallic—like the faintest whisper of gears turning beneath the floor. The walls of the maze shift subtly when you blink, their surfaces rippling with faint, glowing runes that pulse in time with your heartbeat. Each corridor branches into three paths, but the third always seems to *breathe*—a narrow slit of darkness that exhales a cool draft when you press your palm against it.

The first puzzle awaits you at the center of the chamber: a **spherical mirror** suspended from the ceiling by chains, its surface cracked in a perfect spiral. When you step beneath it, your reflection doesn’t mimic your movements. Instead, it *unfolds*—your face splits into three versions, each whispering a different question in unison:

*"Which hand holds the key?"*
*"What is the maze afraid of?"*
*"Do you remember the name of the door you seek?"*

You reach out, and your fingers pass through the glass. The middle reflection—your "true" self, or so it feels—smiles and says, *"The answer is not in the mirror."* The other two dissolve into static, leaving only the sound of a lock clicking open somewhere in the distance.

You turn, and the corridor ahead has changed. The walls are now lined with **clockwork birds**, their brass bodies frozen mid-flight, their beaks pecking at invisible keys. One by one, they spring to life as you approach, their tiny voices singing a dissonant melody. The song is a riddle:

*"We count the hours but never age,
We guard the past but never engage.
Find the bird that sings out of time,
And it will show you the way to climb."*

You spot it immediately: a single bird with a **broken gear** in its chest, its song a single, off-key note. As you touch it, the gear whirs to life, and the bird transforms into a **skeleton key** in your palm. The moment it materializes, the maze *shudders*. The floor tilts, and the walls groan as if waking from a long sleep.

Ahead, a **door** appears where there was none before—its surface carved with the same spiral cracks as the mirror. But this time, the cracks *move*, slithering like living things. A voice, neither male nor female but *layered*, echoes from within:

*"Prove you are not lost.
Solve the door’s hunger."*

The door’s keyhole is a **mouth**, lined with teeth of polished obsidian. The key fits, but the moment you turn it, the teeth *bite down*—not on the key, but on your *memory*. A flash of pain, and suddenly, you *know* the name of the door: **"The Threshold of Unwritten Choices."**

The door swings open.

Beyond it, the maze doesn’t end. It *expands*—into an infinite library where books levitate, their spines pulsing with light. Shelves stretch into the distance, but the air is thick with the scent of **burning pages**. At the center of it all stands a figure: a version of *you*, but older, their hands outstretched as if offering something.

*"You’ve solved the puzzles,"* they say, *"but the maze was never the test. The test was whether you’d stop to wonder why it let you in."*

Then the dream *shifts*—
