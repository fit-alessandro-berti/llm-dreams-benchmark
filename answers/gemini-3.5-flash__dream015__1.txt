...despite the hardships, you decide to build a lighthouse.

But there is no ocean here. Only a vast, undulating desert of grey ash where a great city once stood, its skeletal skyscrapers leaning like forgotten headstones against a bruised, twilight sky. 

The air tastes of copper and old rain. Your boots crunch on the debris of a forgotten civilization as you drag a heavy, rusted iron lantern up the spiraling staircase of a half-collapsed watchtower. You have spent weeks scavenging not for canned food or ammunition, but for mirrors, shards of stained glass from ruined cathedrals, and jars of bioluminescent moss that glows with a faint, pulse-like hum.

When you reach the summit, the wind howls through the hollow concrete structure, threatening to pull you into the abyss below. You ignore the cold. With steady hands, you assemble your masterpiece at the edge of the precipice. You arrange the cracked mirrors so they catch the weak, dying light of the setting sun, multiplying it, bending it, until a brilliant, kaleidoscope beam of emerald, sapphire, and gold pierces the gloom.

You turn the crank, and the light begins to sweep across the dead landscape.

In the distance, you see them. At first, they look like shadows shifting in the ruins—the dangers you were warned about. The beasts, the desperate raiders, the remnants of a broken world. But as the colorful beam washes over them, they stop. 

A pack of wild, mutated wolves halts in the ash, their eyes reflecting the green light. A group of heavily armed scavengers drops their weapons, their faces illuminated in soft blues and warm yellows, looking upward with expressions not of anger, but of childlike wonder. For a brief moment, the light paints the ruined world with the colors of a vibrant, living meadow.

You realize you didn’t build the lighthouse to guide ships. You built it to remind the darkness that the light hasn't forgotten how to find it.

As the wind begins to die down, the colors of the beam start to bleed into the sky, turning the clouds into waves of violet and gold. The gravity of the tower begins to loosen. Your feet drift a few inches off the concrete floor. You are floating now, rising into the warm, illuminated air, leaving the ruined world behind as the dream begins to dissolve into the soft, quiet hum of waking up.
