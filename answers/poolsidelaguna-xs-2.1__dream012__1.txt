The storm lashes at the ship like a thousand fists, and you grip the wheel with white-knuckled resolve. Your crew—weathered faces streaked with salt and sweat—stares at you, their eyes wide with fear and trust. You bark orders over the howl of the wind: *“Secure the starboard lines! Batten down the hatches! All hands to the rails!”*  

But even as your voice cuts through the chaos, you notice something odd. The waves aren’t just rising—they’re *glowing*, their crests flickering with an eerie, bioluminescent blue. The mast ahead creaks under a pressure that shouldn’t exist, and then—*crack*—a section splinters, revealing a strange, pulsing symbol carved into the wood. The crew gasps.  

You hesitate, torn between survival and curiosity. The storm’s eye begins to open before you, a swirling vortex of calm water and starlight. Your first mate grabs your arm. “Captain, we’re sinking!”  

You make a choice. Instead of fleeing, you step to the edge of the deck, arms outstretched. The water parts. The storm stills. And in that silence, you hear it—the voice of the sea itself, ancient and vast: *“You are not the captain here.”*  

The dream dissolves as the ship becomes a speck on an endless ocean, and you wake—no longer to a storm, but to a single, glowing drop of water on your palm.
