The air smells of woodsmoke, iron, and something sweet like blooming jasmine in winter. Horse-drawn carriages clatter past on cobblestones, yet every face you glimpse in the crowd wears a version of your own, frozen at different ages of your life.

As you unfold the map, the ink shifts under your fingers, revealing streets that twist like roots. You realize you need to find the clock tower that ticks backward, for it is the only landmark shown on the page—a tower made of black glass and bone that, according to the map, will be built three centuries from now, in the exact spot where you are standing.

The humming begins then: a low, mechanical thrum vibrating up through the stones, a sound that has no place in this century. You understand, with the strange logic of dreams, that this era isn’t history. It is a story your sleeping mind is weaving to keep you from waking. And the only way out is not to escape forward or backward in time, but to finish the tale— to climb that impossible tower and wind its hands until they point to *now*.
