...as you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns and symbols etched into its surface. They seem to shift and shimmer when you're not looking directly at them, like heat rising from sunbaked pavement. The box is warm to the touch, pulsing with a rhythm that almost matches your own heartbeat.

You trace your fingers along the engravings, and they begin to glow faintly beneath your fingertips—a soft amber light that spreads like veins of liquid gold across the dark metal. The patterns reveal themselves to be a language you don't recognize, yet somehow understand. They whisper of thresholds, of memories left behind, of doors that open only for those who have already walked through them.

A voice—your own, yet not—speaks from somewhere within the box: *"You've been looking for the key, but the key was never lost. The key is what you forgot to remember."*

You feel a strange certainty settle in your chest. The box is yours. It has always been yours. You simply forgot.

When you place your palm flat against the lid, the lock clicks open on its own. Inside, instead of treasure or trinkets, you find:

A small mirror. Your reflection stares back—but older, wearier, smiling knowingly. They mouth three words you cannot hear but understand completely:

*"You made it home."*

The dream shifts. The box dissolves into light, and you realize you are standing in a room you've never seen, yet know every corner of. Photographs line the walls, all featuring you in moments you don't remember living. A door waits at the far end, slightly ajar, and through it spills the sound of laughter—yours, from a life you haven't yet lived.

The dream is asking you a question now, and the only way forward is to answer it yourself.
