You tilt the box, and the light catches something hidden within the carvings: a sequence of constellations you almost recognize. Your fingers trace Orion's belt, and the wood grows warm beneath your touch. A soft click. A hidden seam reveals itself along one edge.

But the box doesn't simply open. It *unfolds*.

Like origami in reverse, the walls collapse outward, panels folding upon panels, each layer revealing another surface covered in even more elaborate patterns—maps of places you've never been but somehow miss, written in a language you cannot read but somehow understand. The pieces keep unfolding, far more surface area than the small box could ever have contained, until you're standing in the middle of something vast.

You look up. The panels have become walls, then corridors, then an entire labyrinth stretching in every direction. The ceiling above mirrors the constellations from the box, and they're rotating slowly.

At the center of the labyrinth—far ahead, yet somehow also right behind you—a door stands alone. No walls on either side. Just a door in an empty field of stars.

From somewhere beyond it, you hear your own voice call your name.

You take a step forward, and the floor gives way beneath you—

You jolt awake, heart pounding, and for just a moment, you swear you can still feel the warm wood beneath your fingertips.
