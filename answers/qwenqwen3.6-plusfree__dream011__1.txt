...not written in ink, but in slow-dissolving frost that breathes against the paper. The first question reads: *What does your hesitation weigh, and where will you leave it when you’re ready to move forward?*

You reach for your pen, but your fingers meet warm wax instead of plastic. As it touches the page, it doesn't leave letters; it leaves impressions. A pressed maple leaf. The groove of a childhood stair. The faint outline of a door you forgot you left open. The ceiling above you softens into a canopy of clouds, and the fluorescent lights hum a lullaby you almost recognize from a life you haven't lived yet.

The next question shifts as you read it: *Name three things you've been told to forget, and show them how to fit in your pocket.* You slide your hand into your coat and pull out a folded train ticket, a cracked compass, and a key that only turns in the dark. You don't write them down. You just set them on the desk, and they settle like stones in a stream, quiet and sure.

The final question appears without a question mark. It simply says: *Begin.*

The exam hall stretches, then folds inward. The desks become stepping stones across a shallow river. The proctor walks past, but their footsteps leave blooming water lilies, and their voice is your own, saying gently, *You already know this.*

You stand. The paper lifts, catches an unseen draft, and spirals upward into a sky the color of dawn. There's no panic. Just the quiet click of recognition, like a key finally finding its lock.

And then the chime rings. Not a school bell, but the soft, familiar ring of your morning alarm. You open your eyes to your own ceiling, the ghost of wax on your thumb, and the unshakable sense that you didn't take the test at all. You simply remembered you were allowed to pass.
