…the atmosphere grows tense. The lights dim, and the hum of the crowd fades into a low, suspenseful silence. You’ve made it to the final round.

The final challenge is revealed: **"The Pitch of Reflection."**

You are led to a podium in the center of a vast, circular room. The walls are covered in mirrors that seem to stretch infinitely, reflecting not just your image, but fleeting glimpses of your past—late nights studying, moments of doubt, small victories.

The host explains the rules: You must pitch a revolutionary idea to a panel of judges who are shadows, their faces indistinguishable. However, there is a twist. **You cannot speak a single word.**

Instead, you must project your vision telepathically. You close your eyes and focus. You feel the weight of every challenge you’ve overcome in the competition—the mental puzzles, the physical endurance tests, the teamwork trials—converging into a single, glowing concept in your mind.

Suddenly, the mirrors begin to change. They stop reflecting the past and start projecting the future you’ve imagined. The judges lean forward, their silhouettes becoming clearer as they witness the world you are building for them.

Just as you feel the connection solidify, a massive gong sounds. The lights snap back on.

The host walks over, holding an envelope. The room is dead silent.

**How does the dream end? Do you wake up before they open it, or do you hear the verdict?**
