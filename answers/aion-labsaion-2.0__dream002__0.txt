The air grows thick and heavy, humming with an electric charge. The once-gentle rustle of leaves becomes a frantic clatter as the wind whips the branches into a frenzy. Above, the sky darkens to a bruised purple, and the first fat raindrops splatter against your skin, cold and startling.

You start to run, but the path ahead seems to twist and elongate, the familiar trees now looming like silent, judgmental giants. Lightning cracks the sky in a blinding white fork, and in that instant of illumination, you see a figure standing in the clearing ahead—a silhouette that feels both utterly alien and deeply familiar.

Thunder rolls, not just above you, but through you, vibrating in your bones. The rain becomes a torrential downpour, soaking you to the skin, but instead of feeling chilled, a strange warmth spreads from your core. The figure in the clearing raises a hand, and the storm seems to bend around it, the rain curving in impossible arcs.

You try to call out, but your voice is swallowed by the wind. As you take a step forward, the ground beneath your feet softens, and you realize you're no longer running on soil, but on something soft and yielding, like moss or… fur. The forest itself is changing, breathing. The bark of the trees pulses with a faint, inner light, like veins of molten gold.

The figure beckons. A part of you screams to turn back, to find shelter, but a deeper, more primal part is drawn forward, into the heart of the storm. This is no ordinary dream, you realize with a jolt of lucidity. This is a threshold. The storm isn't destroying the forest; it's revealing what was always hidden beneath the peace.
