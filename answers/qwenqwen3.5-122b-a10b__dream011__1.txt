The room is growing quieter, not in silence, but in volume. The hum of the fluorescent lights deepens into a heartbeat. You look up at the proctor, but their face is a blur, shifting features like a reflection in a disturbed pond. They nod at you, not with approval, but with recognition.

You realize then that the exam isn't about what you know. It is about what you are willing to let go of.

The questions begin to change again. They stop asking about your future and start asking about your name.
