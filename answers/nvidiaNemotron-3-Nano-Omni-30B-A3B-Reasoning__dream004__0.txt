In a world where colors and shapes shifted like liquid light, I stepped through a forest of towering, translucent spirals that hummed with unseen frequencies. The sky pulsed in slow waves of violet and gold, and the ground beneath my feet rippled like disturbed water, each step leaving a temporary imprint that faded into shimmering dust. I wandered without direction, drawn by a quiet pull toward a clearing where a single, floating canvas hovered in midair, untouched and perfectly blank. Its surface was smooth and luminous, reflecting the kaleidoscopic sky above, yet offering no image—no mark, no hint of what might come. I reached out, my fingers trembling, and as I touched the edge of the canvas, the world around me stilled. The shifting colors froze into a single, radiant hue, and the shapes around me solidified into delicate, crystalline forms. In that suspended moment, I understood: the canvas was not empty, but waiting. It held the potential of all that could be, a silent promise of creation. With a deep breath, I closed my eyes and let the stillness fill me, knowing that when I opened them again, the first stroke would be mine.
