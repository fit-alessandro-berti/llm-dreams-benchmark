**You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to plant a single, slightly crumpled seed you pulled from the pocket of a tattered lab coat.**

At first it feels absurd—poking a tiny speck of potential into cracked, irradiated dirt while keeping one eye on the horizon for raiders or worse. But that night the sky cracks open with impossible rain, and by morning a vivid green shoot has pushed through the wasteland like a middle finger to entropy itself.

Word travels. A sarcastic old scavenger with a robotic arm shows up, then a wide-eyed kid who can coax electricity from broken solar panels, then a three-legged dog that somehow always finds the cleanest water. You turn the ruins into a garden, then a village, then a tiny defiant beacon of “maybe we don’t have to stay screwed.”

Laughter echoes where silence used to reign. Someone starts a terrible post-apocalyptic stand-up routine. The dog gets honorary citizenship.

Just as you’re handing out the first real tomatoes grown in years, the colors begin to bleed at the edges. The sky folds like wet paper. The dog starts talking in your mother’s voice, offering you a taco. The entire landscape ripples, stretches, and—

You wake up in bed, heart still racing, sheets tangled around your legs like radiation blankets. The room is quiet. Normal. Safe.

But for the rest of the day you can’t shake the feeling that somewhere, in some layer of reality, that little green shoot is still growing… and that maybe the real dream is believing we can’t start over with whatever small thing we have left. 

What a weird, hopeful apocalypse. Want to keep dreaming?
