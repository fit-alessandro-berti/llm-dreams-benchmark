...stare at the screen, but the letters keep rearranging themselves. Every time I try to read the project brief, the words slide off the page like water droplets, pooling at the bottom of the monitor.

My boss stands behind me, but when I turn to look, he has the face of my third-grade teacher. \ yesterday,\ just given the project. This makes perfect sense in the dream.

I start typing, but my fingers are too large for the keyboard. Each key I press produces three letters at once. The office around me has grown longer, stretching into a hallway of cubicles that disappears into fog. My coworkers are all working intensely, but when I glance over, their screens are blank.

I realize I've forgotten to wear pants. Nobody seems to notice, which somehow feels worse than if they did.

The clock on the wall has too many numbers. The hands spin backward, then sideways. Someone announces over the intercom that the project is actually a presentation, and I'm supposed to give it in five minutes, in a language I don't speak, to an audience that includes my dead grandmother and a panel of judges made of shadows.

I try to run to the bathroom to gather myself, but the hallway keeps extending. My legs move in slow motion, as if through honey...

...and then I wake up, heart pounding, grateful for the ordinary weight of morning light on my blanket.
