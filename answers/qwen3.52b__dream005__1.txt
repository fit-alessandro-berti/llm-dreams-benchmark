You are at a large social gathering where you only know one person. As you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people and shadows dancing on your face. Suddenly, the crowd disperses into a single line of silence before the light from the ceiling flickers on—revealing that you have been walking through their memories for months without knowing who they were. You look around and see the one person standing in front of you now; their eyes are dark and steady, holding the weight of your own reflection. They turn to you, their hands raised—not to shake or offer, but as if they were holding a bridge that had been missing from all the spaces between you for too long. As the light shifts away, you both laugh as if we're strangers talking in the dark, but only it sounds like the same voice saying something new about what we have lost and found together.

The crowd continues to move in a loop, and when the lights fade completely, there is no one left behind—just the sensation of being watched by your own mind's image of the night that just passed. You walk home on the street, and then you wake up.
