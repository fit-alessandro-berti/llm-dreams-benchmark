As the competition progresses, you face various challenges and **obstacles forged from your own insecurities.** You must solve riddles spoken by judges made entirely of shadow, walk across bridges that only exist when no one is watching them, and answer questions where the answers change color depending on how close you breathe to a microphone made of glass.

The final hurdle comes in silence: you are asked to give a speech about what this job would actually be like if it stopped working entirely. As you speak, the audience fades away, replaced by a room filled only with yourself and your reflection holding up the prize—a golden hourglass that isn't filled with sand, but with light.

The moment you reach the end of the aisle, the competition ends not with applause, but with a sudden warmth in your chest. When you wake up, there are no papers to collect and no trophy case to admire; only a blank white wall where the stage lights should have shone, and a faint echo of your own voice whispering the title back at you.
