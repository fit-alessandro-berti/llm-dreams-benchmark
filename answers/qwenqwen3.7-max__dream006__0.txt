A woman in the third row raises her hand. You point to her, floating a little higher. 

"Excuse me," she says, her voice sounding exactly like your own inner monologue. "But the ocean is leaking."

You look up. The ceiling of the auditorium has dissolved into a deep, bruised-purple sky, and a colossal, bioluminescent whale is swimming slowly through the air above you. Water begins to drip from its glowing belly, but the drops fall upward, rising from the floor to the ceiling. 

You open your mouth to finally deliver the punchline of your speech, to explain the whale, the sand, and the floating water. You take a deep breath, but as you inhale, the auditorium shatters into a million floating geometric shapes. 

You are falling backward into a vast, warm white space. The cello music swells to a deafening crescendo, vibrating right through your chest, until the vibration suddenly turns into the sharp, rhythmic buzzing of your alarm clock. 

Your eyes snap open. You are staring at your bedroom ceiling. The morning light is filtering through the blinds, and for just a fleeting, dizzying second, you swear you can see a single grain of golden sand resting on your pillow.
