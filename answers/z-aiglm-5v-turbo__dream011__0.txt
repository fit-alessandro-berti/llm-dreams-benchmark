As you sit down to start the exam, you notice the questions are **not written in any language you recognize, but rather composed of shifting, three-dimensional geometric shapes that seem to fold in on themselves the longer you stare at them.**

You pick up your pen to write, but the desk beneath your elbows begins to soften, turning from hard wood into warm, pliable flesh. A clock on the wall ticks backward, its hands spinning wildly, and you realize with a sudden, cold dread that you haven’t attended a single class all semester. The student next to you leans over, their face a blur of static, and whispers, *"You're looking for the blue key, not the answers,"* even though you are certain the exam is for Advanced Calculus.

You try to scream, but your voice comes out as the dial-up tone of a modem, and the walls of the lecture hall begin to breathe.
