…open the project folder and find it’s completely empty.

No files. No instructions. Just a blinking cursor and the clock on the wall ticking louder than it should.

Your boss stands behind you, smiling in a way that suggests you should already know what to do.

“Almost done?” he asks.

You look back at the screen. The cursor blinks again, but now it’s blinking in the air, floating above the keyboard. The computer has quietly dissolved into mist.

You try to explain this, but when you speak, spreadsheets slide out of your mouth and scatter across the floor like leaves.

Your coworkers begin collecting them, nodding seriously as if everything makes perfect sense. One of them hands you a stapler that has too many buttons.

“Just compile it,” she says.

You press a button.

The office lights dim. The walls slide apart, revealing an enormous progress bar stretching across the horizon of cubicles. It’s stuck at 99%.

Your boss checks his watch.

“So,” he says calmly, “what’s the holdup?”

You step closer to the progress bar and notice a tiny checkbox underneath it labeled:

“Include reality.”

It’s unchecked.

When you reach down to click it, the floor turns into a keyboard and every step types something enormous across the sky. Somewhere a printer begins screaming.

The progress bar finally moves to 100%.

Everyone applauds.

Then your boss hands you another project folder.

It’s labeled: “Urgent — Due Yesterday.”
