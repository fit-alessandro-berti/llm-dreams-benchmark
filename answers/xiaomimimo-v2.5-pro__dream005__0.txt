# The Dream

You are at a large social gathering where you only know one person. As you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people and…

their conversations wash over you like waves of sound — half-formed words that almost make sense but dissolve when you reach for their meaning.

You pass a cluster of people laughing around a table, and their laughter echoes just a beat too long, stretching and distorting like a recording slowing down. One of them turns and smiles at you knowingly, as if you've been part of the joke all along. You smile back, confused, and keep moving.

The room seems to shift when you're not looking directly at it. Hallways that were short become impossibly long. You find yourself in a kitchen that feels familiar but doesn't belong to anyone you've ever met. Someone hands you a drink you didn't ask for — the glass is warm and the liquid inside shimmers a color you can't quite name.

You catch a glimpse of someone wearing your friend's jacket across the room. You push through the crowd, calling their name, but each step forward seems to carry you two steps sideways. The music gets louder — it's a song you know, but all the lyrics are wrong, rearranged into something strangely profound.

A stranger grabs your arm gently and says, *"You were supposed to bring something."* You look down at your hands and realize you've been carrying a small, wrapped gift the entire time. You don't remember what's inside.

Finally, you spot your friend in a doorway, backlit so you can't quite see their face. They wave. You wave back. But when you reach the doorway, they've already moved into the next room.

And the next room is your childhood bedroom.

And it's morning.

And you wake up.
