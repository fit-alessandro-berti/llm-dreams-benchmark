**You are at a large social gathering where you only know one person. As you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people and** the rooms keep rearranging themselves like a living maze.

You slip between clusters of strangers whose conversations flow in and out of common sense. One circle is debating the proper temperature for folding laundry; when you pass, they all turn as a unit and ask if *you* have seen the person you’re looking for. “They were just here,” one says, pointing to an empty chair that is somehow still warm. “They said the ceiling was lower in the next room.” You leave before the chairs start puling themselves into new seating arrangements.

In the next space a group is tasting invisible desserts, solemnly describing flavors that change mid-sentence—“caramel, no wait, regret; oh, now it’s the first day of school.” They offer you a spoon. You take a taste and suddenly remember the name of the one person you came here for, only for it to dissolve on your tongue. They laugh gently and wave you onward.

Further on, a tightly packed knot of people are practicing the art of quiet applause, their hands brushing together with almost no sound. When you ask if they’ve seen your friend, they gesture toward a doorway that wasn’t there a moment ago. Through it you enter a long gallery where everyone is wearing versions of the same face—yours, slightly wronged by different lighting. They all claim to be helpfully searching too, though none of them move.

Just as the gathering swells and the air thickens with overlapping voices, you feel a familiar hand on your shoulder. You turn. There they are: the one person you know, exactly as remembered and yet entirely unexpected, carrying two drinks as if they have been waiting in the exact spot the crowd rearranged itself around. They smile the way only someone who has also been looking can smile and say, “I found you first.”  

The rooms stop rearranging. The strangers’ conversations blur into a warm background murmur, and for a moment the dream holds still, content.
