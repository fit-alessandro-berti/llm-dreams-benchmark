…and the wind begins to move through the trees in a way that doesn’t sound like wind at all.

At first it is only a whisper, a soft shivering in the leaves above you, but within moments the whole forest seems to inhale. The air turns cool against your skin. The scent of pine and damp earth grows stronger, richer, as if the ground itself has been split open. Far away, thunder rolls across the sky like heavy furniture being dragged through the clouds.

You look up.

The sunlight that had been spilling between the branches is gone. In its place, the sky is layered with deep blue-gray clouds, swirling faster than seems possible. The birds have fallen silent. Even the insects have vanished. The only sound now is the creaking of the trees as they lean toward one another, as though they are sharing a secret about you.

Then the first drop of rain lands on your hand.

It is warm.

Another strikes your cheek, then another, and suddenly rain is falling in silver threads all around you. But instead of soaking into the soil, each drop glows faintly where it lands, tiny points of pale light scattered across the forest floor. You step back in surprise and notice that the path you were following has disappeared. In its place is a narrow trail of luminous stones winding deeper between the trees.

Lightning flashes.

For an instant the forest becomes white and still, and in that bright freeze of time you see something ahead: a door. Not attached to any wall, not standing in any house, just a weathered wooden door set upright between two ancient oak trees. Golden light shines around its edges.

The thunder follows, louder now, shaking your chest.

You don’t know why, but you walk toward the door.

The storm grows wild behind you. Rain whips through the branches. Leaves spiral in the air. The trees groan and bow as if urging you onward. When you reach the door, you see a brass handle shaped like a crescent moon. It is warm beneath your fingers.

You open it.

On the other side is not another part of the forest, but a vast midnight sea under a sky full of stars. The water is perfectly calm, reflecting constellations so clearly that it feels as though the sky continues beneath the waves. A small boat waits at the shore, lantern-lit and empty except for a folded note resting on the seat.

You step into the boat and unfold the paper.

In your own handwriting, it says:

**You were never running from the storm.  
You were being led through it.**

As soon as you finish reading, the thunder stops.

The sea begins to glow from below, soft and blue, and the boat carries you forward without oars, without sound. Above you, a shooting star crosses the sky. Below you, another answers from the depths.

And just before you wake, you have the strange, certain feeling that somewhere beyond the horizon, morning is waiting for you.
