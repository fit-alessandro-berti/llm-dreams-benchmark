You are dreaming. You look up at the swirling whitecaps and realize they aren't water anymore; they are memories from your childhood. 

**And you found that the storm was not coming to end, but to teach you something you wouldn't have known until the end of your life.** You raised your hands like palms toward the darkness so that your crew could look back at the light where it was once hidden, and you whispered, *"We don't need to stop the water."*

**You were not afraid of the dark anymore. You realized that the fear wasn't in the sea—it was just something inside the soul that needed to be let out.**

When the ship finally stopped fighting the waves, it drifted gently toward a horizon where only stars could be seen. **And you knew now that dreams don't end with an awakening; they are just sleepwalking until we stop asking ourselves why we were running in the first place.**
