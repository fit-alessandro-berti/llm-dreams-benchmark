**Dream Continuation:**

The moment the strangers reach out—soft hands on your shoulders, warm voices murmuring reassurance—you feel something shift inside you. The city’s noise fades into a distant hum, and for the first time in what feels like forever, you don’t have to pretend you know where you’re going.

One of them, a woman with silver-streaked braids and a coat that smells faintly of rain and old books, presses a crumpled map into your palm. *"This isn’t the usual way,"* she says with a knowing smile, *"but it’s the one that matters."* The others nod in agreement, their faces glowing faintly, as if lit from within by a light you can’t quite see.

You follow them down a side street you’ve passed a hundred times before, but now the buildings seem to whisper. A café you’ve never noticed before appears, its windows fogged with steam and laughter. Inside, a table is set just for you—tea steeps in mismatched cups, a plate of warm pastries you didn’t ask for, and a single, unopened letter addressed in your own handwriting. The ink shimmers like liquid silver.

*"You’ve been looking for this,"* the woman says, sliding the letter toward you. *"But not the way you thought."*

As you open it, the dream dissolves—not into waking, but into something deeper. The strangers are still there, their forms blending with the city’s shadows, their voices weaving into the wind. You realize: they were never strangers at all. They were always waiting.

And then—

You wake up.

But the letter is still in your hands.
