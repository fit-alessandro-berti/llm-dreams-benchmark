…written in a language you don’t recognize, though the diagrams beside them look strangely familiar—maps of your childhood home, sketches of people you haven’t seen in years, equations that seem to describe memories instead of numbers.

The clock above the door is ticking backward.

Everyone else begins writing confidently, their pencils scratching like rain. You turn the page, hoping for something clearer, and find only one question printed in the center:

**“What do you want to become?”**

You try to answer, but your pencil has turned into a key.

At the front of the room, the professor looks up and smiles as if this is exactly what they expected. Behind them, where the blackboard should be, there is a door you’re certain was not there before.

The bell rings.

No one else moves.

You stand, walk to the door, and unlock it. Bright light spills into the classroom, warm and impossible. On the other side is not a hallway, but a vast open field filled with desks, each one occupied by a different version of yourself—some older, some younger, some living lives you almost chose.

They all look up at you.

And then, together, they whisper:

**“There is no final exam.”**

You wake just as you step into the light, with the feeling that the answer was never something you had to write down.
