As you step onto the stage, you see the crowd looking expectantly at you, and you begin to speak. But the words that leave your mouth are not your own. They are a cascade of images and scents—the salt spray of a forgotten sea, the scent of old books in a dusty attic, the sound of your grandmother's humming. The audience leans in, their faces a blur of emotion. You realize you are not giving a speech; you are telling the story of their collective memories. With each breath, you pluck a thread from their minds and weave it into the air, a tapestry of light and shadow. The forgotten joys, the quiet sorrows, the dreams they had left at the door—you hold them all, and for a moment, the entire hall is a silent, breathing entity, connected by the pulse of a shared past. The final word you speak is their name, and as you say it, the glow envelops you, and you wake, knowing that sometimes, words are just the shadows of a deeper understanding.
