Your boss, usually a steady presence, materializes beside you. But he's not *there*—he's *projected*, a shimmering hologram made of flickering data streams and urgent energy. His voice cuts through the silence of the office, layered with digital distortion:

Then, as the countdown hits **00:00:01**, the mug *explodes*—not with noise, but with light. A thousand tiny, glowing particles erupt, swirling into the air, forming a complex, intricate blueprint of a city suspended in mid-air, breathing clean blue air. The symbols shift: *Photosynthesis engines. Atmospheric scrubbers. Human-ecosystem harmony.* You recognize it—the impossible solution you’ve been researching in your subconscious for years.

And then, the realization hits like a physical force:  
**You weren’t given the project.**  
**You dreamed it into existence.**  
**And now, you are the only one who can *build* it.**

Your hands move before your mind can catch up—fingers dancing across the phantom keyboard, weaving code that feels like breathing. The air hums with potential. The impossible deadline isn't a trap. It's a key.

*"Show me the first step."*

The city in the air pulses, a single, perfect pulse of light... and begins to *breathe*.
