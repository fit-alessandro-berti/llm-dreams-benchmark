Frustrated by the barrier, I decide to bypass words entirely. I reach into my chest, right through my shirt, and pull out my perspective. My friend pauses. Their angry expression melts into sudden, quiet curiosity. They take the indigo light from my hands. As soon as their fingers touch it, the living room we are standing in dissolves. 

We are suddenly ankle-deep in a vast, mirror-like ocean reflecting a starry, magenta sky. The heavy stones of our argument have turned into harmless, floating water lilies. My friend looks at the indigo light, finally understanding exactly how I feel without having to translate it. Then, they reach into their own chest and pull out a sphere of bright, crackling amber light—their perspective. 

They place it in my hands. It feels warm, almost burning, and suddenly I understand their fear, their urgency, and their side of the story perfectly. 

We press the two lights together over the water. They don't clash or shatter. Instead, they swirl into a calm, iridescent green. The tension in my chest vanishes. We aren't angry anymore; we are just two figures alone in an endless, quiet ocean, watching the new green light drift upward into the sky to join the stars. 

I look around to smile at them, but they have turned into a flock of white doves that scatter into the twilight. And then, the dream begins to fade.
