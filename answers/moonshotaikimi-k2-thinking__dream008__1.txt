The office walls begin to breathe, exhaling the smell of burnt coffee and forgotten ambition. Your chair starts sinking into the carpet, which has become a sea of sticky notes. Each one has a task written in your handwriting, but you don't remember writing them. The words spiral inward.

Your boss is still waiting, but now they're a life-sized paperclip. They bend toward you questioningly. The clock hand in your hand twitches, and you realize you're supposed to build the clock, the project, the office, yourself—all from scratch, using only this ticking metal limb and a dream-logic understanding of quantum mechanics.

You try to speak, but your mouth fills with pixels. Behind you, someone who might be your mother but also the company CEO is photocopying a hurricane. The "START" button on your monitor pulses like a heart. You press it. The screen cracks open, and a thousand origami cranes made from missed deadlines fly out, each one whispering: *extension, extension, extension...*

But the paperclip boss is dissolving into toner dust now, and the impossible deadline is no longer a date—it's a place. You're already there. It looks exactly like your childhood bedroom, but the ceiling is a calendar with no numbers, only red circles.
