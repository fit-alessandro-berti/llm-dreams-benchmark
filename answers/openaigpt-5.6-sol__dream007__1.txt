You are in the middle of a heated argument with a close friend. The disagreement is about something important to both of you, and you decide to walk away before either of you says something unforgivable.

But when you turn, the room has vanished.

You’re standing on a narrow bridge suspended over a dark, star-filled ocean. Your friend remains at the other end, still speaking, though their words drift toward you as glowing moths. Some flutter past. Others land on your clothes, burning cold against your skin.

You try to remember what the argument was about, but the details keep changing. First it was a promise. Then a betrayal. Then a small blue key that both of you insist belongs to you.

The bridge begins to unravel beneath your feet.

Your friend stops shouting and holds out their hand. In their palm lies the key.

“I never wanted to keep it,” they say. “I was waiting for you to ask why I had it.”

Below you, the ocean opens one enormous eye.

You step toward your friend. With every step, a forgotten memory rises from the water: the two of you laughing in a kitchen at midnight, sitting together in silence after bad news, making a promise beneath a tree that no longer exists.

By the time you reach them, you no longer care who was right.

You take their hand instead of the key.

The bridge collapses—and together you fall upward, into a sky bright with morning.
