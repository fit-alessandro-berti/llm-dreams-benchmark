For a moment, the chaotic swirls of red and blue freeze against the board, holding their breath. You look down at your own hands, only to see them become part of the painting, transforming from fingers into brushstrokes, then from strokes into clouds.

The colors rush away, leaving no trail, nothing but white smoke drifting upward until your breathing stops and the canvas is gone, replaced by the cold, still silence of waking up on a morning where nothing changes except the sun's position.
