As you trace one of the glowing lines with your fingertip, the air around you grows thick and sweet, smelling of ozone and old parchment. The box doesn't click open. Instead, it dissolves.

The solid form melts away into a cascade of silver mist, revealing not an object inside, but a doorway. Through the mist, you see a vast library where the books are flying like birds, their pages fluttering in a wind that doesn't exist. A figure stands at the threshold—a version of yourself, but older, wearing clothes made of starlight.

They extend a hand, not holding a key, but offering a single, blank page.

"The lock wasn't meant to keep things out," the figure says, their voice echoing as if from a great distance. "It was waiting for you to write the answer."

You reach out to take the page, and as your fingers brush against it, the dream shifts again. You are no longer standing before the box. You are sitting at a desk, pen in hand, and the story is just beginning.
