When you open it, a wind of lavender and starlight spills out, carrying the sound of distant bells. Beyond the doorway is a city made of glass water and folded paper, where the streets rearrange themselves politely beneath your feet.

A cat with seven shadows walks up to you and speaks in a voice like turning pages:

“Ah, you’ve arrived. The moon has been waiting for your signature.”

So you take the silver pen it offers, and on the edge of the sky you sign your name in a language you’ve never learned. Immediately, the clouds bloom into lanterns, the lanterns into fish, and the fish into a staircase rising straight upward through the night.

You climb.

At the top, you find the blank canvas again, only now it is no longer blank. It shows you standing here, painting the scene you are inside.

And in the lower corner, in tiny gold letters, it says:

**Dreams are only unfinished awakenings.**
