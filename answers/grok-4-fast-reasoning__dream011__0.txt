You are about to take the final exam for a course that determines your future career. As you sit down to start the exam, you notice the questions are written in a swirling, iridescent ink that shifts like liquid mercury, forming words that don't stay put. The first one reads: "If a flock of invisible birds carries the weight of your unspoken regrets across a bridge made of forgotten passwords, how many feathers will it take to balance the equation of your soul?"

Panic rises in your chest as you flip to the next page, but the paper feels alive, pulsing under your fingers like a heartbeat. Question two: "Describe the color of silence in the language of extinct stars, using only the shadows from your childhood dreams." Your pen hovers, useless—it's melting into a puddle of rainbow wax that smells like fresh rain and old books.

The proctor, who looks suspiciously like your high school gym teacher but with the wings of a dragonfly, leans in and whispers, "Time's an illusion, kid. Answer with your heart, or the room will fold into origami." Suddenly, the desk warps, and you're sliding down a chute of exam papers into a vast library where bookshelves stretch into infinity. The questions chase you, morphing into riddles that echo your deepest fears: "Why did you hesitate?" "What if you succeed?"

You wake up gasping, the alarm blaring, but a strange certainty lingers—you aced it, whatever "it" was. Your future? It's waiting, rewritten in dream-ink.
