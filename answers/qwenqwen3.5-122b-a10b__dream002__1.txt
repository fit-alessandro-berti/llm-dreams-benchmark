...the air grows heavy, tasting of ozone and burnt sugar. The sky doesn't darken; instead, it fractures like stained glass, revealing a void of swirling gold behind the clouds. The wind doesn't blow—it pulls. You feel the gravity of the forest shift, and suddenly, you are walking on the branches of the trees as if they were the ground. The rain begins, but it is warm and smells like ocean salt. You don't seek shelter. You open your arms, and the storm doesn't soak you; it fills you. You realize you are not walking through the storm, but *as* the storm. The thunder is your voice, and the lightning is your thought. Just as the light becomes too bright to bear, a hand touches your shoulder, and you wake up.
