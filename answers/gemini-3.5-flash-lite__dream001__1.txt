...run. 

The pavement beneath my bare feet feels strangely soft, like warm moss, though the towering skyscrapers around me are made of glittering, translucent obsidian. Neon signs in an alphabet I've never seen pulse with a hypnotic, rhythmic heartbeat. 

I pat my pockets—empty, save for a heavy, ticking pocket watch that isn't mine. When I flip it open, the hands are spinning backward, and instead of numbers, the dial reads only: *ALMOST.*

Panic prickles at the back of my neck. I don't know who I'm supposed to meet, or what the meeting is about, but every instinct in my body screams that being late will cause the city to dissolve. 

I dart into a crowded plaza where people with faces like shifting watercolors drift past, silent as ghosts. I grab the arm of a passing figure—a woman wearing a hat made of real, fluttering butterflies. 

"Excuse me," I pant, "where is the spire? The grand meeting?"

She turns her head slowly, her features blurring into a smooth blank slate. She points a slender finger straight up. 

I look. Rising above the clouds isn't a building, but a massive, silver staircase winding upward into a sky the color of crushed plums. A soft, chiming bell echoes from the top, carrying a strange gravity that pulls me toward it. 

I take a deep breath, leave the chattering crowd behind, and take the first step upward, the metal cold beneath my toes as the ground far below begins to melt away into a sea of stars...
