
You are about to give a speech to a large audience. As you step onto the stage, you see the crowd looking expectantly at you, and you begin to...

**Option 1 (The Anxiety Spiral):**
...speak, but your voice comes out as a faint whisper. The microphone grows to the size of a tree trunk, and your notes are written in a language you don't recognize. The audience's faces blur and shift, becoming people from your past—teachers, old friends, strangers—all staring in silent judgment. You try to run, but your legs feel like they're moving through honey. Just as panic peaks, you wake up with a start, heart pounding, grateful it was only a dream.

**Option 2 (The Surreal Triumph):**
...speak, and your words don't just sound—they take shape. Colorful, glowing symbols float from your mouth, weaving into a tapestry above the crowd that tells a story older than language. The audience isn't just listening; they're breathing in rhythm with your sentences, and their collective awe creates a warm, golden light that fills the room. When you finish, there is no applause—only a profound, shared silence that feels like understanding. You wake with a deep, peaceful certainty.

**Option 3 (The Meta-Dream):**
...speak, and halfway through, you realize you're dreaming. You pause, smile at the audience, and say, "This is a dream, you know." To your surprise, they all nod in unison, some smiling knowingly. A person in the front row shouts, "Finally! We've been waiting for you to notice!" The dream then shifts—you're now in a classroom, being graded on your awareness of the dream state. You wake up, unsure if you passed.

Which version felt most like *your* dream?
