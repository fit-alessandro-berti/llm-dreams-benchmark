The air grows heavy, thick with the scent of rain and ozone. The gentle rustling of leaves sharpens into a frantic, urgent whisper. Above, the sky transforms from dappled blue to a bruised violet in moments, churning clouds racing as if alive.

A deep, resonant hum begins in the earth beneath your feet, vibrating up through your bones. You watch, not in fear but in awe, as the trees themselves seem to lean in, their bark shimmering with an almost metallic light. The first drops of rain don't fall—they *ascend*, rising from the forest floor as glowing silver motes before vanishing into the darkening canopy.

Then, the storm breaks. But it is not water that falls. It is a cascade of pure, liquid starlight, pouring down in sheets that make the air sing with a high, crystalline chime. Each drop that touches your skin leaves a fleeting, cool warmth, and the world around you begins to dissolve into breathtaking patterns of light and shadow, the very concept of 'forest' melting into a symphony of sensation.

You realize you are not just walking through the forest; you are becoming part of its dream, the storm rewriting reality in real-time. You take a step, and the ground ripples beneath you. You look back, and the path you walked has already vanished, replaced by a grove of softly pulsing, luminous fungi that cast the clearing in a gentle, emerald glow.

In the center of the glade, where the storm-light is brightest, you see a single, perfect stone cairn that wasn't there before. It hums with the same frequency as the earth, and you know, with the unquestionable certainty of dreams, that it has been waiting for you all along. The storm begins to quiet, its starlight now falling as a gentle, shimmering mist, and you feel a profound peace settle around you, deeper than the one you started with. The forest breathes out, and so do you.
