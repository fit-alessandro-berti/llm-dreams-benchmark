You are at your workplace, and suddenly, you are given a project with an impossible deadline. Your boss is waiting for you to start, and you open your mouth to protest—but a cascade of sticky notes stamped **URGENT** spills out instead. The office clocks spin wildly until their hands detach and chase you down the hallway like frantic birds. Your boss’s face stretches into a towering hourglass, sand pouring from his eyes as he drones, “It was due yesterday.”

Sticky notes peel from every surface and swarm like angry wasps. Deadline tentacles begin wrapping around your desk. Just as the pressure peaks, a quiet realization settles over you: **this is a dream.**

You take a deep breath and snap your fingers.

Time freezes. The hourglass-boss shrinks into a cheerful golden retriever that wags its tail. The sticky notes flutter away as glowing butterflies. The infinite project collapses into a single elegant solution that completes itself with a soft, satisfying *click*. The entire office dissolves into floating confetti and soft morning light.

You float upward, smiling, already knowing you can handle whatever real deadline waits when you wake.
