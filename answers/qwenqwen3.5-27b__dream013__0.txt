The puzzles themselves start to shift. No longer are they equations or mechanical locks. The first puzzle you encounter is a riddle written in the language of your childhood, the ink floating off the stone and forming small, black birds that flutter around your head. To pass, you don't need to answer; you need to remember the feeling of the room where you learned to speak.

Deeper still, the floor becomes a mirror, and the ceiling becomes the sky, though there is no sun, only a soft, violet hum. The logical thinking that served you so well at the entrance now feels like a heavy coat in a summer heat. You realize the maze isn't testing your intellect, but your willingness to let go of it.

At the very center, there is no door, only a single, open book resting on a pedestal of mist. You open it, and the pages are blank. You reach for a pen that isn't there, and in that moment of reaching, the dream logic snaps. The walls dissolve into white light, the hum becomes the sound of an alarm clock, and you blink your eyes open, the solution to the final puzzle lingering on your lips like a forgotten word.
