The other competitors are faceless silhouettes, their movements synchronized in eerie unison. They don’t speak, but you can feel their ambition radiating off them like heat waves. As you reach the final stage, the arena dissolves into a vast, silent library. The air smells of old paper and ozone. In the center stands a single desk with an empty chair and a blank sheet of paper.

A voice, soft yet commanding, echoes from everywhere and nowhere: *"Define your purpose."*

You pick up the pen. It feels heavy, anchored by the gravity of every choice you’ve ever made. As you hover it over the paper, you realize the ink isn’t black—it’s glowing with the color of your deepest passion. You begin to write, not words, but memories: the late nights spent perfecting a skill, the failures that taught you resilience, the joy of creation. With each stroke, the library around you transforms. The shelves burst open, releasing birds made of light that soar toward a ceiling that has become a starry night sky.

When you finish, the paper lifts into the air, unfolding into a map. It doesn’t show a location, but a path—a winding trail through mountains, forests, and cities, leading to a door labeled with your name. The faceless competitors fade away, leaving you alone in the quiet triumph of self-discovery. You step onto the path, knowing the grand prize wasn’t just the job, but the clarity of who you are becoming.
