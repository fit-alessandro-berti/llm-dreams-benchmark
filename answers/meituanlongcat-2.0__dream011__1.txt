...written in a language you don’t recognize, yet somehow you understand it perfectly.

The first question reads: *If your childhood pet could speak, what secret about your future would it reveal?*

You blink, and paper turns to a window—through it, you see your hometown, but it’s made entirely of books. The second question appears in midair, written in shimmering ink: *What door have you walked past every day without opening?*

Next, the desk becomes a ship’s deck on a calm sea. The third question scrawls itself across the sky: *Whom do you fear disappointing most, and why are you still carrying their voice in your head?*

Glancing around, other students are answering in their own ways—some painting the answers, some whispering to objects, some standing completely still with tears streaming down their faces.

The proctor leans over, her face shifting between faces you’ve known—a grandparent, a teacher, a stranger from a childhood photo. She says softly, “You’re not here to be tested. You’re here to remember what you forgot you wanted.”

The final question materializes between your fingers:  
*What is the first thing you would dare to do if you knew you could not fail—and why are you still waiting?*

As you reach for a pen, the exam hall ripples like a pond, and you wake up—not with answers, but with a strangely clear question pulsing in your chest, long after the dream fades.
