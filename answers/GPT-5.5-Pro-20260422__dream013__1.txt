At first, every chamber feels like a test. Numbers glow on stone doors. Mirrors reflect versions of you making different choices. A bridge appears only when you arrange a row of symbols in perfect order. Each solution takes everything you have, and each time you succeed, the walls shift behind you, sealing the path back.

Deeper inside, the puzzles become stranger.

One room contains a thousand clocks, all ticking at different speeds, but no hands. Another asks you to choose between three doors, each labeled with a memory you thought you had forgotten. In the center of a circular chamber, a glass cube floats in the air. Inside it is a tiny model of the maze itself, and inside that model is another cube, and inside that cube you see yourself, standing exactly where you are.

Then you understand: the maze is not made of walls.

It is made of decisions.

The moment the thought forms, the floor vanishes.

You fall—not downward, but inward—through layers of old choices, abandoned plans, half-finished thoughts, and questions you never answered. The air fills with whispers, not threatening, but urgent. Every puzzle you solved speaks at once, each one repeating the same phrase in a different form:

**There is no center. There is only the next turn.**

You land softly in a narrow corridor lit by a pale blue glow. Ahead is a final door with no lock, no handle, and no inscription. You push against it, but it does not move. You search for hidden mechanisms, patterns in the stone, anything familiar.

Nothing.

Then you stop trying to solve it.

You sit down.

The maze becomes silent.

For the first time, you are not thinking ahead. You are not calculating, comparing, remembering, or predicting. You simply breathe. The blue light brightens. The door begins to dissolve—not opening, but becoming less certain, as if it had only existed because you expected one final obstacle.

Beyond it is not freedom, exactly.

Beyond it is your room.

You are standing beside your own bed, watching yourself sleep. On the bedside table lies a small stone key from the maze. You pick it up, and it is warm in your hand.

Your sleeping self opens one eye and whispers:

“You can wake up now.”

But before you do, you hear the maze rearranging itself somewhere behind you, patient and endless, waiting for the next time you need to find your way through.
