…run, but the streets keep rearranging themselves like sliding puzzle pieces beneath your feet.

Neon signs flicker in languages you almost understand. Every time you ask someone for directions, they smile kindly, point behind you, and vanish into a doorway that wasn’t there a second ago. Your phone map spins in circles, then calmly displays: **“Destination: Almost There.”**

You turn into a narrow alley and find a subway entrance made of red velvet curtains. Inside, the train is silent, full of people in business suits wearing paper masks with your own face printed on them. When the doors open, you step out—not into a station, but into a giant conference room where rain falls softly from the ceiling and nobody seems to notice.

At the far end, a long table stretches into the distance. Someone taps a glass and says, “Ah, finally. You’re early.”

You sit down, soaked, heart pounding, and open your briefcase. Inside is a single key, warm to the touch, and a note in your own handwriting:

**“The meeting is to decide what to wake up as.”**

Before you can ask anything, the city outside the windows folds itself like paper, the lights go out, and every voice in the room whispers at once:

“Choose.”
