**...storm out of the room, slam the door behind you—only to find yourself still standing there facing your friend, the door now somehow behind *them* instead. The argument has twisted into something stranger.**

You’re both shouting about the same core thing that always mattered: whose turn it was to finally leave the city together, or which of you betrayed the promise first, or maybe whose fault the lost years really were. Words blur and reverse mid-sentence. Your friend’s face flickers between the version you love and a younger, almost childhood version of them you’d forgotten existed.

You decide, in that heated moment, to grab the nearest object and hurl it—only the object turns out to be a handful of glowing letters that scatter into the air like fireflies and spell out the exact sentence you were both too proud to say: “I miss us.”  

The letters hover, vibrate, then rearrange themselves into a doorframe made of light. Your friend steps through without looking back. You hesitate, heart still racing from the fight… then follow.  

On the other side the city has melted into endless hallways of half-remembered apartments you once shared. Every door opens onto the same argument playing out again, but quieter each time, until the sound is only your two voices overlapping: soft, tired, almost laughing.  

You reach the last threshold and wake, or think you do—still half inside the dream, the argument unfinished, the yellow light of the letters fading on the ceiling of your real room. You roll over, check your phone, and see their name at the top of your messages.  

The dream ends there, unresolved and tender, the way the best ones sometimes do.
