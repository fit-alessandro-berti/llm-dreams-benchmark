Your boss is still standing there, but now he's also standing by the coffee machine, and somehow also sitting in a meeting room that doesn't exist on this floor. All three versions of him are patiently waiting. "The client needs this by yesterday," one of them says, which makes no sense, but you nod urgently.

You stand up to go to the printer and suddenly you're in your childhood home's kitchen. Your mother is making sandwiches and asking if you've called your grandmother. You haven't. You feel guilty about this, though you're not sure which grandmother she means—both have been gone for years.

The project document is in your hands now, but it's just a single page that reads, over and over: *"You forgot. You forgot. You forgot."*

You look out the window. The parking lot is full of cars, but they're all the same car—*your* car—and you realize you've lost the keys.

Then your phone rings. The caller ID says it's the deadline.

You answer.

And then you wake up, heart pounding, already reaching for your phone to check your work email at 3:47 AM.
