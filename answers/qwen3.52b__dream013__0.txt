You feel a sudden weightlessness as the corridors shift from stone to light, and you realize that each puzzle was never meant to be solved by rules. Instead, every locked door whispered a secret rather than demanding a key. As you move deeper into the labyrinth, logic fades, replaced by an intuitive knowing; you don't need a map, because the wall knows where you stand. When the final light breaks through not above, but from *within* and touches your palm, you know that the maze was always there in your chest, waiting to be felt rather than broken.
