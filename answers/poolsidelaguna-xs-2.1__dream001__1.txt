You find yourself in a vast, unfamiliar city, and you realize you have an important meeting in an hour but no idea where it is. You start to **follow a flickering streetlamp that glows brighter with each step, its light casting long, shifting shadows that seem to spell out directions in a language you almost understand**. The cobblestones beneath your feet ripple like water, and you notice buildings morphing as you pass—towers turning into clock faces, bridges becoming bridges of light.  

You encounter a street vendor selling maps, but when you reach for one, it melts into a flock of paper birds that scatter into the sky. A child nearby hands you a key made of ice, which melts instantly in your palm, leaving behind a single word: *"Beneath."*  

Following the word, you descend into a subway station that doesn’t exist above ground. The train arrives not on tracks but as a stream of liquid starlight. You board, and as the doors close, the conductor—a faceless figure in a coat of swirling constellations—whispers, *"The meeting is where you began."*  

You wake up (or do you?) at your desk, your morning coffee cold, and realize the "meeting" was the job offer you’d forgotten you’d applied for. The city, the key, the stars—all were just the hum of a siren in your chest, the sound of your heartbeat syncing with the city’s pulse.  

But in your pocket, you swear you feel something warm and weightless, like a key they forgot to take.
