You are in a maze filled with complex puzzles. Each puzzle requires intense concentration and logical thinking to solve, and as you move deeper into the maze, you notice something strange: the solutions keep slipping away from you.

You solve a puzzle—a grid of shifting symbols that finally click into place—but when you turn to walk through the opening it created, the wall has already rearranged itself. The answer you were so sure of dissolves like sugar in water. You can almost remember it. Almost.

The corridors stretch and breathe. A door that was on your left is suddenly behind you. You try to read the inscription on an archway, but the letters rearrange themselves every time you blink, spelling words in languages you don't quite know but somehow understand anyway.

There's an urgency here, a sense that you're *supposed* to be somewhere, that something is waiting at the center of the maze. You quicken your pace. The next puzzle is a series of levers, and you know—with that strange dream-certainty—that pulling them in the right order matters enormously. But your hands feel far away, and the levers multiply as you reach for them.

Then you realize: the puzzles aren't really the point. You've been trying to escape, but the maze isn't a trap. It's *you*. Each wall is a thought, each corridor a worry you've been carrying. The center isn't a place—

And here, as dreams tend to do, the image flickers. You're somewhere else now, or maybe the same place, and the urgency has melted into a warm, weightless calm. The puzzles don't matter anymore. You were never really lost.

*Would you like me to continue the dream, or let it drift somewhere new?*
