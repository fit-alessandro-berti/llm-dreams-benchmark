You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the wind howls with the voices of forgotten ancestors, carrying scents of rain from distant childhood summers. Lightning splits the sky into shards of color and swirling galaxies. The rain falls not as water but as liquid starlight; each drop that touches your skin reveals a fleeting memory or a life you might have lived. Thunder speaks in unfamiliar voices calling your name. The trees lean in, their branches twisting into archways of light as the path softens into mist. Seeking shelter, you step into the hollow of an ancient oak whose roots unfurl into a glowing spiral staircase. Emerging on the other side, you find yourself in a vast starlit meadow where the storm has transformed into a gentle aurora. In this place, you realize the forest was never outside you—it was the dreamscape of who you might become.
