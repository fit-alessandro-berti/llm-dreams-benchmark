You are about to take the final exam for a course that determines your future career. As you sit down to start the exam, you notice the questions are **written in a language you’ve never seen before**, yet somehow you understand every word. The paper shimmers faintly, and when you glance up, the clock on the wall is melting like wax, its hands spinning backward.  

The exam room has changed too. The chairs are no longer chairs but **tall, slender trees** growing from the floor, their branches creaking like old piano keys. The proctor—a person you recognize as your childhood mentor, but with their face blurred—whispers, *"There is no wrong answer here. Only truth."*  

You open the first question: *"Describe the color of your fear."*  
You try to write, but your pen draws symbols instead—spirals, maybe, or constellations. The second question asks, *"What do you owe to the person you were five years ago?"*  
The paper begins to breathe.  

Suddenly, the room is flooded with light, and you realize the exam isn’t testing your knowledge. It’s testing your **willingness to let go** of everything you’ve been taught. The final question appears in bold letters: *"Who will you become when no one is watching?"*  

The page is blank.  

And then you wake up—or do you? The sound of a bell rings somewhere far away, and your phone buzzes with a text: *"Don’t forget the exam starts in 10 minutes."*  

Do you proceed… or question the nature of reality?
