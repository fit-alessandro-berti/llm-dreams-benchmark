You are about to take the final exam for a course that determines your future career. As you sit down to start the exam, you notice the questions are written not in ink, but in swirling, luminous smoke that rises from the paper like incense. Each question hovers just above the page, shifting shape as you try to read it.

The first one reads: *“What did you sacrifice to become this person?”*

You blink—and suddenly the room is no longer a sterile exam hall. It’s your childhood bedroom, the walls covered in faded concert posters and half-finished paintings. Your mother’s voice floats from the doorway: “You were always better at dreaming than doing, weren’t you?”

The second question appears: *“Whose voice are you still trying to please?”*

Your father’s face flickers into view—silent, arms crossed, never smiling at your successes. But then, the face shifts. It’s your lover. Your best friend. Your younger self. All of them watching, waiting.

A soft chime sounds. The clock on the wall turns into a hummingbird, flitting between questions, leaving trails of golden dust. The paper beneath your hands dissolves into petals. You realize—you’re not holding a pen. You’re holding a key.

The final question, larger than all the rest, burns with ember-light:

*“If this exam were not about passing—but about remembering—who would you choose to be, right now?”*

And then, the silence.

No one is watching. No one is grading.

You let the key fall.

And you wake up—not with a start, but with a sigh.

The exam was never about the answers.

It was about realizing you’d already taken it.

And you passed… by surrendering.

You sit up in bed. The morning sun is golden. Your phone buzzes. It’s your boss.

“Hey,” they say. “We’ve been waiting. The presentation is in ten minutes. And this time… just be yourself.”

You smile.

For the first time in years, you believe them.
