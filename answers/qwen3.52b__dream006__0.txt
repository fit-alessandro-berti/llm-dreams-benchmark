You begin to realize that you are not speaking to fill the silence, but to wake up their sleep. You begin to feel the words as if they are already vibrating in the air before anyone else can catch them. The speech is no longer about your life or your fears—it is a mirror for the people standing around you. As the last word hangs in the suspended breath of the room, you finish, only to find that they haven't heard yet; instead, the words fade into light where the audience are now watching the dream itself with wonder.
