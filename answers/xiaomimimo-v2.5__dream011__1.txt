...not written in ink, but in faint, shifting pencil lines that rearrange themselves every time you blink. The first question seems to ask about "The foundational theorem of subjective experience," but as you focus, the words morph into "How many shades of blue are in a memory of childhood?"  

You feel a cool draft from a window that wasn’t there before, and notice the other examinees—their faces are familiar yet impossible to name, like half-forgotten classmates. A clock on the wall has hands that spin backward, then forward, then melt slightly like wax.  

You look down at your answer sheet. It’s not paper, but a smooth, cool stone. There’s only one question engraved upon it, in letters you somehow understand without reading:  

*“What would you create, if you knew it would be remembered for exactly one day?”*  

The pencil in your hand is warm, as if it’s been held by someone else just before you. Outside the tall, arched window, the sky isn’t a color you’ve seen before—a deep, living violet streaked with gold.  

You take a breath, feel the strange certainty that this is both the most important test you’ve ever taken, and also not a test at all. And you begin to write.
