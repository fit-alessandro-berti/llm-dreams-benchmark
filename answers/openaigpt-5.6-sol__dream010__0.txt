You are participating in a competition where the grand prize is your dream job. As the competition progresses, you face various challenges and puzzles designed to test not only your skills, but your courage, patience, and imagination.

In the first round, you must give a presentation to an audience of faceless mannequins. Every time you doubt yourself, they grow taller. When you finally stop trying to impress them and speak honestly, they shrink into toy-sized figures and applaud.

The next challenge takes place in a maze built from old report cards, rejection letters, and unfinished to-do lists. A golden thread appears at your feet, but it only moves when you admit something you’re afraid of. With each confession, the maze loosens until the paper walls scatter like leaves.

At last, you enter a vast office at the top of a glass tower. Behind the desk sits an older version of you. They slide a contract across the table, but the job title is blank.

“What position did I win?” you ask.

Your older self smiles. “That was never the real competition.”

The windows dissolve, revealing every place you have ever wanted to go. The blank contract becomes a mirror, and you realize the judges, rivals, and obstacles were all versions of your own expectations.

“You don’t win your dream job,” your older self says. “You choose what makes a life worth waking up for.”

You sign your name. The tower fills with sunrise.

Then you wake with a pen in your hand and one clear sentence written on the page beside you:

**Begin before you feel ready.**
