The air smells sharply of woodsmoke, crushed pine needles, and something metallic, like the taste of a copper coin. You look down and realize you are wearing a heavy, rough-spun wool cloak that wasn't there a moment ago. Your modern clothes are gone, replaced by leather boots caked in pale, silvery mud. 

The architecture is a dizzying blend of medieval timber-framing and ancient, carved stone, twisting up toward a sky that isn't blue, but a bruised, twilight purple, even though the sun seems to be rising. 

As you step onto the cobblestone street, the stones feel soft beneath your boots, yielding slightly like packed moss. A woman walks past you pulling a wooden cart filled with glowing, pearlescent mushrooms. She doesn't look at you, but you notice she leaves no footprints, and the wheels of her cart make no sound. 

You feel a sudden, urgent pull in your chest, a magnetic tug directing you toward the center of the village. In your hand, you realize you are clutching a heavy iron key. It is warm to the touch, and it vibrates in time with a low, melodic hum that seems to emanate from the earth itself.

You follow the sound down a narrow, winding alley where the shadows detach from the walls and stretch out to greet you. At the end of the alley lies a sprawling, overgrown courtyard. In the center stands a colossal, petrified tree made of what looks like black glass. Wrapped around its base are dozens of clocks of all shapes and sizes—pocket watches, grandfathers, sundials—but their hands are spinning wildly, completely out of sync.

The humming is loudest here. You approach the tree and notice a single, glowing keyhole embedded in the glass bark. 

You raise the warm iron key in your hand. Just as the metal tip is about to touch the lock, the purple sky begins to fracture like a broken mirror. A harsh, rhythmic, mechanical sound begins to bleed into the dream—a sound that doesn't belong here at all. 

*Beep. Beep. Beep.*

The glass tree shimmers, turning transparent. You catch a glimpse of your own reflection in it, but it's not you in the wool cloak; it's you, lying in your bed, eyes fluttering beneath closed lids. 

You have a split second to make a choice before you wake up: Do you turn the key and plunge deeper into the dream, or do you let the beeping pull you back to the morning?
