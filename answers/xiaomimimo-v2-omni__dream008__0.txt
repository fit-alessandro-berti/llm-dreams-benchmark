The boss’s office door vanishes behind you, and you’re holding a stack of documents that glow faintly. The first page reads: *“Project: Map the dreams of the city. Deadline: Tomorrow at dawn.”*  

You look up—your coworkers are typing, but their keyboards are silent, and their screens show swirling galaxies instead of spreadsheets. The clock on the wall melts like warm wax, dripping slow, luminous numbers onto the carpet.  

You open your mouth to say it’s impossible, but instead, a single paper crane flutters out and flies toward the ceiling. Your boss smiles, her eyes reflecting starlight.  

“Begin,” she says. “You have all the time you dream.”
