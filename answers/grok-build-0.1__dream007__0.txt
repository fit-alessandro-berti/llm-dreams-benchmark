You decide to stop mid-sentence, take a slow breath, and say, "Tell me what you're actually afraid of losing here." 

The words hang in the air like smoke. Your friend's face softens for a second, then cracks open. Instead of answering, they reach into their chest and pull out a small, glowing box. "This," they say, and open it. Inside is a miniature version of the two of you as children, laughing on a swing set that no longer exists. 

The room begins to tilt. The argument dissolves into light. You both watch the children for a long moment. One of them turns, looks directly at you (the adult you), and waves. 

Then the light takes everything. You wake up with your phone in your hand and a half-written text to your friend already on the screen.
