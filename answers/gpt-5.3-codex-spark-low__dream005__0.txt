• ... you pass from circle to circle, each one a different kind of echo.

  One group sits in a circle of candlelight discussing secrets in perfect whispers. Another laughs too loudly by a table
  of red cups that refill themselves with milk-white liquid, no one ever looking up. Near the staircase, people in old
  school uniforms trade photographs of places that don't exist. You move toward the loudest laugh, then the softest
  voice, then a room of mirrors where everyone claps in sync and forgets to notice you.

  At the far end, by a piano playing a song you almost remember, there is the person you came to find—standing still
  while everyone else circles around them. They look up, instantly, as if they have been waiting. “You found me,” they
  say.

  You look around. Everyone nods as if they knew this line was coming. Then the crowd starts fading at the edges, like
  smoke in daylight, and their hand is warm in yours as the room folds inward into one small, quiet place.
