
The air turns sharp with the scent of ozone, and the leaves above begin to tremble. A low rumble rolls across the canopy, and the sky darkens to an eerie greenish hue. You quicken your pace, but the path behind you has already blurred—swallowed by a wall of wind and rain. Just as the first drops strike your skin like cold needles, the forest parts. A hidden clearing emerges, lit by a shaft of golden light. In its center stands an ancient oak, its branches cradling a glowing key. The storm pauses, waiting. The key hums with warmth. You know, somehow, that it will unlock the memory you’ve forgotten—a voice calling your name from the other side of waking. But the rain is gentle now, almost playful, and the key dissolves into fireflies as the dream folds into dawn.
