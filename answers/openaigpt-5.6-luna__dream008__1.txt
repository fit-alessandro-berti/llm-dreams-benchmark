You are at your workplace, and suddenly, you are given a project with an impossible deadline. Your boss is waiting for you to start, and you realize that every computer in the office is displaying a countdown clock.

You open the file, but instead of instructions, it contains a map of the building. The deadline is only five minutes away.

You run through the corridors, past coworkers who are frozen like statues, until you reach a door that has never been there before. Behind it is your childhood bedroom, except the walls are covered in unfinished tasks: emails, reports, promises, and things you meant to say.

At the center of the room sits a small wooden box. Inside is a single sheet of paper with one question:

**“What would you do if you knew you couldn’t fail?”**

The countdown reaches zero.

You look up—and your boss is standing beside you, no longer angry, just tired. He takes the paper, folds it carefully, and says, “That was the project.”

Then the office dissolves into morning sunlight. You wake before your alarm, still holding a pencil, with the strange feeling that the deadline was not an ending after all, but an invitation to begin.
