You start to **check your phone, but the screen is blank, displaying only a single, pulsating clock face that ticks backward.** 

Panic flares in your chest, cold and electric. You run, but the streets don't stretch in one direction; they fold like origami, alleyways turning into bridges that span impossible distances. You see a signpost, but the letters dissolve into rain as you approach, reforming into a new word: *Hurry.*

Suddenly, the noise of the city—the distant sirens, the chatter of passersby—cuts out. The world goes silent, save for the sound of your own breathing. You stand in front of a massive, unmarked door. You look down at your watch; the hands have stopped entirely, hovering at the meeting time. 

A voice, sounding exactly like your own but echoing from everywhere at once, whispers, *"The meeting isn't about the appointment. It's about the time you spent searching."*

The door opens without a handle. Inside, there is no office, no table, no colleagues. There is only a mirror, and standing before it is you, sleeping in a bed that looks like your actual bed.

Your eyes flutter open. The sun is streaming through the window. The alarm clock beside you is silent. The dream is already gone, but you can still feel the phantom weight of the missing hour, and the strange, lingering certainty that you are already exactly where you need to be.
