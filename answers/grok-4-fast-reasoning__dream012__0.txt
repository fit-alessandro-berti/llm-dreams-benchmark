You are the captain of a ship in the middle of a turbulent sea. Your crew looks to you for guidance as the storm worsens, and you stand at the helm, the salt spray stinging your face like a thousand tiny needles. The wind howls like a chorus of lost souls, whipping the sails into frenzy, and the deck pitches beneath your boots as if the ocean itself is trying to swallow you whole.

"Steady now!" you bellow, your voice cutting through the chaos like a lighthouse beam. The crew—ragged faces etched with fear—clings to the rails, their eyes wide and pleading. You've sailed these waters in waking life, but this is no ordinary gale; the clouds swirl with colors that don't belong in any sky, electric blues and fiery reds bleeding into the waves below.

You grip the wheel, feeling an unnatural pull, as if the sea whispers secrets only you can hear. "Reef the mainsail! Secure the lines!" Orders fly from your lips, and the men scramble, their movements a blur against the lightning-streaked horizon. But deep down, you know this storm isn't just weather—it's a dream's rebellion, the subconscious tide rising to test your resolve.

As the ship crests a monstrous wave, you spot it: a rift in the water, glowing with an otherworldly light, like a portal to forgotten depths. The crew gasps, but you laugh—a wild, defiant sound— and steer straight for it. "Hold fast! We're riding this beast home!"

The world tilts, the storm roars its final protest, and suddenly... calm. The ship glides into still waters under a starlit sky, the crew cheering as ethereal fish leap alongside, their scales shimmering like jewels. You wake with the taste of salt on your lips, the wheel still warm in your hands, wondering if the sea will call you back tonight.
