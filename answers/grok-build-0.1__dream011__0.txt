You notice the questions are written in your own handwriting, but from years ago. The paper feels warm, like it's breathing.

The first question reads: "Why did you stop drawing?" It has no multiple choice options, only a blank space that keeps expanding the longer you stare at it. The second question is simply: "What did you promise yourself at 17 that you still haven't done?" followed by a small, almost apologetic note in the margin: *This one is worth 40%.*

When you turn the page, the questions start moving. They rearrange themselves into a map of roads you never took. One question detaches completely and crawls up your arm like an insect before settling on your wrist, where it becomes a tattoo: "Who were you supposed to become?"

The proctor, who now has your father's face but your mother's voice, leans down and whispers, "You only have until the bell rings to decide which answer is real." The clock on the wall has no hands. Outside the window, you can see your future self walking away down a hallway that shouldn't exist.

You realize you never actually studied for this. You were never even enrolled. You just showed up because someone told you it was important.
