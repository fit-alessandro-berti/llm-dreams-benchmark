You are given a mysterious locked box with no key in sight. As you examine it closely, you notice intricate patterns carved into its surface—swirling lines that seem to shift whenever you look away. The wood feels warm, almost alive, and faintly hums beneath your fingertips.

Curious, you trace one of the patterns. Instantly, the humming grows louder.

The world around you falls silent.

The trees, the sky, even the distant sounds you hadn't noticed before all fade into a strange stillness. Only the box remains vivid. One of the carved lines begins to glow with a soft silver light, followed by another, and another, until the entire box shines like a lantern in the darkness.

A whisper reaches your ears.

Not words exactly, but a feeling—a sense that the box is waiting for something.

You turn it over and discover that there is no keyhole at all.

Instead, there is a smooth circular indentation. Without knowing why, you place your palm against it.

The box clicks.

The lid slowly opens.

Inside, you expect treasure, or perhaps some ancient secret. Instead, you find a tiny glass sphere floating in empty space. Within the sphere is a miniature version of the place where you stand. Tiny trees sway in a tiny wind. Tiny clouds drift overhead.

Then the miniature you looks up.

Directly at you.

The tiny figure raises a hand.

You raise yours in return.

At that exact moment, the miniature world expands outward like a wave. The sphere grows larger and larger until it surrounds you completely. Suddenly you are standing inside it, while the original world shrinks into a distant point of light.

The tiny figure is now life-sized.

It smiles.

"At last," it says. "You found the way in."

Before you can ask what that means, the ground beneath your feet becomes a path of glowing stars stretching toward a horizon filled with impossible floating mountains. Somewhere far ahead, a golden door slowly begins to open.

You take your first step toward it—

—and wake up, with the strange certainty that the dream was not finished.
