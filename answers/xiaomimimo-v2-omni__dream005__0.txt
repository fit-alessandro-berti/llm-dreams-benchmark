*The lights are soft but everywhere at once, like a golden fog. You push through laughter that echoes like wind chimes. The single person you know—let’s call them Alex—had been right beside you moments ago, holding a drink, smiling. Then the crowd swirled like a slow river, and suddenly you were alone.*

You drift past a group discussing philosophy in hushed, urgent tones, their faces lit by phones that glow like tiny moons. One of them turns to you, eyes bright. “Do you believe memories are carried in the blood?” they ask. Before you can answer, the scene dissolves.

Now you’re near a grand piano that no one is playing. A child sits on the bench, pressing silent keys. “The songs are stuck inside,” the child whispers. You nod, feeling a strange weight in your own chest, like unplayed melodies.

Ahead, a circle of friends are sharing a single bowl of something that steams with the scent of cinnamon and old books. They offer you a spoon, but when you reach for it, your hand passes through. They don’t seem to notice.

You turn a corner—where did the corner come from?—and see Alex’s back, their familiar jacket. You call out, but your voice makes no sound. You hurry forward, but the floor stretches like taffy. When you finally reach them, they turn, and it’s not Alex. It’s someone with eyes like calm lakes, who says, “You’re looking for something you already have.”

Confused, you touch your own heart. It’s humming.

Then, through a window that wasn’t there before, you see Alex outside in a quiet garden, looking up at the sky as if waiting for rain. You push through the glass—it yields like water—and step into cool air. The noise of the gathering fades to a distant murmur.

Alex turns, smiles as if you’d only stepped away for a second. “Find anything interesting?” they ask.

You look back at the window, now just a wall. The dream softens at the edges. “Just… echoes,” you say.

Alex hands you a glass of water. It’s real. The garden is real. For now, that’s enough.

*And the dream continues, quiet and deep.*
