You reach for one, and as your fingers brush its bristles, the canvas pulses—a low, resonant hum that vibrates through the ground. The landscape around you freezes mid-change: a tree that was turning from blue to violet stops, its leaves now a gradient of motionless twilight.

You lift the brush and touch it to the blank surface. At once, a stroke of indigo bleeds outward, but instead of staying on the canvas, it flows into the air, wrapping around your arm like a silk ribbon. The world reacts: the frozen tree shatters into a thousand hummingbirds, each one a different shade of the stroke. They swirl around you, singing notes that taste like honey and static.

Now the canvas is no longer blank. It shows a mirror image of you, but your reflection is made of living paint—it blinks, steps forward, and offers you a second brush. "Choose your palette," it whispers, though its lips do not move. Around you, the landscape begins to warp: the ground becomes a checkerboard of sound, the sky twists into a spiral of laughter.

You realize you can paint not just the canvas, but the dream itself. Every decision reshapes the world. What color will you mix next?
