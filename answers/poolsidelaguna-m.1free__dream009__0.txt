
You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to **learn the local language just to survive**.  

The air smells of woodsmoke and wild herbs. You’re lying in a hayloft, sunlight filtering through cracks in a timbered ceiling. Below, voices chatter in a dialect you’ve only read about in history books—medieval English or perhaps Old Norse. Your phone is gone, your clothes are bizarrely anachronistic, and the locals eye you with a mix of suspicion and curiosity.  

You stumble into the village square, where a blacksmith’s hammer rings like thunder. A child points at your sneakers, and an elder mutters something about "witchcraft." Desperate to communicate, you mimic the blacksmith’s gestures, offering to help tend the forge. He squints at you but nods, and as you work, you begin to pick up words—*brand* (fire), *stĺl* (steel)—and realize the villagers speak a dialect that’s a blend of Old Norse and Anglo-Saxon.  

Days pass. You learn to grind grain, mend fishing nets, and navigate by the stars. At night, you scribble symbols in the dirt—a cipher combining modern math and ancient runes—to document your discoveries. But you also notice peculiar things: a scar shaped like a lightning bolt on the village chieftain’s hand, and whispers of a "sky-fire" that struck the forest decades ago.  

One evening, an old crone drags you to a moss-covered monastery. Inside, you find a manuscript written in Latin, its pages detailing a "portal between worlds" sealed by an alchemist centuries ago. It speaks of a "dreamer" who must *choose* to remain or return. The text ends abruptly, the ink fading to blank parchment.  

You glance at your hands. The scar on your wrist—a mark you’ve had since birth—now glows faintly in the candlelight. The crone grins, her teeth blackened with age. "The gateway opens again," she hisses. "But the village will die if you leave. The curse—it feeds on your doubt."  

You wake the next morning with dirt under your nails and the taste of woodsmoke in your mouth. The manuscript is gone, but the scar remains. Somewhere in your pocket, a single ancient coin clinks—a coin that shouldn’t exist in this time. Or perhaps any time.
