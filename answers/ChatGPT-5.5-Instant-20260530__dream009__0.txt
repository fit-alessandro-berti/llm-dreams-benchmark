…figure out not only *where* you are, but *when* you are.

The air smells of wood smoke and damp earth. A dirt road winds between small houses with thatched roofs, and people stare at your clothes as if you've stepped out of a fairy tale. When you ask what year it is, nobody understands the question. Instead, an old woman points toward a distant hill where a bell tower rises above the morning mist.

Feeling both anxious and curious, you begin walking.

Along the way, you notice details that seem strangely familiar: symbols carved into stones, a river that follows a path you somehow recognize, and a market square built where you vaguely remember a modern building should stand. It's as if you've been dropped into the past of a place you once knew.

At the bell tower, a monk is carefully copying pages into a large book. He looks up as you enter.

"I've been expecting you," he says.

You laugh nervously. "That's impossible."

He slides a yellowed page across the table. Drawn on it is a sketch of your face.

Below it are words written in a language you somehow understand:

*"The traveler who arrives from tomorrow must choose: return home unchanged, or stay and change history."*

Before you can ask a question, the tower bell rings. The sound echoes across the countryside, and suddenly the scene around you flickers like a reflection in disturbed water.

Outside, people freeze in place.

The wind stops.

Even the clouds stop moving.

Only you and the monk remain.

He points toward a narrow door hidden behind a bookshelf. Beyond it, a staircase spirals downward into darkness.

"Your answer is waiting below," he says.

You descend.

At the bottom, you find a chamber illuminated by a pale blue light—the only thing that feels remotely modern. In the center stands a device made of brass, crystal, and gears, humming softly. Floating above it is a map showing centuries unfolding like pages in a book.

As you approach, the map shifts and reveals two futures.

In one, you return to your own time. Your life continues exactly as you remember it.

In the other, you remain here. Small actions ripple outward: a conversation, a warning, a forgotten idea shared with the right person. The world changes in countless ways, and generations later, someone looks up at the stars because of a choice you made.

The device begins to glow brighter.

A voice emerges from nowhere and everywhere at once:

*"Time is not asking who you are. It is asking who you will be."*

You reach toward the map—

—and wake up, your hand extended toward the ceiling, with the faint sound of a distant bell still ringing in your ears.
