You wake up in a completely different era, with no modern technology around. The air smells of wood‑smoke and fresh‑baked bread, and the sound of a distant blacksmith’s hammer rings against the cobblestones like a heart‑beat. You blink, feeling the rough‑spun tunic against your skin and the weight of a leather satchel slung across your chest. There are no streetlights, no honking cars—just the low murmur of a bustling market, the clatter of hooves on stone, and the occasional cry of a town‑crier announcing news you can’t quite understand.

As you explore your new surroundings, you realize you need to find a place to stay and a way to secure food before nightfall, when the streets will turn dark and the strangers who seem friendly enough might become less so. The narrow alleyways wind between thatched‑roof cottages and stone‑walled taverns, each doorway a mystery. You spot a weathered signpost with a faded pictogram of a loaf of bread, and you follow it toward a modest inn that looks like it might offer a warm hearth and a bunk for the night.

Inside, the innkeeper—a stout woman with a linen apron stained with soot—eyes you with a mixture of curiosity and suspicion. You fumble for a few copper coins from the satchel, the only currency you have, and she nods, sliding a heavy oak key across the counter. “Room three, top of the stairs,” she says, her voice rough but not unkind. “Don’t stay out after the curfew bell rings. The night watch won’t be kind to strangers wandering the streets.”

You climb the creaking wooden steps, feeling each board protest under your weight, and push open the heavy door to a small chamber. A straw‑filled mattress lies on a low platform, a rough blanket folded at its foot, and a tiny window frames a sliver of the amber sky. You set down your satchel, hearing the clink of a few more coins you didn’t know you had, and stare out at the market below. Children chase each other between stalls, a goat bleats, and somewhere a lute twangs a melancholy tune.

Your mind races. The day is slipping away, and the evening will bring a new set of challenges. You need to find a way to blend in, perhaps by taking on a simple job—helping the baker with the morning’s bread, assisting the blacksmith with the forge, or offering your services to the traveling merchant who is loading his cart with wares. Each task could earn you a few more coins, a meal, and perhaps a guide who knows the lay of the land well enough to point you toward a more permanent shelter or a road that leads back to your own time.

You glance at the leather satchel, noticing a faint glimmer at the bottom—something you didn’t notice before. A small, tarnished brass compass sits nestled beside a folded piece of parchment, its edges frayed and inked with strange symbols you can’t read. The compass needle spins lazily, then steadies, pointing toward the east, where the sun is already beginning to dip behind the hills. On the parchment, a rough sketch shows a winding river, a bridge, and a distant tower crowned with a lantern.

You realize this could be a clue—an invitation to follow a path that might lead you to a place of power, a nexus where the currents of time are thin enough to let you slip back to the present. Yet the inn’s bell tolls, a deep, resonant note that echoes through the stone walls, signaling the start of curfew. You must decide quickly: will you stay hidden in this modest room, eat the stale bread left for you, and wait for morning to seek work and answers? Or will you slip out into the lantern‑lit streets, following the compass toward the river and the mysterious tower, risking the night watch’s scrutiny in hopes of uncovering the secret that brought you here?

The dream pulls you forward, the choices ahead as tangled as the cobblestones beneath your feet, and the whisper of the unknown beckons you to step beyond the safety of the inn and into the pulse of this forgotten age. The night is coming, and with it, the promise of adventure—or peril. The choice is yours, and the compass, the parchment, and the call of the distant tower are already waiting for you to decide which path to take.
