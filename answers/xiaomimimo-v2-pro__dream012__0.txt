"...steady the wheel with both hands. The wood groans beneath your grip, salt spray stinging your eyes. Lightning splits the sky, illuminating faces—pale, frightened, but watching you.

You don’t shout over the wind. You don’t give grand orders. Instead, you lean toward the first mate, your voice low but sure: *’Reef the main sail. Lash everything down. We don’t fight the storm—we move with it, find the eye.’*

The crew doesn’t cheer. They simply turn, hands moving with renewed purpose. You feel it—not control, but *trust*. The ship pitches, but you hold the line, guiding her toward the leeward side of a breaking wave.

And just as the mast creaks like it might snap, the rain eases for a moment. In that brief hush, you see it: a patch of darker water ahead, where the waves roll steadier. The crew sees it too.

You breathe out. *’Steady as she goes,’* you murmur, and for the first time since the storm began, the ship finds her rhythm. Not calm, but a rhythm. And you think—sometimes the captain isn’t the one who commands the storm, but the one who helps others remember they can sail through it.”

*The dream softens. The roar of the sea fades to a distant hush, and you wake with the quiet certainty that even in the worst gales, there’s always a way to hold the course.*
