You can hear your own voice echoing from somewhere ahead, explaining to someone that you "just need five more minutes."

You glance down and notice your hands are now made of paper—covered in the same impossible deadline in red ink. When you try to type, the words you produce are in a language you've never seen, but the rabbit seems to understand. It nods approvingly, then transforms into your childhood bicycle. 

The boss-child claps his hands. "That's the spirit!" he says. The bicycle pedals itself forward, carrying you straight through the floor into a version of the office where everything is underwater. Fish in ties swim past, carrying briefcases. You open your mouth to protest that none of this makes sense, and instead a school of tiny, glowing deadlines swims out.

Just as the panic peaks, your alarm clock (which has somehow followed you here) rings from inside a filing cabinet. The entire scene shivers, like someone shaking a snow globe. You wake up with your heart racing and the faint taste of ink in your mouth, the real deadline still waiting for you tomorrow.
