Despite the hardships, you decide to build a shelter beneath a massive pile of rusted metal and old bones, using nothing but your own hands and memories to guide the wood. You find yourself surrounded by silent, watching shadows where once there were stars, and you notice that even in the dark, the moon is still visible through the broken glass.

As you begin to gather the last piece of a broken radio, you hear the faint hum of a wind chime outside, reminding you that nature does not forget how the world worked before the fall. You decide to keep digging into the rubble until something emerges like a ghost from the earth—a single seed of green life growing in the concrete.

As dawn breaks over the ruin, you carry this small piece of hope inside your backpack, knowing that you can still wake up tomorrow and plant a tree where no one else could. You are dreaming now because it is possible to keep living when everyone else has forgotten what you were once before.
