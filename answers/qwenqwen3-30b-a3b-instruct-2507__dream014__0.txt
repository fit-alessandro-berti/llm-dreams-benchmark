You are feeling lost and alone in a bustling city. Suddenly, a group of kind strangers offers to help you find your way, and you...  
...are swept into a shared rhythm of the city. Not with words, but with gestures. An elderly woman hands you a steaming paper cup of tea that smells like cinnamon and forgotten memories. A teenager with paint-splattered jeans slides a hand-drawn map into your palm—its lines pulsing faintly, like veins. A man with a worn leather satchel hums a tune you’ve never heard but feel you’ve known your whole life, and suddenly, the cacophony of traffic and sirens softens into a gentle, lilting melody.  

You start walking—not toward a destination, but *with* them. The streets shift. Buildings bloom with ivy and glowing lanterns that weren’t there before. The sky, once a bruised gray, stains gold at the edges like a watercolor bleeding into itself.  

And then—just as you wonder if this is real—you notice the map shows your own childhood home… but it’s on a street you’ve never seen, in a neighborhood that shouldn’t exist.  

The strangers don’t speak. But you understand: they’re not leading you home.  
They’re leading you toward *yourself*—the part of you that remembers magic, that still believes in kindness without reason.  

You follow, the warmth of the tea still cradled in your hands, the hum still echoing in your bones.  
And for the first time since you entered this city… you don’t feel lost at all.  

You feel found.
