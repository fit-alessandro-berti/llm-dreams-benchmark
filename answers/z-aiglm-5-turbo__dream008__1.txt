You try to tap the painted letters anyway, hoping sheer willpower will make it work. When you look up at the monitor, the screen isn't showing your desktop; it’s a live feed of a maze made of cubicle walls, and you realize with a jolt of dream-logic that *you* are the one trapped inside it, running endlessly down carpeted corridors.

The fluorescent lights above begin to hum louder and louder, the sound swelling until it feels like a swarm of bees is hovering just inches from your ears. The deadline—the impossible, ridiculous deadline—begins to physically manifest in the room. A giant digital clock appears on the wall, but the numbers are melting, dripping red wax down onto the carpet. 

You turn around to beg for help, but your boss is gone. In their place is your high school math teacher, holding a giant pair of scissors and staring at you expectantly. 

Panic sets in—that heavy, suffocating dream-paralysis where you need to run but your legs are filled with wet concrete. You try to scream that you can't do it, that it's impossible, that you don't even know what the project is, but instead of a scream, you let out a sudden, violent sneeze so powerful it propels you backward...

And you jerk awake in your bed, heart hammering against your ribs, the phantom feeling of melted numbers and spaghetti fingers lingering just long enough for you to let out a massive sigh of relief before reaching over to check the time on your real phone.
