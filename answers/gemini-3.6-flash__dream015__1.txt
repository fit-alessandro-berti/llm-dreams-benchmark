...decide not to build a fortress of iron and wire, but to construct a sanctuary of glass and forgotten light.

I climb the rusted spine of a fallen metropolis, my footsteps making no sound against the shattered pavement. The sky above is a bruised violet, veined with low-hanging storm clouds that hum with static electricity. Down in the shadows, the harsh realities of the wasteland prowl, but up here, the air feels thin, almost weightless—the unmistakable logic of a dream.

In my canvas sack, I am carrying the most valuable treasure left in this broken world: a dozen cracked glass jars filled with dark, rich soil, and a handful of luminescent seeds I found deep in the belly of a dead subterranean vault.

When I reach the exposed roof of the highest skyscraper, overlooking a sea of silent, overgrown ruins, I begin my work. I lay out the shards of mirrored glass I’ve scavenged, arranging them to catch the weak, dying rays of the sun. I pour my last drops of clean water into the dirt, press the glowing seeds beneath the surface, and wait.

Time in the dream moves like watercolor on wet paper. 

Within seconds, the soil warms. Sprouting from the jars are not ordinary plants, but delicate, vine-like structures made of spun emerald and gold that breathe a faint, pulse-like light into the dusk. They climb the rusted steel beams, weaving a canopy of soft, ambient warmth. The howling, toxic wind drops to a whisper, filtered through the blooming glass leaves.

Suddenly, a sound echoes across the silent rooftops—not the roar of a threat, but the soft, clear chime of a bell. 

I look toward the horizon. On the roof of a neighboring tower, miles away, a solitary figure lights a fire in response to mine. Then another light flickers on a distant hill, and another. 

Standing in the center of my impossible garden, high above the danger, I realize we weren't just surviving the end of the world—we were waiting for someone to give it a reason to start again.

And as the glow of the garden grows brighter, the edges of the ruined city slowly melt into soft, golden light, lifting me gently toward the surface of waking.
