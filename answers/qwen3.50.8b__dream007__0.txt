The air in the living room didn't feel right anymore. I felt the heat rising on my forehead as my friend tried to explain why they had never argued about this. She leaned forward, her face inches from mine.

"We have a deal," she said, her voice low and firm.

"Okay," I replied, tapping my chin against the table. "Deal."

"I need to be sure," I said to myself as I stood up and walked away from her. "Make sure you understand that we are discussing something serious."

"And what is it?" she asked, standing between us in a position of power. Her eyes seemed fixed on me with intensity.

"I know," I admitted, looking at the floor where the dust was accumulating. "But we don't need to be careful about who tells the truth."

She stopped and looked at her phone screen. She closed it. "I'm done arguing with you."

"It doesn't matter what they say if it's true," I told myself. "We have a decision to make."

I walked forward, taking my time not to sound more aggressive but just as steady as she was. "You need to think about what you said before making this call. Make sure you know how to react if the other side argues back."

She looked at me with a mix of curiosity and annoyance on her face. "I thought we could discuss it more formally," she said.

"I think that's okay," I agreed, stepping closer until I was near the window. "But don't let anyone stop you from speaking. Just let them know that this is an important topic we both want to discuss."

She nodded and sat down again in her chair. As I watched her leave, I knew the conversation had ended with a decision made within our minds. The question was now clear: what was true for us? What did we have to believe regarding each other or the situation in front of us? And then... what should I do next?

I stood up and walked back across the room. The silence stretched out, but my heart knew that this was just another part of the story. We were a dream together.
