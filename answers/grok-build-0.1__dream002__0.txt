You feel a storm approaching and the air turns electric, heavy with the scent of ozone and wet earth. The sky, once a soft canopy of green-filtered light, darkens to an unnatural bruised purple. Wind howls through the trees like a living thing, tearing leaves from branches and sending them spiraling around you in chaotic dances.

You start to run, heart pounding, but the forest seems to shift around you. Paths that were clear moments ago vanish behind walls of rain that fall sideways, stinging your skin. Lightning flashes, and in the brief illumination you see the trees aren't just trees anymore—their bark is moving, etched with faint glowing symbols that pulse in time with the thunder. One ancient oak reaches a branch toward you like a hand, offering shelter, but when you take it, the wood feels warm and strangely familiar, like touching a memory.

The storm peaks in a deafening roar. Rain turns to something else—thousands of tiny, whispering fragments that aren't water at all, but half-formed thoughts and forgotten names. They press against your mind: questions you never asked, possibilities you never considered. In the chaos, a single clear voice cuts through the noise, speaking directly from the wind: *"What are you really looking for out here?"*

Then, as suddenly as it began, the storm breaks. The clouds tear open to reveal a sky full of unfamiliar stars, and the forest floor is now covered in soft moss that glows with bioluminescent patterns forming a map. You follow it to a small clearing where a single, perfect tree stands. Hanging from one low branch is an old key, warm to the touch. When you take it, the entire dream shivers, and you feel yourself beginning to wake—but not before the key dissolves into light that floods your vision with a single, vivid image: yourself, standing exactly where you are now, smiling as if you've just remembered something important.

You jolt awake in your bed, the sound of distant thunder still echoing in your ears, and the faint taste of rain on your tongue.
