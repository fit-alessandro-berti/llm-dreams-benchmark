You start to panic, your legs pumping but moving as if through thick molasses while the skyscrapers around you begin to sway and rearrange like living origami. You fumble for your phone to check a map, but it dissolves into a handful of old train tickets and colorful feathers that scatter in a sudden wind. Strangers blur past with clock faces ticking at impossible speeds, and when you beg one for directions, they lean in—revealing the face of your childhood self—and whisper, "The meeting is where the forgotten things wait." The streets fold upward into impossible loops, carrying you toward a door that looks exactly like the one from your old family home. You burst through just as the hour strikes... only to find a room full of mirrors showing every version of you, all seated and waiting. The "important meeting" was never about work at all. 

Then your alarm clock rings, pulling you awake in your real bed, heart still racing, with the strange sense that you might have actually arrived on time after all.
