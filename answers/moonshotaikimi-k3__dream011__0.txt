...written in a language you almost recognize. The letters look familiar—an A here, an E there—but they're arranged in combinations that dissolve the moment you try to read them, like words glimpsed through water.

You look around. Everyone else is writing furiously, pens scratching with terrible confidence. The student next to you has already filled three pages. You raise your hand to ask the proctor for help, but when she turns around, she's your third-grade teacher, and she says gently, "You were told about this. We covered it in the unit you missed."

You flip through the exam booklet, searching for one question—just one—that makes sense. Question 7 asks you to *prove the sound of the color blue*. Question 12 is just a drawing of a door. Question 15 is written in your own handwriting, and it says: *You already know the answer.*

The clock on the wall is melting, but somehow it's also moving twice as fast. You look down at your answer sheet and realize with horror that you've been writing the whole time without knowing it—your hand has filled page after page in that same unreadable language, and you can't make it stop.

The bell rings. The proctor collects your exam and smiles. "We'll see," she says, "if this changes anything."

And then you're standing outside, and it's snowing indoors somehow, and you can't remember what career the exam was even for—but you know with absolute certainty that you wrote everything perfectly in a language you've never learned, and somewhere, someone is grading it right now.
