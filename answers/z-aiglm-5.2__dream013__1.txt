...you notice the walls begin to shift. Not physically — not exactly — but in the way dreams shift, where stone becomes velvet becomes water becomes stone again, and you can never quite catch the moment of transformation.

The puzzles grow stranger. One room contains nothing but a single clock running backward, and you realize you must wait for it to reach midnight *before it was ever built* to unlock the door. Another chamber is filled with mirrors that reflect a version of you making different choices — and the solution requires you to mimic the reflection that looks most surprised by its own existence.

You solve them. Each one costs you something — a memory, perhaps, or a name you once knew. The maze takes payment in small forgetfulnesses. By the time you reach what feels like the fifth chamber, you can no longer remember who told you about the maze, or whether you entered willingly.

Then you come to a door that has no lock, no riddle, no mechanism.

It simply says: **"What have you learned that you didn't mean to?"**

You stand there. The silence behind you is total — the maze itself seems to be listening. And somewhere deeper in, past the door, you hear what sounds like your own voice, patiently explaining something to someone you cannot see.

Your hand hovers over the handle.

The dream holds its breath.
