I try to ask them where my friend is, but when I open my mouth, my voice doesn't make a sound—instead, a flurry of iridescent paper butterflies escapes my lips, fluttering uselessly toward the towering, crystal chandeliers.

I push through the crowd, but the more I move, the more the geometry of the room begins to fail. The polished marble floor beneath my feet softens, turning into a carpet of damp, fragrant moss. The frantic orchestral music, which felt so loud moments ago, stretches and slows until it sounds like the deep, rhythmic pulsing of an ocean tide.

Suddenly, I see a flash of a familiar coat—the exact shade of blue my friend always wears—disappearing behind a heavy velvet curtain. I lunged toward it, but as I pull the fabric aside, I don't find a hallway or a room. Instead, I am standing on the edge of a vast, silent library where the books are floating in mid-air like sleeping birds.

I call out their name, and for a second, the world holds its breath. A figure turns around at the end of a long aisle of floating scrolls. My heart leaps, thinking I’ve finally found them, but as they step into the light, I realize they have no face—only a smooth, silver surface that reflects my own wide-eyed, searching expression.

The library begins to tilt. The books start to flap their covers, creating a wind that pulls me upward. I feel myself losing my grip on the ground, floating toward a ceiling that has dissolved into a swirling violet nebula, and just as the weightlessness becomes unbearable, the world shatters into light, and I wake up.
