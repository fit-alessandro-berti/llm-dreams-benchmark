• You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships,
  you decide to keep moving.

  You strip the last usable bolt from a broken irrigation pump and use it to lash together a spear and wrench, then
  follow the dry riverbed toward a faint plume of smoke on the horizon—old habit says people mean danger, but in this
  world, fire is also the last sign of life. Along the way, you barter a pair of clean batteries and a silver spoon for
  a canteen, a map torn from a school atlas, and one warning: the outer districts are crawling with raiders, and the
  checkpoints are gone. By dusk, you reach a collapsed parking garage where a hand-painted sign still reads “WATER: WEST
  WING.” The doors are trapped, but you hear voices. You can either enter and risk discovery, or circle the building and
  try your luck at dawn. You pause, check your breath, and choose to knock. Because in this world, the first rule for
  surviving isn’t fear—it’s controlled courage.
