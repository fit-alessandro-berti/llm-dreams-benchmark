You gratefully accept their offer, and as their hands gently guide you through the thrumming crowd, the city begins to soften around the edges—like watercolor bleeding into new shapes. The strangers’ faces flicker with familiar warmth: one has your grandmother’s laugh, another carries the quiet steadiness of an old friend you haven’t seen in years, and the smallest one holds a lantern made of starlight that never flickers.

They don’t ask where you’re going. Instead they walk beside you, pointing out hidden doorways in alley walls that open into meadows floating above the traffic, cafés where the coffee tastes like childhood summers, and a park bench where time pauses so you can finally cry the tears you’ve been holding since you arrived in this strange place.

The noise of the city fades into a lullaby of distant chimes. You realize you’re no longer lost—you’re simply between chapters. One of the strangers (the one with kind, crinkled eyes) leans close and whispers, “You’ve always known the way. We were only here to remind you.”

The group slowly dissolves into golden light, each one leaving behind a single feather that drifts into your pocket. You stand at the edge of a quiet square you somehow recognize as the center of everything. The city around you is still bustling, but now it feels like home.

You wake with the feather still in your hand and a smile that lingers long after the dream has faded.
