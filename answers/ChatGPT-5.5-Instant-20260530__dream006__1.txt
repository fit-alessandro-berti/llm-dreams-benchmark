...speak, but no sound comes out.

A ripple of confusion moves through the audience. You clear your throat and try again. Silence. Yet somehow, everyone seems to understand you anyway. Faces in the crowd nod as if they can hear words that never leave your lips.

You glance down and realize the stage is slowly transforming beneath your feet. The wooden floor becomes a giant map covered in glowing paths. Each person in the audience stands at the end of one of those paths, and every path leads back to you.

Unsure what else to do, you step onto a glowing line. Instantly, a memory appears in the air before you: a childhood afternoon, a forgotten conversation, a moment of triumph, a moment of regret. The audience watches silently. As you move from path to path, more memories emerge, floating like lanterns above the crowd.

Then you notice something strange. Some of the faces in the audience are people you know. Others are older or younger versions of yourself. A few wear masks that shift constantly, becoming teachers, friends, strangers, and family members all at once.

A voice echoes from somewhere beyond the theater.

"The speech was never about speaking."

The ceiling dissolves into a night sky filled with enormous stars. Each star contains a scene from your life. One by one, they drift downward until they hover just above the audience's heads.

You suddenly know what you are supposed to do.

Instead of talking, you reach up and touch a star. It bursts into thousands of glowing words that swirl through the air like birds. The audience rises from their seats and catches the words in their hands. Every person receives a different message. Some laugh. Some cry. Some smile with relief.

The theater begins to shake. The stars accelerate, circling faster and faster until they form a bright spiral overhead. At its center appears a single empty chair illuminated by golden light.

The mysterious voice speaks again.

"Take your place."

You walk toward the chair. With each step, the audience fades away, the stage disappears, and the theater stretches into infinity. When you finally sit down, the spiral of stars collapses into a single point of light.

The light drifts closer until it rests in the palm of your hand.

Inside it, you see another stage, another audience, and another version of yourself preparing to give the same speech.

Just as that version of you opens their mouth to begin, you wake up, carrying the strange certainty that the speech is still being delivered somewhere, in some dream beyond your own.
