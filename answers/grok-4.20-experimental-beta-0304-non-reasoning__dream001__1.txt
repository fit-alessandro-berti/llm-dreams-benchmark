You start to feel that familiar dream-anxiety rising in your chest, the kind that tastes like copper and makes your legs feel strangely heavy. The city around you is a shifting collage of places you've half-remembered: narrow cobblestone streets from Prague melting into the glass canyons of Tokyo, then suddenly opening into wide boulevards that look like they belong in a forgotten version of Paris.

People hurry past you, but their faces are blurred, like someone forgot to render them properly. You reach into your pocket for your phone and pull out... a small, ornate brass key instead. It feels warm, almost alive.

A woman in a red coat (her face is the only clear one) pauses beside you and says, without looking at you: "The meeting isn't where you think it is. It's in the place you left behind."

She points down an alley that definitely wasn't there a moment ago. At the end of it, you can see the front door of your childhood home, impossibly small and glowing softly, as if lit from within by afternoon sunlight.

Your heart is pounding. You have forty-three minutes left according to the pocket watch that just appeared in your other hand. The hands are moving backwards.

You take one step toward the alley, and the entire city seems to inhale, waiting to see what you'll do next.
