I weave through a cluster of guests standing around a grand fireplace that emits a cold, silver light instead of heat. They are holding delicate teacups filled with swirling, miniature galaxies instead of tea. I ask if they’ve seen the person I’m looking for—the one wearing a familiar, faded indigo coat. Without speaking, three of them point in entirely different directions, their arms elongating unnaturally for a moment before snapping back to normal.

I follow the direction of the middle finger down a hallway that shouldn't exist in a normal house. The moment I step into it, the floor transitions from polished wood to soft, damp moss. The wallpaper features a pattern of eyes that slowly blink as I pass, and the walls themselves seem to breathe, expanding and contracting with a slow, rhythmic pulse.

I stumble through a set of double doors into an impossibly vast, cathedral-like ballroom. Here, a group of figures with faces obscured by smooth, featureless porcelain masks are meticulously peeling oranges. But as the rinds come away, they transform into luminous blue butterflies that flutter up into a vaulted ceiling made entirely of a starry night sky. 

I try to push past them, but my limbs suddenly feel incredibly heavy, as if I am wading through deep, invisible water. My mouth opens to call out my friend's name, but only a faint whisper of radio static emerges. 

Then, I see it. A flash of indigo near a grand, arched window at the far end of the room.

I force my heavy legs to move. The floor stretches like warm taffy, making every step a monumental effort, yet the distance between us somehow begins to close. I finally reach the window, my heart hammering against my ribs with a strange mix of relief and dread. 

The person in the indigo coat turns around. 

But they don't have a face. Instead, where their features should be, there is a perfectly smooth, silver mirror. I lean in, expecting to see my own bewildered expression. Instead, the mirror reflects the room I am currently standing in, but it is completely empty, bathed in the soft, gray, quiet light of early morning.

The mirrored figure raises a hand and presses an index finger to where its lips would be in a gentle "shh" gesture. The silver surface ripples like a disturbed pond, and the entire world begins to dissolve into a warm, humming white noise, pulling me gently and inevitably toward the edge of waking up.
