You are dreaming.

You are at a large social gathering where you only know one person. As you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people and...

...the first cluster is gathered around a long table that seems to stretch into infinity, covered in glowing chessboards where the pieces are arguing with each other in tiny, squeaky voices. A woman with three noses (all of them smiling) waves you over and insists you take her place because “the black queen keeps cheating with time travel.” You politely decline, but she hands you a glowing pawn anyway; it feels warm and alive in your palm, like holding a small heartbeat.

The next group is dancing in slow motion on a floor made of shifting clouds. Everyone is wearing formal evening wear except for the person in the center, who is dressed as a giant pineapple and keeps shouting, “I am the true fruit of destiny!” They beckon you to join the conga line, but the line keeps looping back on itself, so you never actually move forward. One dancer whispers that your friend was here “three dreams ago” and left a message written in condensation on the ceiling: *Look up, then look left, then trust the elevator that doesn’t exist.*

You push through a curtain of hanging beads that taste like strawberry when you brush past them and stumble into a quieter circle of people sitting cross-legged on enormous beanbags shaped like sleeping cats. They’re all speaking in perfect unison about the weather on a planet none of them have ever visited. One of them—whose face keeps gently morphing between your childhood best friend and a friendly owl—leans in and says, “Your person is on the balcony that only appears when you stop looking for it.”

You thank them, turn around… and suddenly you’re on that balcony. The night air smells like old books and fresh rain. There, leaning against the railing with their back to you, is the one person you know. They turn, smile that familiar smile, and say, “Took you long enough. I saved you the last slice of the dream cake.”

They hand you a glowing piece of cake that tastes exactly like every happy memory you’ve ever had at once. The two of you stand there watching the party below turn into a slow river of light, people laughing and floating upward like fireflies. Your friend nudges you gently.

“Ready to wake up?” they ask.

You nod, but not yet. Not quite yet.

The dream lingers a little longer, warm and kind, before it lets you go.
