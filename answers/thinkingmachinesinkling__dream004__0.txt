...the moment your fingers brush its surface, the blankness shivers.

It isn’t empty—it’s *listening*. Around you, the landscape’s shifting colors hesitate, then begin to flow toward the canvas like rivers reversing course. A cobalt spiral that had been dancing in the sky dips down and bleeds into the upper left corner. A shape like a melting clock, but made of singing glass, presses itself into the center, leaving a trail of silver echoes.

You reach out to touch it, and your hand doesn’t leave a mark. Instead, the canvas pulls *you* into its weave. Suddenly you are both viewer and pigment—your thoughts become strokes of crimson and teal, your breath the white space between forms. The frame expands, or perhaps you shrink; the edges dissolve into the surrounding dream, and you realize there was never a boundary between the painting and the world.

You are the blank canvas now, constantly being rewritten by the dream itself—color without name, shape without fixed meaning, floating in a gallery that has no walls and no waking.
