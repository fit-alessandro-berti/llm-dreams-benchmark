...the rules keep changing while you are still playing.

The first challenge is an interview, but the panel is made of clock faces that melt when you answer correctly. You must build a bridge out of your own forgotten passwords to reach the second round, where you are asked to solve a puzzle that is also your childhood bedroom, rearranged by strangers.

Other competitors appear—some are versions of you at different ages, others are made of paper or static. You realize the "dream job" isn't a specific title or office, but a state of being: the permission to never stop becoming.

In the final round, the prize is handed to you. It is a key made of light, but when you grasp it, you understand you have been the competition all along—the obstacles were just your own potential, dressed up as fear. You wake up not with the job in hand, but with the certainty that the work was never separate from the dream itself.
