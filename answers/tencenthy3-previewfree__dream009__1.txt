...**blend in before someone realizes you don't belong.**

The air smells of woodsmoke and damp earth—sharp, heavy, and utterly foreign to your modern lungs. You push yourself up from the dirt, finding your clothes have transformed into rough-spun wool and leather, scratchy against your skin. Above you, thatched cottages lean against one another, their windows glowing with the flicker of candlelight rather than LEDs.

**You look down at your hands.** They are calloused and marked with ink stains—you are no longer a visitor; you *are* a scribe.

Panic rises in your throat, but the sound of heavy boots on the cobblestones forces it down. A Town Crier stomps past, ringing a brass bell and shouting about a royal decree. You realize with a jolt that you are in the heart of **17th-century London**, and the streets are teeming with merchants, rats, and the murmur of a population that doesn't take kindly to strangers asking questions.

**Your mission for this dream sequence:**

1.  **The Masquerade:** You spot a pile of discarded laundry near a washbasin. You must quickly swap your modern sneakers for leather boots and hide your synthetic fabrics. If the wrong person sees the stitching of your clothes, they might call you a witch.
2.  **The Acquisition:** You are starving. The smell of roasted chestnuts and ale wafts from a nearby tavern called *The King’s Head*. You reach into your pocket and find a single gold coin—strange, heavy, and stamped with a face you recognize from history books. You need to buy food, but you must haggle like a local, or the merchant will realize you don't know the value of money.
3.  **The Information:** You overhear two guards talking near a fountain. They mention a "Great Fire" that is rumored to start soon, or perhaps a plague cart rattling down the lane. You realize that while you are living their present, you know their future.

**The dream twists:**
As you take a bite of stale bread, the sky suddenly darkens, not with clouds, but with the shadow of something massive—a clock tower that seems to be melting, or perhaps a sky filled with hot air balloons bearing royal banners.

**What do you do next? Do you try to warn them of the future, or do you keep your head down and try to find a way back to your own time?**
