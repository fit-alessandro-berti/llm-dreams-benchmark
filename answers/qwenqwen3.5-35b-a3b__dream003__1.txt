You realize then that the key was never meant to be found *for* you, but made *from* you.

You close your eyes and let the vibration sync with your own heartbeat. The box is no longer heavy; it feels like a feather weight of a secret you’ve carried since birth. Slowly, without any mechanical click, the seams between the lid and the base begin to glow. A soft, amber light spills out—not burning, but illuminating.

The lid doesn't lift; it dissolves into mist.

Inside, there is no gold, no jewels, and no object. There is only a mirror, and in that reflection, you see yourself standing outside of this room, looking in through the window. Outside the room, the sky is turning a bruised purple. The dream is ending, but the box isn't empty.

As the light fades, you understand: the box wasn't a container for things. It was a container for *time*. And you’ve just opened a minute that wasn't yours to spend. You wake up, but the warmth of the metal stays in your hands for hours.
