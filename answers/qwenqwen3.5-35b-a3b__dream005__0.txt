One group argues over a clock that counts down to tomorrow, their voices rising in pitch like violins stretching to a snap. Another is made entirely of paper cranes, flapping rhythmically against the marble floor, while you pass through them like a ghost, feeling only the crinkle of wings.

The hallway stretches longer the faster you move. It smells of ozone and old books. You find yourself running on the ceiling now, the floor turning to liquid glass beneath your heels, but you don't fall because you know you aren't supposed to. 

Then, you see them. They are standing in the center of the room, wearing your own coat. They hold a lantern that burns with a cold, blue flame. You reach out, and their hand passes through yours, feeling like static electricity. You want to ask them why they brought you here, but your mouth is filled with gravel, and the sound of the gathering shifts to the roar of a train moving through a tunnel.

They turn to you, and their face is your own, but younger, eyes wide with the realization that the dream has just begun. 

Then, the blue light of the lantern swallows the room, and you are suddenly standing on the edge of your mattress, the morning sun blindingly white against the dark of the sheets.
