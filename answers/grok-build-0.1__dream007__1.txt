You are in the middle of a heated argument with a close friend. The disagreement is about something important to both of you, and you decide to pause mid-sentence, step back, and ask them a simple question: "What are you actually afraid of losing here?" 

Their face softens in the strange, liquid way faces do in dreams. The room around you begins to dissolve into swirling fog, but instead of vanishing, the fog condenses into a vast, starlit plain where the two of you are suddenly standing on opposite ends of an old wooden bridge. The argument doesn't disappear—it becomes the bridge itself, creaking and swaying with every word you exchange. 

You walk toward each other. With each step the planks beneath your feet turn into memories: the time they helped you move, the night you stayed up talking until dawn, the stupid inside joke that still makes you both laugh. By the time you meet in the middle, the bridge has become a bench. You sit down together. The important thing you were fighting about is still there, but it feels smaller now, like a single bright star rather than the whole sky.

Your friend looks at you and says, "I was scared you wouldn't understand." You realize you were scared of the same thing. The dream doesn't end with a dramatic resolution or a winner—it just softens. The stars above you rearrange themselves into a shape that looks suspiciously like both of your silhouettes, side by side. 

You wake up with the strange, lingering feeling that the argument is already half-healed, even if you haven't spoken yet.
