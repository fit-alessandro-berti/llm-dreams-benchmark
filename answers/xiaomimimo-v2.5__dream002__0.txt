Thunder rumbles — not overhead yet, but somewhere distant, like the sky is clearing its throat before a great announcement. Fat drops of rain begin to fall, each one heavy and warm against your skin.

Then something strange happens.

You notice a **flickering light** ahead between the trunks — soft, amber, almost like a lantern. As you move toward it, the rain seems to slow around you, as if time itself is thickening. Each droplet hangs suspended for just a moment longer than it should.

The light leads you to a **small stone bridge** arching over a stream you hadn't noticed before. Under the bridge, sheltered from the storm, sits an old wooden door — standing alone, unattached to any wall.

It has **no frame, no house, no walls** — just a door, weathered and carved with symbols that seem to shift when you're not looking directly at them.

The storm roars behind you.

The door waits.

A single raindrop hangs motionless an inch from your nose.
