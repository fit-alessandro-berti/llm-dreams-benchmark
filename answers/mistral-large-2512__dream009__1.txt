You wake up on a bed of damp straw, the scent of woodsmoke and earth thick in the air. The room is dim, lit only by flickering torchlight that casts long, wavering shadows across rough-hewn stone walls. Outside, the distant clang of a blacksmith’s hammer echoes, mingling with the lowing of cattle and the murmur of voices speaking in a tongue that sounds familiar yet strange—Old English, perhaps, or something even older.

You sit up, your modern clothes replaced by a coarse linen tunic and woolen hose, your feet bare on the cold floor. A leather pouch lies beside you, its contents clinking softly. Inside: a few silver coins stamped with unfamiliar symbols, a flint and steel, a folded scrap of parchment with a crude map, and a small, polished stone that hums faintly when you touch it.

As you step outside, the world unfolds like a living tapestry. The village is a cluster of thatched cottages, smoke curling from their chimneys, surrounded by fields of golden wheat and grazing sheep. Beyond the palisade, a dense forest looms, its edges shrouded in mist. A river glints in the distance, its banks lined with willows. There are no cars, no planes, no hum of electricity—just the raw, unfiltered pulse of life.

But something is *wrong*. The villagers eye you with a mix of curiosity and suspicion. A child points and whispers, *"The stranger from the sky."* An old woman with milky eyes grabs your wrist, her grip like iron. *"You carry the mark,"* she hisses, tracing a symbol on your palm—one that wasn’t there before. *"The old ones are waking. You must go to the standing stones before the blood moon rises."*

Before you can ask what she means, a horn blares. The villagers scatter, grabbing tools and weapons. A rider thunders into the square, his horse lathered in sweat. *"Raiders!"* he shouts. *"From the north—they burn everything!"*

Panic erupts. The village elder, a grizzled man with a scarred face, shoves a rusted sword into your hands. *"You fight or you die,"* he growls. *"Choose."*

But as you grip the hilt, the ground trembles. The sky darkens unnaturally, as if the sun is being swallowed. The villagers scream, pointing at the horizon where the forest seems to *breathe*, the trees twisting into grotesque shapes. The air smells of ozone and something older—something *hungry*.

The rider grabs your arm. *"Forget the raiders. The stones. *Now.*"*

And then you’re running, the sword heavy in your hand, the world around you unraveling like a frayed thread. The path to the standing stones is lined with figures—pale, gaunt things with too many joints, their eyes hollow. They whisper in a language that makes your teeth ache.

At the center of the stone circle, a figure stands, robed in black, their face hidden. They raise a hand, and the sky *splits*—not with lightning, but with *memory*. Visions flash: a battlefield, a burning city, a cave where something ancient stirs. And then, a voice, neither male nor female, but *vast*:

*"You are the key. The door is open. Will you close it… or walk through?"*

The dream fractures. The stones begin to sink into the earth, the robed figure dissolving like smoke. The last thing you see is the polished stone in your pouch—now glowing like a dying star.

And then—

---

**You wake up.**

Your heart hammers. The room is dark, your sheets tangled. Your palm *burns* where the symbol was. And on your nightstand, half-buried under your phone, is a small, smooth stone.

It wasn’t there before.
