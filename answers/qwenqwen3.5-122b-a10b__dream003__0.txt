You lean in closer, and the air around the box smells like rain on hot pavement and old books.

As you trace a swirling groove with your thumb, the pattern glows with a soft, amber light. A low hum vibrates through the box, resonating in your chest. Suddenly, the edges of the box soften, turning from cold iron into something like woven smoke.

You don't open it. Instead, the box opens *you*.

The lid dissolves into a swarm of golden moths that flutter upward, carrying your consciousness with them. You are no longer standing in a room; you are floating in a library where the books are made of clouds and the words fly off the pages like birds. The box was never meant to hold something. It was meant to let you out.

And then, the alarm clock rings, but the moths are still in your hand, glowing faintly in the morning light.
