...Despite the hardships, you decide to take off your shoes. 

The ash covering the ground shouldn't feel soft, but it does. It feels like warm, powdered sugar between your toes. The jagged, rusted skeletons of old cars and the shattered glass from skyscrapers lose their sharp edges in this strange light; everything seems blunt, wrapped in a thick layer of gray velvet.

You walk past the hollowed-out ruins. Somewhere in the peripheral shadows, there are things with too many limbs and heavy, dragging steps— the dangers you are supposed to fear. But as you walk, you don't hide. You just hum a tune you don't remember learning, and the heavy dragging stops. When you glance back, the monsters are just tall, sad piles of dark coats, shivering in the wind. 

You decide you are tired of surviving. You want to build something. 

Reaching into your pocket, you expect to find nothing, but your hand closes around a smooth, heavy marble. When you pull it out, it isn't a marble at all, but a tiny, pulsing sphere of golden light, humming with a low, sweet frequency. 

You crouch down and push the sphere into the ash. You don't need water; you just whisper a secret to the dirt—a word you make up on the spot that sounds like *home*.

Instantly, a tree erupts from the earth. It doesn't have bark; its trunk is made of woven blue neon tubes, and its leaves are panes of stained glass in shades of turquoise and crimson. The glass chimes softly as a breeze blows through it, a breeze that suddenly smells heavily of rain and fresh bread. 

The scarcity of the world doesn't apply here. You reach up and pluck a glass leaf, and the moment it snaps off, it transforms into a perfectly ripe peach in your palm. You take a bite, and the juice tastes like relief.

The shivering coat-monsters from the shadows slowly shuffle over. They sit at the base of the neon tree, holding out empty, frayed sleeves. You pick more leaves, turning them into peaches, into canteens of clean water, into thick woolen blankets, handing them out until there is nothing left to fear.

You lean your back against the warm, buzzing neon trunk and look up at the sky. The sky, which was a perpetual, choking brown, begins to crack like an old shell. Through the fractures, a blinding, beautiful white light pours through, turning the gray ash into a field of glittering snow.

The light gets brighter and brighter, the chiming of the glass leaves grows louder, and the ground beneath you feels less like a wasteland and more like the surface of a cloud. The light completely swallows the world...

And you open your eyes. You are in your bed. The room is dim with early morning light, the sheets are tangled around your legs, and the alarm clock is blaring its jagged, mechanical scream, pulling you entirely out of the garden.
