You find yourself in a post-apocalyptic world, with resources scarce and dangers everywhere. Despite the hardships, you decide to head toward the faint crackle of a radio signal you picked up on a battered device—hoping to find other survivors.

The landscape is a skeleton of what once was. Skeletal buildings claw at a permanently overcast sky. The air tastes of rust and old ash. In your pack: half a bottle of filtered water, a multi-tool, and a photograph of a sunlit coast you can barely remember.

You move through the overgrown streets, every sense alert. The distant groan of shifting metal could be the wind… or something else. Your goal is the old transmission tower on the hill, a silhouette against the bruised twilight.

As you approach a collapsed highway overpass, you see three paths:

1.  **The Drainage Culvert:** A dark, concrete tunnel snaking under the rubble. It would provide cover, but you can't see what's inside.
2.  **The Shattered Department Store:** A husk of glass and steel. You spot the glint of possible supplies in the debris, but the structure groans ominously.
3.  **The Clear Path Along the Riverbed:** A dried-up river offers open sightlines, but you'd be completely exposed.

The rain begins to fall—a gritty, acidic drizzle. What do you do?
