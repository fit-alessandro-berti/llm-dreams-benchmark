The patterns seem to shift as you watch them—swirling like ink dropped in water, resolving for a heartbeat into shapes that almost mean something. A spiral. A doorway. A single unblinking eye.

You trace a finger along one groove without quite deciding to, and it feels warm, faintly pulsing, like the box has a heartbeat of its own. The lock isn't a lock at all, you realize—it's a small brass dial etched with symbols that don't belong to any language you know, and yet you understand them anyway, the way you understand things in dreams: not by reading but by remembering.

You turn the dial. It clicks in a way that seems louder than it should be, echoing off walls you didn't notice a moment ago. The room around you has changed—or maybe it was always like this and you're only now seeing it: shelves of identical boxes stretching up into a ceiling that isn't there, each one waiting, each one humming faintly with the same warmth.

The lid of your box lifts on its own, just a crack, and light spills out—not bright, but deep, the color of a memory you can't quite place. A voice, or maybe just a feeling shaped like a voice, says:

*"You already know what's inside. You put it there."*

You lean closer, breath caught, and see—

what do you think is inside? I can keep dreaming it either way you like: something personal and emotional, something strange and surreal, or something quietly unsettling.
