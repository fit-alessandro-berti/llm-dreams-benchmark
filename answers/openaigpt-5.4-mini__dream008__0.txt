You pull open “confidence,” and out steps a version of you wearing sunglasses and holding a cup that says **YOU GOT THIS**. They nod toward the project and say, “The deadline isn’t impossible. It’s just dreaming.”

So you begin.

Tasks rearrange themselves into a dance. Emails fold into paper birds and fly off toward the ceiling. The impossible project becomes a puzzle, then a map, then a bridge. Your boss is still waiting, but now they’re not impatient—they’re curious.

And as the last piece clicks into place, the office transforms into sunrise.

You finish just in time to notice that the impossible deadline was written in moonlight, and moonlight, as everyone knows, can always be rewritten.
