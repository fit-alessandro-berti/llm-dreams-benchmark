The leaves begin to tremble, first softly, then all at once, as if the whole forest has drawn a breath. A cold wind sweeps down the path behind you, carrying the scent of rain, moss, and something strangely familiar—like a place you have been before but cannot name.

You quicken your pace.

Ahead, the trail bends toward a clearing where an old stone arch stands alone, covered in ivy. There is no wall attached to it, no building, only the arch and the darkness beneath it. Lightning flashes, and for an instant you see someone standing on the other side: a figure in a rain-soaked cloak, holding a lantern.

They raise one hand and beckon.

Thunder rolls through the forest, but instead of frightening you, it sounds almost like a drumbeat. The storm breaks. Rain falls in silver sheets, yet none of it touches you. The drops hang around you in the air like tiny glass stars.

You step beneath the arch.

The forest disappears.

Now you are standing on the shore of a vast black lake under a calm, moonlit sky. The storm is gone, but you can still hear it faintly behind you, as though it belongs to another world. Across the water, a house glows with warm golden light. Its windows are open, and from inside comes the sound of laughter.

The figure with the lantern stands beside you.

“You made it before the storm,” they say.

You turn to ask who they are, but their hood falls back, and you realize you are looking at yourself—older, kinder, and smiling as if you have been waiting here for a long time.

They hand you the lantern.

The light inside is not flame, but a small swirling sky full of stars.

“Take this,” they say. “You’ll need it when you wake.”

And as you look down into the lantern, the stars begin to rearrange themselves into a path, one that leads not across the lake, but inward, through memories, forgotten hopes, and doors you once thought were closed forever.

Then the wind rises again, gentle this time.

You hear your name spoken from very far away.

The lantern glows brighter.

And you wake with the feeling that somewhere, deep in the forest of your mind, a warm light is still waiting for you.
