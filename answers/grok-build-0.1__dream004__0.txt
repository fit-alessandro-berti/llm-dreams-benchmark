You reach out to touch the blank canvas, and the moment your fingers brush its surface, it begins to ripple like liquid light. Colors bleed from the shifting world around you—impossible shades that don't exist in waking life—pouring onto the canvas in swirling vortices of geometry. Triangles melt into spirals, and spirals fracture into faces that might be yours, or perhaps no one's at all.

The canvas pulls you in. Suddenly you're standing *inside* it, the landscape inverted: the ground above you, the sky a vast, pulsing void below. The blankness has become a mirror that refuses to reflect anything static. Shapes form and unform—cities made of sound, rivers of forgotten memories, a flock of birds that are also equations. One of them lands on your shoulder and whispers in a voice like cracking paint: "What will you paint now that the world is yours to rewrite?"

You feel a brush materialize in your hand, heavy with potential. The colors wait, trembling. What do you create?
