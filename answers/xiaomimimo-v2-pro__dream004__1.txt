...and beside it rests a brush that seems to hum with quiet energy, as if it's been waiting for me all along.

I pick it up. The bristles feel warm against my fingertips, and the moment I hold it, the landscape around me seems to pause—just for a breath—and *watch*.

I touch the brush to the canvas, and where I expect paint, something far stranger emerges. A color I've never seen before bleeds outward—not quite blue, not quite gold, but something in between that feels like the sound of wind chimes looks. It moves on its own, curling into shapes that almost make sense before dissolving into something new.

I paint a tree, and its branches grow right off the canvas, stretching into the sky where they catch drifting cubes of moonlight. I paint a river, and I can suddenly hear it—though the sound isn't water. It's laughter, layered and overlapping, like a crowd of old friends remembering the same joke.

I realize the canvas isn't just a surface. It's a *window*, and whatever I imagine passes through it, transforming on the way into this world. I start painting faster—a door, a staircase, a clock with no numbers—and each one reshapes the landscape around me. The ground becomes a mosaic of everything I've ever loved and forgotten.

Then I pause, brush hovering, and wonder: *Am I painting the dream, or is the dream painting me?*

The brush hums a little louder, as if to say: *Does it matter?*

I smile and keep going. There's so much canvas left. 🎨
