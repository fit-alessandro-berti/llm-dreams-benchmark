...walk away.

Your feet carry you before your mind catches up. The kitchen dissolves behind you—her voice calling your name becomes the sound of wind through pine needles. The linoleum gives way to moss. You're walking down a hallway that stretches longer than any apartment has a right to be, doors lining both sides, each one painted a different shade of blue.

You try the first handle. Locked.

Second. Locked.

Third swings open into a childhood bedroom—yours, but wrong. The posters on the walls are blank white paper. The closet door hangs open, revealing not clothes but a narrow staircase descending into darkness. You hear her laughter echoing up from below, young and uncomplicated, the way she laughed before the thing you can't name happened between you.

You descend.

The stairs end in a library where the books have no titles. You pull one down, open it, and find pages filled with handwriting—yours and hers, tangled together, sentences interrupting each other mid-thought. Every argument you've ever had, recorded in real time. You flip toward the end. The last entry is still being written, the ink wet, the words forming as you watch:

*I didn't mean—*

*You never mean—*

*Then why—*

A hand rests on your shoulder. Not hers. Your own hand, from a version of you that's older, tired, wearing a ring you don't recognize. This other-you shakes its head. *Don't read the ending. It changes every time you look.*

The library catches fire. Not with flames—with paper. Pages burning upward into birds. You wake up gasping, your phone screen lighting the dark room with a text from her: *Can we talk?*

Your thumb hovers over the screen. The dream presses against your ribs like a held breath.
