…open your laptop, but the screen is filled with an ocean.

Emails drift beneath the waves like pale fish. Your calendar has become a tide chart. Every meeting is scheduled for yesterday.

Your boss stands behind you, smiling too widely.

“Just get the first draft to me before lunch,” they say.

You look at the clock. Its hands are spinning backward, faster and faster, until they fly off and land on the floor like tiny silver needles.

You begin typing anyway.

Each word you write appears on the walls of the office in enormous black letters. The fluorescent lights flicker. Your coworkers turn slowly in their chairs and applaud without making a sound.

Then the printer starts printing page after page of blank paper. You pull one free, and it has only one sentence written on it:

**WAKE UP. THE DEADLINE IS NOT REAL.**

But when you look up, your boss is holding a new stack of assignments.

“This one,” they say, “was due before you were born.”
