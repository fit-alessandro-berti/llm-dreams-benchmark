You try to protest, but your chair sprouts roots, anchoring you to the floor, which ripples like a black mirror. A flock of origami cranes made of sticky notes bursts from your inbox, each one carrying a different impossible task — build a spreadsheet out of water, format the cloud, align the stars into a PowerPoint. Your boss’s sunflower-head begins to hum the company’s hold music as the walls start peeling upward, turning into origami skyscrapers. You laugh—because in a dream, you somehow know—that the impossible deadline is just a riddle the universe is asking, and the answer was always *sleep*.
