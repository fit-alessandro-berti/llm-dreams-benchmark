Thunder rolls, but it sounds wrong—too melodic, almost like a held chord on some enormous instrument. I look up and the clouds are moving in a spiral, slow and deliberate, like they're draining toward a point directly above me.

There's a door.

It's just standing there between two birches, no walls around it, weathered wood with a brass handle gone green with age. It wasn't there a second ago. Rain starts—but it's warm, and it smells like static electricity and crushed rosemary.

I know, the way you know things in dreams, that I'm supposed to open it before the storm fully arrives. My hand is already reaching for the handle. Something on the other side is waiting, patient and old, and I get the feeling it's been waiting a very long time.

The handle turns easily.

Light spills out—not blinding, but *warm*, gold like late afternoon sun, completely at odds with the storm-dark forest around me. And there's a sound coming through, faint at first: someone laughing. Familiar laughing, though I can't place whose.

I step through—
