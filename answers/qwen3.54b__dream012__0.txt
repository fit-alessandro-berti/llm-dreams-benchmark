You close your eyes against the howling wind, forcing yourself to hear the rhythm of the waves instead. As you reach out for the wheel, your hand meets not cold iron, but something warm and pulsing—like a heartbeat within bone. You shout your command, a voice that echoes strangely through the mist: "Hold steady! The storm is only a memory waiting to drown!" And as the crew nods in obedience, the sea begins to smooth itself into an endless night. You realize with a sudden clarity that the ship has no name, nor coastlines, but only your own breath guiding it; you stop fighting the current and accept the water, knowing now that calm is not what comes after the chaos, but what remains inside you.
