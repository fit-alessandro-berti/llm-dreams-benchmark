...intricate patterns and spiraling symbols etched into the metal, shifting slightly when you’re not looking directly at them. They feel familiar, like a language you almost remember from childhood.

When you run your fingers across the surface, the patterns warm beneath your touch. The box hums softly, like a distant choir heard through water. One of the spirals rearranges itself into the shape of your palm.

Without thinking, you place your hand over it.

The room around you dissolves into fog.

The box opens—not outward, but inward, folding into impossible layers. Inside is not an object but a small sky, deep and midnight-blue, scattered with slow-moving stars. One of the stars drifts toward you, pulsing gently, as if it recognizes you.

When it reaches your fingertips, you suddenly remember:

You were the one who locked the box.
And whatever is inside was never meant to stay closed forever.
