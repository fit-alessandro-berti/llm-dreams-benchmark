You notice the questions are written on paper that seems to absorb light, casting a shimmering halo around each page and making the text appear as though it is hovering slightly above your index finger. But they aren't just paper anymore; they are glowing with an internal warmth that feels like you're staring at your own hands through the glass of your mind.

The ink doesn't dry; it seems to be slowly evaporating, turning into a mist before you blink.

You notice the questions are **wrongly easy.** 

But why? You can see them clearly in your vision now, floating just below the line of sight, not like they're part of a test sheet but rather pieces of your own memory rearranged. Some question marks look like tears on a windowpane, and others seem to be writing themselves out with every breath you take.

One of them flickers with words in a language that isn't in your vocabulary, asking you if you know the name of a color that exists only when no one is looking at it. You feel a sudden urge to answer it, but there's no way to stop your hand from reaching out, and the moment you touch the question, the paper suddenly snaps shut like a hinge you just opened, sealing the mystery inside the crease.

You notice the questions are **too easy.**

But that's why they feel real. They don't ask what will happen; they ask *why* you're still sitting there. You take another breath, thinking about the exam room where everyone else is checking their phones or nodding off into a slumber.

The paper hums under your touch as the ink begins to run. It looks like blood on parchment. And then, suddenly, one question lights up in green. **Your name.** But it's not a pen name. It's typed directly in your brain.

You notice the questions are **unanswerable.** 

But you have no choice but to answer them as if they were instructions for the universe itself. You press paper into the palm of your hand, and something shifts beneath that texture. You realize that the exam room outside, where you're supposed to sit alone in a chair waiting for the bell to ring, has actually turned back to dust and gone silent. The questions are no longer there to be answered yet. They have simply been... *seen* by your mind before this moment began.

You feel a sudden, sharp clarity that isn't part of the exam structure at all. It's an echo of the silence outside. You close your eyes again, and the questions don't appear anymore on the page. You're just sitting in the room, holding the paper now only as a heavy thing against someone else's hand, and you realize that you are dreaming of a future where every question is already solved in the silence of your sleep.
