You are the captain of a ship in the middle of a turbulent sea. Your crew looks to you for guidance as the storm worsens, and you grip the wheel with both hands, trying to keep the bow pointed into the towering waves.

The sky is an unnatural shade of violet. Lightning flashes silently, illuminating faces that seem familiar and unfamiliar at the same time. Every crew member appears to be someone you've known at a different stage of your life.

A sailor rushes to your side and points ahead.

"There!" he shouts, though somehow you hear him clearly despite the wind.

Far beyond the crashing water, a lighthouse stands on the horizon. Its beam sweeps across the sea, but instead of white light, it casts golden paths that briefly solidify on the surface of the water before dissolving again.

The compass beside you begins spinning wildly.

"Captain," another crew member says, "the compass won't tell us where to go anymore."

You look down and realize the compass face has changed. Instead of directions, it displays memories: a childhood home, a classroom, a train station, a doorway you've never opened.

The storm intensifies. Waves rise like mountains.

Then the lighthouse beam settles on a single point in the darkness.

Instinctively, you turn the ship toward it.

The sea immediately becomes calm around the vessel, forming a narrow corridor through the chaos. On either side, the storm continues to rage, but your ship glides forward as if carried by an invisible current.

The crew watches in awe.

As you approach the lighthouse, you notice it isn't standing on land at all. It rests atop an enormous sea turtle whose shell is covered with glowing constellations. The stars on its back slowly rearrange themselves into a map.

The turtle lifts its head and speaks.

"You were never steering through the storm," it says. "You were steering through your fear of it."

Before you can answer, the ship begins to rise. The ocean beneath transforms into clouds, and the sails fill with starlight instead of wind.

The crew cheers.

Ahead, beyond the clouds, a harbor appears. Every building seems to be made from pieces of places you've visited in your life. Warm lights glow from every window.

As the ship enters the harbor, bells ring across the water.

You step onto the dock and turn to thank your crew, but the ship is gone.

So is the harbor.

So is the sea.

You are standing alone in a quiet room. On a small wooden table sits the same compass.

For the first time, it points steadily in one direction.

You reach out to see where it leads—

—and wake up just before your fingers touch it.
