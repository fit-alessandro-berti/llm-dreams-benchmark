...build a sanctuary not of concrete and steel, but of living, breathing glass.

You raise your hands, and where your fingers trace the air, translucent walls shimmer into existence. They are not fragile; they are woven from hardened light and the resilience of the earth itself. You call it **The Prism**.

Around you, the gray, choking smog of the wasteland presses against the walls, but inside, the air is crisp and filtered by the humming vegetation that grows in spirals along the base of the dome. You discover that within this dream, your despair has transformed into a strange, architectural creativity.

You walk to the center of the sanctuary and plant a single seed in the dirt floor. As you exhale, the seed erupts into a towering tree with leaves of iridescent blue. Its roots dig deep into the scarred earth, siphoning pure water from forgotten aquifers, while its branches spread outward, creating a canopy that glows with a soft, lunar light.

**The Encounter:**
Just as you admire your work, a shadow moves outside the glass. A scavenger—clad in rags and rusted armor—stops dead in their tracks, staring at the impossible structure. They raise a jagged weapon, threatening to shatter your peace.

But you do not fear. You walk to the wall and press your palm against the cold surface. The glass ripples like water. Instead of an attack, you project a vision through the wall: a memory of a time before the bombs, a field of sunflowers turning their faces to a golden sun.

The scavenger lowers their weapon. Their shoulders drop. They step closer, not to break in, but to look.

**The Resolution:**
You realize then that survival isn't just about hoarding canned food and ammunition. It’s about preserving beauty in a world that has forgotten it. You wave your hand, and a section of the wall dissolves, inviting them in.

In this dream, the apocalypse isn't the end. It is merely the clearing of the canvas, and you are the artist painting a new dawn.
