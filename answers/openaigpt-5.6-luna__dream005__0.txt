You are at a large social gathering where you only know one person. As you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people and each group insists that your friend has just left—through the garden, upstairs, into the kitchen, or out the red door at the end of the hall.

You follow the directions, but the house keeps changing. The staircase bends back into the ballroom. The kitchen opens onto a beach. Outside, hundreds of people are wearing identical masks, all turned toward the sea.

Then you hear your name being called.

You hurry toward the voice and find your friend standing beneath a tree covered in small, glowing clocks. They smile with relief and say, “I’ve been looking everywhere for you.”

Before you can answer, the music stops. Everyone at the party turns toward you. Your friend takes your hand and whispers, “Whatever happens, don’t tell them why you came.”

A door appears in the trunk of the tree. You open it and step through—into your own bedroom, where the party is still going on, and where another version of you is standing by the window, waiting for someone to arrive.
