...and as you reach toward it, your fingers dissolve into ribbons of cerulean light that spiral onto the surface, painting themselves.

The canvas drinks the color thirstily, then exhales—and the painting begins to breathe. Hills of violet mist rise from the fibers. Trees made of whispered words grow sideways, their leaves flickering between geometric certainty and soft organic blur.

You step closer. Your reflection appears in the canvas, but not as you are—instead, you are a kaleidoscope version of yourself, fragmented into a mosaic of moments: a childhood laugh here, a forgotten sorrow there, all shimmering like oil on water.

The canvas tilts forward, swallowing you.

Now you are *inside* the painting. The ground beneath your feet is made of brushstrokes—thick, impasto ridges you can feel between your toes. The sky is a wash of wet watercolor, still dripping and blending at the edges where peach bleeds into midnight.

A figure approaches. It has no fixed form—shifting between a heron, a woman, a tower of stacked spheres, a melody you almost recognize.

"You're the artist and the art," it says, in a voice like wind chimes made of glass hearts.

It extends something toward you—a brush, a pen, a wand, a seed—changing shape with each heartbeat.

"What will you create?" it asks.

The blank spaces around you hum with possibility, waiting.
