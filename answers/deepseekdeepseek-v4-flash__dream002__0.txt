You feel a storm approaching and quicken your pace, but the trees around you begin to twist and groan, their branches reaching out like skeletal fingers. The sky turns a bruised purple, and the wind howls with a voice that sounds almost human. Then, the rain starts—not drops, but tiny, shimmering crystals that tinkle as they hit the leaves. Each crystal pulses with a faint light, and as they land on your skin, they whisper fragments of forgotten memories. Ahead, a massive oak splits open, revealing a spiral staircase descending into the earth, lit by a soft, golden glow. Thunder rumbles, and you hear a voice—your own—echo from the staircase: "Come down, before the storm remembers who you are."
