...find my bearings before the sun sets.

The air smells different here—woodsmoke and something earthier, like damp hay and animal musk. I'm standing on a dirt road that winds between fields of golden wheat, and in the distance, I can see the silhouette of a walled town, its stone towers catching the last amber light of day.

My clothes have changed too. Rough linen tunic, leather belt, worn boots that feel like they've walked a thousand miles before I ever slipped my feet inside them. There's a small pouch at my hip with a few odd coins and a piece of folded parchment I can't quite read—the letters swim in ways that feel both foreign and dreamlike.

As I walk toward the town, a farmer passes me with a cart pulled by a shaggy ox. He nods as though he knows me. \ again, eh?\ my ears tell me it isn't English.

I nod back, uncertain, and keep walking.

At the town gate, a guard in a rust-spotted helmet waves me through without a glance. Inside, the streets are narrow and crooked, lined with timber-framed houses leaning into each other like gossiping neighbors. Children chase a dog past a blacksmith's forge. A woman hangs washing from a line strung between windows.

I realize I need to find shelter, food, and—somehow—a way to understand why I'm here. Is this a punishment? A gift? A test?

Just as I'm about to ask a passing stranger for help, a bell begins to toll from the church tower, and everyone around me stops and turns their faces skyward, as though waiting for something I cannot yet see...

And then I wake up, the bell still echoing somewhere behind my eyes.
