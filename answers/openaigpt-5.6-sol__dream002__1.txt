Wind rushes through the branches, turning every leaf silver. Thunder rolls beneath the earth rather than across the sky, and the path ahead splits into three glowing trails. Before you can choose, the first raindrop falls upward from a puddle and lands in the clouds.

A white deer steps from the shadows. Between its antlers hangs a tiny lantern, flickering with warm blue light. It looks back at you, waiting.

You follow.

The storm grows wild, but around the deer there is perfect stillness. Soon the forest opens onto a clearing where an old wooden door stands alone, framed by ivy. There is no wall, no house—only the door. Carved into it are the words:

**WHAT YOU SEEK IS ALREADY WAITING.**

You reach for the handle. The moment you touch it, the thunder stops.

Beyond the door is your childhood bedroom, exactly as you remember it, except that the ceiling is filled with stars. On the bed sits a small box wrapped in red string. Inside, you find a single feather and a note written in your own handwriting:

**When the storm returns, remember—you are not lost. You are being called home.**

The lantern goes out behind you.

You turn, but the forest is gone. The stars begin to drift down around you like snow, and as one touches your forehead, you wake with the faint sound of rain against the window.
