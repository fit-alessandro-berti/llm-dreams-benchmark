You are walking through a peaceful forest when suddenly the weather changes dramatically. You feel a storm approaching and the sky darkens from cerulean to a deep, bruised violet in what feels like seconds. The trees, which had been swaying gently, now bend and groan as if whispering urgent warnings to one another.

You notice the birds have gone completely silent.

The first drop of rain hits your arm, but it's warm — impossibly warm — and glows faintly blue where it lands on your skin. You look down and watch it trace a luminous trail across your wrist before fading.

Thunder rolls, but it doesn't sound like thunder. It sounds like a voice, deep and resonant, speaking a language you somehow almost understand — like a word sitting on the tip of your tongue.

You realize your feet are no longer touching the ground.

The forest floor has become a layer of fog, and you're hovering just inches above it. The moss and fallen leaves ripple beneath you like the surface of a pond. You don't feel afraid. Instead, there's a strange recognition, as if some part of you has been waiting for this moment.

Ahead, between the ancient trunks, you see a light — not lightning, but something steadier. Something deliberate. It pulses gently, calling you forward.

The rain falls harder now, each drop a tiny star against the darkness.

You take a step forward into the air, and it holds you.

Where do you go next?
