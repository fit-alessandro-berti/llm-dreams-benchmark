The puzzles shift. They are no longer mechanical gears or sliding tiles, but abstract emotional riddles. To pass through a doorway, you must not find a key, but recall the exact feeling of a childhood summer afternoon or solve a paradox involving a color that doesn't exist. 

The air grows thick with the scent of ozone and old parchment, and the silence becomes a sound of its own—a low, humming vibration that matches the frequency of your own thoughts. You notice that with every puzzle solved, a piece of your own memory detaches and embeds itself into the walls, becoming a new puzzle for someone else to solve.

Finally, you reach the center. There is no exit, no trophy, and no monster. There is only a mirror reflecting not your face, but a complex, shimmering geometric shape that is constantly unfolding. You realize the maze wasn't designed to keep you out or lead you to a destination; the maze *is* you. Every turn you took, every logical leap you made, was simply you mapping the architecture of your own consciousness.

As you reach out to touch the mirror, the glass ripples like water, and the logic of the maze collapses into a single, blinding point of light, pulling you upward and outward until the walls dissolve into the morning mist of waking up.
