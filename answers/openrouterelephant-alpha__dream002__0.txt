...you feel a storm approaching and the sky fractures like glass, splitting into a million prismatic shards that hover in the air. The light from each shard is different—one pool of liquid gold, another deep ocean blue, a third the color of a bruise.

You don’t run. In dreams, panic is the only real enemy, and you know this.

You stop walking and plant your feet into the moss. The ground hums. The wind doesn’t howl; it *sings*, weaving through the branches of the ancient trees, turning their leaves into a score of rustling violins. Each note is a color. The higher the note, the brighter the hue.

The approaching storm isn't an ending. It's a canvas.

You lift your hands, and the light shards begin to drift toward you, orbiting your limbs like curious satellites. They pass through you without cold, leaving trails of static warmth. You realize they aren't light at all—they are *possibilities*. The life you didn't live, the words you didn't say, the versions of yourself that exist in other timelines, all condensed into crystalline energy.

A particularly large shard, the color of forgotten birthdays, drifts close. You reach out and touch it.

The dream shifts. You are no longer walking. You are falling, not down, but *through* the layers of the sky. The trees become a memory, the forest floor a story you once told. The storm is now inside you—a gentle, electric pressure behind your ribs.

You land softly on a path made of stardust. It winds upward, leading not to a destination, but to a mirror. You look into it, and your reflection is not your own. It is the forest, whole and breathing, looking back at you with your eyes.

The storm has passed. You are the calm. You are the clearing. And the dream continues, not with weather, but with the quiet hum of being part of everything.
