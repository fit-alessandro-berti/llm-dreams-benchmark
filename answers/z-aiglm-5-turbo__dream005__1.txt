I scan the room and finally spot a flash of the specific color jacket my friend was wearing. Relief washes over me. I push through a group of people who are floating a few inches off the ground, ignoring their protests, and reach out to grab the shoulder of the person in the jacket.

Suddenly, the floor turns to soft, damp sand. I look up, and the "room" is gone. We are on a desert cliff at twilight. The groups of people are now sitting at desks, typing furiously on typewriters that produce no paper, just clicking sounds that sync up with the crashing waves below.

I realize I don't remember my friend's name anymore. I can picture their eyes, but the word for them is gone. I stand there, paralyzed by the realization that I am searching for a concept I can no longer define, while the sand beneath my feet slowly begins to swallow my shoes.
