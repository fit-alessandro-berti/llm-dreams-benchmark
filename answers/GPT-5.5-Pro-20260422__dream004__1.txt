The canvas tilts toward you like a face. Its white surface ripples, and every color in the sky pauses mid-change, holding its breath. You reach out, and your fingertips sink into the fabric as though it were cool water.

A drop of blue escapes from your hand.

It does not fall. It blooms.

The blue becomes a doorway, then a bird, then a staircase spiraling upward into nowhere. Around it, circles unfold into moons, triangles purr like sleeping cats, and long ribbons of green wind themselves around your ankles, gently tugging you closer.

You laugh, though you are not sure why, and the sound turns into gold dust.

The canvas drinks it in.

Suddenly, the whole landscape bends toward the blankness. Mountains made of glass lean down to watch. Clouds shaped like keys unlock themselves. A river of shifting violet runs backward through the air and pours into the corner of the canvas, where it becomes a tiny house with a red door.

The door opens.

Inside is another canvas.

And on that canvas, you see yourself standing in a world where colors and shapes are constantly changing, reaching toward a blank white surface.

For a moment, you feel the dream notice you.

Then the paint begins to move on its own. It draws a sun with too many eyes, a forest of hands waving goodbye, a silver path leading into the sky. At the very center, it paints a small black dot.

The dot grows.

Not into darkness, but into quiet.

All the colors stop changing. The shapes settle. The impossible world becomes still, as if it has finally exhaled.

From the silence, a voice made of brushstrokes whispers:

“Now choose what wakes up with you.”

You look down and find a paintbrush in your hand.

The bristles are made of your own eyelashes.

And before the dream can end, you paint a single door—bright, familiar, and almost real enough to open.
