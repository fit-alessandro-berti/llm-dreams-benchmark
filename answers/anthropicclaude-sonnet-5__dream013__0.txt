# The Dream Continues...

...notice that the walls themselves seem to shift subtly whenever you look away, as if the maze is breathing. Each puzzle you solve doesn't open a door—it rearranges the very fabric of the corridors around you.

The first puzzle appears as glowing symbols etched into an obsidian wall: a sequence of numbers that seems mathematical but keeps changing meaning depending on how long you stare at it. You solve it—somehow—and the wall dissolves into mist, revealing a room filled with mirrors, each showing a slightly different version of yourself solving a different puzzle.

As you press forward, the puzzles become stranger:
- A door that only opens if you can explain why it should stay closed
- A staircase that requires you to count your own footsteps backward before you've taken them
- A riddle whispered by a voice that sounds like your own, but slightly delayed, as if echoing from a future version of you

You start to realize that the maze isn't testing your logic in the way you expected—it's testing your *acceptance* of impossible things. The more you try to force conventional reasoning onto the puzzles, the more tangled and labyrinthine they become. But the moment you simply *accept* a paradox and move with it, the path ahead simplifies, straightens, glows softly with an inviting light.

Deep in the maze's heart, you find a final chamber. In the center floats a single key, rotating slowly, made of the same shifting material as the walls. As you reach for it, you understand—perhaps this is the moment you wake up, or perhaps the key opens not a door, but your own eyes.

---

*What happens when you take the key? Do you want to keep dreaming, or wake up?*
