The walls of the maze shift subtly with each puzzle you solve, the geometry of the stone bricks reconfiguring. The air grows cooler, and the silence is broken only by the soft *click* of mechanisms and the echo of your own footsteps.

The puzzles evolve. What began as logical mazes and patterned sequences now incorporates fragments of memory—a childhood melody woven into a series of pressure plates, a face from a dream reflected in a mirror that must be aligned just so to reveal the next path. Concentration feels like holding water in cupped hands; the deeper you go, the more the surface trembles with distraction.

Then, in a circular chamber, you find not a lock or a lever, but a single, pulsing cube of soft light hovering in the air. It shows you a scene: yourself, solving the puzzles, but the *you* in the vision is always a step ahead, just turning the corner you’re about to reach. The final puzzle, you realize, isn’t in the maze ahead, but in the maze behind. To proceed, you must solve the riddle of the dreamer who is both you and not you.

Do you reach for the cube, or do you turn around to face the path you’ve already walked?
