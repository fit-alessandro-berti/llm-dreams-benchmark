You are at a large social gathering where you only know one person. As you try to find them, you encounter various groups of people and...  

**The first group** is huddled in a corner, laughing loudly but speaking in a language you almost understand—like half-remembered childhood rhymes. Their faces are blurred, shifting between familiar and grotesque, and when one of them turns to you, their eyes are mirrors reflecting the room’s dim, golden light. You realize they’re discussing a secret you once confided in someone long ago, though you can’t place who that was.  

**The second group** is mid-dance, moving in perfect unison to a song you’ve never heard but somehow know word for word. Their movements are hypnotic, almost mechanical, and as you watch, they begin to fade into the wallpaper’s floral pattern—vivid reds and greens bleeding into one another. You notice your own reflection in the mirrored wall behind them: your face is younger, smiling, but your clothes are from a different era.  

**A couple** nearby is arguing quietly, but their words are in reverse, and when you strain to listen, the phrases rearrange themselves into a question: *“Do you remember the night you forgot to look back?”* The woman’s hand grips the man’s wrist too tightly, and her nails leave claw marks that quickly erase themselves.  

**A child** approaches, dragging a dog with the same face as your missing person. The dog’s bark is a recording of your own voice, saying, *“You left something behind.”* The child vanishes, leaving only a single red balloon tied to a chair—its string trailing across the floor like a lifeline.  

You turn a corner and find the person you seek standing beneath a chandelier made of clocks. They’re wearing your clothes, but their posture is wrong, too rigid, and when they speak, their voice is a chorus of the other strangers you’ve met. They mouthed two words: *“Not yet.”*  

The lights flicker, and suddenly, the entire room is underwater. You’re both drowning and floating, and the laughter of the first group echoes like a lullaby. You reach for the red balloon, but your hand passes through it, and the water clears to reveal a door you don’t remember being there.  

Behind it, the dream ends—not with an answer, but with the echo of a question you’ve never asked yourself: *Who were you before you started looking for them?*  

---  
This dream weaves themes of identity, memory, and the tension between seeking connection and self-discovery. The surreal encounters mirror internal conflicts: fear of the unknown, the weight of past choices, and the ambiguity of who we are versus who others expect us to be. The ending leaves you suspended between longing and acceptance, as dreams often do.
